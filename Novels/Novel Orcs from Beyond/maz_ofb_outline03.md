# BOOK THREE: CONVERGENCE

**Target:** ~55,000 words / 11 chapters
**Theme:** Sacrifice. The cost of saving a world, and the paradox at
the heart of time.

---

## Chapter 1 — A Place He Could Call Home

**POV:** Aleron
**Timeline:** Early 811 AW (flashback, overlapping with Book One)
**Location:** Fort Hope
**Word Target:** ~3,000 words

Aleron's decade of stability at Fort Hope. The closest thing to peace.
Then the shockwave from Remular's activation. He falls to his knees. He
knows exactly where it came from. He packs his robes, his curved blade,
his tuning forks, and leaves. A man walking away from the only home he
has found, toward the destiny he has spent a lifetime dreading and
seeking in equal measure.

---

## Chapter 2 — The Defense of Amara

**POV:** Rotating among all protagonists
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Amara
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

The Orceri launch a major assault on Amara. The heroes defend the city.
Every ally matters. Street-by-street combat. Orceri commanders, skymages
on dragon mounts, dwarven hulks. Lyanorra fights in dragon form openly.
The three protagonists anchor different defensive positions. A large-
scale battle testing every bond the trilogy has built. The Orceri
withdraw. This was a probe. The real assault comes through the portal.

---

## Chapter 3 — Rangithyr Prepares

**POV:** Rangithyr
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Ta'Puh, overlooking portal construction
**Word Target:** ~5,000 words

The godslayer oversees portal construction at Gaal'Tarak from the
Ta'Puh side. Strategic review. These defenders are resourceful but few.
He selects his best forces. He polishes his mana scimitar, the weapon
that blocked Stoneshaper's killing blow eight hundred years ago. For the
first time in centuries, he feels alive. If this being enters Mazariim,
nothing the heroes have faced will compare.

---

## Chapter 4 — The Road to Gaal'Tarak

**POV:** Rotating, primary Aizune
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Journey through the Kirak Mountains
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

The heroes learn the portal nears completion. Aleron explains what must
be done: reach the portal, use his gift to seal it. He omits what he
suspects about the cost. The journey east. Character conversations that
feel like farewells. Aizune and Aleron discuss faith and destiny.
Smiggles and Iskar discuss pragmatism and the future. Lyanorra is
quieter than usual.

---

## Chapter 5 — Unthil in the Dark

**POV:** Unthil
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Ruins of Gaal'Tarak
**Word Target:** ~5,000 words

The Orceri assassin secures positions within the ruins. His playful
exterior masks lethal competence. The ruins feel wrong: temporal
distortions, echoes from empty corridors, stone that seems newer than
it should be. The portal shimmers and grows. Orceri forces mass on the
other side. Rangithyr's silhouette visible through the rift.

---

## Chapter 6 — The Collapsed Citadel

**POV:** Rotating among protagonists
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Ruins of Gaal'Tarak
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

Entry into Gaal'Tarak. Once-magnificent dwarven architecture in rubble.
Unstable tunnels, flooded passages. Temporal distortions: flickers, déjà
vu, fractured time. Orceri defenders in confined spaces. The heroes push
deeper, each encounter more costly.

---

## Chapter 7 — Ozmakir's Mirror

**POV:** Ozmakir
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Ozmakir's laboratory, Ta'Puh
**Word Target:** ~5,000 words

Ozmakir monitors events through his mirrors. Reflects on his discovery
of alternate realities, the mathematics of vibrational frequencies,
eleven conquered worlds. He watches the heroes fighting through the
ruins and feels something unfamiliar: uncertainty. Reports to Rangithyr.
The godslayer's response: "Good."

---

## Chapter 8 — Into the Deep

**POV:** Rotating among protagonists
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Deep within Gaal'Tarak
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

Fighting through the heart of the ruins toward the portal chamber.
Orceri infantry, a biomancer, Unthil striking from shadows. Desperate
battles. Each protagonist pushed to their limit. Aleron's amulet glows
green constantly. His body weakens with each step closer to the weak
point. He refuses to stop.

---

## Chapter 9 — The Portal

**POV:** Rotating, primary Aizune
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Portal chamber, Gaal'Tarak
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

The portal chamber. The shimmering distortion in the air, growing.
Through it, massed Orceri forces. Rangithyr's silhouette: over eight
feet, blue-skinned, scimitar in hand. The heroes hold the chamber while
Aleron reaches the portal. A desperate last stand. Each protagonist at
their absolute limit. Lyanorra fights with everything. The line bends
but does not break.

---

## Chapter 10 — The Gift of Thalaman

**POV:** Aleron, then Aizune
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Portal chamber, Gaal'Tarak
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

Aleron reaches the portal. He turns to the heroes. Gratitude, farewell,
understanding. He places his hands on the rift and pours his gift into
the breach. His amulet shatters. Energy erupts from his hands, his
face, exactly as the pyramid murals depicted. The portal collapses.
Aleron's body becomes light, becomes energy, becomes a shockwave
traveling backward through time. The citadel shakes. The heroes flee.
The disaster that destroyed Gaal'Tarak fifty years ago is happening
now, caused by the man who came to stop it. They do not understand the
paradox. They know only that their friend is gone and the portal is
sealed. One hundred and one years.

---

## Chapter 11 — After

**POV:** Rotating among all protagonists
**Timeline:** Late 811 AW
**Location:** Kirak Mountains, then Amara
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

The heroes emerge into daylight. Each processes the loss: Aizune prays,
feeling Minerva more keenly, knowing she still lives because of what
just happened. Smiggles sits in uncharacteristic silence, then speaks
of the mathematics of sacrifice. Iskar looks east, toward Moamon,
toward his captured friend, toward the future.

Return to Amara. The city heals. Aldorph pours drinks. Lionous offers
prayers. Lyanorra watches from a rooftop in dragon form, then shifts
to the small green elven girl and disappears into the crowd.

Somewhere beneath desert sands, Sugnim feels a tremor. He closes his
fox eyes, smiles his toothy smile, and whispers, "Well done."

The trilogy closes.
