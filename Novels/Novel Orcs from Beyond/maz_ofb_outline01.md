# ORCS FROM BEYOND — TRILOGY OUTLINE
## Revised Draft

---

# BOOK ONE: INCURSION

**Target:** ~55,000 words / 11 chapters
**Theme:** Discovery. The world is larger, stranger, and more dangerous
than anyone imagined.

---

## Chapter 1 — The Wizard's Grief

**POV:** Zanthar (prologue perspective)
**Timeline:** 310 BW, then flash-forward to 287 BW
**Location:** Steppes of Broken Stone, Zanthar's underground tower
**Word Target:** ~3,000 words

Zanthar, a fugitive wizard fleeing the Zebulban-controlled Dukhan Empire
after his wife Tatyana was taken and sacrificed for her secret worship of
Tiovin, has hidden himself beneath the desolate Steppes of Broken Stone.
After years of work, he activates his machine for the first time.
Lightning and spells pour into the glass globe, the three great rings
begin to spin, and his consciousness is projected outward. He sees the
familiar Steppes, then the coast, then a city where the Zebulban ziggurat
should stand but does not. He looks up: three moons, not two. Alternate
realities are real.

Flash forward 23 years. Grey-haired, half-mad, surrounded by dozens of
mirrors showing versions of himself living different lives across infinite
worlds (farmer, orator, soldier), Zanthar has never found another version
of Tatyana. In a moment of clarity he realizes his own life was never
lived. He activates the machine one last time, thinking of her. His
concentration falters. He is vaporized. A shockwave collapses the tower
above.

Centuries pass. The images dance in the mirrors. And one day, the shadows
begin to move on their own.

**Purpose:** Establishes the central artifact, the concept of alternate
realities, and the melancholy human cost of obsession. The Whisper of
Zanthar's origin. Sets the tone for the trilogy.

## Chapter 2 — The Battle That Made Two Worlds

**POV:** Omniscient battlefield perspective, close on Rangithyr at the
critical moment
**Timeline:** Close to 1 BW
**Location:** Fort Hope (in the reality that will become Ta'Puh)
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

**Reader orientation note:** The chapter opens with the climactic battle
of the War to End All Wars, presented as history the reader has not yet
learned but will recognize through context. The battle plays out exactly
as it did in Mazariim's timeline: Zuel's avatar fighting Mordrion and
Quarzel, the desperate alliance of all races, Demious Stoneshaper's
heroic charge. At the critical moment, everything changes. The ogre-magi
general Rangithyr slips from the ethereal plane and blocks Stoneshaper's
killing blow. Zuel decapitates the dwarven hero. In the heroes' world,
this blow landed and ended the War. Here, it did not. This is the moment
two realities diverge.

The chapter covers: Zuel destroying Quarzel (permanently, with burning
blue energy pouring from the god of war's avatar). Zuel destroying
Mordrion's avatar (spiked knee driven through his chest, divine energies
streaking toward the heavens). Stoneshaper's charge and Rangithyr's
intervention. Zuel's victory roar. The reader should feel the weight of
what was lost: a single blocked sword stroke condemned an entire world.

**Key detail:** In Chapter 3, the heroes' world is established as the one
where the War ended differently. The reader retroactively understands
Chapter 2 as the road not taken, the parallel history that created the
Orceri civilization. This delayed recognition is intentional.

**Purpose:** Establishes Rangithyr as the ultimate antagonist. Creates the
historical foundation for the Orceri. The epic scope and divine-level
combat sets the ceiling for what power looks like in this world.

## Chapter 3 — The Free City

**POV:** Rotating among protagonists (primary: Aizune)
**Timeline:** Early Garn (April) 811 AW
**Location:** Free City of Amara
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

Amara, the cosmopolitan port city on the Bay of Urgot. Aizune Silverwind
and Smiggles von Hoofel already know each other from prior work together
in the Merchant Nations. They have been hired by Aldorph Broomspatch of
the Red Raven Trading Coaster for a specific job: documents and items
of value were taken during orc raids on his caravans, and Aldorph has
learned they were brought back to the orcs' lair, an abandoned keep deep
in the Steppes of Broken Stone. He wants the items retrieved and the orc
threat eliminated.

As they prepare to leave Amara, they encounter Iskar, a Sabonaar human
bard and swordsman who has been independently investigating the orc raids for
his own reasons (the raids disrupted contacts he needs for a personal
mission involving a friend captured in Moamon). Iskar has intelligence
about the keep's location and defenses. A pragmatic alliance forms.

The three converge with aligned interests but different motivations.
Character dynamics established: Aizune's spiritual thoughtfulness and
Smiggles' irreverent brilliance (an existing rapport), Iskar's street-
sharp suspicion and self-reliance. They do not yet fully trust each
other, but the complementary nature of their skills is immediately
apparent: Aizune's divine senses and social navigation, Smiggles' arcane
firepower and analytical mind, Iskar's infiltration expertise and bardic
versatility.

**Purpose:** Introduces all three protagonists and Aldorph. Establishes
Amara as the campaign hub. Sets up the inciting mission with a specific
and personal hook. Plants seeds for Iskar's personal plotline (Moamon
friend).

## Chapter 4 — Blood on the Steppes

**POV:** Rotating among protagonists
**Timeline:** Garn 811 AW
**Location:** Steppes of Broken Stone, Zanthar's Keep
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

The journey through the desolate Steppes: no vegetation, orc-infested
terrain, the trade route visible in the distance. The keep comes into
view, crude stone built on older rubble. The heroes discover the network
of secret passages running through the walls (original to Zanthar's
tower construction, unknown to the current orc occupants). Using these
passages, they observe the orcs through spy-holes, plan their assault,
and strike with tactical advantage.

The clearing of the keep proceeds room by room: orc warriors, adepts,
the orc-snake Aspei (freed from enslavement, provides brief intelligence
before departing). Culminates in the confrontation with Kru'ka'ta, the
orc shaman-cleric whose negative energy channeling and death-domain
magic make him the most dangerous opponent they have faced. A hard-won
victory.

The heroes find Kru'ka'ta's notes about a machine in the lower level
and an excavation leading down to older stonework. They also find
Aldorph's stolen documents and goods. But the excavation beckons.

**Purpose:** First major combat sequence. Establishes each protagonist's
combat capabilities. The secret passages create tactical storytelling
rather than room-by-room dungeon crawl. Kru'ka'ta is a satisfying minor
antagonist whose defeat feels earned.

## Chapter 5 — The Room of Mirrors

**POV:** Primary Aizune, secondary Smiggles
**Timeline:** Garn 811 AW
**Location:** Zanthar's ruins beneath the keep
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

Descent into the lower level. Different, older stonework. Draconic runes
and protective phrases carved into walls. The mirror room: dozens of
mirrors of different sizes and designs, each containing a flickering
animation of the same face living a different life. On the floor, two
dead orcs frozen in horrifying screams, emaciated. The Whisper of Zanthar
is atmospheric menace here: shadows that move wrong, cold spots, the
sense of being watched. One or two encounters where it drains strength
from a hero before retreating into the mirrors.

Deeper: the machine room. Three great rings covered in Draconic runes
and gemstone powders. The glass globe on its central base. Two corpses:
Remular, the withered husk of a monk of Paladine, and Kuulatch, a
strangely well-groomed orc with extraordinary gear (adamantine weapons
of unusually fine quality, unfamiliar coins, items that establish
immediately that this orc is nothing like the savages above).

Aizune activates the machine. Energy surges through him. His
consciousness is projected: he sees the Steppes, then rises, sees the
coast, and a city where Amara should be but different, ruled by
civilized orcs. Enslaved humans and dwarves in chains. No elves. No
halflings. The elven lands razed completely. A massive orc army marching
across the destroyed forests toward what appears to be a portal. Three
moons in the sky instead of two. The vision ends.

The heroes are shaken and confused. They take Remular's body for return
to its monastery. They take the Orceri spy's gear for identification.
They depart the keep, carrying questions that will reshape their lives.

**Purpose:** The central discovery of Book One. The machine connects
Chapter 1 (Zanthar's creation) to the present. The Orceri spy's body
provides physical evidence. The vision provides the existential threat.
The reader now understands what Chapter 2 was: the history of the world
they just glimpsed.

## Chapter 6 — Unscheduled Contact

**POV:** Split between heroes (primary) and Li'Zhan (secondary)
**Timeline:** Garn 811 AW
**Location:** Amara and the Steppes (heroes); Ta'Puh airborne monitoring
station (Li'Zhan)
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

**Hero thread:** Departure from the keep. On the road back, Lyanorra
appears: a small green elven girl, playful and energetic, claiming dreams
of adventure. Something about her is not quite right but the heroes
cannot identify what. She is suspiciously capable (returns with horses
in half the expected time). They grudgingly allow her to accompany them.

Return to Amara. Aldorph is thrilled with the recovered items and pays
generously. He becomes an ongoing contact. The heroes discuss what they
saw in the machine. Smiggles reasons it through: not the past (the orcs
won), not the future (too different from now), therefore another plane.
Iskar examines the Orceri spy's gear: the craftsmanship is beyond
anything in Mazariim. They need expert confirmation.

The heroes decide to return Remular's body to the monastery at Blowing
Rock before pursuing answers about the Orceri items.

**Orceri thread (intercut):** High above the Orceri cities, Commander
Li'Zhan stands on his giant dirigible. The elven hivemind sensoriums
report in their polyphonic voice: "Unscheduled contact, south by
southeast 14 miles. Alternate prime, unknown. Contact lost." Li'Zhan
almost chokes. He contacts covert operatives via wrist communicator:
"I have need of your services, and the need for secrecy." The enemy has
noticed Mazariim's existence.

**Purpose:** Dual-thread structure introduces the Orceri perspective for
the first time. Lyanorra's introduction. The heroes begin processing the
discovery analytically. Li'Zhan's reaction establishes the Orceri as a
civilization with command structure, surveillance technology, and the
capacity for strategic response.

## Chapter 7 — Blowing Rock

**POV:** Primary Aizune
**Timeline:** Garn 811 AW
**Location:** Blowing Rock, Monastery of Paladine
**Word Target:** ~5,000 words

A peaceful half-day journey south from Amara. The small village of
Blowing Rock: quiet, pastoral, unremarkable except for the monastery
of Paladine on the hill overlooking the town. The Valiant Halls of the
Dragon, with its Ejiin-inspired courtyard gardens, marble statue of
Paladine with draconic features and crossed lances.

The heroes arrive and present Remular's body to Lord of Justice Lionous
Harker. A Madronian man with blonde locks pulled back and blue eyes
telling of deep loss and equally deep devotion. He looks younger than
his forty-five years. He receives the body with grief and profound
gratitude. Remular's story emerges: a street child from Amara who
escaped slavers, wandered for days, found Blowing Rock and the
monastery, and grew into a devoted monk-paladin who set out to
investigate the orc raids and never returned.

Lionous offers the heroes the hospitality of the monastery. He speaks
of Paladine's teachings, of honor and justice. He is moved by the
heroes' willingness to bring back a stranger's body rather than leave
it in a ruin. He pledges: if any of them fall in their journeys, bring
the body to him and he will call upon Paladine to restore them. The
offer of raise dead services, given in genuine gratitude, establishes a
life-saving bond.

A quiet, warm chapter. Respite between the horrors of the keep and the
revelations to come.

**Purpose:** Establishes Lionous as a moral anchor. Creates the practical
safety net of resurrection services. Provides emotional breathing room.
The contrast between Blowing Rock's peace and the Orceri world glimpsed
through the machine amplifies what is at stake.

## Chapter 8 — The Wandering Elf

**POV:** Aleron (flashback) interwoven with present-day hero POV
**Timeline:** Flashback: 741 AW, 752 AW. Present: Garn/Hinter 811 AW
**Location:** Flashback: Vand'ardhon (elven continent), various tribes.
Present: Amara
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

**Present thread:** Back in Amara after Blowing Rock, the heroes visit a
local gathering place. An elf approaches: long black hair streaked with
prominent white, leaning on a quarterstaff, wearing simple robes and a
hood. He speaks of heroes and destiny with earnest eccentricity.
"Aleron Windstrider." He says he needs their help. For what? "To save
the world, of course," he says with a straight face. They think him
insane, and indeed the elf does seem a little off. He has been following
them since the keep, he explains. He felt the machine's activation from
Fort Hope, hundreds of miles away.

He asks them to join hands. They do, skeptically. In an instant, they
are floating in the Astral Sea: a limitless blue void stretching to
infinity. A heartbeat later, they are back. His gift from Thalaman is
real. The heroes agree to help him, each for different reasons.

**Flashback thread (woven through the present scenes):** Aleron at age
55 on Vand'ardhon. Nightmares of orcs destroying his homeland. The
owlbear attack. His first involuntary shift: a lush grain field under
a softer blue sky with no moons, a brawny female elf descending in a
whirlwind, calling him "a visitor from the world of the living." Waking
by a creek with a nosebleed and a large white streak in his black hair.

Years of wandering from tribe to tribe. Incorporeal entities following
him back from shifts. A portal to a water plane flooding crops. The
divination with Vocinder the priest of Mau: "You are touched by
Thalaman." The involuntary shift that took twelve surrounding elves to
the Abyss. Pale-white manes devouring them. Aleron unharmed. Twelve
dead. He ran, knowing he was destined for a life of loneliness.

**Purpose:** Introduces the deuteragonist. His power is demonstrated,
not just described. The flashbacks reveal the cost of his gift and
create empathy for a character who will carry the trilogy's final
sacrifice. The contrast between present-day eccentricity and past trauma
adds depth.

## Chapter 9 — The Confirmation

**POV:** Rotating, primary Smiggles
**Timeline:** Hinter (May) 811 AW
**Location:** Journey to Instar, then the City of Mages
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

Aleron suggests seeking expert help to confirm the nature of the Orceri
spy's items and to obtain a planar tuning fork that could locate the
alternate reality they glimpsed through the machine. Instar, the City
of Mages, has both the scholarly resources and the arcane capability.

Journey east. During travel, Lyanorra reveals her dragon nature when
danger threatens the group (she shifts form to save them). Iskar's
long-standing suspicion is confirmed. Aleron is visibly unsettled by
her presence. Lyanorra "seemed to avoid Aleron, who must smell of
madness and chaos to her." The mutual avoidance between the chaotic
planar priest and the lawful evil dragon is noted but not resolved.

Arrival at Instar: a city illuminated by permanent magical lights,
wizard towers dominating the skyline, social status determined entirely
by arcane ability. Non-magical characters find themselves treated as
second-class citizens. Smiggles is fascinated; Iskar is uncomfortable;
Aizune navigates with social grace.

The heroes are granted an audience with members of the Thaumaturgic
Council. Aleron's demonstrated planar abilities and the physical
evidence of the Orceri items command attention. The Council examines
Kuulatch's gear: the adamantine weapons and other items were
manufactured in another alternate material plane. The physical
properties are finer than anything produced in Mazariim. The threat is
scientifically confirmed.

The Council agrees to create a planar tuning fork attuned to Ta'Puh,
but the process requires the Orceri items themselves as calibration
material, and the work will take approximately one week. The heroes
must wait. In exchange for the tuning fork, the Council requires the
heroes to share all intelligence gathered during any crossing into
Ta'Puh, establishing Instar as an informed ally with its own strategic
interests in the interdimensional threat.

The week in Instar: Smiggles haunts the arcane libraries and workshops,
absorbing knowledge and making contacts among the city's mages (while
carefully avoiding any connection to Snarf Copperthumb, whose cousin
leads the Guilded Cog). Iskar works the taverns and gathering places,
building a network of contacts in a city where information is currency.
Aizune visits the temples and speaks with Ariano Ignescreo, the ifrit
cleric of Mau on the Council, about the theological implications of
alternate realities where entities were destroyed. Lyanorra is
conspicuously at ease in Instar, navigating the city with a familiarity
she does not explain.

The heroes receive the tuning fork. They depart Instar with both proof
and the means to act.

**Purpose:** Combines the confirmation beat and the tuning fork
acquisition into a single Instar sequence. The week-long wait provides
character development space in a rich setting. Lyanorra's comfort in
Instar plants seeds for her backstory. The Council's demand for
intelligence establishes a political dimension to the alliance. Tighter
pacing, one location, no filler quest.

## Chapter 10 — Preparations

**POV:** Rotating, primary Aizune
**Timeline:** Hinter/Mord (May/June) 811 AW
**Location:** Amara
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

Back in Amara with the tuning fork. Aleron explains what comes next:
using his planar travel gift combined with the tuning fork to cross
into Ta'Puh. He briefs the heroes on the dangers. His abilities allow
the group to travel between planes, but every use costs him physically.
If he takes damage during transit, there is a chance of involuntary
shifts to random planes. He needs them to protect him.

Preparations: polymorph magic to disguise themselves as Orceri (witch's
brew). Spells of recall. Equipment and supplies. Each hero prepares in
their own way. Aizune prays to Minerva, knowing her counterpart was
destroyed in the reality they are about to enter. Smiggles studies
everything they know about Orceri technology and society. Iskar plans
contingencies and escape routes.

Character interactions: Aizune and Aleron discuss faith. Smiggles and
Iskar discuss practical survival. Lyanorra watches with unreadable
green eyes, offering to come along. Iskar objects. The party debates.
She comes.

Aleron does not speak of his destiny in specific terms. But Aizune,
watching the elf with the white-streaked hair leaning on his
quarterstaff, senses something the others do not.

**Purpose:** Breathing room before the crossing. Character development
through preparation. Deepens relationships and establishes emotional
stakes for the Ta'Puh sequence that opens Book Two. Quiet tension.

## Chapter 11 — The Watcher Above

**POV:** Split between Yzrat (opening) and heroes (closing)
**Timeline:** Mord (June) 811 AW
**Location:** Mazariim airspace (Yzrat); Steppes of Broken Stone (heroes)
**Word Target:** ~5,500 words

**Yzrat thread:** The elegant skymage receives his assignment. He
admires his mana implants (the glow of mana stripped from so-called
gods). He dresses in form-fitted bluish-black robes, goggles, mithril
riding boots. His blue dragon mount is biomantically enhanced, capable
of interdimensional travel. Unthil, his lieutenant, appears from shadow
with a playful "BOO!" They cross to Mazariim.

**Hero thread:** The heroes return to the Steppes, intending to use the
machine one final time to confirm coordinates before the crossing. They
activate it, peer deeper into Ta'Puh, and note details for their
infiltration. They depart the keep.

**Yzrat thread (closing):** Invisible at extreme altitude on his dragon
mount, Yzrat watches the heroes leave the keep. He assesses them: "So
weak, well... most of them. But among them were some with power.
Anomalies, but... nevertheless, something to be watched and planned
for." He reaches into a leather bag and removes a three-inch maggot-
like creature. With a calm toss, he drops it. It falls from great
height, lands near the keep. Twelve hours later, the purple worm it
becomes destroys the keep and Zanthar's machine.

The Orceri deny resources rather than engage directly. The machine that
started everything is gone.

**Book One ends:** The heroes have the tuning fork, the knowledge, the
allies, and each other. The enemy knows they exist. The machine is
destroyed. There is no going back, only forward, into the world the
orcs made.
