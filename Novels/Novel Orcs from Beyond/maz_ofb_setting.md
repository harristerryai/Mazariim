## Cosmology Overview

### The Multiverse

The "multiverse" is the framework in which all the various universes
exist. There are an infinite number of universes spread out in the
infinite 11-dimensional expanse of the multiverse. The laws of nature
are often different in each universe in varying degrees. In some,
gravity and electromagnetism are the forces at work, while in others
the forces of earth, air, fire, and water make the world function. In
some the forces of magic exist, in others they do not. In some gravity
has too weak an attraction and stars never formed, and in others an
omnipotent divine being created the stars and planets themselves.

Each universe contains its prime material plane and all of its
subsequent extra-planar dimensions, as well as an almost limitless
number of alternate material planes and their extra-planar dimensions.
Each prime material plane can have unique extra-planar dimensions since
it is the birth of entities themselves that create the dimension, and
these entities would most certainly form differently in each.

Although there is no "center" to the multiverse, in the infinite
expanse there exist "filaments" of universes, regions in unimaginable
distances that are less barren and empty than others. The universe in
which Mazariim is located lies in the 11th-dimensional center of a
region that has many other universes in close proximity, comparatively
speaking. Consequently, this particular universe has had multiple
visitors from other universes, be they powerful deific entities or
pockets of refugees.

### This Universe

The universe in which Mazariim is located has important characteristics
to note. This universe was created by the collision of two previous
and now destroyed universes, creating a new universe that expanded
outward infinitely fast and for billions of years evolved similarly to
the way our real universe is theorized to have been born. Entities did
not arrive or exist until relatively recently in the time of the
universe, and they by no means hold complete dominion over the world.

The universe has similar forces to our real world, namely gravity and
electromagnetism, but has an additional fundamental force, the
"magical" force, which allows all of the magic in the universe to
function. This force overpowers the others, preventing the existence
of many real-world technologies, such as things that function on
electricity or nanotechnology.

The boundaries of the universe have an inverse relationship with the
amount of magical force an entity or traveler possesses. Therefore, it
is more difficult for a greater deity from another universe to come to
Mazariim than a lesser deity or powerful adventurer. Powerful entities
cannot completely enter the prime material plane or the universe itself
and must send a part of themselves in the form of an avatar.

## Religion in Mazariim

In the universe of Mazariim, the gods do not hold sway over the powers
of creation and existence. They are merely sentient beings as well,
capable of being banished or even destroyed. The true nature of
entities, however, is unknown to most, and most of the denizens of the
world worship them as powers and deities nevertheless.

### Qualities of Entities

Entities are beings formed from the clumping and attraction of magical
energies in the limitless vastness of space. These beings are not
immortal — they are timeless. They do not age or require food, water,
or sleep. Anything that would dissipate or destroy their energy could
in theory kill an entity, and entities that fight each other can
destroy a rival permanently. Some entities take thousands of years to
form, others take billions.

While an entity is forming, its consciousness forms in a kind of
slumber, deep inside gathering wisdom through uninterrupted thought
and meditation in ways normal creatures cannot possibly imagine.
Eventually, something triggers the entity to awaken — something as
simple as the passing of time to the presence of another entity or the
explosion of a nearby star. If the being is of sufficient magical mass
the entity will instantly form its pocket dimension known as an extra-
dimensional plane, becoming a greater entity. The plane could have
something to do with the conditions near the entity or could be
completely random in its nature and physical laws. If their magical
mass is insufficient to form its plane, the entity will almost always
immediately find a suitable plane upon which to form a small realm,
sometimes separate but often merged with the original plane. Entities
from other universes never form their planes, instead merging with one
of the existing planes. In addition, entities from other universes, no
matter how powerful, are considered lesser entities. If an entity is of
a small magical mass, it will reside in another plane of existence as
a minor entity without its realm, or even exist in the prime material
plane, and subsequently be much easier to destroy.

When an entity wants to enter the prime material plane again from its
plane, it must send a portion of its energy known as an avatar instead
of traveling itself, due to its magical mass not allowing it to detach
itself from its plane. An avatar is only a portion of the power of the
original, and entities are not killed if their avatar is destroyed.
They are however weakened and need to wait 101 years to gain enough
energy to pierce the barrier between worlds and send another avatar.

## Solar System and Celestial Bodies

### The Solar System

Mazariim occupies the habitable zone of a four-planet solar system.
Distances are measured in Astronomical Units (AU), where 1 AU equals
the distance from Mazariim to its sun.

| Planet | Distance | Description |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Dicia** | 0.29 AU | Small, rocky, barren. Year of 92 days. Comparable to Mercury. No known life. |
| **Mazariim** | 1.0 AU | Habitable world. Two moons. Home of all known sentient civilizations. |
| **Orpheous** | ~10 AU | Gas giant with visible rings (on clear nights). Multiple moons. Comparable to Saturn. |
| **Intiago** | ~17 AU | Massive gas giant. Multiple moons including Lystra, where dragon progenitors originated before fleeing irradiation. Comparable to Neptune. |

### The Two Moons of Mazariim

Mazariim has two moons, both visible to the naked eye and deeply
embedded in the cultures, calendars, and mythologies of every
sentient race.

**Io'el** ("Night Star" in Elven) — The larger, silver-white moon.
Keeps a traditional lunar cycle of 28 days, perfectly aligned with
the Common Calendar (each month begins at the full moon of Io'el).
Io'el is the timekeeper, the reliable constant by which all
civilizations mark their months and seasons.

**Randir'el** ("Wandering Star" in Elven) — The smaller, pale gold
moon. Has an irregular orbit, swinging to further extremes and
appearing larger and smaller throughout its seven-month cycle.
Randir'el's unpredictable path has made it a symbol of change,
fortune, and the unknown across many cultures. When both moons are
full simultaneously, the elves call the conjunction **Arathalion**
("the Watching"), a rare event considered deeply significant.

**Novel significance:** In Mazariim's reality, two moons cross the
sky. In the Orceri reality of Ta'Puh, a third moon exists: small,
reddish, occupying a position between the other two. The presence
or absence of this third moon is the immediate visual marker that
distinguishes the two realities. Every scene that shows the sky
should reinforce which world the characters occupy.

### Planar Architecture

Unlike many cosmologies, the extra-dimensional planes of Mazariim
have no fixed arrangement. There is no ring of planes organized by
alignment, no distinction between Inner and Outer Planes, no World
Tree or River Styx connecting them. All extra-dimensional planes
simply float within the Astral Sea, formed when entities of
sufficient magical mass spontaneously create pocket dimensions
anchored to their consciousness.

The **Astral Plane** was the first to form, created when Mau
awakened and his gaze spread across all of time and space. Every
subsequent plane exists within it. The **Ethereal Plane** and
**Shadow Plane** are transitive, overlapping the prime material
plane and serving as conduits for souls traveling to Heaven (via
the Ethereal) and Hell (via the Shadow). The **Plane of Dreams**
connects to the subconscious minds of sleeping sentients, with
its darkest regions bordering the **Far Realm**, source of all
aberrations.

### Alternate Material Planes

An infinite number of alternate material planes exist within the
Mazariim universe, each vibrating at a slightly different frequency,
occupying the same space but completely unaware of one another.
Some are near-perfect copies with only minor differences. Others
are historically divergent, where a single pivotal event occurred
differently and an entire alternate civilization evolved from the
change. Still others differ biologically or cosmologically.

Each alternate prime material plane has its own set of extra-
dimensional planes, since entities form differently in each reality.
Only the Astral Plane is shared across all alternates.

The Orceri of Ta'Puh discovered alternate realities approximately
400 years ago and developed the ability to view, enter, and
ultimately conquer them. They have subjugated 11 alternate realities
before discovering Mazariim. Key conquered alternates include a
gnomish clockwork world (source of dirigible technology), a psionic
elf world (population absorbed into the hivemind sensoriums), a
beast world (biomantic raw material for leviathans), and an
Akovarian world (source of near-complete mana mastery enabling
sky citadels).

### Universe Boundary Mechanics

The boundaries of the Mazariim universe have an inverse relationship
with magical power: the more magical force an entity possesses, the
harder it is to cross into the universe. Greater entities from other
universes cannot fully enter and must send avatars. This is why
entities like Paladine (from Krynn), Mordrion and Zuel (from Oerth),
and others arrived in diminished forms and are classified as lesser
entities regardless of their original power. When an avatar is
destroyed, the entity requires 101 years to gather sufficient energy
to pierce the boundary and send another.

### The Old Gods

The "Old Gods" or "Ancient Ones" consist of Mau, Zebulba, Pyrantor,
Iquinias, Vuleala, Dirgin, Asti, and Gruhathyrir. These entities came
before the other deities that seemed to focus more on a particular
race or ideology. The Old Gods are a more pure, less emotional set of
entities, their nature far more alien than the entities to follow.

#### Mau — The Oracle, The Watcher, The First Awakened

Mau is the oldest entity in existence, the first consciousness to
awaken in the universe approximately 2 billion years ago. Unlike other
entities, Mau did not form from a star, planet, or elemental force —
Mau simply was, coalescing from the fundamental fabric of reality
itself. Mau does not speak. Mau does not act. Mau watches. Mau knows.
The Oracle's nature is utterly alien and incomprehensible to mortals.
Mau perceives not just the present, but past and future, not just this
reality but alternate timelines and parallel universes.

#### Zebulba — The Entropic Void

Zebulba is entropy given consciousness — the inevitable heat death of
the universe, the final darkness, the silence after the last heartbeat.
Unlike death gods of other pantheons who guide souls or maintain
balance, Zebulba is pure negation. The Old God does not hate life, for
hatred implies emotion. Zebulba simply is — the mathematical certainty
that all systems decay. Zebulba formed within and absorbed a black
hole, drawing a tiny but maniacal following of zealots.

## History of Mazariim

### The Timeless Era

The history of Mazariim begins with the merging of primordial reality
streams from predecessor dimensions on a scale that defies explanation.
The universe came into being and expanded at an unimaginable rate.
Matter was almost perfectly spread out across this expanse, but the
forces of gravity and magic took hold, and after hundreds of millions
of years the first stars began to illuminate the universe.

At the same time, the forces of magic and gravity began to work
differently, with collections of magic and energy slowly forming larger
and larger clumps, gaining immense size and power. These large
collections of magic would one day unexpectedly and spontaneously form
sentient beings. These beings became known to the learned and educated
as entities, although for most of the inhabitants of the world they
would be called "gods" and "powers."

It would be during the last few hundred thousand years that a few dozen
of the most intelligent species would begin to differentiate from each
other, eventually forming a complex ecosystem and living planet.
Although many would continue to our present day, three races dominated
the others: Elves, Dwarves, and Humans.

### Era of Awakening

Elves were the first race to show many of the behaviors of modern
intelligent creatures, including the use of fire, language, mathematics,
and tools. Elven civilization developed across millennia on the isolated
continent of Vand'ardhon. Around 3,402 BW, Minerva made direct contact
with elven leaders in The Enlightening, imparting vast knowledge and
imbuing two ideals: deep respect for nature and the land, and absence
of the need for personal possessions or land ownership.

While Elves had a continent upon which to spread civilization freely,
the Dwarves and Humans that lived upon the continent of Nigal had to
contend with each other. Human and Dwarven tribes clashed from very
early on, competing for territory and resources. With time, the Dwarves
settled in the mountainous areas to the northeast, while Humans tended
to settle along the more coastal areas of the southwest.

Named Yrugas, the first great human nation was born when a group of
dragons began to teach humans arcane magic around 4,000 BW. The rule
of the human mages was barbaric and inhumane, with Madron ruling for
some 900 years.

### Era of Arrival

Several entities began to form bonds with different aspects of
humanity. The entity known as Zuel had spent decades hoarding magical
power and building an army of orcs, goblins, and ogres. On another
world called Oerth, gods Moradin and Gruumsh battled. The surge of
magical power from their grievous wounds hurled both into Mazariim
where they became mortal entities: Mordrion the Dwarf Father and Zuel
the Devourer (1,866 BW). Other entities arrived as well: Alagalothor,
Thalaman, and Sheeva from Talernon (1,492 BW); Paladine from Krynn
(1,414 BW).

### Era of Unity

It was some 800 years ago that an event so momentous occurred that the
keeping of years itself was reset. Zuel had built a million-strong army
and began to march across the lands. In the war's darkest hour, Minerva
divided her essence among the Seven Artharans. Only through the alliance
of all races — the Concord of Unity at Fort Hope — were they able to
save the world. The War ended with Demious Stoneshaper defeating Zuel's
avatar. The unity forged at Fort Hope evolved into the Federation of
Man.

## The War to End All Wars

### The First Year (4 BW)

The War began in the summer of 4 BW. Hundreds of thousands of orcs and
goblinkind poured from mountain ranges. Led by thirteen ogre-magi
generals, by winter large numbers of dwarves had been displaced and
many dwarven holds overtaken.

### The Second Year (3 BW)

Strange frog-like daemons began attacking coastal cities, led by an
immense sea creature with dozens of 100-foot tentacles. Ocean-bound
trade came to a halt as a dozen coastal cities were wiped off the map.

### The Third Year (2 BW)

The various entities became more involved. Hinterhin helped feed and
hide millions of refugees. Rivoleen helped defend the gnomish lands.
Mordrion and half a dozen other minor entities led their forces into
battle, with a number destroyed defeating ogre-magi generals. Minerva
divided her essence among the Seven Artharans — the seven leaders of
the elven nations — granting phenomenal power and a psychic command
network. At Fort Hope, the Concord of Unity was formed, uniting all
races.

Elven casualties were catastrophic: 30,000 warriors deployed to Nigal;
12,000 died (40% casualty rate), and 75% of the elven navy was
destroyed in a daemon ambush.

### The Final Year (1 BW)

The last year was the most brutal. Zuel's avatar personally led a
countless army toward Fort Hope. In the final battle, Zuel destroyed
Mordrion's avatar, Quarzel (permanently), and several lesser entities.
Demious Stoneshaper, wielding Mordrion's blessed warhammer, shattered
Zuel's avatar. The orcish horde broke and fled.

### Aftermath

The official end date was 3 Quarz 1 AW. Demious Stoneshaper became
king of the northern dwarven realms. Stranded elven survivors were
gradually evacuated; 500-800 chose to remain on Nigal, forming the
first permanent elven diaspora. Famine and disease ravaged the world
for the next decade. The unity forged at Fort Hope evolved into the
Federation of Man.

## Lost Empires

### The Continent of Akovar — The First Civilization

Akovar was the first advanced human civilization, rising approximately
13,000 BW on an isolated continent. Guided by Asti, Akovarians
mastered healing and ley-line magic while other races remained
primitive. Around 6,830 BW, the Dragon War struck when chromatic
dragons attacked. Priests transferred Asti's essence into the Mask of
the Pharaoh and teleported the population. The ley-lines exploded,
destroying the capital and killing most attacking dragons.

### Yrugas — The Mageocracy of Pre-History

Founded around 4,000 BW when dragons began teaching humans arcane
magic, Yrugas was a brutal mageocracy. Madron ruled for some 900
years. The fall around 2,700 BW came through magical catastrophe and
slave revolts. The concentrated death created the Hollow Sepulcher —
Zebulba's greatest temple.

### The Dukhan Empire

Beginning about 1,001 BW, the small city-state of Dukhan grew into a
Zebulba-worshiping theocracy controlling six major cities in the Jinook
Valley. In 213 BW, Soros un-Kahn's ritual to summon Zebulba's avatar
failed catastrophically, killing all living things near the ziggurats
and destroying the Empire.

### The Empire of Simir

Starting in 291 AW, King Petrov Simir of Asim assimilated the southern
Merchant Nations through economic conquest. In 686 AW, King Tiberius
Simir was assassinated, plunging the empire into civil war. Within
decades, only Asim and Moamon remained.

## Races and Peoples

### Important Setting Notes

**No Underdark:** Unlike many fantasy settings, Mazariim has no
worldwide network of subterranean realms. Underground areas exist as
discrete locations.

**No Half-Breeds:** Humans cannot produce offspring with other races.
Each race is genetically distinct.

**Gods as Entities:** The gods of Mazariim are powerful but mortal
beings called "entities" — intelligences formed from mana rather than
omnipotent creators.

### Humans

Humans were the last of the major races to begin developing
civilizations. They are the most adaptable race, with enormous variety
in culture, government, and lifestyle.

#### Madronian Culture

The humans of the Inner Kingdoms region. Named after the wizard-king
Madron who ruled Yrugas for some 900 years. Their regional dialect is
Madra, a flowing language featuring over fifteen vowels and many silent
letters. Real-world analog: 17th-18th century European.

#### Sabonaar Culture

The humans of the Merchant Nations on the Sabon Peninsula. Masters of
shipbuilding. Their dialect is Sabo, practical and spelled phonetically.
Real-world analog: Spanish/Italian/Portuguese.

### Elves

Elves were the first race to show many behaviors of modern intelligent
creatures. Elven civilization developed across millennia on the isolated
continent of Vand'ardhon. Guided by Minerva since 3,402 BW, elves live
in tribal societies with deep respect for nature and no concept of
personal property. Their culture is comparable to historical Native
American cultures.

The Church of Minerva has two branches: Cundo'elda (clerics/oracles/
inquisitors — stewards of the elves) and Cundo'dori (druids/rangers —
stewards of the land).

#### Elven Subraces Relevant to the Trilogy

**Ya'sehrim (High Elf):** Homeland Yaara'sehan. Black or blue hair,
pale skin, dark eyes. Aleron's subrace.

**Raladrim (Plains Elf):** Homeland Ra'Lad. Straight dark hair, tan
skin. Share plains with wemics. Less insular worldview than other
subraces.

### Dwarves

Dwarves are clan-based, honor-driven people who have inhabited the
mountains and hills of Nigal for over twenty thousand years. Masters
of metalcraft and engineering, known for terraced farming, ancestor
worship, and incredible skill with arms and armor. The arrival of
Mordrion in 1,866 BW unified the dwarven peoples.

**Gormdawi (Mountain Dwarves)** inhabit the northern reaches — dark
hair and eyes, strict tradition, rune magic, steam engineering, coffee
cultivation.

**Bryndawi (Hill Dwarves)** inhabit the south — red, blonde, or light
brown hair, green or blue eyes, fierce independence, tattoo magic,
elemental magics.

### Halflings (Hin)

Halflings live on the peaceful island of Shalazar, where they have
developed a communal society focused on agriculture and harmony. Almost
universally pacifistic. Patron entity is Hinterhin. In the Ta'Puh
reality, halflings were exterminated entirely, and Shalazar was
conquered by Rangithyr.

### Gnomes

Gnomes originated in the far eastern mountains of Nigal, developing
alongside the Bryndawi dwarves. Inventors of steam technology, renowned
jewelers, clockmakers, and appraisers. Worship Rivoleen. Capital at
Zhonhylite. Smiggles von Hoofel is gnomish.

### Other Races

**Orcs** — Primary antagonists during the War, led by Zuel. Still
exist in remote regions, mostly hostile. The Orceri of Ta'Puh are a
civilization-scale divergence of this race.

**Goblins** — Allies of orcs during the War, cunning and cruel, live
in tribal groups.

**Dragons** — Ageless masters originally from doomed Lystra (moon of
Intiago). Taught humans arcane magic. Giganzalt and Lyanorra are
dragon characters in the trilogy.

## World Geography

### Overview

The world of Mazariim consists of three major continents: Nigal,
Vand'ardhon, and Akovar.

**Nigal** — Home to humans, dwarves, and gnomes. Divided into the
Inner Kingdoms, Merchant Nations, Lands of Chaos, Horselands, Ancient
South, Lands of Honor, Mespoian Peninsula, Frozen Isles, and Dwarven
and Gnomish realms.

**Vand'ardhon** — Ancestral home of the elves, divided into eight
major elven realms. Also includes Shalazar, the homeland of the
halflings.

**Akovar** — Distant continent with mysterious history involving
ancient dragon civilizations and ley-line mastery. Remains largely
isolated.

## The Federation of Man and Fort Hope

### The Federation of Man

**"United we stood against the darkness. United we shall remain."**
— Inscription above the gates of Fort Hope

The Federation of Man is not a nation but a confederation — a political
alliance binding the major nations and city-states of the Inner
Kingdoms into a unified body for mutual defense, trade, and governance.
Founded in 1 AW in the immediate aftermath of the War to End All Wars.

The Federation has three branches: the Praetorian Council (legislative),
the High Court (judicial), and the Federation Military (border forts,
road patrols, naval forces). The Circle of the Eastern Watch, twelve
specialists at Fort Stoneshaper, coordinates supernatural threat
response.

### Fort Hope — Capital of the Federation

Originally a military outpost, now a sprawling city serving as the
political heart of the Federation. Not part of any member nation —
belongs to the Federation itself. Aleron lived here for approximately
ten years before the campaign begins. It was here he felt the
interdimensional vibration from Zanthar's Machine.

#### Historical Background

Amara's past dates back to Sabonaar fishing villages along the Bay of
Urgot. The Dukhan Empire assimilated the coastal cities and ruled for
over seven hundred years, widening the harbor and dredging the bay.

In 213 BW, the Dukhan Empire fell when the Zebulban high priest Soros
un-Kahn's catastrophic ritual killed most major cities' populations.
Amara was destroyed and depopulated. After the War to End All Wars,
dwarves helped rebuild, and Amara was slowly repopulated over
centuries.

In 291 AW, Amara was assimilated into the Empire of Simir. In 686 AW,
the Empire fell when King Tiberius Simir was assassinated. In 692 AW,
Baron Feraldo Bastien seized control, declared the "Free City of
Amara," built defenses and economy using paid enforcers, a merchant
guild, and a thieves guild to protect investments and maintain order.
He expanded the harbor and warehouse district, attracted new trade
routes, and governed more fairly. Three generations later, Baron Armand
Bastien rules one of the biggest trade hubs in western Nigal.

In 733 AW, Moamon went to war with Amara. Both suffered heavy losses
before a truce brokered by Instar wizards and the Federation. Tensions
remain.

## Magic System Details

### Mana

Along with gravity and electromagnetism, magic is one of the forces of
the universe. The actual particles of this magical force are called
mana. A small, diffuse layer of mana covers the whole world and all of
the higher dimensions of the Mazariim universe.

Spellcasters manipulate this infinitesimally small amount of mana to
produce effects, either through precise arcane formulas, an innate
understanding of magical laws, divine incantations, or druidic prayers.

**Antimagic:** An antimagic field pushes these particles away, removing
the source of power for spells and supernatural abilities.

**Inner Mana Sources:** Some creatures such as golems, undead,
elementals, and outsiders have a tiny inner source of mana, imbued
during creation. Magical artifacts have tiny concentrations imbued
during creation with expensive components.

**Powerful Creatures:** Dragons, the Favored (agents of entities), and
entity avatars have larger amounts of mana within them.

### Magical Writings

All spells, no matter what type, have their written roots in Draconic
— as it was the dragons who taught the earliest arcanists magic. Arcane
writings are always written completely in Draconic. Divine spells are
written in forms specific to the faith combined with Draconic (e.g.,
Tiovin prayer books combine Celestial and Draconic; druid spells
combine Druidic and Draconic).

### Racial Magical Traditions

**Elven Nexus-Weaving (Ritual Accretion):** Not a ley-line technique.
A circle of casters slowly builds a charge of shaped mana over hours
or days, forming a temporary locus where magic becomes easier in a
narrow band of effects. Such loci have strict boundaries and collapse
if interrupted.

**Dwarven Rune-Smithing:** Engineering-grade enchantment. A rune is a
mechanism designed to do one thing reliably — using carved symbols,
inlaid metals, and measured spacing. Excels at wards, keys/permissions,
and durable utility effects. Built as chains: trigger rune → condition
rune → effect rune ("three-stone logic").

**Gnomish Gemscrolls:** A cut and prepared gem as a one-use spell
container. The caster compresses a spell's mana-pattern into a stable
lattice; anyone who knows the trigger can release the stored effect.
Creates a portable market in practical magic.

**Human Dragon-Taught Shortcuts:** Compressed invocations and harsh
optimizations derived from Draconic formulae. Chase results requiring
more time, mana, or rarer circumstances — but risk mana burn, mental
aftershocks, or cascading instability.

**Halfling Hearth-Weaving:** Small protections with large lived impact:
hearth-wards for safety, road-luck charms, communal oaths. Power is
social and routine-bound — stronger when anchored to hospitality,
shared meals, repeated journeys.

### Portals and Teleportation Circles

**Teleportation circles** allow spellcasters to use Teleportation
Circle or Teleport spells to reach mystical circles inscribed in
permanent locations. Each circle includes a unique sigil sequence.
Many major temples, guilds, and important places have permanent
teleportation circles.

**Portals** are rarer but allow travel without spellcasting ability —
often requiring a proper key (word, gesture, or item). Can have
restrictions on timing, conditions, and activation.

## Monsters and Outsiders

### What Are Outsiders?

Almost all extra-dimensional planes have inhabitants, collectively
known as Outsiders. Most are formed when souls (magical imprints of
biological minds) depart for higher-dimensional destinations. These
magical energies are often used by the entity or the plane itself to
create outsiders. Outsiders follow hierarchies, from the lowliest imp
to the mightiest pit fiend. They do not need to sleep, eat, drink, or
breathe.

### Monster Types

**Celestials & Servants of Good:** Angels (Tiovin), Archons (Paladine),
Guardinals (Tiovin), Azatas (Minerva), Agathions (Rivoleen), Sherpas
(Ethereal Plane guides).

**Fiends & Servants of Evil:** Devils (Taovan), Demons (Og'thallith),
Daemons (Zuel), Yugoloths (Hell), Demodands (Sheeva), Div (Xannith),
Kytons (Shadow Plane), Night Hags (Plane of Dreams).

**Servants of Law & Chaos:** Aeons (Mau), Axiomites (Gyru),
Inevitables (Guzon), Proteans (Kazrokath), Slaadi (Thalaman).

**Elemental & Natural Beings:** Elementals, Genies, Kami (Ashura), Fey
(Feywild), Faer'peri (Hinterhome).

**Death & Darkness:** Nightshades (Zebulba), Oni (Khan).

**Titans & Mighty Outsiders:** Titans (Quarzel), Animal Lords (Garnak).
