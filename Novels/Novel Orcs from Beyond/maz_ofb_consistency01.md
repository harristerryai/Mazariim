## CRITICAL STANDING RENAME

**Paladine → Pala'dinae**

All references to the entity formerly written as "Paladine" are now
"Pala'dinae" (pronounced pah-lah-DY-nay). This applies universally
to all prose, dialogue, internal monologue, location names, and
reference materials. The rename affects: the entity itself, the
Valiant Halls of the Dragon (monastery of Pala'dinae), Lionous
Harker's deity, Remular's order, the marble statue at Blowing Rock,
and any future references. All prior chapter prose that used
"Paladine" should be considered as reading "Pala'dinae" for
continuity purposes. Future chapters must use "Pala'dinae"
exclusively.

### Standing Rules — Complete Consolidated List

**Core Prose Rules:**

- No em dashes or en dashes anywhere in prose, dialogue, or narrative. Use comma, period, colon, semicolon, or restructured clause. Standard hyphens in compound words are permitted.
- No spell names in prose. Characters refer to effects, not spell names. Spells described through sensory details only.
- No game mechanics terms in prose (class levels, stats, spell lists, etc.).
- No modern slang or contractions in narrative voice.
- No ornate metaphors in action sequences (use clarity style).
- Dense sentences never exceed 60 words.
- Avoid exposition dumps without sensory anchoring.
- Very long sentences limited to 3% of total content.
- Complex paragraphs limited to 3% maximum.

**Character Voice Rules:**

- Gnomish expressions (Smiggles): 2-4 per chapter. "Calibrates" most versatile; "Oy!" for reactive moments; others situational.
- Iskar comedic beat: 1 clear beat per chapter minimum. Rooted in physicality, performance, or rolled Rs.
- Iskar rolled R spelling: "frrriend," "misforrtune," "worrth," "interresting," etc. Consistent in dialogue.
- Aizune formal diction: complete sentences, no contractions, measured.
- Aleron pause/hesitation pattern: "I had... hoped"; "the trick is not to... to sort"; ellipsis-style breaks.
- Aleron's cold-air herald: localised, sharp cold precedes his physical arrival; planar in nature; consistent when he enters a scene.

**Lyanorra Voice Rules:**

- V/w substitution: deployed sparingly when amused/at ease/performing; surfaces 4-5 times per encounter; disappears in guarded/scholarly moments (itself a clue). Inconsistent because the person is inconsistent.
- "Mmmmm" thinking sound: predatory quality; 2-3 uses per chapter maximum.
- Accent drops when sincere: significant character moments only.
- Future pattern: accent becomes LESS pronounced (surface disguise fades); scholarly phrases become MORE frequent (deeper truth surfaces). Inverse pattern mirrors identity layers.
- Apple motif: use sparingly; do not overuse.

**Scene Construction Rules:**

- Vision hierarchy (darkness scenes): Dwarves/Gnomes have darkvision (see in total natural darkness, greyscale). Elves/Halflings have low-light vision (need SOME light). Humans have normal vision. Supernatural darkness defeats ALL types. Before writing any darkness scene, determine light conditions and assign visual capability correctly.
- Spirit-touched blade: always "spirit-touched" in prose, never "ghost touch."
- Single-sentence paragraphs: maximum 3 per chapter. Reserved for genuine tonal shift, major decision, or chapter-level punctuation.
- Dual-thread chapter structure: established in Ch. 6 (heroes/Li'Zhan intercut). Used when showing Orceri perspective without heroes' knowledge.

**Recurring Motifs:**

had seen orcs in his nightmares, understood the interdimensional

- He has carried this gift for seventy years without understanding
  its purpose.
- He has always felt there was a purpose, a reason he was chosen.
- His nightmares show fire, destruction, vast shapes, something

  the Orceri, Ta'Puh, or the specific nature of the threat.

  the planar capability.

### Vocinder — Voice Established

**Character:** Vocinder, elderly priest of Mau, hermit oracle,
cave on eastern slopes of Vand'ardhon.

**Voice pattern:** Inverted/rearranged sentence structure. Subject
frequently placed at end of clause. Verbs shifted from standard
position. Reminiscent of speech patterns where syntax reflects a
mind that processes information in non-linear order. More eloquent
than simple inversion; carries genuine wisdom and precision despite
unusual construction.

**Examples from prose:**

- "Touched by Thalaman, you are."
- "Remove it, I cannot. No one on this continent can remove it."
- "Woven into you it is, at a level that predates your birth and
  may postdate your death."
- "Know this, I do not. But know this, I do:"
- "Given randomly, the gift was not. Nothing randomly does
  Thalaman do."
- "The latter, I suspect."
- "Mean something, all things do."
- "A wall, there is. Between what is and what should not be.
  Thin, this wall grows."
- "There you will stand. Why, I cannot say. But there, you will
  be."

**Voice rules for future appearances:**

- Inverted syntax is consistent but not mechanical; varies
  between full inversion and partial rearrangement.
- Longer declarative statements may use standard syntax for
  emphasis by contrast (e.g., "No one on this continent can
  remove it" is standard syntax amidst inversions, lending it
  additional weight).
- Physical descriptions: milky eyes, weathered hands, gum-smile
  with no reassurance, one-eye-open diagnostic gaze.
- Speaks after long silences. Economy of speech.
- Content is precise and prophetic without being specific; gives
  shape and direction without revealing destination.

**Vocinder's prophecy content (Ch. 8, revised):**

- Confirmed Thalaman's touch; cannot remove it.
- Gift was not random; has purpose.
- Purpose will find Aleron (not the reverse).
- Added: A wall between realities grows thin. When it breaks,
  Aleron will be there. Why, Vocinder cannot say.
