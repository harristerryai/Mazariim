### SECTION 10 (MAGIC SYSTEM TRACKER) — NEW STANDING RULES

**Standing Rule — Spell Naming in Prose:**

Spell names are never capitalised or used as proper nouns in
prose text. Spells are identified through sensory signatures,
Draconic syllable counts, and descriptive references. The
tracking tables in Section 10 use conventional names for
internal reference only.

Examples of correct prose usage:
- "A translucent radiance intercepted the bolt" (not "Shield of Faith intercepted")
- "A dispelling working, targeted at Sevan's divine barrier" (not "Dispel Magic, targeted at Sevan's Shield of Faith")
- "The ray, six syllables of precisely articulated Draconic" (not "The Scorching Ray")
- "Mordecai's spectral guardians" (not "Mordecai's Spirit Guardians")
- "Mordecai's dispelling workings" (not "Mordecai's Dispel workings")

**Standing Rule — Tier References in Prose:**

Do NOT use the word "tier" or tier classifications (low-tier,
mid-tier, high-tier) in prose text. Spell power level is
communicated through sensory description: syllable count,
intensity of visual/auditory/olfactory effects, physical
impact on surroundings, and effort required from the caster.

The tier system exists in the Consistency Bible tracking tables
for internal reference only. It never appears in the novel text.

Correct: "Six syllables this time, longer, the sustained
invocation of a more demanding working"

Incorrect: "Six syllables this time, longer, the sustained
invocation of a mid-tier working"

**Standing Rule — Spell Power Communication Methods:**

When the narrative needs to convey that a spell is more or less
powerful than another, use the following techniques:

- Syllable count (established: 1-3 = minor, 4-6 = moderate, 7-9 = major)
- Duration of casting (brief murmur vs sustained invocation)
- Sensory intensity (faint shimmer vs light with "weight and substance")
- Physical effects on caster (effortless vs visibly demanding)
- Environmental impact (localised vs room-filling)
- Descriptive framing ("a more demanding working," "a sustained invocation," "the simplest of workings")

### SECTION 4 (SENSORY AND DETAIL TRACKER) — PORTAL APPEARANCE

**Standing Rule — Portal Physical Description:**

The portal is a SPHERE, not a disc. All portal descriptions
must use "sphere" or equivalent curved/spherical language.
The word "disc" must not appear in any portal description.

The view through the portal is INVERTED/REVERSED, as through
a curved lens. The destination appears flipped within the
sphere's curved surface, giving the portal its distinctive,
slightly disorienting visual quality.

Correct terms: "sphere," "curved surface," "inverted image,"
"reversed perspective"

Incorrect terms: "disc," "flat surface," "beyond its surface"
(implies flat plane)

| Near-failure | Sphere guttering; far wall visible through curved surface; inverted image fading; translucent where once opaque |
| Stable but diminished | Sphere shimmers; inverted image present but dim within curved surface; ~1/10 energy |
