## Introduction

Welcome to the Cypher System Magical Item Compendium. In this document you will find conversions of many popular Dungeons & Dragons magical items from the 5th edition and 3rd edition OGL sources. Additionally, rules for all categories of magical items, including finding the level of an item and crafting magical items, are given. All items described follow the guidelines presented in determining level and tier.

This document works best with the Cypher System Spell Compendium, and uses the Intellect costs for spells presented in that document for determining costs, levels, and tiers of magical items.

---

## Additional Rules

**Artifacts vs Cyphers:** When reading any description of an ability that describes how many cyphers a character can hold, apply it instead to artifacts.

**Artifact Limits:** Artifacts have limits on the number you can carry instead of cyphers. The more powerful and permanent magics within artifacts (permanent magical items) interfere with each other, while cyphers like potions, scrolls, and wands can be carried in any number (if you can find or make them).

**Bonuses & Stacking:** When in doubt, count a bonus as an asset.

**Abilities & Stacking:** Magical items that duplicate class abilities or spells do not stack with abilities or spells the PC knows, uses, or casts, even if the ability has a different name. For example, a PC with the Battlecaster ability does not gain a bonus from the Rod of Spell Battle, nor does a PC with the Spell Focus ability gain a bonus from a Ring of Spell Focus.

**Intellect Spell Costs:** Intellect costs for spells come from the Cypher System Spell Compendium.

**Magical Item Tier:** Items are considered low, mid, or high tier, depending on their level.

| Item Level | Tier |
|:----------:|:----:|
| 1–3        | Low  |
| 4–6        | Mid  |
| 7–10       | High |

## Potions

Potions are single-use cyphers, usually a spell that only affects the caster. Potions are usually made only with low or mid tier spells with an Intellect cost less than or equal to 5, save a few rare potions, oils, and healing elixirs listed below. All material component costs are paid during a potion's creation.

### Potion Levels / Costs

| Potion Level | GP Cost | Intellect Cost |
|:------------:|--------:|:--------------:|
| 1            |   10 gp | 1              |
| 2            |   25 gp | 2              |
| 3            |   50 gp | 3              |
| 4            |  100 gp | 4              |
| 5            |  200 gp | 5              |

Potions do not require a recipe as long as the spell to be used is known. Potions are usually the same level as their Intellect cost. Healing potions are an exception.

Potions that are applied externally are called oils. Once made, potions can leave no options to the imbiber to determine (hence separate enlarge and reduce potions). Potions cannot be created with extra effects given from Effort. Retrieving and/or using a potion is an action that draws an attack of opportunity.

## Scrolls

Scrolls are single-use cyphers that spellcasters can use to cast a spell or learn a spell from another caster. To use a scroll, the spell must be of the same type as the caster (mage, priest, druid, paladin, ranger, etc). Mid tier and high tier scrolls are more valued because they are much more rare and coveted for new spells rather than use on the battlefield. Sometimes scrolls are spells stored in gems, runes or other single-use format; if they are usable by others without the spell being on their spell list, add 25% to the cost of the item. All material component costs are paid during a scroll's creation.

Once created, scrolls leave no options to the user. Scrolls cannot be created with extra effects given from Effort. Retrieving and/or using a scroll is an action that provokes an attack of opportunity.

## Wands

Wands are multi-use cyphers that cast a single low tier or mid tier spell, with an Intellect cost less than or equal to 5, a limited number of times. All wands have 25 charges when created. To use a wand, the wielder needs to have the spell in the wand on their spell list. All material component costs are paid during a wand's creation.

### Wand Levels / Costs

|:----------:|----------:|:--------------:|
| 1          |   100 gp  | 1              |
| 2          |   200 gp  | 2              |
| 3          |   500 gp  | 3              |
| 4          | 1,000 gp  | 4              |

Wands do not require a recipe as long as the spell to be used is known.

Once created, wands leave no options to the user. Wands cannot be created with extra effects given from Effort. Retrieving and/or using a wand is an action that provokes an attack of opportunity. Wands cannot have more than one instance of a spell active at once, as with spells.

Magical armor needs a recipe to be created, be it researched or found. Each magical ability or power needs at least one specific spell or special material to be created.

Once attuned to magical armor, the wearer knows the effects of the armor, although an identify spell might be needed to learn command words or other specifics on how to initiate powers (DM call). Using a power or ability from magical armor is an action, and does not draw an attack of opportunity.

Armor cannot exceed ten levels when adding together abilities.

Most armor lists the number of assets to Defense and its additional Armor bonus first, and includes additional descriptive words, such as +1/+1 chainmail (which gives one asset to Defense and an additional point of Armor) or +2/+5 leather armor of fire resistance (which gives two assets to Defense and Armor 5 against fire).

### Special Materials

**Adamantine:** Armor made out of this material protects the wearer from critical hits, making defense roll failures of a 1 a standard miss rather than a critical hit.

**Mithril:** Armor made out of this metal is considered one category lighter, such as chainmail that is light armor, or plate mail that is medium armor.

**Cold Iron:** Armor made of this painstakingly slow process is more resistant to the natural attacks of planar creatures, giving Armor 5 against them.

Magical rods need a recipe to be created, be it researched or found. Each magical ability or power needs at least one specific spell or special material to be created.

Once attuned to a magical rod, the wearer knows the constant effects of the rod, although an identify spell might be needed to learn command words or other specifics on how to initiate powers (DM call). ### Rod of Absorption

**Rod, high tier artifact (Level 9)**

While holding this rod, you can use your reaction to absorb a spell that is targeting only you and not with an area of effect. The absorbed spell's effect is canceled, and the spell's energy—not the spell itself—is stored in the rod. The energy has the same pool points as the spell when it was cast. The rod can absorb and store up to 50 pool points of energy over the course of its existence. Once the rod absorbs 50 pool points, it can't absorb more. If you are targeted by a spell that the rod can't store, the rod has no effect on that spell.

When you become attuned to the rod, you know how many pool points of energy the rod has absorbed over the course of its existence, and how many pool points of spell energy it currently has stored.

If you are a spellcaster holding the rod, you can use pool points stored in it to cast spells you have prepared. You use the stored pool points in place of your own, but otherwise cast the spell as normal.

A newly found rod has 1d10 pool points of spell energy stored in it already. A rod that can no longer absorb spell energy and has no energy remaining becomes non-magical.

---

### Rod of Alertness

**Rod, high tier artifact (Level 8)**

This rod has a flanged head and the following properties.

While holding the rod, your Perception checks and rolls for Initiative have an asset.

While holding the rod, you can use an action to cast one of the following spells from it once per day: detect evil and good, detect magic, or detect poison and disease.

As an action, you can plant the haft end of the rod in the ground, whereupon the rod's head sheds bright light in a short radius and dim light in a long radius. While in that bright light, you and any creature that is friendly to you gain an asset to Defense rolls, and can sense the location of any invisible hostile creature that is also in the bright light.

The rod's head stops glowing and the effect ends after 10 minutes, or when a creature uses an action to pull the rod from the ground. This property can't be used again until the next dawn.

---

### Rod of Cancellation

**Rod, high tier artifact (Level 7)**

This dreaded rod is a bane to magic items, for its touch drains an item of all magical properties. The item touched must defend against a level 6 attack to prevent the rod from draining it. If a creature is holding it at the time, then the item can use the holder's Intellect defense instead. In such cases, contact is made by making an attack. Upon draining an item, the rod becomes brittle and cannot be used again.

---

### Rod of Metamagic

**Rod, mid tier artifact (Level 6)**

While holding this rod, when you cast a spell you can use the Enlarge Spell and Extend Spell abilities, using Effort as normal for the metamagic abilities.

---

### Rod of Negation

**Rod, high tier artifact (Level 7)**

While holding this rod, your checks for Dispel Magic (both versions) are eased two steps.

---

### Rod of Potency

**Rod, mid tier artifact (Level 5)**

While holding this rod, when you cast a spell you get a free level of Potency. When you use a level of Effort on a spell attack, you get an additional level of Potency. Potency helps spells resist dispel magic, remove curse, lesser restoration, and similar magics.

---

### Rod of Rulership

**Rod, high tier artifact (Level 7)**

You can use an action to present the rod and command obedience from each creature of your choice that you can see within long range of you. Each target must defend against a level 5 Intellect attack or be charmed by you for 8 hours. While charmed in this way, the creature regards you as its trusted leader. If harmed by you or your companions, or commanded to do something contrary to its nature, a target ceases to be charmed in this way. The rod can't be used again until the next dawn.

---

### Rod of Security

**Rod, high tier artifact (Level 8)**

While holding this rod, you can use an action to activate it. The rod then instantly transports you and up to 9 other willing creatures you can see to a paradise that exists in an extraplanar space. You choose the form that the paradise takes. It could be a tranquil garden, lovely glade, cheery tavern, immense palace, tropical island, fantastic carnival, or whatever else you can imagine. Regardless of its nature, the paradise contains enough water and food to sustain its visitors. Everything else that can be interacted with inside the extraplanar space can exist only there. For example, a flower picked from a garden in the paradise disappears if it is taken outside the extraplanar space.

For each hour spent in the paradise, a visitor regains pool points as if it had spent a recovery roll. Also, creatures don't age while in the paradise, although time passes normally. Visitors can remain in the paradise for up to 10 days divided by the number of creatures present (round down).

When the time runs out or you use an action to end it, all visitors reappear in the location they occupied when you activated the rod, or an unoccupied space nearest that location. The rod can't be used again until ten days have passed.

---

### Rod of Spell Battle

**Rod, mid tier artifact (Level 4)**

While holding this rod, when you use a level of Effort on a spell attack, you get a free level of Effort on damage.

---

### Rod of Spell Power (school)

**Rod, mid tier artifact (Level 6)**

While holding this rod, you are trained in spell attacks from one school of magic, one element, or one foci. If you are already trained, then you are specialized.

Magical weapons need a recipe to be created, be it researched or found. Each magical ability or power needs at least one specific spell or special material to be created.

Once attuned to a magical weapon, the wearer knows the effects of the weapon, although an identify spell might be needed to learn command words or other specifics on how to initiate powers (DM call). Using a power or ability from a magical weapon is an action, and does not draw an attack of opportunity.

Most weapons list the number of assets to Attack and its additional damage bonus first, and includes additional descriptive words, such as +1/+1 longsword (which gives one asset to Attack and an additional point of slashing damage) or +2/+3 flaming waraxe (which gives two assets to Attack and 3 points of additional fire damage).

Weapons cannot exceed ten levels when adding together abilities.

### Special Materials

**Adamantine:** Weapons made out of this material bypass certain types of Armor, such as the Armor provided by spells such as Stoneskin, or the resistance of some creatures against bludgeoning, slashing, and piercing damage.

**Mithril:** Weapons made out of this metal are lighter than normal weapons, weighing only half as much. Medium weapons become one step lighter (light weapons) as far as who can wield them. Heavy weapons cannot be made from mithril.

**Cold Iron:** Weapons made in this painstakingly slow process can bypass the Armor of many types of planar creatures.

**Silver:** Weapons coated in silver can bypass the Armor of lycanthropes as well as many types of planar creatures.

## Wondrous Items

Wondrous represent any items that do not fall into other categories easily. Magical clothing, rings, amulets, cloaks, robes, horns, bracelets, goggles, bracers, belts, and anything similar enchanted with magic are all wondrous items. The cumulative abilities of the magical item determines its level. A wide variety of spells and materials go into making wondrous items.

## Appendix 1: Conditions

### Blinded

A blinded creature can't see and automatically fails any task or check that requires sight. Attack and Defense tasks by the creature are hindered by two steps.

---

### Charmed

A charmed creature can't attack the charmer or target the charmer with harmful abilities or magical effects. A charmed creature's Intellect defense tasks against the charmer that involve social interactions are hindered.

---

### Deafened

A deafened creature can't hear and automatically fails any task or check that requires hearing.

---

### Frightened

A frightened creature's skill checks and attacks are hindered while the source of its fear is within line of sight. The creature can't willingly move closer to the source of its fear.

---

### Grappled

A grappled creature's speed becomes 0, and it can't benefit from any bonus to its speed. The condition ends if the grappler is incapacitated (see the condition). The condition also ends if an effect removes the grappled creature from the reach of the grappler or grappling effect, such as when a creature is hurled away by the thunder wave spell.

---

### Incapacitated

An incapacitated creature can't take actions.

---

### Invisible

An invisible creature is impossible to see without the aid of magic or a special sense. For the purpose of hiding, the creature is heavily obscured. Attack and defense tasks against an invisible creature are hindered two steps.

---

### Paralyzed

A paralyzed creature is incapacitated (see the condition) and can't move or speak. The creature automatically fails Might and Speed tasks. Attack rolls against the creature are eased two steps. Any attack that hits the creature is a critical hit if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature.

---

### Petrified

A petrified creature is transformed, along with any object it is wearing or carrying, into a solid inanimate substance (usually stone). Its weight increases by a factor of ten, and it ceases aging. The creature is incapacitated (see the condition), can't move or speak, and is unaware of its surroundings. Attack rolls against the creature are eased two steps. The creature automatically fails Might and Speed tasks. The creature is immune to poison and disease, although a poison or disease already in its system is suspended, not neutralized.

---

### Poisoned

A poisoned creature is hindered on all tasks.

---

### Prone

A prone creature's only movement option is to crawl, unless it stands up and thereby ends the condition. The creature is hindered on attack rolls. A defense roll against the creature is hindered if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature. Otherwise, the defense roll is eased.

---

### Restrained

A restrained creature's speed becomes 0, and it can't benefit from any bonus to its speed. Attack and defense rolls by the creature are hindered. Spellcasters cannot cast spells with somatic components while restrained. It is impossible to attack with weapons unless they have the light property.

---

### Stunned

A stunned creature is incapacitated (see the condition), can't move, and can speak only falteringly. The creature automatically fails Might and Speed tasks. Defense tasks by the creature are hindered.

---

### Unconscious

An unconscious creature is incapacitated (see the condition), can't move or speak, and is unaware of its surroundings. The creature drops whatever it's holding and falls prone. The creature automatically fails Might and Speed tasks. Any attack that hits the creature is a critical hit if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature.
