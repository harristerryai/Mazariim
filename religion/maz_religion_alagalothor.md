# Alagalothor

**Power Level**: Greater entity  
**Portfolio**: Magic, spellcasting  
**Title(s)**: Dragon Mage, Archmage of Archmages  
**Dominion**: Astral Plane  
**Foci**: Studies Abjuration, Studies Conjuration, Studies Divination, Studies Enchantment, Studies Evocation, Studies Illusion, Studies Necromancy, Studies Transmutation, Worships Arcana  
**Worshipers**: Spellcasters, magical creatures  
**Favored Race**: Human  
**Favored Weapon**: Staff  
**Alignment**: N  
**Symbol**: Magical tendrils wrapped around a dragon eye

---

## General Overview

Alagalothor (ah-la-go-LOW-thor) is the patron deity/entity of magic and spellcasters. He embodies magic in all forms, as well as the sense of discovery in creating new magics and spells as well as magical items.

Alagalothor's church emphasizes the discovery and creation of magic, spells, and other related wonders, in addition to the spread of knowledge and understanding of magic, the universe, and the gods. His churches are often associated with libraries, and sometimes his followers provide magical services to the community at large. His clergy are called Arcanologists and are known to not discriminate when it comes to any type of magic, including the dark arts.

Alagalothor appears in mythology (or on rare occasions, as an avatar) in one of two forms. The first is of a large, powerful silver dragon covered in arcane runes with a large third eye. The other is of a slender tall Dragonborn archmage, wearing a robe with dozens of eyes, floating rather than walking. Alagalothor has appeared from time to time for various reasons to many archmages, as well as in the ancient past when he destroyed a device created by a mortal named Gyru that allowed the human to ascend to godhood.

Although Alagalothor is a human deity, he is venerated by almost every race. Humans have great respect for Alagalothor, but many do not appreciate his tolerance of what some call evil magic. Elves have concerns about the young god of magic, not nearly as old as their deity Minerva. Dwarves think of Alagalothor as a human deity, with the majority of magics worth using being divine and probably from Mordrion. Halflings like Alagalothor, especially the many halfling illusionists. Gnomes are very fond of Alagalothor, with several gnomes holding high positions in his clergy.

Followers of Alagolthor have no set time of worship, with small prayers offered throughout the day for various events, such as spellcasting being exceptionally powerful or precise, or even opening a tome to the right page right away.

## Holy Days

Alagolothorians enjoy two major holidays throughout the year. The **Celebration of Mana** occurs on the third Sunday after the autumnal solstice. This festival brings together spellcasters from across the land for a time of festivities featuring great magical shows of illusions and fireworks that light up the night sky. The celebration serves as a traditional time for making pacts and truces, with many magical agreements and partnerships formed during this sacred period.

The **Night of Stars** is a revered day of sharing magics, celebrated on the evening of the summer solstice when the longest day gives way to darkness. During this holy night, spellcasters gather to teach one another their magical knowledge, share newly discovered spells, and exchange arcane secrets. It is said that those who share their magic with another spellcaster on this night will gain luck for the rest of the year, blessed by Alagalothor himself for their generosity of knowledge.

## Clergy & Temples

The holy clergy of Alagolothor is called **Arcanologists**, and they are the protectors, researchers, and purveyors of magical spells and items across Mazariim. They wear blue and purple garb, usually emblazoned with a dragon eye wrapped in tendrils of magic. Temples to the god of magic are usually adorned with magical effects and illusions and are places of reverence and wonder. All races of sentients are welcome in the clergy of Alagolothor, as are all dispositions of morality and ethics.

## Dogma

*Embrace magic. Feel it flow through you and everything around you. Master the manipulation of magic. Create new spells, and forge magical items. Teach the ways of magic to others, and share its wonders.*

## Mythology

Alagolthor is said to have arrived in this universe over a thousand years before the War (1492 BW), although different faiths tell this story in different ways. According to the holy texts in the Library of the Ages, Alagolothor arrived along with two other entities, Thalaman and Sheeva, from another universe. Alagolothor was said to once be a dragon, and Thalaman a time-traveling plane hopping archmage. Sheeva was a powerful deity, the goddess of poison and revenge. She was locked in battle with the dragon and archmage, and Thalaman worked tremendous magic that ripped apart their universe using the power of Sheeva's avatar to power a spell that was too unstable. The magic collapsed in on itself and caused a state of vacuum decay that spread exponentially through that universe. Alagolothor was able to whisk himself away to another universe, but in the process transported Thalaman and Sheeva with him, a portion of Sheeva's power being transferred to the dragon and archmage, the three of them arriving in Mazariim as entities.

Another famous legend about Alagolothor involves his intervention with a mortal who created a machine to ascend to godhood. The dwarf Gyru activated the machine, absorbing a large amount of mana and becoming effectively immortal. The avatar of Alagolothor immediately destroyed the machine, shattering the device and sending its parts across the planet. Gyru was then taken to Utopia by Alagolothor, where he began to take in infinite wisdom from Guzon.
