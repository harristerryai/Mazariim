# Asti, The Pyramid

**Portfolio:** Healing, dragons, positive energy, ley-lines, light  
**Power Level:** Greater Entity (Old God)  
**Title:** The Pyramid, Keeper of the Lines, The Golden Watcher  
**Dominion:** Positive Energy Plane  
**Alignment:** Lawful Good (LG)  
**Symbol:** Pyramid with ley-lines intersecting it  
**Favored Weapon:** Whip  
**Worshipers:** Healers, dragons, ley-line researchers, those who fight undead  
**Favored Race:** Dragons (particularly metallic)  
**Foci:** Worships Life, Worships Light, Worships Protection, Worships Arcana

---

## General Overview

Asti is unique among the Old Gods—not merely an elemental force or principle of entropy, but an architect. Born approximately 20,000 years before the War to End All Wars, Asti emerged not from Mazariim itself but from the cosmic collision that created this universe. Unlike its alien peers, Asti possesses purpose, intention, and something approaching morality.

The Pyramid (as it is often called) is intrinsically linked to Mazariim's ley-line network—the vast web of magical energy that flows through the planet like arteries carrying blood. Most scholars believe Asti didn't merely discover these lines but created them, establishing a foundation for all magic that would follow.

Where other Old Gods are distant and incomprehensible, Asti actively engages with mortals—healing the sick, empowering paladins, guiding clerics. Yet its alien nature remains. Conversations with Asti are described as speaking with living geometry, its thoughts arriving as mathematical proofs rather than words, its compassion expressed through precise calculations of optimal outcomes.

Asti manifests as a perfect golden pyramid approximately twenty feet tall, its surface inscribed with ever-shifting glyphs that represent magical formulae. Ley-lines become visible in its presence, converging on the pyramid like iron filings to a magnet. When it speaks, light pulses from its apex in patterns that observers somehow understand as language. Some ancient texts describe an earlier form—a humanoid figure with draconic features clothed in golden light—but if this was ever true, Asti abandoned that shape millennia ago in favor of pure geometric perfection.

## Holy Days

The vernal equinox of 21 Garn (April 21) brings the Convergence, celebrating the anniversary of the First Convergence temple's founding. Line-Watchers gather at major ley-line nodes for mass healing rituals. The sick and injured travel miles to attend, and miraculous healings are common as Asti's power flows strongest on this day. The ceremonies continue from dawn through sunset, with golden light streaming from pyramid temples across the land.

On 15 Pyran (July 15), the Dragon's Flight honors metallic dragons and their service to Asti. Line-Watchers make pilgrimages to The Dragon Watch temple, where dragons gather for ceremonies. Non-dragon worshipers are permitted to attend, witnessing aerial displays and receiving blessings from both dragons and clergy. The sky fills with gleaming forms as bronze, copper, brass, silver, and gold dragons perform intricate flight patterns above the temple.

The Geometric Proof occurs on 30 Rivol (August 30), the day when initiates complete their training by solving Asti's theorem. Each candidate stands before the assembled clergy and receives a mathematical-magical puzzle directly from Asti's consciousness. Those who perceive the answer through geometric intuition are promoted to full Line-Walker status. Failures are rare but devastating—those who cannot perceive the answer are forever barred from advanced ranks, though they may continue serving in support roles.

The Day of the Lines arrives on 1 Iquin (September 1), when Line-Walkers embark on annual pilgrimages along major ley-line routes, checking for corruption and purging any undead infestations. Communities along these routes provide hospitality, knowing the Line-Walkers protect them from negative energy. The pilgrims mark their progress by placing small pyramid cairns at minor ley-line intersections, creating a network of sacred markers across the land.

The winter solstice brings the Golden Vigil, the longest night when Line-Watchers maintain continuous vigil at pyramid temples, channeling light through the ley-line network to push back the darkness. This is both literal (magical lights blazing from temples) and metaphorical (combating Zebulba's influence). Throughout the night, the faithful take turns maintaining the flow of positive energy, ensuring the light never falters until dawn arrives.

## Clergy & Temples

Asti's clergy are called Line-Watchers, a mix of clerics, paladins, and specialized mages who dedicate themselves to maintaining the ley-line network and channeling positive energy to heal the sick and fight undead. Unlike the disorganized cults of other Old Gods, Line-Watchers maintain a formal hierarchy based on understanding of magical theory and geometric principles.

The organizational structure places Apex-Keepers at the top as high priests who have achieved deep communion with Asti and become capable of perceiving ley-lines without magical aid. Below them serve the Convergence-Wardens, mid-ranking clergy who maintain temples at major ley-line intersections. Line-Walkers comprise junior priests and paladins who travel ley-line routes, investigating disruptions and purging corruption. At the foundation are Initiate Calculators, apprentices learning to perceive and channel magical geometry.

Line-Watchers wear white robes trimmed with gold, decorated with geometric patterns representing ley-line nodes. Senior clergy incorporate pyramid symbolism through pyramid-shaped headdresses, pendants, or staff-heads. Many carry whips as both weapon and symbol, the multiple cords representing ley-lines converging. The vestments serve not only as identification but as focusing tools for channeling positive energy along the ley-line network.

Line-Watchers observe three primary rituals in their daily practice. The Alignment is a daily meditation performed while facing the nearest major ley-line node, during which practitioners adjust their internal energies to match the network's flow. The Convergence involves group rituals at ley-line intersections where multiple Line-Watchers channel energy together for mass healings or to purge undead from an area. The Geometric Proof serves as a coming-of-age ritual where initiates must solve a complex magical theorem posed directly by Asti itself.

Asti's temples are always built at ley-line convergence points—locations where multiple lines of magical energy intersect. The architecture follows strict geometric principles, with pyramid shapes dominant. The First Convergence beneath modern-day Naarlam is the oldest known temple, dating to the Akovar civilization around 12,000 BW. Seven major ley-lines converge here, creating a nexus of extraordinary power. The temple itself is a seven-tiered pyramid carved from a single piece of white marble, its chambers containing the most complete records of early healing magic.

The Dragon Watch in the Stonetooth Mountains is maintained jointly by Line-Watchers and a council of metallic dragons. Built atop a mountain that serves as a natural ley-line anchor, the temple houses a permanent portal to the Positive Energy Plane. Smaller shrines exist throughout Mazariim, often marked simply by a pyramid-shaped cairn of stones placed precisely at a ley-line intersection.

## Dogma

Asti's teachings blend mathematical precision with compassionate purpose. The first principle holds that all life is sacred geometry. Living beings are complex equations written in flesh and spirit, meaning that to heal is to correct mathematical errors while to kill carelessly is to vandalize the universe's calculations. Every death diminishes the grand equation, while every healing restores balance.

The second teaching demands maintaining the network. Ley-lines are the foundation of all magic and must be protected, studied, and kept free from corruption. A damaged ley-line is like a severed artery where healing stops and undeath spreads. Line-Watchers dedicate their lives to monitoring and repairing these cosmic channels, ensuring positive energy flows freely across the world.

The third principle establishes that light opposes void. Positive and negative energy are mathematical opposites. Where Zebulba brings entropy, Asti brings order. Where undeath spreads, healing must follow. This is not morality but cosmic balance—a fundamental law of magical physics that cannot be negotiated or compromised.

The fourth teaching insists that knowledge must be earned. Magic is not a gift but a calculation. Those who would channel ley-line energy must first understand its geometry. Lazy magic users who rely on raw power without comprehension are dangerous to themselves and others, risking catastrophic magical failures that damage the very network they draw upon.

The final principle declares that dragons are treasured. Of all mortal races, dragons naturally perceive ley-lines and instinctively understand magical geometry. They are Asti's favored children, and to harm a metallic dragon is considered blasphemy. The chromatic dragons' ancient campaign against pyramid temples remains the greatest crime in Line-Watcher theology.

## Mythology

Asti emerged approximately 20,000 years before the War to End All Wars, born not from Mazariim itself but from the cosmic collision that created this universe. Unlike its peers, Asti formed from the interaction between realities, where incompatible laws of physics sought equilibrium. This unique origin granted Asti something the other Old Gods lacked—purpose, intention, and something approaching morality. Asti perceived reality through geometric patterns and living mathematics, and set about establishing what would become the ley-line network, invisible channels where magical energy could flow according to predictable geometric rules.

The Akovarians were Asti's first worshipers, an advanced civilization of mages and healers who arose on the continent of Akovar between approximately 13,000 and 6,830 BW. Long before most mortal races had risen beyond tribal existence, the Akovarians were the first truly advanced society on Mazariim. They didn't merely worship Asti—they collaborated with it, using advanced magic to help refine the ley-line network. During this golden age, Asti was most active, manifesting frequently to teach healing arts and geometric magic. The Akovarians built hundreds of pyramid temples at ley-line nodes, creating a civilization where disease was rare, undead were unknown, and magic flowed as predictably as water through aqueducts.

Disaster struck around 6,830 BW when chromatic dragons, jealous of the metallics' favored status with Asti, launched a campaign to destroy pyramid temples and corrupt ley-lines. Entire flights of dragons from around Mazariim converged on Akovar, drawn by the power of the ley-line network. The Akovarian clergy had foreseen this threat and created a desperate contingency—the Mask of the Pharaoh, a legendary artifact constructed from priceless jewels and exotic metals, designed to hold Asti's essence if the Great Temple was ever in mortal danger.

During the final battle at the Great Temple, as powerful great wyrms assaulted the capital, the arcanists and priests enacted their last stand. In a desperate ritual, they transferred Asti's avatar—the entity's entire consciousness and power—into the Mask of the Pharaoh. Simultaneously, they wove a massive transportation spell to evacuate the majority of the population to a far-away land. The ley-lines, suddenly deprived of Asti's stabilizing presence, surged wildly and exploded. The magical detonations killed most of the attacking dragons and destroyed the capital and other major cities. Survivors found their bodies mutating from the magical radiation, with some infused with dragon blood, creating the first dragonborn.

The transported Akovarians arrived in what is now the Ancient South, but the ritual had terrible side effects. High priest Thz'zar Ja'laao went mad from channeling the Mask's power, and the population lost their memories of Akovar. Survivors eventually founded the Varkonan culture, guarding the Mask as a sacred but poorly understood relic. Asti was imprisoned within the Mask—not dormant by choice, but trapped, unable to manifest or intervene. The ley-line network was shattered, with fully one-third of the lines corrupted or severed. The Akovarian civilization collapsed immediately.

For nearly 7,645 years from 6,830 BW to 815 AW, Asti remained imprisoned within the Mask of the Pharaoh, hidden in distant Varkonan lands. The ley-lines slowly healed themselves through natural magical processes, but without the Pyramid's guidance, many became twisted or unstable. Necromancy flourished in this period—with fewer healers and weaker positive energy flows, undeath spread more easily. Line-Watchers became guardians of lost knowledge, maintaining crumbling pyramid temples and desperately trying to preserve Akovarian healing techniques. They didn't know Asti was imprisoned—they believed the entity was simply dormant, recovering from the ancient catastrophe. Most people forgot Asti entirely, remembering only vague legends of a Golden Age when disease didn't exist.

In 815 AW, a group of adventurers traveled to Akovar to explore the ancient ruins. After piecing together the civilization's history and learning of the Mask of the Pharaoh, they journeyed to the Ancient South (Varkonan territories) and recovered the artifact from the Nepherites Pyramid, where it had been hidden for millennia. Returning to Akovar's ruined capital, they fought their way through a red dragon clan that had claimed the city. At the Grand Temple—the site where Asti's avatar had once hovered—they performed the release ritual. Asti's essence burst free from the Mask in a blaze of golden light, and the entity's consciousness finally awakened after 7,645 years of imprisonment.

The ley-lines across Akovar blazed back to life. Healing energy pulsed through the islands. Ancient pyramid temples filled with light for the first time in millennia. The ripples of Asti's return were felt across Mazariim, stirring other long-sleeping entities and powerful creatures from their slumber. Asti has been active and conscious since 815 AW, slowly working to restore what was lost and rebuild the ley-line network across the world. Each year, new ley-line nodes are discovered. Ancient pyramid temples, buried or forgotten, are revealed through dreams sent to Line-Watchers. The Old God is patient—having waited nearly eight thousand years, it can wait as long as necessary to restore what was lost.

Asti's relationships with other entities define much of its modern influence. Zebulba stands as Asti's perfect opposite—positive versus negative, healing versus entropy, order versus decay. They are locked in eternal opposition, though neither truly fights. They simply exist in contradiction. Where Zebulba's influence spreads undeath, Asti's ley-lines purge it away. Mau the Oracle observed Asti's birth and seems to understand its purpose. Some Line-Watchers believe Mau and Asti formed an accord where Mau would observe and record while Asti would actively heal and protect.

Mordrion maintains a complex relationship with Asti, respecting the ancient entity's power and its role in establishing magical infrastructure, but seeing its worship as outdated. Many Line-Watchers are also followers of Mordrion, seeing no contradiction in honoring both. Tiovin the human god of healing often claims credit for miracles worked through Asti's ley-lines. Line-Watchers find this amusing but don't dispute it—healing matters more than credit.

Alagalothor the Dragon Mage studied extensively at pyramid temples and incorporated Asti's geometric magic into his teachings. Alagalothor's Arcanologists and Asti's Line-Watchers cooperate frequently on magical research. Metallic dragons revere Asti as a creator-figure and protector. Many believe Asti shaped the first metallic dragons from pure positive energy and mathematical principle. Chromatic dragons hate Asti for favoring their enemies and still occasionally attack pyramid temples out of ancient spite.

Asti's resurgence has been quiet but steady since 815 AW. The Restored Line, a network of approximately 200 Line-Watchers across Mazariim headquartered at The First Convergence in Naarlam, coordinates ley-line research, trains new initiates, and responds to magical corruption. Several metallic dragon flights maintain formal devotion to Asti, protecting pyramid temples and serving as advisors to Line-Watchers. Many who study healing magic or magical theory encounter Asti's teachings and become worshipers even if they never formally join the clergy. Those who hunt undead or explore ancient ruins often adopt Asti as a patron, appreciating the practical benefits of healing magic and undead-fighting abilities even if they don't understand the geometric theology. With the ley-line network's resurgence, magical academies are reopening Akovar ruins and studying ancient pyramid temples, leading to renewed scholarly interest in the Old God.

The lines converge. The calculations balance. Light overcomes void.
