# Mordrion

- Portfolio: Dwarves, battle, smithing
- Power Level: Greater entity (original universe: Oerth)
- Title(s): The Dwarf Father, The All-Hammer
- Dominion: Utopia
- Foci: Worships Forge, Worships Strength, Worships War
- Worshipers: Dwarves, warriors, smiths
- Favored Race: Dwarf
- Favored Weapon: Warhammer
- Alignment: LN
- Symbol: Anvil with crossed warhammers

---

## General Overview

Mordrion is the patron deity of dwarvenkind and the most widely worshiped entity among dwarves in Mazariim. He arrived from another universe (Oerth) in 1866 BW during the height of the Dwarven Wars, when the northern Gormdawi clans and southern Bryndawi clans were locked in brutal conflict over unification under the Dwarven Empire. His timely arrival and intervention brought peace to dwarvenkind and ushered in an era of unprecedented growth, prosperity, and mastery of forge and battle.

Mordrion teaches that strength, honor in battle, and perfection of the forge are the pillars of dwarven society. He embodies the ideals of hard work, tenacity, loyalty, and the creation of masterful arms and armor. All dwarven smiths invoke his name when crafting weapons and armor, and his clergy serves as advisors to clan elders, jarls, and kings throughout the dwarven holds.

Though Mordrion is lawful in nature, emphasizing order, tradition, and the importance of clan and ancestry, he is also a god of war and understands that battle is sometimes necessary to defend what dwarves hold dear. During the War to End All Wars, Mordrion led dwarven forces personally before his avatar was destroyed in combat with Zuel the Devourer in 1 BW.

Mordrion's temples can be found in every dwarven town and hold, from the grand underground cathedrals of the Dwarven Empire to smaller shrines in independent southern settlements. His worship has also spread to some humans, gnomes, and other races who value craftsmanship and martial prowess.

## Holy Days

**Day of Unity** falls on the 15th of Duraz (March 15) and commemorates the end of the Dwarven Wars in 1849 BW when Mordrion brokered peace between the Gormdawi and Bryndawi. This celebration brings together dwarven clans from across Nigal in recognition that unity is strength and that dwarvenkind must never again war against itself. The day features great feasts where clans that once fought now share mead and food in friendship. The exchanging of gifts between clans reinforces bonds of alliance and goodwill, with master crafters often presenting their finest works to representatives of other clans. Ceremonial peace treaties are renewed, reminding all dwarves of Mordrion's teaching that disagreements must be settled through words and arbitration rather than blood.

**The Forging** occurs on the 1st of Gyru (June 1) and is the holiest day for followers of Mordrion. Every forge in every dwarven hold is blessed at dawn by Hammerpriests who invoke Mordrion's name and ask for his favor upon the works to be created in the coming year. Master smiths present their finest works in temple ceremonies, competing for recognition and the honor of having their creations displayed in Mordrion's temples. Young dwarves come of age on this day and receive their first warhammer or tools, marking their transition into adulthood and their entry into their chosen craft. The day concludes with demonstrations of smithing skill, feasting, and the sharing of techniques between masters and apprentices.

**Stoneshaper's Vigil** takes place on the 30th of Mord (December 30) and honors the dwarven hero Demious Stoneshaper who slew Zuel's avatar in the final battle of the War to End All Wars. Dwarves hold vigil through the night, recounting tales of heroes past and present, ensuring that the deeds of the brave are never forgotten. Warriors sharpen their weapons in preparation for the new year, symbolically readying themselves for whatever challenges may come. Hammerpriests lead the assembled dwarves in reciting the names of those who fell during the War, and families light candles in memory of lost ancestors. The vigil ends at dawn when the sun rises on the new year, representing hope and renewal.

**Ancestor's Feast** occurs on various dates determined by each clan and celebrates the founding of that particular clan and honors its lineage. Hammerpriests recite the names of ancestors going back generations, sometimes taking hours to complete the full genealogy. Families gather to share stories of those who came before—tales of great smiths, brave warriors, wise leaders, and ordinary dwarves who lived honorable lives. Young dwarves learn their clan's history and understand their place in the long chain of generations. Each clan maintains its own traditions for this feast, with some emphasizing martial accomplishments while others focus on craftsmanship or leadership.

## Clergy & Temples

Mordrion's clergy are called Hammerpriests (or Thulgrom in Dwarvish). They wear ceremonial armor adorned with the symbol of the anvil and crossed warhammers, often incorporating steel, iron, and mithral into their vestments. The armor serves both symbolic and practical purposes—it represents Mordrion's dual nature as god of both forge and battle, and it allows Hammerpriests to fight alongside warriors when necessary. Senior priests bear master-crafted warhammers as both symbols of office and weapons of war, and these weapons often become legendary artifacts passed down through generations of clergy.

The Hammerpriests serve multiple roles in dwarven society. They act as spiritual leaders conducting rituals, blessings of forges, and ceremonies marking major life events such as namings, comings of age, marriages, and funerals. As advisors to rulers, they offer counsel to jarls, clan elders, and the royal family of the Dwarven Empire, providing not only spiritual guidance but practical wisdom drawn from centuries of accumulated knowledge. Many Hammerpriests are accomplished smiths themselves, serving as master crafters who create blessed weapons and armor that are sought after across Mazariim. They also serve as war chaplains, accompanying dwarven armies into battle and inspiring warriors with divine strength, healing the wounded, and performing last rites for the fallen.

The hierarchy of the Hammerpriests is based on both spiritual authority and mastery of craft. A priest who can create masterwork weapons and armor while also demonstrating deep understanding of Mordrion's teachings earns greater respect than one who excels in only one area. This ensures that the clergy remains practical and connected to the everyday concerns of dwarven life rather than becoming disconnected from their people.

Temples of Mordrion are magnificent structures carved from living stone, featuring grand forges, anvils, and armories. The largest temples in the Dwarven Empire contain forges that have burned continuously for centuries, tended by the Hammerpriests and fed with coal blessed in Mordrion's name. These forges are used to create masterwork weapons and armor, and dwarves from across Nigal make pilgrimages to have their greatest works blessed in these sacred fires. Some believe that items forged in temple fires and blessed by Hammerpriests carry a trace of Mordrion's own power.

Temple architecture emphasizes strength and permanence, with massive stone columns supporting vaulted ceilings, intricate metalwork adorning doors and railings, and halls filled with the weapons and armor of legendary dwarven heroes. Walking through a major temple is like walking through dwarven history—each artifact tells a story, each name carved in stone represents a life lived in service to clan and craft. Many temples also house archives preserving clan lineages, ancestral records, and the history of great battles, making them centers of learning as well as worship.

Smaller shrines can be found in mines, frontier settlements, and military outposts. These simple shrines typically feature an anvil, a burning forge (if fuel permits), and Mordrion's symbol carved into stone. Even in the most remote locations, dwarves maintain these shrines and make offerings before beginning dangerous work.

## Dogma

The teachings of Mordrion, as recorded in the Kodex Thulgrom (The Hammer's Code), emphasize several core principles that define dwarven culture and character. The first teaching calls followers to **perfect their craft**, whether as smith, warrior, or artisan. Dwarves must dedicate themselves to mastery, understanding that every blade must be sharp, every armor plate fitted true, and every task completed with excellence. Mediocrity is not acceptable—a dwarf who does shoddy work brings shame upon themselves and their clan. This teaching drives dwarves to become the finest craftsmen in Mazariim, creating works that last for centuries.

The doctrine instructs dwarves to **honor their ancestors**, recognizing that their lineage stretches back through the ages. Dwarves must remember those who came before, uphold their honor, and pass their clan's legacy to the next generation. This is not merely reverence for the dead but an active obligation—each dwarf is a link in a chain connecting past to future, and breaking that chain through dishonorable behavior or failure to teach the young is a grave failing.

Followers are taught to **stand firm in battle**, understanding that when war comes they must not falter but defend their kin, their hold, and their honor with steel and stone. Cowardice is the greatest shame a dwarf can bear. Mordrion understands that not all dwarves are warriors, but all must have the courage to defend what matters when the time comes. This teaching has made dwarves renowned as fearsome fighters who never retreat and never surrender.

The teaching emphasizes that followers must **serve their clan**, recognizing that individual glory means nothing if their clan suffers. Dwarves must work for the good of their people, understanding that cooperation and mutual support bring prosperity to all. The clan is more than family—it is identity, purpose, and legacy. A dwarf who abandons their clan or places personal gain above their clan's welfare violates one of Mordrion's most sacred teachings.

The doctrine calls dwarves to **unite dwarvenkind**, remembering that the Dwarven Wars nearly destroyed their people. Never again shall dwarf raise axe against dwarf. Disagreements must be settled through words and arbitration rather than blood. When Mordrion first arrived, he found dwarves killing each other in brutal civil war. His greatest gift was not just ending that war but instilling in dwarvenkind the understanding that their survival depends on unity.

Finally, followers are instructed to **share their knowledge**, understanding that the secrets of the forge, of battle tactics, and of stonework are gifts to be passed down. Dwarves must train apprentices, share techniques, and ensure dwarven craftsmanship endures. Hoarding knowledge out of pride or greed weakens the entire race. This teaching has created a culture of mentorship where master craftsmen take pride in training the next generation to surpass them.

## Mythology

Long ago on a world called Oerth in another universe, two mighty gods warred for eons: Moradin the god of the dwarves and Gruumsh the god of the orcs. Their conflict was ancient beyond mortal reckoning, stretching back to the creation of their respective races. Moradin sought to build, to create, to establish order and craft. Gruumsh sought to conquer, to destroy, to prove orcish supremacy through endless warfare. The two gods clashed repeatedly, with their battles shaping the history of Oerth.

Their final battle reached a cataclysmic climax when Gruumsh's spear tore out Moradin's eye as Moradin's axe cleaved Gruumsh's left hand. The surge of magical power from these grievous deific wounds tore open the fabric between universes. Reality itself screamed as the barrier between worlds was ripped apart. Both gods were sucked through the tear and hurled across infinite distances into the universe of Mazariim.

The journey between universes drained them terribly. Crossing that impossible gulf consumed vast amounts of their divine essence, and both fell into a coma-like slumber that lasted for unknown ages. When they finally awoke, they had been changed by the different magical laws of this new reality. Moradin became Mordrion, and Gruumsh became Zuel. Both realized they were no longer immortal as they had been on Oerth. Their power had diminished significantly—they were now entities rather than true gods, powerful but mortal in the sense that they could be destroyed.

Mordrion arrived in Mazariim in 1866 BW, awakening in the dwarven lands during the height of the Dwarven Wars. The northern Gormdawi clans sought to unify all dwarves under the Dwarven Empire, while the southern Bryndawi clans fiercely resisted, seeing unification as the loss of their independence and traditions. The war had raged for generations, with brother killing brother and clan feuds growing deeper with each passing year.

Mordrion appeared to dwarves in many of their most populous cities, manifesting in visions, speaking to clan elders, and performing miracles that proved his divine nature. He declared that the dwarven race needed to be unified and that he had arrived to help them achieve what they could not accomplish alone. His message was not one of domination but of reconciliation—he respected both northern and southern traditions and sought to preserve what was valuable in each while ending the bloodshed.

By 1849 BW, through diplomacy, displays of divine power, and wisdom, Mordrion had helped the dwarves broker peace. He mediated disputes, proposed compromises, and demonstrated that unity did not require uniformity. The Dwarven Wars ended, and both northern and southern dwarves accepted Mordrion as their patron. From that time forward, dwarven holds expanded into new territories, gained knowledge of rare and challenging metals like mithral and adamantine, learned to create masterful arms and armor that were the envy of all races, and solidified their embrace of the mountains and valleys of Nigal.

The worship of Mordrion gradually replaced the veneration of Mau and the elemental gods (especially Dirgin) as the dominant faith among dwarves. Many older dwarves initially resisted, viewing Mordrion as an outsider who did not understand dwarven ways. But Mordrion proved himself by honoring dwarven traditions, learning their customs, and speaking Dwarvish fluently. He did not demand the abandonment of the old ways but rather incorporated them into his teachings. To this day, many dwarves still pay homage to Dirgin as the ancient Stonefather, and Mordrion encourages this, seeing it as respect for what came before.

During the War to End All Wars between 3 BW and 1 AW, Mordrion played a crucial role. When the War began around 3 BW, Mordrion took a direct role in defending dwarvenkind and all of Mazariim from the orcish hordes led by his ancient enemy, now called Zuel. The conflict was personal for both entities—their ancient grudge from Oerth reignited with renewed fury. Dwarves suffered more losses than any other race during the War, with most holds overtaken and their population decimated. The southern kingdoms were nearly wiped out entirely, and even the mighty northern Empire saw its population reduced by more than half.

Mordrion led dwarven forces personally, fighting alongside his people and inspiring legendary acts of heroism. His presence on the battlefield turned certain defeats into victories. Warriors who saw Mordrion fight alongside them found courage they didn't know they possessed. Smiths working in his presence created weapons of unprecedented quality. His avatar became a rallying point for all free peoples, not just dwarves.

In the final battle of 1 BW, as Zuel's avatar led a countless army toward Fort Hope, Mordrion confronted his ancient foe once again. The two titans clashed in single combat while armies surged around them. Their battle shook the earth and split the sky. In that spectacular final confrontation, Mordrion's avatar was destroyed by Zuel—the orc god's spear finally claiming the vengeance Gruumsh had sought for eons.

But immediately after Mordrion fell, Zuel himself was defeated by a dwarven hero named Demious Stoneshaper, who wielded Mordrion's blessed warhammer. In his final moment, Mordrion had passed his weapon to Stoneshaper and blessed him with enough divine power to land the killing blow. Zuel's avatar was destroyed, and the orcish army, seeing their god fall, broke and fled.

Though Mordrion's avatar was banished for 101 years, his sacrifice ensured the survival of dwarvenkind. His final act—empowering a mortal dwarf to succeed where he had failed—demonstrated the core of his teaching: that mortals must forge their own destiny, that gods can guide but mortals must act, and that the greatest glory comes from achieving what seems impossible.

When Mordrion's avatar finally returned in 102 AW, he found a unified dwarven people rebuilding their civilization and reclaiming their ancestral holds. The survivors of the War had not forgotten his sacrifice. They had preserved his temples, maintained his forges, taught his ways to the next generation, and ensured that his return would be to a people worthy of his patronage.

Over the eight centuries since the War, Mordrion's worship has continued to grow. Trade in dwarven weapons and armor flourishes throughout Mazariim, with kingdoms paying premium prices for dwarven-forged blades and armor. Alliances with other races have strengthened, particularly with humans, gnomes, and halflings. The Common language developed during the War era has allowed dwarven culture to influence and be influenced by other societies, though dwarves remain proud of their unique heritage.

Today, Mordrion's temples stand as centers of dwarven life, and his clergy continues to guide dwarvenkind through both prosperity and challenge. Mordrion maintains good relations with Dirgin (whom many dwarves still revere as the ancient earth father), Rivoleen (patron of the gnomes and a fellow craftsman deity), and Tiovin (patron of good humans with whom dwarves have strong alliances). He respects Paladine for his honor and martial prowess, seeing in the dragon-descended entity a kindred spirit who values duty and courage.

Mordrion's eternal enemy remains Zuel the Devourer, his ancient foe from Oerth. Though both have lost avatars and been temporarily banished, neither has forgotten their grudge. Their conflict defines much of the tension between dwarves and orcs in Mazariim. Mordrion also opposes entities of chaos and corruption who threaten the order and craft he values.

Mordrion has a complicated relationship with Minerva, respecting the elven deity's dedication to her people but sometimes clashing over philosophies of warfare versus pacifism. Where Minerva teaches that violence should be avoided whenever possible, Mordrion teaches that some threats can only be answered with steel. Both agree on the importance of protecting their people, but they differ on methods and philosophy.
