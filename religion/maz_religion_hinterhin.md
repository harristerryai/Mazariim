# Hinterhin

**Portfolio:** Halflings, harvest, nature, family  
**Power Level:** Greater entity  
**Title:** Father of the Hin, The Harvest Lord  
**Dominion:** Hinterhome  
**Foci:** Worships Nature, Worships Life, Worships Luck  
**Worshipers:** Halflings, farmers, families  
**Favored Race:** Halfling (Hin)  
**Favored Weapon:** Sling  
**Alignment:** CG  
**Symbol:** Sheaf of wheat crossed with a walking stick

---

## General Overview

Hinterhin is the beloved patron deity of the halfling people (who call themselves *hin*), awakened in 2971 BW. He embodies the halfling ideals of community, family, good food, beautiful weather, and the simple joys of a peaceful life. Unlike more martial or ambitious deities, Hinterhin promotes contentment, harmony with nature, and the importance of festivals and celebration.

Hinterhin is credited in halfling legend with running his deific hands through the soil of Shalazar (the halfling homeland), making it incredibly fertile and productive. He waved his hands to make the weather perpetually beautiful and bountiful—or so the stories go. Whether through divine intervention or natural fortune, Shalazar is indeed blessed with rich farmland and favorable growing conditions, allowing halflings to become the finest farmers and cooks in all of Mazariim.

While Hinterhin assists his people indirectly through agricultural bounty and good weather, he rarely intervenes directly in worldly affairs. His influence is subtle and gentle, like a warm breeze or a timely rain. During the War to End All Wars, Hinterhin helped feed and hide millions of refugees, demonstrating that nurturing and protecting life can be just as heroic as battle.

Hinterhin's afterlife realm, **Hinterhome**, is a paradise of endless feasts, beautiful gardens, and joyful family reunions. Halfling souls gather there to celebrate with ancestors, enjoy perfect weather, and live in eternal comfort and community.

## Holy Days

Halflings celebrate more holidays than any other race in Mazariim, observing over three dozen throughout the year. **First Planting** on the first of Quarz (January 1) marks the new year with the blessing of seeds and the planting of the first crops, as Greenwardens bless every field and families exchange seeds as tokens of good fortune. **Midsummer Feast** on the twenty-first of Hin (July 21) celebrates the longest day of the year with an all-day festival featuring music, dancing, games, and an enormous communal feast where every family contributes a dish and the feasting continues until sunset.

**Harvest Home** on the first of Zuel (October 1) marks the first major harvest with thanksgiving, parades of decorated wagons, and harvest competitions for the largest pumpkin, best pie, and similar achievements, with the day ending in a great bonfire and storytelling. **Festival of Lanterns** on the thirtieth of Mord (December 30) takes place on the longest night of the year, when halflings light thousands of lanterns and candles to guide Hinterhin's blessings into the new year while families gather to share stories, sing songs, and make wishes for the year ahead. **Hearthfire Day** on the fifteenth of Gyru (June 15) celebrates community and family as villages organize games, feasts, and gift exchanges, with the tradition of inviting someone new into your home for a meal, whether a stranger, a traveler, or a neighbor you wish to know better.

## Clergy & Temples

Hinterhin's clergy are called **Greenwardens** (or *Duni'hin* in the Halfling tongue). They wear simple robes in earth tones—greens, browns, and golds—decorated with wheat sheaves, flowers, and other natural symbols. Many carry wooden walking sticks carved with family crests and blessed by Hinterhin.

The Greenwardens serve as both spiritual and judicial leaders in halfling society. They act as spiritual guides leading festivals, blessing harvests, and performing marriages and naming ceremonies. As community leaders, they mediate disputes, organize communal work, and ensure the needs of the village are met. Their role as agricultural experts involves teaching farming techniques, predicting weather, and blessing fields for bountiful harvests. They also serve as healers, tending to the sick and injured using herbal remedies and divine magic.

Unlike many other faiths, Hinterhin's clergy do not form a rigid hierarchy. Each village or settlement has its own Greenwarden (or several), and they gather occasionally for regional festivals but operate independently.

Halfling temples to Hinterhin are modest and welcoming structures, often featuring open-air gardens, community kitchens, and gathering halls. Rather than grand stone cathedrals, these temples are built of wood, thatch, and fieldstone, blending seamlessly into the countryside. The heart of every temple is the **Hearthfire**, a communal fire pit or oven where food is prepared for festivals and shared meals. Surrounding the temple are gardens where herbs, vegetables, and flowers grow in abundance. Many temples also include a **Harvest Hall** where the community stores surplus food to be distributed to those in need. Temple architecture emphasizes warmth, welcome, and accessibility. No one is turned away from a temple of Hinterhin, and travelers can always find a meal and a place to rest.

## Dogma

The teachings of Hinterhin, as recorded in the Book of Simple Joys, emphasize the importance of cherishing family and community, recognizing that your family and neighbors are your greatest treasure, caring for them, celebrating with them, and never letting petty grievances divide you. The doctrine teaches that one should work the land, understanding that agriculture is sacred and that by tending fields with love and respect, the earth will provide, wasting nothing and sharing abundance. Followers are instructed to celebrate life's blessings, not waiting for grand occasions to celebrate but recognizing that every good harvest, every birth, and every friendship is worthy of a festival, with joy being its own reward.

The faith teaches that one should live simply and peacefully, understanding that wealth and power bring only worry and conflict, while a full belly, a warm hearth, and good friends are worth more than gold. Respect for nature's balance requires taking only what you need, giving thanks for what you receive, and always leaving the land better than you found it. The teaching to welcome strangers reminds followers that the traveler at your door may be Hinterhin himself in disguise, encouraging them to offer food and shelter freely, knowing that kindness will return. Finally, the embrace of luck acknowledges that life is unpredictable and fortune favors those with open hearts, teaching that when luck comes your way you should share it, and when misfortune strikes, your community will catch you.

## Mythology

The halflings had already been living on the island of Shalazar for centuries when Hinterhin awakened in 2971 BW. Before his arrival, halfling druids who worshiped the elemental gods controlled society, and life was simple but harder than it would become. When Hinterhin first made contact with the halflings, he appeared not as a towering figure of power but as a humble halfling farmer, walking barefoot through the fields with a walking stick and a sack of seeds. He taught the halflings better farming techniques, introduced new crops, and blessed the land itself.

According to legend, Hinterhin knelt and ran his hands through the rich, dark soil of Shalazar, imbuing it with divine fertility. He then stood and gestured to the sky, calming the storms and bringing gentle rains and abundant sunshine. From that day forward, Shalazar became the most productive farmland in all of Mazariim.

Over the centuries following Hinterhin's awakening from 2970 to 2500 BW, his clergy slowly integrated into leadership positions alongside the elemental druids. Rather than conflict, this transition was peaceful—many druids saw Hinterhin as a natural extension of their own elemental worship, and the two traditions coexist harmoniously to this day. The Greenwardens established the tradition of communal living, where the needs of the village supersede the needs of the individual. Work is divided according to talent, and everyone contributes to the common good. This system has served the halflings well, creating a society with little crime, no nobles or serfs, and a strong sense of shared purpose.

When the orcish invasion began during the War to End All Wars from 3 BW to 1 AW, halflings were among the most vulnerable peoples in Mazariim. Shalazar, though an island, was not immune to attack, and refugees from the mainland flooded into halfling lands seeking safety. Hinterhin's greatest act during the War was not martial but nurturing: he blessed the fields of Shalazar and the surrounding regions to produce far beyond their natural capacity, feeding millions of refugees. Halfling farmers worked day and night to harvest and distribute food, and the Greenwardens organized massive relief efforts. Additionally, Hinterhin used his influence over weather and luck to hide refugee convoys from orcish scouts, guiding them to safety through fog and misdirection. While other entities fought with sword and spell, Hinterhin fought with bread and sanctuary.

After the War, halflings returned to their peaceful lives, but their reputation as master farmers and generous hosts spread throughout Mazariim. Many halflings began traveling beyond Shalazar, settling in human and elven lands, opening inns and farms, and sharing their agricultural expertise. Today, Hinterhin's worship is found wherever halflings live, and even non-halflings respect his teachings. Temples and shrines dot the countryside, and over three dozen festivals are celebrated throughout the year, most tied to agricultural cycles, harvests, and seasonal changes.

Hinterhin is on good terms with Minerva, as many halflings also revere her, and with Tiovin, sharing ideals of goodness and community. He maintains friendly relations with Rivoleen, as both favor freedom and peaceful living. He respects Dirgin and the elemental gods, as halflings still honor them. Hinterhin has no true enemies but opposes Zuel, Quarzel, and other war-focused entities. He also dislikes Xannith, whose greed contradicts halfling values of generosity and contentment. Hinterhin is indifferent to most other entities, caring little for cosmic struggles as long as his people can live in peace.
