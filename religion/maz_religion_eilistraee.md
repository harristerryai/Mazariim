# Eilistraee

**Power Level:** Lesser Entity (Immigrant from Abier-Toril/Faerun)  
**Portfolio:** Song, moon, beauty, dance, drow redemption, elves  
**Title:** The Dark Maiden, Lady of the Dance, Moonlight's Promise  
**Dominion:** Elysium (shared with Minerva)  
**Foci:** Worships Moon, Worships Nature, Is Descended From Fey  
**Worshipers:** Drow seeking redemption, elves, bards, hunters, druids, rangers  
**Favored Race:** Elf (especially Drow)  
**Favored Weapon:** Bastard sword  
**Alignment:** Chaotic Good  
**Symbol:** Bastard sword crossed with the moon

---

## General Overview

Eilistraee is redemption incarnate—the belief that no soul is beyond salvation, that even those born into darkness can choose the light, that beauty and joy exist even in the deepest despair. She is not the goddess of elven supremacy like some of her kin or of nature's harsh cycles like Vuleala. She is the goddess of second chances for the damned, of moonlit dances in the forest, of hope for those who have none.

Where Rivoleen represents elven high culture and magical mastery, and Minerva represents ancient knowledge and wisdom, Eilistraee represents something more personal and intimate: the small kindnesses that save souls. A song sung in the darkness. A hand extended to an enemy. A dance under the stars. These are her gifts, and they are deceptively powerful.

She arrived on Mazariim in 1377 BW from the universe of Abier-Toril, also known as Faerun—a cosmos so saturated with divine power that even minor gods wielded continent-shaping magic. In that distant universe, she witnessed great tragedies and chose to follow those who had been cast into darkness, not to rule them, but to offer them a path back to the light.

For millennia in Faerun, Eilistraee walked the Underdark, singing to drow who had never seen the sun, teaching them that tyranny was not their only destiny. She built hidden groves where drow could dance under moonlight, taught them the joy of song, and protected those brave enough to seek freedom. She was never powerful—dark forces ensured that—but she was persistent.

Then came the Spellplague, a catastrophic magical event in Faerun. Her brother Vhaeraun (god of drow thieves and male rebellion) attempted to assassinate Eilistraee, believing that absorbing her power would make him strong enough to challenge the forces of darkness. The two fought, and in the chaos, both were believed dead. The drow who had followed Eilistraee mourned, believing their last hope had been extinguished.

But Eilistraee did not die. A fragment of her divine essence—along with a small group of faithful drow—was torn from Faerun and cast into the planar void. They drifted for subjective years, though time has no meaning between universes, until they emerged on Mazariim in 1377 BW.

Minerva found them first. The ancient goddess of knowledge recognized Eilistraee immediately—not as an invader but as a kindred spirit. Both were goddesses who valued enlightenment over conquest, who offered wisdom rather than demanding worship. Minerva welcomed Eilistraee to Elysium, her own planar domain, and gave her space to heal.

The drow who arrived with Eilistraee became the first of Mazariim's surface drow—dark elves who rejected the tyranny and cruelty of the Underdark and chose a new life in the sunlight, or at least under the moon. They were few—perhaps only a few hundred—but they were fervent. They established hidden groves in elven forests, built shrines under moonlight, and began teaching that drow did not have to be evil.

Eilistraee's arrival had profound consequences for Mazariim. Most importantly, it provided proof that drow could be good. Before 1377 BW, drow on Mazariim were universally feared as demon-worshiping slavers and assassins, but Eilistraee's followers demonstrated that dark elves were not inherently evil but rather victims of cruel indoctrination. The alliance with Minerva created a theological bridge between elven culture and human or universal knowledge, with Eilistraee's followers often serving as intermediaries between isolated elven communities and the wider world. Over time, integration with elven theology occurred as Rivoleen (goddess of elven magic and culture) initially viewed Eilistraee with suspicion due to her following of dark elves, but over centuries the two reached an uneasy truce wherein Eilistraee does not challenge Rivoleen's authority over high elves while Rivoleen does not interfere with Eilistraee's redemption of drow.

Eilistraee appears as a tall, graceful drow woman with ebony skin that seems to absorb light, silver hair that moves as though caught in a gentle breeze, and eyes like twin full moons. She carries a singing sword—a bastard sword that hums with moonlight and whispers songs of hope. She wears little armor, often just moonsilk wraps, because she believes freedom of movement is sacred. She dances constantly, even in conversation, as though standing still would cause her to fade.

Her ultimate goal is not power or dominion—it is redemption for every drow soul. She wants to free the drow from the tyranny of darkness and cruelty, to show them that beauty, joy, and freedom are not weaknesses but strengths. She will never give up. Even if every drow on Mazariim rejects her, she will keep singing, keep dancing, keep offering hope. Because Eilistraee believes that one saved soul is worth eternity.

## Holy Days

The **First Dance** marks the anniversary of Eilistraee's arrival on Mazariim in 1377 BW, celebrated on the thirteenth of Rivoleen (June 13). Followers gather in moonlit groves to reenact the first dance when Eilistraee's original drow refugees danced for the first time in freedom, celebrating survival and hope through all-night dancing, singing of the Hymn of Freedom, ritual sword-blessing, initiation of new Silverhairs, sharing stories of successful escapes from the Underdark, and consuming moonflower wine.

The **Moonlit Hunt** occurs monthly at each full moon as a celebration combining worship and practical training. Followers hunt, usually deer or wild boar, under moonlight, dance to honor the prey's sacrifice, and feast together while learning survival skills necessary for drow refugees unfamiliar with surface wilderness. The observance includes moonlight hunting, ritual butchering and cooking, feasting under the stars, sharing of stories, teaching survival skills, and moonlight dancing until dawn.

The **Night of Silver Tears** on the twenty-seventh of Zebulba (December 27) provides a solemn remembrance of drow who died trying to escape the Underdark's tyranny, including those killed by assassins, those who could not adapt to surface life and took their own lives, and those recaptured and dragged back to the depths. Followers light candles, sing mourning songs, and renew their vows to continue Eilistraee's work through candlelit vigils, singing of The Lament of the Lost, reading names of the dead, renewal of vows, silent moonlight walks, and planting moonflowers in memory of the fallen.

The **Sword Moon** celebrates the winter solstice, the longest night of the year and a night sacred to moon goddesses. Followers hold a festival of music, storytelling, and swordplay where young drow who have completed their first year of freedom receive their first blessed bastard sword. The observance features an all-night festival with bardic competitions, sword-dancing demonstrations, coming-of-age ceremonies for redeemed drow, gift exchanges, a massive communal feast, and a dawn greeting where worshipers watch the sunrise together as a symbol of hope.

## Clergy & Temples

Eilistraee's clergy are called **Silverhairs** (regardless of actual hair color)—those who have been touched by moonlight and chosen to dance in Eilistraee's name. The hierarchy is loose and based on personal deeds rather than formal rank. The highest rank is **Moondancer**, typically a twelfth-level or higher cleric, bard, or ranger who has led dozens of drow to freedom and performed legendary acts of redemption. Currently Moondancer Tala Moonwhisper, a drow woman who escaped the Underdark, freed 200 enslaved surface elves, and established three hidden moonlit groves, serves as a fourteenth-level cleric in this role.

**Swordsingers** serve as senior clergy at ninth through thirteenth level who combine martial prowess with divine magic, wielding bastard swords (Eilistraee's favored weapon) and protecting surface drow communities from Underdark assassins as both warriors and priests, equally capable of leading a battle hymn or a moonlight dance. **Nightingales** operate as mid-level clergy at sixth through ninth level who travel the world searching for drow who wish to escape the Underdark, singing Eilistraee's songs in dark places, leaving signs for those seeking freedom, and guiding refugees to safety. Many are bards or rangers. Full clergy members at third through sixth level maintain moonlit groves, teach redeemed drow how to live in surface societies, and perform rituals under the full moon as teachers, counselors, and guardians. **Moonblessed** represent novices at first through third level who are newly redeemed drow or surface elves who have taken vows to serve Eilistraee, learning to live in the light literally and metaphorically while often struggling with trauma from their past lives. Notably, Eilistraee has no formal titles like High Priestess or Archcleric, as she believes hierarchy breeds tyranny, having witnessed the cruel theocracies of the Underdark, so her clergy self-organize based on need and ability.

Daily practice centers on the **Moonlit Dance**, where every full moon followers gather in groves to dance from moonrise to moonset in joyful, athletic, and sacred movement. Participants dance barefoot, wear minimal clothing, and lose themselves in movement that constitutes Eilistraee's primary form of worship. **Songweaving** permeates daily life as followers sing throughout the day, from work songs and traveling songs to lullabies, with music being sacred to Eilistraee such that even humming while cooking is considered prayer. Sword practice is mandatory since Eilistraee knows her followers will be hunted by Underdark assassins and rival drow houses, requiring every Silverhair to be able to defend themselves and others, with practice always done under open sky when possible. Followers are encouraged to perform small, anonymous acts of kindness such as leaving food for the hungry, healing the sick without asking payment, and protecting travelers, reflecting Eilistraee's belief that kindness itself is revolutionary.

Eilistraee has no grand cathedrals or stone temples. Her sacred sites are always outdoors, under open sky, where moonlight can reach. **Moonlit Groves** are hidden forest clearings where silver moonflowers grow and the canopy opens to the sky. These groves are used for dances, rituals, and sanctuary for fleeing drow. They are warded against scrying and protected by guardian animals such as wolves, owls, and silver stags. Locations are secret—passed by word of mouth only. The **Glade of First Light** marks where the original drow refugees first danced on Mazariim, though its location remains classified. **Silverleaf Grove** near Rivoleen's high elven cities provides a cautiously tolerated site where surface elves and drow worship together. The **Singing Stones** at an Underdark entrance offers a grove that extends underground, allowing drow to worship without yet venturing to the surface.

**Moonlit Shrines** are small roadside shrines—often just a bastard sword thrust into the ground with moonflowers growing around it. These mark safe havens for fleeing drow and serve as meeting points for Nightingales. In Elysium, within Minerva's planar domain, Eilistraee has claimed a region called the **Dances Under Stars**—an eternal twilight forest where the moon is always full, the air smells of night-blooming jasmine, and drow petitioners can dance forever without fear.

## Dogma

No soul is beyond redemption. Even in darkness, there is beauty. Even in despair, there is hope. Eilistraee teaches that every person—no matter their birth, past actions, or circumstances—deserves a chance to choose goodness. This is especially true for drow, who are born into tyrannical theocracies that demand evil.

The Five Moonlit Truths form the core of Eilistraee's philosophy. The first truth holds that **Freedom is Sacred**, declaring that no person should be enslaved physically, mentally, or spiritually, that cruel hierarchies are abominations, and that every drow deserves the freedom to choose their own path. The second truth teaches that **Beauty Heals**, recognizing that art, music, dance, and natural beauty are not frivolous but rather heal trauma, restore hope, and remind us why life is worth living, making beauty itself a form of resistance against tyranny. The third truth proclaims that **Kindness is Strength**, understanding that compassion and mercy are not weaknesses but rather the hardest virtues to practice and the most powerful weapons against hatred, with a single kind act capable of saving a soul.

The fourth truth affirms that the **Night is Not Evil**, celebrating darkness, moonlight, and the night as beautiful and sacred, and clarifying that the fact that drow have dark skin and live in darkness does not make them evil, since evil is a choice, not a skin color or culture. The fifth truth encourages followers to **Dance Like No One is Watching**, holding joy, spontaneity, and freedom of expression as sacred while recognizing that rigid hierarchy, strict rules, and fear of judgment are tools of tyrants. Eilistraee wants her followers to live fully, love freely, and dance without shame.

## Mythology

Eilistraee's story begins not on Mazariim but in the distant universe of Abier-Toril. In that faraway cosmos, she witnessed the fall of her people into darkness and made a choice that would define her existence—she followed them into exile, not to rule them, but to offer them hope of redemption.

For millennia, Eilistraee was a voice crying in the darkness. She sang to drow who had never known kindness, built hidden groves where they could dance under stolen moonlight, and taught that cruelty was not the only path. Her followers were always few, always hunted, always persecuted by overwhelming dark powers. Yet Eilistraee persisted, saving souls one at a time, believing that even one drow freed from tyranny justified an eternity of effort.

The Spellplague that ravaged Faerun brought catastrophe to Eilistraee's work. Her brother Vhaeraun, god of drow male rebellion and thieves, launched a desperate assassination attempt, believing that by absorbing her divine essence he could become powerful enough to challenge the forces of evil directly. The battle between siblings tore at the fabric of reality. Both were believed destroyed. Dark priestesses celebrated what they thought was the final death of the goddess who dared offer drow another way. The few surface drow who had followed Eilistraee mourned, believing their last hope had been extinguished.

But divine essence is not so easily destroyed. A fragment of Eilistraee's consciousness, along with several hundred of her most faithful drow followers, was ripped from Faerun and cast into the void between universes. They drifted through timeless chaos until they emerged on Mazariim in 1377 BW, confused, weakened, but alive.

Minerva, the ancient goddess of knowledge who dwells in Elysium, sensed their arrival and investigated. What she found moved her—a battered goddess and her traumatized followers, refugees from a cosmic catastrophe, still singing songs of hope despite having lost everything. Minerva recognized in Eilistraee a kindred spirit, another deity who valued enlightenment over conquest and offered wisdom rather than demanding worship. She welcomed Eilistraee to share Elysium, giving the Dark Maiden space to heal and her followers sanctuary to rebuild their shattered faith.

The drow who arrived with Eilistraee became the first surface drow of Mazariim. They were few—perhaps only a few hundred souls—but they carried within them the knowledge that drow could choose goodness, that tyranny was not inevitable, that redemption was possible. They established the first moonlit groves in hidden clearings, built shrines under open sky, and began the slow, painful work of proving to a hostile world that dark elves could be trusted.

The early years were brutal. Mazariim's drow dwelt in deep Underdark cities, worshiping demon lords and cruel powers, and they viewed Eilistraee's followers as traitors deserving death. Surface elves remembered millennia of drow raids and slavery, and were unwilling to believe that any dark elf could be trustworthy. Eilistraee's clergy were hunted by assassins from below and met with suspicion and violence from surface dwellers above. Many of the original refugees died in those early years, but those who survived became the foundation of a movement.

Slowly, painfully, surface drow communities took root. A moonlit grove here, a small temple there, a handful of redeemed drow living quietly among surface folk. Each success was hard-won. Each drow who escaped the Underdark represented a victory against impossible odds. Each act of kindness performed by Eilistraee's followers chipped away at centuries of prejudice.

Rivoleen, goddess of elven culture and magic, initially viewed Eilistraee with deep suspicion. How could drow, raised in cruelty, truly change? But over centuries, evidence mounted. Surface drow died protecting elven communities from raiders. Drow healers tended wounded soldiers without asking their race. Drow bards sang songs that moved even the most prejudiced hearts. Rivoleen never embraced Eilistraee—the wounds between surface elves and drow ran too deep—but an uneasy truce emerged. Rivoleen would not interfere with Eilistraee's redemption work, and Eilistraee would not challenge Rivoleen's authority over high elven culture.

Minerva remains Eilistraee's closest ally, a friendship born from mutual respect and shared values. Both goddesses believe that knowledge and enlightenment matter more than power, that every soul deserves the chance to learn and grow, that rigid hierarchies breed tyranny. Eilistraee's followers often study in Minerva's libraries, and Minerva's scholars occasionally attend moonlit dances, finding in the celebration of beauty a complement to their pursuit of wisdom.

The demon cults and cruel powers of the Underdark have made destroying Eilistraee's work a priority. They cannot tolerate the existence of redeemed drow—their very presence proves that tyranny is not inevitable, that drow can choose another path. Assassins hunt surface drow with fanatic dedication. Dark priestesses offer enormous rewards for information leading to moonlit groves. Entire drow houses dedicate themselves to rooting out and destroying any drow who shows mercy or hesitation, lest hope spread. The war between light and darkness is fought in shadows, with individual souls as the prize—every drow saved is a victory for the Dark Maiden, every surface drow killed a triumph for the cruel powers below.

By the modern era of 811 AW, Eilistraee's faith remains small but fervent. Surface drow number perhaps five to ten thousand across all of Mazariim, a tiny fraction compared to the countless demon-worshipers dwelling in Underdark cities. Yet their cultural impact far exceeds their numbers. They have proven that drow can be good, that redemption is real, that even those born into darkness can choose the light. The **Moonlit Refuge Network**, a secret system of safe houses and sympathetic guides, helps dozens of drow escape the Underdark each year. The **Singing Blades**, an elite order of drow paladins and rangers, hunt demon cultist assassins before they can strike at moon drow communities. Surface drow increasingly serve as diplomats, adventurers, and scholars, slowly earning the trust their ancestors spent millennia destroying.

Eilistraee knows the work is far from over. The cruel powers of the deep are ancient and patient, with eternity to crush hope, while the Dark Maiden has only her followers' finite lives to build it. But Eilistraee also knows something the darkness does not understand—that hope, once kindled, is almost impossible to extinguish. Every drow who sees moonlight for the first time, every act of kindness that breaks through hatred's armor, every moonlit dance that celebrates freedom—these are seeds that will grow, slowly but inevitably, until even the deepest darkness must give way to dawn.
