# **Xorancon** (ZOR-an-kon)

**Power Level**: Greater Entity (Far Realm Native)  
**Portfolio**: Aberrations, beholders, dreams of madness, alien tyranny  
**Title(s)**: The Eye Tyrant, The First Beholder, The Dream That Dreams  
**Dominion**: Far Realm (The Hive of Eyes)  
**Foci**: Is Descended From Aberrations, Bargains With Aberrations, Worships Tyranny  
**Worshipers**: Beholders, aberrant cultists, those seeking forbidden knowledge  
**Favored Race**: None (abhors all "normal" life)  
**Favored Weapon**: None (eye rays)  
**Alignment**: Chaotic Evil  
**Symbol**: Central eye surrounded by ten lesser eyes

-----

## Origin

**Year of Awakening:** ~17,000 BW  
**Era:** Late Timeless Era

Xorancon awakened in the late Timeless Era (~17,000 BW), but unlike other entities, did not form in the material universe. As the fabric of space-time rippled from the creation of multiple extra-dimensional planes, tears and weaknesses formed between realities. Xorancon's consciousness emerged from these cracks—a being of impossible geometry and anti-logic. The entity created the **Far Realm**, divided into unknown layers each slightly out of phase, where reality operates by rules incomprehensible to mortal minds. Xorancon manifests as a titanic beholder-like entity, though its true form defies description. The Far Realm exists partially outside normal space-time, making it simultaneously older and younger than the universe itself.

---

General Overview

Xorancon is alien intelligence made flesh—or rather, made eye-stalks. An entity from the Far Realm (dimension beyond sanity where physics is optional and geometry is malicious), Xorancon takes the form of a massive beholder—a floating sphere covered in eyes, each capable of reality-warping magic.

Xorancon did not ascend to godhood; Xorancon dreamed godhood into existence. In the Far Realm, thought and reality are the same. Xorancon dreamed of being a god, and thus became one. Worse, Xorancon's dreams spawn all beholders across the multiverse—each beholder is a fragment of Xorancon's nightmare given independent existence.

Every beholder believes itself supremely superior to all other beings (including other beholders). This narcissistic megalomania is inherited from Xorancon, who considers all non-aberrant life to be mistakes in reality that must be corrected. Beholders don't just hate other species—they consider their existence offensive.

Xorancon communicates through dreams that cause madness. Those who perceive its thoughts directly suffer psychic trauma—visions of impossible geometries, sensations of being watched by infinite eyes, the certainty that reality is wrong and must be destroyed. Cultists who worship Xorancon embrace this madness, believing insanity is enlightenment.

The entity resides in the Hive of Eyes—a region of the Far Realm that is simultaneously Xorancon's body, mind, and domain. Imagine a space where every direction you look contains an eye staring back. The eyes blink in patterns that rewrite local physics. Gravity is a suggestion. Color has taste. Sound has texture. Up votes for president.

Holy Days

The Dreaming occurs on new moons, when cultists gather for mass meditation to receive Xorancon's dream-visions. They sit in darkness, staring into polished spheres or gazing at painted eyes on chamber walls, allowing their minds to drift toward the Far Realm. Those who succeed report visions of infinite eyes watching from impossible angles, geometry that moves when observed, and the overwhelming sensation that reality itself is merely a thin membrane stretched over something vast and hungry. Many cultists emerge from these meditations with new eyes growing somewhere on their bodies, considered a blessing from the Eye Tyrant.

The Watching takes place during full moons, a ritual where cultists stare at each other for hours without blinking. This practice is believed to strengthen their connection to Xorancon's all-seeing nature. Participants arrange themselves in circles, each person's gaze locked onto another's eyes, maintaining absolute stillness. Those who blink are deemed weak and forced to observe from the outer circle. The ritual continues until dawn, and those who succeed without blinking often develop minor telepathic abilities or gain the unsettling ability to see in complete darkness.

The Transformation is celebrated whenever cultists undergo visible aberrant mutations. When an extra eye appears, when skin turns rubbery and translucent, when fingers fuse into tentacles—these changes are honored as improvements over flawed humanoid form. The transforming cultist is placed at the center of the shrine while others chant prayers in languages that hurt to pronounce. The ceremony concludes with the transformed individual receiving a new name in the clicking, humming tongue of aberrations.

The Eye Opens is observed whenever beholders attack civilized areas. Cultists praise these assaults as Xorancon's will manifesting in the Material Plane, viewing destruction wrought by eye rays as divine judgment against inferior life forms. Some cultists even travel to sites of beholder attacks to gather samples of disintegrated matter or petrified victims, keeping these as holy relics that prove the superiority of aberrant existence over mundane reality.

Clergy & Temples

The Eyes of the Void serve as Xorancon's clergy—cultists who worship aberrations and actively seek to become them. The hierarchy mirrors the anatomy of a beholder, with the Central Eye serving as high priest. This position is usually held by a transformed human-turned-aberration, though the current holder is unknown as they keep dying from self-inflicted improvement surgery. Cultists view these deaths not as failures but as transcendence, believing the deceased have merged completely with Xorancon's dreams.

Eye Stalks are senior cultists, typically psionics or wizards specializing in transmutation magic. They've undergone significant physical changes and can communicate telepathically with one another. These individuals lead rituals and guide newer members through their transformations. Lesser Eyes are full cultists who have undergone partial aberrant transformation—perhaps growing extra eyes, developing rubbery skin, or manifesting minor psionic abilities. They handle the day-to-day operations of the shrines and recruit new members.

Observers are junior cultists still studying forbidden lore, learning to read texts written in languages that cause nosebleeds and migraines. They practice meditation techniques designed to weaken the barrier between their minds and the Far Realm. The Watched occupy the lowest tier—those who have seen Xorancon in dreams and cannot forget, compelled to seek out the cult but not yet fully committed. Many never progress beyond this stage, driven mad by recurring visions before they can complete initiation.

Temples are hidden underground or in abandoned places far from civilization. These Shrines of the Watching are dark spaces covered in eye symbols—thousands of them. Cultists carve eyes into walls, floors, and ceilings. Some eyes are painted in phosphorescent pigments that glow faintly in darkness. Some are real, grafted from beholder victims or grown via aberrant magic, preserved in arcane solutions and set into alcoves where they continue to move and blink. The shrines smell of ozone and rotting fish, and the air vibrates with a frequency just below hearing that causes prolonged visitors to develop splitting headaches.

Long-term worshipers undergo involuntary mutations that progress over months and years. Extra eyes grow on their bodies—on palms, shoulders, the back of the neck. Skin becomes rubbery and slick to the touch, exuding a faintly luminescent mucus. Telepathic abilities manifest, allowing wordless communication but bringing with them intrusive thoughts from other aberrant minds. Limbs elongate and split, becoming tentacles with minds of their own. Eventually, the most devoted transform fully into aberrations—gibbering mouthers, otyughs, or rarely, weak beholders themselves. These transformed cultists are revered as having achieved perfection.

Dogma

Xorancon's core teaching states that reality is flawed, flesh is flawed, and only aberration is perfect. Become other. Become eye. Become Xorancon. This simple directive drives all cult activity, pushing followers toward physical and mental transformation regardless of the cost to their humanity.

Reality is diseased according to Xorancon's first truth. The Material Plane's physics—its predictable gravity, its linear time, its three dimensions—are errors in existence. The Far Realm represents correct reality, where thought shapes matter and geometry obeys desire rather than mathematics. Cultists work to thin the barriers between planes, creating weak points where Far Realm physics can leak through and corrupt the mundane world.

Humanoid form is weak, Xorancon teaches, because symmetry, bones, and predictable biology impose limitations on potential. Two eyes provide only binocular vision; ten eyes provide omnidirectional awareness. Two arms restrict what can be grasped; ten tentacles allow simultaneous manipulation of reality itself. Cultists reject their birth forms as evolutionary dead ends, embracing mutations as improvements over the baseline inadequacy of humanoid design.

Sanity is blindness, the third truth declares. What mortals call madness is actually the ability to perceive true reality beyond the comforting lies of conventional perception. Those who see impossible angles, who hear colors and taste sounds, who understand that three-dimensional space is merely a shadow of higher geometries—these individuals are enlightened, not insane. Cultists deliberately induce madness through meditation, hallucinogenic substances, and exposure to aberrant artifacts, believing each break from sanity brings them closer to Xorancon's perspective.

The Eye sees all, the final truth states. Xorancon watches everything through its infinite gaze. Privacy is an illusion; secrets are impossible. Every action, thought, and desire is observed and catalogued. Rather than finding this surveillance oppressive, cultists find it reassuring, believing that being seen by the Eye Tyrant validates their existence. To be unwatched would be to cease to matter.

Cultists revere beholders as Xorancon's children—perfect beings who embody alien superiority. When beholders attack civilizations, cultists assist them, viewing destruction as holy work. They scout targets, disable defenses, and spread misinformation to help beholders succeed. To serve a beholder is the highest honor a cultist can achieve, even if that service ends in disintegration by eye ray.

Mythology

Xorancon has existed since before time had meaning, dwelling in the Far Realm where causality is negotiable and history writes itself backward as often as forward. The entity did not originate on Mazariim or in any part of the standard multiverse. It emerged from the spaces between realities, from the conceptual void where unthinkable thoughts accumulate mass and consciousness.

In the Far Realm, Xorancon dreamed. The dream was simple: I am god. I am supreme. I am eye, and eye sees all, and what eye sees becomes real. The dream gained substance. The substance gained power. The power gained self-awareness. Xorancon became what it dreamed, achieving godhood through sheer narcissistic insistence that it deserved divinity.

But the dream did not stop. Xorancon continued dreaming, and each dream spawned a beholder—a fragment of the Eye Tyrant's megalomania given independent form. These beholder-spawn inherited their creator's conviction of absolute superiority, each believing itself to be the pinnacle of existence and all other beings to be mistakes. Beholders spread across the multiverse, establishing lairs wherever they could dominate lesser creatures, and each one reinforced Xorancon's influence simply by existing.

Beholders are apex predators—intelligent, paranoid, and armed with antimagic and devastating eye rays. They establish lairs in deep caverns, abandoned ruins, and underground complexes, dominating everything within reach. They view all other beings as inferior at best and offensive at worst. Xorancon does not directly command them; most beholders are too narcissistic to consciously serve anyone. But every beholder's existence glorifies the entity, spreading the philosophy that alien superiority trumps mundane life.

Xorancon's servants include all manner of aberrations. Gibbering mouthers, otyughs, ropers, neogi, and occasionally mind flayers are drawn to the entity's influence like moths to flame. These creatures do not worship in any traditional sense—they simply exist in states of reality-rejection that resonate with Xorancon's nature. Transformed cultists who have mutated into aberrations retain intelligence but lose empathy, morality, and sanity, becoming living extensions of the Eye Tyrant's will.

A unique servant known as the Gaze of Xorancon appears during major rituals—a massive floating eye with no body, just a single enormous eye perhaps ten feet in diameter. It hovers above cultist gatherings, its pupil dilating and contracting as it focuses on participants. Those who meet its gaze receive madness-inducing visions of the Far Realm, sometimes driving them permanently insane but occasionally granting them fragmentary psionic powers or prophetic abilities.

Xorancon's influence manifests wherever beholders nest. Reality grows thin around beholder lairs, causing minor aberrations to spawn spontaneously and physics to behave unpredictably. Aberrant cults form in cities and towns, secret societies worshiping alien entities and working to undermine the stability of normal existence. Mad prophets emerge from these cults, individuals who have glimpsed the Far Realm and cannot unsee it, compelled to spread visions of tentacles and eyes and geometries that should not exist.

Mutation outbreaks occur in areas where the barrier between planes has weakened. People develop extra limbs, eyes in strange places, or skin that changes texture when observed. Most of these mutations kill their hosts within days, but some stabilize, creating communities of the changed who struggle to exist in a world that fears and rejects them. Forbidden knowledge circulates among scholars desperate enough to risk sanity—texts describing aberrant biology, Far Realm physics, and the mathematical formulae that allow travel between dimensions.

The connection between Xorancon and other entities is minimal. Og'thallith also spreads madness, but Og'thallith channels demonic chaos from the Abyss while Xorancon represents the structured wrongness of the Far Realm. They are competitors rather than allies, each trying to corrupt reality in fundamentally incompatible ways. Ry'le is another Far Realm entity, younger than Xorancon and focused on infiltration through shapechanging. The two ignore each other; Xorancon views Ry'le's subtlety as weakness, while Ry'le considers Xorancon's obviousness tactically foolish.

Kazrokath, the demon lord of change and mutation, shares some philosophical overlap with Xorancon. Both embrace transformation and reject static existence. But Kazrokath represents chaotic evolution and adaptation, while Xorancon embodies alien wrongness that rejects evolution as too slow and predictable. They neither cooperate nor oppose each other, existing in parallel tracks of reality-corruption.

The Eye opens. Reality screams. This is improvement. So say the Eyes of the Void, and they mean it. Every mutation, every maddened prophet, every beholder lair represents progress toward Xorancon's vision of a perfected multiverse where aberration is normal and normalcy is the aberration. The Eye Tyrant dreams on, and the dream continues spawning nightmares that believe themselves superior to all creation.
