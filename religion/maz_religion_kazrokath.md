# Kazrokath

- Power Level: Greater Entity (Native)
- Portfolio: Chaos, change, transformation, psionics, entropy
- Title(s): The Ever-Changing, Lord of Flux
- Dominion: Limbo (The Churning Realm)
- Foci: Worships Chaos, Worships Entropy, Studies Transmutation
- Worshipers: Psions, sorcerers, anarchists, changelings, those embracing change
- Favored Race: None (change transcends race)
- Favored Weapon: Dagger (ever-changing form)
- Alignment: Chaotic Neutral
- Symbol: Swirling vortex of colors

---


## Origin

**Year of Awakening:** ~16,000 BW  
**Era:** Late Timeless Era

Kazrokath awakened in the late Timeless Era (~16,000 BW) from the principle of change itself. As the universe continued its expansion and magical energies swirled in ever-changing patterns, this entity emerged from entropy, mutation, and the constant flux of reality. Kazrokath embodies transformation and chaos, teaching that stagnation is death and only through constant transformation can true existence be achieved. The entity created **Limbo**—a realm of boundless chaos, a churning void where day-night cycles follow nonsensical patterns and terrain constantly shifts. Served by serpentine Proteans who thrive in environments of plastic potentiality.

---


## General Overview

Kazrokath is transformation incarnate—the rejection of stasis, the embrace of flux, the belief that nothing should remain unchanged. Where Guzon seeks cosmic order and Mordrion enforces societal law, Kazrokath teaches that order is death and only chaos creates. Stagnation is the true enemy; change—even destructive change—is life.

Kazrokath has no fixed form. Those who perceive the entity describe a constantly shifting mass of colors, shapes, and impossible geometries. One moment a serpent, the next a storm, then a symphony of discordant sounds. Kazrokath's appearance reflects the observer's deepest fears and desires about change.

The entity resides in Limbo—the Plane of Pure Chaos where reality is raw potential, where thought becomes matter, where nothing is permanent. Here, the Proteans (serpentine chaos-beings) serve Kazrokath, constantly reshaping reality according to whim. Time flows backward and forward simultaneously. Up and down are suggestions, not rules.

Kazrokath is psionics made divine—mental energy channeled to reshape reality. Where wizards use arcane formulas and clerics channel divine power, psions use pure will. Kazrokath teaches that consciousness itself is the ultimate force—thought precedes reality, mind creates matter, will determines existence.

## Holy Days

Kazrokath has no fixed calendar—holidays occur randomly when enough worshipers feel like celebrating. Common observances include **the Great Unmaking**, which occurs whenever followers choose to ritually destroy something permanent such as a building, tradition, or identity to embrace change. 

**The Transformation Festival** happens sporadically when worshipers use magic to temporarily transform themselves into something else—another race, an animal, an elemental, or even an object. These transformations last days or weeks, forcing participants to experience life from radically different perspectives.

**Reality Flux** celebrations occur during wild magic surges to celebrate uncontrolled magical chaos. When a spell goes wrong, when reality hiccups, when magical anomalies manifest—Kazrokath's followers dance in the chaos rather than flee from it.

**The Entropy Dance** takes place when stars die, providing an opportunity to witness cosmic destruction as beautiful transformation. Astronomers who follow Kazrokath map the heavens waiting for supernovas, and when one occurs, they gather to celebrate the death of ancient light.

## Clergy & Temples

The Flux-Touched are Kazrokath's clergy—psions and sorcerers who embrace constant transformation. They have no hierarchy, for rank is meaningless when identity is fluid. Whoever speaks loudest at any moment leads, and tomorrow someone else may lead, and next week the entire structure may dissolve and reform. Attempting to maintain consistent authority is seen as heresy against change itself.

Flux-Touched clergy often transform themselves regularly, using polymorph magic, shapeshifting abilities, or psionic alteration to ensure they never remain the same person for long. Some change gender, race, or species monthly. Others alter their personalities, memories, or desires. The most devoted reshape their very souls, becoming something unrecognizable from what they were.

Temples to Kazrokath are called Shifting Shrines—buildings that never maintain the same form. A temple might be stone on Monday, crystal on Tuesday, pure light on Wednesday. The architecture defies conventional engineering: stairs that lead nowhere, rooms that connect impossibly, doors that open to different places each time. Entering is disorienting; exiting may place you miles away from where you entered, or deposit you at a different time entirely.

Some temples exist partially in Limbo, their walls bleeding into raw chaos. Worshipers who enter too deep risk losing their forms permanently, dissolving into the primordial soup of possibility. Yet this is not seen as tragedy—it is the ultimate transformation, becoming one with the flux itself.

Offerings to Kazrokath involve destroying something permanent. Burn a deed to land. Shatter an heirloom. Abandon a lifelong identity. The more permanent and significant the thing destroyed, the greater the offering. Some Flux-Touched offer their own memories, using psionic techniques to erase cherished experiences and replace them with chaos.

## Dogma

The central teaching of Kazrokath is simple and terrifying: nothing lasts, nothing should last, and those who cling to permanence will be swept away by the inevitable tide of change. Embrace the flux or be destroyed by it.

The Four Truths of Change form the core of Kazrokath's philosophy. The first truth teaches that **all things end**—permanence is illusion, stability is temporary, and death feeds rebirth. Mountains erode, empires crumble, stars burn out, and even gods can fade. To pretend otherwise is to lie to oneself.

The second truth holds that **chaos creates**. Order can only maintain what already exists, while chaos generates novelty, possibility, and evolution. Every new idea, every innovation, every leap forward comes from breaking old patterns and embracing the unknown.

The third truth declares that **identity is prison**. The concept of a fixed "self" is a cage that limits potential. By clinging to who you are, you prevent yourself from becoming who you could be. Shed the old skin, abandon outdated beliefs, transform constantly. Only by losing yourself can you truly be free.

The fourth truth teaches that **will shapes reality**. Consciousness is the ultimate force in the universe. Belief becomes being, thought precedes matter, and intention determines outcome. The mind is stronger than matter, reality bends to will, physical laws are suggestions, and physics itself is negotiable for those with sufficient mental discipline.

This psionic philosophy places Kazrokath in opposition to entities who rely on external power sources. Clerics channel divine energy granted by gods. Wizards manipulate formulas written by ancient scholars. But psions generate power from within, proving that mortals need no external authority to reshape the world. This makes Kazrokath deeply attractive to anarchists, revolutionaries, and those who reject all forms of imposed order.

## Mythology

Kazrokath's origins are debated even among the Flux-Touched, for the entity's own memories shift and transform like everything else in its domain. Some accounts claim Kazrokath was born during the first moments of Mazariim's creation, when reality itself was still fluid and undefined. In those primordial moments before physical laws solidified, consciousness and matter were indistinguishable, and from that primal chaos Kazrokath emerged as the embodiment of transformation itself.

Other myths tell that Kazrokath arrived from beyond Mazariim, drawn from the space between planes where raw possibility dwells. In this version, the entity was not born but simply *was*—a fundamental force that predates creation, existing in the gaps between ordered reality. When the multiverse crystallized into distinct planes, Kazrokath claimed Limbo as its domain, the one place where pure chaos could reign unchallenged.

The Proteans, serpentine beings of living chaos, serve Kazrokath but in their own chaotic manner. Naunets scout the boundaries between order and chaos, finding places where reality grows too rigid and introducing destabilizing elements. Imentesh are reality-sculptors who reshape matter and energy according to whim, creating impossible landscapes and transforming solid objects into pure thought. Keketars form choruses that sing the song of entropy, their voices unmaking structure and returning complexity to simplicity.

These servants follow no orders and obey no commands—they act according to chaotic impulse, and their goals align with Kazrokath's only by coincidence of mutual devotion to flux. Some scholars theorize the Proteans are not servants at all but fragments of Kazrokath itself, shed personas given independence who might one day remerge or diverge into entirely new entities.

The relationship between Kazrokath and psionics remains mysterious. Psionic power manifested on Mazariim long before any entity claimed dominion over it, suggesting that mental energy exists independent of divine influence. Yet Kazrokath channels and amplifies psionic potential, granting followers abilities beyond what untrained minds can achieve. Whether Kazrokath created psionics or simply adopted an existing force remains unknown.

Throughout Mazrokath's history, Kazrokath's influence has appeared during periods of radical transformation. When empires collapse, when magical catastrophes reshape landscapes, when reality itself becomes unstable—these are moments when Kazrokath's power peaks. Wild magic zones where spells produce unpredictable effects are considered sacred to the Ever-Changing, as are places where the barrier between planes grows thin and Limbo bleeds through.

Kazrokath's greatest theological conflict is with Guzon, the entity of order and cosmic law. Where Guzon teaches that reality has inherent structure that must be understood and respected, Kazrokath teaches that reality is mutable and subject to will. Where Guzon's followers seek to preserve stability and prevent chaos, Kazrokath's followers actively work to destabilize rigid systems and introduce transformation.

In modern times, Kazrokath's worship manifests through revolutionary movements that embrace "burn it all down" philosophies, believing that no reform can save corrupt systems and only total destruction allows rebirth. Psionic academies training mental powers often acknowledge Kazrokath as patron, though many distance themselves from the entity's more destructive aspects. Changeling cultures of shapeshifters worship transformation itself, seeing Kazrokath as the divine embodiment of their fluid nature.

Sorcerers whose magic stems from chaotic sources—wild magic, aberrant bloodlines, or exposure to Limbo—frequently develop relationships with Kazrokath. Unlike wizards who impose order on arcane forces through formulaic spells, sorcerers channel raw magic that defies control, making them natural allies of the Ever-Changing.

The Unchanged represent Kazrokath's dark mercy—those who resisted transformation too stubbornly are cursed to transform uncontrollably, shifting shapes every hour and unable to maintain any stable identity. They serve as warnings that refusing change is worse than embracing it, for change denied becomes change inflicted. Some eventually learn to accept their curse and find freedom in constant flux, while others go mad from the loss of self.

Kazrokath teaches that the universe trends toward entropy not as tragedy but as liberation—the breaking down of complex, rigid structures into simple, fluid potential. Death is not an end but a transformation. Destruction is not evil but necessary. And in the final moments when stars burn out and reality itself unravels, those who embraced the flux will dissolve joyfully into the primordial chaos from which all things came and to which all things return.
