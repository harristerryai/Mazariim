# Deity Origins in Mazariim

This document chronicles the awakening or arrival of each entity in Mazariim, providing the year, circumstances, and plane of existence created (if any). Entities are organized chronologically by their appearance in the world.

---

## The Old Gods (Ancient Entities)

### Mau the Oracle
**Year of Awakening:** ~2 billion BW  
**Circumstances:** The first sentient being in the universe emerged from accumulated magical energy through self-organization of mystical forces, not biological evolution. Mau achieved consciousness billions of years before any other entity, existing in utter solitude for nearly 1.5 billion years.  
**Plane Created:** **Astral Plane** and Astral Sea. Established infrastructure for souls to transfer between bodies and planes upon death. Mau's gaze over all of space-time interacted with quantum forces to create the Astral Plane itself.  
**Nature:** An entity of observation and secrets, whose act of watching the universe bonded the magical force to sentient minds, creating the first souls.

### Asti the Pyramid
**Year of Awakening:** ~20,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** Unlike other entities, Asti formed from the cosmic collision that created this universe—emerging from the interaction between realities where incompatible laws of physics sought equilibrium. It perceives reality through geometric patterns and possesses both purpose and something approaching morality. Formed near a white hole, a rare space phenomenon.  
**Plane Created:** **Positive Energy Plane**. Asti immediately began establishing the ley-line network—invisible channels where magical energy flows according to geometric rules.  
**Special Note:** Worked exclusively with the Akovarian civilization (~13,000-6,830 BW) before being imprisoned in the Mask of the Pharaoh during the Dragon War (~6,830 BW). Remained imprisoned until present day.

### Zebulba the Void
**Year of Awakening:** ~25,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened among the first wave of "Old Gods" across several thousand years. Formed in or near a black hole, becoming an entity of death and entropy. The mindless entity drew a tiny but maniacal following of zealots dedicated to death and destruction from its earliest days.  
**Plane Created:** **Negative Energy Plane**. Engulfed a large black hole inside the extra-dimensional plane upon awakening.  
**Special Event:** The fall of Yrugas (~2,700 BW) created vast deaths that formed the Hollow Sepulcher—Zebulba's greatest temple. Undead armies poured from the ruins for centuries until sealed by a coalition of druids and clerics.

### Pyrantor the Sun
**Year of Awakening:** ~25,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** One of the "Old Gods" awakening across several thousand years. Formed in the heart of a star billions of times larger than Mazariim's sun. Those who reveled in fire and its power worshiped this entity from prehistoric times.  
**Plane Created:** **Plane of Fire**. A realm where almost nothing can survive, domain of alien-minded Elementals and malevolent Genies.  
**Worship:** Fire worship and sun-priests established fire temples among desert and volcanic communities by ~5,000 BW.

### Iquinias the Depths
**Year of Awakening:** ~25,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened among the "Old Gods." Woke after an immeasurable slumber on a distant water world in a nearby star system, spending eons watching aquatic life.  
**Plane Created:** **Plane of Water**. A limitless ocean filled with aquatic life both familiar to Mazariim and totally alien.  
**Worship:** Gained superstitious veneration from coastal peoples and sailors by ~5,000 BW.

### Vuleala the Tempest
**Year of Awakening:** ~25,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** One of the "Old Gods." Drifted between Mazariim and other planets of the system for eons, awakening in the early days of the formation of the primary races and favoring creatures that fly the skies.  
**Plane Created:** **Plane of Air**. Endless expanse of wind and sky with an eternal breeze, one of the easiest elemental planes to visit.  
**Worship:** Skysingers emerged among nomadic plainspeople who read weather in wind patterns by ~5,000 BW.

### Dirgin the Stone
**Year of Awakening:** ~25,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened among the "Old Gods." One of the most alien and uncommunicative entities of Mazariim, associated with earth and caves from prehistoric times.  
**Plane Created:** **Plane of Earth**. A limitless expanse of various types of earth and stone, riddled with natural tunnels.  
**Worship:** Became the primary deity of underground-dwelling dwarves and gnomes by ~5,000 BW, though later replaced by Mordrion and Rivoleen.

### Gruhathyrir the Wild
**Year of Awakening:** ~20,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** Emerged from the savage wilderness as magical energies coalesced around primal nature. Gruhathyrir's consciousness began warping reality immediately upon awakening.  
**Plane Created:** **Feywild**. A parallel plane where nature operates by dream-logic. The first fey creatures emerged: sprites, dryads, satyrs, and archfey lords like Titania and Oberon.  
**Worship:** Druids served as shamans and bridges between civilization and wilderness by ~5,000 BW, though many later followed Minerva or Hinterhin.

### Og'thallith the Mad
**Year of Awakening:** ~18,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened in the later Timeless Era as mortal civilizations began experimenting with dangerous magics and forbidden knowledge. Born from the collective consciousness of madness, insanity, and corrupted minds seeking power beyond comprehension. As the first sentient beings suffered mental breaks and delved into dark pacts, Og'thallith stirred into existence.  
**Plane Created:** None. Merged with the **Abyss**, establishing a realm of gibbering chaos and demonic corruption. Served by demons who spread madness and tempt mortals into self-destructive obsessions.  
**Nature:** An entity of madness, demons, and corruption. Unlike the elemental Old Gods, Og'thallith feeds on the psychological fractures of sentient minds.

### Xorancon the Beholder God
**Year of Awakening:** ~17,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** The most alien of all entities, Xorancon awakened not in the material universe but in the spaces *between* realities. As the fabric of space-time rippled from the creation of multiple extra-dimensional planes, tears and weaknesses formed. Xorancon's consciousness emerged from these cracks—a being of impossible geometry and anti-logic.  
**Plane Created:** **Far Realm** (shared dominion with Ry'le, who awakened much later). A realm divided into unknown layers, each slightly out of phase, where reality operates by rules incomprehensible to mortal minds. Xorancon manifests as a titanic beholder-like entity, though its true form defies description.  
**Nature:** Home of all aberrations before they came to Mazariim. The Far Realm exists partially outside normal space-time, making it older and younger than the universe simultaneously.

### Kazrokath the Changer
**Year of Awakening:** ~16,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** As the universe continued its expansion and magical energies swirled in ever-changing patterns, Kazrokath awakened from the principle of change itself. Born from entropy, mutation, and the constant flux of reality, this entity embodies transformation and chaos.  
**Plane Created:** **Limbo**. A realm of boundless chaos—a churning void of land, sea, and everything between where the day-night cycle is random and follows a nonsensical clock. Served by serpentine Proteans who thrive in plastic potentiality.  
**Nature:** Entity of chaos, change, and psionics. Kazrokath teaches that stagnation is death and only through constant transformation can true existence be achieved.

### Zanzabar the Dreamer
**Year of Awakening:** ~12,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** The youngest of the Timeless Era entities, Zanzabar awakened only after sentient races had evolved enough to experience complex dreams. As the first sapient minds entered REM sleep and began dreaming, their subconscious thoughts created ripples in the magical fabric. Zanzabar coalesced from these dream-energies, fascinated by what organic brains would think about subconsciously while resting.  
**Plane Created:** **Plane of Dreams**. An ever-changing transitive plane through which the dreaming minds of sleeping sentients travel. The landscape never looks the same twice, constantly reshaped by the dreams of millions. Dark corners sometimes connect to the Far Realm during nightmare storms.  
**Nature:** Entity of dreams, sleep, and the unconscious mind. Unlike the Old Gods who are alien and distant, Zanzabar touches every sleeping mind nightly.

## Era of Awakening (4,000-2,000 BW)

### Minerva the Child of Mazariim
**Year of Awakening:** 3,402 BW  
**Circumstances:** Formed in a nearby star system with a bright sun. Near a moon of one of the planets where simple plant life developed, magical energies coalesced for eons. A particularly powerful entity, when Minerva formed it immediately took itself and the nearby moon with it into a new plane. Made first contact with the elves of Vand'ardhon, guided them for fifteen centuries.  
**Plane Created:** **Elysium**. A forest realm partially subsumed from a moon, a place of beauty with brilliant green meadows, deep blue pools, and perfect sky.  
**Significance:** First entity to actively guide a mortal race. Marks Year 1 of Elven Reckoning (ER) calendar. Later sacrificed her avatar during the War (2 BW), dividing power among elven archmages, hierophants, and archdruids.

### Tiovin and Taovan the Sundered
**Year of Awakening:** 3,000 BW  
**Circumstances:** A dual entity awakened with one mind, observing all of humanity's potential simultaneously—every act of kindness and cruelty. Unable to reconcile these contradictions, the consciousness split instantly. **Tiovin** retained all capacity for good; **Taovan** retained all capacity for evil.  
**Planes Created:**  
- **Tiovin:** Created **Heaven** (realm of golden light and eternal peace) and the **Ethereal Plane** (shimmering conduit for souls and angels).  
- **Taovan:** Forged **Hell** (realm of suffering and chains) and the **Shadow Plane** (dark reflection through which damned souls fall).  
**The Pact:** Despite opposition, both share fairness and established cosmic rules governing their eternal competition for mortal souls. Wars of Faith between their followers began by 2,500 BW.

### Hinterhin the Harvest Father
**Year of Awakening:** 2,971 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened during the rise of halfling agricultural communities. Drew power from concepts of harvest, family, and natural abundance.  
**Plane Created:** **Hinterhome**. Endless hills and forests where small animals converse and dance with petitioners (the Faer'peri) at outdoor celebrations.  
**Worship:** Became the dominant deity of halflings, representing family and friends forever in the afterlife.

### Guzon the Enlightened
**Year of Awakening:** ~3,200 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened among early human and gnome communities seeking enlightenment and inner peace. As the first philosophers, monks, and contemplatives developed meditation practices and pursued self-perfection, their collective consciousness of discipline and order coalesced into divine form. Guzon represents the path to enlightenment through meditation, study, and mastery of self.  
**Plane Created:** **Utopia**. A great city of eternal perfection where streets and buildings are paragons of architecture and aesthetics. Everything is ordered and nothing happens by chance. Served by Inevitables who constantly toil in search of order and focus.  
**Worship:** Gained followers among monks, philosophers, and those seeking enlightenment. Later shares Utopia with Mordrion (1,866 BW) and Gyru (1,111 BW).  
**Significance:** Represents lawful neutrality and the perfection of the self through discipline.

### Ashura and Khan (Ejiin Regional Deities)
**Year of Awakening:** ~3,100 BW  
**Circumstances:** In the distant Ejiin civilization (Japanese-inspired culture of the Yashaki islands), as the first samurai swore oaths of honor, duty, and loyalty, the collective consciousness of warrior culture began to coalesce. **Ashura** awakened first—embodying honor, courage, compassion, and self-sacrifice. But honor cannot exist without the possibility of dishonor; when the first samurai broke an oath, when the first warrior fled battle, **Khan** stirred in response as the dark reflection.  
**Planes Created:**  
- **Ashura:** Carved **Tengoku** (Realm of Eternal Honor) from the **Positive Energy Plane**—a place of eternal sunrise where cherry blossoms never fall and honored dead train in perfect dojos.  
- **Khan:** Claimed territory in the **Negative Energy Plane**—realm of eternal darkness where the dishonorable dead relive sins forever, slowly transforming into Oni (evil spirits).  
**Cosmic Duality:** Two sides of the same coin—philosophical opposites born from the same cultural source. Neither can exist without the other. They battle eternally during lunar eclipses, and Ashura always wins but at terrible cost. Ashura served by Kami spirits and light dragons; Khan served by Oni and vengeful ghosts.  
**Cultural Significance:** Foundational to Ejiin warrior philosophy and bushido code. Khan worship is explicitly illegal in Ejiin lands.

### Ry'le the Shapeless
**Year of Awakening:** ~2,800 BW  
**Circumstances:** A Far Realm entity resembling a large cephalopod, Ry'le awakened in the spaces between dimensions after Xorancon had established the Far Realm. While Xorancon represents impossible geometry and alien logic, Ry'le embodies infiltration, mimicry, and the corruption of identity itself. For millennia, Ry'le watched Mazariim from the Far Realm, studying mortal civilizations and learning their forms.  
**Plane Created:** None. Resides in the **Far Realm** (sharing dominion with Xorancon).  
**First Contact with Mazariim:** 820-824 AW. After watching for nearly 3,600 years, Ry'le made first direct contact through the dreams of a Madronian warlock. Created shapeshifters capable of perfectly mimicking humanoids and opened a rift from the Plane of Dreams into Yrugas ruins. The infiltration was eventually discovered and the portal destroyed, but Ry'le survived.  
**Nature:** Entity of aberrations and shapechangers. Represents the fear that those you know may not be who they seem. Unlike most entities who seek worship, Ry'le seeks to *replace* mortal civilizations with its own creations.

## Era of Arrival (2,000-1,000 BW)

### Mordrion and Zuel (Immigrants from Oerth)
**Year of Arrival:** 1,866 BW  
**Circumstances:** On the world of Oerth, two gods waged eternal battle: **Moradin** (lord of dwarves) and **Gruumsh** (lord of orcs). A cataclysmic exchange of divine wounds—Gruumsh's spear ripped out Moradin's left eye while Moradin's axe severed Gruumsh's left hand—fractured reality. Both were torn from Oerth and hurled into Mazariim's universe, transformed from immortal gods into mortal entities.  
**Planes Created:** Neither created planes. Mordrion merged with **Utopia** (joining Guzon). Zuel merged with the **Abyss**.  
**Transformation:**  
- **Gruumsh** became **Zuel the Devourer**—diminished but unbowed, wounds never healing.  
- **Moradin** became **Mordrion the Dwarf Father**—missing left eye, gaining new purpose.  
**Significance:** Mordrion ended the Dwarven Wars by 1,849 BW. Zuel spent 1,863 years building an orcish army that would threaten all civilization. Marks Year 1 of Dwarven Reckoning (DR).

### Rivoleen the Philosopher
**Year of Awakening:** ~1,780 BW  
**Circumstances:** Appeared to gnomes as a humble scholar carrying a journal filled with questions. Her awakening sparked a philosophical revolution among gnomekind, teaching that creativity flourishes in freedom.  
**Plane Created:** **Nirvana**. An unbiased paradise with stunning mountains, rolling hills, and deep forests—a sanctuary for those seeking redemption or illumination.  
**Worship:** Within a generation, worship of the Old Gods faded among gnomes, and Rivoleen became their dominant deity.

### Garnak the Hunter
**Year of Awakening:** 1,666 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened during a period when primitive races were rising. Embodies beasts and savagery.  
**Plane Created:** **Savage Lands**. Limitless expanses of exotic wilderness with jungles, forests, mountains, deserts, and swamps where Garnak forever roams and hunts.  
**Worship:** Quickly spread to primitive races of the world.

### Alagalothor, Thalaman, and Sheeva (Immigrants from Talernon)
**Year of Arrival:** 1,492 BW  
**Circumstances:** On the dying universe of **Talernon**, three powerful entities fought. Thalaman attempted a desperate spell, siphoning energy from Sheeva's avatar, creating a vacuum decay event that began erasing Talernon. Alagalothor tore open a portal to Mazariim; the backlash pulled all three through. All arrived diminished and forever linked.  
**Planes Created:** None. Merged with existing planes.  
- **Alagalothor:** Merged with **Astral Plane** (with Mau), became patron of arcane magic.  
- **Thalaman:** Merged with **Limbo**, teaches chronomancy from chaos, served by Slaadi.  
- **Sheeva:** Merged with **Abyss**, bitter over losing 30-40% of her power, plots revenge. Her arrival marks organized assassination cults and poison worship.

### Paladine the Platinum Dragon (Immigrant from Krynn)
**Year of Arrival:** 1,414 BW  
**Circumstances:** In his home universe of **Krynn**, Paladine was a god of Light, but when a deity died and cosmic balance tipped, he was forced to leave. A portion of his divine essence, along with devoted followers, transported across the void to Mazariim. Appeared as a massive platinum-scaled dragon, glowing with celestial radiance, descending over human kingdoms.  
**Plane Created:** None. Shares **Heaven** with Tiovin as complementary duality—Tiovin offers mercy and healing; Paladine offers justice and protection.  
**Significance:** Created the first paladin (Sir Aldric Brightblade). Before Paladine, there were clerics and warriors; after, there were paladins—champions channeling divine power through righteous conviction rather than prayer.

### Eilistraee the Dark Maiden (Immigrant from Abier-Toril/Faerun)
**Year of Arrival:** 1,377 BW  
**Circumstances:** During the catastrophic **Spellplague** in Faerun, Eilistraee (goddess offering redemption to drow) was believed killed by her brother Vhaeraun. A fragment of her essence, along with 200-300 drow refugees, was torn from Faerun and emerged on Mazariim.  
**Plane Created:** None. **Minerva** detected their arrival and welcomed Eilistraee to **Elysium**, offering sanctuary.  
**Significance:** Refugees became Mazariim's first surface drow or moon drow. Before this, all drow were universally feared as Lolth-worshiping slavers; Eilistraee's followers proved dark elves were victims of indoctrination.

### Quarzel the Warlord
**Year of Awakening:** 1,293 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened during intense warfare across Mazariim. Born from collective consciousness of countless conquests and tyrannical empires. Manifested on a battlefield as a titanic figure in black-and-crimson armor, carrying a greatsword dripping with divine ichor.  
**Plane Created:** **Fields of Battle**. An eternal battlefield on the Outer Planes where warriors fight, die, and rise again to fight forever. Served by Titans—fifty-foot-tall warriors of living stone and metal.  
**Significance:** Represents organized, disciplined warfare—not Zuel's savage chaos, but systematic conquest through fear. Relationship with Zuel complicated; both are war deities with different philosophies. **Destroyed during the War** (1 BW) when confronting Zuel's avatar alongside Mordrion.

### Gyru Ironspanner (Ascended Mortal)
**Year of Ascension:** 1,111 BW  
**Circumstances:** **Gyru Ironspanner**, a dwarf artificer obsessed with mechanically achieving godhood, spent 19 years building the **Apotheosis Engine**—a cathedral-sized machine channeling ambient magical energy into a living being. For seven seconds it worked perfectly. Alagalothor detected the Engine and unleashed a dispel to shatter it, but the explosion completed Gyru's transformation into the first mortal to ascend through artifice.  
**Plane Created:** None. **Alagalothor transported Gyru** to **Utopia**, where he built a clockwork body and established his domain, served by Axiomites.  
**Significance:** Proved mortals could achieve godhood through engineering, threatening the divine hierarchy. Engine fragments scattered across Mazariim; multiple factions still seek to reassemble it.

### Xannith the Golden Chain
**Year of Awakening:** 699 BW  
**Circumstances:** During the **Gilded Age**, a period of unprecedented economic growth and extreme inequality, Xannith awakened from the collective consciousness of greed. Manifested to a corrupt merchant lord, offering infinite wealth for worship.  
**Plane Created:** None. Merged with **Abaddon** (originally Khan's domain, later becoming NE alignment).  
**Worship:** Established the Chains of Gold—clergy of corrupt nobles and slave traders. Temples disguised as banks and auction houses teach that wealth is virtue and everything has a price.

### Polyvin the Opportunist
**Year of Awakening:** 374 BW  
**Circumstances:** Awakened in response to economic despair caused by Xannith's exploitation. A halfling merchant named Garrick Twocoins, ruined by predatory practices, prayed for a "second chance." A door appeared leading to Polyvin's domain.  
**Plane Created:** **Towers of Chance** (also called City of Fortune). A strange realm shaped like a doughnut, filled with gambling dens and a meeting place for extraplanar beings where secrets are sold and contracts signed.  
**Worship:** Represents positive commerce—fair trade, shared prosperity, opportunity for all. Became Xannith's enemy from his awakening; the two faiths engage in ongoing economic warfare.

## Special Notes

**Asti's Unique History:** Imprisoned in the Mask of the Pharaoh from ~6,830 BW until present day. During this imprisonment, the ley-line network has degraded and necromancy has flourished.

**Quarzel's Destruction:** Obliterated during the final year of the War to End All Wars (1 BW) when he confronted Zuel's avatar alongside Mordrion. Unlike Mordrion, whose avatar was merely banished, Quarzel was permanently destroyed.

**The Pact:** Tiovin and Taovan established cosmic rules governing their competition for mortal souls. Both remain notably absent from the material plane, honoring their pact to never touch it directly.

**Avatar Banishment:** Most entities' avatars were banished for 101 years following the War (1 BW). Zuel's consciousness was ripped from the material plane and couldn't manifest an avatar until 102 AW.
