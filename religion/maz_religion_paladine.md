# Paladine

- Power Level: Greater Entity (Immigrant from Krynn)
- Portfolio: Honor, chivalry, justice, protection of the innocent
- Title(s): The Platinum Dragon, Draco Paladin, The Valorous, The Just
- Dominion: Heaven (shares with Tiovin)
- Foci: Worships Order, Worships Protection, Is Descended From Dragons
- Worshipers: Paladins, knights, nobles, dragon-blooded, soldiers, lawful good clerics
- Favored Race: Human (though revered by all good-aligned races)
- Favored Weapon: Longsword
- Alignment: Lawful Good
- Symbol: Silver dragon on a blue field

---

## General Overview

Paladine is the god of honor, justice, and righteous strength. He teaches that power without virtue is tyranny, and virtue without power is helpless. A true champion must possess both—the skill to defeat evil and the wisdom to wield that skill justly.

He is not native to Mazariim. Paladine arrived in 1414 BW from Krynn, another universe far removed from this one. In his home cosmos, Paladine was one of the gods of Light, locked in eternal conflict with Takhisis, Queen of Darkness. But when a great cosmic imbalance occurred—when a deity died and the scales of existence tipped dangerously—Paladine was forced to leave Krynn to prevent his presence from destabilizing the remaining balance. A portion of his divine essence, along with some of his most devoted followers, was transported across the void to Mazariim.

He arrived as a silver-scaled dragon of immense size, radiant with celestial light, speaking in a voice like thunder and sunlight. His first words to the people of Mazariim were simple: "I am Paladine. I bring justice. Will you walk with me?" And thus, the paladins were born.

Before Paladine's arrival, there were clerics, priests, and holy warriors. But there were no paladins in the truest sense—warriors bound by sacred oaths, empowered by divine conviction, wielding both sword and spell in the name of justice. Paladine's arrival created the archetype that would define heroism on Mazariim for millennia to come.

His primary form is that of a platinum-scaled dragon, larger than an ancient wyrm, with wings that shimmer like polished silver and eyes that burn with golden light. He can also take the form of a regal human or elven knight clad in brilliant plate armor, or a wizened sage offering counsel. But his true form—the one his faithful see in visions—is always the dragon.

Paladine shares Heaven as his dominion, coexisting peacefully with Tiovin, the god of mercy and healing. Where Tiovin offers compassion and forgiveness, Paladine offers structure and accountability. Together, they form the pillars of lawful good—mercy tempered by justice, compassion strengthened by resolve. Their churches often work together, with Tiovin's clerics healing the wounded and Paladine's paladins defending the innocent.

Unlike many immigrant deities who struggle to find a foothold in Mazariim's crowded pantheon, Paladine was welcomed. His arrival coincided with rising threats—warlords, bandits, tyrannical rulers, monstrous horrors—and his teachings of honor, courage, and justice resonated powerfully with those who sought to fight back. Within a century, his worship had spread across the human kingdoms, and paladins bearing his symbol rode forth to become legends.

## Holy Days

**The Day of Oaths** falls on the 15th of Tio (April 15) and stands as the holiest day in Paladine's calendar. On this day, new paladins take the Oath of the Silver Dragon and are formally inducted into the Order. Temples hold grand ceremonies where squires kneel before silver altars and speak the ancient vows that will bind them for life. Existing paladins renew their vows, reaffirming their commitment to honor, justice, and protection. The day unfolds as a time of reflection, commitment, and celebration. Squires become knights in ceremonies witnessed by their peers and mentors. Feasts and tournaments honor the occasion, allowing newly minted paladins to demonstrate their skills while veterans share wisdom. Stories of legendary paladins are recounted to inspire the next generation, reminding all present of the weight and glory of the oaths they carry.

**The Vigil of Justice** is observed on the 30th of Tio (April 30) as a solemn day honoring paladins who have fallen in the line of duty. Followers spend the day in quiet reflection, visiting graves and memorials scattered across Mazariim. At sunset, they light candles and recite the names of the fallen, speaking each name aloud so that the dead are remembered not as statistics but as individuals who gave everything for their principles. Vigils are held at gravesites and in temple halls, where families of deceased paladins are honored and supported. Prayers for the fallen rise into the evening air, asking that their souls rest in Heaven and that their sacrifices never be forgotten.

**The Silver Dawn** is celebrated on the 1st of Paladine (May 1) and commemorates Paladine's arrival on Mazariim in 1414 BW. It marks the beginning of the Age of Paladins and the spread of justice across the world, serving as a day of hope, renewal, and rededication to righteous action. Sunrise ceremonies welcome the dawn with prayers and hymns, processions wind through cities as knights in full armor ride through the streets displaying Paladine's silver dragon banner. The day emphasizes charitable works such as feeding the poor, rebuilding homes damaged by monsters or war, and offering free healing to those in need. Tournaments and competitions of skill allow paladins and knights to test themselves against each other in friendly combat, demonstrating the martial prowess that makes them effective protectors.

**The Dragon's Justice** is observed on the 15th of Paladine (May 15) and dedicates itself to doing justice rather than merely speaking of it. Paladins are encouraged to actively seek out and oppose evil by hunting down bandits, freeing slaves, or challenging corrupt officials. It is a day of action rather than ceremony. Many paladins embark on quests specifically timed for this holy day, coordinating with local authorities to address outstanding wrongs. Courts hold special sessions to hear cases of injustice, corrupt officials are called to account, and wrongs are righted through both legal means and direct action when necessary.

**The Feast of Honor** is celebrated on the 1st of Quarz (January 1), coinciding with the New Year shared by many cultures. Paladine's followers, however, celebrate it as a day of honor and integrity above all else. Oaths made on this day are considered especially binding, carrying weight beyond even normal sacred vows. Families gather to celebrate the year that was and commit to the year ahead. Debts are settled, disputes are resolved peacefully, and old grudges are formally forgiven or declared ended. Oaths and contracts are signed with particular solemnity, and feasts emphasize honor and community, bringing together people from all walks of life to share food and fellowship.

## Clergy & Temples

Paladine's clergy divides into two primary branches. The Order of the Silver Dragon forms the militant arm of the faith, composed almost entirely of paladins, cavaliers, and holy knights bound by the Oath of the Silver Dragon. This sacred vow demands courage to face danger without faltering even unto death, honor through truth and fidelity to one's word, justice in upholding the law and punishing wrongdoing fairly, protection of the innocent and helpless above all else, and humility in serving others rather than oneself, recognizing that glory is fleeting while righteousness endures eternally.

The Order's hierarchy rests on deeds rather than seniority. The Grandmaster of the Order stands as the greatest living paladin of Paladine, chosen by a council of peers and confirmed through divine visions. Currently Dame Celestria Dawnblade, a half-elf paladin of 18th level who once fought a pit fiend alone to save a village of children, holds this position. Dragon Knights are veteran paladins of 12th level or higher who have proven themselves through legendary deeds; approximately 30 Dragon Knights serve across Mazariim at any given time. Knights of the Silver Wing are full paladins of 7th through 11th level who have completed their training and taken the Oath, traveling Mazariim as independent agents who right wrongs wherever they find them. Squires are paladins in training of 3rd through 6th level, assigned to a Knight or Dragon Knight as apprentices to learn both martial skill and moral philosophy through direct mentorship. Initiates are those of 1st or 2nd level who aspire to paladinhood and undergo trials to prove their worthiness before taking the Oath.

The Council of the Platinum Throne forms the second branch, consisting of clerics, priests, and scholars who manage temples, conduct ceremonies, and provide counsel. The Council interprets Paladine's will, mediates disputes between paladins when their interpretations of the Code conflict, and ensures that justice is administered fairly rather than harshly. The Voice of Paladine leads the Council; currently High Priest Aldren Silverthrone, a human cleric of 16th level based in the Grand Cathedral of Justice in the capital of the Inner Kingdoms, holds this sacred office.

Paladine's clergy wear silver-trimmed blue robes or blue-and-silver plate armor as vestments. Knights of the Silver Wing wear full plate emblazoned with a silver dragon, the armor polished to brilliance and maintained with meticulous care. High-ranking clerics wear cloaks lined with platinum thread, a mark of their office and connection to Paladine's draconic nature. The holy symbol—a silver dragon on a blue field—appears prominently, usually as a pendant worn around the neck, a shield emblem displayed in battle, or a cloak clasp visible to all who approach.

Paladine's temples are grand, soaring structures designed to inspire awe and reverence. They feature high vaulted ceilings with stained glass windows depicting legendary paladins and their deeds, allowing colored light to stream down upon worshipers below. Silver altars stand at the heart of each temple, where oaths are sworn and justice is dispensed with appropriate solemnity. Training yards surround the temples, where squires practice swordplay and horsemanship under the watchful eyes of their mentors. Halls of Honor display the names, deeds, and relics of fallen paladins, serving as both museum and memorial to inspire current and future generations.

The largest and most famous temple is the Grand Cathedral of Justice in the Inner Kingdoms, a towering structure visible for miles around. Aspiring paladins from across Mazariim come here to take their oaths, making pilgrimage to stand before the silver altar where Sir Aldric Brightblade, the first paladin, swore his vows over 2,200 years ago.

Another notable site is the Monastery of the Platinum Wing in the mountains near Blowing Rock. This remote monastery trains paladins in discipline, meditation, and martial prowess, combining the martial focus of knighthood with the spiritual discipline of monasticism. It was here that Remular, a monk-paladin mentioned in the Orcs From Beyond campaign, completed his training before setting out to investigate the orc raiders that ultimately led to his tragic end.

Sacred sites include battlefields where great evils were defeated. These locations are often marked by monuments—stone markers, statues of fallen heroes, or simple shrines maintained by local villagers—and serve as pilgrimage sites for paladins seeking inspiration. Standing where legendary paladins once fought and died reminds modern paladins of the weight of their calling and the price of justice.

## Dogma

Paladine's teachings emphasize honor, justice, and the protection of the innocent, all bound together in what his followers call the Code of the Silver Dragon. The central teaching declares that honor stands above all, justice above self, and courage must shine in the face of darkness. Paladine teaches that true strength lies in righteous action. A warrior who kills for glory is a murderer. A warrior who kills to protect the innocent is a hero. The difference is not in the act, but in the intent behind it.

The doctrine instructs followers to uphold the law, recognizing that law is the foundation of civilization and that without law, there is only chaos and suffering. Paladine is Lawful Good, not merely Good. He teaches that law and order are essential to a functioning society, providing structure and predictability that allow people to live peaceful lives. However, he also teaches that unjust laws must be challenged—not through rebellion or violence, but through reform and principled resistance. A paladin may disobey an unjust order, but must be willing to face the legal consequences and argue for change within the system rather than tearing the system down.

The teaching commands followers to protect the weak, declaring that the strong exist to shield the weak and that power is a burden, not a privilege. Paladine teaches that those with strength have a duty to protect those without it. This applies to physical strength, political power, wealth, or knowledge. To hoard power for personal gain is sinful. To use power to help others is righteous. This teaching drives paladins to defend villages from monsters, challenge corrupt nobles who abuse their authority, and stand between innocents and any threat that would harm them.

The doctrine demands that followers face evil without fear, warning that evil thrives when good men do nothing and commanding them to stand and fight even if they stand alone. Paladine despises cowardice, but not in the traditional sense. He does not expect his followers to be fearless—he expects them to act despite their fear. A paladin who is terrified but charges the dragon anyway is braver than a warrior who feels no fear at all. Courage is not the absence of fear but the willingness to do what is right regardless of the cost.

Finally, Paladine teaches that redemption is possible, offering the principle that even the darkest soul can return to the light if they choose, instructing followers to offer the chance but to strike down those who refuse. Paladine teaches that redemption is always possible—but it must be chosen freely. A paladin should offer mercy and a chance to surrender to every foe. If the foe accepts, they are given a fair trial and a chance to atone for their crimes. If they refuse and continue to harm innocents, they are destroyed without hesitation or regret.

This teaching puts Paladine's paladins in frequent conflict with Tiovin's clerics, who emphasize mercy and forgiveness above justice. Paladine's followers argue that unearned forgiveness enables evil, allowing villains to exploit compassion and continue their crimes. Tiovin's followers argue that justice without mercy is cruelty, that redemption requires grace rather than punishment. The debate continues across temples and philosophical texts, with no resolution in sight.

## Mythology

Paladine's arrival in 1414 BW was dramatic and transformative. According to ancient records preserved by Minerva's elven loremasters, a brilliant light appeared in the sky above the human kingdoms of the time. It descended slowly, growing brighter and larger, until it resolved into a massive platinum dragon glowing with divine radiance. The sight was visible for hundreds of miles, and those who witnessed it later described feeling an overwhelming sense of peace and safety—as though the world itself had taken a breath after holding it for millennia.

The dragon landed on a hilltop (now the site of the Grand Cathedral of Justice) and spoke in a voice that resonated not just in ears but in hearts: "I am Paladine, and I have journeyed far. I seek those who would stand against evil, not for glory or wealth, but because it is right. I seek the valorous. Will you walk with me?"

A group of knights, clerics, and common soldiers approached the dragon with a mixture of awe and determination. The first to kneel was Sir Aldric Brightblade, a human knight known for his unshakable honor and refusal to compromise his principles even when it cost him wealth and position. Paladine touched Aldric's forehead with a claw, and divine power flooded through him. When Aldric stood, he was no longer merely a knight—he was the first paladin of Mazariim, transformed by divine power and sacred oath into something greater than human.

Within days, dozens more took the Oath. Within years, hundreds. Within a century, paladins bearing Paladine's symbol rode across Mazariim, bringing justice to the lawless and hope to the oppressed. They fought bandits terrorizing trade routes, challenged corrupt nobles who abused their subjects, defended villages from monstrous raids, and became living symbols of righteous strength.

Before Paladine, there were no paladins in the sense that Mazariim now knows them. Clerics channeled divine magic, but they were primarily healers, advisors, and spiritual guides rather than warriors. Warriors fought with skill and courage, but they were not divinely empowered and did not channel supernatural abilities. Paladine created a synthesis—the holy warrior who fights with both sword and spell, who channels divine power through righteous conviction rather than through prayer and worship.

The key was the Oath. Unlike clerics, who are granted power through worship, prayer, and devotion to their deity, paladins are granted power through sacred vows. By swearing the Oath of the Silver Dragon and living by its code, a warrior creates a metaphysical bond with Paladine himself. This bond allows divine magic to flow through them, granting healing touch, smiting power against evil, and auras of protection that shield allies. The power comes not from Paladine granting it as a gift, but from the paladin earning it through unwavering commitment to their principles.

Paladins became Mazariim's answer to overwhelming evil—champions who could face demons, devils, dragons, and warlords with equal resolve. They were elite warriors who combined martial skill with divine magic, tactical acumen with moral certainty, and personal courage with institutional support.

Paladine's influence is strongest in the Inner Kingdoms, the human nations that first embraced his teachings. Here, his temples are grand architectural achievements, his paladins are respected members of society, and his Code is woven into the legal framework of civilization. Laws reflect Paladinian principles of justice, fairness, and protection of the innocent. Courts reference the Code when rendering judgments. Nobles who aspire to honor often take counsel from paladins on matters of ethics and governance.

The Holy Theocracy of Tentigo (a Tiovinian nation) initially resisted Paladine's influence, fearing competition with Tiovin for the hearts of the faithful. However, over time, a compromise was reached through dialogue between the two faiths. Tiovin's church would handle mercy, healing, and forgiveness—offering compassion to the repentant and tending to the sick and suffering. Paladine's order would handle justice, defense, and military action—protecting the innocent and punishing the wicked. The two faiths became complementary rather than competitive, and today they often cooperate closely. Tiovinian clerics heal soldiers wounded in battle while Paladine's paladins defend the wounded from further harm. Paladine's paladins bring criminals to justice while Tiovinian clerics offer them chances at redemption.

When the War to End All Wars erupted in 3 BW, Paladine's paladins rode to battle immediately. They fought alongside the armies of Minerva (elves), Mordrion (dwarves), and Hinterhin (halflings) against the orcish hordes led by Zuel the Devourer. Paladins served as elite strike forces—slaying orcish champions in single combat, disrupting demonic rituals before they could be completed, and defending critical strongholds that could not be allowed to fall. Their presence was a beacon of hope in humanity's darkest hour, proof that courage and honor could stand against overwhelming evil.

Remular, the monk mentioned in the Orcs From Beyond campaign, was a paladin-monk hybrid trained at the Monastery of the Platinum Wing. His sacrifice—investigating the orc keep and dying while trying to understand the inter-dimensional machine that threatened the region—exemplifies Paladine's teachings: courage in the face of unknown danger, sacrifice for the greater good, and the pursuit of justice even unto death. His death was mourned, but his example inspired a generation of paladins who followed.

In the 811 years since the war, Paladine's worship has remained strong. His paladins continue to travel Mazariim, hunting down evil wherever it hides. They are particularly active in hunting undead cults (particularly followers of Zebulba who raise the dead for evil purposes), opposing Taovan's devil-worshiping cults that corrupt souls through false promises, defending villages from monstrous threats ranging from goblin raids to dragon attacks, mediating disputes between nations to prevent unjust wars and promote peaceful resolution of conflicts, and training new generations of paladins to carry on the sacred work.

Paladine's faith has also spread to other races. Elves who value his emphasis on protection sometimes take the Oath, becoming rare but respected elven paladins who combine elvish grace with Paladinian conviction. Dwarves respect his code of honor and martial discipline, with some dwarven warriors becoming paladins particularly in regions where dwarves and humans fought together against Zuel. Even some halflings, despite their generally peaceful nature, seek to become paladins to defend their homes from the threats that periodically emerge. However, humans remain his primary followers, with human paladins making up the vast majority of the Order.

Paladine's relationships with other entities shape much of Mazariim's religious landscape. Paladine and Tiovin are natural allies, both Lawful Good entities sharing Heaven as their dominion. Tiovin focuses on mercy and healing; Paladine focuses on justice and protection. They balance each other perfectly, creating a comprehensive approach to goodness that addresses both compassion and accountability. Their churches cooperate frequently, and many adventuring parties include both a Tiovinian cleric and a Paladinian paladin working in perfect harmony.

Minerva, the elven goddess of knowledge and beauty, respects Paladine's honor and discipline. Elven paladins (though rare) often worship both Minerva and Paladine, seeing no contradiction between wisdom and valor. Their churches exchange knowledge and magical research, with Minervan scholars studying Paladine's Code and Paladinian strategists learning elven combat techniques.

Mordrion, the dwarven deity, respects Paladine for his martial prowess and honor. Dwarven paladins are uncommon but exist, particularly in regions where dwarves and humans fought together against Zuel. Mordrion and Paladine share mutual respect for craft, honor, and the willingness to stand against evil.

Hinterhin, the halfling god of hearth and home, appreciates Paladine's emphasis on protecting the innocent. Hinterhin's followers often call upon Paladine's paladins to defend halfling villages from monsters, knowing that the paladins will answer such calls without hesitation or expectation of reward.

Paladine's greatest enemy is Taovan, the god of corruption and tyranny. Taovan represents everything Paladine opposes—deceit, cruelty, and the corruption of virtue for personal gain. Taovan finds Paladine's paladins delicious prey—turning a righteous paladin into a blackguard through corruption is one of Taovan's greatest pleasures, a perversion of virtue that feeds his power. The two faiths are locked in eternal conflict, with Paladine's paladins hunting Taovan's cultists and Taovan's agents attempting to corrupt paladins through temptation, blackmail, or torture.

Zuel the Devourer remains a bitter enemy. Paladine remembers the War to End All Wars, the countless innocents slaughtered by Zuel's orcs, and the near-destruction of civilization. Paladine's paladins were instrumental in defeating Zuel, and now they hunt his followers relentlessly, ensuring that orckind never again rises to threaten Mazariim. Zuel respects Paladine's strength but sees him as an obstacle to be destroyed, a source of resistance that must be overcome before orcish supremacy can be achieved.

Og'thallith, the demon lord of madness, is anathema to Paladine's order and discipline. Paladins are trained to resist demonic corruption through mental discipline and divine protection, and they frequently lead crusades against Og'thallith's cults, destroying the madmen who serve chaos and attempting to heal those in the early stages of corruption.
