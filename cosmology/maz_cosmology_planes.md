# Extra-Dimensional Plane Descriptions

All of the following planes float within the Astral Sea, with no physical connections between them. Unlike other campaign settings, there is no distinction between "Inner Planes" and "Outer Planes" in Mazariim—all are simply extra-dimensional planes formed when entities reach sufficient magical mass.

## Abaddon
**Alignment:** Neutral Evil  
**Founding Entity:** Khan  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Daemons, Demodands

Abaddon is a wasteland realm of endless ash fields, volcanic mountains, and rivers of molten rock. It is the home of the Daemons and Demodands, servants of entropy and destruction who seek to unmake creation itself. Souls consumed by despair and nihilism find their way here, where they are transformed into servants of oblivion.

## Abyss
**Alignment:** Chaotic Evil  
**Founding Entity:** Zuel  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Demons, Div

The Abyss is an infinite plane of chaos and evil, comprised of countless layers, each more horrifying than the last. It was formed when Zuel the Devourer arrived in the Mazariim universe. The Abyss is home to demons and div, creatures of pure malevolence who seek only to destroy and corrupt. Each layer of the Abyss reflects the twisted nature of its demonic inhabitants—burning wastelands, frozen hells, fetid jungles, and worse.

## Astral Plane
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Mau  
**Additional Entities:** Alagalothor  
**Inhabitants:** Aeons, astral travelers, assorted Outsiders

The Astral Plane is the vast, limitless sky-blue ocean of empty space, with wisps of vapors and smoke of different colors stretching across huge distances. The Astral Plane was the first extra-dimensional plane to form and is the scaffolding in which all the other extra-dimensional planes exist. The Astral Plane is coexistent with every part of the prime material plane, and as such can be used to travel the vast distances between all other places and other dimensions near instantaneously. Teleport and other translocational magics transport the traveler to the Astral Plane for the most infinitesimal of moments and then back to the prime in the new location.

The Astral Plane formed long ago when the first entity awakened, Mau the Watcher. As he flared into existence, his gaze spread over all of time and space, and his act of observation interacted with the tiny quantum world, creating the Astral Plane. Mau does not exist in any one place in his domain, and his intelligence and persona are far beyond anything mortals and even some other entities can comprehend.

The main inhabitants of the Astral Plane are the Aeons, caretakers of the universe and servants of Mau. The Aeons tirelessly travel between the planes, carrying out tasks unknown to mortals involving life and death, creation, and entropy. The Watchers also travel the Astral, living outside the confines of time and space, hailing from an unknown alternate prime material plane where humans conquered magic. Additionally, the entity Alagolothor exists in the Astral Plane, always studying new ways to use magic as well as carefully observing how the mortals of Mazariim use it. There are no githyanki, githerazi, or floating husks of dead gods in the Astral Plane.

Scattered throughout the Astral, one can find pulsating extra-dimensional pools of energy, often perceived to be different colors depending on which other plane the pools lead to. Travelers using the astral projection spell often use these to travel to other planes without using their real forms. Additionally, a strange phenomenon known as the psychic wind has been known to cause travelers to get lost and suffer all manner of mental maladies.

## City of Fortune
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Polyvin  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** various petitioners, outsiders, and proxies

The City of Fortune is a strange realm, in some ways similar to the city known as Sigil in other universes. It is a large city that has its geometry in the shape of a doughnut, and in the city are limitless festhalls and gambling dens, where one can bet on almost anything. The plane is also a meeting place for various extra-planar beings, a place where secrets are quietly sold and contracts signed, alliances made and broken, and where various power groups related to the planes try to gain power. Polyvin is said to rarely make an appearance, although he has been known to intercede and destroy powerful proxies for various known reasons. Polyvin has no race of Outsiders to call his own, although he can create multiple servants through copies of himself to do his bidding in the city.

## Elysium
**Alignment:** Chaotic Good  
**Founding Entity:** Minerva  
**Additional Entities:** Eilistraee  
**Inhabitants:** Azatas, Angels

A forest realm partially subsumed from a moon, Elysium is a place of beauty where one could marvel at brilliant green meadows dotted with starburst flowers, pools as deep blue as a jay's plumage, and silver clouds drifting against a perfect sky. The plane itself seems to vibrate with its own sense of life and intensity. It is usually a peaceful place, and tranquility seems to seep into the bones and souls of those that cross it. Even though some think of Azatas, the celestials of Minerva, as the most elf-like of the celestial races, many of them have animal, elemental, or even stranger features and body parts. Many take whimsical forms, resembling the good fey with whom they are sometimes associated. Angels also frequent Elysium, oftentimes serving Eilistraee or attending to things for other entities of good.

## Ethereal Plane
**Alignment:** all Good  
**Founding Entity:** Tiovin  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Angels, Sherpas

The misty, fog-shrouded transitive plane known as the Ethereal Plane is a timeless plane that allows the transition of souls to Heaven. Places and landmarks from the prime material plane are visible from the Ethereal, ghostly images and representations, all incorporeal with no form or mass. Many spells and creatures access the Ethereal Plane, such as blink or displacer beasts, relying on it for temporary travel around barriers and in and out of combat.

The Ethereal Plane was formed when Tiovin and Taovan created it to help ferry the souls of the good to Heaven. Parts of it are coexistent with the prime material plane and parts of the Shadow Plane, and other parts are coexistent with Heaven, and travelers can sometimes catch a glimpse of a soul on its way to its heavenly destination.

The main inhabitants of the Ethereal Plane are incorporeal undead and spirits. As parts of the Ethereal Plane overlap the prime material plane and the Shadow Plane, spirits and incorporeal creatures of any alignment can be found. Additionally, the spirit guides known as Sherpas can sometimes be found, searching for lost souls to help them become more self-actualized and ascend to Heaven as well as helping defend against the depraved servants of the Shadow Plane, the Kytons.

The incredible energies that helped create the Ethereal Plane have a dangerous side effect known as ether cyclones. These giant columns of ethereal mists swirl in a pattern similar to tornadoes on the prime material plane and can cause travelers and natural inhabitants alike to be thrown far from their position or even hurled into Heaven or the Astral Plane.

## Far Realm
**Alignment:** Chaotic Evil  
**Founding Entity:** Xorancon  
**Additional Entities:** Ry'le  
**Inhabitants:** aberrations, Ry'lehe

In the Far Realm, titanic creatures swim through nothingness, preoccupied with madness. Unspeakable things whisper awful truths to those who dare listen. For mortals, knowledge of the Far Realm is a triumph of mind over the rude boundaries of matter, space, and eventually sanity. It is the home of all aberrations, their place of origin before they came to Mazariim.

The Far Realm is divided into an unknown number of layers. Each layer is only slightly out of phase with the next, and passing between layers requires only a thought. It's possible to stand in one layer and see dozens of others, all overlapping yet somehow distinct.

The Far Realm is home to both Xorancon and Ry'le, entities that look like a giant beholder and a large cephalopod, respectively. On very rare occasions, a group of aberrations will escape to the Plane of Dreams from the Far Realm, once in a while manifesting on the Prime Material Plane and becoming a new monster in the world.

## Feywild
**Alignment:** Chaotic Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Gruhathyrir  
**Additional Entities:** many minor entities related to animals and nature  
**Inhabitants:** Fey

The Feywild is the ancestral home of all fey. This eternal primordial wilderness was created by Gruhathyrir in a time just before the main races ruled the lands, his creatures stuff of the faerie tales told to children. Although most Fey are native to the Prime Material plane, some ancestor of theirs was originally from the Feywild. Many parts of the Feywild resemble the real world, with the exception of buildings and structures, giving visitors a strange feeling of what the world would be like without civilization. Dozens of minor entities live in the Feywild, most related to animals, nature, or trickery.

## Fields of Battle
**Alignment:** Lawful Evil  
**Founding Entity:** Quarzel  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** petitioners, Titans

The sprawling Fields of Battle are a constantly changing set of landscapes in which huge battles between past warriors take place forever, warriors reliving their most glorious moments as the servants of Quarzel, the Titans, stand and watch and sometimes even wade into battle, their gigantic forms looming large over the battlefields. Between the eternal battles, the faithful petitioners of Quarzel drink and revel in festhalls according to their bravery in their lives.

## Heaven
**Alignment:** any Good  
**Founding Entity:** Tiovin  
**Additional Entities:** Paladine  
**Inhabitants:** Angels, Archons, Guardinals

The soaring mountain of Heaven towers high above the clouds in the sky of Tiovin's realm. This realm of honor and compassion is divided into three main sections, each corresponding with either law, neutrality, or chaos but always concerning good. Heaven's slopes are filled with planned, orderly cities and tidy, cultivated gardens and orchards. Though they began their existences as mortals, Heaven's native Angels see law and good as indivisible halves of the same exalted concept, and array themselves against the cosmic perversions of evil. Heaven is a realm of pure righteousness, where the forces of good gather to aid those on less virtuous planes and help worthy souls find rest.

In addition to Angels, Tiovin is served by the neutral good Guardinals and the chaotic good Eladrin in different sections of Heaven. The entity Paladine also resides in Heaven, a deity of law and goodness from another universe, and is served by Archons.

## Hell
**Alignment:** any Evil  
**Founding Entity:** Taovan  
**Additional Entities:** many minor entities related to devils  
**Inhabitants:** Devils, Yugoloths

Hell is famed throughout the Great Beyond for its innumerable dangers and torments. The skies above range from gloomy and depressing on the best of days to burning red with ash and fire, or, in realms like Erebus, absent. The exact nature of the clouds and illumination varies from each section of Hell to the next, but there is never anything comforting or soothing in the view. Hell is formed of three sections based on alignment, each tailored to the whim of its ruling archdevil, where the souls of evil mortals and victims of the devils' machinations endure unending torments. Each section of Hell corresponds to its own alignment or even smaller realm based on an ethos. Taovan rules Hell with his Devils made from his most faithful of petitioners, but Yugoloths serve in the neutral and chaotic sections of Hell as well. Many minor entities make their home in Hell as well with their own smaller realms of suffering or torment.

## Hinterhome
**Alignment:** Chaotic Good  
**Founding Entity:** Hinterhin  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Faer'peri

The endless hills and forests of Hinterhome are the lands of Hinterhin and his petitioners, the Faer'peri. Small animals converse and dance with the Faer'peri at endless outdoor celebrations with huge heaps of foods, ales, and fireworks. A testament to their way of life, the afterlife for Halflings is the promise of family and friends forever. Very little traffic in and out of Hinterhome occurs very often, although a Faer'peri might venture to other planes once in a while when wanderlust does occur.

## Limbo
**Alignment:** Chaotic Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Kazrokath  
**Additional Entities:** Thalaman  
**Inhabitants:** Proteans, Slaadi

Limbo is a realm of boundless chaos, a churning void of land, sea, and everything else that can or could exist between. Limbo is almost impossible to map, and no reliable directional constant like "north" exists on this plane. Even relatively stable terrain moves and changes. The day-night cycle in Limbo is random, shifting without apparent reason and following a nonsensical clock that none can predict.

The Maelstrom defies the efforts of mapmakers, as its very character shifts and flows like the tide of an unseen ocean, to which the borderlands are but shores and calm shallows. Crystalline forests melt like candle wax into shallow brine seas with jewel-like icebergs and then sublimate to vast parched deserts, all within the stretch of days. Still, islands of stability do exist within the depths of the Maelstrom. Petty gods, exiled fiends, and fallen celestials rule such islands, and even the rare stronghold of a mortal wizard or priest-king can be found here, holding itself against the metaphysical lapping tide. Deeper still, the mysterious serpentine Proteans thrive within an environment of plastic potentiality, doing unknown tasks in the name of the mysterious Kazrokath. The disgusting and insane Slaadi also inhabit Limbo, doing the bidding of the strange entity Thalaman.

## Negative Energy Plane
**Alignment:** Chaotic Evil  
**Founding Entity:** Zebulba  
**Additional Entities:** Khan  
**Inhabitants:** Undead, Nightshades, Oni

The Negative Energy Plane is one of the most hostile planes in the Great Beyond, and exploration beyond its fringes, where islands and precipices from reality form somewhat stable and sheltered regions, should be attempted only by the most well-prepared adventuring groups. There are no astronomical bodies to speak of in the "skies" of this plane, nor is there a workable analog to north. To an observer, there's little to see on the Negative Energy Plane. It is a dark, empty place, an eternal pit where a traveler can fall until the plane itself steals away all light and life. The Negative Energy Plane is the most uncaring and intolerant of life. Only creatures immune to its life-draining energies can survive there.

The Negative Energy Plane formed when the entity Zebulba awoke from slumber near a large black hole, engulfing the black hole inside the extra-dimensional plane. Various elemental spirits known as Nightshades serve to spread the entropy of Zebulba, while the mysterious minor entity Khan is served by his Oni as well as Undead servants in his spreading of evil.

## Nirvana
**Alignment:** Neutral Good  
**Founding Entity:** Rivoleen  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Agathions

Nirvana is an unbiased paradise, its stunning mountains, rolling hills, and deep forests all match a visitor's expectations of a pastoral paradise, but Nirvana also contains mysteries that lead to enlightenment. Nirvana is a sanctuary and a place of respite for all who seek redemption or illumination. Created by the entity Rivoleen, its pastoral wilds are home to intelligent animal petitioners, animalistic Agathions, and other creatures of pure good. Nirvana holds mysterious secrets to higher states of existence, and pilgrims and inhabitants alike seek to explore and unearth those mysteries through their interactions on the plane.

## Plane of Air
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Vuleala  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Elementals, Genies

The endless expanse of wind and sky with an eternal breeze makes the Plane of Air one of the easiest of the planes based on elements to visit. It is the home of Vuleala, an entity that drifted between Mazariim and the other planets of the system for eons, awakening in the early days of the formation of the primary races and favoring the creatures that fly the skies of Mazariim. The incomprehensible Elementals and malevolent Genies make their home here, coming out of their timeless existence when summoned to the Prime Material plane.

## Plane of Dreams
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Zanzabar  
**Additional Entities:** many minor entities without a home plane  
**Inhabitants:** dreamselves, Night Hags

The ever-changing landscape of the Plane of Dreams is a transitive plane through which the dreaming minds of sleeping sentients oftentimes travel. Surroundings and pseudo-real creatures always changing, the Plane of Dreams never looks the same twice.

Ages ago, the entity known as Zanzabar came into being after his gaze had watched the minds and dreams of sentient creatures of Mazariim, fascinated by what their strange organic brains would think about subconsciously while resting and regenerating. His realm became a transitive plane that magically allowed some of the dreamers with particularly strong or creative minds to encounter each other and even interact.

The main inhabitants of the Plane of Dreams are dreamselves, the subconscious manifestation of sleeping sentients from all around Mazariim. Additionally, spellcasters have been known to use the Plane of Dreams for communication with the sleeping, as well as other more nefarious purposes. The entity Zanzibar calls the Plane of Dreams home, as well as an unknown number of quasi-entities, thought to have come from the Far Realm.

Although the surroundings of the Plane of Dreams change as different dreamers unknowingly alter their terrain and occupants, dark corners of the plane created by minds evil and chaotic sometimes connect in subtle ways to the place of nightmares, known as the Far Realm. The changing borders of the Plane of Dreams that are transitive with the Far Realm manifest themselves as nightmare storms, horrifying clouds of multi-hued lightning, thunderous roaring winds, and driving rains. Getting caught in one can not only be dangerous from the storm's effect but from the connection it makes, allowing aberrations to enter the Plane of Dreams from the Far Realm.

## Plane of Earth
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Dirgin  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Elementals, Genies

The Plane of Earth is a limitless expanse of various types of earth and stone, riddled with natural tunnels formed from various natural phenomena. It is the home of Dirgin, one of the most alien and uncommunicative entities of Mazariim. The incomprehensible Elementals and malevolent Genies make their home here, coming out of their timeless existence when summoned to the Prime Material plane.

## Plane of Fire
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Pyrantor  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Elementals, Genies

When an ancient slumbering entity awoke next to a huge sun, the Plane of Fire was born as Pyrantor came into existence. A place where almost nothing can survive, the Plane of Fire is rarely visited by mortals, instead the domain of the alien-minded Elementals and malevolent Genies, existing in a strange timeless state until summoned to the Prime Material plane.

## Plane of Water
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Iquinias  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Elementals, Genies

Far from Mazariim in a nearby star system, the entity Iquinias woke after an immeasurably long slumber, his eons spend watching a water world full of aquatic life. His plane is a limitless ocean, filled with aquatic life both familiar to Mazariim and totally alien. The incomprehensible Elementals and malevolent Genies make their home here, coming out of their timeless existence when summoned to the Prime Material plane.

## Positive Energy Plane
**Alignment:** Lawful Good  
**Founding Entity:** Asti  
**Additional Entities:** Ashura  
**Inhabitants:** Elementals, Angels, Kami

The Positive Energy Plane has no surface and is akin to the Plane of Air with its wide-open nature. However, every bit of this plane glows brightly with innate power. This power is dangerous to mortal forms, which are not made to handle it. Despite the beneficial effects of the plane, it is one of the most hostile of the Inner Planes. An unprotected character on this plane swells with power as positive energy is forced upon her. Then, because her mortal frame is unable to contain that power, she is immolated, like a mote of dust caught at the edge of a supernova. Visits to the Positive Energy Plane are brief, and even then travelers must be heavily protected.

The plane originally formed when the entity Asti became conscious near a white hole, a rare space phenomenon. From there, various elemental spirits roam as well as the spirits called Kami and Angels on errands from Heaven. Although most are formless spirits without the minds of normal sentients, when they leave the plane they are either as elementals or with mind and form like the Kami, the servants of Ashura, another entity of healing and good.

## Savage Lands
**Alignment:** Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Garnak  
**Additional Entities:** many minor entities related to animals  
**Inhabitants:** magical beasts, Animal Lords

The limitless expanses of exotic natural world wilderness serve the petitioners and their masters, the Animal Lords. Their master, Garnak, forever roams and hunts the prey that roams in his realm. Petitioners that served Garnak well in life ascend into Animal Lords, exaggerated versions of their respective animals. Jungles, forests, mountains, deserts, swamps . . . all are to be found in the Savage Lands.

## Shadow Plane
**Alignment:** all Evil  
**Founding Entity:** Taovan  
**Additional Entities:** none  
**Inhabitants:** Kytons, Devils, incorporeal undead

The dark, disturbing transitive plane known as the Shadow Plane is a timeless plane that allows the transition of souls to Hell. Places and landmarks from the prime material plane are visible from the Shadow, ghostly images and representations with horrific twists, all incorporeal with no form or mass. Many spells and creatures access the Shadow Plane, such as shadow walk and some spells which create quasi-real illusions, using shadowstuff to help create the effects.

The Shadow Plane was formed when Taovan created it to help ferry the souls of the evil to Hell. Parts of it are coexistent with the Prime Material Plane and parts of the Ethereal Plane, and other parts are coexistent with Hell, and travelers can sometimes catch a glimpse of a soul on its way to its hellish destination.

The main inhabitants of the Shadow Plane are incorporeal undead and spirits. As parts of the Shadow Plane overlap the Prime Material Plane and the Ethereal Plane, spirits and incorporeal creatures of any alignment can be found. Additionally, the horrifying agents of evil known as Kytons can sometimes be found, searching for lost souls to help corrupt and drag to hell, as well as helping attack and destroy the agents of the Ethereal Planes and Heaven, the Sherpas.

Spending too much time on the Shadow Plane can be detrimental to one's mental state. The strange phenomenon called shadow despair is a manifestation of the dark energies traveling through the Shadow Plane. Over long periods of time, the exposure can have greater and more permanent effects.

## Utopia
**Alignment:** Lawful Neutral  
**Founding Entity:** Guzon  
**Additional Entities:** Mordrion, Gyru  
**Inhabitants:** Inevitables, Gromthi, Axiomites

A great city of eternal perfection, Utopia's streets and buildings are paragons of architecture and aesthetics; everything is ordered and nothing happens by chance. Guzon's creations, the Inevitables, constantly toil in a seemingly endless search for order and focus. The Gromthi inhabit the part of the city that Mordrion inhabits, with amazing architecture in the most dwarven of styles with the sounds of dwarven revelry and the recitation of names of honor always in the air. The glowing crystalline axiomites serve Gyru in a huge clockwork laboratory full of golems and experiments.
