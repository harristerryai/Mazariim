# Cypher System Fantasy Type Ability Index

## Document Purpose
This index catalogs all abilities available to the 10 character types in the Cypher System Fantasy Ancestries & Types supplement. Each ability lists which character types can access it and at which tier levels.

**Priority System for Descriptions:**
1. Custom/revised abilities from the Ancestries & Types PDF (marked with *) - these supersede all other sources
2. Standard abilities from Cypher System Core Rulebook (page references in parentheses)

## Alphabetical Ability Index

### A

**Able Assistance** (108)
- **Available to:** Bard (T5)
- **Description:** (2 Intellect points): Assist another with a task they are attempting. If you succeed on a task of the same difficulty, the character you're helping gains an asset on the task. Action.

**Acrobatic Attack** (108)
- **Available to:** Magus (T3, T4), Rogue (T3, T4)
- **Description:** (3 Speed points): You leap into the air, performing a forward roll or spectacular acrobatic maneuver. If you succeed on a Speed-based roll, you can make a melee attack as part of the same action. You have an asset on this attack roll. Action.

**Advanced Command** (108)
- **Available to:** Bard (T3)
- **Description:** (7 Intellect points): You give a specific command to another intelligent creature. If it chooses to listen, any task it attempts during its next action is eased by three steps. Action.

**Advantage to Disadvantage** (109)
- **Available to:** Fighter (T2)
- **Description:** (3 Intellect points): You use trickery and deception to turn one asset that an enemy has into a hindrance for them instead. The effect lasts for one minute. Action.

**Again and Again** (109)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T6), Fighter (T6), Paladin (T6), Magus (T6), Ranger (T6), Rogue (T6)
- **Description:** (5 Speed points): Once per round, you can take an extra action on your turn. You can do this for the next minute. Action to initiate.

**Alley Rat** (109)
- **Available to:** Rogue (T6)
- **Description:** You are trained in tasks related to sneaking, climbing, and staying hidden in urban environments. Enabler.

**All-Out Con** (109)
- **Available to:** Rogue (T6)
- **Description:** (5 Intellect points): You attempt to convince one intelligent creature of an outrageous claim. The GM assigns a difficulty to the task, but it is always at least one step higher than the creature's level. A successful roll does not make the creature believe you, but it does cause the creature to seriously consider your claim and spend at least one round thinking about it. Action.

**Amazing Effort** (109)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T4), Fighter (T4), Paladin (T4), Magus (T4)
- **Description:** (5 Might, Speed, or Intellect points): When you apply Effort, you can apply a free level of Effort on the same action. You can do this once per ten-minute rest period. Enabler.

**Ambusher** (109)
- **Available to:** Rogue (T4)
- **Description:** (4 Speed points): You attack from hiding or from a position of surprise, making a melee or ranged attack against one creature. If you successfully hit your target, you inflict 6 additional points of damage. Action.

**Anecdote** (109)
- **Available to:** Bard (T1)
- **Description:** (2 Intellect points): You can lift the spirits of a group within short range and help them relax. For the next hour, those who heard your anecdote gain an asset on their next Intellect-based task. Action.

**Animal Form** *
- **Available to:** Druid (T1)
- **Description:** See Animal Shape (3+ Intellect points): You use your action to magically assume the shape of an animal that you have seen before. The animal can be medium size or smaller. You remain in this form for up to 1 hour. You can renew the duration of the Animal Shape while still in form without changing back and forth. You revert to normal form if you fall unconscious or become debilitated, or die. Each form has two modifications reflecting the creature chosen.

**Animal Senses and Sensibilities** (109)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T3), Druid (T3), Ranger (T3)
- **Description:** You are trained in listening and spotting things. Enabler.

**Answering Attack** (110)
- **Available to:** Magus (T4), Rogue (T4)
- **Description:** (3 Might or 3 Speed points): If you are struck by a melee attack, you can immediately make a melee attack in return. You can use this ability only once per round. Enabler.

**Anticipate Attack** (110)
- **Available to:** Bard (T4)
- **Description:** (4 Intellect points): You can sense when an attack is about to happen and prepare for it. You gain an asset on Speed defense rolls against attacks for one minute. Action.

**Applying Your Knowledge** (110)
- **Available to:** Mage (T3), Bard (T3)
- **Description:** You're trained in two areas of knowledge of your choice. Enabler.

**As If One Creature** (110)
- **Available to:** Druid (T6), Ranger (T6)
- **Description:** You and your companion are so close, you act and move as if you are one creature. While your companion is within immediate range, you share one initiative, one set of actions, and you can use either your position or your companion's to determine range. Enabler.

**Assassin Skills** (110)
- **Available to:** Rogue (T1)
- **Description:** You are trained in stealth and disguise tasks. Enabler.

**Assassin Strike** (110)
- **Available to:** Rogue (T5)
- **Description:** (5 Speed points): If you successfully attack a creature that was previously unaware of your presence, you deal 6 additional points of damage. Enabler.

**Assume Control** (111)
- **Available to:** Bard (T6)
- **Description:** Assume Control (6+ Intellect points): You control the actions of another creature you have interacted with or studied for at least a round. This effect lasts for ten minutes. The target must be level 2 or lower. Once you have assumed control, the target acts as if it wants to accomplish your desire to the best of its ability, freely using its own best judgment unless you use an action to give it a specific instruction on an issue-by-issue basis. In addition to the normal options for using Effort, you can choose to use Effort to increase the maximum level of the target. Thus, to attempt to command a level 5 target (three levels above the normal limit), you must apply three levels of Effort. When the effect ends, the target remembers everything that happened and reacts according to its nature and your relationship to it; assuming control might have soured that relationship if it was previously a positive one. Action to initiate.

**Attack and Attack Again** (111)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T5)
- **Description:** Attack and Attack Again: Rather than granting additional damage or a minor or major effect, a natural 17 or higher on your attack roll allows you the option of immediately making another attack. Enabler.

**Attack Flourish** (111)
- **Available to:** Ranger (T1)
- **Description:** (1 Speed point): When you make a melee weapon attack, you add stylistic flourishes. If you hit on the attack, you can choose one of the following effects: distract the foe so the next attack by one of your allies is eased, or cause the foe to be off balance so your next attack is eased. Enabler.

**Augment Summoning** *
- **Available to:** Druid (T3), Mage (T3), Priest (T3)
- **Description:** When you cast a summoning spell and spend a level of Effort, the summons is one level higher, has +1 Armor, and has one more point of Health per level. Enabler.

**Awareness** (111)
- **Available to:** Rogue (T3)
- **Description:** You are trained in all tasks involving perception. Enabler.

### B

**Babel** (112)
- **Available to:** Bard (T1)
- **Description:** After hearing a language spoken for a few minutes, you can speak it and make yourself understood. If you continue to use the language to interact with native speakers, your skills improve rapidly, to the point where you might be mistaken for a native speaker after just a few hours of speaking the new language. Enabler.

**Bash** (112)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T1), Fighter (T1), Paladin (T1)
- **Description:** (1 Might point): This is a pummeling melee attack. Your attack inflicts 1 less point of damage than normal, but dazes your target for one round, during which time all tasks it performs are hindered. Action.

**Battle Management** (112)
- **Available to:** Bard (T6)
- **Description:** (4 Intellect points): As long as you use your action each round giving orders or advice, attack and defense actions taken by your allies within short range are eased. Action.

**Battlecaster** *
- **Available to:** Druid (T2), Mage (T2), Priest (T2)
- **Description:** When you spend a level of Effort on a spell attack, you gain a free level on damage. Enabler.

**Beast Call** (112)
- **Available to:** Druid (T5)
- **Description:** (5 Intellect points): You summon a horde of small animals or a single level 4 beast to help you temporarily. These creatures do your bidding for as long as you focus your attention, but you must use your action each turn to direct them. Creatures are native to the area and arrive under their own power, so if you're in an unreachable place, this ability won't work. Action.

**Beast Companion** (112)
- **Available to:** Druid (T1), Ranger (T1)
- **Description:** A level 2 creature of your size or smaller accompanies you and follows your instructions. You and the GM must work out the details of your creature, and you'll probably make rolls for it in combat or when it takes actions. The beast companion acts on your turn. As a level 2 creature, it has a target number of 6 and 6 health and it inflicts 2 points of damage. Its movement is based on its creature type (avian, swimmer, and so on). If your beast companion dies, you can hunt in the wild for 1d6 days to find a new one. Enabler.

**Beast Eyes** (112)
- **Available to:** Druid (T4)
- **Description:** (3 Intellect points): By linking to the creature from your Beast Companion ability, you can perceive through its senses if it is within 1 mile (1.5 km) of you. This effect lasts up to ten minutes. Action to establish.

**Better Surprise Attack** (113)
- **Available to:** Rogue (T4)
- **Description:** If attacking from a hidden vantage, with surprise, or before an opponent has acted, you get an asset on the attack (if you have Surprise Attack, this is in addition to the asset from that ability). On a successful hit with this surprise attack, you inflict 2 additional points of damage (for a total of 4 additional points of damage if you have Surprise Attack). Enabler.

**Blend In** (113)
- **Available to:** Bard (T3)
- **Description:** (4 Intellect points): When you blend in, creatures still see you, but they attach no importance to your presence for about a minute. While blending in, you are specialized in stealth and Speed defense tasks. This effect ends if you do something to reveal your presence or position—attacking, using an ability, moving a large object, and so on. If this occurs, you can regain the remaining period of effect by taking an action to focus on seeming innocuous and as if you belong. Action to initiate or reinitiate.

**Blessing of the Gods** (114)
- **Available to:** Paladin (T1), Priest (T1)
- **Description:** As a servant of the gods, you can call up blessings in their name. This blessing depends on the god's general demeanor and area of influence. Choose two of the abilities described in the Blessing of the Gods section. Enabler.

**Blinding Attack** (115)
- **Available to:** Ranger (T6), Rogue (T6)
- **Description:** (3 Speed points): If you have a source of light, you can use it to make a melee attack against a target. If successful, the attack deals no damage, but the target is blinded for one minute. Action.

**Blink of an Eye** (115)
- **Available to:** Magus (T4)
- **Description:** (4 Speed points): You move up to 1,000 feet (300 m) in one round. Action.

**Block** (115)
- **Available to:** Magus (T1), Ranger (T1), Rogue (T1)
- **Description:** (3 Speed points): You automatically block the next melee attack made against you within the next minute. Action to initiate.

**Blood Fever** (115)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T3)
- **Description:** When you have no points in one or two Pools, you gain an asset to attacks or defense rolls (your choice). Enabler.

**Brew Potion** *
- **Available to:** Druid (T1), Mage (T1), Priest (T1)
- **Description:** You know how to brew potions, oils, and elixirs. Creating potions requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

### C

**Call Dead Spirit** (117)
- **Available to:** Priest (T5)
- **Description:** (6 Intellect points): At your touch, the remains of a creature dead no longer than seven days appears as a manifest (and apparently physical) spirit, whose level is the same as it had in life. The raised spirit persists for up to a day (or less, if it accomplishes something important to it before then), after which it fades away and cannot return again. The raised spirit remembers everything it knew in life and possesses most of its previous abilities (though not necessarily its equipment). In addition, it gains the ability to become insubstantial as an action for up to a minute at a time. The raised spirit is not beholden to you, and it does not need to stay near you to remain manifest. Action to initiate.

**Call Otherworldly Spirit** (117)
- **Available to:** Priest (T6)
- **Description:** (6 Intellect points): You summon a spirit creature that manifests for up to a day (or less, if it accomplishes something important to it before then), after which it fades away and cannot be summoned again. The spirit is a creature of level 6 or lower, and it can be substantial or insubstantial as it wishes (using an action to change). The spirit is not beholden to you, and it does not need to stay near you to remain manifest. Action to initiate.

**Calm** (118)
- **Available to:** Bard (T4)
- **Description:** (3 Intellect points): Through jokes, song, or other art, you prevent a living foe from attacking anyone or anything for one round. Action.

**Calm Stranger** (118)
- **Available to:** Bard (T2)
- **Description:** (2+ Intellect points): You can cause one intelligent creature to remain calm as you speak. The creature doesn't need to speak your language, but it must be able to see you. It remains calm as long as you focus all your attention on it and it is not attacked or otherwise threatened. In addition to the normal options for using Effort, you can choose to use Effort to calm additional creatures allied with your initial target, one additional creature per level of Effort applied. Action.

**Cantrip** *
- **Available to:** Druid (T1), Mage (T1), Priest (T1)

You can cast these spells to perform minor magical effects as you choose, though the effects cannot harm or damage creatures or objects. This power includes the Light, Message and Hand spells. Additionally, it includes Prestidigitation for Mages, Thaumaturgy for Priests, and Druidcraft for Druids. Action.

**Capable Warrior** (118)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T4), Fighter (T4), Paladin (T4), Magus (T4), Ranger (T4)

**Captivate or Inspire** (118)
- **Available to:** Bard (T4)

Either your words keep the attention of all NPCs that hear them for as long as you speak, or your words inspire all NPCs that hear them to function as if they were one level higher for the next hour. In either case, you choose which NPCs are affected. If anyone in the crowd is attacked while you're trying to speak to them, you lose the crowd's attention. Action to initiate.

**Careful Aim** (118)

**Closed Mind** *

**Combat Casting** *
- **Available to:** Druid (T2), Mage (T2), Priest (T2)

**Combat Prowess** (120)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T1), Fighter (T1), Paladin (T1), Magus (T1)

**Communication** (121)
- **Available to:** Druid (T2), Ranger (T2)

**Confounding Banter** (121)
- **Available to:** Bard (T4)
- **Description:** (4 Intellect points): You spew a stream of nonsense to distract a foe within immediate range. On a successful Intellect roll, your defense roll against the creature's next attack before the end of the next round is eased. Action.

**Control the Field** (121)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T1), Fighter (T1), Paladin (T1), Magus (T1)
- **Description:** (1 Might point): This melee attack inflicts 1 less point of damage than normal, and regardless of whether you hit the target, you maneuver it into a position you desire within immediate range. Action.

**Control the Savage** (122)
- **Available to:** Druid (T6), Ranger (T6)
- **Description:** (6 Intellect points): You can control a calm nonhuman beast within 30 feet (9 m). You control it for as long as you focus all your attention on it, using your turn each round. The GM has final say over what counts as a nonhuman beast, but unless some kind of deception is at work, you should know whether you can affect a creature before you attempt to use this ability on it. Aliens, extradimensional entities, very intelligent creatures, and robots never count. Action.

**Controlled Fall** (122)
- **Available to:** Magus (T3), Ranger (T3), Rogue (T3)
- **Description:** When you fall while you are able to use actions and within reach of a vertical surface, you can attempt to slow your fall. Make a Speed roll with a difficulty of 1 for every 20 feet (6 m) you fall. On a success, you take half damage from the fall. If you reduce the difficulty to 0, you take no damage. Enabler.

**Craft Wand** *

**Create** (122)
- **Available to:** Magus (T5)
- **Description:** (7 Intellect points): You create something from nothing. You can create any item you choose that would ordinarily have a difficulty of 5 or lower (using the crafting rules). Once created, the item lasts for a number of hours equal to 6 minus the difficulty to create it. Thus, if you create a set of sturdy manacles (difficulty 5), it would last for one hour. Action.

**Critter Companion** (123)
- **Available to:** Mage (T1)
- **Description:** A level 1 creature accompanies you and follows your instructions. This creature is no larger than a large cat (about 20 pounds, or 9 kg) and is normally some sort of domesticated species. You and the GM must work out the details of your creature, and you'll probably make rolls for it in combat or when it takes actions. The critter companion acts on your turn. As a level 1 creature, it has a target number of 3 and 3 health, and it inflicts 1 point of damage. Its movement is based on its creature type (avian, swimmer, and so on). If your critter companion dies, you can search an urban or wild environment for 1d6 days to find a new one. Enabler.

**Crowd Control** (123)
- **Available to:** Bard (T6)

Your control is limited to simple verbal commands like "Stop," "Run away," "Follow that guard," "Look over there," or "Get out of my way." All affected creatures respond to the command unless you specifically command them otherwise. In addition to the normal options for using Effort, you can choose to use Effort to increase the maximum level of the targets or affect an additional five people. When the Crowd Control ability ends, the creatures remember your commands but don't remember being controlled—your commands seemed reasonable at the time. Action to initiate.

- **Description:** (1 Might point): When you use a bashing or bladed weapon in both hands and apply Effort on the attack, you get a free level of Effort on the damage. Enabler.

**Curious** (123)
- **Available to:** Ranger (T2), Rogue (T2)
- **Description:** You're always curious about your surroundings, even on a subconscious level. Whenever you use Effort to attempt navigation, perception, or initiative tasks in an area that you've only rarely or never visited before, you can apply an additional free level of Effort. Enabler.

### D

**Damage Dealer** (124)

**Danger Instinct** (124)

**Deadly Aim** (125)
- **Available to:** Barbarian (T3), Fighter (T3), Paladin (T3), Magus (T3), Ranger (T3)

Enabler.

**Demeanor of Command** (127)

**Dirty Fighter** (128)

**Disarming Strike** (129)

- **Description:** (4 Speed points): For one minute, you automatically deflect or dodge any ranged projectile attacks. However, on your next turn after you're attacked with ranged projectiles, all your other actions are hindered. Action to initiate.

**Divide Your Mind** (130)
