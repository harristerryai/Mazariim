# Mage (Wizard)

**Type:** Arcane Spellcaster  
**Role:** The Mage is the arcane spellcaster equivalent to the D&D Wizard, focusing on intelligence-based magic, spell preparation, and versatile spellcasting.

---

## Stat Pools

| Stat | Starting Value |
|------|----------------|
| **Might** | 7 |
| **Speed** | 9 |
| **Intellect** | 12 |

**Additional Points:** You get 6 additional points to divide among your stat pools as you wish.

---

## First Tier Mage

**Effort:** Your Effort is 1.

**Edge:** Your Intellect Edge is 1.

**Artifact Use:** You can attune to 3 artifacts.

**Weapon and Armor Proficiencies:** You are proficient in light weapons. You are not proficient in the use of armor. If you are not practiced with a certain type of armor but wear it anyway, the Speed penalties further increase by 1 and you cannot cast spells.

**Skills:** Choose three of the following skills; you are trained in those skills:
- Appraise
- Craft
- Insight
- Investigation
- Knowledge
- Language
- Navigation
- Perception
- Profession
- Research
- Spellcraft
- Vehicle

**Magical Training:** You are trained in the basics of magic. Your magical abilities are considered spells. You are practiced in Spellcraft. Enabler.

**Spellcasting:** Your character has learned how to cast additional spells using formulas. You require a spellbook. Spells require somatic components (one hand) and verbal components (Draconic language). Enabler.

**Spell Slots:** You have 4 spell slots. Pick 6 low-tier spells from the Mage spell list for your spellbook.

**Spellcasting Skills:** Choose one of the following skills you are untrained in; you are trained in this skill:
- Spell Focus (one school of magic, one damage type, or focus)
- Skill with Defense (Intellect)

### Special Abilities

Choose **Cantrip** along with three other abilities listed below.

- **Cantrip (1 Intellect point)***:
From your extensive study of magic, you have learned some minor spells called cantrips. You can cast these spells to perform minor magical effects as you choose, though the effects cannot harm or damage creatures or objects. This power includes the Light, Message and Hand spells. Additionally, it includes Prestidigitation for Mages, Thaumaturgy for Priests, and Druidcraft for Druids. Action.

- **Critter Companion**:
A level 1 creature accompanies you and follows your instructions. This creature is no larger than a large cat (about 20 pounds, or 9 kg) and is normally some sort of domesticated species. You and the GM must work out the details of your creature, and you'll probably make rolls for it in combat or when it takes actions. The critter companion acts on your turn. As a level 1 creature, it has a target number of 3 and 3 health, and it inflicts 1 point of damage. Its movement is based on its creature type (avian, swimmer, and so on). If your critter companion dies, you can search an urban or wild environment for 1d6 days to find a new one. Enabler.

- **Brew Potion***:
You know how to brew potions, oils, and elixirs. Creating potions requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.


- **Enlarge Spell***:
You can spend a level of Effort to increase the range of a spell one step (touch to immediate, immediate to short, short to long, long to very long). Enabler.

- **Extend Spell***:
You can spend a level of Effort to change the duration of a spell from one/ten minutes to one hour, one hour to eight hours, or eight hours to twenty-four hours. Enabler.

- **Knowledge Is Power**:
Choose two noncombat skills in which you are not trained. You are trained in those skills. Enabler.

- **Knowledge Skills***:
You are trained in two skills in which you are not already trained (Appraise, Animal Handling, Language, Knowledge, Research). Enabler.


- **Investigative Skills***:
You are trained in two skills in which you are not already trained (Insight, Investigation, Navigation, Perception, Survival). Enabler.


- **Monster Lore**:
Monster Lore: You are trained in identifying monsters and their weaknesses. Enabler.

- **Scribe Scroll***:
You know how to scribe spells onto scrolls. Inscribing scrolls requires time, gold, a place to work. Enabler.

- **Resonance Field (1 Intellect point)**:
Faint lines in a color you choose form a tracery over your entire body and emit faint light. The effect lasts for one minute. Whenever a creature within immediate range makes an attack against you, the pattern energizes to block the attack. You can make an Intellect defense roll in place of the defense roll you would normally make. If you do so and you get a minor effect, the creature attacking you takes 1 point of damage. If you get a major effect, the creature attacking you takes 4 points of damage. Action to initiate.

- **There's Your Problem**:
There's Your Problem: You are trained in tasks related to figuring out how to solve problems with multiple solutions. Enabler.

## Second Tier Mage

**Artifact Use:** You can attune to 4 artifacts.

**Spellcasting:** You gain 2 additional spell slots (total: 6). Pick 3 low-tier spells to add to your spellbook.

**Spellcasting Skills:** Choose one of the following skills you are untrained in; you are trained in this skill:
- Spell Focus (one school of magic, one damage type, or focus)
- Skill with Defense (Intellect)

### Special Abilities

Choose one of the following:

- **Battlecaster***:
When you spend a level of Effort on a spell attack, you gain a free level on damage. Enabler.

- **Closed Mind***:
You have +3 Armor against damage that selectively targets your Intellect Pool (which normally ignores Armor). Enabler.

- **Combat Casting***:
Your spellcasting concentration and your defensive casting rolls are eased one step. Enabler.

- **Craft Wondrous Item***:
You know how to create miscellaneous magical items. Enabler.

- **Enhanced Intellect**:
You gain 3 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

- **Quick Study**:
Quick Study: You learn from repetitive actions. You gain a +1 bonus to rolls for similar tasks after the first time (such as operating the same device, picking the same kind of lock, or disabling the same type of trap). Once you move on and do other things—usually after the passage of at least a few hours—you lose the familiarity with the task. Enabler.

- **Spell Penetration***:
If the target of your spell has modifications to Resist Magic, this ability negates one level of this protection. Enabler.

---

## Third Tier Mage

**Spellcasting:** You gain 2 additional spell slots (total: 8). Pick 3 low or mid-tier spells to add to your spellbook.

**Spellcasting Skills:** Choose one of the following skills you are untrained in; you are trained in this skill:
- Spell Focus (one school of magic, one damage type, or focus)
- Skill with Defense (Intellect)

### Special Abilities

Choose two of the following:

- **Applying Your Knowledge**:
You're trained in two areas of knowledge of your choice. Enabler.

- **Augment Summoning***:
When you cast a summoning spell and spend a level of Effort, the summons is one level higher, has +1 Armor, and has one more point of Health per level. Enabler.

- **Craft Arms and Armor***:
You know how to create magical weapons and armors. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). Enabler.

- **Craft Wand***:
You know how to create magical wands, items that cast spells but have limited charges. Enabler.

- **Enhanced Intellect**:
You gain 3 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

- **Natural Spell***:
You can spend a level of Effort to cast spells while in animal shape, polymorph, shapechange, or any other altered shape that disallows spellcasting. Enabler.

- **Spell Potency***:
Your spells gain a level of Potency, making them harder to dispel. When you spend a level of Effort on a spell attack, you gain an additional level of Potency. Enabler.

- **Spell Power***:
Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus. Your spells from this school do one additional point of damage. Enabler.

- **Subtle Spell***:
You can spend two levels of Effort to cast a spell without verbal or somatic components. Enabler.

## Fourth Tier Mage

**Artifact Use:** You can attune to 5 artifacts.

**Spellcasting:** You gain 2 additional spell slots (total: 10). Pick 3 low or mid-tier spells to add to your spellbook.

**Spellcasting Skills:** Choose one: Specialized in Greater Spell Focus or Mastery with Defense (Intellect); OR Trained in Spell Focus or Skill with Defense (Intellect)

**Martial Skills:** Choose one (untrained): Skill with Attacks or Skill with Defense

### Special Abilities

Choose one of the following:

- **Energy Substitution***:
You can spend a level of Effort to change the damage type of a spell. Types include fire, lightning, cold, acid, or thunder. Enabler.

- **Greater Spell Penetration***:
If the target of your spell has modifications to Resist Magic and you have the Spell Penetration ability, this ability negates two levels of this protection. Enabler.

- **Improved Companion**:
You gain a level 3 follower. They are not restricted on their modifications. You can take this ability multiple times, each time gaining another level 3 follower. Alternatively, you could choose to advance a level 2 follower you already have to level 3, or a level 3 follower to level 4. Enabler.

- **Improved Counterspell***:
When you cast dispel magic or other similar magic, you gain a free level of Effort. Enabler.

- **Knowledge Is Power**:
Choose two noncombat skills in which you are not trained. You are trained in those skills. Enabler.

- **Knowing the Unknown (6 Intellect points)**:
By accessing the resources appropriate to your character, you can ask the GM one question and get a general answer. The GM assigns a level to the question, so the more obscure the answer, the more difficult the task. Generally, knowledge that you could find by looking somewhere other than your current location is level 1, and obscure knowledge of the past is level 7. Action.

- **Pay It Forward (3 Intellect points)**:
You can pass on what you've learned. When you give another character a suggestion involving their next action that is not an attack, their action is eased for one minute. Action.

- **Widen Spell***:
You can spend two levels of Effort to double the area of a spell. Enabler.

## Fifth Tier Mage

**Spellcasting:** You gain 2 additional spell slots (total: 12). Pick 3 spells of any tier to add to your spellbook.

**Spellcasting Skills:** Choose one: Specialized in Greater Spell Focus or Mastery with Defense (Intellect); OR Trained in Spell Focus or Skill with Defense (Intellect)

**Martial Skills:** Choose one: Skill with Attacks, Skill with Defense, or Experienced in Armor

### Special Abilities

Choose two of the following:

- **Craft Rods and Staves***:
You know how to create magical rods, items of power that can cast spells without depletion. You know how to create magical staves, items of power that can add spells to a caster's spell list. Enabler.

- **Enhanced Intellect Edge**:
You gain +1 to your Intellect Edge. Enabler.

- **Greater Enhanced Intellect**:
You gain 6 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

- **Greater Spell Power***:
Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus you have Spell Power in. Your spells from this school do two additional points of damage. Enabler.

- **Learned a Few Things**:
You are trained in two areas of knowledge of your choice, or specialized in one area of knowledge of your choice. Enabler.

- **Resist Magic***:
You gain an asset in all types of defense actions to resist magical effects. Enabler.

---

## Sixth Tier Mage

**Artifact Use:** You can attune to 6 artifacts.

**Spellcasting:** You gain 2 additional spell slots (total: 14). Pick 3 spells of any tier to add to your spellbook.

**Spellcasting Skills:** Choose one: Specialized in Greater Spell Focus or Mastery with Defense (Intellect); OR Trained in Spell Focus or Skill with Defense (Intellect)

**Martial Skills:** Choose one: Specialized in Mastery with Attack or Mastery with Defense; OR Trained in Skill with Attacks, Skill with Defense, or Experienced in Armor

### Special Abilities

Choose one of the following:

- **Divide Your Mind (7 Intellect points)**:
You split your consciousness into two parts. For one minute, you can take two actions on each of your turns, but only one of them can be to use a special ability. Action.

- **Greater Enhanced Intellect**:
You gain 6 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

- **Greater Resist Magic***:
You gain two assets in all types of defense actions to resist magical effects. Enabler.

- **Knowledge Is Power**:
Choose two noncombat skills in which you are not trained. You are trained in those skills. Enabler.

- **Tower of Intellect**:
Tower of Intellect: You are trained in Intellect defense tasks. If you are already trained, you are specialized in those tasks instead. Enabler.

- **Read the Signs (4 Intellect points)**:
You examine an area and learn precise, useful details about the past (if any). You can ask the GM up to four questions about the immediate area. Action.

- **Usurp Artifact***:
Note: This ability appears to be a variant of 'Usurp Cypher' adapted for artifacts in the fantasy setting. Choose one artifact that you carry. The artifact must have an effect that is not instantaneous. You destroy the artifact and gain its power, which functions for you continuously. You can choose when to activate the power as normal. This works only once. You can't use this ability on the same type of artifact twice. Action to initiate.
