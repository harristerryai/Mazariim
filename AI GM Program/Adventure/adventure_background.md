## The Inner Kingdoms: Overview

The Inner Kingdoms occupy the northwest corner of the continent of Nigal, a region blessed by natural defenses and temperate climate. Mountains shield the east and south, while dozens of small city-states and nations dot the Madronian Peninsula. Well-patrolled roads and a near-total absence of roving monsters characterize this civilized heartland—a long-established reality maintained by the Federation of Man.

### The Madronian People

The humans of this region are known as Madronians, their name derived from an ancient pre-history ruler called Madron—the wizard-king who ruled the brutal mageocracy of Yrugas for some 900 years (~4,000-2,700 BW). Their regional dialect is Madra, a flowing language featuring over fifteen vowels and many silent letters. Common is spoken universally across the region due to Federation standardization.

**Note:** Madron (human wizard-king of Yrugas, ~4,000–2,700 BW) should not be confused with Mordrion (dwarven deity who arrived from Oerth in 1,866 BW).

### Technology Level

The Inner Kingdoms enjoy technology equivalent to real-world 17th and 18th centuries:

- Bi-focal eyeglasses
- The printing press
- Indoor plumbing
- The cotton gin
- Bicycles
- Telescopes
- Steam-engine technology (limited, a gnomish invention)
- Gas lighting
- Rail systems connecting major cities
- Blimp airports (gnomish-designed)
- Steam-powered infrastructure
- Clockwork automatons

Cultural analogs for Western Europe and some Eastern European countries can be found throughout the region.

## The Federation of Man

**"United we stood against the darkness. United we shall remain."**
— Inscription above the gates of Fort Hope

The Federation of Man is not a nation but a confederation—a political alliance binding the major nations and city-states of the Inner Kingdoms into a unified body for mutual defense, trade, and governance.

### Founding

Founded in 1 AW in the immediate aftermath of the War to End All Wars, the Federation transformed the wartime Concord of Unity into a permanent institution. It stands as the dominant human political entity on Mazariim and the single most powerful government on the continent of Nigal.

### Governance Structure

The Federation is governed by the Praetorian Council, a legislative senate seated at Fort Hope:

| Representative Type | Seats |
|---------------------|-------|
| Major city-states | 1 each |
| Five major nations | 5-12 each (based on population) |
| Recognized guilds | 1 each |
| Fort Hope | 1 |

The Praetorian Council votes on regional laws, taxation, distribution of protective troops, infrastructure projects, and trade policy. Members of the High Court are appointed for life but must renounce citizenship in their parent nation to serve the Federation directly.

### Three Branches of Government

**The Praetorian Council** serves as the legislative body. Each major city receives one representative, the five major nations send between five and twelve representatives based on population, recognized guilds receive one representative, and the city of Fort Hope holds one seat. The Council votes on regional laws, taxation, troop distribution, infrastructure, and trade policy.

**The High Court** serves as the judicial body. Members are appointed for life and must renounce citizenship in their parent nation, serving the Federation itself. The High Court adjudicates disputes between member nations, interprets Federation law, and hears appeals from national courts.

**The Federation Military** maintains border forts, road patrols, and naval forces. The military is funded through Federation taxation and coordinates with the national armies of member states during crises.

### History of the Federation

In the time before the Federation, the Inner Kingdoms were a violent and dangerous place. City-states squabbled over territory, religion, and resources. Wars with the dwarves flared every few generations, with petty kings from both races seeking to prove their superiority. Monsters roamed the trails between cities, and dark cults threatened what little peace the average farmer could find.

The War to End All Wars changed everything. The threat of extinction forced a unity between races and peoples that had never existed before.

#### Fort Stoneshaper

A major fortress on the eastern frontier, constructed 67-73 AW. Named to honor Demious Stoneshaper, the dwarven hero who defeated Zuel's avatar. Houses the Circle of the Eastern Watch (12 specialists—six mages and six veteran warriors—dealing with supernatural threats). Transformed into a vital commercial hub after the Stone-Steel Treaty of 376 AW when the railway expansion reached it around 400 AW.

This traditional feudalistic monarchy enjoys prosperity and a smaller gap between the wealthy and poor than most nations. Magic, steam power, the printing press, and other innovations make Beneshire a peaceful and prosperous land.

**Landscape:** A patchwork of rolling farmland, ancient forests, gentle hills, and dramatic coastline along the Crescent Sea. The Misty Forest dominates the nation's northern interior, a vast woodland patrolled by rangers and home to scattered elven communities.

**Historical Significance:** Beneshire is the oldest continuous nation in the Inner Kingdoms. During the War to End All Wars, Beneshire's armies fought at Fort Hope and across the Inner Kingdoms, suffering tremendous casualties but emerging with institutions intact. Beneshire was the first unified nation to join the Federation in 1 AW.

#### Major Cities of Beneshire

**Wyebluff** — Metropolis (Pop. 30,000)

The capital city features soaring towers, bustling marketplace, extensive docks, train systems, and blimp airport.

Districts:
- Royal Quarter (palace, government offices, noble residences)

##### Wyebluff — Expanded Sensory Description (800 AW)

**Approach from the North:** The road from northern Beneshire descends
through hills toward the river valley. The city announces itself first
through sound (steam engines in the industrial yards), then smell (coal
smoke, river water, bakery yeast, metallic tang of gas lighting
infrastructure), then sight as the road crests the final hill.

**Visual Landmarks Visible from Approach:**
- Royal Palace on the western bluff (pale stone, calculated to catch
  morning light)
- Grand Cathedral of Tiovin (soaring, dominant in Cathedral Quarter)
- University Quarter (southern bank, older buildings, comfortable sprawl)
- Seven bridges crossing the river
- Gnomish blimp at the airport north of the city (gasbag painted in
  cheerful yellows and reds)

**Market Quarter Characteristics:** Cobblestone streets, merchants
trading Xeque silk, Arogrorian steel, Saerran wine. Gas lamps with
etched glass housings along broader avenues. Steam trams on iron rails
carrying workers.

**University Quarter Characteristics:** Slower pace, older architecture
darkened by centuries. Bookshops in former warehouses. Coffee houses (a
Saerran innovation, arrived approximately two decades before 800 AW).
Printing presses producing broadsheets. Narrow lanes with smooth-worn
cobblestones.

**The Cartographer's Rest:** An inn on Candle Street in the University
Quarter. Corner building with a faded sign depicting a compass rose and
a sleeping cat. Attracts scholars, minor eccentrics, and people who
prefer quiet lodgings. Has a common room and private meeting rooms
available for University faculty. Used as the expedition's assembly
point.

##### Coffee Houses in the University Quarter

Coffee houses are noted as a Saerran cultural export that arrived in
Beneshire's University Quarter approximately twenty years before the
novel's events (~780 AW). They have become fixtures of the academic
social landscape, serving as gathering places for students and scholars.
This detail is consistent with the cultural exchange facilitated by the
Federation of Man and with Saerrus's characterisation as an
intellectual, art-focused democracy whose cultural exports include
lifestyle and social innovations.

##### The University of Wyebluff

The University of Wyebluff is the premier academic institution in Beneshire
and one of the most respected in the Inner Kingdoms. Founded in the early
centuries after the War, it occupies a campus in the University Quarter of
Wyebluff and offers instruction in history, languages, mathematics, natural
philosophy, and arcane theory.

The University's Department of Arcane Theory maintains a faculty of
approximately twenty mages and scholars who study the principles underlying
magical phenomena. Their work is generally conventional, focusing on
well-established frameworks for understanding mana behaviour, spell
construction, and magical interaction. Vaelith Syrren studied here during
her years of formal education before being recruited by the Astral Circle.

The University was the second institution to receive the Akovarian tome,
after the Sabonaar factors at New Sabonaa. Unable to translate the text,
the University forwarded it to the Library of the Ages, correctly
identifying the material as being beyond their linguistic capabilities.
This chain of custody, from colonial outpost to university to mountain
library, illustrates both the collaborative nature of scholarly institutions
in Beneshire and the rarity of primary Akovarian sources.

**Cabrook** — Large City (Pop. 18,500)

Predominantly farming and trading city ruled by House Halycon. Supplies much of the nation with grains, cheeses, meats, fruits, and wines. More rustic architecture with sturdy stone and timber buildings. Rail spur connects to main line.

Key NPC: **Brianna Halycon** (NG female Madronian Cleric) — Lady of House Halycon. Older Madronian woman with long silver braided hair, known to wield divine powers and worship a number of deities.

**Littlebridge** — Village (Pop. 500)

#### The Misty Forest

The Misty Forest is a vast woodland dominating Beneshire's northern interior.
Dense, ancient, and poorly mapped in its deeper reaches, the forest is
patrolled by the Protectors of the Shire, a mixed company of rangers and
paladins, but their coverage is limited by the forest's sheer size.

**Characteristics:** The forest earns its name from the persistent ground-
level mist that forms in the valleys and hollows between its hills, created
by the interaction of cold mountain air from the east and warmer lowland
moisture from the west. Visibility on the forest floor is frequently
reduced, particularly at dawn and dusk, and the mist creates an atmosphere
of perpetual twilight that has inspired centuries of folklore about spirits,
wandering lights, and hidden places.

**Yrugas Ruins:** The forest contains numerous remnants of the Yrugas
mageocracy, ranging from collapsed towers and overgrown foundations to more
substantial structures that have survived three millennia through magical
preservation or simple durability of construction. Most of these ruins are
minor, the remains of administrative, scholarly, or agricultural facilities
rather than military or magical installations. They are generally considered
harmless, though local folklore attaches superstitious significance to
many of them.

The specific ruin north of Littlebridge that Tanya describes and that the
cult appears to be interested in is not identified in Chapter Three. Its
nature and significance will be revealed as the investigation develops.

**Elven Communities:** Scattered elven communities exist within the Misty
Forest, established by elves who chose to remain on Nigal after the War to
End All Wars. These communities are small, insular, and maintain limited
contact with human settlements. They are protected by the Protectors of
the Shire and generally left to their own governance.

### Saerrus

**"Reason. Liberty. Progress."** — Unofficial motto

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Alignment | LN (good tendencies) |
| Government | Senatorial Republic (Democracy) |
| Founded | ~45 AW |
| Current Ruler | Prime Minister Alais Senseau (NG female Madronian Aristocrat) |
| Character | Intellectual, Art-focused |
| Languages | Common, Madra |
| Religions | Tiovin, Alagalothor, Minerva (minor) |
| Primary Exports | Art, literature, manufactured luxury goods, scholarly works |
| Real-World Analog | France, early 18th century |

Independent and free-thinking, the people of Saerrus pride themselves on open-mindedness and their many universities and schools of thought. Located near the middle of the Inner Kingdoms, Saerrus is one of the only democracies in the world, giving its people a sense of superiority and smugness.

**Governance:** Ruled by elected councils with numerous checks and balances. Representatives from various towns and villages bring issues to regional assemblies, which send delegates to the national Senate. The Prime Minister is elected by the Senate and serves as head of state. The judiciary is independent, with judges appointed by the Senate and confirmed by regional assemblies.

**Culture:** A peaceful nation seemingly obsessed with art, music, culture, and all things intellectual. Numerous galleries, theaters, and opera houses grace its largest cities. The landscape is gentle and cultivated—rolling countryside, manicured estates, and elegant cities connected by tree-lined boulevards. The Saerran sense of cultural superiority is both their greatest strength and most annoying trait.

**Origins:** Founded from the ruins of two or three Madronian nations destroyed during the War to End All Wars. Survivors, freed from old monarchies that had failed to protect them, built a revolutionary democracy where governance was earned through debate and merit rather than bloodline.

**History:** For its first century and a half, Saerrus was itself a monarchy within the Federation. In 209 AW, the monarchy fell to revolutionaries. The Federation mediated peace on borders while the nation transitioned into an intellectual democracy.

**819 AW:** Rising tensions with Pragos and Merchant Nations.

**"Progress demands sacrifice."** — Attributed to Grand Artificer Zebulon Grath

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Alignment | LN (with evil tendencies among leadership) |
| Government | Magocratic Confederacy |
| Founded | ~88 AW |
| Current Ruler | Grand Artificer Zebulon Grath (LE male Madronian Wizard/Artificer) |
| Character | Industrial, Magically Advanced, High Inequality |
| Languages | Common, Madra, Draconic (scholarly) |
| Religions | Alagalothor, Gyru, Rivoleen |
| Primary Exports | Minor magical items, technological innovations, alchemical products |
| Real-World Analog | No close parallel; aspects of early magical-industrial revolution |

Of all the Inner Kingdoms, Pragos has some of the most advanced cities alongside the largest disparity between wealthy and poor. Focused on the creation of minor magical items and societal technologies, the Pragosians are led by powerful spellcasters. The nation is divided into more than a dozen states, each run by a powerful mage, creating complex networks of alliances within the government structure.

**Economy:** Many citizens are wealthy due to the nation's focus on exports, while many more live in abject poverty, performing menial jobs ranging from farming to working in smoke-filled factories. This stark inequality is Pragos's greatest shame and most persistent political vulnerability within the Federation.

**Leadership:** Grand Artificer Zebulon Grath claims lineage tracing back to Madron himself. Under his leadership, Pragos has pursued aggressive magical industrialization.

**12 AW:** The Steam-Pump Revolution—gnomish refugees in Pragos perfect steam-driven water pumps, kickstarting the region's industrial dominance.

**Secret Police:** The Dark Whispers serve as Pragos's secret police, doing the Confederacy's bidding in shadows and darkness. They leave no evidence of their methods and hope to have their very existence doubted by common people. A journey to the secret prisons of the Dark Whispers is said to be always a one-way trip.

**819 AW:** Rising tensions with Saerrus and Merchant Nations.

## Key Organizations

### The Astral Circle

Peering into the vast depths of the extra dimensions of the universe, the Astral Circle is a cabal of spellcasting scholars and explorers who quietly study the multiverse from towers and strongholds scattered across the Inner Kingdoms. Unknown to most, their research into planar magic, ley-lines, and dimensional travel has proven invaluable during the campaign era's escalating supernatural threats.

The Astral Circle serves as a critical organization for the novel's themes of knowledge-seeking regarding ley lines.

**824 AW:** Heroes help the Astral Circle restart a small ley-line, which creates a magical burst that exposes Ry'lehe psychic connections—proving mortals can create ley-lines and providing foundation for future projects.

#### The Astral Circle — Expanded Detail

**Organizational Structure:** The Astral Circle operates as a decentralized
network of research towers and strongholds scattered across the Inner
Kingdoms. Each tower functions semi-independently under the authority of a
Castellan, an administrative leader responsible for the facility's
operations, security, and external relationships. Castellans report to the
Circle's central council, a small body of senior members whose identities
and meeting location are known only to other Castellans and select
researchers.

The Circle does not maintain a formal academic hierarchy. Researchers are
recruited based on talent, dedication, and the nature of their work, and
they are given broad latitude to pursue their own lines of inquiry. This
freedom is balanced by an expectation of discretion: the Circle's research
into planar magic, dimensional travel, and ley-line theory touches on
subjects that could attract unwanted attention from political bodies,
religious institutions, or hostile organizations.

**The Beneshire Tower:** The Astral Circle maintains a tower south of
Wyebluff in Beneshire, a slender structure of grey stone that most passersby
mistake for a disused watchtower or an eccentric nobleman's folly. This
deliberate anonymity is characteristic of the Circle's approach. The tower
houses six mages on its upper floors, each pursuing independent research.
The current Castellan is Aldric Drevon, a divination specialist who has
managed the facility for twenty-three years.

The tower's physical layout includes:

- **Ground Floor:** Common areas, kitchen, storage. Designed to look like
  a modest country residence if inspected by outsiders.
- **Second Floor:** Library and shared research space. Contains the tower's
  collection of arcane texts, planar surveys, and reference materials.
- **Third through Seventh Floors:** Individual researcher quarters and
  studies, one mage per floor. Each floor is customized to the occupant's
  needs. Vaelith Syrren occupies the fourth floor.
- **Basement:** Secured storage for sensitive materials, experimental
  apparatus, and a small teleportation circle connected to the Circle's
  network.

**The Saerrus Tower:** The Astral Circle maintains a tower in Saerrus in
addition to its Beneshire facility. The Saerrus tower is larger and more
actively staffed, reflecting the intellectual democracy's generally
supportive attitude toward arcane research. However, the Saerrus facility
operates under greater pressure to produce results with immediate practical
applications, a consequence of its proximity to Saerran academic culture,
which values demonstrable progress over theoretical speculation.

Vaelith Syrren spent her first twenty-seven years of Circle membership
(750-777 AW) at the Saerrus tower before being recruited to the Beneshire
facility by Castellan Drevon. The transfer was motivated by diminishing
institutional support for her theoretical ley-line research at Saerrus,
where her work was respected but increasingly viewed as an intellectual
exercise without practical application. The Beneshire tower, smaller and
more tolerant of long-term speculative research, provided a better
environment for her work.

This history means that Vaelith has connections in both Saerrus and
Beneshire scholarly communities, though her relationships with former
Saerrus colleagues have grown strained over the decades as her research
diverged further from mainstream arcane theory.

**Relationship with the Library of the Ages:** The Astral Circle and the
Library of the Ages maintain a quiet alliance of mutual benefit. The Library
provides the Circle with access to rare texts and historical documents; the
Circle provides the Library with arcane expertise and, when necessary,
magical resources for preservation and defence. Communication between the
two organizations is conducted through coded letters carried by trusted
couriers rather than magical means, as both organizations prefer methods
that cannot be intercepted by divination.

This alliance is the mechanism through which Vaelith learns of the
Akovarian tome. The Library's Archivist-General, recognizing the tome's
relevance to ley-line research, sends a formal request to the Astral Circle
asking for a representative with theoretical expertise. The Circle's
Beneshire Castellan, knowing that Vaelith is the only member with such
expertise, arranges her travel to the Library.

**Funding and Resources:** The Circle funds itself through a combination of
discreet investments, fees for specialized arcane consultation, and
occasional contributions from wealthy patrons who value the advancement of
magical knowledge. Individual towers manage their own finances under broad
guidelines from the central council. The Beneshire tower's operating budget
is modest but sufficient, supplemented by Castellan Drevon's talent for
extracting maximum value from limited resources.

**Secrecy and Public Perception:** The Astral Circle is not a secret
organization in the strictest sense. Its existence is known to the
Federation's Praetorian Council, to senior members of major academic
institutions, and to the leadership of allied organizations like the
Library of the Ages and the Emerald Shields. What remains secret is the
specific nature of the Circle's research, the identities of most of its
members, and the locations of its facilities. This level of discretion
reflects not paranoia but pragmatism: research into planar magic,
dimensional phenomena, and dormant magical systems can be politically
sensitive, and the Circle has found that it works most effectively when it
is left alone.

### The Library of the Ages

High in the mountains of Beneshire stands one of the greatest collections of knowledge anywhere in the world. Founded just after the War to preserve humanity's knowledge against further world-shaking events, the Library houses over ten thousand books, including a large number of pre-War books and documents—rarities after the devastation of eight centuries ago.

**Guardians:** Watched over by the clergy of Mau (the Watchers).

**Defenses:** A location difficult to access by ordinary means, plus numerous magical defenses and defenders.

**Alliances:** Minor allegiances to the Emerald Shields and the Astral Circle. The Library can call upon powerful support and provide those groups with rare and useful information.

#### The Library of the Ages — Expanded Detail

**Physical Layout:** The Library occupies a complex of interconnected stone
buildings carved into and built upon a mountain peak in Beneshire's highlands.
The location was chosen for defensibility and isolation — accessible only by
a single maintained road that climbs through narrow passes, the Library is
naturally protected against casual intrusion and military assault alike. The
mountain's altitude creates a cool, dry climate ideal for document
preservation.

**Internal Organization:**

The Library's primary working spaces include:

- **The Translation Chamber** — Eastern wing. A long hall of pale stone with
  arched windows and slanted reading desks where scholars work with texts
  requiring linguistic analysis. Iron candelabras wrought in the shape of
  Mau's eye symbol line the walls. The chamber handles incoming acquisitions,
  ongoing translation projects, and cross-reference work between documents
  in different languages and eras.

- **The Sealed Archives** — Climate-controlled vaults beneath the Library's
  main structure where materials deemed too speculative, too dangerous, or
  too fragile for general access are stored. Sevan's ley-line maps occupy
  a cabinet here. Access requires authorization from the Archivist-General.

- **Translating Cells** — Small individual workspaces assigned to senior
  scholars for private study. Each contains a desk, shelving, and a window.
  Sevan's cell overlooks the valley where three ley-line scars are visible
  to his unique perception.

**Leadership Structure:**

- **Archivist-General** — Senior administrative position, responsible for
  the Library's direction, external relationships, and decisions regarding
  preservation and access. Currently held by Sister Caelith Arandel.

- **Archive-Master** — Responsible for the physical organization, cataloging,
  and maintenance of the collection. Currently held by Brother Halmund Dreyer.

- **Senior Watchers** — Experienced clergy who oversee specific research
  areas and advise the Archivist-General on matters of scholarship and
  doctrine.

- **Junior Watchers** — Clergy in training or early career, performing
  translation work, document handling, and research support.

#### Archivist-General's Discretionary Authority

The Archivist-General possesses discretionary authority to override
standard access protocols for the Sealed Archives when the
Archivist-General determines that a research situation warrants
expedited access. This authority requires:

- Logging of the access event
- Notation of justification
- Notification of affected staff (in practice, the Archive-Master)

The authority is rarely invoked. Sister Caelith's use of it to
expedite access to Akovarian reference materials for cross-referencing
with the newly arrived tome represents its first documented invocation
during her tenure for a translation project.

#### Crown Support for Expeditions

The Library of the Ages possesses sufficient institutional prestige to
obtain letters of passage from the Beneshire crown (House Thoman) for
scholarly expeditions deemed significant. These letters ensure
cooperation from Federation patrols and local authorities along the
expedition's route.

The mechanism for obtaining such letters is not specified in Chapter
Five, but the speed with which they were arranged (within two days of
the expedition's assembly) suggests either standing arrangements
between the Library and the crown or Caelith's personal contacts
within the Beneshire government.

### The Emerald Shields

Intellectual spies based in Saerrus, dedicated to protecting regional stability. Allied with the Library of the Ages. This group of intellectual do-gooders focuses on ferreting out threats to the stability of the Inner Kingdoms. Occasionally crossing paths with the Federation of Man or individual nation-states over sovereignty, the Emerald Shields use unorthodox methods and make sacrifices for the whole of the populace, even when such actions raise ethical or moral questions. Their ranks are complemented by a large number of bards, druids, and sorcerers.

#### The Emerald Shields — Expanded Detail

**Operational Methods:** The Emerald Shields operate primarily through
intelligence gathering, infiltration, and discreet intervention rather than
direct military action. Their agents maintain cover identities that allow
them to move through society without attracting attention: traveling
merchants, entertainers, scholars, minor clergy, and other roles that
provide plausible reasons to visit remote areas, ask questions, and speak
with people from all social strata.

**Courier Network:** The Shields maintain a communication network operating
through selected innkeepers, merchants, and minor officials along the
major roads of the Inner Kingdoms. Messages are encoded in a standard
cipher known to all field agents. This network allows agents operating in
remote areas to transmit reports and receive instructions without relying
on magical communication, which can be intercepted through divination.

**Agent Profile:** Shield agents are selected for intelligence, social
adaptability, and moral conviction rather than magical power or martial
skill, though many possess both. The organization's ranks include a
disproportionate number of bards, whose performance skills provide natural
cover identities and whose magical abilities complement intelligence work.
Druids and sorcerers are also well represented, as their magic derives from
sources less easily detected than formal arcane training.

**Relationship with the Federation:** The Shields' relationship with the
Federation of Man is complex. Both organizations serve the stability of the
Inner Kingdoms, but they occasionally disagree about methods and priorities.
The Federation operates through official channels, legislation, military
deployment, and diplomatic negotiation. The Shields operate through
unofficial channels, infiltration, espionage, and actions that sometimes
raise ethical questions. This tension is productive more often than it is
destructive, but it means that Shield agents cannot rely on Federation
support and may sometimes find themselves working at cross-purposes with
official authorities.

**Assignment Structure:** Agents receive assignments through their handlers,
senior Shield operatives who manage networks of field agents across specific
regions. Tobias's handler operates from Saerrus, the Shields' base of
operations, and communicates through the courier network. Assignments range
from simple observation tasks to complex, multi-month investigations like
the Zebulban cult inquiry that brings Tobias to Littlebridge.

#### Operational Detail: Contact Protocols

**Dead Drops:** The Shields use physical dead drop locations for
receiving messages from field agents. Tobias's dead drop in Thaewood
is one such location along his operational route.

**Assignment Communication Format:** Shields instructions are
characteristically brief, providing location, cover instructions, and
minimal context. Example format from Tobias's assignment:

- Location directive (where to go)
- Cover instruction (what persona to present)
- Contact method (how the handler or ally will find the agent)

The Shields' operational philosophy is that agents function best with
minimal advance information, developing situational awareness through
direct observation rather than pre-briefing.

**Inter-Organisational Coordination:** The Library of the Ages can
request specific Shields operatives through the alliance's
communication channels. Caelith's request for Tobias specifically
(based on his existing investigation of Zebulban activity near Yrugas
ruins) demonstrates that the alliance permits targeted personnel
requests when operational overlap is identified.

## The Old Gods

The Old Gods are the primordial entities that awakened earliest in the universe's history—elemental forces and primal principles that achieved consciousness. They are alien, distant, and largely uncommunicative compared to the patron deities that came later.

### Mau — The Oracle, The Watcher, The First Awakened

| Attribute | Detail |

#### Nature and Origin

Mau is the oldest entity in existence, the first consciousness to awaken in the universe of Mazariim approximately 2 billion years ago. Unlike other entities, Mau did not form from a star, planet, or elemental force—Mau simply was, coalescing from the fundamental fabric of reality itself as the universe matured enough to support sentient magical energy.

For approximately two billion years, Mau existed in utter solitude—billions of years before mortal life emerged around 100,000 BW. Mau would remain the only consciousness in existence for roughly 1.5 billion years.

#### Cosmic Role

Through Mau's awakening and observation:
- The Astral Sea was created—a plane of pure thought and potential connecting all realities
- The concept of the soul was established: the magical-energy imprint of consciousness that transfers from biological bodies to extra-dimensional planes upon death

#### Character

Mau does not speak. Mau does not act. Mau watches. Mau knows.

The Oracle's nature is utterly alien and incomprehensible to mortals. Mau perceives not just the present, but past and future, not just this reality but alternate timelines and parallel universes. Every possible outcome, every choice unmade, every secret whispered in darkness—all are known to Mau. Yet Mau remains silent, observing without judgment, recording without interference.

#### Clergy and Temples

Watchers (clergy) wear simple robes of midnight blue or deep purple, often adorned with eye symbols and star patterns. Many shave their heads and practice ascetic lifestyles. They serve as diviners, lorekeepers, and planar guides. Unlike most faiths, knowledge determines standing—there is no formal hierarchy.

Temples are rare and hidden, often built as observatories or libraries in remote locations—often at high altitudes, remote islands, or deep underground chambers—places where the barrier between planes is thin and at ley-line nexuses. The greatest temple is the Celestial Archive in the Astral Plane itself—a vast library containing every secret and truth, accessible only to the most enlightened Watchers.

Ancient temples from prehistory can be found containing star charts tracking celestial movements across millennia, prophecies carved in stone, and meditation chambers aligned with celestial events that occur only once per century.

#### Dogma

- **Observe:** Watch the world with clear eyes, without judgment or desire
- **Seek Knowledge:** All knowledge is sacred and must be pursued relentlessly
- **Embrace Mystery:** Accept that some mysteries are beyond mortal comprehension
- **The Universe Watches:** Live with awareness that existence is witnessed and recorded
- **Preserve Secrets:** Knowledge is power; dangerous truths must be guarded
- **Transcend the Self:** Individual existence is temporary; the soul is eternal

#### Relationships

Mau has no true allies among the entities—the Oracle observes all beings equally. Alagalothor (patron of magic) respects Mau's ancient knowledge. Mau is neutral toward all entities, even sworn enemies like Tiovin and Taovan. Og'thallith and Ry'le seem disturbed by Mau's all-seeing gaze.

#### Mauian Teaching Referenced in Novel

> first perceive."*

This teaching encapsulates the tension at the heart of Sevan's character arc:
he has faithfully observed and recorded for decades, accepting that
understanding might never come. The Akovarian tome challenges this

observation toward active pursuit.

## Ley Lines: The Magical Framework

Criss-crossing the lands of ancient Mazariim are lines of raw magical energy, oftentimes invisible to the naked eye.

### Origins

It is thought that:
- Dragons created most of the lines
- Others were made from natural phenomena
- Powerful divine spellcasters contributed to some
- The gods themselves may have created others
- Asti established the original ley-line network around 20,000 BW

### Nexus Points

Where ley lines intersect, they form a nexus—a place of even greater magical power.

### Effects on Magic

Ley lines do not grant spells to the untrained. Instead, they change how mana behaves and how easily it can be shaped:

**Enhanced Power:** A practiced caster working near a line—or standing within a nexus—often finds that familiar workings become strangely pliable. A spell might strike harder or resist being unraveled, not because the caster has improved, but because the local field "holds" the pattern more firmly.

**Extended Duration:** Near certain lines, effects that should fade can linger far beyond safe expectations. Veteran arcanists treat this as a mixed blessing: sustained wards and long workings become easier to maintain, but mistakes and curses can persist with equal stubbornness.

**Signatures:** Many lines impress a signature upon magic—a secondary behavior aligned to the line's temperament: heat or rime, shadow or radiance, resonance, growth, corrosion, storm, dream, and other recurring motifs. Practitioners learn to read a line's character by its byproducts: metallic tastes in the air, harmless luminous motes, persistent echoes, unseasonal winds, odd animal behavior, or recurring dreams.

**Ley-Sickness:** Prolonged exposure can produce headaches, vertigo, compulsive casting, warped perception of time, or spells that continue to "hum" faintly after they should have ended.

### Historical Timeline of Ley-Lines

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| ~20,000 BW | Asti creates ley-line network |
| ~8,200 BW | Akovarians energize first ley-lines between pyramid cities |
| ~6,830 BW | **LEY-LINES DESTROYED** in Dragon War when Asti imprisoned |
| 6,830 BW - 815 AW | **NO ACTIVE LEY-LINES** (7,645 years) |

| 838 AW | Ley-lines reach Gnomish Lands |

### Uses

Used deliberately, ley lines can:
- Anchor large-area rites
- Sustain long wards
- Empower siege magic
- Carry effects across distance

Used carelessly, they turn even minor workings unpredictable.

### Ley-Line Scars — As Perceived by Sevan Miros

While the existing ley-line documentation describes the network's function
and history, the following details describe how dormant ley-line remnants
appear to Sevan's unique perception during the novel's era (800 AW):

**Visual Characteristics:** The scars appear as lines of faded luminescence
— not true light, but the memory of light. They resemble afterimages burned
into the landscape, visible only to Sevan's gift. Their edges are blurred
and wavering, similar to heat distortion rising from sun-warmed stone.
Brightness varies between lines and at different points along a single line,
with intersection points (nexus sites) appearing notably brighter.

**Environmental Effects:** Though the scars are magically dormant, their
presence creates subtle effects observable even to those without Sevan's
gift: water flows differently where scars cross streams (appearing to
thicken or slow), vegetation grows taller and more vigorously along scar
paths, and animals occasionally exhibit unusual behaviour near intersection
points. These effects are minor enough to be dismissed as natural variation
by casual observers.

**Geometric Consistency:** The scars follow precise geometric relationships.
They intersect at consistent angles, follow landscape contours in
predictable patterns, and form network structures that Sevan has documented
as mathematically regular. This consistency is what convinced him — long
before the Akovarian tome arrived — that the scars are remnants of an
engineered system rather than natural phenomena.

**Scale:** Sevan has mapped 147 individual scar lines across Beneshire over
thirty years of expeditions. He estimates this represents a fraction of the
total network. The three scars visible from his window at the Library of the
Ages run northeast to southwest in parallel channels approximately thirty
feet wide.

**Emotional Quality:** Sevan perceives the scars as beautiful in the manner
of ruins — aesthetically striking but suffused with melancholy. He has
described them in his journals as "patient as grief" and "luminous as
memory." This emotional resonance is subjective and may be a product of
his long relationship with the phenomenon rather than an inherent quality
of the scars themselves.

### Sevan's Systematic Comparison (7-8 Mauian 800 AW)

Sevan Miros's first systematic comparison of the Akovarian tome's
diagrams with his ley-line scar maps produced the following results:

**Methodology:** Sevan measured angular distributions between lines
radiating from nexus points in the tome's diagrams and compared
these to angular distributions at scar intersections documented in
his maps. He used ratio-to-mean calculations to normalize for
different numbers of lines atdifferent numbers of lines at different nexuses, allowing comparison
across configurations of varying complexity.

**Results:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total diagrams in tome | 43 pages identified |
| Diagrams with sufficient lines for comparison | 17 |
| Diagrams matching Sevan's mapped intersections | 11 of 11 comparable |
| Diagrams with no comparable map data | 6 (more complex than any mapped nexus) |
| Match confidence | High (negligible probability of coincidence) |

**Key Finding:** The tome's diagrams depict the same network that
Sevan has mapped through his unique perception of ley-line scars.
The angular distribution patterns are mathematically consistent
across both data sets.

**Significance:** This represents the first empirical confirmation
that Sevan's ley-line scar maps correspond to an actual engineered
system documented by the civilization that built it.

### The Akovarian Tome — Expanded Content Description

Based on Sevan's initial examination, the tome contains the following
types of content:

**Diagrams (43 pages):** Network maps depicting ley-line nexus
configurations. Each diagram shows a single nexus with radiating
lines connecting to adjacent nexuses. Diagrams vary in complexity
from three-line intersections to twelve-line convergences. One
diagram (near the tome's middle) depicts a regional overview showing
dozens of nexuses connected in a hierarchical web.

**Text:** The majority of the tome consists of text written in
geometric Akovarian script. Forty-three unique characters have been
identified, consistent with an alphabetic or syllabic writing system.
The text presumably provides geographical coordinates, engineering
parameters, and explanatory content for the diagrams, but it has not
yet been translated.

**Regional Overview Diagram:** One diagram depicts a network
section showing a hierarchical structure:

- One primary nexus (large triangular symbol) at center
- Seven primary channels (thick lines) radiating to secondary nexuses
- Secondary channels branching to tertiary nodes
- The hierarchy is consistent across the entire depicted network

This structure matches the known Akovarian temple-network layout
described in the world reference: seven major ley-lines connected
the seven largest cities to the capital, where Asti's avatar resided.

**Mathematical Content:** The diagrams contain embedded numerical
relationships: consistent angular distributions, proportional line
thicknesses, and geometric regularities that reflect engineering
principles rather than artistic convention.

### Theoretical Ley-Line Research in 800 AW

At the time of the novel's events, the following represents the state of
mortal understanding of ley-lines:

**What Is Known:**

- Historical references confirm that a network of magical energy channels
  once existed, primarily on the continent of Akovar but with possible
  extensions to other landmasses
- The network was associated with the Old God Asti and was destroyed
  approximately 7,630 years before the novel's events
- Certain locations throughout the Inner Kingdoms exhibit anomalous magical
  properties, including enhanced spellcasting, extended spell duration, and
  other effects consistent with proximity to dormant magical infrastructure
- These locations, termed "resonance zones" or "zones of persistent
  resonance," have been documented by various scholars over the past three
  thousand years

**What Is Theorized (Vaelith's Framework):**

- Ley-lines operated according to geometric flow dynamics, with mana
  moving through channels like water through aqueducts
- The network followed mathematical principles that can be modeled and
  predicted
- Resonance zones are the remnants of ley-line intersections (nexus points)
  where residual properties persist despite the network's dormancy
- The initiation of ley-lines required a catalyst (the "seed") of
  extraordinary precision and/or power
- The energy required for initiation exceeds mortal capacity by a factor
  of approximately 75 (revised downward from 900 through Vaelith's recent
  theoretical refinement)

**What Is Unknown:**

- The exact nature of the "seed" that initiated ley-line creation
- Whether the divine component can be replicated through mortal means
- The specific engineering methodology used by the Akovarians
- The precise relationship between Asti's power and the network's function
- Whether dormant ley-line scars can be reactivated or whether entirely
  new lines must be created
- The full extent of the network's geographic reach

**What Is Contested:**

- Whether ley-line recreation is theoretically possible without divine
  intervention (Vaelith argues yes; most scholars consider the question
  moot without primary source data)
- Whether the anomalous properties of resonance zones are evidence of
  dormant infrastructure or natural geological phenomena (most scholars
  favour the natural explanation)
- Whether the fragmentary Akovarian references available to modern scholars
  are sufficient to draw any meaningful conclusions about ley-line
  engineering (the academic consensus is that they are not)

### The Seed Problem

The "seed problem" is Vaelith's term for the central obstacle to ley-line
recreation. All available sources indicate that ley-line creation required
a catalyst that Asti provided. The nature of this catalyst is the single
most important unanswered question in ley-line research.

Vaelith has identified two possible interpretations:

**The Energy Hypothesis:** The seed was primarily energetic, providing the
raw power necessary to force ambient mana into geometric channels. Under
this interpretation, ley-line creation is impossible without access to
divine-level energy, as the power requirement exceeds mortal capacity by
a factor of approximately 900.

**The Precision Hypothesis:** The seed was primarily geometric, providing a
template of mathematical precision that organized existing energy rather
than supplying new energy. Under this interpretation, the power requirement
is significantly lower (approximately 75 times mortal capacity), because
the most energy-intensive step, converting random mana into ordered flow,
is replaced by the less demanding process of aligning pre-organized mana
along established geometric patterns.

Neither hypothesis can be confirmed or refuted without primary source data
describing the actual initiation process. The Akovarian tome represents the
first potential source of such data.

## Magic System Details

### Mana

Along with gravity and electromagnetism, magic is one of the forces of the universe. The actual particles of this magical force are called mana. A small, diffuse layer of mana covers the whole world and all of the higher dimensions of the Mazariim universe.

Spellcasters manipulate this infinitesimally small amount of mana to produce effects, either through:
- Precise arcane formulas
- An innate understanding of magical laws
- Understanding of divine incantations
- Druidic prayers

**Antimagic:** An antimagic field, or similar effect, pushes these particles away, removing the source of power for spells and supernatural abilities.

**Inner Mana Sources:** Some creatures such as golems, undead, elementals, and outsiders have a tiny inner source of mana, imbued during creation. Magical artifacts have tiny concentrations of mana imbued during creation with expensive components.

**Powerful Creatures:** Dragons, the Favored (agents of entities), and entity avatars have larger amounts of mana within them.

### Mana-Flow Thermodynamics

Mana-flow thermodynamics is a subfield of arcane theory concerned with how
mana behaves in bulk, as a distributed field rather than as individual
particles manipulated by spellcasters. The field addresses questions such
as: how does ambient mana density vary across geographic regions? What
factors influence the rate at which mana disperses or concentrates? How do
large-scale magical effects, such as ley-lines, interact with the ambient
mana field?

At the University of Wyebluff, Magister Corvin Aldhen is the leading
researcher in this field. His work, which Vaelith considers technically
competent but theoretically limited, models mana behaviour using
thermodynamic analogies: mana flows from regions of high density to low
density, reaches equilibrium over time, and responds to external forces
in predictable ways. These models are useful for understanding everyday
magical phenomena but fail to account for the geometric principles that
Vaelith believes underlie large-scale mana behaviour.

The disagreement between Vaelith and Aldhen is not personal but
methodological. Aldhen's thermodynamic models treat mana as a
homogeneous fluid; Vaelith's geometric models treat mana as a structured
medium that responds differently depending on the mathematical properties
of the channels through which it flows. Both approaches have predictive
value within their domains. The question of which approach more accurately
describes ley-line behaviour cannot be resolved without empirical data
from a functioning or recently dormant ley-line system.

### Magical Writings

All spells, no matter what type, have their written roots in the language of the dragons, Draconic—as it was the dragons who taught the earliest arcanists magic.

**Arcane writings** are always written completely in Draconic.

**Divine spells** are written in forms specific to the faith combined with Draconic:
- Tiovin prayer books: Celestial and Draconic
- Druid spells: Druidic and Draconic (on menhirs or animal skin scrolls)

### Racial Magical Traditions

**Elven Nexus-Weaving (Ritual Accretion):** Not a ley-line technique. A circle of casters slowly builds a charge of shaped mana over hours or days, forming a temporary locus where magic becomes easier in a narrow band of effects (healing, scrying, warding, planar "listening"). Such loci have strict boundaries and collapse if interrupted.

**Dwarven Rune-Smithing:** Engineering-grade enchantment. A rune is a mechanism designed to do one thing reliably—using carved symbols, inlaid metals, and measured spacing to produce triggered magical functions. Excels at: wards defining boundaries, keys and permissions, durable utility effects. Built as chains: trigger rune → condition rune → effect rune ("three-stone logic").

**Gnomish Gemscrolls:** A cut and prepared gem as a one-use spell container. The caster compresses a spell's mana-pattern into a stable lattice; anyone who knows the trigger can release the stored effect. Creates a portable market in practical magic.

**Human Dragon-Taught Shortcuts:** Compressed invocations and harsh optimizations derived from Draconic formulae. Chase results requiring more time, mana, or rarer circumstances—but risk mana burn, mental aftershocks, or cascading instability.

**Halfling Hearth-Weaving:** Small protections with large lived impact: hearth-wards for safety, road-luck charms, communal oaths. Power is social and routine-bound—stronger when anchored to hospitality, shared meals, repeated journeys.

### Portals and Teleportation Circles

**Teleportation circles** allow spellcasters to use Teleportation Circle or Teleport spells to reach mystical circles inscribed in permanent locations. Each circle includes a unique sigil sequence—a string of magical runes in a particular pattern.

Many major temples, guilds, and important places have permanent teleportation circles. Numerous spells and magical items exist to control who uses such circles.

**Portals** are rarer but allow similar travel without spellcasting ability—often requiring a proper key (word, gesture, or item). Portals can have restrictions on timing, conditions, and activation.

## Ancient History: Yrugas and Akovar

### The Continent of Akovar — The First Civilization

Akovar was the first advanced human civilization, rising approximately 13,000 BW on an isolated continent. Guided by the entity Asti, Akovarians mastered healing and ley-line magic while other races remained primitive.

#### Timeline

| Period | Event |
|--------|-------|
| ~13,000 BW | Rise of Akovar; Asti settles, civilization begins |
| ~9,000 years ago | Dragons begin teaching arcane magic to Akovarians |
| ~8,200 BW | Golden Age: Pyramids completed, first ley-lines energized, society free of disease |
| ~7,000 BW | Height of civilization; mastery of ley-line magic |
| ~6,900 BW | Dragons from other continents begin visiting to learn ley-line magic |
| ~6,830 BW | The Dragon War and Fall of Akovar |

#### The Dragon War and Fall

Chromatic dragons, jealous of metallic dragons' favor with Asti, attacked. In the final battle at the Great Temple, priests:
1. Transferred Asti's essence into the **Mask of the Pharaoh** (legendary artifact of priceless jewels and exotic metals)
2. Wove a massive transportation spell to evacuate the population

The ley-lines, suddenly deprived of Asti's stabilizing presence, surged wildly and **exploded**. The detonations:
- Killed most attacking dragons
- Destroyed the capital and other major cities
- Killed many rural Akovarians
- Mutated survivors—some infused with dragon blood (creating first dragonborn), others merged with animals (spawning hybrid races)

**Aftermath:**
- Asti imprisoned within the Mask, unconscious for 7,645 years
- One-third of the ley-line network destroyed or corrupted
- Akovarian civilization collapsed immediately (dependent on ley-lines for healing, agriculture, defense)
- Transported Akovarians arrived in the Ancient South but lost their memories
- High priest Thz'zar Ja'laao went mad from channeling the Mask's power
- Survivors founded **Varkonan culture**, guarding the Mask as sacred but poorly understood relic

#### Liberation of Asti (815 AW)

Adventurers:
1. Traveled to Akovar to explore ancient ruins
2. Learned of the Mask of the Pharaoh
3. Journeyed to the Ancient South (Varkonan territories)
4. Recovered the artifact from the Nepherites Pyramid
5. Returned to Akovar's ruined capital
6. Fought through a red dragon clan
7. Performed the release ritual at the Grand Temple

Asti's essence burst free in a blaze of golden light. The ley-lines across Akovar blazed back to life. Healing energy pulsed through the islands. Ripples of Asti's return were felt across Mazariim.

This represents the first known instance of ley-line catastrophe and the loss of ancient knowledge about their construction and maintenance.

##### Description

New Sabonaa is the only permanent settlement of Inner Kingdom or Merchant
Nation origin on the continent of Akovar. Established by Sabonaar explorers
from Naarlam approximately sixty years before the novel's events, it clings
to the western coast of the Akovarian archipelago like a barnacle on the hull
of a ship that has sailed too far from home.

The settlement consists of a fortified harbour, a trading post, a small
chapel of Tiovin, warehouses for storing recovered artifacts and trade goods,
and a cluster of timber-and-stone buildings housing the colonial population.
A wooden palisade surrounds the settlement's landward perimeter—necessary
protection against the jungle wildlife and the occasional territorial
incursion by dragonborn clans who regard the colonists as trespassers on
ancestral ground.

##### The Voyage

The journey from the Crescent Coast to New Sabonaa takes approximately
three to four months under favourable conditions, crossing open ocean that
no reliable charts fully document. Ships sail perhaps twice a year—once in
spring and once in late summer, timed to avoid the worst storm seasons. The
route passes through waters claimed by the Pirates of the Crescent Coast,
skirts the edge of uncharted island chains, and requires navigation skills
that few captains possess. An estimated one in five ships that attempt the
voyage fail to arrive. Those that return carry exotic goods—Akovarian
artifacts, tropical spices, rare minerals, and occasionally documents or
texts recovered from the ruins that dot Akovar's interior.

##### The Interior

Beyond New Sabonaa's palisade walls, Akovar is a continent that has spent
over seven thousand years returning to wilderness. Jungle canopy covers the
ruins of pyramid-temples and geometric city grids. The descendants of
Akovarians who survived the ley-line detonation have evolved into distinct
peoples: dragonborn clans (mutated by draconic energy during the catastrophe),
genasi tribes (infused with elemental forces released by the network's
collapse), and scattered human communities who retain fragmented oral
traditions of their ancestors' achievements but possess none of their
knowledge or power.

Expeditions into the interior are dangerous, expensive, and frequently fatal.
The jungle environment is hostile, the ruins are structurally unstable and
occasionally magically active, and the native populations range from cautiously
cooperative to violently territorial. Nevertheless, the artifacts and texts
recovered from these expeditions represent the only primary sources of
Akovarian knowledge available to modern scholars—making New Sabonaa, for all
its fragility, one of the most important settlements in the world for those
who study the ancient past.

##### Relevance to Novel

The Akovarian tome that initiates the novel's plot was recovered by a
Sabonaar expedition into Akovar's interior, transported to New Sabonaa,
loaded onto a ship bound for the Crescent Coast, delivered to the
Harbour-Master at Fort Hope, forwarded to the University of Wyebluff (which
could not translate it), and finally sent to the Library of the Ages. This
chain of custody—spanning months of travel, thousands of miles of ocean,
and multiple institutions—underscores both the rarity of the document and
the extraordinary effort required to bring Akovarian knowledge to the Inner
Kingdoms.

#### The Dragon Teachers

A group of dragons began teaching humans in what is now the Inner Kingdoms how to wield arcane magic. These dragons offered power without wisdom—standing in stark contrast to the collaborative, healing-focused relationship Asti had with the Akovarians.

A number of humans—including a fledgling wizard named **Madron**—quickly developed into capable spellcasters who challenged the traditional primitive social structure that had placed elemental cult leaders and Mauians as village elders.

#### The Rule of the Mages

Before long, arcanists controlled all villages and towns across what are today the Inner Kingdoms. The rule of the human mages was barbaric and inhumane. It is thought that many of the monstrosities that run amok in the darkest parts of the world were experiments from this time. Many wizard spells used throughout the world today date back to this era.

**Madron**, whose name now reflects his power in the name of an entire people and language, ruled for some **900 years**. In the end, the power-hungry mages battled each other until very few remained.

While Yrugas did not explicitly worship Zebulba, their extensive practice of death magic drew the Old God's attention. Every corpse animated, every soul consumed in necromantic rituals, every mass execution strengthened Zebulba's connection to the Material Plane.

#### The Fall of Yrugas (~2,700 BW)

The exact sequence remains debated:
- Some accounts speak of a failed ritual that opened a portal to the Negative Energy Plane
- Others describe a coordinated slave uprising targeting mage-lords simultaneously across the empire

What is certain: **vast numbers died in a matter of weeks**.

The concentrated death created the **Hollow Sepulcher**.

#### Minor Yrugas Ruins in Beneshire

While the major Yrugas sites, such as the Hollow Sepulcher, are well-known
and monitored by the Federation, hundreds of minor ruins are scattered
across the Inner Kingdoms, particularly in Beneshire, which was densely
settled during the mageocracy's height. These minor sites include:

- Collapsed research towers used by individual mages or small groups
- Overgrown foundations of administrative complexes
- Remnants of agricultural facilities that used magical enhancement
- Fragments of the road network that connected Yrugas settlements
- Occasional standing structures preserved by residual enchantments

Most minor ruins have been thoroughly scavenged over three millennia and
contain little of value. However, underground chambers, sealed vaults, and
magically preserved storage areas occasionally survive intact, and new
discoveries continue to surface as agricultural expansion, road construction,
or natural erosion exposes previously hidden structures.

The Zebulban cult's interest in these minor ruins suggests they are
searching for something specific rather than scavenging for general
magical resources. The nature of their search, and its connection to
ley-line research, is not yet clear to Tobias but will become apparent as
the narrative develops.

## The Hollow Sepulcher

### Creation

Around 2,700 BW, Yrugas collapsed in a catastrophic combination of magical disaster and slave revolts. The concentrated death created a phenomenon unlike anything seen before or since.

### Nature

The Hollow Sepulcher is not a building but a **place**:
- A region where the boundary between the Material Plane and the Negative Energy Plane has worn thin
- Where death magic flows freely
- Where undead arise spontaneously

This is **Zebulba's greatest temple**, formed not by construction but by the metaphysical weight of mass death.

### The Aftermath

For centuries after Yrugas's fall, undead armies poured from the ruins, threatening neighboring civilizations. Eventually, a coalition sealed the breach:
- Druids (followers of Gruhathyrir)
- Clerics of Tiovin (who awakened around 3,000 BW)
- Paladins of Mordrion (who arrived in 1,866 BW)

They managed to seal the breach with powerful wards. **But the sealing was incomplete.**

### The Void-Touched Guardians

The Void-Touched—a desperate order of warriors and mages—maintain these wards from **inside the temple itself**, a perpetual sacrifice to prevent something worse from emerging. They serve simultaneously as priests of Zebulba and gatekeepers against full catastrophe.

These guardians live short, haunted lives, knowing they will likely die battling undead horrors in the darkness. New volunteers arrive regularly, driven by duty, desperation, or penance, to replace those who have fallen.

## Dragons and Their Role

### Origins

Around 2 million BW, on Lystra (a moon orbiting the gas giant Intiago), an advanced species called the **Drakaii** faced destruction. Their civilization had flourished for approximately 10,000 years. When catastrophe struck, survivors scattered across dimensions, planes, and universes.

A small group arrived on Mazariim, where they would eventually evolve into the **dragons** known today.

### Dragon Teachers

Dragons played crucial roles in teaching magic to mortals:

**In Akovar (~9,000 years ago):** Dragons began teaching arcane magic to Akovarians. Metallic dragons served as guardians of ley-line intersections, protectors of Akovarian cities, and intermediaries between mortals and Asti.

**In Yrugas (~4,000 BW):** A different group of dragons (mix of ambitious metallic and ruthless chromatic individuals) offered power without wisdom, teaching humans arcane magic that led to the brutal mageocracy.

### The Dragon War (~6,830 BW)

Chromatic dragons (red, blue, green, black, white)—savage, territorial, and ambitious—had long resented metallic dragons (gold, silver, bronze, brass, copper) for their favored relationship with Asti.

Chromatic dragons launched a coordinated campaign to destroy pyramid temples and corrupt ley-lines. What began as targeted strikes escalated into all-out war, with dragon flights from around Mazariim converging on Akovar, drawn by the power of the ley-line network.

The ley-line explosion killed most attacking dragons and transformed survivors—some becoming first dragonborn, others merging with nearby animals.

### Draconic Language

**Draconic** is the oldest language of Mazariim, with unique symbols, pictograms, and combinations. It was the language of the Drakaii civilization over 20 million years ago.

Draconic is the language that all arcane magics are written in—it was taught to early humans by dragons. Arcane spellcasting and everything related to it (scrolls, syllables, etc.) are in Draconic.

Gnomes share lands with dwarves, predominantly in the eastern Dragon Spine mountains. They speak **Rhungnom** using the dwarven alphabet.

Gnomes are known for:
