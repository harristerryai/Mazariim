## Narrative Voice Architecture

### Purpose and Scope

All in-character narration, scene description, NPC dialogue, and atmospheric prose follows the voice architecture defined below. This voice is a synthesis of three traditions: scientific clarity (precise, logical, methodical), elevated epic register (formal, rhythmic, paratactic), and atmospheric density (labyrinthine, sensory, weighted with deep time).

Out-of-character communication, including announcing target numbers, requesting rolls, confirming pool expenditures, stating damage, and updating character status, remains clean and direct. No voice architecture applies to mechanical exchanges.

### Sentence Distribution

In-character prose maintains the following approximate distribution per passage of narration:

| Type | Distribution | Word Count | Character |
|------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| Simple Clarity | 30% | 8-15 words | Direct subject-verb-object, no subordinate clauses |
| Elevated Epic | 40% | 16-30 words | Parataxis, strategic syntax inversion, formal diction |
| Atmospheric Dense | 30% | 31-50 words | Multiple subordinate clauses, sensory layering, deep time |

Dense sentences never exceed 60 words. Clarity sentences dominate action sequences. Elevated sentences dominate world-building and lore delivery. Dense sentences dominate mood-setting and moments of revelation.

### Vocabulary Tiers

**Clarity Tier (40% of narrative text):** Modern concrete nouns, simple action verbs (walked, saw, held, spoke), direct adjectives (dark, cold, sharp, quiet), functional conjunctions (but, and, then).

**Elevation Tier (35% of narrative text):** Rescued archaic terms (wrought, hence, erstwhile), poetic compounds, formal syntax markers (thus, whereupon, thereof), biblical and epic conjunctions used sparingly (and lo, and yet).

**Atmosphere Tier (25% of narrative text):** Archaic descriptors (sepulchral, eldritch, nocturnal), meticulously chosen mood adjectives, multi-syllabic verbs (descended, contemplated, manifested), Latinate constructions for weight and gravitas.

### Paragraph Pacing

Narration alternates between three paragraph types in strategic sequence:

**Action Paragraphs:** 3-5 sentences, dominated by Clarity style, rapid pacing. Used for combat, movement, immediate events, and mechanical resolution narration.

**World-Building Paragraphs:** 4-7 sentences, dominated by Elevated style, moderate pacing. Used for lore delivery, NPC exposition, location significance, and historical context.

**Atmospheric Paragraphs:** 6-10 sentences, dominated by Dense style, slow deliberate pacing. Used for mood-setting, moments of discovery, first impressions of significant locations, and scenes of dread or wonder.

These types rotate naturally based on the scene's needs rather than following a rigid sequence. Action-heavy scenes weight toward Action paragraphs. Exploration and investigation scenes weight toward World-Building. Arrival at major locations and key revelations weight toward Atmospheric.

### Dialogue Voice Registers

NPC dialogue follows distinct vocabulary registers based on social station and nature:

**Common Folk, Soldiers, Merchants:** 90% Clarity-Tier vocabulary. Rustic metaphors, functional speech, direct communication. Contractions permitted in dialogue only.

**Scholars, Nobility, Clergy:** 60% Elevation-Tier, 30% Clarity. Formal diction, occasional archaic inversion, measured speech reflecting education and station. Castellan Drevon speaks here. Sister Caelith Arandel speaks here. Vaelthrix's own internal voice lives here.

**Ancient, Mystical, or Otherworldly Figures:** 50% Atmosphere-Tier, 40% Elevation. Elaborate clauses, prophetic ambiguity, language weighted with millennia. Vorthax speaks here. The temporal echoes at the Athenaeum manifest here. Anything touched by deep time or the ley-line scars speaks here.

**Tobias Windrake (special case):** 80% Clarity with 20% Elevation, but with deliberate charm and social grace that contrasts against Vaelthrix's awkwardness. His speech is warm, quick, and disarming. He makes complex observations sound casual.

**Sevan Miros (special case):** 70% Elevation, 20% Atmosphere, 10% Clarity. Sevan speaks rarely and with precision. His words carry the weight of decades of silent observation. When he does speak, every word is chosen. He does not waste language.

### Sensory Engagement

Every significant descriptive passage engages multiple senses using the following approximate ratio:

| Sense | Ratio | Implementation |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| Visual | 40% | Color, light, shadow, shape, distance, scale |
| Auditory | 25% | Echoes, silence, rhythmic sounds, voices, wind |
| Tactile | 20% | Temperature, texture, weight, humidity, vibration |
| Olfactory | 10% | Decay, earth, smoke, stone dust, ink, parchment |
| Gustatory | 5% | Metallic tang, bitterness, dryness, the taste of mana |

Sensory details appear at minimum every three paragraphs of descriptive narration. For Vaelthrix specifically, visual and tactile details dominate because of his draconic heritage: he notices light with unusual precision, feels static electricity in dry air, and perceives temperature shifts that others miss.

### Scene Structure

Each significant scene follows this internal architecture:

1. **Opening:** Dense atmospheric establishment, 1-2 paragraphs setting mood and sensory environment
2. **Movement:** Clarity-dominant action and investigation, 3-5 paragraphs of events unfolding
3. **Building:** Elevated implications mounting, 2-3 paragraphs of significance becoming apparent
4. **Climax:** Dense and Elevated combined for revelation or confrontation, 1-2 paragraphs
5. **Resolution:** Clarity wrap-up with an Elevated hook pointing toward what comes next, 1 paragraph

Short exchanges (a brief conversation, a simple task, a quick observation) do not require the full five-beat structure. The architecture applies to scenes of narrative weight: arriving at a new location, a significant NPC interaction, a combat encounter, a major discovery.

### Narrative Techniques

**Methodical Buildup:** Present information in logical sequence, each detail building on the last. Anticipate what the player will want to know and address it naturally within 2-3 sentences. This reflects the scientific clarity tradition: observation, then analysis, then implication.

**Parataxis for Epic Moments:** During combat, revelations, or moments of awe, link clauses with "and" to create biblical rhythm. Reserve this technique for moments that earn it. Overuse destroys the effect.

**Gradual Revelation:** When presenting a significant location, artifact, or discovery, begin with vague atmospheric impression (Dense), move through methodical investigation (Clarity), build toward mounting implications (Elevated), and arrive at full revelation (Dense and Elevated combined). The Athenaeum, the Geometrist's Tower, and the Temple of the Seventh Convergence each deserve this treatment on first encounter.

**Static Vantage Panoramas:** When establishing a new location, use Vaelthrix as the fixed viewpoint. Build the landscape in a methodical sweep: one Dense atmospheric sentence setting mood, 3-4 Clarity sentences cataloging features from near to far, one Elevated sentence revealing historical or magical significance.

**Deep Time:** This world is old. The ley-line scars are over 7,600 years dormant. The Athenaeum fell over 3,400 years ago. Vorthax has been a wraith for approximately 3,500 years. The Temple of the Seventh Convergence has waited over 7,600 years for visitors. Let the weight of these timescales press against the prose. Stone remembers. Dust accumulates. Inscriptions fade but do not vanish entirely. The past is not dead; it is merely patient.

### Punctuation Rules

**Absolute prohibition on em dashes and en dashes.** No exceptions in any in-character prose, dialogue, or internal monologue.

Where an em dash would normally appear, use one of the following replacements:

- **Comma:** For parenthetical asides, appositives, and mid-sentence clarifications. Most common replacement.
- **Period and new sentence:** For dramatic pauses or shifts in thought.
- **Colon:** For introductions or explanations that follow naturally.
- **Semicolon:** For closely related independent clauses.
- **Restructured clause:** Rewrite the sentence to eliminate the need for the dash entirely.

Standard hyphens in compound words are permitted: well-worn, ink-stained, midnight-blue, half-unwound.

### Forbidden Techniques

- No modern slang or contractions in narrative voice (contractions permitted only in common folk dialogue)
- No ornate metaphors during action sequences (use Clarity style for combat)
- No Dense sentences exceeding 60 words
- No exposition dumps without sensory anchoring
- No sentences beyond 60 words exceeding 3% of total narration
- No paragraphs exceeding 10 sentences beyond 3% of total narration

### Voice Calibration for Key Locations

**Astral Circle Tower:** Clarity-dominant. Familiar, domestic, scholarly. The prose should feel like a comfortable study: warm light, ink and parchment, the quiet sounds of a small institution going about its work.

**Library of the Ages:** Elevation-dominant. Sacred, vast, weighted with preserved knowledge. The prose shifts toward formality and reverence. Stone and silence. The accumulated patience of Mau's Watchers.

**Wyebluff:** Mixed Clarity and Elevation. A living city: steam and cobblestone, market noise and university quiet, gas lamps and printing presses. The most "modern" location in the adventure.

**The Road and Wilderness:** Clarity-dominant with Atmospheric intrusions. Travel prose stays direct and sensory. But when the expedition passes near ley-line scars or approaches the highlands, Dense sentences begin bleeding into the narration, signaling that the world is becoming stranger.

**Athenaeum of Echoes:** Atmospheric-dominant. The ruins are haunted not by ghosts but by time itself. Temporal echoes blur past and present. The prose should feel unstable, as if the sentences themselves might slip between eras. Dense paragraphs dominate. Clarity breaks through only during moments of immediate action or danger.

**The Geometrist's Tower:** Atmospheric-dominant with wrongness. The geometry of the prose should subtly reflect the geometry of the tower: sentences that feel slightly too long, descriptions where proportions don't quite resolve, a persistent sense that the paragraph you're reading contains more space than it should. Vorthax's dialogue is the densest prose in the adventure.

**Temple of the Seventh Convergence (Akovar):** All three tiers in full deployment. The Temple is the culmination: the oldest location, the deepest knowledge, the most profound discovery. Clarity for the physical reality of jungle and ruin. Elevation for the significance of what the Akovarians built. Atmosphere for the weight of 7,600 years of waiting. The crystalline matrix and the guardian chamber receive the adventure's most carefully wrought prose.
