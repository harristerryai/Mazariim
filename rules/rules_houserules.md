# Cypher House Rules

**Fantasy Campaign Additional Rules**

This document consolidates all additional rules for running D&D-style fantasy campaigns in the Cypher System, drawn from the Ancestries & Types, Spell Compendium, and Creature Compendium supplements.

## General Skills

Fantasy games benefit from a uniform set of skills. The suggested list includes:

**Available Skills:** Acrobatics, Appraise, Animal Handling, Athletics, Craft, Disable Device, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Knowledge (Arcana, History, Nature, Religion), Language, Navigation, Perception, Performance, Persuasion, Profession, Research, Sleight of Hand, Spellcraft, Stealth, Survival, Tools, and Vehicle.

### Untrained Skills
For skills requiring specific knowledge (e.g., Spellcraft, Research), the **maximum level of success** an untrained PC can achieve is level 3.

### Specialization
Characters should not be specialized in any skill until **Tier 3**.

### Rolling 1 on a Skill Check
If a 1 is rolled, savings from Edge do not apply. This cost cannot drop a pool below 1.

### Armor Speed Penalty
Speed-based skills (Acrobatics, Athletics, Movement, Sleight of Hand, Stealth) are **hindered by steps equal to the armor's penalty** in addition to extra Effort costs.

## Martial Skills

### Skill with Attacks
Training in specific weapon groups (e.g., light bladed, swords, bows). Does not stack with different overlapping groups.

### Skill with Defense
Training in Might, Speed, or Intellect defense.

### Experienced with Armor
Removes the first level of Armor Speed Penalty.

### Extra Attack
Allows a second melee or ranged attack per round.

### Mastery with Attacks/Defense
Specialized training in previously trained attack or defense groups.

### Mastery with Armor
Removes the second level of Armor Speed Penalty.

### Triple Attack
Grants a third attack per round.

## Spellcasting Rules

### Magical Training
Every spellcaster receives this ability for free:
- **Training:** Trained in magic basics (artifacts/cyphers) and the Spellcraft skill
- **Components:** All spells require somatic (one hand free) and verbal (Draconic syllables) components
- **Armor Restriction:** Cannot cast spells in physical armor you are not trained in using
- **Enabler**

### Spell Slots
Classes like Paladin, Druid, Mage, Priest, Magus, Ranger, and Bard gain specific numbers of spell slots per tier.

### Preparing Spells
Casters require a spellbook or holy text. **Swapping spells requires one uninterrupted hour of study** soon after a ten-hour recovery.

### Spellcasting Skills

#### 1. Spell Focus
- **Training:** Attacks with a school of magic, damage type, or focus are eased
- **Alternative:** Reduces spell cost by 1 Intellect point if not used for an attack
- **Enabler**

#### 2. Greater Spell Focus
- **Specialized:** Attacks with chosen school/type/focus are specialized
- **Alternative:** Reduces spell cost by 2 Intellect points
- **Enabler**

#### 3. Spellcraft
Used for:
- Identifying spells
- Analyzing/disarming magical traps
- Defensive casting (Level 4 task)
- Concentration checks

### Magic Rolls

#### Rolling a 1
Edge does not apply to the Intellect cost.

#### Rolling a 20
The spell costs no pool points OR has an additional beneficial effect.

### Defensive Casting
A **level 4 Spellcraft or Intellect task** to avoid an Attack of Opportunity in melee. Failure allows the enemy to attack; damage disrupts the spell.

### Concentration Checks
If damaged while casting, make a **Spellcraft check** against a level equal to the damage taken to avoid disruption.

### Spell Potency
Effort can be spent to make spells harder to dispel or counter (+1 level per Effort).

### Effort on Spells

#### Attacks (Heighten)
Effort on spell attacks eases the attack.

#### Damage
- **Single-target spells:** +3 damage per Effort
- **Area spells:** +2 damage per Effort

#### Area/Duration
Requires specific abilities like Enlarge Spell or Widen Spell.

### Spamming Spells
Only **one instance of a spell can be active at once**; casting it again ends the first instance.

### Spell Components
All spells have:
- **Verbal:** Draconic syllables
- **Somatic:** Forceful gestures (one hand minimum)
- **Material:** Components (typically not tracked)

### Spell Damage and Armor
Damage is reduced by Armor unless noted otherwise. Ambient area damage or lingering damage often ignores normal armor but is affected by elemental armor.

### Intellect Costs
Generally, the cost is the **original D&D spell level +1** (Low/Mid Tier) or **+2** (High Tier).

### Anti-Stacking
Spell bonuses are generally treated as assets. Only one instance of most buffs/effects can be active at a time.

## Metamagic Abilities

Special abilities (mostly for spellcasters) allow altering spells using Effort:

### Enlarge Spell
You can spend **a level of Effort** to increase the range of a spell one step (touch to immediate, immediate to short, short to long, long to very long). **Enabler.**

### Extend Spell
You can spend **a level of Effort** to change the duration of a spell from one/ten minutes to one hour, one hour to eight hours, or eight hours to twenty-four hours. **Enabler.**

### Widen Spell
You can spend **two levels of Effort** to double the area of a spell. **Enabler.**

### Subtle Spell
You can spend **one level of Effort** to cast a spell without either verbal or somatic components. **Enabler.**

### Energy Substitution
You can spend **one level of Effort** to change a spell's damage type (Fire, Cold, Lightning, Acid, Thunder, etc.). **Enabler.**

## Combat Rules

### 5-Foot Steps
Characters can take a small step (5 feet / immediate distance) without provoking attacks of opportunity.

### Attacks of Opportunity
Provoked by:
- Casting spells in melee
- Using potions/items in melee
- Standing up from prone
- Moving out of melee range

### Dual Wielding
When wielding two weapons:
- Make **two attacks** per action
- Both attacks are **hindered by one step**
- Requires appropriate proficiency (Dual Light Wield, Dual Medium Wield)

### Damage Track
Recovery rolls **do not restore hale state**; rest is required:
- **Impaired:** 1 day of rest
- **Debilitated:** 1 week of rest

## Creature Rules

### Health Determination
A creature's base health is calculated based on its Level and Size:
- **Small:** 3 Health per level
- **Medium:** 4 Health per level
- **Large:** 5 Health per level
- **Huge+:** 6 Health per level
- **Might Bonus:** +1 Health per level for +1 Might tasks; +2 Health per level for +2 Might tasks

### Creature Level Conversion (from D&D Hit Dice)
- 0-1 HD: Level 1
- 2-3 HD: Level 2
- 4-5 HD: Level 3
- 6-8 HD: Level 4
- 9-10 HD: Level 5
- 11-12 HD: Level 6
- 13-15 HD: Level 7
- 16-18 HD: Level 8
- 19-21 HD: Level 9
- 22-24 HD: Level 10
- 25-27 HD: Level 11
- 28-30 HD: Level 12
- 31+ HD: Level 13+

**Special Modifiers:**
- Extra-planar creatures: +1 Level
- Dragons: +2 Levels

### Combat and Stat Modifiers
**Task Level Adjustments:** Modifications stack from stats and skills. For example, a level 2 creature with +1 Speed and +1 Stealth acts as level 4 for Stealth.

### Armor Conversion (from D&D AC)
- AC 10-12: 0 Armor
- AC 13-15: 1 Armor
- AC 16-19: 2 Armor
- AC 20-23: 3 Armor
- Every +4 AC beyond 23: +1 additional Armor

**Shields:** Provide an Asset for Speed defense vs attacks.

**Size Penalties to Defense:**
- Large creatures: -1 level to Speed defense
- Huge+ creatures: -2 levels to Speed defense

### Monster Effort Rules
Monsters possess **one level of Effort per creature level**, typically applied to damage or attacks:
- **Level 1-3:** 1 level at once, 1–3 times per encounter
- **Level 4-6:** Up to 2 levels at once, 2–5 times per encounter
- **Level 7-10:** Up to 3 levels at once, 3–10 times per encounter

## Equipment & Economy

### Artifacts vs Cyphers
**Artifacts** have carry limits because their permanent magics interfere with each other. **Cyphers** (potions, scrolls) do not count toward this limit.

### Gold Pieces
This system replaces the standard Cypher gear approach for better wealth tracking in fantasy campaigns. Use standard D&D gold piece values for equipment and treasure.

### Starting Gear
Characters begin with class-appropriate starting equipment plus gold based on their background or a standard starting amount (typically 100-200 gp).

---

## Experience & Advancement

### Removed XP Awards
This system removes the standard Cypher XP award system in favor of milestone or session-based advancement typical of fantasy campaigns.

## Magical Items

### Artifacts vs Cyphers
When reading any description of an ability that describes how many cyphers a character can hold, apply it instead to artifacts. Artifacts have limits on the number you can carry instead of cyphers. The more powerful and permanent magics within artifacts (permanent magical items) interfere with each other, while cyphers like potions, scrolls, and wands can be carried in any number (if you can find or make them).

### Bonuses & Stacking
When in doubt, count a bonus as an asset.

### Abilities & Stacking
Magical items that duplicate class abilities or spells do not stack with abilities or spells the PC knows, uses, or casts, even if the ability has a different name. For example, a PC with the Battlecaster ability does not gain a bonus from the Rod of Spell Battle, nor does a PC with the Spell Focus ability gain a bonus from a Ring of Spell Focus.

### Intellect Spell Costs
Intellect costs for spells come from the Cypher System Spell Compendium.

### Magical Item Tier
Items are considered low, mid, or high tier, depending on their level.

| Item Level | Tier |
|------------|------|
| 1-3        | Low  |
| 4-6        | Mid  |
| 7-10       | High |

## Cyphers (Potions, Scrolls, Wands)

### Potions
Potions are single-use cyphers, usually a spell that only affects the caster. Potions are usually made only with low or mid tier spells with an Intellect cost less than or equal to 5, save a few rare potions, oils, and healing elixirs. All material component costs are paid during a potion's creation.

#### Potion Levels / Costs

| Intellect Cost | GP Cost |
|----------------|---------|
| 1              | 10 gp   |
| 2              | 25 gp   |
| 3              | 50 gp   |
| 4              | 100 gp  |
| 5              | 200 gp  |

Potions do not require a recipe as long as the spell to be used is known. Potions are usually the same level as their Intellect cost. Healing potions are an exception. Potions that are applied externally are called oils. Once made, potions leave no options to the imbiber to determine (hence separate enlarge and reduce potions). Potions cannot be created with extra effects given from Effort. Retrieving and/or using a potion is an action that draws an attack of opportunity.

### Scrolls
Scrolls are single-use cyphers that spellcasters can use to cast a spell or learn a spell from another caster. To use a scroll, the spell must be of the same type as the caster (mage, priest, druid, paladin, ranger, etc). Mid tier and high tier scrolls are more valued because they are much more rare and coveted for new spells rather than use on the battlefield. Sometimes scrolls are spells stored in gems, runes or other single-use format; if they are usable by others without the spell being on their spell list, add 25% to the cost of the item. All material component costs are paid during a scroll's creation.

#### Scroll Levels / Costs

| Level | Intellect Cost | GP Cost   |
|-------|----------------|-----------|
| 1     | 1              | 10 gp     |
| 2     | 2              | 25 gp     |
| 3     | 3              | 50 gp     |
| 4     | 4              | 100 gp    |
| 5     | 5              | 200 gp    |
| 6     | 6              | 500 gp    |
| 7     | 7-8            | 1,000 gp  |
| 8     | 9-10           | 2,000 gp  |
| 9     | 11-12          | 3,000 gp  |
| 10    | 13+            | 5,000 gp  |

Scrolls do not require a recipe as long as the spell to be used is known. Once created, scrolls leave no options to the user. Scrolls cannot be created with extra effects given from Effort. Retrieving and/or using a scroll is an action that provokes an attack of opportunity.

### Wands
Wands are multi-use cyphers that cast a single low tier or mid tier spell, with an Intellect cost less than or equal to 5, a limited number of times. All wands have 25 charges when created. To use a wand, the wielder needs to have the spell in the wand on their spell list. All material component costs are paid during a wand's creation.

#### Wand Levels / Costs

| Intellect Cost | GP Cost   |
|----------------|-----------|
| 1              | 100 gp    |
| 2              | 200 gp    |
| 3              | 500 gp    |
| 4              | 1,000 gp  |
| 5              | 2,000 gp  |

Wands do not require a recipe as long as the spell to be used is known. Once created, wands leave no options to the user. Wands cannot be created with extra effects given from Effort. Retrieving and/or using a wand is an action that provokes an attack of opportunity. Wands cannot have more than one instance of a spell active at once, as with spells.

## Artifacts (Armor, Rods, Staves, Weapons)

### Armor
Armor represents any armor or shield enchanted with magic. The cumulative abilities of the magical armor determines its level. A wide variety of spells and materials go into making magical armor.

#### Armor Levels / Costs

| Level | Minimum Cost |
|-------|--------------|
| 1     | 1,000 gp     |
| 2     | 2,000 gp     |
| 3     | 3,000 gp     |
| 4     | 5,000 gp     |
| 5     | 7,500 gp     |
| 6     | 10,000 gp    |
| 7     | 20,000 gp    |
| 8     | 30,000 gp    |
| 9     | 50,000+ gp   |
| 10    | 100,000+ gp  |

#### Armor Ability Levels

| Ability / Power                    | Level Cost    |
|------------------------------------|---------------|
| Asset to Speed Defense             | +3 lv (2 max) |
| Asset to Might Defense             | +2 lv (2 max) |
| Asset to Intellect Defense         | +2 lv (2 max) |
| Standard Armor (per point)         | +3 lv (2 max) |
| Elemental Armor (per point)        | +1 lv (5 max) |
| Ward Armor (per point)             | +1 lv (5 max) |
| Pool Points (per 4 points)         | +3 lv (2 max) |
| Skill Asset                        | +3 lv (2 max) |
| Edge                               | +4 lv (2 max) |
| Immunity                           | +5 (high tier)|
| Low Tier ability 1/day             | +2 lv         |
| Low Tier ability constant          | +3 lv         |
| Mid Tier ability 1/day             | +3 lv (mid)   |
| Mid Tier ability constant          | +4 lv (mid)   |
| High Tier ability 1/day            | +4 lv (high)  |
| High Tier ability constant         | +5 lv (high)  |
| Adamantine                         | +2 lv         |
| Mithril                            | +2 lv         |
| Cold Iron                          | +2 lv         |

Armor cannot exceed ten levels when adding together abilities.

**Special Materials:**
- **Adamantine:** Protects from critical hits (defense failures of 1 are standard misses)
- **Mithril:** Armor is one category lighter
- **Cold Iron:** Armor 5 against natural attacks of planar creatures

Magical armor needs a recipe to be created. Using a power or ability from magical armor is an action and does not draw an attack of opportunity.

### Rods
Rods are unique magical artifacts that have enabler abilities or unique powers not found on other magical items. To use a rod, the wielder needs to hold it in one hand. Rods are always at least mid tier.

#### Rod Levels / Costs

| Level | Minimum Cost |
|-------|--------------|
| 4     | 2,500 gp     |
| 5     | 5,000 gp     |
| 6     | 7,500 gp     |
| 7     | 10,000 gp    |
| 8     | 20,000 gp    |
| 9     | 30,000+ gp   |
| 10    | 50,000+ gp   |

Magical rods need a recipe to be created. Using a power or ability from a magical rod is an action and does not draw an attack of opportunity.

### Staves
Staves are magical artifacts that have spells enchanted within them, allowing the wielder to access them and use their Intellect pool, Edge, and enabler abilities to affect the casting as if they had the spells memorized. To use a staff, the wielder needs to hold it in one hand. Staves are always at least mid tier.

#### Staff Levels / Costs

| Level | Minimum Cost |
|-------|--------------|
| 4     | 2,500 gp     |
| 5     | 5,000 gp     |
| 6     | 7,500 gp     |
| 7     | 10,000 gp    |
| 8     | 20,000 gp    |
| 9     | 30,000+ gp   |
| 10    | 50,000+ gp   |

To use a spell from a staff, the user must have the Spellcasting ability, and the spell must be on their character type spell list. Additionally, the spell cannot be of a tier higher than they can cast. Using a power or ability from a magical staff is an action and draws an attack of opportunity as a spell would.

Weapons represent any melee or ranged weapon, or ammunition, enchanted with magic. The cumulative abilities of the magical weapon determines its level. A wide variety of spells and materials go into making magical weapons.

**Special Materials:**

- **Mithril:** Weapon is one category lighter
- **Cold Iron:** Effective against fey and certain planar creatures
- **Silvered:** Effective against lycanthropes and certain undead

Magical weapons need a recipe to be created. Using a power or ability from a magical weapon is an action and does not draw an attack of opportunity.

## Wondrous Items
Wondrous items are catch-all magical items that don't fit into the other categories (armor, rods, staves, weapons, or cyphers). They include rings, cloaks, boots, belts, amulets, gloves, and other miscellaneous magical items. The level and cost of wondrous items varies widely based on their powers and abilities, following similar guidelines to other artifacts.
