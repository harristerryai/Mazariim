# Racial Magic Systems & Ley Lines for Mazariim (Cypher System)

---

## FRAMEWORK: RACIAL MAGIC SYSTEMS

Each system below provides mechanical effects using Cypher System terminology: easing/hindering tasks, Effort, Pool costs, assets, damage, and duration. The house rule of 3 skill levels (trained/specialized/expert) at Tiers 1–2/3–4/5–6 is incorporated where relevant.

---

### 1. ELVEN NEXUS-WEAVING (Ritual Accretion)

**Lore Summary:** Elven magic is *not* ley-line dependent. A circle of casters builds shaped mana over hours or days using prepared stones, inks, and harmonic geometry, creating a temporary locus where specific effects become easier. The locus has strict boundaries, a set duration, and collapses if interrupted. Pushing too hard creates dramatic results — or catastrophic failures.

#### Building a Locus (Ritual)

The lead caster makes an Intellect task. Difficulty is based on the desired effect tier: Tier 1–2 effects = difficulty 3, Tier 3–4 effects = difficulty 5, Tier 5–6 effects = difficulty 7. Each additional trained caster in the circle provides an asset (maximum 2 assets from helpers). The ritual takes a minimum of 1 hour per difficulty level (so a difficulty 5 locus takes at least 5 hours). Material components (prepared stones, inks, harmonic geometry patterns) are required — absence hinders the roll by 2 steps.

#### Locus Effects (while active within the marked space)

The locus provides benefits in a narrow band of effects chosen at creation (healing, scrying, warding, planar listening, etc.). Spells matching the locus's band cost 1 fewer Intellect point (minimum 0). Casters within the locus gain an asset on spell attacks or effects matching the band. Duration of matching spells is doubled. The locus lasts for 1 hour per level of success on the creation roll, to a maximum of 24 hours.

#### Pushing the Locus ("Held Breath")

A caster can attempt to *push* a locus beyond its designed parameters. This requires spending 1 additional level of Effort beyond what was already applied. On success: the locus provides 2 free levels of Effort on the next matching spell, or triples spell duration instead of doubling it. On failure (the Intellect roll misses the target number): the locus collapses violently. Every creature within the locus takes Intellect damage equal to twice the locus difficulty, ignoring Armor. The caster who pushed it descends one step on the damage track. All spells active within the locus end immediately.

#### Contamination Risk

If a creature hostile to the circle's intent enters the locus, or if a spell of an opposing type is cast within it, the locus makes a "stability check" — the lead caster must succeed on an Intellect defense roll at the locus's difficulty or the locus collapses as if pushed and failed.

#### Entity Attention

Creating a locus of difficulty 5 or higher has a 1-in-1d6 chance per hour of attracting the attention of an entity, fey creature, or other being sensitive to mana concentrations. The GM determines what notices and how it responds.

#### Skill Integration

Characters can become trained (Tiers 1–2), specialized (Tiers 3–4), or expert (Tiers 5–6) in "Nexus-Weaving." Each level eases the locus creation roll by 1 step.

### 2. DWARVEN RUNE-SMITHING

**Lore Summary:** Dwarven runes are engineering-grade enchantment. A rune is a mechanism designed to do one thing reliably. Runes use carved symbols, inlaid metals, and measured spacing to create triggered magical functions. The craft uses "three-stone logic": a trigger rune (wake), a condition rune (judge), and an effect rune (act). Runes excel at wards/boundaries, keys/permissions, and durable utility effects. The craft is slow and materials-heavy but legendary for reliability — runes still function centuries later.

#### Inscribing a Rune (Crafting)

Rune-smithing is an Intellect task. The difficulty equals the desired rune level (a level 3 ward is difficulty 3). Time required: 1 day per rune level for simple runes, 1 week per rune level for complex rune chains. Materials required: appropriate metals, carving tools, and the specific inlay materials (mithril for high-level runes, iron and platinum for adamantium-grade work). The crafter must have the appropriate crafting abilities (Brew Potion, Craft Wondrous Item, Craft Arms and Armor, etc., depending on the rune type).

#### Three-Stone Logic (Rune Chains)

A complete rune system consists of three linked components. The Trigger Rune defines *when* it activates: step on a threshold, speak a keystone word, spill blood on a seal, strike a plate, turn a lock, break a line. The Condition Rune defines *who* it applies to: clan-marks, guild signs, physical tokens, specific creatures, or "everyone." The Effect Rune defines *what happens*: damage, alarm, reinforcement, lock/unlock, heat buffering, corrosion resistance, stability, etc.

Each component is inscribed separately. The total difficulty of the complete chain equals the highest individual component. A crafter can create a trigger-only rune (simpler, faster) or a full three-stone chain (more complex, more capable).

#### Rune Effects

Dwarven runes provide specific, reliable, repeatable effects. Ward runes: Creatures crossing the boundary must make a defense roll against the rune's level or suffer the effect (damage, paralysis, alarm, etc.). The ward functions indefinitely until physically destroyed or dispelled. Key runes: Only creatures meeting the condition can pass, open, or activate the protected object/area. Utility runes: Provide a permanent asset to a specific task (stability against collapse eases relevant Might defense rolls by 1 step; corrosion resistance adds +1 Armor against acid; forge heat-buffering eases fire resistance by 1 step). These last indefinitely.

#### Reliability

Dwarven runes never misfire under normal conditions. They can be dispelled (Intellect task against the rune's level) or physically destroyed (the rune must be damaged on the object damage track). Runes resist dispelling: any attempt to magically dispel a dwarven rune is hindered by 1 step because of the craft's inherent stability.

#### Limitations

Runes cannot produce effects that move, change, or adapt. A rune does one thing, exactly the same way, every time. Runes cannot be placed on living creatures (the mana pattern destabilizes). Runes require expensive materials: level 3+ runes require mithril inlay (expensive), level 5+ require adamantium components (very expensive to exorbitant). Rune-smithing cannot be rushed — attempts to inscribe faster than the minimum time hinder the crafting roll by 2 steps per time category reduced.

#### Skill Integration

Characters can become trained (Tiers 1–2), specialized (Tiers 3–4), or expert (Tiers 5–6) in "Rune-Smithing." Each level eases the inscription roll by 1 step. At expert level, the crafter can inscribe rune chains as if they were one level lower in difficulty (minimum 1).

### 3. GNOMISH GEMSCROLLS

**Lore Summary:** Gnomes treat cut gems as one-use spell containers. The caster compresses a spell's mana-pattern into a stable lattice; anyone who knows the trigger can release the stored effect without training. Quality matters: cut, clarity, and impurities affect stability. Gemscrolls are politically sensitive — guilds mark legitimate stones with microscopic cutter-signatures, churches oppose "unearned miracles," and criminals prize them for smuggling.

#### Creating a Gemscroll (Crafting)

Creating a gemscroll is an Intellect task. The difficulty equals the spell's tier level +1 (because compression is harder than casting). The caster must know the spell being stored and must pay its full Intellect cost during creation. Time required: 1 hour per spell tier level. Materials: a properly cut gem of appropriate quality. Gem quality affects reliability (see below).

#### Gem Quality Tiers

**Flawed gems (inexpensive):** Can store Tier 1 spells only. 1-in-1d6 chance of misfire when triggered. Misfires produce random sensory artifacts (bright flash, loud sound, wrong color) and the spell may target randomly (hindered attack roll).

**Standard gems (moderate cost):** Can store up to Tier 3 spells. Reliable — no misfire chance under normal conditions.

**Fine gems (expensive):** Can store up to Tier 5 spells. The stored spell gains an asset on its attack roll or eases the target's defense difficulty by 1 step (creator's choice at time of creation).

**Exceptional gems (very expensive):** Can store any tier spell. The stored spell gains a free level of Effort applied to either accuracy or damage (creator's choice at creation).

#### Using a Gemscroll

Anyone who knows the trigger can activate the gemscroll. Triggers include: a word, squeeze-pattern, tap sequence, breath on a facet, or similar cue. Activation is an action. No Intellect cost is required by the user — the mana is already stored. The spell uses the *creator's* relevant stats for any attack rolls or effect levels, determined at the time of creation. The gem is consumed (crumbles to dust) after use.

#### Detection & Forgery

Identifying a gemscroll requires an Intellect roll against the spell's level. Gnomish guild members are trained in this identification (eased). Forging a guild signature on an illegitimate gemscroll is an Intellect task, difficulty 5, hindered by 2 steps against a gnomish appraiser.

#### Black Market Concerns

Gemscrolls containing offensive spells (damage, mind control, etc.) are restricted in most civilized areas. Possession of unmarked combat gemscrolls is treated as carrying concealed weapons at best, and evidence of criminal intent at worst.

#### Skill Integration

Characters can become trained (Tiers 1–2), specialized (Tiers 3–4), or expert (Tiers 5–6) in "Gemscroll Crafting." Each level eases the creation roll by 1 step. Expert-level gnomish gemcrafters can create gemscrolls in half the normal time.

### 4. HALFLING HEARTH-WEAVING, ROAD-LUCK, AND QUIET WARDS

**Lore Summary:** Halfling magic rarely looks like battlefield spellcraft. It's an art of small protections with large lived impact: hearth-wards that make homes safer, road-luck charms that nudge coincidence away from disaster, and communal oaths that punish betrayal. The power is social and routine-bound — stronger when anchored to hospitality, shared meals, repeated journeys, and promises the community keeps.

#### Hearth-Ward (Ritual, Requires a Home/Shelter)

A halfling caster (typically a Greenwarden cleric or druid of Hinterhin) performs a blessing on a dwelling. The ritual takes 1 hour and costs 2 Intellect points. The ward lasts for 1 month, or until the home is abandoned or the inhabitants break guest-right (refusing a traveler food and shelter without just cause).

Effects within the warded home: All recovery rolls made inside are eased. Poison and disease defense rolls are eased. Sleep is restful — a ten-hour recovery roll restores an additional 2 points to any Pool. Hostile creatures attempting to enter must succeed on an Intellect defense roll against the ward's level (equal to the caster's tier + the number of continuous years the ward has been maintained on this dwelling, maximum level 8). Failure means the creature is reluctant to enter and all its actions inside are hindered for 1 hour. At Tier 5+, the caster can add a specific prohibition to the ward (e.g., "no undead," "no one bearing weapons with intent to harm"). Creatures matching the prohibition must succeed on the defense roll or cannot enter at all.

#### Road-Luck Charm (Enabler, Requires Travel)

Before a journey, a halfling caster blesses travelers with road-luck. The blessing costs 1 Intellect point per person blessed (maximum of 6 people). Duration: the length of the journey, up to 1 week.

While the charm is active: The first natural 1 rolled each day is treated as a natural 2 (avoiding GM intrusion). Navigation and perception tasks during travel are eased. If the blessed traveler is ambushed or surprised, they get an asset on their initiative roll. The charm ends immediately if the traveler commits an act of unprovoked violence, theft from an innocent, or breaks a sworn oath during the journey.

#### Communal Oath (Ritual, Requires Willing Participants)

Two or more willing creatures swear an oath in the presence of a halfling caster. The ritual takes 10 minutes and costs 3 Intellect points. The oath is bound — all parties who swore feel a warm certainty when acting in accordance with it, and a cold unease when contemplating violation.

If a party violates the oath, they immediately suffer: All their actions are hindered for 24 hours, they cannot benefit from any hearth-ward or road-luck charm for 1 month, and the other parties to the oath immediately know the oath has been broken (though not necessarily the details). Oaths can only bind *willing* participants — magical compulsion or deception invalidates the binding. The oath lasts for the agreed-upon duration (commonly 1 year and a day, or "until this task is complete").

#### Social Amplification

All halfling magic is strengthened by social context. Hearth-wards on homes that have hosted guests within the last week gain +1 to their level. Road-luck charms cast after a shared meal provide an additional asset on the first dangerous encounter of the journey. Communal oaths sworn at a festival or harvest celebration increase the violation penalty duration to 1 week instead of 24 hours.

#### Skill Integration

Characters can become trained (Tiers 1–2), specialized (Tiers 3–4), or expert (Tiers 5–6) in "Hearth-Weaving." Each level reduces the Intellect cost of halfling magical workings by 1 (minimum 0) and increases hearth-ward level by 1.

## FRAMEWORK: LEY LINES

**Lore Summary:** Lines of raw magical energy criss-cross Mazariim, often invisible. Most were created by dragons; others by natural phenomena, divine casters, or entities. Where lines intersect, they form nexuses of greater power. Ley lines don't grant spells — they change how mana behaves and how easily it can be shaped. Effects include: enhanced spell power, extended duration, line-specific signatures (fire, ice, shadow, growth, dream, etc.), and the danger of ley-sickness from prolonged exposure.

### Proximity Effects

Ley line proximity is measured in three zones, each providing escalating benefits and risks.

#### Near a Ley Line (within long range of the line)

All spellcasters sense the line (Intellect task, difficulty 2, to perceive it; trained casters sense it automatically). Spells cast in this zone gain a minor benefit: the caster can apply the Extend Spell ability for free (no Effort cost) — durations increase by one step. The line's signature manifests subtly: metallic tastes, faint luminous motes, unseasonal breezes, odd animal behavior, recurring dreams among sleepers.

#### On a Ley Line (within immediate range of the line itself)

All benefits from "Near" apply, plus: the caster gains a free level of Effort on any spell cast. This free Effort can be applied to attack, damage, duration, or range — caster's choice, declared before rolling. Spells matching the line's signature (e.g., fire spells on a fire-aspected line) gain an additional asset on the attack roll or ease the target's defense by 1 step. Spells *opposing* the line's signature (e.g., cold spells on a fire line) are hindered by 1 step. Dispel attempts against spells cast on the line are hindered by 1 step — the line "holds" patterns more firmly. The line's signature may impress itself on spells: a healing spell on a fire-line might cauterize wounds (healing but leaving scar tissue); a ward on a dream-line might cause intruders to experience disorienting visions.

#### At a Nexus (intersection of two or more ley lines)

All benefits from "On a Line" apply for each line present, plus: the caster gains 2 free levels of Effort (from two intersecting lines) or 3 free levels (from three or more lines, which is extremely rare). Spell damage increases by +2 per intersecting line. Spells that match *both* lines' signatures gain a major effect: double damage, double area, or double duration (caster's choice). Reality itself becomes pliable: the GM may declare that unusual or normally impossible magical effects become possible at nexuses (creating permanent enchantments, opening portals, or performing rituals that would otherwise require entity-level power). The risk of unintended effects is dramatically higher. Any spell failure at a nexus triggers a wild magic surge — the GM makes a free intrusion with a magical twist related to the nexus's signatures.

### Ley-Sickness

Prolonged exposure to ley line energy causes **ley-sickness**, which progresses in stages.

**Exposure tracking:** For every continuous hour spent on a ley line (or 10 minutes at a nexus), the character must make a Might defense roll. The difficulty starts at 2 and increases by 1 for each subsequent check.

**Stage 1 — Resonance (failed first check):** Headaches, faint humming in the ears, a metallic taste. All Might-based tasks are hindered for 1 hour after leaving the line. Minor inconvenience.

**Stage 2 — Attunement (failed second check):** Vertigo, perception distortions (colors seem wrong, distances seem off), and an urge to cast spells even when unnecessary. Intellect defense rolls are hindered for 4 hours after leaving the line. Spells cast during this stage have a 1-in-1d6 chance of acquiring a persistent "hum" — a faint magical echo that lasts 1d6 hours after the spell should have ended, potentially alerting enemies or causing minor unintended effects.

**Stage 3 — Saturation (failed third check):** Compulsive casting (the character must succeed on a difficulty 4 Intellect defense roll each round to avoid casting a random known spell), warped perception of time (the character believes minutes have been hours or vice versa), and spells that refuse to end cleanly. The character descends one step on the damage track. Recovery from Saturation requires 24 hours away from all ley lines.

**Stage 4 — Overload (failed fourth check or spending more than 8 continuous hours at a nexus):** The character's body begins to conduct mana uncontrollably. They glow faintly with the line's signature energy. All creatures within immediate range take 1 point of ambient damage per round (matching the line's signature type). The character descends to debilitated on the damage track. Recovery requires a week away from all ley lines and a successful difficulty 6 Intellect task (or divine/druidic healing of equivalent level).

### Safety Practices

Experienced practitioners use safety techniques to resist ley-sickness. **Grounding stances** (trained in "Ley Line Lore"): Eases ley-sickness defense rolls by 1 step. **Counter-sigils** (carrying prepared dwarven runes): Eases ley-sickness defense rolls by 1 additional step. **Insulating circles** (drawn before extended work): Prevents exposure checks for the first hour. **Time limits** (discipline): Simply leaving before checks become dangerous — the most reliable method.

### Line Signatures

Each ley line has a signature that affects magic used near it. Common signatures include:

**Fire/Heat:** Fire spells enhanced; cold spells hindered. Byproducts: warm ground, heat shimmer, fire-tolerant vegetation.

**Frost/Rime:** Cold spells enhanced; fire spells hindered. Byproducts: perpetual chill, frost patterns on stone, breath visible year-round.

**Shadow/Darkness:** Illusion and necromancy enhanced; light and healing hindered. Byproducts: deeper shadows, sounds seem muted, animals avoid the area.

**Radiance/Light:** Healing and protection enhanced; necromancy and illusion hindered. Byproducts: brighter daylight, plants grow toward the line, sense of comfort.

**Growth/Vitality:** Nature and healing magic enhanced; destruction spells hindered. Byproducts: lush vegetation, rapid plant growth, large insects.

**Corrosion/Entropy:** Destruction and transmutation enhanced; healing and protection hindered. Byproducts: rust, decay, brittle materials, sour smells.

**Storm/Lightning:** Evocation and force effects enhanced; subtle magic hindered. Byproducts: static charge, hair standing on end, frequent localized storms.

**Dream/Psyche:** Divination and enchantment enhanced; force effects hindered. Byproducts: vivid dreams, déjà vu, distant whispers, sleepwalking among residents.

**Resonance/Sound:** Bardic and sonic magic enhanced; stealth magic hindered. Byproducts: echoes, phantom music, stones that hum when struck.

**Binding/Stasis:** Abjuration and wards enhanced; teleportation and movement magic hindered. Byproducts: stillness, dust that doesn't settle, time feels slow.

### Ley Lines and Racial Magic Interactions

Each racial tradition interacts with ley lines differently.

**Elven Nexus-Weaving near a Ley Line:** Creating a locus near (but not on) a ley line does not benefit from the line — nexus-weaving is explicitly independent of ley lines. However, if a locus is accidentally created *on* a ley line, the line's energy floods the locus, adding the line's signature to all effects and doubling both benefits and risks. Most elven circles deliberately avoid ley lines for this reason.

**Dwarven Runes on a Ley Line:** Runes inscribed on a ley line gain +2 to their effective level for the purpose of resisting dispel attempts. However, the rune also acquires the line's signature, which may alter its effect (a fire-line ward rune might burn intruders instead of merely blocking them). Dwarven runesmiths consider this a feature, not a bug — they deliberately site important wards on ley lines when possible.

**Gnomish Gemscrolls on a Ley Line:** Gemscrolls activated on a ley line release their stored spell as if the gem were one quality tier higher (flawed functions as standard, standard as fine, etc.). However, flawed gems activated on ley lines have a 1-in-1d6 chance of absorbing the line's energy and exploding, dealing the stored spell's damage to everyone in immediate range.

**Halfling Hearth-Weaving on a Ley Line:** Hearth-wards placed on a dwelling that sits on a ley line gain the line's signature as an additional protective quality (a home on a radiance line makes undead and shadow creatures unable to enter without a defense roll at 2 steps harder). Road-luck charms for journeys along ley lines double their duration. However, communal oaths sworn on ley lines cannot be broken *at all* while on the line — the oath becomes supernaturally binding in the line's vicinity, which can be either a powerful commitment tool or a terrifying trap.

## Summary: How Each Race Differs

| Tradition | Core Mechanic | Strength | Weakness | Ley Line Interaction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Elven Nexus-Weaving** | Slow ritual, temporary locus, strict boundaries | Flexible, cooperative, no material cost beyond components | Time-intensive, fragile, attracts entity attention | Independent of ley lines; dangerous if overlapping |
| **Dwarven Rune-Smithing** | Permanent inscribed effects, three-stone logic | Incredibly reliable, lasts centuries, resists dispelling | Slow, expensive, inflexible, cannot adapt | Runes gain level vs. dispelling; acquire signature |
| **Gnomish Gemscrolls** | One-use spell gems, anyone can trigger | Portable, democratic, marketable | Consumed on use, quality-dependent, politically sensitive | Gem quality effectively increases; flawed gems may explode |
| **Halfling Hearth-Weaving** | Social/routine-bound protections | Subtle, long-lasting, community-strengthening | Weak in combat, requires social bonds, collapses if oaths broken | Gains signature protections; oaths become unbreakable on-line |
