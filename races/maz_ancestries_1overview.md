# Mazariim Ancestries - Overview

## Chapter 1 - Ancestries

The five major ancestries available to players are the traditional fantasy races from fantasy TTRPGs, namely Dwarves, Elves, Gnomes, Halflings, and Humans. Their history, society, and religions are outlined, as are other important details pertinent to role-play and campaign lore.

## Dwarves

Dwarves are clan-based, honor-driven people who have inhabited the mountains and hills of Nigal for over twenty thousand years. Masters of metalcraft and engineering, dwarves are known for their terraced farming, ancestor worship, and incredible skill with arms and armor. The arrival of the god Mordrion in 1866 BW unified the dwarven peoples and ushered in an era of prosperity, though the War would later devastate their holds.

**Subraces:**
- **Gormdawi (Mountain Dwarf)** - Northern dwarves with dark hair and eyes, traditionalists who value order and clan loyalty. Known for rune magic, steam-powered engineering, and coffee cultivation.
- **Bryndawi (Hill Dwarf)** - Southern dwarves with red, blonde, or light brown hair, more independent and varied in their ways. Known for astrology, numerology, tattoo magic, and elemental magics.

**Key Traits:** Clan-based society, ancestor veneration, masters of metalworking, terraced farming, worship of Mordrion, honor and loyalty.

## Elves

Elves have the oldest civilization in Mazariim, stretching back over four thousand years. Guided by the entity Minerva since 3402 BW, elves live in tribal societies with deep respect for nature and no concept of personal property. Their culture is comparable to Native American peoples, with diverse ways of life based on geography.

**Subraces:**
- **Ma'Sehrim (Common Elf)** - Mixed heritage elves
- **Ya'sehrim (High Elf)** - Ancient bloodline, black or blue hair, pale skin
- **Tawarim (Wood Elf)** - Forest dwellers, green and brown coloration, insular
- **Gaear'rim (Ocean Elf)** - Coastal people, brown and blue hues
- **Farondrim (Wandering Elf)** - Hunter-gatherers, varied appearance
- **Raladrim (Plains Elf)** - Plains dwellers, share lands with wemics
- **Yav'daerim (Valley Elf)** - Most interaction with humans, red/black/blonde hair
- **Aeglirim (Mountain Elf)** - Mountain dwellers, gray and white hair

**Key Traits:** Tribal structure, nature reverence, no personal property, guided by Minerva, masters of wooden weapons and mithral, diverse lifestyles from coastal fishers to prairie nomads.

## Gnomes

Gnomes originated in the far eastern mountains of Nigal, developing alongside the Bryndawi dwarves. They are the inventors of steam technology and are renowned for their skill as jewelers, clockmakers, and appraisers. Gnomes worship Rivoleen, a god of enlightenment and thought, and value family, creativity, and independence above all.

**Subraces:**
- **Durazgrom (Rock Gnomes)** - Live deeper underground, earth-toned coloration, inventors of steam power and advanced technology. More conservative since the War.
- **Rothgrom (Forest Gnomes)** - Surface dwellers with blonde, red, brown, or green tones. More playful and connected to nature, often work with animal allies. Many druids among them.

**Key Traits:** Clan-based families, incredible eyesight and steady hands, masters of steam technology and clockwork, jewelers and appraisers, worship of Rivoleen, capital at Zhonhylite.

## Halflings

Halflings (hin) live on the peaceful island of Shalazar, where they have developed a communal society focused on agriculture and harmony. They are masters of farming and cooking, and almost universally pacifistic. Hin society is structured around collective needs, with labor divided according to talent and goods shared equally.

**Subraces:**
- **Tala'nitya (Lightfoot)** - Taller and more charming, often in craftsmanship, artisan work, and social/leadership roles
- **Tala'beleg (Stout)** - Shorter and tougher, often in farming, building, and the rare defensive roles

**Key Traits:** Communal society, masters of agriculture, incredible cooks, pacifists, worship of Hinterhin, rounded half-buried homes, over three dozen festivals per year, family names reflect professions.

## Humans

Humans were the last major race to develop civilization and have the most diverse societies and cultures in Mazariim. From feudal kingdoms to democratic city-states, from nomadic horse tribes to steam-powered metropolises, human civilization encompasses nearly every form of social organization. Humans cannot interbreed with other races - there are no half-elves or half-orcs in Mazariim.

**Subraces (Nationalities):**
- **Azala** - Southeastern Nigal, tan to dark skin, South American analog
- **Chak'ti** - Central Nigal, horse nomads, Asian steppe analog (Russia/China)
- **Ejiin** - Yashaki Islands, honor-based culture, Japanese/Korean analog
- **Madronians** - Inner Kingdoms, most advanced, 17th-18th century European analog
- **Laveri** - Lavantine Peninsula, democratic city-states, Greek/North African analog
- **Nonds** - Frozen Isles, raiders and warriors, Norse/Scandinavian analog
- **Sabonaar** - Merchant Nations, master sailors, Spanish/Italian/Portuguese analog
- **Varkonan** - Southern Nigal, ancient civilization with god-kings, Egyptian analog
- **Mixed Nationality** - Individuals of combined heritage

**Key Traits:** Extreme cultural diversity, masters of horse domestication, most advanced technology (in Inner Kingdoms), worship of Tiovin and many other deities, unified during the War 800 years ago.

## Other Races

While not typically player characters, many other sentient races inhabit Mazariim:

**Orcs** - Primary antagonists during the War to End All Wars, led by god Zuel. Still exist in remote regions, mostly hostile.

**Goblins** - Allies of orcs during the War, cunning and cruel, live in tribal groups in wilderness areas.

**Giants** - Various types (hill, stone, frost, fire, cloud), enemies of gnomes, inhabit mountainous regions.

**Wemics** - Noble leonine creatures, share plains with Raladrim elves, live in tribal prides.

**Drow** - Brought to Mazariim by goddess Eilistraee in 1377 BW, bloodlines now absorbed into elven subraces. Not a playable race.

**Far Realm Entities** - Beholders, Mind Flayers, and Aboleths originate from the nightmarish Plane of Dreams, not from an "Underdark."

**Angels and Devils** - Servants of Tiovin (good) and Taovan (evil), engaged in eternal battle of morality.

**Others** - Centaurs, minotaurs, lizardfolk, kobolds, gnolls, pixies, sprites, and various fey creatures exist in smaller populations.

## Important Setting Notes

**No Underdark:** Unlike many fantasy settings, Mazariim has no "Underdark" or worldwide network of subterranean realms. Underground areas exist as discrete locations (dwarven holds, gnome settlements, natural caves) but are not part of a vast interconnected system.

**No Half-Breeds:** Humans cannot produce offspring with other races. Each race is genetically distinct.

**The War:** The War to End All Wars (approximately 3 BW to 0 AW) was a global orc invasion that forced all races to unite. This event shaped modern Mazariim and established lasting alliances between formerly hostile peoples.

**Gods as Entities:** The gods of Mazariim are powerful but mortal beings called "entities" - intelligences formed from mana rather than omnipotent creators. They use avatars to enter the Prime Material Plane and can be defeated or banished.

**Common Language:** Developed after the War as a trade tongue between all races, allowing cooperation and commerce across cultural boundaries.
