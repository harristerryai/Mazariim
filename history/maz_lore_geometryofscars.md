prequel-era worldbuilding materials. It expands on locations, organizations, ancient
sites, magical theory, and other elements relevant to the Mazariim RPG campaign setting.

**SETTING ERA NOTES**: Many of these details describe the state of the world around
800 AW, approximately 42 years before the current campaign era (842 AW). Some
locations and organizations may have changed between 800 AW and the present day.

## The Inner Kingdoms — Expanded Detail

### Sensory Character of the Region

The Inner Kingdoms occupy the northwest corner of the continent of Nigal, a region
blessed by natural defenses and temperate climate. Mountains shield the east and
south, while dozens of small city-states and nations dot the Madronian Peninsula.
Well-patrolled roads and a near-total absence of roving monsters characterize this
civilized heartland — a long-established reality maintained by the Federation of Man.

## The Federation of Man — Expanded Detail

**"United we stood against the darkness. United we shall remain."**
— Inscription above the gates of Fort Hope

The Federation of Man is not a nation but a confederation — a political alliance
binding the major nations and city-states of the Inner Kingdoms into a unified body
for mutual defense, trade, and governance.

### Governance Structure

The Federation is governed by the Praetorian Council, a legislative senate seated
at Fort Hope. Major city-states receive one representative each, the five major
nations send between five and twelve representatives based on population, recognized
guilds receive one representative, and the city of Fort Hope holds one seat. The
Council votes on regional laws, taxation, troop distribution, infrastructure, and
trade policy.

The High Court serves as the judicial body. Members are appointed for life and must
renounce citizenship in their parent nation, serving the Federation itself. The High
Court adjudicates disputes between member nations, interprets Federation law, and
hears appeals from national courts.

The Federation Military maintains border forts, road patrols, and naval forces,
funded through Federation taxation and coordinating with national armies during crises.

### Fort Hope — Districts and Notable Locations

The political heart of the Federation. Originally a military outpost, now a
sprawling city with distinct districts: Fort District (oldest section, administrative
and military heart), Temple District (religious institutions, hospitals, places of
learning), Upper Market and Docks Districts (prosperous commercial heart), Lower
Market and Docks Districts (grittier underside, black market), Merchant District
(guild operations, trade administration), Noble District (wealthy residential
quarter), Industrial District (manufacturing, processing, heavy industry), Elventown
(elven community, culture, institutions), and the Dwarven Ghetto (dwarven quarter,
insular and pious).

Notable locations within Fort Hope include the Grand Cathedral of the Lord (Tiovin)
— the largest temple in the Inner Kingdoms; the Halls of the Silver Dragon (Paladine)
— headquarters for paladin orders; Praetorian Halls — the legislative chambers; the
High Court and Jails; University Arcanum — a prestigious arcane institution; the
Academy of the Third Eye — a school for divination and psionics; the Towers of
Alchemy; the Temple of the Magister (Alagalothor); Mordrion's Anvil — the dwarven
temple; Temple of the Ancients — a multi-faith temple for the Old Gods; the Shrine
to Minerva in Elventown; Big Ben Clockhouse — a massive clocktower of gnomish
engineering and the city's most recognizable landmark; the Griffon's Respite — a
famous inn frequented by adventurers; and the Grand Bazaar — a massive covered
marketplace.

### Fort Stoneshaper

A major fortress on the eastern frontier, constructed 67-73 AW. Named to honor
Demious Stoneshaper, the dwarven hero who defeated Zuel's avatar. Houses the Circle
of the Eastern Watch (12 specialists — six mages and six veteran warriors — dealing
with supernatural threats). Transformed into a vital commercial hub after the
Stone-Steel Treaty of 376 AW when the railway expansion reached it around 400 AW.

The most technologically advanced nation in all of Mazariim, Beneshire is known as
one of the most benevolent and charitable nations of the Inner Kingdoms. This
traditional feudalistic monarchy enjoys prosperity and a smaller gap between the
wealthy and poor than most nations. Magic, steam power, the printing press, and
other innovations make Beneshire a peaceful and prosperous land.

The landscape is a patchwork of rolling farmland, ancient forests, gentle hills,
and dramatic coastline along the Crescent Sea. The Misty Forest dominates the
nation's northern interior, a vast woodland patrolled by rangers and home to
scattered elven communities.

Beneshire is the oldest continuous nation in the Inner Kingdoms. During the War to
End All Wars, Beneshire's armies fought at Fort Hope and across the Inner Kingdoms,
suffering tremendous casualties but emerging with institutions intact. Beneshire
was the first unified nation to join the Federation in 1 AW.

#### Wyebluff — Capital City

The capital city features soaring towers, a bustling marketplace, extensive docks,
train systems, and a blimp airport. Districts include the Royal Quarter (palace,
government offices, noble residences), Cathedral Quarter (religious institutions,
hospitals, charity houses), University Quarter (academies, libraries, student
housing), Market Quarter (grand bazaar, merchant offices, guild halls), Harbor
District (docks, warehouses, shipyards, sailor's taverns), and the Commons
(residential areas for working population).

The approach from the north descends through hills toward the river valley. The
city announces itself first through sound (steam engines in the industrial yards),
then smell (coal smoke, river water, bakery yeast, metallic tang of gas lighting
infrastructure), then sight as the road crests the final hill. The Royal Palace sits
on the western bluff in pale stone calculated to catch morning light. The Grand
Cathedral of Tiovin dominates the Cathedral Quarter. The University Quarter sprawls
comfortably on the southern bank in older buildings. Seven bridges cross the river.
Gnomish blimps sit at the airport north of the city, their gasbags painted in
cheerful yellows and reds.

The Market Quarter features cobblestone streets where merchants trade Xeque silk,
Arogrorian steel, and Saerran wine. Gas lamps with etched glass housings line
broader avenues. Steam trams on iron rails carry workers through the district.

The University Quarter maintains a slower pace with older architecture darkened by
centuries. Bookshops occupy former warehouses. Coffee houses (a Saerran cultural
export that arrived approximately two decades before 800 AW) serve as gathering
places for students and scholars. Printing presses produce broadsheets. Narrow
lanes feature smooth-worn cobblestones.

##### The University of Wyebluff

The University of Wyebluff is the premier academic institution in Beneshire and one
of the most respected in the Inner Kingdoms. Founded in the early centuries after
the War, it occupies a campus in the University Quarter and offers instruction in
history, languages, mathematics, natural philosophy, and arcane theory. The
University's Department of Arcane Theory maintains a faculty of approximately twenty
mages and scholars who study the principles underlying magical phenomena.

##### Coffee Houses

Coffee houses are a Saerran cultural export that arrived in Beneshire's University
Quarter approximately twenty years before 800 AW (~780 AW). They have become
fixtures of the academic social landscape, serving as gathering places for students
and scholars. This is consistent with the cultural exchange facilitated by the
Federation of Man and with Saerrus's characterization as an intellectual,
art-focused democracy whose cultural exports include lifestyle and social innovations.

#### Cabrook

Predominantly a farming and trading city ruled by House Halycon (Pop. ~18,500).
Supplies much of the nation with grains, cheeses, meats, fruits, and wines. More
rustic architecture with sturdy stone and timber buildings. Rail spur connects to
main line.

**Key NPC:** Brianna Halycon (NG female Madronian Cleric) — Lady of House Halycon.
Older Madronian woman with long silver braided hair, known to wield divine powers
and worship a number of deities.

#### Littlebridge

Ruled by the Bakerson family. Can call upon at least 25 militia.

Key Locations include the Misty Dawn Inn (primary inn and gathering place), Pragos
Magical Wares (small shop selling potions and scrolls), and the Local Tiovin
Congregation (modest chapel).

#### The Misty Forest

The Misty Forest is a vast woodland dominating Beneshire's northern interior. Dense,
ancient, and poorly mapped in its deeper reaches, the forest is patrolled by the
Protectors of the Shire, a mixed company of rangers and paladins, but their coverage
is limited by the forest's sheer size.

The forest earns its name from persistent ground-level mist that forms in the
valleys and hollows between its hills, created by the interaction of cold mountain
air from the east and warmer lowland moisture from the west. Visibility on the
forest floor is frequently reduced, particularly at dawn and dusk, and the mist
creates an atmosphere of perpetual twilight that has inspired centuries of folklore
about spirits, wandering lights, and hidden places.

The forest contains numerous remnants of the Yrugas mageocracy, ranging from
collapsed towers and overgrown foundations to more substantial structures that have
survived three millennia through magical preservation or simple durability. Most are
minor remains of administrative, scholarly, or agricultural facilities rather than
military or magical installations. Scattered elven communities also exist within the
forest, established by elves who chose to remain on Nigal after the War to End All
Wars.

| Alignment | LN (good tendencies) |

| Founded | ~45 AW |

| Religions | Tiovin, Alagalothor, Minerva (minor) |
| Primary Exports | Art, literature, manufactured luxury goods, scholarly works |
| Real-World Analog | France, early 18th century |

Independent and free-thinking, the people of Saerrus pride themselves on
open-mindedness and their many universities and schools of thought. One of the only
democracies in the world, giving its people a sense of superiority and smugness.
Ruled by elected councils with numerous checks and balances. The Prime Minister is
elected by the Senate and serves as head of state.

A peaceful nation seemingly obsessed with art, music, culture, and all things
intellectual. Numerous galleries, theaters, and opera houses grace its largest
cities. The landscape is gentle and cultivated — rolling countryside, manicured
estates, and elegant cities connected by tree-lined boulevards.

Founded from the ruins of two or three Madronian nations destroyed during the War
to End All Wars. For its first century and a half, Saerrus was itself a monarchy
within the Federation. In 209 AW, the monarchy fell to revolutionaries, and the
Federation mediated peace on borders while the nation transitioned into an
intellectual democracy.

## Key Organizations — Expanded Detail

### The Astral Circle

Peering into the vast depths of the extra dimensions of the universe, the Astral
Circle is a cabal of spellcasting scholars and explorers who quietly study the
multiverse from towers and strongholds scattered across the Inner Kingdoms. Unknown
to most, their research into planar magic, ley-lines, and dimensional travel has
proven invaluable during the campaign era's escalating supernatural threats.

The Circle operates as a decentralized network of research towers and strongholds.
Each tower functions semi-independently under the authority of a Castellan, an
administrative leader responsible for the facility's operations, security, and
external relationships. Castellans report to the Circle's central council, a small
body of senior members whose identities and meeting location are known only to other
Castellans and select researchers.

The Circle does not maintain a formal academic hierarchy. Researchers are recruited
based on talent, dedication, and the nature of their work, and they are given broad
latitude to pursue their own lines of inquiry. This freedom is balanced by an
expectation of discretion.

The Astral Circle is not a secret organization in the strictest sense. Its existence
is known to the Federation's Praetorian Council, to senior members of major academic
institutions, and to the leadership of allied organizations like the Library of the
Ages and the Emerald Shields. What remains secret is the specific nature of the
Circle's research, the identities of most of its members, and the locations of its
facilities.

The Circle funds itself through a combination of discreet investments, fees for
specialized arcane consultation, and occasional contributions from wealthy patrons
who value the advancement of magical knowledge.

#### Known Astral Circle Facilities

The Astral Circle maintains towers in both Beneshire and Saerrus. The Beneshire
tower is located south of Wyebluff, a slender structure of grey stone that most
passersby mistake for a disused watchtower or an eccentric nobleman's folly. This
deliberate anonymity is characteristic of the Circle's approach. The tower houses
six mages on its upper floors, each pursuing independent research.

The Saerrus tower is larger and more actively staffed, reflecting the intellectual
democracy's generally supportive attitude toward arcane research. However, the
Saerrus facility operates under greater pressure to produce results with immediate
practical applications.

#### Alliance with the Library of the Ages

The Astral Circle and the Library of the Ages maintain a quiet alliance of mutual
benefit. The Library provides the Circle with access to rare texts and historical
documents; the Circle provides the Library with arcane expertise and, when necessary,
magical resources for preservation and defense. Communication between the two
organizations is conducted through coded letters carried by trusted couriers rather
than magical means, as both organizations prefer methods that cannot be intercepted
by divination.

### The Library of the Ages

High in the mountains of Beneshire stands one of the greatest collections of
knowledge anywhere in the world. Founded just after the War to preserve humanity's
knowledge against further world-shaking events, the Library houses over ten thousand
books, including a large number of pre-War books and documents.

Watched over by the clergy of Mau (the Watchers), the Library is a location
difficult to access by ordinary means, plus it possesses numerous magical defenses
and defenders. It maintains minor allegiances to the Emerald Shields and the Astral
Circle.

The Library occupies a complex of interconnected stone buildings carved into and
built upon a mountain peak in Beneshire's highlands. The location was chosen for
defensibility and isolation — accessible only by a single maintained road that climbs
through narrow passes. The mountain's altitude creates a cool, dry climate ideal
for document preservation.

#### Internal Organization

Key spaces include the Translation Chamber (eastern wing, a long hall of pale stone
with arched windows and slanted reading desks where scholars work with texts
requiring linguistic analysis), the Sealed Archives (climate-controlled vaults
beneath the Library's main structure where materials deemed too speculative,
dangerous, or fragile for general access are stored), and individual Translating
Cells (small workspaces assigned to senior scholars for private study).

#### Leadership

The Archivist-General serves as the senior administrative position, responsible for
the Library's direction, external relationships, and decisions regarding preservation
and access. The Archive-Master is responsible for the physical organization,
cataloging, and maintenance of the collection. Senior Watchers oversee specific
research areas, and Junior Watchers perform translation work, document handling,
and research support.

The Archivist-General possesses discretionary authority to override standard access
protocols for the Sealed Archives when a research situation warrants expedited
access.

#### Library Holdings on Akovar (~800 AW)

The Library of the Ages possesses extremely limited Akovarian references: six single
mentions (brief references in texts primarily about other subjects) and three
substantive descriptions (detailed passages providing meaningful information), for
a total of nine scattered references across the Library's pre-War collection.

The three substantive references all agree that an entity called Asti was
intrinsically connected to the ley-line network, using language such as "gave life
to the sacred lines," "dwelling within the geometry of flowing power," and "when
[Asti] was taken from the world, the lines died and the light went from the land."
However, they contradict each other on the nature of Asti's connection to the
network, the cause of the network's destruction, and the fate of the Akovarian
people after the destruction.

The Library possesses sufficient institutional prestige to obtain letters of passage
from the Beneshire crown (House Thoman) for scholarly expeditions deemed significant.

### The Emerald Shields

Intellectual spies based in Saerrus, dedicated to protecting regional stability.
This group of intellectual do-gooders focuses on ferreting out threats to the
stability of the Inner Kingdoms. Their ranks are complemented by a large number of
bards, druids, and sorcerers.

The Shields operate primarily through intelligence gathering, infiltration, and
discreet intervention rather than direct military action. Their agents maintain cover
identities that allow them to move through society without attracting attention:
traveling merchants, entertainers, scholars, minor clergy, and other roles that
provide plausible reasons to visit remote areas and ask questions.

The Shields maintain a communication network operating through selected innkeepers,
merchants, and minor officials along the major roads of the Inner Kingdoms. Messages
are encoded in a standard cipher known to all field agents.

The Shields' relationship with the Federation of Man is complex. Both organizations
serve the stability of the Inner Kingdoms, but they occasionally disagree about
methods and priorities. The Federation operates through official channels; the
Shields operate through unofficial channels, infiltration, espionage, and actions
that sometimes raise ethical questions.

### The Umbral Hand

Fanatical worshipers of Zebulba who seek nothing short of the annihilation of the
universe. Led by a shadowy figure known as Noctuminalis (who awakens in 819 AW),
the ranks of the Umbral Hand are filled with clerics and oathbreaker paladins as
well as all manner of undead. They constantly seek ways to bring down governments,
destroy opposing religions, and help the arrow of time continue its path toward
disorder and entropy.

Before Noctuminalis's awakening, Zebulban cult cells operate independently and
without coordination from a supreme authority. Individual cells are self-directed,
varying in size from small covens to organized operations of approximately twenty
members.

Typical cell structure consists of an inner circle of four to five members handling
leadership and specialist functions, and an outer circle of approximately sixteen
members performing routine tasks, surveillance, and labor. Cells systematically
investigate sites of magical resonance, particularly Yrugas ruins, searching for
locations where the boundary between the Material Plane and the Negative Energy
Plane is thin.

### Other Organizations (Brief Reference)

**The Dark Whispers** — The secret police of Pragos. One of the most feared groups
of the Inner Kingdoms, purported to not even exist. A journey to the secret prisons
of the Dark Whispers is said to be always a one-way trip.

**Church of Tiovin (Inner Kingdoms)** — With major churches in almost every major
city, the Church ministers to spiritual needs while assisting the sick, hungry, and
poor. Unlike the Tiovinians from southern nations such as Tentigo, the Inner
Kingdoms churches do not set out on crusades or sell forgiveness for gold.

**Protectors of the Shire** — Rangers and paladins, many of them elven, sworn to
protect Beneshire's remaining forests and lands from harm. Usually work with the
Federation of Man and the royal guard of Beneshire.

**Pirates of the Crescent Coast** — A large organized force of pirates under a
mysterious leader known as the Chimera. Enormous Federation resources are spent
fighting these raiders, and merchants lose incredible amounts of gold and goods
every year.

**Circle of the Eastern Watch** — Based at Fort Stoneshaper, this council of twelve
specialists (six powerful mages and six veteran warriors) coordinates the
Federation's response to supernatural threats plaguing the eastern territories.
The Circle meets weekly, sharing intelligence, coordinating responses, and adjusting
bounties for adventurers brave enough to venture into dangerous lands.

**Cults** — Dozens of small cults dedicated to forces both primal and evil are
scattered across the Inner Kingdoms: Taovanian cults, warlock cabals dedicated to
fey entities or Far Realm creatures, elemental entity worshipers, and minor entities
seeking to ascend.

## Spellcasting — Sensory Characteristics

All spellcasting on Mazariim produces observable effects that scale with the spell's
power. Spells are not subtle; even minor workings announce themselves to anyone
nearby through a combination of verbal, somatic, and sensory phenomena.

### Verbal Components

Spellcasters speak words of Draconic to shape mana into effect. The complexity of
the incantation scales directly with the spell's power:

| Spell Tier | D&D Equivalent | Draconic Syllables | Delivery |
|------------|----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Cantrip | Level 0 | 1 syllable or shaped exhalation | Barely noticeable |
| Low | Levels 1-3 | 1-3 syllables | A murmur, a single word, a brief phrase |
| Mid | Levels 4-6 | 4-6 syllables | A spoken sentence, deliberate and measured |
| High | Levels 7-9 | 7-9 syllables | A sustained invocation, resonant and commanding |

The syllables are not arbitrary; they are precise phonetic keys that shape how mana
responds to the caster's will. Mispronunciation does not merely weaken a spell; it
changes what the spell does, sometimes dangerously. Low-tier syllables taste of
metal or ozone; mid-tier invocations produce a buzzing sensation in the teeth and
jaw; high-tier incantations resonate in the chest and can be felt by nearby observers
as a vibration in the air itself.

### Somatic Components

Most spells require hand movements complementing the verbal component, ranging from
a single finger-trace for a cantrip to elaborate geometric gestures for high-tier
workings. Somatic components serve a functional purpose: they help the caster
visualize and direct the geometric patterns through which mana flows.

### Observable Effects

Spellcasting produces the following sensory effects that scale with power:

**Visual Effects:** Low-tier spells produce subtle visual disturbances (a flicker of
light, a brief shimmer). Mid-tier spells generate unmistakable visual phenomena
(colored light, visible geometric patterns forming briefly in the air). High-tier
spells produce dramatic displays (sustained light, visible mana currents,
environmental effects such as shadows deepening or colors shifting).

**Auditory Effects:** Beyond the caster's spoken Draconic, spells produce secondary
sounds. Low-tier workings create faint hums or soft chimes. Mid-tier spells generate
audible tones or harmonic resonances. High-tier castings produce sounds felt as much
as heard: deep subsonic vibrations or sustained tones that linger.

**Olfactory Effects:** Most spells produce a brief scent. Low-tier workings leave
traces of ozone, copper, or warm stone. Mid-tier spells produce stronger scents
tied to the spell's school (sulphur for fire magic, petrichor for divination, cold
iron for abjuration). High-tier castings can fill a room with scent intense enough
to taste.

**Tactile Effects:** Nearby observers may feel pressure changes, temperature shifts,
or tingling sensations. Effects range from noticeable only within arm's reach
(low-tier) to felt across a large hall or courtyard (high-tier).

### Detection and Concealment

Because spellcasting produces observable sensory effects, casting in public or in
the presence of others is rarely discreet. Specialized techniques exist for reducing
a spell's sensory footprint, including metamagic practices, certain enchanted items,
and the Still Spell and Silent Spell disciplines taught at advanced academies. These
techniques are uncommon, difficult to master, and imperfect.

## Mana-Flow Thermodynamics

Mana-flow thermodynamics is a subfield of arcane theory concerned with how mana
behaves in bulk — as a distributed field rather than as individual particles
manipulated by spellcasters. The field addresses questions such as: how does ambient
mana density vary across geographic regions? What factors influence the rate at which
mana disperses or concentrates? How do large-scale magical effects, such as
ley-lines, interact with the ambient mana field?

Mainstream scholarship models mana behavior using thermodynamic analogies: mana flows
from regions of high density to low density, reaches equilibrium over time, and
responds to external forces in predictable ways. These models are useful for
understanding everyday magical phenomena but may fail to account for geometric
principles underlying large-scale mana behavior.

An alternative approach treats mana not as a homogeneous fluid but as a structured
medium that responds differently depending on the mathematical properties of the
channels through which it flows. Both approaches have predictive value within their
domains. The question of which approach more accurately describes ley-line behavior
cannot be resolved without empirical data from a functioning or recently dormant
ley-line system.

## Ley-Line Scars

While the existing ley-line documentation describes the network's function and
history, the following details describe how dormant ley-line remnants appear in the
modern era.

### Visual Characteristics

The scars appear as lines of faded luminescence — not true light, but the memory of
light. They resemble afterimages burned into the landscape. Their edges are blurred
and wavering, similar to heat distortion rising from sun-warmed stone. Brightness
varies between lines and at different points along a single line, with intersection
points (nexus sites) appearing notably brighter. Perceiving scars requires either
magical detection or extremely rare innate gifts.

### Environmental Effects

Though the scars are magically dormant, their presence creates subtle effects
observable even to those without special perception: water flows differently where
scars cross streams (appearing to thicken or slow), vegetation grows taller and more
vigorously along scar paths, and animals occasionally exhibit unusual behavior near
intersection points. These effects are minor enough to be dismissed as natural
variation by casual observers.

### Geometric Consistency

The scars follow precise geometric relationships. They intersect at consistent
angles, follow landscape contours in predictable patterns, and form network
structures that are mathematically regular. This consistency indicates the scars are
remnants of an engineered system rather than natural phenomena.

### Scale

Hundreds of individual scar lines have been identified across Beneshire alone.
Typical scars run in parallel channels approximately thirty feet wide.

## The Seed Problem

The "seed problem" is the central obstacle to ley-line recreation. All available
sources indicate that ley-line creation required a catalyst that Asti provided. The
nature of this catalyst is the single most important unanswered question in ley-line
research.

Two possible interpretations exist:

**The Energy Hypothesis:** The seed was primarily energetic, providing the raw power
necessary to force ambient mana into geometric channels. Under this interpretation,
ley-line creation is impossible without access to divine-level energy, as the power
requirement exceeds mortal capacity by a factor of approximately 900.

**The Precision Hypothesis:** The seed was primarily geometric, providing a template
of mathematical precision that organized existing energy rather than supplying new
energy. Under this interpretation, the power requirement is significantly lower
(approximately 75 times mortal capacity), because the most energy-intensive step
— converting random mana into ordered flow — is replaced by the less demanding
process of aligning pre-organized mana along established geometric patterns.

Neither hypothesis can be confirmed or refuted without primary source data describing
the actual initiation process.

## The Akovarian Tome

A rare Akovarian text recovered by a Sabonaar expedition into Akovar's interior,
transported to New Sabonaa, shipped to the Crescent Coast, forwarded to the
University of Wyebluff (which could not translate it), and finally sent to the
Library of the Ages. This chain of custody — spanning months of travel, thousands
of miles of ocean, and multiple institutions — underscores the rarity of primary
Akovarian sources.

### Contents

The tome contains 43 pages of network diagrams depicting ley-line nexus
configurations. Each diagram shows a single nexus with radiating lines connecting
to adjacent nexuses, varying in complexity from three-line intersections to
twelve-line convergences. One diagram (near the tome's middle) depicts a regional
overview showing dozens of nexuses connected in a hierarchical web.

The majority of the tome consists of text written in geometric Akovarian script with
forty-three unique characters, consistent with an alphabetic or syllabic writing
system. The text presumably provides geographical coordinates, engineering parameters,
and explanatory content for the diagrams.

The regional overview diagram depicts a network section showing a hierarchical
structure: one primary nexus (large triangular symbol) at center, seven primary
channels (thick lines) radiating to secondary nexuses, and secondary channels
branching to tertiary nodes. This structure matches the known Akovarian
temple-network layout where seven major ley-lines connected the seven largest cities
to the capital.

### Significance

Systematic comparison of the tome's diagrams with independently mapped ley-line scar
intersections in Beneshire shows mathematically consistent angular distribution
patterns across both data sets. This represents the first empirical confirmation
that modern ley-line scar observations correspond to the actual engineered system
documented by the civilization that built it.

New Sabonaa is the only permanent settlement of Inner Kingdom or Merchant Nation
origin on the continent of Akovar. Established by Sabonaar explorers from Naarlam,
it clings to the western coast of the Akovarian archipelago. The settlement consists
of a fortified harbor, a trading post, a small chapel of Tiovin, warehouses for
storing recovered artifacts and trade goods, and a cluster of timber-and-stone
buildings. A wooden palisade surrounds the settlement's landward perimeter —
necessary protection against jungle wildlife and occasional territorial incursions
by dragonborn clans.

### The Voyage

The journey from the Crescent Coast to New Sabonaa takes approximately three to four
months under favorable conditions, crossing open ocean that no reliable charts fully
document. Ships sail perhaps twice a year, timed to avoid the worst storm seasons.
The route passes through waters claimed by the Pirates of the Crescent Coast and
requires navigation skills that few captains possess. An estimated one in five ships
that attempt the voyage fail to arrive.

### The Interior

Beyond New Sabonaa's palisade walls, Akovar is a continent that has spent over seven
thousand years returning to wilderness. Jungle canopy covers the ruins of
pyramid-temples and geometric city grids. The descendants of Akovarians who survived
the ley-line detonation have evolved into distinct peoples: dragonborn clans (mutated
by draconic energy during the catastrophe), genasi tribes (infused with elemental
forces released by the network's collapse), and scattered human communities who
retain fragmented oral traditions of their ancestors' achievements but possess none
of their knowledge or power.

Expeditions into the interior are dangerous, expensive, and frequently fatal. The
artifacts and texts recovered from these expeditions represent the only primary
sources of Akovarian knowledge available to modern scholars.

## Minor Yrugas Ruins in Beneshire

While the major Yrugas sites such as the Hollow Sepulcher are well-known and
monitored by the Federation, hundreds of minor ruins are scattered across the Inner
Kingdoms, particularly in Beneshire, which was densely settled during the
mageocracy's height. These include collapsed research towers, overgrown foundations
of administrative complexes, remnants of agricultural facilities that used magical
enhancement, fragments of the road network, and occasional standing structures
preserved by residual enchantments.

Most minor ruins have been thoroughly scavenged over three millennia and contain
little of value. However, underground chambers, sealed vaults, and magically
preserved storage areas occasionally survive intact, and new discoveries continue to
surface as agricultural expansion, road construction, or natural erosion exposes
previously hidden structures.

## Three Ancient Exploration Sites

The following three sites represent significant locations for ley-line research,
each containing knowledge relevant to understanding the ancient ley-line network.

### Site One: The Athenaeum of Echoes

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Official Yrugas Name | Athenaeum Resonantia (The Athenaeum of Echoes) |
| Common Name | The Echo Halls / The Whispering Ruins |
| Type | Yrugas Ruins (Research and Academic Complex) |
| Original Function | Center for studying pre-Yrugas magical phenomena and ancient texts |
| Location | Eastern Beneshire, rocky highlands east of the Misty Forest |
| Era Built | ~3,600 BW (expanded multiple times) |
| Era Destroyed | ~2,700 BW (Fall of Yrugas) |
| Current State | Surface structures collapsed; underground levels partially intact; magically unstable zones |
| Nearby Settlements | Village of Thornwick (12 miles west, population ~200) avoids the area |

#### Historical Background

The Athenaeum of Echoes was founded during Yrugas's middle period by a consortium of
wizard-lords who recognized that their mageocracy, for all its power, was built upon
incomplete foundations. Magic had existed before Yrugas. The dragons who taught the
first human mages spoke of earlier civilizations, of powers now lost, of patterns in
the world that no living creature fully understood.

Unlike most Yrugas institutions — which focused on accumulating personal power,
creating weapons, or controlling populations — the Athenaeum was dedicated to
understanding. Its founders believed that recovering lost magical knowledge would
grant them advantages over rival wizard-lords, but their methods were scholarly
rather than purely exploitative, making the Athenaeum unusual among Yrugas
facilities.

The complex grew over nine centuries, expanding from a single tower into a sprawling
campus of interconnected buildings, underground vaults, and specialized research
chambers. At its height, the Athenaeum employed over two hundred mages, scribes,
translators, and support staff.

#### Three Primary Divisions

**The Resonance Observatory** occupied the eastern wing, built around a central tower
that rose two hundred feet above the surrounding highlands. This division studied
"resonance phenomena" — locations where magic behaved differently, where spells were
stronger or weaker, where the fabric of reality seemed thin or thick. The
Observatory's primary instrument was the Oculus Terra — a massive magical apparatus
combining enchanted lenses, scrying pools, and crystalline matrices that allowed
mages to perceive magical geography across vast distances. Through the Oculus,
researchers mapped over three hundred "zones of resonance" throughout the Inner
Kingdoms, unknowingly creating the most comprehensive record of ley-line scar
locations that would exist for over three thousand years.

**The Translation Chamber** occupied the western wing, devoted to linguistic and
historical research. Scholars worked to decipher texts from pre-Yrugas civilizations.
The most prized possessions were seventeen fragments of Akovarian text, acquired
through unspecified means. Partial translations suggested references to "lines of
power," "geometric flows," and "the pyramid's heart."

**The Conjunction Archive** occupied the underground levels, a vast library containing
every text, map, observation record, and theoretical treatise produced by the
researchers. The Archive also stored "anomalous artifacts" — objects exhibiting
unexplained magical properties, often recovered from resonance zones.

#### The Scar Nexus

The Athenaeum's location was chosen because early Yrugas surveyors identified it as
a "zone of persistent resonance." The founders had built atop a minor ley-line nexus
where three ley-line scars intersected beneath the highlands. The scars no longer
functioned but retained a shadow of their former nature, creating the "resonance"
that attracted attention.

This nexus made the Athenaeum ideal for magical research: spells were slightly more
stable, slightly more powerful, slightly easier to control. Scrying and divination
worked better here than almost anywhere else. The nexus also made the Athenaeum
dangerous. Reality was thin here. Researchers reported vivid dreams, moments of
disorientation, glimpses of places that did not exist. Several mages went mad;
others simply vanished.

#### Notable Discoveries

**The Resonance Atlas** — A comprehensive map of over three hundred ley-line scar
locations categorized by type and intensity.

**The Seventeen Fragments** — Partial translations of Akovarian texts referencing
"geometric patterns of power" and "the flow between sacred points."

**The Echo Hypothesis** — A theoretical framework proposed by the scholar Meridax the
Listener (~3,100 BW), suggesting that resonance zones were remnants of an artificial
system — "echoes of a structure that no longer exists." Meridax was ridiculed by
contemporaries but his hypothesis was essentially correct.

**The Conjunction Stones** — Over forty crystalline artifacts recovered from resonance
zones that grew warm near other resonance zones, hummed at frequencies just below
hearing, and occasionally projected brief images. These were actually Akovarian
ley-line monitoring devices, long since depleted but still faintly active.

#### The Fall

When Yrugas collapsed in ~2,700 BW, the Athenaeum suffered the same fate as most
mageocracy institutions. The slave revolt reached the highland complex on the third
day of the uprising. Surface buildings burned. The great Oculus Terra was toppled.
But the underground levels survived — the Conjunction Archive's vault doors proved
equally effective against mundane assault. The magical catastrophe that created the
Hollow Sepulcher sent shockwaves through the ley-line scars, further destabilizing
the nexus, but the Archive's deep chambers were shielded.

For over three thousand years, the Archive has waited — sealed, undisturbed,
containing the original Akovarian fragments, the Resonance Atlas, the Conjunction
Stones, and centuries of research notes.

#### Current State

**Surface Ruins:** A maze of collapsed walls, fire-blackened stone, and encroaching
vegetation. The Observatory tower's lower forty feet remain standing. Fragments of
the Oculus Terra can still be found in the debris field — shards of enchanted crystal
that occasionally spark with residual magic.

**Underground Levels:** The Conjunction Archive extends three levels below ground:
Level One (Processing Halls — partially flooded), Level Two (Primary Archive — main
storage area with climate-control enchantments that may still function), and Level
Three (Secured Vault — the most valuable and dangerous materials behind independent
defensive wards).

**Hazards:** Undead remnants animated by the nexus's energy and the Hollow
Sepulcher's distant influence. Magical instability causing spells to behave
unpredictably. Temporal echoes — vivid sensory impressions of events from the
Athenaeum's operation or destruction. Structural decay and potential collapse.
Local legends speak of "The Watcher" — a robed figure seen at the Observatory
tower's base on certain nights that stands motionless for hours then vanishes.

#### Key Contents

The Resonance Atlas (Level Two), the original seventeen Akovarian fragments (Level
Three vault), the Conjunction Stones (Level Three), research notes spanning centuries,
Meridax the Listener's Echo Hypothesis treatise, and baseline observation records of
the nexus.

### Site Two: The Geometrist's Tower

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Common Name | The Geometrist's Tower / The Twisted Spire / Thornspire |
| Type | Yrugas Ruins (Private Research Tower) |
| Original Function | Personal research facility for ley-line scar studies |
| Location | Remote highlands of eastern Beneshire, near the border with Arogror |
| Era Built | ~2,850 BW |
| Era Destroyed | ~2,700 BW (Fall of Yrugas) |
| Current State | Partially standing; reality distortion; haunted by a greater wraith |

#### Historical Background

The tower was built by Vorthax, a Yrugas-era wizard who studied at the Athenaeum of
Echoes for thirty years before being marginalized for his theory that the resonance
zones were remnants of an artificial network — "geometric flows" created by a
pre-Yrugas civilization. Rejected by the establishment, Vorthax used his family's
wealth to purchase a remote tract of highland territory sitting at the intersection
of five major ley-line scars, the most complex nexus he had ever identified.

The completed tower rose seven stories, its silhouette distinctively twisted — each
level rotated slightly from the one below, creating a subtle spiral. The interior
geometry was stranger still, with rooms that seemed larger than the exterior allowed
and corridors that curved in impossible ways.

For 155 years, Vorthax pursued research along three tracks: developing mathematical
models of ley-line mechanics, attempting to manipulate the ley-line scars through
practical experimentation, and seeking evidence of the civilization that built the
original network. His models predicted behaviors that matched his observations with
uncanny accuracy, making this the most comprehensive ley-line research ever conducted
by a mortal.

#### The Portal

Seventy years into his research, Vorthax discovered a portal buried beneath his
tower — predating not only his construction but Yrugas itself. The portal was a wound
in reality where the ley-line destruction of 6,830 BW had torn through to the
continent of Akovar. Over ten years, Vorthax learned to activate and stabilize the
portal, then spent fifty years making expeditions to Akovarian ruins, recovering
texts, artifacts, and observations that validated his theories. He learned the flows
were called "ley-lines," powered by an entity called Asti, and the geometric
principles underlying their construction.

However, Vorthax also learned he lacked a critical component. Ley-lines required a
"seed" — a source of divine or draconic power to initiate the flow. He had neither.

#### The Fall and Transformation

When Yrugas collapsed in ~2,700 BW, Vorthax chose to stay in his tower rather than
flee through the portal, unwilling to abandon his life's work. His wards held
against rebel forces, but when the Hollow Sepulcher was created, the magical
shockwave channeled enormous necrotic energies through the nexus directly into the
tower. Vorthax was caught at the exact moment of an experiment to create a ley-line
seed. The two forces interacted in impossible ways.

He became a greater wraith — incorporeal, intelligent, possessing the magical power
of a wizard-lord, but bound to the ley-line scars. He cannot leave the nexus beneath
his tower, but through those scars his perception extends along every branch and
intersection, sensing disturbances across hundreds of miles. For approximately 3,500
years, Vorthax has existed in this state — aware, obsessed, and trapped.

His sanity has not survived intact. He remembers being Vorthax, but that person
feels distant. He is now something more like a force than a person — the embodiment
of obsessive knowledge, of understanding without wisdom, of brilliance corrupted by
endless frustration.

#### Current State

**The Approach:** The highlands radiate wrongness perceptible even to magically
insensitive visitors. Animals avoid the region; plants grow stunted and twisted. An
invisible boundary marks where the nexus's effects intensify dramatically.

**The Exterior:** The seven-story tower remains standing, preserved by unintended
magical forces. Walls do not meet at proper angles. Windows are the wrong shape. The
spiral becomes more pronounced the longer one looks.

**The Interior:** Proportions do not match exterior observations. The central
staircase has a different number of steps each time it is climbed.

Lower Floors (1-3) housed support functions and have suffered the most decay. Middle
Floors (4-5) contain Vorthax's living quarters and a library of thousands of
volumes, preserved by minor wards. Over a hundred personal journals document 155
years of research. Upper Floors (6-7) contain the primary research laboratories with
crystalline matrices, geometric frameworks, and mana-focusing devices still faintly
active. The highest chamber's walls are covered with notes, diagrams, and equations
— a complete summary of his life's work.

**Below Ground:** Three basement levels descend into bedrock following the nexus's
geometry. The lowest level contains the portal to Akovar — a permanent gateway,
a vertical disc of shimmering energy ten feet in diameter, opening onto the ruined
Temple of the Seventh Convergence.

**The Wraith:** Vorthax is not always present in perceivable form. When living beings
enter his domain, he notices — eventually. He manifests as a robed figure that
flickers between visibility and transparency. His behavior depends on visitors'
nature: scholars interest him (he craves intellectual engagement after millennia of
isolation), thieves infuriate him, and those who might complete his research
represent his greatest hope and greatest fear.

#### Key Contents

Vorthax's complete 155-year research archive, the portal to Akovar, documentation
of the "seed" problem and all attempted solutions, Akovarian translations, techniques
for manipulating ley-line scars, and the slave quarters on level two — a haunting
reminder of the Yrugas mageocracy's human cost.

### Site Three: The Temple of the Seventh Convergence

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Akovarian Name | Aset-Khemu-Sekhept (Temple of the Seventh Sacred Joining) |
| Common Name | The Temple of the Seventh Convergence |
| Type | Akovarian Ruins (Pyramid-Temple Complex) |
| Original Function | Major ley-line nexus management; religious center; knowledge repository |
| Location | Central island of the Akovar archipelago |
| Era Built | ~8,100 BW (construction spanned approximately a century) |
| Era Destroyed | ~6,830 BW (Dragon War; ley-line detonation) |
| Current State | Partially collapsed; deep chambers intact; claimed by jungle |
| Environment | Tropical; uninhabited by intelligent beings for millennia |
| Access | Via portal in Geometrist's Tower basement |

#### Historical Background

The Temple of the Seventh Convergence was the last of seven great ley-line nodes
constructed by the Akovarian civilization, completed around 8,000 BW. Its location
on a large island in the center of the archipelago made it a natural hub, receiving
energy from the capital and distributing it to the southern islands. The pyramid rose
three hundred feet above the jungle floor, its limestone casing blazing white in the
tropical sun. Seven smaller pyramids surrounded the main structure. The complex
housed over two thousand permanent residents and served as a pilgrimage site for tens
of thousands more.

#### The Three Orders

The Temple was served by three orders of priests:

**The Luminaries** were religious leaders — healers, teachers, and intermediaries
between the people and Asti's divine presence. Their magic was purely divine, granted
directly by Asti.

**The Geometers** were technical specialists — engineers, mathematicians, and
artificers who understood how the ley-line network actually functioned. Many combined
divine and arcane traditions in ways unique to Akovar.

**The Archivists** were record-keepers — scholars who maintained the temple's
extensive libraries and preserved knowledge for future generations.

#### The Dragon War and Catastrophe

The Temple was attacked early in the Dragon War (~6,850 BW). A flight of chromatic
dragons struck without warning, but the temple's defenders fought back effectively —
the ley-line nexus amplified their magic. The first assault was repelled, but over
twenty years the garrison was gradually worn down by attrition.

When the Akovarian priests at the capital transferred Asti's essence into the Mask
and initiated mass teleportation, the ley-line network convulsed. At the Temple,
the terminus ruptured, releasing millennia of accumulated magical energy in seconds.
The upper levels were vaporized. Only the deepest chambers, carved from bedrock and
shielded by the earth, survived.

The priests who survived longest were those who embraced a terrible adaptation. Wild
magic flooding the deep chambers offered a form of preservation — suspension between
life and death requiring no food, water, or hope. Some accepted this fate willingly,
becoming guardians of knowledge they could not bear to see lost.

#### Current State

**The Engineering Core (Deepest Level):** Accessible via the portal from the
Geometrist's Tower. A maze of chambers and specialized facilities that once managed
the ley-line nexus. Key locations include the Flow Chamber (containing a fifty-foot-
tall crystalline focusing matrix), the Monitoring Station (with devices that retain
residual function), the Archive Vault (the most valuable collection of ley-line
knowledge in existence, with approximately two-thirds surviving), and the Guardian
Chamber (where suspended priests have waited for over seven millennia in stasis
fields maintained by wild magic — neither alive, dead, nor undead).

**The Administrative Levels (Middle Depths):** More damage from collapse and flooding.
Contains the Secondary Archive (copies of important documents), Training Halls
(where young Geometers learned their craft — containing a complete educational
curriculum), and the Communication Center (non-functional equipment but potentially
surviving records).

**The Upper Ruins (Surface Level):** The pyramid's upper levels were destroyed and
claimed by jungle. The surface includes the collapsed Observation Platform (where
astronomers tracked celestial movements relevant to ley-line operation), seven
Subsidiary Pyramids (less damaged than the main structure), and Inscription Walls
(exterior surfaces with religious texts and historical records in protected areas).

**Hazards:** Tropical environment (heat, humidity, insects, disease), wildlife
evolved in a wild-magic environment with potentially unusual abilities, dense
vegetation, structural instability, wild magic saturation causing spell instability
and spontaneous magical effects, and the risk of mutation from prolonged wild magic
exposure.

#### Key Contents

Complete ley-line engineering documentation in the Archive Vault, information about
the "seed" mystery (what Asti's role was in ley-line creation), the Flow Chamber's
crystalline matrix as physical proof of ley-line engineering principles, the
suspended guardian priests as potential witnesses to Akovarian civilization,
educational curriculum materials from the Training Halls, and artifacts demonstrating
Akovarian engineering techniques.

## Mauian Religious Customs

The following Mauian customs and teachings are practiced at the Library of the Ages
and other temples of Mau:

**Core Teaching:** "Mau sees all things, and in the seeing, all things become real.
That which is observed cannot be unobserved. That which is recorded cannot be
unrecorded. The Watcher's duty is not to understand, but to preserve — for
understanding may come to those who follow, even if it escapes those who first
perceive."

**"I see you"** — Traditional greeting, acknowledging another's existence without
presuming to know their state or intentions. Also spoken to inanimate objects,
natural phenomena, or abstract concepts as an act of observation.

**Coded knock-sequences** — The Library's clergy use specific knock patterns to
identify themselves before entering private spaces.

**Midnight-blue robes** — The traditional garment of Mau's Watchers, appearing
almost black in dim lighting, with subtle silver embroidery forming eye symbols
and geometric star patterns along hems and cuffs. Rank is denoted by additional
silver embroidery at the cuffs.
