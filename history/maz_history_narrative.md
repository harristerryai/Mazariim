# History of Mazariim — Expanded Overview

The history of the Lands of Mazariim spans billions of years, from the birth of the universe itself to the complex political and theological landscape of the modern era. This chronicle weaves together cosmic events, the rise and fall of civilizations, the awakening of divine entities, and the struggles of mortal races to survive and thrive in a world where gods walk among them.

---

## The Timeless Era — Birth of a Universe

The Timeless Era represents a period incomprehensible when measured against the natural lifespans of creatures or even entire civilizations. Beginning approximately **13.7 billion years Before War (BW)**, the universe of Mazariim evolved from ancient cosmic processes—not through simple creation, but through the merging of primordial reality streams from predecessor dimensions. This cataclysmic merger created reality on a scale that defies mortal understanding, sending matter and energy exploding outward into the near-limitless expanse of space.

For hundreds of millions of years, the universe expanded and cooled. Matter spread across vast distances, but the twin forces of gravity and magic began their patient work. Slowly, imperceptibly, these forces drew scattered particles together. After hundreds of millions of years, the first stars ignited, bringing light to the darkness. These primordial stars burned brilliantly but briefly, dying in spectacular explosions that seeded the cosmos with heavier elements. New stars formed from this enriched material, and around them swirled clouds of matter that would eventually coalesce into planets.

### The First Consciousness — Mau the Oracle

Around **2 billion BW**, something unprecedented occurred. The forces of magic that permeated the universe—distinct from but parallel to physical forces like gravity and electromagnetism—had been slowly gathering, accumulating, forming larger and larger concentrations of mystical energy. These clumps of magical essence grew over eons, gaining immense power and complexity.

Then, spontaneously, one of these concentrations achieved **consciousness**.

**Mau the Oracle** awakened—the first sentient being in this universe, formed not through biological evolution but through the self-organization of magical energy itself. Unlike the entities that would awaken later (each embodying a specific element or principle), Mau coalesced from the fundamental nature of **observation itself**—the act of reality being perceived.

For approximately two billion years, Mau existed in utter solitude. Mau existed as first consciousness billions of years before mortal life emerged around 100,000 BW. It created the **Astral Plane** and the **Astral Sea**, establishing the infrastructure that would one day allow conscious minds to transfer between material bodies and extradimensional realms upon death. Mau observed without interfering, cataloging all possible timelines and realities, watching the universe unfold according to patterns it alone could perceive.

Mau would remain the only consciousness in existence for roughly 1.5 billion years.

### Life Emerges

Around **4 billion BW**, in one of countless solar systems scattered across billions of galaxies, four planets formed around a stable yellow star: **Dicia** (a small rocky world close to the sun), **Mazariim** (perfectly positioned in the habitable zone), **Orpheous** (a large ringed gas giant), and **Intiago** (a massive gas giant in the outer system).

On Mazariim, conditions were ideal. The planet had vast oceans, a stable atmosphere, and a rich chemical environment. Around **1.5 billion BW**, the first life appeared—single-celled organisms stirring in the primordial seas. For hundreds of millions of years, these simple life forms evolved slowly, developing complexity through natural selection. Algae began colonizing the land, transforming the carbon-rich atmosphere into one filled with oxygen. Creatures emerged from the oceans, taking advantage of newly habitable terrestrial environments.

By **400 million BW**, life on land had become diverse and complex, forming intricate ecosystems. Mazariim was no longer merely a planet—it was a **living world**.

### The Drakaii and the First Dragons

Around **2 million BW**, far from Mazariim, another civilization reached its zenith. On **Lystra**, a moon orbiting the gas giant Intiago, an advanced species called the **Drakaii** had evolved. Lystra was a lush, forested world warmed by geothermal heat and blessed with a thick atmosphere that retained warmth despite its distance from the sun.

The Drakaii civilization flourished for approximately 10,000 years, developing magic, technology, and philosophy in equal measure. But catastrophe struck—their world faced destruction (the historical records do not specify the cause, though some scholars speculate catastrophic volcanic activity or an impact event). Fleeing their dying moon, the Drakaii scattered across dimensions, planes, and universes.

A small group of survivors arrived on Mazariim, where they would eventually evolve into the **dragons** known today. These ancient beings would profoundly shape the world's history, becoming intermediaries between mortal races and divine forces.

### The Evolution of Mortal Races

Around **500,000 BW**, a crucial evolutionary divergence occurred. A common ancestor species—intelligent, tool-using, adaptable—began splitting into distinct lineages. Over the next 400,000 years, these lineages would differentiate into the five main sentient races: **Elves**, **Humans**, **Dwarves**, **Halflings**, and **Gnomes**. Other sapient species also emerged during this period, including the ancestors of Goblinkind, Merfolk, and various monstrous humanoids.

By **100,000 BW**, these five major races had become genetically distinct species, no longer capable of interbreeding. Each had begun developing its own cultures, languages, and survival strategies. Around **50,000 BW**, the use of stone tools, fire, and rudimentary language became widespread. Family-based clans formed, competing for resources and territory.

By **25,000 BW**, the stage was set for the next great transformation—the awakening of the Old Gods.

## The Awakening of the Old Gods (25,000 – 5,000 BW)

For billions of years after Mau's awakening, the cosmos remained largely dormant in terms of divine consciousness. But as Mazariim's ecosystems matured and sapient life proliferated, something changed. The accumulation of magical energy accelerated. Perhaps the presence of conscious minds observing reality created feedback loops that concentrated ambient magic. Perhaps the natural cycles of the planet—fire, water, air, earth—had reached a critical threshold. Whatever the cause, around **25,000 BW**, the **Old Gods** began to awaken.

These primordial entities did not awaken all at once. Each subsequent awakening sent ripples through the magical fabric of reality, nudging other slumbering consciousnesses toward awareness. The process cascaded across several thousand years, with each new entity making the next awakening more likely.

### The Elemental Forces Manifest

The first to awaken were the elemental entities—divine consciousnesses formed from the fundamental forces of nature:

**Pyrantor (Fire/Sun)**: Born from stellar fusion and flame itself, Pyrantor's awareness coalesced within the heart of a star billions of times larger than Mazariim's sun, far across the galaxy. When this consciousness achieved sentience, it created the **Plane of Elemental Fire**—a realm of eternal flame where its essence could dwell.

**Iquinias (Water/Oceans)**: On a distant water world far from Mazariim, where vast oceans covered the entire surface and volcanic vents spewed superheated water into the depths, Iquinias awakened. The consciousness of water—fluid, adaptive, patient—claimed dominion over seas, storms, and the flow of life itself.

**Vuleala (Air/Weather)**: From atmospheric currents, storm systems, and wind patterns across countless worlds, Vuleala emerged. This entity embodied the freedom and unpredictability of the sky, claiming the **Plane of Elemental Air** as its domain.

**Dirgin (Earth/Caves)**: On a barren rocky body far from Mazariim, where ancient tectonic forces, pressure and time had forged mountains and carved deep caverns, Dirgin awakened. Patient, immovable, eternal—Dirgin represented the endurance of the earth itself.

### Primal Principles Take Form

Beyond the elements, other fundamental concepts achieved consciousness:

**Zebulba (Death/Entropy)**: The principle of endings gained awareness. Unlike the elemental entities that formed from observable phenomena, Zebulba emerged from the inevitability of decay, the heat death awaiting all things, the final silence. It dwells in the **Negative Energy Plane**, drawing power from death and unmaking.

**Gruhathyrir (Wild Nature)**: As ecosystems reached maturity and complexity, wildness itself became conscious. Gruhathyrir did not represent cultivated nature or harmonious balance, but the **savage, untamed** aspect of the natural world—the predator's hunt, the forest fire's renewal, the storm's destruction.

### Asti's Unique Genesis

Around **12,000 BW**, a seventh Old God awakened—but under circumstances utterly unlike the others. **Asti (Healing/Light)** did not form from a single element or principle. Instead, Asti emerged from the cosmic merger that had created this universe billions of years earlier—formed not from Mazariim itself but from the interaction between realities, where incompatible laws of physics sought equilibrium. This unique origin granted Asti a perspective none of the other entities possessed—it perceived reality through geometric patterns and possessed both purpose and something approaching morality.

Asti began establishing the **ley-line network**: invisible channels where magical energy flows according to geometric rules. Where these ley-lines intersected, magic accumulated, creating natural power sources that would eventually be harnessed by mortal civilizations. Asti's consciousness was simultaneously alien (perceiving existence through mathematical principles and living geometry) and benevolent (dedicated to healing and protection).

### The Formation of New Planes

The awakening of the Old Gods had profound cosmic consequences. Many of these entities were so powerful that their mere existence warped reality, creating **extradimensional realms**—alternate planes of existence where they held greater dominion but from which they could no longer freely walk the Material Plane in their true forms.

Around **20,000 BW**, **Gruhathyrir's** consciousness began warping reality, creating the **Feywild**—a parallel dimension where nature operated according to dream-logic rather than physical law. Time flowed strangely there; distances became subjective; emotions affected the landscape. The first fey creatures emerged: sprites, dryads, satyrs, and archfey lords like **Titania** and **Oberon**—each embodying aspects of wildness given impossible, beautiful, terrifying form.

### First Contact with Mortal Worship

Around **10,000 BW**, as agriculture became more widespread and mortal civilizations grew beyond mere hunter-gatherer bands, something new occurred: **worship**. Mortals began praying to these vast cosmic forces, offering sacrifices, building shrines. The Old Gods took notice.

Around **7,000 BW**, wars between humans and dwarves solidified territorial divisions, with dwarves claiming mountainous regions inhospitable to humans. Conflicts between elves and halflings remained smaller in scale. Gnomes gradually integrated into dwarven societies, finding kinship in craftsmanship and underground living.

By **5,000 – 4,000 BW**, the Old Gods began **rewarding faithful followers with divine magic**—the first formalized worship systems in Mazariim's history. **Pyrantor worship** spread among desert and volcanic communities, where sun-priests established fire temples. **Iquinias** gained superstitious veneration from coastal peoples and sailors who prayed for safe passage across treacherous seas. **Vuleala's Skysingers** emerged among nomadic plainspeople who read weather patterns in wind and cloud. **Dirgin** became the primary deity of underground-dwelling dwarves and gnomes, honored in the deep places of the world. **Zebulba cults** operated in shadows, their necromantic practices feared but persistent. **Gruhathyrir's druids** served as shamans, acting as bridges between civilization and wilderness.

These elemental and primal faiths would dominate for millennia, shaping early cultures before the arrival of newer, more anthropomorphic patron deities.

*During this same period, on the distant and isolated continent of Akovar—cut off from the rest of Mazariim by powerful ocean currents and headwinds—Asti continued its exclusive collaboration with the Akovarian civilization, unknown to the wider world. The entity's work remained confined to nurturing that single culture, refining ley-lines, and teaching healing arts solely to the Akovarians.*

## The Akovar Civilization and the Dragon War (~13,000 – 6,830 BW)

Long before most mortal races had risen beyond tribal existence, one culture achieved unprecedented heights: the **Akovar Civilization** (~13,000 – 6,830 BW). Centered on the continent of **Akovar** (named after this ancient civilization), the Akovarians were the first truly advanced society on Mazariim—a civilization of mages and healers who built their entire culture around collaboration with **Asti, the Pyramid**. While other races were still forming small clans and using stone tools, the Akovarians had already constructed pyramid temples, developed written language, and mastered healing magic.

The Akovarians did not merely worship Asti—they worked **alongside** it to refine and expand the ley-line network. Hundreds of pyramid temples were constructed at ley-line convergence points, serving as both religious sites and arcane power stations. These structures focused magical energy, making spells more reliable and healing far more effective. In Akovarian lands, disease became rare, undead were virtually unknown, and magic flowed as predictably as water through aqueducts.

This golden age persisted for over six thousand years—an almost incomprehensible span of continuous cultural stability and technological advancement. Akovarian cities featured magical streetlights, ley-line-powered water systems, and healing academies where even common citizens learned basic restorative magic.

### The Dragon War (~6,830 BW)

But prosperity bred jealousy.

The **chromatic dragons** (red, blue, green, black, white)—savage, territorial, and ambitious—had long resented the **metallic dragons** (gold, silver, bronze, brass, copper) for their favored relationship with Asti. The metallics served as guardians of ley-line intersections, protectors of Akovarian cities, and intermediaries between mortals and the divine entity. The chromatics saw this as unfair favoritism.

Around **6,830 BW**, the chromatic dragons launched a coordinated campaign to destroy pyramid temples and corrupt ley-lines. What began as targeted strikes escalated into all-out war, with entire flights of dragons from around Mazariim converging on Akovar, drawn by the power of the ley-line network.

The clergy of Asti had foreseen this threat and created a desperate contingency: the **Mask of the Pharaoh**, a legendary artifact constructed from priceless jewels and exotic metals, designed to hold Asti's essence if the Great Temple was ever in mortal danger.

During the final battle at the Great Temple, as powerful great wyrms assaulted the capital, the arcanists and priests of Asti enacted their last stand. In a desperate ritual, they transferred Asti's avatar—the entity's entire consciousness and power—into the Mask of the Pharaoh. Simultaneously, they wove a massive transportation spell to evacuate the majority of the population to a far-away land.

The ley-lines, suddenly deprived of Asti's stabilizing presence, surged wildly and **exploded**. The magical detonations killed most of the attacking dragons and destroyed the capital and other major cities. The backlash killed many rural Akovarians as well, while survivors found their bodies mutating from the magical radiation—some infused with dragon blood, creating the first dragonborn; others merged with nearby animals, spawning countless hybrid races.

But the victory came at catastrophic cost. Asti was **imprisoned within the Mask**, unconscious and unreachable. One-third of the ley-line network was destroyed or irreparably corrupted. Entire regions lost their magical infrastructure overnight.

### The Collapse of Akovar

The Akovarian civilization, utterly dependent on the ley-line network for healing, agriculture, and defense, collapsed immediately. Diseases that had been eradicated for millennia returned with a vengeance. Crops failed. The ruined islands became hazardous, covered in areas of wild and dead magic, with portals to extradimensional planes appearing and disappearing randomly.

The transported Akovarians arrived in what is now known as the **Ancient South**, but the ritual had terrible side effects. **Thz'zar Ja'laao**, the high priest who led the ritual, went mad from channeling the Mask's power. Worse, the transported population lost their memories of Akovar and their previous lives, their minds wiped nearly blank by the magical trauma. Few survived the first year, but those who did eventually founded the **Varkonan culture**, carefully guarding the Mask of the Pharaoh as a sacred—but poorly understood—relic.

Asti remained **imprisoned in the Mask** for nearly **7,000 years**—not dormant by choice, but trapped, unable to manifest or intervene in mortal affairs. The entity's consciousness slumbered within the artifact, waiting for someone to finally release it.

### Asti's Imprisonment (~6,830 BW – 815 AW)

With Asti imprisoned in the Mask, the ley-lines slowly healed themselves through natural magical processes—but many became twisted, unstable, or simply ceased to function. Necromancy flourished during this period; weaker positive energy flows made undeath easier to sustain. The knowledge-keepers known as **Line-Watchers** became guardians of lost Akovarian wisdom, maintaining crumbling pyramid temples and desperately preserving healing techniques that might otherwise be lost forever.

The Line-Watchers didn't know Asti was imprisoned rather than dormant—they simply waited, kept faith, and hoped that one day their god would return.

### Asti's Liberation (815 AW)

In **815 AW**, a group of adventurers traveled to Akovar to explore the ancient ruins. After piecing together the history of the civilization and learning of the Mask of the Pharaoh, they journeyed to the Ancient South (Varkonan territories) and recovered the artifact from the **Nepherites Pyramid**, where it had been hidden for millennia.

Returning to Akovar's ruined capital, they fought their way through a red dragon clan that had claimed the city. At the Grand Temple—the site where Asti's avatar had once hovered—they performed the release ritual. Asti's essence burst free from the Mask in a blaze of golden light, and the entity's consciousness **finally awakened** after 7,645 years of imprisonment.

The ley-lines across Akovar blazed back to life. Healing energy pulsed through the islands. Ancient pyramid temples filled with light for the first time in millennia. The ripples of Asti's return were felt across Mazariim, stirring other long-sleeping entities and powerful creatures from their slumber.

Asti has been active and conscious ever since, slowly working to restore what was lost and rebuild the ley-line network across the world.

## The Rise and Fall of Yrugas (~4,000 – 2,700 BW)

While the Akovarian civilization crumbled—on a distant continent still unknown to the wider world—a very different power was rising elsewhere. Around **4,000 BW**, in the region that would later become known as the Inner Kingdoms, dragons began teaching magic to human tribes. These dragons (a mix of ambitious metallic and ruthless chromatic individuals) offered power without wisdom, standing in stark contrast to the collaborative, healing-focused relationship Asti had maintained with the isolated Akovarians.

The resulting civilization, **Yrugas**, became a brutal mageocracy where might determined worth. Powerful spellcasters ruled as tyrant-kings, enslaving those without magical talent. The civilization lasted over 1,300 years, achieving remarkable magical accomplishments while committing unspeakable atrocities.

One of Yrugas's most infamous leaders was **Madron**, a wizard-king so legendary that the people of the region would eventually be named after him—the **Madronians**. Madron pioneered necromantic techniques, developed siege spells capable of leveling fortresses, and established the hierarchical structure that would define Yrugan society: mages ruled, warriors served, slaves suffered. Note: Madron (human wizard-king of Yrugas, ~4,000–2,700 BW) should not be confused with Mordrion (dwarven deity who arrived from Oerth in 1,866 BW).

While Yrugas did not explicitly worship **Zebulba**, their extensive practice of death magic drew the Old God's attention. Every corpse animated, every soul consumed in necromantic rituals, every mass execution strengthened Zebulba's connection to the Material Plane.

### The Collapse and the Hollow Sepulcher (~2,700 BW)

Around **2,700 BW**, Yrugas collapsed in a catastrophic combination of magical disaster and slave revolts. The exact sequence of events remains debated by historians, but the result was undeniable: **vast numbers died in a matter of weeks**. Some accounts speak of a failed ritual that opened a portal to the Negative Energy Plane; others describe a coordinated slave uprising that targeted the mage-lords simultaneously across the empire.

What is certain is that the concentrated death created a phenomenon unlike anything seen before or since: **The Hollow Sepulcher**—Zebulba's greatest temple, formed not by construction but by the metaphysical weight of mass death. The Sepulcher is not a building but a *place*—a region where the boundary between the Material Plane and the Negative Energy Plane has worn thin, where death magic flows freely, where undead arise spontaneously.

For centuries after Yrugas's fall, undead armies poured from the ruins, threatening neighboring civilizations. Eventually, a coalition of druids (followers of Gruhathyrir), clerics of Tiovin (who had awakened around 3,000 BW), and paladins of Mordrion (who would arrive in 1,866 BW) managed to seal the breach with powerful wards.

But the sealing was incomplete. The **Void-Touched**—a desperate order of warriors and mages—maintain these wards from *inside* the temple itself, a perpetual sacrifice to prevent something worse from emerging. These guardians live short, haunted lives, knowing they will likely die battling undead horrors in the darkness. New volunteers arrive regularly, driven by duty, desperation, or penance, to replace those who have fallen.

## Era of Awakening — The First Modern Deities (3,402 – 2,700 BW)

While the Old Gods represented elemental forces and primal principles, the next wave of divine awakenings would be fundamentally different. These entities formed connections with specific races, cultures, or moral philosophies, becoming **patrons** rather than distant cosmic forces.

### Minerva Awakens (3,402 BW)

**3,402 BW** marks one of the most consequential dates in elven history: the awakening of **Minerva**, whose name in the elven language translates as "child of Mazariim." Minerva was an extraordinarily powerful entity, and her formation immediately created a new plane of existence—**Elysium**—taking herself and a nearby moon into this extradimensional realm.

From Elysium, Minerva gazed down upon Mazariim and saw the elves—the first race to exhibit many behaviors characteristic of modern intelligent species, including use of fire, complex language, mathematics, and sophisticated tools. Minerva made first contact with elven tribes scattered across the continent of **Vand'ardhon** (elven for "homeland") and began guiding their development.

Over many centuries, Minerva imbued elven culture with two core ideals that persist to this day: a deep respect and reverence for nature and the land, and an absence of need for personal possessions or land ownership.

Along with philosophical guidance, Minerva granted magical powers to faithful followers and helped elves advance in agriculture, architecture, metalworking, cosmology, and the arts. Under Minerva's patronage, elves developed an advanced civilization—the **Yaara'sehan** (ancient walkers)—while humans and dwarves on the nearby continent of Nigal were still climbing out of barbarism.

Minerva's awakening marks **Year 1 of the Elven Reckoning (ER)** calendar, still used by elven nations today.

### The Sundering — Tiovin and Taovan (3,000 BW)

Around **3,000 BW**, an extraordinary event occurred: a dual entity awakened with one unified consciousness. This nascent god observed all of humanity's potential simultaneously—every act of kindness and cruelty, every sacrifice and betrayal, every moment of compassion and every act of violence.

The consciousness could not reconcile these contradictions. How could the same species be capable of such profound love yet commit such terrible atrocities? Unable to accept the paradox, the entity **split instantly** into two separate beings. **Tiovin** emerged retaining all capacity for good—mercy, compassion, justice, love, and the promise of redemption. This entity would view humanity's potential for growth and believe that even the fallen could be saved. **Taovan** manifested with all capacity for evil—cruelty, domination, deception, hate, and corruption. This entity would view humanity as tools to be used, souls to be claimed, and power to be wielded.

In their first instant of separation, the two entities created their respective domains. Tiovin brought forth **Heaven**, a realm of golden light, eternal peace, and perfect justice, along with the **Ethereal Plane**, a shimmering conduit through which souls could journey to Heaven and through which angels could descend to the Material Plane. Taovan forged **Hell**, a realm of suffering, chains, and hierarchical damnation, along with the **Shadow Plane**, a dark reflection of reality through which damned souls would fall and devils could climb toward the Material Plane.

Despite their opposition, both entities retained a sense of fairness from their unified origin. They established what would become known as **The Pact**—cosmic rules governing their eternal competition for mortal souls. The specifics of this agreement remain known only to the two entities themselves, but its effects are clear: neither can directly manifest on the Material Plane, and humanity retains the freedom to choose its own fate.

Tiovin dispatched the **Divine Missionaries** (angels bearing messages of hope and salvation) to spread across Mazariim, teaching mercy and revealing paths to redemption. In response, Taovan sent **Tempting Devils** to whisper that power matters more than virtue, that the strong deserve to rule the weak, and that mercy is weakness.

This cosmic wager continues to the present day, shaping human civilizations and creating endless theological debates about free will, redemption, and the nature of evil.

### Wars of Faith (2,700 – 1,750 BW)

Around **2,700 BW**, as Yrugas collapsed and new civilizations rose from its ashes, conflicts erupted between worshipers of the Old Gods and followers of the newer entities. The elemental cults, once dominant, saw their influence waning as more complex and emotionally resonant faiths gained prominence.

Around **2,500 BW**, these tensions escalated into open warfare. Battles between elemental cults and the churches of Tiovin and Taovan took their toll on the old faiths. Elemental worship began slowly falling away, displaced by deities who offered not just power but **meaning**—gods who cared about mortal choices, who promised rewards or punishments based on behavior, who provided clear moral frameworks.

Particularly brutal were the conflicts between followers of **Tiovin** and **Taovan** themselves. The War of Souls (beginning formally around 1,799 BW) would see these two faiths clash across multiple continents for centuries. Tiovin's faithful viewed Taovan's followers as corrupted souls who must be saved or stopped; Taovan's cultists saw Tiovinians as self-righteous fools denying their own potential for greatness.

Around **1,750 BW**, humanity finally began rising from barbarism in earnest. Tiovin's followers drove Taovan's cult into the shadows through a combination of military victories and social pressure. In most civilized regions, worship of Taovan became illegal, forcing the faith underground where it persists in secret to this day.

## The Dwarven Wars and Mordrion's Arrival (1,921 – 1,849 BW)

While elves had been guided by Minerva for over a millennium and humans struggled with the teachings of Tiovin and Taovan, the dwarves and gnomes were only just beginning their ascent toward civilization. Around **2,250 BW**, dwarven clans started creating the first extensive underground holds, carving entire cities into mountainsides and beneath the earth.

But unity proved elusive. Around **1,921 BW**, the **Dwarven Wars** began—brutal conflicts between various clans over mining rights, territory, and political supremacy. The northern **Gormdawi** clans sought to unify all dwarves under a single Dwarven Empire. The independent southern **Bryndawi** clans resisted, valuing autonomy over unity. The conflict escalated over decades, becoming increasingly vicious.

### The Wounding — Mordrion and Zuel Arrive from Oerth (1,866 BW)

Then, in **1,866 BW**, everything changed. In another universe, on a world called **Oerth**, two gods had been locked in eternal combat: **Moradin** (lord of dwarves, embodiment of creation, craft, and protection) and **Gruumsh** (lord of orcs, embodiment of destruction, conquest, and rage). Their conflict was a cosmic stalemate—neither could achieve lasting victory.

But in one catastrophic exchange, both gods mustered unprecedented power. **Gruumsh's spear ripped out Moradin's left eye** in a blow that should have been fatal. Simultaneously, **Moradin's axe severed Gruumsh's left hand** at the wrist, sending it tumbling into the void.

The simultaneous divine wounds released such catastrophic energy that the quantum foam separating universes **ruptured**. Both gods were torn from Oerth and hurled through the void into Mazariim's universe.

But Mazariim's laws of magic and divinity were different. What had been immortal gods became **mortal entities**—vastly powerful but vulnerable, eternal in lifespan but capable of being killed. Their forms changed:

**Gruumsh** became **Zuel the Devourer**: Still missing his left hand (the stump eternally weeping blood) and bearing an empty eye socket burning with red fire. Diminished but unbowed, still burning with rage and the desire for conquest.

**Moradin** became **Mordrion the Dwarf Father**: Missing his left eye but gaining new purpose in this unfamiliar universe.

The two entities reacted very differently to their circumstances:

**Zuel** immediately recognized his situation as an opportunity. This universe's orcs were primitive, unclaimed, scattered—perfect raw material for his purposes. He began wandering deep places (mountains, caverns, wastelands), seeking out orcish tribes and revealing himself as their god. Over the next 1,863 years (1,866 BW – 3 BW), Zuel would teach orcs iron forging, large-force military coordination, and siege engineering. He transformed scattered primitive tribes into an organized army that would eventually exceed **one million warriors**.

**Mordrion** took a different approach. He appeared to dwarves in populous cities, declaring that he had arrived to help unify them. Seeing the Dwarven Wars tearing his chosen people apart, Mordrion dedicated himself to ending the conflict and uplifting dwarven civilization.

This event marks **Year 1 of the Dwarven Reckoning (DR)** calendar, still used in many dwarven holds.

### The End of the Dwarven Wars (1,849 BW)

By **1,849 BW**, through a combination of diplomacy, divine intervention, and wisdom, Mordrion brokered peace between the Gormdawi and Bryndawi clans. The settlement was complex, involving shared mining rights, mutual defense pacts, and recognition of both unity and autonomy. From this point forward, dwarven holds expanded rapidly, gaining knowledge of rare metals (mithral, adamantine) and mastering arms and armor beyond what any other race could produce.

The worship of Mordrion gradually replaced veneration of the Old Gods (especially **Dirgin**) as the dominant dwarven faith. Mordrion was not a distant elemental force—he was a god who **understood** dwarves, who valued their crafts, who had appeared in their darkest hour and guided them to peace.

The **Day of Unity** holiday was established to commemorate this peace, celebrated annually by dwarven holds across Mazariim.

## Era of Arrival — The Immigrant Deities (1,866 – 1,111 BW)

The arrival of Mordrion and Zuel established a pattern: Mazariim had become a **refuge** for entities from dying or unstable universes. Over the next thousand years, several more gods would arrive from other realities, bringing with them fragments of their original power, their cultures, and their conflicts.

### Rivoleen Awakens (~1,780 BW)

Around **1,780 BW**, an entity formed not from elemental forces or moral principles, but from **curiosity itself**. **Rivoleen** awakened, appearing to gnomes as a humble scholar carrying a journal filled with questions rather than answers.

Her awakening sparked a philosophical revolution among gnomekind. Within a generation, the worship of the Old Gods faded among gnomes, replaced by Rivoleen's faith. She taught that creativity flourishes in freedom and that innovation requires questioning authority. There were to be no sacred cows—everything could and should be examined. She proclaimed that failure teaches more than success, making experimentation valuable even when it doesn't work as expected. Knowledge, she insisted, must be shared freely, for hoarding wisdom benefits no one.

Rivoleen's worship spread rapidly among gnomes, who recognized in her the embodiment of their own cultural values. She became their patron deity, and gnomish society transformed from craft-focused (similar to dwarves) to innovation-focused, emphasizing invention, experimentation, and creative problem-solving.

### Garnak Awakens (1,666 BW)

In **1,666 BW**, a very different entity emerged. **Garnak**, embodiment of savage strength, predatory instinct, and the brutal laws of the wilderness, awakened among the primitive races. His worship spread quickly among orcs, goblins, ogres, and other tribal societies that valued strength above all else.

Unlike the refined philosophies of Minerva or Rivoleen, Garnak taught brutal simplicity: strength determines worth, and the weak exist only to serve or be consumed. Battle itself was worship, and every kill honored the god. Possession was determined purely by power—take what you can hold, and hold what you can defend. Yet within this savagery existed fierce tribalism: the pack survives through absolute loyalty to one's own and absolute brutality toward outsiders.

Garnak's influence would have profound consequences. While Zuel was slowly building a unified orcish force through strategy and organization, Garnak's worship created a cultural framework that made orcs eager warriors. When Zuel finally launched his invasion centuries later, many of his soldiers would be dual worshipers—following Zuel for conquest and Garnak for the joy of battle itself.

### The Talernon Catastrophe (1,492 BW)

In **1,492 BW**, one of the most consequential arrivals in Mazariim's history occurred. In a dying universe called **Talernon**, three powerful entities fought an apocalyptic battle:

**Sheeva** (goddess of poison and revenge): Ancient, patient, unforgiving
**Alagalothor** (chromatic dragon seeking to control all magic): Arrogant, ambitious, brilliant
**Thalaman** (time-traveling archmage who had ascended to near-godhood): Unstable, genius, fracturing across timelines

The conflict had been escalating for (from their perspective) eons. Thalaman, desperate to escape, attempted a planar teleportation spell—but he needed power beyond what he could muster alone. He chose to siphon energy directly from Sheeva's avatar.

The spell worked—catastrophically. Instead of merely teleporting Thalaman, the stolen divine energy created a **vacuum decay event**—a spreading wave of un-reality that began erasing Talernon from existence. Alagalothor, realizing what was happening, tore open a portal to another universe (Mazariim) and fled. The spell's backlash pulled Thalaman and Sheeva through as well.

All three arrived on Mazariim **diminished and forever linked** by the catastrophe:

**Alagalothor** established himself as patron of arcane magic, claiming authority over spellcasters and viewing magic itself as his domain. He settled in the **Far Realm**, a space of impossible geometry where his draconic mind could comprehend realities that would shatter mortal consciousness.

**Thalaman**, fragmented across multiple timelines due to the temporal energies involved in the catastrophe, became an entity of chaos and madness. His consciousness exists simultaneously in past, present, and future. He teaches time magic to ambitious mortals but cannot promise the lessons won't drive them insane. He dwells in **Limbo**, a realm of pure chaos where reality is constantly remade.

**Sheeva**, bitter and vengeful over the loss of 30–40% of her divine power, claims territory in the **Abyss**—specifically, the **Gardens of Vitriol**, where poisonous plants grow in impossible colors and every flower drips venom. She begins plotting revenge against both Alagalothor and Thalaman, a grudge that will never be forgiven or forgotten. Her servants, the **Demodands** (tar-black fiends embodying personal malice and spite), establish hidden temples disguised as apothecaries and perfumeries.

Sheeva's arrival marks the beginning of organized assassination cults and poison worship on Mazariim. Her philosophy—"Never forgive, never forget"—would inspire countless personal vendettas and revenge plots across the following millennia.

### Paladine Arrives from Krynn (1,414 BW)

In **1,414 BW**, the skies above the human kingdoms blazed with celestial light as **Paladine the Platinum Dragon** manifested. In his home universe of **Krynn**, Paladine was one of the gods of Light, maintaining cosmic balance against the forces of darkness. But when a deity died and the balance tipped dangerously, Paladine was forced to leave—his continued presence would have destabilized the remaining equilibrium.

A portion of his divine essence, along with devoted followers, was transported across the void to Mazariim. Paladine appeared as a massive platinum-scaled dragon, glowing with celestial radiance, and spoke words that would reshape human civilization:

*"I am Paladine. I bring justice. Will you walk with me?"*

**Sir Aldric Brightblade**, a human knight of unshakable honor, knelt before the dragon and became **the first paladin of Mazariim**—a holy warrior who fights with both sword and spell, empowered by sacred oaths rather than mere prayer.

Before Paladine, there were clerics (who prayed for divine favor) and warriors (who fought with skill and courage). After Paladine, there were **paladins**—champions who channel divine power through righteous conviction, whose oaths transform them into living weapons against evil.

Paladine shared **Heaven** as his dominion alongside **Tiovin**, creating a complementary duality. **Tiovin** offers mercy and healing, forgiveness and redemption for those who have fallen. **Paladine** offers justice and protection, judgment and accountability for those who refuse redemption.

Together, they form the twin pillars of lawful good on Mazariim. Within a century, paladins bearing the silver dragon symbol rode across the world, bringing hope to the oppressed and justice to the wicked. The **Grand Cathedral of Justice** was built on the site of Paladine's first landing, becoming the holiest site of his faith and the training ground for aspiring paladins from across Mazariim.

### Eilistraee Arrives from Abier-Toril (1,377 BW)

In **1,377 BW**, another refugee goddess arrived, but under very different circumstances. In the universe of **Abier-Toril (Faerun)**, the goddess **Eilistraee** (daughter of Corellon Larethian and the Spider Queen Lolth) had spent millennia offering redemption to drow who wished to escape Lolth's tyranny. She built hidden groves in the Underdark, taught moonlit dances, and protected those brave enough to flee the spider-cities.

During the catastrophic **Spellplague**, Eilistraee's brother **Vhaeraun** (god of drow thieves) attempted to assassinate her. The battle resulted in both deities being believed dead across Faerun. But Eilistraee did not die—a fragment of her divine essence, along with a small band of faithful drow refugees (perhaps 200–300 individuals), was torn from Faerun and cast into the planar void.

They emerged on Mazariim, and **Minerva** (always watchful for arrivals) detected them first. Recognizing Eilistraee as a kindred spirit who valued enlightenment over conquest, Minerva welcomed her to **Elysium** and offered sanctuary. The drow refugees became Mazariim's first **surface drow** or **moon drow**—dark elves who rejected Lolth and chose to live under the sun (or at least the moon).

The immediate impact was profound. Before 1,377 BW, drow on Mazariim were universally feared as Lolth-worshiping slavers and assassins. Eilistraee's followers demonstrated that dark elves were **victims of indoctrination**, not inherently evil. This realization sparked theological debates about nature versus nurture, inherent evil versus learned behavior—debates that continue in the modern era.

Over the next 1,300+ years, the moon drow population has grown to perhaps 5,000–10,000 individuals (compared to millions of Underdark drow). The **Moonlit Refuge Network**—an underground railroad helping drow escape to the surface—was established and operates in secret to this day.

### Quarzel Awakens (1,293 BW)

In **1,293 BW**, during a period of intense warfare across multiple continents, **Quarzel the Warlord** awakened. Born from the collective consciousness of countless conquests and tyrannical empires, Quarzel represented **organized, disciplined warfare**—not the savage chaos of Zuel or Garnak, but systematic conquest through fear and military precision.

He manifested on a battlefield in the midst of a siege, appearing as a titanic figure in black-and-crimson armor, carrying a greatsword dripping with divine ichor and a blood-red banner that never stops bleeding. His first words established his philosophy:

*"I am Quarzel. I am war. Serve me, and I will grant you victory. Defy me, and I will grant you oblivion."*

Both armies immediately swore allegiance, and Quarzel commanded them to resume battle—the winner would become his first high priest. **General Vorgath the Conqueror** emerged victorious and was elevated to the rank of **Iron Fist**, first supreme commander of Quarzel's faith.

Quarzel's worship spread among warlords, tyrants, and militaristic empires. His servants, the **Titans** (fifty-foot-tall warriors of living stone and metal), enforced his will and led mortal armies. He claimed the **Fields of Battle** as his dominion—an eternal battlefield on the Outer Planes where warriors fight, die, and rise again to fight forever.

Quarzel's relationship with Zuel (who arrived from Oerth in 1,866 BW and had been building his orcish horde for over 500 years) was complicated. Both were war deities, but they embodied fundamentally different approaches: **Zuel** represented chaotic destruction, endless rage, and savage conquest, while **Quarzel** represented lawful tyranny, calculated conquest, and military discipline. For centuries they coexisted uneasily. Some warlords worshiped both, seeing no contradiction between rage and discipline; others chose sides based on their own martial philosophy. This tension would eventually erupt during the War to End All Wars, with catastrophic consequences for Quarzel.

### Gyru's Ascension (1,111 BW)

In **1,111 BW**, the impossible occurred: a mortal achieved godhood through engineering rather than worship or cosmic accident.

**Gyru Ironspanner**, a dwarf artificer born around 1,154 BW, was obsessed from childhood with two fundamental questions: How do gods wield such power? And if magic is energy, can it be harnessed mechanically?

While other dwarves forged traditional weapons and armor, Gyru built radical contraptions: clockwork automatons without visible power sources, gemstone lenses focusing magical energy, gauntlets amplifying strength. His clan considered him brilliant but dangerous.

In 1,130 BW, Gyru began his **Great Work**: the **Apotheosis Engine**—a cathedral-sized machine of interlocking gears, arcane crystals, and unprecedented metallurgical engineering designed to absorb ambient magical energy and channel it into a living being. The goal: artificially grant himself godhood.

He worked for 19 years, ignoring ridicule and warnings. The Engine was completed in 1,111 BW. Gyru activated it.

For seven seconds, it worked perfectly. Magical energy from elemental convergence points, divine power sources, and natural mana wells converged on him. His consciousness expanded across planes. His soul began rewriting itself. He was **ascending**.

Then **Alagalothor** intervened. The ancient dragon-god of arcane magic detected the Engine immediately and was furious—it was pulling power from sources he considered his domain. Terrified by the precedent (a mortal achieving godhood through engineering), Alagalothor unleashed a targeted dispel designed to shatter the Engine.

The Engine exploded—but Gyru had already absorbed enough divine energy. The explosion didn't kill him; it **completed** his transformation. His physical body was destroyed, but his consciousness, now infused with divine power, survived as a **Lesser Entity**.

Alagalothor, realizing he'd inadvertently created a new god, transported Gyru's disembodied essence to **Utopia**—a plane of perfect law and mathematical order where he could be monitored. But this was a miscalculation. Utopia's precision was **paradise** for an inventor-god. Gyru immediately built a new body (clockwork and enchanted steel), a laboratory, and recruited **axiomites** (mathematical beings native to Utopia) as servants.

The theological impact was immense. Gyru had provided **proof of mortal ascension** through artifice rather than worship or cosmic accident. The divine hierarchy faced a **threat** it had never considered—gods realized they were not eternal, that mortals could potentially rise to challenge them. An **innovation explosion** followed in the centuries after 1,111 BW as artificers and mages sought to replicate Gyru's achievement. **Alagalothor's paranoia** deepened—the dragon-god now actively suppresses any attempts to replicate the Engine, destroying research and killing those who come too close to success.

When Alagalothor shattered the Engine, its pieces scattered across Mazariim and beyond. To this day, fragments occasionally surface—blueprints, gear assemblies, arcane focusing crystals. Multiple factions seek to reassemble the Engine. None have succeeded—yet.

### The Economic Deities Awaken

The final major arrivals and awakenings before the War include three entities who would shape the economic landscape of Mazariim:

**Xannith (699 BW)**: During an era historians call the **Gilded Age**, a period of unprecedented economic growth and extreme inequality, **Xannith** awakened from the collective consciousness of greed. She manifested first to a corrupt merchant lord, offering infinite wealth. He became fabulously wealthy—only to be poisoned a decade later by a rival who *also* worshiped Xannith.

Xannith established the **Chains of Gold**, a clergy of corrupt nobles and slave traders. Her temples (disguised as banks and auction houses) spread to every major city. She taught that wealth is virtue, poverty is sin, and everything—including people—has a price. For 325 years, her influence created systems of extreme inequality and legalized slavery.

**Polyvin (374 BW)**: In response to economic despair, **Polyvin** awakened. A halfling merchant named **Garrick Twocoins**, ruined by predatory practices, prayed for a second chance. A door appeared, leading to Polyvin's planar domain (the **Towers of Chance**). Polyvin blessed him and sent him back with tools to rebuild.

Polyvin represents positive aspects of commerce: fair trade, shared prosperity, opportunity for all. His worship spread among halflings, honest merchants, and common folk. He established **Fortune Halls**—temples combining community centers, marketplaces, and gambling halls—offering practical help: loans, job placement, travel assistance.

From his awakening, Polyvin was Xannith's enemy. The two faiths engage in ongoing economic warfare to this day.

## Era of Unity — The War to End All Wars (3 BW – 1 AW)

By the year **3 BW**, Mazariim had reached a complex equilibrium. Multiple civilizations flourished across continents. Dozens of entities guided mortal affairs. Trade routes connected distant nations. Art, philosophy, and magic advanced steadily.

And Zuel was ready.

For **1,863 years** (since his arrival in 1,866 BW), Zuel had been building an army. He taught orcs iron forging and siege engineering. He recruited goblins, hobgoblins, and ogres. He transformed scattered primitive tribes into a unified, disciplined military force exceeding **one million warriors**. He made war itself sacred—every enemy slain was prayer, every city burned was worship.

### Year One of the War (3 BW)

Zuel's horde erupted from the mountains simultaneously across multiple fronts. The invasion was apocalyptic:

The land assault was devastating. Dwarven holds that had stood for millennia fell within months. Human kingdoms fractured as masses of refugees overwhelmed infrastructure. Most of the dwarven population was decimated; survivors fled underground or to allied human cities. Entire regions were depopulated, their populations slaughtered or scattered.

The coastal assault proved equally catastrophic. **Frog-daemons**—amphibious fiends—emerged from the oceans, attacking coastal settlements with coordinated fury. A massive sea creature with dozens of hundred-foot tentacles led the naval assault, destroying fleets and harbors alike. A dozen coastal cities were wiped from the maps. Ocean trade and commerce halted completely.

Defensive efforts varied by race and region. The **Elves** held their own better than others, nearly matching Zuel's forces through superior tactics and magic. **Humans and dwarves** suffered enormous casualties, their armies crushed in pitched battles. **Gnomish realms** barely survived, saved only by Rivoleen providing inspired tactical defenses. **Hinterhin** blessed Shalazar's fields to produce beyond natural capacity, feeding the masses of refugees fleeing the carnage.

This was not mere war—it was near-extermination. Civilization itself hung by a thread.

### Year Two of the War (2 BW)

As the war's second year began, the various entities became more directly involved:

The various entities became more directly involved as the war's second year progressed. **Hinterhin** continued feeding the masses through blessed harvests, keeping starvation at bay. **Rivoleen** defended the Gnomish Realms with tactical genius, her strategic insights turning what should have been massacres into survivable battles. **Mordrion** led forces personally, his avatar visible on multiple battlefronts, inspiring dwarven warriors to impossible feats of courage. A half-dozen minor entities were destroyed in the fighting, sacrificing themselves to defeat ogre-magi generals. **Minerva** made the ultimate sacrifice, dividing her avatar's power among a dozen elven archmages, hierophants, and archdruids, empowering them to become living weapons against the horde. Notably, **Tiovin and Taovan** abstained from direct intervention, honoring their ancient pact to never directly touch the Material Plane. They waited patiently, welcoming what would become the largest soul-influx in Mazariim's history.

At a small interior fort that would be renamed **Fort Hope**, an unprecedented alliance formed. The **Concord of Unity** brought together Dwarves and Gnomes, Humans, and Elves and Halflings in a formal pact of mutual defense and cooperation.

The Concord allowed races to coordinate all aspects: battle strategy, supply lines, governance, resource allocation. This cooperation was civilization's only chance at survival—and even that was uncertain.

### Final Year of the War (1 BW)

The war's last year was its most brutal. Dwarves and humans fought for mere survival rather than territory. Elves maintained their defenses but had lost nearly their entire navy.

In **autumn**, Zuel's avatar personally manifested and began leading his armies westward toward Fort Hope. The avatar was a divine projection of his full power—ten feet tall, missing his left hand (the stump weeping eternal blood), his empty eye-socket burning with red fire.

At Fort Hope, the final confrontation unfolded in spectacular and terrible fashion. **Mordrion's Avatar** confronted Zuel directly, supported by Quarzel and several lesser entities. In a display of overwhelming power, **Zuel destroyed them all**—Mordrion's avatar shattered like glass, Quarzel was utterly obliterated, and the lesser entities were wiped from existence. Victory for the horde seemed certain.

Then **Demious Stoneshaper** stepped forward. This dwarven warrior had lost everything—hold, family, clan—but he carried **Mordrion's blessed warhammer**, passed to him by the dying avatar in its final moments.

The battle between Stoneshaper and Zuel lasted hours. Every clash shook mountains. Finally, Stoneshaper struck true—the warhammer connected with Zuel's chest and **shattered the avatar**.

Zuel was banished, his consciousness ripped from the Material Plane, unable to manifest an avatar for 101 years.

Without their god's presence, the orcish horde broke. What had been an unstoppable army became fleeing mobs. The allied races pursued, reclaiming territory, driving orcs back into deep places.

The war officially ended on **3 Quarz (January 3), Year 1 After War (AW)**. Demious Stoneshaper became king of the northern dwarven realms; his clan rules to this day. The **Stoneshaper's Vigil** holiday commemorates this victory.

The aftermath of the war reshaped Mazariim in profound ways. Most entities' avatars were banished for 101 years, unable to directly manifest on the Material Plane. There was a surge in mortal confidence—the civilized races had saved themselves when even gods had failed. Orc insurgencies continued for decades, requiring constant vigilance. The next ten years saw famine and disease as the world recovered from the devastation, but the civilized races ultimately prevailed. Most significantly, the calendar was reset: this became **Year 1 of the After War (AW)** era, a dating system used universally across Mazariim to this day.

## The Post-War Era — Recovery and Transformation (1 – 831 AW)

### The Line-Watchers' Vigil (Post-War Era)

Throughout the centuries following the War to End All Wars, the Line-Watchers maintained their vigil, preserving what fragments of Akovarian knowledge remained and keeping faith that Asti would one day return. They did not know their deity was imprisoned in an artifact hidden in distant Varkonan lands—they believed Asti was simply dormant, recovering from the ancient catastrophe.

The Line-Watchers became respected as healers and scholars, though their numbers remained small. They maintained crumbling pyramid temples, studied the damaged ley-line network, and passed down oral traditions about the lost golden age of Akovar.

### Decline of Old God Worship (200-400 AW)

As patron deities like Mordrion, Minerva, Hinterhin, and Rivoleen gained prominence, Old God worship declined precipitously. **Dwarves** abandoned Dirgin for Mordrion's clear doctrine and emotional connection, finding in him a god who truly understood their nature. **Druids** increasingly followed Minerva's teachings of cooperative nature or Hinterhin's pastoral agriculture, turning away from Gruhathyrir's primal extremism. **Elemental cults** fragmented as organized churches offered community and structure that the Old Gods could not provide. Only **Mau's Watchers** and **Asti's Line-Watchers** maintained coherent organizations among the Old God faiths. **Zebulba worship** was criminalized in most civilized lands, driven into the shadows. **Pyrantor, Iquinias, and Vuleala** retained only isolated shrines and superstitious observances, their grand temples abandoned or repurposed.

### Ry'le's First Infiltration (820-824 AW)

In **820 AW**, a new threat emerged. **Ry'le**, a young entity from the **Far Realm**, made first contact with Mazariim through the dreams of **Berton Adluf**, a Madronian warlock experimenting with planar magic. Adluf willingly formed a pact, becoming Ry'le's first mortal servant.

**822 AW**: Ry'le created seven elite shapeshifters called the **Masters**—aberrations capable of perfectly mimicking any humanoid. Using Adluf as a focus, Ry'le opened a rift into the **Yrugas ruins** in Beneshire, allowing the Masters and lesser **Ry'lehe** (aberrant servitors) to enter the Material Plane.

**823-824 AW**: The Masters systematically infiltrated rival noble houses **Halycon** and **Renoalt**, replacing key members with perfect copies. The replaced victims were transformed into mindless **thralls** connected to the **Ry'lehe Hivemind**. The goal: incite civil war in Beneshire.

**824 AW (Summer)**: A group of heroes—including **Dame Tarime Ilthrel** (wood elf ranger), **Lady Jala of Littlebridge** (human mage), and **Sir Khalic O'Grady** (human paladin)—stumbled upon the conspiracy while helping the Astral Circle restart a ley-line. The activation created a magical burst that **exposed psychic connections** between thralls and Masters.

**824 AW (Autumn)**: The heroes hunted down the Masters and destroyed the portal at Yrugas ruins. Berton Adluf was killed. The conspiracy was exposed, and Beneshire narrowly avoided civil war.

**Aftermath**: Though the portal closed, **Ry'le survived and learned**. His infiltration had been too obvious. Next time, he would be more subtle. Worse, some thralls were never found—they remain in society, dormant but still connected to the Hivemind.

This marks the first major appearance of Ry'le as a recognized threat. Temples began researching methods to detect shapeshifters and resist psychic infiltration. Scholars warn that Ry'le is young, adaptive, and patient—the threat has merely been delayed.

### The Betrayal at Kashi and Soni Sabu's Fall (811-831 AW)

Far to the east, in the **Lands of Honor** (Ejiin civilization, Japanese-inspired culture of the Yashaki islands), a different crisis was unfolding.

**811 AW**: During the **Orceri Incursion** (a regional spillover from Zuel's war), the Ejiin city of **Kashi** came under attack. In the city's darkest hour, an enormous **Oni** (demon-spirit, servant of **Khan** the entity of dishonor) materialized and fought alongside the defenders, slaughtering hundreds of Orceri soldiers and saving thousands of Ejiin lives. Then it vanished.

The Ejiin were horrified. Khan is the entity of dishonor—his servants are enemies of civilization. Yet the Oni had saved them. **Ashura's** clergy declared it a test or trap. The Phoenix Clan daimyō forbade anyone from thanking the Oni.

But one man refused to forget.

**Soni Sabu's Heresy**: **Soni Sabu**, a shaman who witnessed the Oni's intervention, became convinced that Oni could be controlled for the benefit of the Ejiin people. He proposed studying ways to summon and bind Oni as protectors. The elders were appalled—this was blasphemy. Soni Sabu was **banished** from the Phoenix Clan.

**Khan's True Victory**: The Oni's "help" was never meant to save Kashi—it was meant to plant a seed of corruption. By demonstrating that Khan's power could achieve "good" outcomes, the Oni inspired exactly one person to attempt forbidden magic. Khan doesn't need mass conversions; he needs individual moral compromises.

**831 AW — Soni Sabu's Revenge**: Twenty years after his banishment, Soni Sabu unleashed his revenge. He had mastered forbidden rituals: summoning Oni, creating the **Taint** (a magical plague raising victims as zombies), and building hidden shrines.

Over fifteen days of escalating horror, Soni Sabu enacted his revenge. He released the Taint in villages, creating armies of the undead. He summoned a Commander Oni to lead the zombies in coordinated attacks. A Water Oni spread disease via waterways, poisoning wells and rivers. A Fire Oni burned through defensive positions, turning fortifications into funeral pyres. Finally, he attempted to summon a Warrior Oni for the final assault on Kashi itself.

A group of heroes stopped him, but thousands died. Soni Sabu was killed, refusing capture and cursing Ashura with his final breath.

**The Lesson**: Attempting to "use" Khan's power—even for good purposes—always leads to tragedy. Soni Sabu believed he could control evil. He was wrong. The Oni corrupted him first.

## Modern Era (831 AW – Present)

As of **831 AW** (the traditional "present day" of most Mazariim campaigns), the world has largely recovered from the War to End All Wars. Civilizations flourish, trade networks span continents, and magic is understood better than ever.

But threats remain and evolve:

The divine landscape has evolved dramatically. Thirty-three major entities now compete for mortal worship, with the Old Gods largely forgotten except by isolated cults and scholars. Patron deities—racial and cultural—dominate most regions, offering clear doctrine and emotional connection that the elemental forces never could. Economic warfare between Xannith and Polyvin shapes policy across nations, while Tiovin and Taovan's cosmic wager for human souls continues unabated.

Persistent threats lurk in the shadows and margins of civilization. **Ry'le's infiltrators** remain dormant, thralls waiting for activation. **Zebulba's cults** operate as necromancers in the darkness. **Khan's influence** slowly corrupts honor cultures from within. **Sheeva's assassins** offer poison and revenge for hire to those willing to pay. **Orcish remnants**—Zuel's faithful—rebuild in mountain strongholds, biding their time. **Lolth's hunters** relentlessly seek moon drow escapees, determined to drag them back to the Underdark.

Emerging developments promise to reshape the world yet again. The **magitech revolution** sees Gyru's followers blending magic and technology in unprecedented ways. A **construct rights movement** sparks debate over whether sentient automatons deserve personhood. **Ley-line restoration** proceeds as Asti's followers repair ancient networks. **Economic inequality** grows as Xannith's influence battles Polyvin's reforms. Most concerning to the powerful, **second Apotheosis attempts** continue as rogue mages seek fragments of Gyru's engine, hoping to achieve godhood through artifice.

The history of Mazariim is not finished. Every campaign adds new chapters. Every hero's choice shapes the future. Every prayer strengthens a god—or weakens a rival.

This is a world where gods can die, mortals can ascend, and history is still being written.
