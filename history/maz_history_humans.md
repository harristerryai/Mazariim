

# Human History of Mazariim

## PART ONE: THE ANCIENT ERAS (100,000 BW – 2,000 BW)

**~100,000 BW — The Separation**
The five mortal races become genetically distinct. Humans (Madronians) begin populating the Inner Kingdoms of Nigal in scattered hunter-gatherer tribes.

**~20,000 BW — The Awakening of Asti**
Asti forms from the cosmic collision that created the universe and establishes the **ley-line network**.

**~13,000 BW — The Rise of Akovar**
The first advanced human civilization rises on the isolated continent of Akovar, guided by Asti to master healing and ley-line magic.

**~6,830 BW — The Dragon War & The Great Exodus**
Dragons destroy Akovar. Refugees teleport to the Ancient South of Nigal, losing their memories but retaining pyramid-building skills to become the Varkonans.

**~6,500 BW — Founding of Nepherites (Ancient South)**
Varkonan refugees build their first great city, establishing the Pharaonic Empire.

**~5,800 BW — Founding of Uadjit (Ancient South)**
Expansion southward leads to a city known for genie-binders and decadence.

**~4,000 BW — The Rise of Yrugas (Inner Kingdoms)**
Dragons teach magic to humans in the north. The brutal mageocracy of Yrugas rises, ruled by Wizard-King Madron.

**~3,850 BW — The First Binding (Inner Kingdoms)**
In the early days of Yrugas, the first successful binding of a human soul to a construct is achieved by the wizard-lord Kaelthos, creating the first rudimentary Shield Guardian.

**~3,500 BW — Migration Wave: The Southern Split**
Madronian humans migrate south along the western coast to escape Yrugas, eventually becoming the Sabonaar people.

**~3,500 BW — Migration Wave: The Eastern Split**
Varkonan humans migrate east along the southern coast, eventually becoming the Ejiin people.

**~3,300 BW — The Copper Standard (Merchant Nations)**
Coastal tribes in the Sabon region agree on the "Shell-Weight" standard for trade, facilitating the first organized commerce between the south and north.

**~3,200 BW — Founding of Miyasaki (Lands of Honor)**
Ejiin migrants establish the Shogunate capital on the Yashaki Islands.

**~3,000 BW — The Sundering**
A dual entity splits into Tiovin (Good) and Taovan (Evil), sparking the eternal war for human souls.

**~2,900 BW — The Night of Green Fire (Inner Kingdoms)**
A magical experiment in the Yrugas capital goes awry, turning the sky emerald for three nights. Children born during this week are found to have natural aptitude for illusion magic.

**~2,800 BW — Founding of Veppankarai (Lands of Honor)**
Island settlements form a distinct ninja-run autocracy.

**~2,700 BW — The Fall of Yrugas**
The mageocracy collapses in a magical disaster (creating the Hollow Sepulcher) and slave revolts.

**~2,700 BW — Migration Wave: The Mage Refugees**
Surviving Yrugas mage-lords flee south to the Lavantine Peninsula, mixing with natives to form the Laveri culture.

**~2,650 BW — Founding of Kruta (Lavantine Peninsula)**
The Yrugas refugees establish a slavetrading magocracy in the swamps.

**~2,500 BW — Wars of Faith Begin**
Open conflict erupts between elemental cults and the newer churches of Tiovin and Taovan.

**~2,500 BW — Migration Wave: The Plains Split**
Madronian humans migrate east into the central plains, becoming the Chak'ti nomads.

**~2,500 BW — Founding of Khonayn (Horselands)**
The Chak'ti establish their sacred "Ancestor Holy Grounds".

**~2,400 BW — The Rebellion of Broken Spears (Horselands)**
A Chak'ti leader attempts to unite the tribes against elemental cults. The "Spear-Breaker" tactic becomes a staple of Chak'ti warfare against magic users.

**~2,200 BW — Migration Wave: The Jungle Split**
Varkonan migrants move northeast into the tropical jungles, adapting pyramid architecture to the rainforest to become the Azala.

**~2,100 BW — The Varkonan Canal (Ancient South)**
Slaves and mages construct a massive irrigation canal connecting the River of Life to the arid western deserts, doubling food production for a century.

**~2,000 BW — Migration Wave: The Northern Split**
Madronian explorers move north through Gnomish lands to the Frozen Isles, becoming the Nond people.

**~2,000 BW — Founding of Javalh (Frozen Isles)**
The Nond establish their first "Vikingesque" port city.

## PART TWO: THE ERA OF ARRIVAL (1,866 BW – 1 BW)

**1,866 BW — The Wounding**
Mordrion and Zuel arrive from Oerth. Zuel begins uniting the orc tribes.

**1,799 BW — The War of Souls**
Open civil war erupts across human lands between the churches of Tiovin and Taovan.

**~1,750 BW — Victory of Tiovin**
Taovan's open worship is crushed and driven underground; Tiovin becomes the dominant human faith.

**~1,650 BW — Founding of Beneshire (Inner Kingdoms)**
The oldest continuous nation in the Inner Kingdoms is founded as a hereditary monarchy.

**1,602 BW — The Starfall Plague (Lavantine Peninsula)**
A meteorite impact in the swamps of Ubyra releases a strange gas. The local Laveri tribes develop a resistance to toxins after a generation of sickness.

**~1,500 BW — Migration Wave: The Swamp Split**
Varkonan migrants move north into the Lavantine swamps, mixing with natives to become the Ubyra tribes.

**~1,500 BW — Settlement of Tortuganae (Forgotten Isles)**
An archipelago refuge for non-humans is established.

**1,414 BW — The Silver Descent**
Paladine arrives from Krynn, creating the first Paladins and revolutionizing human warfare.

**1,328 BW — Unification of Sabonaa (Merchant Nations)**
Coastal tribes unite into the Sabonaar Empire, precursor to the modern Merchant Nations.

**1,250 BW — The Codification of Law (Inner Kingdoms)**
Beneshire establishes the "Tablets of Common Right," the first human legal code to limit the power of nobility, influenced by Tiovin.

**~1,200 BW — Founding of Brahjhin (Frozen Isles)**
A second major Nond city is founded, based on meritocracy and honor.

**1,001 BW — Rise of the Dukhan Empire (Merchant Nations)**
A Zebulba-worshipping theocracy expands to dominate the Sabon Peninsula.

**889 BW — The War of the False Prophet (Merchant Nations)**
A charismatic leader in Dukhan claims to be an avatar. The civil war lasts three years until he is exposed as a charlatan using illusions.

**~750 BW — Founding of Arogror (Inner Kingdoms)**
Industrious hillsfolk establish a nation known for mining and stoicism.

**720 BW — The Iron-Hull Experiment (Frozen Isles)**
Nond shipwrights attempt to plate a longship in iron. The ship sinks, but the forging techniques lead to superior plate mail.

**699 BW — The Gilded Age**
Xannith awakens, gaining favor among human nobles and merchants.

**~650 BW — Founding of Xeque (Inner Kingdoms)**
A hedonistic merchant oligarchy rises between the Inner Kingdoms and the south.

**612 BW — The Velvet Embargo (Inner Kingdoms)**
A trade dispute between Xeque and Arogror over luxury textiles leads to a five-year "cold war" of assassinations.

**555 BW — The Plague of Madness**
A devastating plague fragments the nation of Sabonaa; Naarlam emerges as the primary successor state.

**~550 BW — Founding of Tentigo (Merchant Nations)**
A warmongering theocracy dedicated to Tiovin rises.

**472 BW — Tiovesh Named**
The town of Vesh becomes Tiovesh, capital of the Holy Theocracy of Tentigo.

**412 BW — The Cecastaa Rebellion**
Eastern territories rebel against Naarlam, sparking a civil war that leads to the founding of Cecastaa.

**~400 BW — Founding of Sharma-Ad (Horselands)**
Chak'ti nomads unite under a mobile "Tent-City" capital.

**374 BW — Polyvin Awakens**
Entity of fair trade awakens to oppose Xannith, gaining followers among honest merchants.

**350 BW — The Discovery of Black Powder (Lands of Honor)**
Ejiin alchemists seeking immortality accidentally create "thunder-dust," initially used for fireworks.

**213 BW — Fall of Dukhan**
A failed ritual destroys the Dukhan Empire, leaving a power vacuum in the Merchant Nations.

**205 BW — The Great Stallion Drift (Horselands)**
A magical phenomenon causes all horses in the central plains to migrate west; Chak'ti tribes follow and conflict with Sabonaar settlers.

**~180 BW — Founding of Zzan (Lavantine Peninsula)**
Dragons claim territory in the cold northern peninsula, ruling over humans.

**150 BW — The Whispering Madness (Lavantine Peninsula)**
A cult of Og'thallith poisons city wells. The resulting hysteria leads to the "Silent Year," where no trade enters or leaves the peninsula.

## PART THREE: THE WAR & MODERN ERA (4 BW – 842 AW)

**4 BW — The War Begins**
Zuel's horde invades. Human kingdoms fracture under the assault.

**2 BW — The Concord of Unity**
Humans, Dwarves, Elves, Gnomes, and Halflings sign a mutual defense pact at Fort Hope.

**1 AW — The Victory at Fort Hope**
The War ends with Zuel's banishment. The "Common" language is adopted globally.

**1 AW — Founding of the Federation of Man**

The human nations of the Inner Kingdoms (Beneshire, Arogror, and later Saerrus and Pragos) formalize their wartime alliance into a permanent republic governed by the Praetorian Council at Fort Hope. This establishes the Federation as the dominant human power in the world, ensuring safe roads and monster-free territories across the northwest of Nigal.

**12 AW — The Steam-Pump Revolution (Inner Kingdoms)**
Gnomish refugees in Pragos perfect steam-driven water pumps, kickstarting the region's industrial dominance.

**~45 AW — Founding of Saerrus (Inner Kingdoms)**
Built from the ruins of war-destroyed nations, Saerrus rises as an intellectual democracy.

**55 AW — The Battle of the Silver Bridge (Inner Kingdoms)**
A border skirmish between Saerrus and Beneshire is settled by a duel of champions rather than mass combat to preserve post-War population.

**~88 AW — Founding of Pragos (Inner Kingdoms)**
Industrial arcanists form a confederacy to pool magical resources after the War.

**99 AW — Cecastaa Independence**
Cecastaa gains full recognition of independence from Naarlam.

**115 AW — The Cartographer's Guild Founded (Merchant Nations)**
Established in Naarlam, the guild discovers fast-moving trade winds that cut travel time to the Frozen Isles in half.

**~120 AW — Migration Wave: Post-War Refugees**
Displaced Laveri and refugees form a survivalist meritocracy in the chaotic south, founding Medan.

**172 AW — First Tiovinian Crusade**
Tentigo launches a holy war to convert neighboring heathen lands.

**~180 AW — Founding of Soractian (Lavantine Peninsula)**
Democratic explorers establish a new city-state dedicated to discovery.

**209 AW — Saerrus Revolution**

The monarchy of Saerrus falls in a popular uprising. The Federation of Man mediates peace on the borders while Saerrus transitions into an intellectual democracy, eventually solidifying its membership in the Federation.

**219 AW — Second Tiovinian Crusade**
Tentigo launches a second crusade into the Ancient South.

**240 AW — The False Peace of Amara (Merchant Nations)**
A treaty between rival merchant families bans assassination. It holds for six months before the "Night of Poisoned Wine."

**291 AW — Rise of the Empire of Simir**

King Petrov Simir of Asim conquers the southern Merchant Nations (Amara, Instar, Moamon). The Empire rises in part as a rival power to the Federation of Man's dominance in the north, seeking to establish a comparable hegemony over the southern trade routes.

**305 AW — The Glacier's Retreat (Frozen Isles)**
A warming period allows the Nond to settle further north, discovering ancient ruins encased in ice.

**~350 AW — Founding of Nond (Frozen Isles)**
The Nond people expand further north into the deep cold.

**376 AW — The Stone-Steel Treaty**

The Federation of Man signs a historic trade and peace treaty with the Dwarven Empire, opening formal commerce across the Dragon Spines and leading to the construction of the Great Rail—an ambitious underground railway linking human and dwarven territories.

**397 AW — Third Tiovinian Crusade**
Tentigo launches a third crusade into the Lands of Chaos.

**410 AW — The Great Library Fire (Inner Kingdoms)**
A magical fire consumes the west wing of the University of Fort Hope; centuries of genealogical records are lost.

**512 AW — The Invention of the Aether-Scope (Inner Kingdoms)**
Artificers in Pragos create a device that can see into the Ethereal Plane. Banned by the Church of Tiovin as heretical.

**~600-620 AW — The Federation Expansion**

A wave of nationalism drives the Federation to pressure the Merchant Nations (particularly Naarlam and Cecastaa) into joining. Trade blockades and border skirmishes occur, but the expansion is ultimately voted down by the Praetorian Council, leading to new trade treaties instead.

**620 AW — The Sugar Riots (Merchant Nations)**
The Empire of Simir raises taxes on imported sugar. Riots in port cities force the Emperor to execute his treasurer.

**686 AW — Fall of the Empire of Simir**
King Tiberius is assassinated; the empire fractures into civil war.

**689 AW — Thaumaturgic Council takes Instar**
Wizards seize control of Instar, establishing a formal Magocracy.

**692 AW — Founding of the Free City of Amara**
Baron Bastien declares independence from the crumbling Simir Empire.

**733 AW — The Amara-Moamon War**
Open conflict erupts between the Free City of Amara and the city of Moamon; ends in an uneasy truce.

**745 AW — The Exhibition of Wonders (Inner Kingdoms)**
Saerrus hosts a world fair debuting the steam-automaton "The Iron Valet," sparking a twenty-year patent war.

**759 AW — The Fires of Moamon**
Dwarven refugees are displaced by mysterious fires outside Moamon, increasing tensions in the region.

**788 AW — The Leviathan's Corpse (Merchant Nations)**
A massive dead sea monster washes up near Naarlam, creating a temporary "Bone-Craft" economic boom.

**802 AW — The Midnight Sun (Lands of Honor)**
A magical phenomenon causes the sun to remain visible for 24 hours, interpreted as an omen of Ashura.
