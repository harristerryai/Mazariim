# Monsters & Outsiders

This chapter details the various types of monsters, outsiders, and extraplanar beings found in the Mazariim setting. Most of these creatures are classified as "Outsiders"—beings native to the extra-dimensional planes who serve various entities and fulfill specific roles within the cosmic order.

## What Are Outsiders?

Almost all extra-dimensional planes have inhabitants, collectively known as Outsiders. This includes celestials, elementals, fey, and fiends, as well as some creatures with other subtypes, such as constructs or undead. Most are formed when the magical energies mortals think of as souls (really magical imprints of biological minds, whose quantum state collapses when observed by Mau) depart for higher-dimensional destinations. Often, but not always, these magical energies are used by the entity or even the plane itself to create outsiders. These outsiders often follow a hierarchy or have a way of climbing in the ranks, from the lowliest imp to the mightiest pit fiend. Most naturally serve the entity that rules over the extra-dimensional plane, often as emissaries, spies, or even more forceful duties. Outsiders do not need to sleep, eat, drink, or breathe, and have different immunities, depending on their plane of origin. When spellcasters use magic to summon creatures to aid them, they are almost always summoning outsiders to do their bidding.

## Monster Types in Mazariim

The following types of creatures are detailed in this chapter:

### Celestials & Servants of Good
- **Angels** — Servants of Tiovin in Heaven (lawful, neutral, and chaotic good)
- **Archons** — Lawful good servants of Paladine in Heaven
- **Guardinals** — Neutral good servants of Tiovin in Heaven
- **Azatas** — Chaotic good servants of Minerva in Elysium
- **Agathions** — Neutral good servants of Rivoleen in Nirvana
- **Sherpas** — Spirit guides in the Ethereal Plane helping souls ascend

### Fiends & Servants of Evil
- **Devils** — Lawful evil servants of Taovan in Hell
- **Demons** — Chaotic evil servants of Og'thallith in the Abyss
- **Daemons** — Neutral evil servants of Zuel in Abaddon
- **Yugoloths** — Neutral and chaotic evil servants in Hell
- **Demodands** — Shapeshifting servants of Sheeva in Abaddon
- **Div** — Tempting servants of Xannith in Abaddon
- **Kytons** — Torturers and corruptors in the Shadow Plane
- **Night Hags** — Evil hags in the Plane of Dreams

### Servants of Law & Chaos
- **Aeons** — Enigmatic caretakers of Mau in the Astral Plane
- **Axiomites** — Living mathematical constructs serving Gyru in Utopia
- **Inevitables** — Enforcers of cosmic law serving Guzon in Utopia
- **Proteans** — Serpentine chaos servants of Kazrokath in Limbo
- **Slaadi** — Insane chaos creatures serving Thalaman in Limbo

### Elemental & Natural Beings
- **Elementals** — Simple spirits of Air, Earth, Fire, and Water
- **Genies** — Malevolent elemental spirits seeking revenge for past servitude
- **Kami** — Elemental spirits serving Ashura in the Positive Energy Plane
- **Fey** — Woodland creatures from the Feywild and Prime Material
- **Faer'peri** — Halfling-animal spirits in Hinterhome

### Death & Darkness
- **Nightshades** — Elemental spirits of entropy serving Zebulba
- **Oni** — Evil servants of Khan in the Negative Energy Plane

### Titans & Mighty Outsiders
- **Titans** — Giant warriors serving Quarzel in the Fields of Battle
- **Animal Lords** — Powerful shapeshifting animal spirits serving Garnak

### Dragons & Drakaii
- **Dragons** — Ageless masters of magic, immigrants from doomed Lystra
- **Drakaii** — Multi-armed reptilian progenitors of dragons

### Unique Beings
- **Favored** — Mortals empowered by entities with special powers
- **Gromthi** — Dwarven spirits in Mordrion's realm in Utopia
- **Ry'lehe** — Aberrations from the Far Realm

## Using This Chapter

Each monster type below is described with its origin, nature, and role in the cosmos. Individual creature stat blocks and detailed information can be found in the Cypher System reference documents. When creating adventures or encounters, consider the planar origin and alignment of these creatures, as their motivations and tactics will reflect their home planes and the entities they serve.
