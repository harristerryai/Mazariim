# Druid

**Type**: Nature Spellcaster
**Role**: Divine nature spellcaster who channels primal magic

## Starting Stat Pools
- **Might**: 10
- **Speed**: 8
- **Intellect**: 10
- **Additional Points**: As determined by your campaign's character progression chart

## Starting Edge
Intellect Edge 1

## Weapon and Armor Proficiencies
You are practiced in light weapons. You are practiced in light armor. If you are not practiced with a certain type of armor but wear it anyway, the Speed penalties further increase by 1 and you cannot cast spells.

## Starting Skills
Choose three skills from the following list in which you are not already trained: Appraise, Animal Handling, Athletics, Craft, Insight, Investigation, Knowledge, Language, Navigation, Perception, Profession, Research, Spellcraft, Stealth, Survival, Vehicle.

## Divine Patron
You serve a divine patron, an entity associated with nature, the elements, balance, or the primal world (such as Minerva, Hinterhin, Gruhathyrir, Vuleala, Pyrantor, Iquinias, or Dirgin). You channel divine energy from your patron. You and your GM should work together to determine the nature of your relationship with this entity.

## Magical Training
You have learned magical methods for changing yourself, creatures, and the environment around you. Some druids take the magic given by their patron and use it to shape the world. Others see themselves as vessels, or avatars, of their patron's will and use their patron's magic to perform miracles. Whatever the source of your power, you share these characteristics:

- You are trained in Spellcraft
- Most spells you cast require gestures and/or speaking aloud (somatic and verbal components)
- You must have your spell focus, spellbook, or holy text available to cast a spell
- Casting a spell costs stat pool points
- Some powerful spells cost experience points in addition to normal costs

## Spellcasting
Your magic is stored in a spellbook or holy text containing six low-tier spells plus any additional spells you learn. At any given time, you can prepare a number of spells equal to 4 plus your Intellect Edge plus your level. After a ten-hour recovery roll, you can revert any previous preparation and prepare a new set of spells. After you make your ten-hour recovery roll, you must spend at least one hour studying your spellbook to choose and prepare spells.

New spells are added to your spellbook as you gain levels. At low-tier levels, you add low-tier Druid spells. At mid-tier levels, you add low-tier or mid-tier Druid spells. At high-tier levels, you add Druid spells of any tier.

## Low-Tier Abilities (Levels 1–3)

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Spell Focus**: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus. You are trained in Spellcraft rolls to cast spells of that school, damage type, or focus. Alternatively, if the spell is not an attack, reduces its Intellect cost by 1 point.
- **Skill with Intellect Defense**: You are trained in Intellect defense tasks.

### Special Abilities

**Animal Form*** (3+ Intellect points): You use your action to magically assume the shape of an animal that you have seen before. The animal can be medium size or smaller. You remain in this form for up to 1 hour. You can renew the duration of the Animal Shape while still in form without changing back and forth. You revert to normal form if you fall unconscious or become debilitated, or die. Action.

**Animal Senses and Sensibilities**: You are trained in listening and spotting things. Enabler.

**Augment Summoning***: When you cast a summoning spell and spend a level of Effort, the summons is one level higher, has +1 Armor, and has one more point of Health per level. Enabler.

**Battlecaster***: When you spend a level of Effort on a spell attack, you gain a free level on damage. Enabler.

**Beast Companion**: A level 2 creature of your size or smaller accompanies you and follows your instructions. You and the GM must work out the details of your creature, and you'll probably make rolls for it in combat or when it takes actions. The beast companion acts on your turn. As a level 2 creature, it has a target number of 6 and 6 health and it inflicts 2 points of damage. Its movement is based on its creature type (avian, swimmer, and so on). If your beast companion dies, you can hunt in the wild for 1d6 days to find a new one. Enabler.

**Brew Potion***: You know how to brew potions, oils, and elixirs. Creating potions requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Cantrip*** (1 Intellect point): From your extensive study of magic, you have learned some minor spells called cantrips. You can cast these spells to perform minor magical effects as you choose, though the effects cannot harm or damage creatures or objects. This power includes the Light, Message and Hand spells. Additionally, it includes Druidcraft. Action.

**Closed Mind***: You have +3 Armor against damage that selectively targets your Intellect Pool (which normally ignores Armor). Enabler.

**Combat Casting***: Your spellcasting concentration and your defensive casting rolls are eased one step. Enabler.

**Communication** (2 Intellect points): You can convey a basic concept to a creature that normally can't speak or understand speech. The creature can also give you a very basic answer to a simple question. Action.

**Craft Arms and Armor***: You know how to create magical weapons and armors. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Craft Wand***: You know how to create magical wands, items that cast spells but have limited charges. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Craft Wondrous Item***: You know how to create miscellaneous magical items. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Enhanced Intellect**: You gain 3 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

**Enlarge Spell*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to increase the range of a spell one step (touch to immediate, immediate to short, short to long, long to very long). Enabler.

**Extend Spell*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to change the duration of a spell from one minute or ten minutes to one hour, one hour to eight hours, or eight hours to twenty-four hours. Enabler.

**Eyes Adjusted**: You can see in extremely dim light as though it were bright light. You can see in total darkness as if it were extremely dim light. Enabler.

**Investigative Skills***: You are trained in two skills in which you are not already trained (Insight, Investigation, Navigation, Perception, Survival). Enabler.

**Knowledge Skills***: You are trained in two skills in which you are not already trained (Appraise, Animal Handling, Language, Knowledge, Research). Enabler.

**Mount**: A level 3 creature serves you as a mount and follows your instructions. While you're mounted on it, the creature can move and you can attack on your turn, which provides an asset to your attack. If your mount dies, you can hunt in the wild for 3d6 days to find a new one. Enabler.

**Natural Spell*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to cast spells while in animal shape, polymorph, shapechange, or any other altered shape that disallows spellcasting. Enabler.

**Resonance Field** (1 Intellect point): Faint lines in a color you choose form a tracery over your entire body and emit faint light. The effect lasts for one minute. Whenever a creature within immediate range makes an attack against you, the pattern energizes to block the attack. You can make an Intellect defense roll in place of the defense roll you would normally make. If you do so and you get a minor effect, the creature attacking you takes 1 point of damage. If you get a major effect, the creature attacking you takes 4 points of damage. Action to initiate.

**Scribe Scroll***: You know how to scribe spells onto scrolls. Inscribing scrolls requires time, gold, a place to work (library, shrine, or grove), and must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Sculpt Flesh** (2 Intellect points): You cause a willing creature's fingers to lengthen into claws and their teeth to grow into fangs. The effect lasts for ten minutes. The target's unarmed attacks are eased and inflict 4 points of damage. Action.

**Soothe the Savage** (2 Intellect points): You calm a nonhuman beast within 30 feet (9 m). You must speak to it (although it doesn't need to understand your words), and it must see you. It remains calm for one minute or for as long as you focus all your attention on it. The GM has final say over what counts as a nonhuman beast, but unless the creature is very dangerous, it does not have a chance to attack before it is calmed. Action.

**Spell Penetration***: If the target of your spell has modifications to Resist Magic, this ability negates one level of this protection. Enabler.

**Spell Potency***: Your spells gain a level of Potency, making them harder to dispel. When you spend a level of Effort on a spell attack, you gain an additional level of Potency. Enabler.

**Spell Power***: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus. Your spells from this school do one additional point of damage. You may take this ability multiple times, each time choosing a new spell school. Enabler.

**Stronger Together**: When you and your companion from the Beast Companion ability are within immediate distance of each other, you inflict 2 additional points of damage when you attack and both of you gain an asset to defense actions. Enabler.

**Subtle Spell*** (2 levels of Effort): You can spend two levels of Effort to cast a spell without verbal or somatic components. Enabler.

**Wilderness Encouragement** (3 Intellect points): While in the wilderness, or when talking about your time in the wilderness, your stirring words of encouragement grant a target within short range that can understand you 1d6 points to one Pool. You can't use this ability on the same creature again until they've made a recovery roll. Action.

**Wilderness Explorer**: While taking any action (including fighting) in the wild, you ignore any penalties due to natural causes such as tall grass, thick brush, rugged terrain, weather, and so on. Enabler.

**Wilderness Life**: You are trained in two of the following: climbing, swimming, navigation, or identifying plants and creatures. Enabler.

**Wilderness Lore**: You are trained in wilderness navigation and in identifying plants and creatures. Enabler.

## Mid-Tier Abilities (Levels 4–6)

### Martial Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Skill with Attacks**: Choose one weapon type or weapon group. You are trained in attacks using that weapon type.
- **Skill with Defense**: Choose one type of defense task (Might, Speed, or Intellect). You are trained in that defense.
- **Experienced in Armor**: You reduce the Speed penalty for wearing armor by 1.

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Greater Spell Focus**: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus in which you have Spell Focus. You become specialized in Spellcraft rolls to cast spells of that school, damage type, or focus. Alternatively, if the spell is not an attack, reduces its Intellect cost by 2 points.
- **Mastery with Intellect Defense**: If you are already trained in Intellect defense, you become specialized. If untrained, you become trained.

### Special Abilities

**Beast Call** (5 Intellect points): You summon a horde of small animals or a single level 4 beast to help you temporarily. These creatures do your bidding for as long as you focus your attention, but you must use your action each turn to direct them. Creatures are native to the area and arrive under their own power, so if you're in an unreachable place, this ability won't work. Action.

**Beast Eyes** (3 Intellect points): By linking to the creature from your Beast Companion ability, you can perceive through its senses if it is within 1 mile (1.5 km) of you. This effect lasts up to ten minutes. Action to establish.

**Craft Rods and Staves***: You know how to create magical rods, items of power that can cast spells without depletion. You know how to create magical staves, items of power that can add spells to a caster's spell list. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Energy Substitution*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to change the damage type of a spell. The original spell and new damage type must be from the following list: fire, lightning, cold, acid, or thunder. Enabler.

**Enhanced Intellect Edge**: You gain +1 to your Intellect Edge. Enabler.

**Greater Spell Penetration***: If the target of your spell has modifications to Resist Magic and you have the Spell Penetration ability, this ability negates two levels of this protection. Enabler.

**Greater Spell Power***: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus you have Spell Power in. Your spells from this school do two additional points of damage. You may take this ability multiple times, each time choosing a new spell school. Enabler.

**Improved Companion**: You gain a level 3 follower. They are not restricted on their modifications. You can take this ability multiple times, each time gaining another level 3 follower. Alternatively, you could choose to advance a level 2 follower you already have to level 3, or a level 3 follower to level 4. Enabler.

**Improved Counterspell***: When you cast dispel magic or other similar magic, you gain a free level of Effort. Enabler.

**One With the Wild** (6 Intellect points): For the next hour, natural animals and plants within long range will not knowingly harm you or those you designate as allies. In addition, your Might Edge, Speed Edge, and Intellect Edge increase by 1, and if you make any recovery rolls during this period, you recover twice as many points as normal. Action to initiate.

**Resist Magic***: You gain an asset in all types of defense actions to resist magical effects. Enabler.

**The Wild Is on Your Side** (5 Intellect points): While you're in the wilderness, foes within short range are tripped by rocks, tangled in vines, bitten by insects, and distracted or confused by small animals, which hinders all their tasks for ten minutes. Action to initiate.

**Widen Spell*** (2 levels of Effort): You can spend two levels of Effort to double the area of a spell. Enabler.

**Wilderness Awareness** (4 Intellect points): You can extend your senses up to a mile in any direction and ask the GM a very simple, general question about that area, such as "Where is the largest source of water?" or "Is there a settlement nearby?" If the answer you seek is not in the area, you receive no information. Action.

## High-Tier Abilities (Levels 7–10)

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Supreme Spell Focus**: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus in which you have Greater Spell Focus. You become expert in Spellcraft rolls to cast spells of that school, damage type, or focus. Alternatively, if the spell is not an attack, reduces its Intellect cost by 3 points.
- **Expertise with Intellect Defense**: If you are already specialized in Intellect defense, you become expert. If trained, you become specialized. If untrained, you become trained.

### Special Abilities

**As If One Creature**: You and your companion are so close, you act and move as if you are one creature. While your companion is within immediate range, you share one initiative, one set of actions, and you can use either your position or your companion's to determine range. Enabler.

**Control the Savage** (6 Intellect points): You can control a calm nonhuman beast within 30 feet (9 m). You control it for as long as you focus all your attention on it, using your turn each round. The GM has final say over what counts as a nonhuman beast, but unless some kind of deception is at work, you should know whether you can affect a creature before you attempt to use this ability on it. Aliens, extradimensional entities, very intelligent creatures, and robots never count. Action.

**Divide Your Mind** (7 Intellect points): You split your consciousness into two parts. For one minute, you can take two actions on each of your turns, but only one of them can be to use a special ability. Action.

**Greater Enhanced Intellect**: You gain 6 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

**Greater Resist Magic***: You gain two assets in all types of defense actions to resist magical effects. Enabler.

**Usurp Artifact**: Choose one artifact that you carry. The artifact must have an effect that is not instantaneous. You destroy the artifact and gain its power, which functions for you continuously. You can choose when to activate the power as normal. This works only once. You can't use this ability on the same type of artifact twice. Action to initiate.

**Wild Camouflage** (4 Intellect points): By drawing your clothing about you just so and using various tricks and your deep knowledge of your surroundings, you become invisible in the wilderness for ten minutes. While you are invisible, this asset eases your stealth and Speed defense tasks by two steps. This effect ends if you do something to reveal your presence or position — attacking, using an ability, moving a large object, and so on. If this occurs, you can regain the remaining invisibility effect by taking an action to focus on hiding your position. Action to initiate or reinitiate.
