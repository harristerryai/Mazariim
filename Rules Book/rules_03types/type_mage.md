# Mage

**Type**: Arcane Spellcaster
**Role**: Intelligence-based arcane spellcaster focusing on spell preparation and versatile spellcasting

## Starting Stat Pools
- **Might**: 7
- **Speed**: 9
- **Intellect**: 12
- **Additional Points**: As determined by your campaign's character progression chart

## Starting Edge
Intellect Edge 1

## Weapon and Armor Proficiencies
You are practiced in light weapons. You are not practiced in the use of armor. If you are not practiced with a certain type of armor but wear it anyway, the Speed penalties further increase by 1 and you cannot cast spells.

## Starting Skills
Choose three skills from the following list in which you are not already trained: Appraise, Craft, Insight, Investigation, Knowledge, Language, Navigation, Perception, Profession, Research, Spellcraft, Vehicle.

## Magical Training
You are trained in the basics of magic. Your magical abilities are considered spells. You are practiced in Spellcraft. Most spells you cast require gestures and/or speaking aloud (somatic and verbal components). You must have your spell focus or spellbook available to cast a spell. Casting a spell costs stat pool points. Some powerful spells cost experience points in addition to normal costs.

## Spellcasting
Your magic is stored in a spellbook containing six low-tier spells plus any additional spells you learn. At any given time, you can prepare a number of spells equal to 4 plus your Intellect Edge plus your level. After a ten-hour recovery roll, you can revert any previous preparation and prepare a new set of spells. After you make your ten-hour recovery roll, you must spend at least one hour studying your spellbook to choose and prepare spells.

New spells are added to your spellbook as you gain levels. At low-tier levels, you add low-tier Mage spells. At mid-tier levels, you add low-tier or mid-tier Mage spells. At high-tier levels, you add Mage spells of any tier.

## Low-Tier Abilities (Levels 1–3)

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Spell Focus**: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus. You are trained in Spellcraft rolls to cast spells of that school, damage type, or focus. Alternatively, if the spell is not an attack, reduces its Intellect cost by 1 point.
- **Skill with Intellect Defense**: You are trained in Intellect defense tasks.

### Special Abilities

**Applying Your Knowledge**: You're trained in two areas of knowledge of your choice. Enabler.

**Augment Summoning***: When you cast a summoning spell and spend a level of Effort, the summons is one level higher, has +1 Armor, and has one more point of Health per level. Enabler.

**Battlecaster***: When you spend a level of Effort on a spell attack, you gain a free level on damage. Enabler.

**Brew Potion***: You know how to brew potions, oils, and elixirs. Creating potions requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Cantrip*** (1 Intellect point): From your extensive study of magic, you have learned some minor spells called cantrips. You can cast these spells to perform minor magical effects as you choose, though the effects cannot harm or damage creatures or objects. This power includes the Light, Message and Hand spells. Additionally, it includes Prestidigitation. Action.

**Closed Mind***: You have +3 Armor against damage that selectively targets your Intellect Pool (which normally ignores Armor). Enabler.

**Combat Casting***: Your spellcasting concentration and your defensive casting rolls are eased one step. Enabler.

**Craft Arms and Armor***: You know how to create magical weapons and armors. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Craft Wand***: You know how to create magical wands, items that cast spells but have limited charges. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Craft Wondrous Item***: You know how to create miscellaneous magical items. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Critter Companion**: A level 1 creature accompanies you and follows your instructions. This creature is no larger than a large cat (about 20 pounds, or 9 kg) and is normally some sort of domesticated species. You and the GM must work out the details of your creature, and you'll probably make rolls for it in combat or when it takes actions. The critter companion acts on your turn. As a level 1 creature, it has a target number of 3 and 3 health, and it inflicts 1 point of damage. Its movement is based on its creature type (avian, swimmer, and so on). If your critter companion dies, you can search an urban or wild environment for 1d6 days to find a new one. Enabler.

**Enhanced Intellect**: You gain 3 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

**Enlarge Spell*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to increase the range of a spell one step (touch to immediate, immediate to short, short to long, long to very long). Enabler.

**Extend Spell*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to change the duration of a spell from one minute or ten minutes to one hour, one hour to eight hours, or eight hours to twenty-four hours. Enabler.

**Investigative Skills***: You are trained in two skills in which you are not already trained (Insight, Investigation, Navigation, Perception, Survival). Enabler.

**Knowledge Is Power**: Choose two noncombat skills in which you are not trained. You are trained in those skills. Enabler.

**Knowledge Skills***: You are trained in two skills in which you are not already trained (Appraise, Animal Handling, Language, Knowledge, Research). Enabler.

**Monster Lore**: You are trained in identifying monsters and their weaknesses. Enabler.

**Natural Spell*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to cast spells while in animal shape, polymorph, shapechange, or any other altered shape that disallows spellcasting. Enabler.

**Quick Study**: You learn from repetitive actions. You gain a +1 bonus to rolls for similar tasks after the first time (such as operating the same device, picking the same kind of lock, or disabling the same type of trap). Once you move on and do other things — usually after the passage of at least a few hours — you lose the familiarity with the task. Enabler.

**Resonance Field** (1 Intellect point): Faint lines in a color you choose form a tracery over your entire body and emit faint light. The effect lasts for one minute. Whenever a creature within immediate range makes an attack against you, the pattern energizes to block the attack. You can make an Intellect defense roll in place of the defense roll you would normally make. If you do so and you get a minor effect, the creature attacking you takes 1 point of damage. If you get a major effect, the creature attacking you takes 4 points of damage. Action to initiate.

**Scribe Scroll***: You know how to scribe spells onto scrolls. Inscribing scrolls requires time, gold, a place to work (library, shrine, or grove), and must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Spell Penetration***: If the target of your spell has modifications to Resist Magic, this ability negates one level of this protection. Enabler.

**Spell Potency***: Your spells gain a level of Potency, making them harder to dispel. When you spend a level of Effort on a spell attack, you gain an additional level of Potency. Enabler.

**Spell Power***: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus. Your spells from this school do one additional point of damage. You may take this ability multiple times, each time choosing a new spell school. Enabler.

**Subtle Spell*** (2 levels of Effort): You can spend two levels of Effort to cast a spell without verbal or somatic components. Enabler.

**There's Your Problem**: You are trained in tasks related to figuring out how to solve problems with multiple solutions. Enabler.

## Mid-Tier Abilities (Levels 4–6)

### Martial Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Skill with Attacks**: Choose one weapon type or weapon group. You are trained in attacks using that weapon type.
- **Skill with Defense**: Choose one type of defense task (Might, Speed, or Intellect). You are trained in that defense.
- **Experienced in Armor**: You reduce the Speed penalty for wearing armor by 1.

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Greater Spell Focus**: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus in which you have Spell Focus. You become specialized in Spellcraft rolls to cast spells of that school, damage type, or focus. Alternatively, if the spell is not an attack, reduces its Intellect cost by 2 points.
- **Mastery with Intellect Defense**: If you are already trained in Intellect defense, you become specialized. If untrained, you become trained.

### Special Abilities

**Energy Substitution*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to change the damage type of a spell. The original spell and new damage type must be from the following list: fire, lightning, cold, acid, or thunder. Enabler.

**Greater Spell Penetration***: If the target of your spell has modifications to Resist Magic and you have the Spell Penetration ability, this ability negates two levels of this protection. Enabler.

**Greater Spell Power***: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus you have Spell Power in. Your spells from this school do two additional points of damage. You may take this ability multiple times, each time choosing a new spell school. Enabler.

**Improved Companion**: You gain a level 3 follower. They are not restricted on their modifications. You can take this ability multiple times, each time gaining another level 3 follower. Alternatively, you could choose to advance a level 2 follower you already have to level 3, or a level 3 follower to level 4. Enabler.

**Improved Counterspell***: When you cast dispel magic or other similar magic, you gain a free level of Effort. Enabler.

**Knowledge Is Power**: Choose two noncombat skills in which you are not trained. You are trained in those skills. Enabler.

**Knowing the Unknown** (6 Intellect points): By accessing the resources appropriate to your character, you can ask the GM one question and get a general answer. The GM assigns a level to the question, so the more obscure the answer, the more difficult the task. Generally, knowledge that you could find by looking somewhere other than your current location is level 1, and obscure knowledge of the past is level 7. Action.

**Pay It Forward** (3 Intellect points): You can pass on what you've learned. When you give another character a suggestion involving their next action that is not an attack, their action is eased for one minute. Action.

**Widen Spell*** (2 levels of Effort): You can spend two levels of Effort to double the area of a spell. Enabler.

## High-Tier Abilities (Levels 7–10)

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Supreme Spell Focus**: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus in which you have Greater Spell Focus. You become expert in Spellcraft rolls to cast spells of that school, damage type, or focus. Alternatively, if the spell is not an attack, reduces its Intellect cost by 3 points.
- **Expertise with Intellect Defense**: If you are already specialized in Intellect defense, you become expert. If trained, you become specialized. If untrained, you become trained.

### Special Abilities

**Craft Rods and Staves***: You know how to create magical rods, items of power that can cast spells without depletion. You know how to create magical staves, items of power that can add spells to a caster's spell list. Creating magical items requires time, gold, and a place to work (library, shrine, or grove). The creator must meet any minimum tier requirements or material component requirements. Enabler.

**Divide Your Mind** (7 Intellect points): You split your consciousness into two parts. For one minute, you can take two actions on each of your turns, but only one of them can be to use a special ability. Action.

**Enhanced Intellect Edge**: You gain +1 to your Intellect Edge. Enabler.

**Greater Enhanced Intellect**: You gain 6 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

**Greater Resist Magic***: You gain two assets in all types of defense actions to resist magical effects. Enabler.

**Learned a Few Things**: You are trained in two areas of knowledge of your choice, or specialized in one area of knowledge of your choice. Enabler.

**Read the Signs** (4 Intellect points): You examine an area and learn precise, useful details about the past (if any). You can ask the GM up to four questions about the immediate area. Action.

**Resist Magic***: You gain an asset in all types of defense actions to resist magical effects. Enabler.

**Tower of Intellect**: You are trained in Intellect defense tasks. If you are already trained, you are specialized in those tasks instead. Enabler.

**Usurp Artifact**: Choose one artifact that you carry. The artifact must have an effect that is not instantaneous. You destroy the artifact and gain its power, which functions for you continuously. You can choose when to activate the power as normal. This works only once. You can't use this ability on the same type of artifact twice. Action to initiate.
