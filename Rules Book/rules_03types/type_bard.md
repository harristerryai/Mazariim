# Bard

**Type**: Explorer
**Role**: Artistic performer who uses music, lore, and magic to inspire allies and confound enemies

## Starting Stat Pools
- **Might**: 9
- **Speed**: 8
- **Intellect**: 11
- **Additional Points**: As determined by your campaign's character progression chart

## Starting Edge
Intellect Edge 1

## Weapon and Armor Proficiencies
You are practiced in light weapons. You are practiced in light armor.

## Starting Skills
Choose any four skills in which you are not already trained.

## Magical Training
You have learned magical methods for aiding allies and hindering enemies. Some bards learn magic through academic study, while others discover their power through performance and natural talent. Whatever the source of your power, you share these characteristics:

- You are trained in the basics of magic
- Most spells you cast require gestures and/or speaking aloud (somatic and verbal components)
- You must have your spell focus, spellbook, or performance instrument available to cast a spell
- Casting a spell costs stat pool points
- Some powerful spells cost experience points in addition to normal costs

## Spellcasting
Your magic is stored in a spellbook, songbook, or other recording method containing six low-tier Bard spells plus any additional spells you learn. At any given time, you can prepare a number of spells equal to 4 plus your Intellect Edge plus your level. After a ten-hour recovery roll, you can revert any previous preparation and prepare a new set of spells. After you make your ten-hour recovery roll, you must spend at least one hour studying your spellbook or practicing your performance to choose and prepare spells.

New spells are added to your spellbook as you gain levels. At low-tier levels, you add low-tier Bard spells. At mid-tier levels, you add low-tier or mid-tier Bard spells plus one spell from any spell list. At high-tier levels, you add Bard spells of any tier plus one spell from any spell list.

## Low-Tier Abilities (Levels 1–3)

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Spell Focus**: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus. You are trained in Spellcraft rolls to cast spells of that school, damage type, or focus. Alternatively, if the spell is not an attack, reduces its Intellect cost by 1 point.
- **Skill with Intellect Defense**: You are trained in Intellect defense tasks.

### Special Abilities

**Anecdote** (2 Intellect points): You can lift the spirits of a group within short range and help them relax. For the next hour, those who heard your anecdote gain an asset on their next Intellect-based task. Action.

**Babel**: After hearing a language spoken for a few minutes, you can speak it and make yourself understood. If you continue to use the language to interact with native speakers, your skills improve rapidly, to the point where you might be mistaken for a native speaker after just a few hours of speaking the new language. Enabler.

**Basic Follower**: You gain a level 2 follower. One of their modifications must be persuasion. You can take this ability multiple times, each time gaining another level 2 follower. Enabler.

**Calm Stranger** (2+ Intellect points): You can cause one intelligent creature to remain calm as you speak. The creature doesn't need to speak your language, but it must be able to see you. It remains calm as long as you focus all your attention on it and it is not attacked or otherwise threatened. In addition to the normal options for using Effort, you can choose to use Effort to calm additional creatures allied with your initial target, one additional creature per level of Effort applied. Action.

**Demeanor of Command** (2 Intellect points): You project confidence, knowledge, and charisma to all who see you for the next hour. Your demeanor is such that those who see you automatically understand that you are someone important, accomplished, and with authority. When you speak, strangers who are not already attacking give you at least a round to have your say. You gain a free level of Effort that can be applied to one persuasion task you attempt during this period. Action to initiate.

**Disincentivize** (1 Intellect point): You hinder all actions attempted by any number of targets within short range who can understand you. You choose which targets are affected. Affected targets' actions are hindered for one round. Enabler.

**Encouragement*** (1 Intellect point): While you maintain this ability through ongoing inspiring oration, allies within short range ease one task type (defense, attack, or skill training). Action to initiate, free to maintain.

**Enhanced Potential**: You gain 3 points to divide among your stat Pools however you wish. Enabler.

**Enlarge Spell*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to increase the range of a spell one step (touch to immediate, immediate to short, short to long, long to very long). Enabler.

**Extend Spell*** (1 level of Effort): You can spend a level of Effort to change the duration of a spell from one minute or ten minutes to one hour, one hour to eight hours, or eight hours to twenty-four hours. Enabler.

**Fast Talk** (1 Intellect point): When speaking with an intelligent creature who can understand you and isn't hostile, you convince that creature to take one reasonable action in the next round. A reasonable action must be agreed upon by the GM; it should not put the creature or its allies in obvious danger or be wildly out of character. Action.

**Gather Intelligence** (2 Intellect points): When in a group of people (a caravan, a palace, a village, a city, etc.) you can ask around about any topic you choose and come away with useful information. You also get a good idea of the general layout of the location and note presence of major sites or obscure details. Action to initiate; takes about an hour.

**Good Advice**: When you help another character, they gain an additional asset beyond the normal benefit of helping. Enabler.

**Greater Enhanced Intellect**: You gain 6 points to your Intellect Pool. Enabler.

**Impart Ideal** (4 Intellect points): You instill an ideal, goal, or belief into a creature within short range that can understand you. This can't cause a creature to do something it strongly opposes or harm itself. After a minute or two, the ideal seems like the target's own idea. The effect lasts for one day. Action.

**Inspire Aggression** (2 Intellect points): Your words twist the mind of a character within short range who is able to understand you, unlocking their more primitive instincts. As a result, they gain an asset on their Might-based attack rolls for one minute. Action.

**Inspiration** (4 Intellect points): By speaking to and inspiring a creature, you ease the creature's next action by two steps. Action.

**Inspiring Ease** (4 Intellect points): You speak words of encouragement to a creature within short range. Their next action is eased by two steps. Action.

**Interaction Skills***: You are trained in two skills in which you are not already trained (Animal Handling, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, or Performance). Enabler.

**Knowledge Is Power**: Choose two noncombat skills in which you are not trained. You are trained in those skills. Enabler.

**Knowledge Skills***: You are trained in two skills in which you are not already trained (Appraise, Animal Handling, Language, Knowledge, Research). Enabler.

**Levity**: Through wit, charm, humor, and grace, you are trained in all social interactions other than those involving coercion or intimidation. During rests, you put friends and comrades at ease so much that they gain +1 to their recovery rolls. Enabler.

**Natural Charisma**: You are trained in all social interactions, whether they involve charm, learning a person's secrets, or intimidating others. Enabler.

**Speedy Recovery** (3 Intellect points): Your words enhance the normal regenerative ability of a character within short range. When they make a recovery roll, their rest time is halved, so a ten-minute recovery roll takes five minutes, a one-hour roll takes thirty minutes, and a ten-hour roll takes five hours. Action.

**Spin Identity** (2+ Intellect points): You convince all intelligent creatures who can see, hear, and understand you that you are someone or something other than who you actually are. You don't impersonate a specific individual; instead, you convince the creatures that you are a different category of person (a local instead of an outsider, a wealthy person instead of a poor one, a merchant instead of a wizard, and so on). The effect lasts for up to an hour. Action.

**Understanding** (1 Intellect point): You observe or study a creature or object. Your next interaction with that creature or object gains one asset. Action.

**Unexpected Betrayal**: Within a round or two of successfully using Enthrall, Fast Talk, or a similar ability on a target within immediate distance, the first attack you make on that target is eased by two steps. Once you use Unexpected Betrayal on a target, using your abilities or attempting simple persuasion on that target is permanently hindered by two steps. Enabler.

## Mid-Tier Abilities (Levels 4–6)

### Martial Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Skill with Attacks**: Choose one weapon type or weapon group. You are trained in attacks using that weapon type.
- **Skill with Defense**: Choose one type of defense task (Might, Speed, or Intellect). You are trained in that defense.
- **Experienced in Armor**: You reduce the Speed penalty for wearing armor by 1.

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Mastery with Intellect Defense**: If you are already trained in Intellect defense, you become specialized. If untrained, you become trained.

### Special Abilities

**Able Assistance** (2 Intellect points): Assist another with a task they are attempting. If you succeed on a task of the same difficulty, the character you're helping gains an asset on the task. Action.

**Anticipate Attack** (4 Intellect points): You can sense when an attack is about to happen and prepare for it. You gain an asset on Speed defense rolls against attacks for one minute. Action.

**Applying Your Knowledge**: You're trained in two areas of knowledge of your choice. Enabler.

**Blend In** (4 Intellect points): When you blend in, creatures still see you, but they attach no importance to your presence for about a minute. While blending in, you are specialized in stealth and Speed defense tasks. This effect ends if you do something to reveal your presence or position — attacking, using an ability, moving a large object, and so on. If this occurs, you can regain the remaining period of effect by taking an action to focus on seeming innocuous and as if you belong. Action to initiate or reinitiate.

**Calm** (3 Intellect points): Through jokes, song, or other art, you prevent a living foe from attacking anyone or anything for one round. Action.

**Captivate or Inspire**: You can use this ability in one of two ways. Either your words keep the attention of all NPCs that hear them for as long as you speak, or your words inspire all NPCs that hear them to function as if they were one level higher for the next hour. In either case, you choose which NPCs are affected. If anyone in the crowd is attacked while you're trying to speak to them, you lose the crowd's attention. Action to initiate.

**Closed Mind***: You have +3 Armor against damage that selectively targets your Intellect Pool (which normally ignores Armor). Enabler.

**Confounding Banter** (4 Intellect points): You spew a stream of nonsense to distract a foe within immediate range. On a successful Intellect roll, your defense roll against the creature's next attack before the end of the next round is eased. Action.

**Discerning Mind**: You have +3 Armor against damaging attacks and damaging effects that target your mind and Intellect. Defense rolls you make against attacks that attempt to confuse, persuade, frighten, or otherwise influence you are eased. Enabler.

**Discipline of Watchfulness** (7 Intellect points): You keep your allies on their toes with occasional questions, jokes, and even mock drills for those who care to join in. After spending 24 hours with you, your allies can apply a free level of Effort to any initiative tasks they attempt. This benefit is ongoing while you remain in the allies' company. It ends if you leave, but it resumes if you return to the allies' company within 24 hours. If you leave the allies' company for more than 24 hours, you must spend another 24 hours together to reactivate the benefit. You must spend the Intellect point cost each 24 hours you wish to keep the benefit active. Enabler.

**Expert Follower**: You gain a level 3 follower. They are not restricted on their modifications. You can take this ability multiple times, each time gaining another level 3 follower. Alternatively, you could choose to advance a level 2 follower you already have to level 3 and then gain a new level 2 follower. Enabler.

**Feint** (2 Speed points): If you use one action creating a misdirection or diversion, in the next round you can take advantage of your opponent's lowered defenses. Make a melee attack roll against that opponent. You gain an asset on this attack. If your attack is successful, it inflicts 4 additional points of damage. Action.

**Flee** (6 Intellect points): All non-allies within short distance who can hear your dreadful, intimidating words flee from you at top speed for one minute. Action.

**Flex Skill**: At the beginning of each day, choose one task (other than attacks or defense) on which you will concentrate. For the rest of that day, you're trained in that task. You can't use this ability with a skill in which you're already trained to become specialized. Enabler.

**Grand Deception** (3 Intellect points): You convince an intelligent creature of something wildly and obviously untrue. Action.

**Greater Enhanced Potential**: You gain 6 points to divide among your stat Pools as you wish. Enabler.

**Heightened Skills**: You are trained in two tasks of your choice (other than attacks or defense) in which you are not already trained. Enabler.

**Knowing the Unknown** (6 Intellect points): By accessing the resources appropriate to your character, you can ask the GM one question and get a general answer. The GM assigns a level to the question, so the more obscure the answer, the more difficult the task. Generally, knowledge that you could find by looking somewhere other than your current location is level 1, and obscure knowledge of the past is level 7. Action.

**Lead by Inquiry**: You keep your allies on their toes with occasional questions, jokes, and mock drills. After spending 24 hours with you, your allies are treated as if trained in tasks related to perception. This benefit is ongoing while you remain in your allies' company. It ends if you leave, but it resumes if you return to your allies' company within 24 hours. If you leave your allies' company for more than 24 hours, you must spend another 24 hours together to reactivate the benefit. Enabler.

**Master Entertainer**: Your Inspire action works more effectively, easing your allies' tasks by two steps rather than one step. Enabler.

**Oratory** (4 Intellect points): When speaking with a group of intelligent creatures that can understand you and aren't hostile, you convince them to take one reasonable action in the next round. A reasonable action must be agreed upon by the GM; it should not put the creatures or their allies in obvious danger or be wildly out of character. Action.

**Perfect Stranger** (3 Intellect points): You alter your posture and way of speaking and make a small but real alteration to an outfit (such as putting on or taking off a hat, reversing a cloak, and so on). For the next hour (or as long as you keep up the alteration), even creatures that know you well don't recognize you. All tasks related to hiding your true identity during this period gain one free level of Effort. Action to initiate.

**Quick Wits**: When performing a task that would normally require spending points from your Intellect Pool, you can spend points from your Speed Pool instead. Enabler.

**Read the Signs** (4 Intellect points): You examine an area and learn precise, useful details about the past (if any). You can ask the GM up to four questions about the immediate area. Action.

**Spell Potency***: Your spells gain a level of Potency, making them harder to dispel. When you spend a level of Effort on a spell attack, you gain an additional level of Potency. Enabler.

**Spur Effort** (5 Intellect points): You select an ally within immediate range. If that character applies Effort to a task on their next turn, they can apply a free level of Effort on that task. Enabler.

**Stimulate** (6 Intellect points): Your words encourage a target you touch who can understand you. The next action it takes is eased by three steps. Action.

**Strategize** (6 Intellect points): If you and your allies spend at least ten minutes going over a plan of action, all of you gain one free level of Effort that can be applied to one task you attempt during the execution of that plan within the next 24 hours. Action to initiate.

**Suggestion** (5+ Intellect points): You suggest an action to a creature within immediate range. If the creature can hear and understand you, it must make an Intellect defense roll or carry out your suggested action on its next turn. The creature must be level 2 or lower. The effect of your suggestion lasts for up to a minute. Following your suggestion can't be obviously dangerous to the creature, and the action can be an inaction, such as someone standing still, but not an action that someone else must take, such as providing you with a weapon. In addition to the normal options for using Effort, you can choose to use Effort to increase the maximum level of the target. Thus, to suggest an action to a level 5 target (three levels above the normal limit), you must apply three levels of Effort. Action to initiate.

**Telling** (2 Intellect points): This ability provides an asset to any tasks for attempting to deceive, persuade, or intimidate. Each use lasts up to a minute. Action to initiate.

## High-Tier Abilities (Levels 7–10)

### Spellcasting Skills (choose per progression chart)
- **Greater Spell Focus**: Choose one spell school, damage type, or focus in which you have Spell Focus. You become specialized in Spellcraft rolls to cast spells of that school, damage type, or focus. Alternatively, if the spell is not an attack, reduces its Intellect cost by 2 points.
- **Expertise with Intellect Defense**: If you are already specialized in Intellect defense, you become expert. If trained, you become specialized. If untrained, you become trained.

### Special Abilities

**Advanced Command** (7 Intellect points): You give a specific command to another intelligent creature. If it chooses to listen, any task it attempts during its next action is eased by three steps. Action.

**Assume Control** (6+ Intellect points): You control the actions of another creature you have interacted with or studied for at least a round. This effect lasts for ten minutes. The target must be level 2 or lower. Once you have assumed control, the target acts as if it wants to accomplish your desire to the best of its ability, freely using its own best judgment unless you use an action to give it a specific instruction on an issue-by-issue basis. In addition to the normal options for using Effort, you can choose to use Effort to increase the maximum level of the target. Thus, to attempt to command a level 5 target (three levels above the normal limit), you must apply three levels of Effort. When the effect ends, the target remembers everything that happened and reacts according to its nature and your relationship to it; assuming control might have soured that relationship if it was previously a positive one. Action to initiate.

**Band of Followers**: You gain four level 3 followers. They are not restricted on their modifications. Enabler.

**Battle Management** (4 Intellect points): As long as you use your action each round giving orders or advice, attack and defense actions taken by your allies within short range are eased. Action.

**Crowd Control** (6+ Intellect points): You control the actions of up to five creatures in short range. This effect lasts for one minute. All targets must be level 2 or lower. Your control is limited to simple verbal commands like "Stop," "Run away," "Follow that guard," "Look over there," or "Get out of my way." All affected creatures respond to the command unless you specifically command them otherwise. In addition to the normal options for using Effort, you can choose to use Effort to increase the maximum level of the targets or affect an additional five people. When the Crowd Control ability ends, the creatures remember your commands but don't remember being controlled — your commands seemed reasonable at the time. Action to initiate.

**Inspiring Success** (6 Intellect points): You speak words of encouragement and inspiration to one creature within short range. If that creature attempts a task within the next round, you treat the roll as if it were one step better than it actually was. This is not the same as easing the task; you actually manipulate the result. Action.

**Mind of a Leader** (6 Intellect points): When you develop a course of action to deal with a future situation, you can ask the GM one very general question about what is likely to happen if you carry out the plan, and you will get a simple, brief answer. Action.

**Read the Signs** (4 Intellect points): You examine an area and learn precise, useful details about the past (if any). You can ask the GM up to four questions about the immediate area. Action.

**Recruit Deputy**: You gain a level 4 follower. They are not restricted in their modifications. You and the GM must work out the details of your follower. Enabler.

**Tower of Intellect**: You are trained in Intellect defense tasks. If you are already trained, you are specialized in those tasks instead. Enabler.

**Vindictive Performance** (5 Intellect points): You select one individual from the audience who is able to understand you. During your performance, you heap indirect but biting derision on this target. If you succeed, the target doesn't realize that they've become the victim of your performance until you wrap up the entertainment at a moment you choose in a way that strikes home. The target suffers 6 points of Intellect damage (ignores Armor) and loses their next turn. One or more actions to initiate.
