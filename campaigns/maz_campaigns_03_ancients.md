# 03 - Wrath of the Ancients (819 AW)

## Campaign Overview

The results of large-scale planar disturbances from previous campaigns manifest in the "Wrath of the Ancients" as the long-sleeping shadow dragon Noctuminalis awakens. Working from the shadows, the dragon creates magical weapons tied to the elements and manipulates elemental cults to cause widespread destruction known as the Elemental Crisis.

## Campaign Background

### The Ancient Ones and Noctuminalis

The Ancient Ones were the primordial gods of the ancient world, entities of immense power who shaped the early cosmos. Among the dragons who observed their work was Noctuminalis, an ancient shadow dragon of exceptional cunning and patience.

Noctuminalis discovered that the worship of Zebulba (the god of death and undeath) was more efficient for gathering power than direct confrontation. For millennia, he worked to infiltrate and control Zebulban cults, positioning himself as a hidden master behind various death cults throughout Mazariim.

### The Awakening

The magical disturbances from the Orceri Incursion (811 AW) and especially the reawakening of Asti's ley-lines (815 AW) stirred Noctuminalis from his long slumber. Rather than reveal himself directly, the dragon began using dream magic to manipulate powerful mortals to do his bidding.

### The Umbral Hand

Noctuminalis took control of the Zebulban cult known as the Umbral Hand, a secretive organization that had operated in the shadows for centuries. Through this cult, he began orchestrating what would become the Elemental Crisis.

### The Elemental Weapons and Cults

Using guile and illusion, Noctuminalis created powerful magical weapons tied to the four classical elements—Earth, Air, Fire, and Water. He then motivated the long-forgotten elemental cults of the region to come back to life, filled with fervor for their elemental deities. Each cult believed they were serving their ancient elemental lords, unaware they were all pawns of the shadow dragon who styled himself as the "Elder Elemental Eye."

### Goals

Noctuminalis's goals were multi-layered:
1. Test the strength and unity of the Inner Kingdoms
2. Gather worshippers and magical power
3. Create chaos to mask other operations
4. Identify powerful individuals who might oppose future plans
5. Open permanent gateways to the elemental planes for future exploitation

## Campaign Synopsis

### Setting

The campaign is centered on the Inner Kingdoms and specifically the metropolis of Fort Hope. The region becomes ground zero for earthquakes, tidal waves, wildfires, and unnatural storms as the elemental cults begin their reign of destruction.

### Phase 1: Discovery - The Beginnings of Chaos

The initial phase of discovery involves the very beginnings of the attacks and chaos Noctuminalis hopes to cause.

**Early Adventures:**
- Investigation of strange natural disasters
- Discovery of elemental cult activity
- Tracking a missing diplomatic delegation
- First encounters with cultists possessing elemental weapons
- Realization that the disasters are coordinated, not random

### Phase 2: The Haunted Keeps

The heroes discover that the elemental cults have established forward bases called the "Haunted Keeps"—ancient fortifications that have been reoccupied and corrupted by elemental magic.

**Key Objectives:**
- Clear each Haunted Keep of cultists
- Recover intelligence about cult leadership
- Disrupt elemental rituals causing regional disasters
- Discover teleportation circles leading to cult headquarters

### Phase 3: Tyar-Zaruk - The Cult Headquarters

Following teleportation circles, the heroes reach Tyar-Zaruk, an ancient underground complex where the elemental cults have gathered. Here, each cult maintains its own section dedicated to its element.

**Challenges:**
- Navigate the dangerous underground complex
- Face elemental generals leading each cult
- Discover clues about the true mastermind (though Noctuminalis's identity remains hidden)
- Find the path to the Umbral Fane

### Phase 4: The Umbral Fane

The heroes pursue the two remaining elemental cult generals to their elemental nodes and must seal off the gateways to the elemental planes before they become permanent.

**The Final Confrontation:**
- Battle through elemental-infused terrain
- Face the most powerful cult leaders
- Close the planar gateways before they stabilize
- Discover hints of the "Elder Elemental Eye" (without learning Noctuminalis's true identity)

## Campaign Outcomes

### Victory Conditions

**If the Heroes Succeed:**
- The Elemental Crisis is averted
- Earthquakes, tidal waves, and other disasters cease
- The elemental cults shrink back into the shadows
- Portals to the elemental planes are sealed
- The Inner Kingdoms recognize the heroes as saviors

**Partial Success:**
- Some portals remain partially open
- Elemental cults are weakened but not destroyed
- Natural disasters decrease but don't fully stop
- Cult remnants continue operating secretly

### The Hidden Truth

Unknown to the heroes and the world, the true mastermind behind the Elemental Eye identity—Noctuminalis—escapes detection. The shadow dragon considers the campaign a successful test and information-gathering operation, learning much about the heroes and the strength of the nations while revealing little about himself.

### Political Consequences

During this time and for the next decade:
- Tensions between rival countries in the Inner Kingdoms begin to rise
- Saerrus shows its independent streak again
- Pragos begins to exert pressure in secretive ways in the Praetorian Senate of the Federation of Man
- Small wars break out among the Merchant Nations (though none rise to the level of large territorial changes or widespread death)

## Legacy

The Elemental Crisis becomes a defining moment for a new generation of heroes. While the Orceri Incursion united the older generation, this crisis forges new bonds and creates new champions who will be needed for the threats yet to come.

The earthquakes and upheavals caused by the Elemental Crisis also have an unintended consequence: they unearth ancient artifacts that had been buried for millennia—including the Devil Cube that will feature in a future campaign.
