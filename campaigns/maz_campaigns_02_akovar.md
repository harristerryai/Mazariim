# 02 - Rebirth of Akovar (815 AW)

## Campaign Overview

"The Rebirth of Akovar" campaign is a semi-sandbox set of adventures that tell the story of Akovar, an ancient civilization that reached incredible levels of proficiency with magic long before most humans were even civilized. It is also the tale of the entity known as Asti, an elemental deity formed of pure positive energy. Players have the chance to bring back Asti from imprisonment in a Varkonan artifact and thereby reawaken ley-lines in Akovar and other parts of the globe.

## Campaign Background

### The Birth of Asti (20,000 Years Before the Great War)

Long ago, twenty thousand years before the Great War, an entity awoke born of pure energy and creation, its mana gathering around a white hole, attracted to its exotic particles spewing from another part of the universe. As the new entity collapsed and created the Positive Energy Plane, Asti was born. Its gaze fell upon the world of Mazariim, and Asti took a female form, observing that females were the creatures reproducing and creating new life.

### Asti's Avatar and the Rise of Akovar (~12,000 Years Ago)

Watching the primitive inhabitants of the planet, her alien mind observed the decay of death and the sorrow it caused and wanted to go against the entropy, against the arrow of time. She sent her alien avatar to a small set of islands far from the bulk of the world, an island where seafaring people from a far-away continent had settled, a group of less than a thousand humans that were suffering from the ruggedness of the islands.

Asti's appearance in a small valley around twelve thousand years ago was greeted with wonder and fear by primitive humans. Almost no entities had shown themselves to the sentient races of Mazariim, and Asti's unspeaking, unmoving mass of energy and light hovered over a small clearing, almost like a small sun that gave off no heat. The humans thought it a god, and began to worship the silent avatar, erecting a small stone pyramid under it with a large temple complex.

### The Gifts of Asti

Time would pass, and the humans began to discover small differences about themselves. The plagues that had ravaged their numbers faded when someone lived within a few miles of Asti, and people who prayed at the temple or were even close to it began to heal faster. Decades later, the Akovarians found that they were living longer, with the elders of the clan beginning to be older and older, some living past 100 years of age. Soon some of the supplicants who worshiped Asti began to use magic to perform feats of healing, and these healers began to travel to the other villages, healing the sick and commanding reverence and obedience. The nation of Akovar was born.

Over the next two thousand years, the population of Akovar would surge as people lived longer lives, gaining in other ways as well from Asti's influence. The Akovarians very slowly were becoming smarter, stronger, and wiser, Asti's magics helping evolution along its course at a much-accelerated pace.

### Dragons and Arcane Magic (~9,000 Years Ago)

It was around nine thousand years ago that dragons began to take notice of the Akovarians and their magical proficiency, albeit only with divine magics. A few of these inquisitive dragons began to teach a number of Akovarians how to use arcane magics, teaching the Akovarians the dragon language as well as the secrets of how magic worked and interacted with the thin fabric of mana that suffused the universe. The new wizards and sorcerers and warlocks would study with the clerics and druids of Asti and incorporate themselves into an indispensable part of the clergy and governmental life.

### The Ley-Lines (8,200 BW)

As the human tinkering with arcane magics was beginning, the Akovarians had made great strides in a number of fields over the many centuries. Astronomy, cosmology, engineering, and mathematics had come as far as the humans and other races of the modern world, and these sciences would soon mesh with arcane sorcery to forever change the isles of Akovar. Dragons watched for what seemed like an instant to creatures that live for a thousand years as the "talking apes" known as Akovarians unraveled the secrets of mana through intellect and sheer tenacity.

Around 8200 BW the Akovarians completed large stone pyramids at their capital and a number of other cities and energized the first ley-lines, rivers of magical energy that stretched between the cities and the capital, powered by Asti's avatar. The magical energies not only spread Asti's healing powers to the farthest reaches of the islands, but it also allowed the priests and magi of Asti to perform great feats of magic, altering spells while casting and powering great rituals. The Akovarian civilization continued to flourish, and reached its height around 7000 BW, filled with a long and rich history that rivals most of today's civilizations. Soon, though, as with any civilization, Akovar would fall.

### The Dragon War (6,900 BW)

It was around 6900 BW that dragons from around Mazariim would begin to journey to Akovar, few in numbers at first, but eventually attracting entire flights of dragons. The magic of the ley-lines was felt by creatures with mana all around the globe, and the dragons began to battle with the Akovarians over land and eventually the avatar of Asti.

### The Fall and the Mask of the Pharaoh (6,830 BW)

During a grand battle at the Great Temple in 6830 BW, the arcanists and priests of Asti made a last stand against a number of powerful great wyrms, transferring the power of Asti into the Mask of the Pharaoh and weaving a powerful ritual that transported the majority of the population to a far-away undetermined land as the ley-lines surged and exploded. The explosion of magical energy killed most of the dragons who were at the capital, while other smaller explosions destroyed the cities and killed many of the rural Akovarians. The survivors became changed, their genetic code mutating and adapting, and with each generation some of the humans became more and more infused with dragon blood or the blood of the animals they were near, creating dragonborn and all manner of cross-breed races.

### Legacy and Modern Akovar

The explosion also left the islands covered in areas of wild and dead magic, with portals to extra-dimensional planes haphazardly appearing and disappearing, and the islands dangerous for those who did not know the land. The head priest and arcanist, Thz'zar Ja'laao, who had led the ritual, found himself stranded and insane from the trauma, becoming a powerful lich over the next few hundred years. Meanwhile, the transported Akovarians settled in the Ancient South and became the Varkonan culture, keeping the Mask of the Pharaoh hidden and sacred.

Today, Akovar is dominated by dragonborn, with dragons still present but fewer in number. The islands remain largely isolated from the wider world due to impossible headwinds that prevent ocean travel to its shores.

## Campaign Synopsis

### Campaign Structure

The campaign is divided into three main phases:

1. **Exploration of Akovar** - Discovery and investigation of the ancient ruins
2. **The Search for the Mask** - Journey to the Ancient South
3. **Restoring the Ley-Lines** - The final confrontation and restoration

### Phase 1: Exploration of Akovar

During the first phase of the campaign, the heroes travel to Akovar and begin to explore the islands. They arrive at New Sabonaa, a small Sabonaar outpost island, and can begin their explorations from there.

**Key Locations to Explore:**

**Iseueri** - A swamp ruin inhabited by black and bronze dragons, containing ancient archives and artifacts.

**Mibampes** - A planar horror lair where reality is unstable, home to aberrations that emerged from rift experiments.

**Ptei** - A mountain repository guarded by silver and white dragons who respect the ancient Akovarian knowledge and protect it from looters.

**Amaunet (The Capital)** - Contains the Grand Temple where Asti's avatar once resided. This is ground zero for the magical explosion and is heavily guarded by red dragons.

**Rival Groups:**
- Madronian anti-heroes seeking treasure and power
- Varkonan nationalists who view Akovar as their sacred homeland
- Dragon clans with conflicting agendas

### Phase 2: The Search for the Mask of the Pharaoh

The second part of the campaign involves the players searching for the Mask of the Pharaoh in the far-off lands of the Ancient South (Varkonan territories).

**Key Steps:**

1. **Consult the Shi'ars of Uadjit** - Seek information from Varkonan sages about the Mask's location
2. **Navigate Varkonan Politics** - Deal with nationalist factions who guard their heritage jealously
3. **Delve into Nepherites Pyramid** - A dangerous Varkonan pyramid complex where the Mask has been hidden for millennia
4. **Pursue by Rival Groups** - Competitors who also seek the Mask's power

### Phase 3: Restoring the Ley-Lines

In the final part of the campaign, the heroes need to enter the capital of the Akovarian ruins and defeat the red dragon clan that lairs there and release the mana from the Mask of the Pharaoh to restart the ley-lines.

**The Final Challenge:**
- Infiltrate or assault Amaunet (the ruined capital)
- Defeat the red dragon clan led by powerful great wyrms
- Reach the Grand Temple at the heart of the city
- Release Asti's essence from the Mask at the proper ritual site
- Choose the fate of the power: restore the ley-lines or claim it for personal mythic ascension

**Consequences:**

**If the Ley-Lines Are Restored:**
- Asti's healing magic spreads across Akovar and beyond
- Ley-lines pulse across the globe, visible to magical creatures everywhere
- Ancient entities and dragons stir from slumber (setting up future campaigns)
- Akovar begins a new golden age of healing and prosperity

**If the Power Is Claimed (Claw of Zuel):**
- One character achieves mythic power levels
- Divine intervention from other entities becomes likely
- The ley-lines remain dormant
- Personal consequences for defying an entity's purpose

## Campaign Consequences

The reawakening of Asti and the ley-lines sent ripples throughout the multiverse, again causing long-sleeping entities as well as other powerful creatures such as dragons and liches to stir from slumber. This directly led to the Elemental Crisis in the next campaign.
