# 01 - Orcs From Beyond (811 AW)

## Campaign Overview

The plotline for the "Orcs From Beyond" campaign revolves around orcs from an alternate prime material plane named Ta'Puh and their attempt to subjugate the world of Mazariim. With the help of the priest Aleron Windstrider, the heroes will attempt to figure out who the invaders are and discover a means of preventing their egress into our universe, protecting Aleron until the time comes in the finale when he will fulfill his purpose (hopefully).

## Campaign Background

### Ta'Puh - The Orceri Homeworld

The Orceri come from an alternate reality where the orcs were triumphant during the War To End All Wars, and subsequently enslaved the other races and control their globe completely. Ta'Puh roughly translates as "orc kings" in their language, and it is fitting for them.

The orcs from this strange historically-divergent reality are very different than the ones who live in the shadows and barren lands of Mazariim. When they conquered the humans, elves, and dwarves of their world some 800 years ago, they quickly spread and multiplied, absorbing knowledge from their new slaves and quickly advancing in their understanding of all things both mundane and magical. They have created huge cities on the ruins of the old empires, and have enslaved and domesticated the other races into perverse and abominable versions of their former selves.

### Interdimensional Conquest

Some 400 years ago, the Orceri sages and adepts learned of the existence of alternate realities, and slowly developed the ability to see, and eventually enter, these other prime material planes. With new lands to now rule over in the name of Zuel, the Orceri soon began to march into a number of worlds ill-equipped to stop these intelligent and resourceful orcs. With each world, the Orceri gained more resources, slaves, and space with which to expand, as well as new knowledge that was unknown in their own homeworld. Now, some 400 years and 11 alternate realities conquered from when they began their inter-dimensional conquests, the orceri have unintentionally discovered the world of Mazariim, and are considering it for their next invasion.

### Zanthar's Machine

It was in the year 310 BW that the wizard Zanthar, on the run and accused of crimes in what was then the Dukhan Empire, finally stopped and settled in what is known today as the Steppes of Broken Stone. There he crafted a small tower, disguised it as a group of boulders, and excavated tunnels to create a hidden domicile that would be protected from most visitors. Within a few years, Zanthar finally finished a prototype of a magical device that could peer into the many alternate dimensions around us. On its first use, he used it to peer into a universe where the Orceri lived, although at the time his scrying went undetected. Sadly, the machine was hard to control, and a surge of magical energies vaporized Zanthar into dust. A magical shockwave spread outward and then inward, and the tower portion of Zanthar's home crumbled into rubble. And so 1000 years would pass, none aware of what was beneath the earth . . .

### The Machine's Reawakening (811 AW)

Now, in the present-day year of 811 AW, we find that orcs and goblins infest the Steppes of Broken Stone. A large group of orcs, led by the shaman Kru'ka'ta, have taken over a small fort that hobgoblins and bugbears had built decades earlier out of the rubble of the tower. These orcs have been harassing trade shipments from Amara to Instar, hired by unscrupulous Amaran merchants trying to eliminate competitors. Months had gone by, and word had spread to the small town of Blowing Rock and a monastery of Paladine. One of the monks, a human by the name of Remular, had set out to follow a number of the caravans and follow any attackers back to their lair to learn of their numbers and determine if they could be defeated once and for all. Upon reaching the small keep, Remular was able to gain entrance and search much of the stone fortress, finding an excavation that the orcs had begun. This excavation led to a lower level, part of Zanthar's old tower, and to the machine. Sadly, Remular was killed trying to discover the use of the machine, and its brief activation by someone without magical abilities sent out a vibration across both universes. This inter-dimensional vibration was heard by two - the Orceri from Ta'Pah, and Aleron Windstrider.

### Aleron Windstrider - The Chosen of Thalaman

All the while, Aleron has been waiting near the keep, trying to discover what he had felt. He feels that his magical ability given to him by Thalaman has a purpose and that he is destined to do something important. Aleron has a magical gift given to him by Thalaman. This spell-like ability allows him to use any spell that relates to interdimensional travel, including teleport, dimension door, astral projection, plane shift, gate, etherealness, shadow walk, and any others the DM sees fit to use. These spells have a physical toll on him, so he has always used them sparingly. Now, however, after years in the secretive priesthood of Thalaman, Aleron thinks he may have found his destiny.

It would be a month later that the Orceri would send a scout to discover more of this world of Mazariim as well as the source of the inter-dimensional disturbance. The scout gained access to the keep and the lower level, and found the machine, although he did not try to use it. Ironically, the orcs in the keep discovered the spy and overwhelmed him with sheer numbers. They left his body in the room and quickly left the lower level of the keep, frightened of curses and other black magics from the machine.

## Campaign Synopsis

### Campaign Structure

There are two phases to the campaign, the first being discovery, and the second being engagement. In the first phase, the heroes and the Orceri are learning about each other and the alternate reality their enemies come from. This part of the campaign follows a more linear path. Once both groups (or one group, if the players are slow) learn about what is happening, however, the campaign changes and there are a number of small adventures that can be played in any order, depending on the actions of the heroes and the reactions of the Orceri.

### Phase 1: Discovery (Linear Adventures)

**Adventure 1: The Keep in the Steppes**
The players are recruited by an Amarian merchant to accompany a caravan heading to Instar in hopes of getting attacked by orcs that seem to have singled out this merchant's shipments. The heroes are to follow the orcs back to their lair and eliminate them if possible. The orcs lair in an old abandoned keep to the east in the Steppes of Broken Stone. It is within this keep that they will find Zanthar's machine as well as the remains of Remular the monk and an Orceri spy. The heroes will hopefully discover the machine and defeat the orcs, and return the monk's body to the village of Blowing Rock.

**Adventure 2: Siege of the Undead**
The players arrive in Blowing Rock and return the body of Remular to the monastery, only to discover the small town is besieged by the undead. Unbeknownst to the heroes, a small Zebulban cult has been growing in some ruins nearby, and they have finally gained enough strength to try and challenge the paladins and monks of the monastery of Paladine. The heroes will hopefully discover the location of the Zebulban cult and eliminate it once and for all, thereby gaining favor with [name], namely the ability to have a party member raised from the dead, albeit for an expensive price.

**Adventure 3: Arkhan's Hold**
The heroes will be approached by Aleron, and he will try to gain a sense of the player's motives after following them since the battle at the keep. He will try to join forces with them and suggest they go and see a dwarven sage in the town or Arkhan's Hold. While there, the sage will help the group identify some of the coins and other items found by the Orceri spy as well as find information on their world. While there, the dwarven leaders will ask the heroes to be arbiters in a dispute between two dwarven clans. The heroes will hopefully successfully navigate the intricacies of dwarven tradition as well as learn more about alternate realities and Aleron's gifts.

**Adventure 4: Instar and the Tuning Fork**
The heroes and Aleron head to Instar to check for information on a number of topics as well as to find someone capable of creating the tuning forks used during interdimensional travel. A wizard is more than willing to help them if they will help him with gathering some data on a magical creature roaming a certain section of countryside near the city. The heroes will hopefully find the beast and gather the data and survive, and thereby gain access to the tuning fork needed to find the alternate reality the Orceri live in.

### Phase 2: Engagement (Sandbox Adventures)

From this point onward, any of the following adventures can take place in any order, depending on what the players want to do:

**Scout the Orceri Homeworld**
This is a dangerous but necessary mission, to learn about the enemy and its strengths and weaknesses. Using the tuning fork and Aleron's powers, the heroes can venture into Ta'Puh to gather intelligence.

**Investigate an Orceri Attack**
At some point, the Orceri will launch a preliminary attack on a small town to test the strengths of the heroes' homeworld. They will arrive at Winderbourgh and attack the town, and Aleron will feel their presence as they cross over. Aleron can take the group quickly to the battle but will be weakened during the process.

**Activate the Machine**
If correctly used, the machine can peer into other alternate realities, but it is locked onto the coordinates of the Orceri world. If used, however, it will bring about an attack on the players from an Orceri interplanar strike force. If the heroes repel the enemy, they will not attack again. If they flee, the Orceri will destroy the machine.

**Gain Allies**
The players might try to plead their case to any number of groups, from the Instar mages to the Amara Merchants' Guild. With good RP, the heroes might be able to convince large power groups to be ready for attacks.

**Find the Orceri Commander**
Gutsy players might try to take the fight to the Orceri and learn of which commander is overseeing any possible invasion and eliminate them. A tough fight, if the players do succeed they will delay the Orceri for a time, but when the Orceri do come again they will be better prepared and greater in numbers.

### The Final Adventure: Gaal'Tarak

After some or all of those adventures are completed, the final event can take place. In the final adventure, Aleron will learn of the creation of a one-way portal into Mazariim deep within the ruins of Gaal'Tarak, a dwarven hold that suffered an unknown disaster some fifty years earlier. There, the heroes must help Aleron get to the portal. If the heroes are successful, they will allow Aleron to fulfill his destiny, giving his life and "gift" to help close the portal and block the entrance of the Orceri for 101 years into Mazariim. This action will, unknowingly to the players, be what destroys the dwarven citadel – a wave of destructive magical energy traveling backward in time.

## Campaign Consequences

The Orceri Incursion unified nations and mended racial rifts, but left the world dealing with tens of thousands of advanced orcs possessing powerful magical devices. More significantly, the sealing of the Orceri sent ripples through the universe, nudging ancient entities into more wakeful states—consequences that would manifest in future campaigns.
