# Mazariim Campaigns - Overview

## Recent History

The recent history of the Inner Kingdoms and Mazariim was created by the previous campaigns the players have gone through. Some thirty-five years ago at the turn of the eighth century since the War, the lands of Nigal were in turmoil. The Inner Kingdoms were on the verge of dissolution as squabbles between Saerrus and the Federation of Man were reaching a boiling point stoked by the other nations. The Dwarves from the mountains nearby had been in skirmishes with the Federation of Man as well. The Sabonaar had begun a series of expansionist expeditions to the far-off lands of the Azala and there was war brewing between Cecasta and Sabonna. The Elves were on the cusp of banning all non-elves save halflings from their lands. By the turn of the eighth century, the world was slowly returning to a place it was before the War.

## Campaign Timeline

### 811 AW - Orcs From Beyond

In 811, things changed as the races were forced to work together once more as they did some eight centuries prior. In the "Orcs From Beyond" campaign, the players helped the Chosen of Thalaman, a wizardly madman named Aleron Windrider, on his journey to save the world from the invading Orceri, orcs from another alternate timeline in which they had won the War and had eventually become more advanced, eventually beginning to conquer alternate prime material planes. With the help of the players, the Orceri were sealed inside their own alternate prime material plane, but the world's cities and nations still had to deal with tens of thousands of advanced orcs with magical devices of power most mages of our world had not even imagined. Nations became more unified and racial rifts mended in the aftermath of the devastation which came to be known as the Orceri Incursion. Unbeknownst to most, the sealing of the Orceri in their own plane sent ripples through the universe, nudging entities into more wakeful states . . .

### 815 AW - Rebirth of Akovar

Four short years later in 815 in the "Rebirth of Akovar" campaign, these same heroes would journey across the ocean and discover the lands of Akovar, a place lost to time by its isolation from the other two main continents of Mazariim. Learning of it from Sabonaar expansionists, the heroes found a huge set of islands ruled by dragons and inhabited by all manner of strange intelligent races not seen before. Dragonborn, aasimar, tieflings, genesai, kenku, tabaxi . . . all had evolved in Akovar after a magical disaster thousands of years ago. The players eventually journeyed to the Varkonan lands and found the Mask of the Pharaohs, an ancient relic that contained the essence of the entity Asti, and subsequently reignited the lay-lines that had been created thousands of years ago by Asti, an entity of light and healing. Asti was free, the ley-lines pulsed across Akovar, and again the ripples of magic and power spread throughout the multiverse, again causing long-sleeping entities as well as other powerful creatures such as dragons and liches to stir from slumber.

### 819 AW - Wrath of the Ancients

In 819 the results of these large-scale disturbances were seen in the "Wrath of the Ancients" campaign as the long-sleeping dragon Noctuminalis awoke from a long respite, taking control of the Zebulban cult known as the Umbral Hand. Working from the shadows, the dragon created magical weapons tied to the elements and using guile and illusion motivated the long-forgotten elemental cults of the area to come back to life, be filled with fervor for their gods, and begin a reign of destruction that would be known as the Elemental Crisis. New sets of heroes stepped forward to help end the earthquakes, tidal waves, and other natural disasters the cults were causing. In the end, the crisis was averted and the old cults shrank back into the shadows to worship their old elemental deities in more quiet and sinister ways, with portals to the elemental planes being shut by the heroes but without the revelation of the true mastermind behind the identity of the Elemental Eye, Noctuminalis. During this time and for the next decade, the tensions between rival countries in the Inner Kingdoms began to rise as Saerrus again was showing its independent streak, and Pragos began to exert pressure in various secretive ways in the Praetorian Senate of the Federation of Man. Small wars began to also break out among the Merchant Nations, although none rose to the level of large swaths of territory switching ownership or widespread death.

### 824 AW - Lords of Madness

Again in 824, the entity to be known as Ry'le, awakened by the planar disturbances, found his place in the Far Realm and began his plans. In the "Lords of Madness" campaign, a new set of heroes had to stop a group of shapeshifting aberrations from causing the dissolution of Beneshire. It was during the moments in which the heroes had helped the Astral Circle restart a small ley-line that the presence of the Ry'lehe was revealed, after which the heroes helped avert a civil war between two cities in Beneshire, eventually rooting out enough of the shapeshifters to cause them to escape and flee, the remnants of their portal to the Far Realm closed and an entire village saved from the thralls of the Ry'lehe.

### 832 AW - Secrets of the Shadows & Honor and Steel

In 832, two separate events occurred in other parts of the world, both in part due to prior campaign events.

**Secrets of the Shadow Realm**
In the "Secrets of the Shadow Realm" campaign, an ancient artifact was unearthed due to earthquakes that occurred in the Elemental Crisis. This evil artifact, known as the Devil Cube, merged a small portion of the Lands of Chaos with the Shadow Plane, and only due to the actions of brave heroes was the artifact and its master Davos destroyed and the lands returned to normal, a stunning defeat for the Taovinians at the hands of the heroes.

**Honor and Steel**
Meanwhile, in the far off Ejiin lands in the "Honor and Steel" campaign, the shaman Soni Sabu had some twenty years prior been witness to the entity Khan's avatar helping defend the Ejiin city of Kashi during the Orceri Incursion, and subsequently was banished from his clan for suggesting the Oni might be worth manipulating to do their bidding, only to prepare for twenty years and then cause an army of plague zombies to pour over a part of the island. New heroes helped not only save a city but prevent the zombie horde from reaching its destination and eventually defeated Soni Sabu and sent him deep into the underworld.

### 835 AW - Cosmic Journeys

In the year 835, the "Cosmic Journeys" campaign begins with heroes from two previous campaigns joining forces with the Astral Circle and helping research and protect the fledgling ley-line mortal spellcasters had dared to create. High-level planar exploration became the focus as four antagonist groups (the Umbral Hand, the Dark Whispers, the Nergalu Dragons, and the Watchers) competed for control or study of the ley-line, leading to adventures across multiple planes of existence including the Astral Plane, the Abyss, and the City of Fortune.

### 836 AW - Children of the Fey

Just one year later in 836, in the "Children of the Fey" campaign, a green dragon named Xunog and his fey ally Azolova attempted to transform Morning Valley into part of the Feywild by performing rituals at three ancient druid menhirs during successive full moons. Heroes had to race against time to disrupt these summoning rituals before reality itself was permanently altered. The campaign featured conflicts with kobolds, lizardmen, and a corrupted hermit druid, culminating in a confrontation with both Xunog and Azolova themselves.

### 837 AW - Saving Phandelver

In 837, the "Saving Phandelver" campaign brought heroes to the mining settlement of Phandelver, where they dealt with threats from goblinoid forces, explored lost dwarven holds, and uncovered ancient magical secrets buried beneath the earth. The campaign featured classic dungeon exploration combined with rescue missions and the discovery of powerful artifacts.

### 838 AW - Fire & Ice

In 838, the "Fire & Ice" campaign combined megadungeon exploration with political intrigue. As ley-lines continued to spread and stabilize across Mazariim, their energies reached the Gnomish Lands where the ghost of mad necromancer Salvatore Togglesprocket was revitalized in the Abomination Vaults (Gauntlight Ruins), while simultaneously political tensions between Clan Azure and the Children of Pyre threatened civil war in the gnomish city of Chumbawamba. Heroes had to delve into progressively dangerous vault levels while preventing the gnomish capital from tearing itself apart, eventually discovering the connections between the vault's resurgence and the political crisis.

### 839 AW - Gods of the Fall

In 839, the "Gods of the Fall" campaign began as five proto-entities from interstellar space crashed into the Varkonan lands like meteors. The entity Khemset sought to collect the five pieces of the ancient "Machine of Gyru" to drain mana from other beings and ascend to true godhood. This became a global race as heroes traveled to the Frozen Isles of Nond, the Mespoian Peninsula, the Merchant Nations, the Dwarven Nations, and beyond to collect the machine parts before Khemset could. The other four entities—Zonth, Menot, Nakar, and Anumata—each pursued their own agendas, sometimes helping and sometimes hindering the heroes.

### 842 AW - Coming of the Lines (Final Campaign)

By 842, the cumulative effects of decades of planar disturbances reached a critical mass. The ultimate campaign begins where the proliferation of unstable ley-lines caused global chaos. Behind all the devastation stood Noctuminalis, the shadow dragon who had been manipulating events since the Elemental Crisis. His endgame: transform Mazariim into a land of the dead where he would reign as an undead god-king. He orchestrated five simultaneous catastrophic plans: blot out the sun, crash the moons, expand the Shadow Plane, banish the aspects of Minerva, and open a massive Far Realm portal. Multiple parties of high-level characters had to complete five critical missions simultaneously to save the planet, culminating in a final confrontation with Noctuminalis himself. The outcome of this campaign would determine the fate of all Mazariim.

## Campaign Structure

The Mazariim campaigns follow a connected narrative arc that spans multiple decades, with each campaign building upon the consequences of previous adventures. Player characters sometimes carry over between campaigns, creating a living, evolving world history. The campaigns generally follow these themes:

- **Planar Consequences**: Actions in one campaign create ripples throughout the multiverse
- **Ancient Powers Awakening**: Long-dormant entities and civilizations respond to disruptions
- **Ley-Line Magic**: A recurring element connecting multiple campaign arcs
- **Racial Unity**: Repeated need for different races to cooperate against major threats
- **Alternate Realities**: Exploration of parallel worlds and their impact on Mazariim
- **Escalating Threats**: Each campaign raises the stakes from local to regional to global to planar to cosmic
- **Noctuminalis's Long Game**: The shadow dragon's manipulations span decades and multiple campaigns
- **New Entities Arising**: Proto-gods, Far Realm aberrations, and cosmic beings enter the world stage
