# Elven Ruins & Fallen Settlements of Mazariim
*Catalog of Destroyed, Abandoned, and Fallen Elven Sites (100,000 BW - 1 AW)*

This document catalogs ruins from both Pre-Enlightenment (100,000 BW - 3,402 BW) and Post-Enlightenment (3,402 BW - 1 AW) eras. Each entry includes location, history, current condition, dangers, treasures, and campaign hooks.

---

## PART I: PRE-ENLIGHTENMENT RUINS (100,000 BW - 3,402 BW)

### MAJOR FALLEN NATIONS & CONFEDERATIONS

#### **The Starwood Confederation (11,200 - 9,327 BW)**
- **Duration**: 1,873 years
- **Territory**: ~50,000 square miles of ancient forest
- **Peak Population**: ~8,000 elves
- **Government**: Rotating capital among seven clan seats
- **Cause of Fall**: Political fragmentation after Thornheart Compromise; three expansionist clans departed

**Potential Ruins:**

**1. The Seven Seats**
Seven ancient clan capitals scattered throughout Tawarwaith forests.
- **Features**: Council halls built into living trees, druidic sacred groves, residential districts, earthwork fortifications
- **Condition**: Overgrown but structurally sound; living tree architecture still viable
- **Dangers**: Territorial fey creatures, territorial druids protecting "sacred history," awakened treants mistaking visitors for loggers
- **Treasures**: Ancient druidic artifacts, Proto-Elven texts carved in wood, sacred groves with residual fey magic
- **Difficulty**: Easy to Moderate (depending on fey mood)
- **Campaign Hooks**: 
  - Druid seeks ancient ritual preserved in one seat
  - Fey court demands party retrieve stolen artifact from ruin
  - Historian needs texts from all seven seats to solve historical mystery

**2. Thornheart Amphitheater**
Site where Thornheart Compromise (9,327 BW) was signed, ending Starwood Wars.
- **Location**: Central Tawarwaith, neutral ground
- **Features**: Natural amphitheater with stone benches, central standing stone where treaty was signed
- **Condition**: Still maintained as sacred historical site by druids
- **Secrets**: Hidden chambers beneath amphitheater house original treaty documents in mithril cases; maps showing lost Starwood territories
- **Difficulty**: Easy (unless druids are hostile)
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Legal dispute over forest territory requires examining original treaty
  - Standing stone begins glowing/resonating—ancient magic awakening?
  - Hidden chamber discovered contains prophecy about future crisis

**3. Expansionist Border Camps**
Three fortified villages established by departing clans.
- **Population**: 300-800 each
- **Abandoned**: ~9,300 BW when clans fully integrated into Farondrim wanderer culture
- **Condition**: Heavily overgrown; wooden structures collapsed; stone foundations remain
- **Treasures**: Military equipment (bronze weapons, leather armor), boundary markers showing territorial claims, letters and journals explaining exodus
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Descendants of departing clans seek to reclaim ancestral lands
  - Boundary markers used to settle modern territorial dispute
  - Journal reveals location of hidden weapons cache

### DESTROYED SETTLEMENTS - RELIGIOUS CONFLICT

#### **Brotherhood of the Black Flame Fortress (10,200 BW)**
Death cult stronghold destroyed during holy war against Zebulba worshippers.

- **Location**: Northern mountains, near modern Aeglirech territory
- **Population**: ~200 necromancers plus countless undead
- **Duration**: ~150 years of operation
- **Cause of Destruction**: Holy war by coalition of Vuleala and Gruhathyrir followers
- **Current Name**: "The Obsidian Tower" or "Deathspire"

**The Ruins:**
- **Structure**: Seven-story tower built from black volcanic glass; underground catacombs extend five levels below
- **Condition**: Heavily warded by holy magic; entrance sealed with divine barriers
- **Dangers**: Lingering undead trapped in sealed sections, necromantic traps still active, taint of death magic causing madness, residual Zebulba influence
- **Treasures**: Forbidden necromantic texts (dangerous knowledge), phylacteries (some possibly still occupied), death-magic artifacts, cursed ritual implements
- **Secrets**: Rumored vault on lowest level contains original archlich still dormant, waiting for seal to weaken
- **Difficulty**: Hard to Legendary (depending on how deep party ventures)
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Seal weakening; Minervan church needs adventurers to reinforce or permanently destroy site
  - Scholar seeks specific necromantic text to counter modern death cult
  - Accidental breach releases ancient undead; party must re-seal or destroy them

**The Bone Fields**
- **Location**: Surrounding battlefield where final assault occurred
- **Features**: Barren wasteland where thousands died; nothing grows even 10,000+ years later
- **Condition**: Death-magic hotspot; animals avoid area
- **Dangers**: Spontaneous undead manifestations on certain nights, death magic warps living tissue, whispers drive travelers mad
- **Treasures**: Ancient weapons blessed by Old Gods (still potent), anti-necromancy armor, holy symbols from fallen crusaders
- **Difficulty**: Moderate to Hard
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Specific anniversary causes massive undead uprising
  - Blessed weapon needed to fight modern threat; only source is Bone Fields
  - Death cult attempts to harvest battlefield's residual death energy

### THE DROWNING CITIES (4,800 BW)

Four major coastal towns destroyed when catastrophic flooding struck western and southern shores. Religious fanatics blamed Iquinias (Sea/Storm entity); druids argued natural disaster. The tragedy sparked theological crisis and religious fanaticism.

**Total Deaths**: 3,000-4,500 elves  
**Cause**: Disputed—either divine punishment (religious view) or natural tsunami (druidic view)  
**Ongoing Mystery**: Evidence suggests both natural and supernatural elements

#### **1. Tirithorn (Watch of the Tides)**
Major coastal city renowned for tide prediction and weather forecasting.

- **Pre-Disaster Population**: ~1,200
- **Location**: Western coast, southern region
- **Submersion**: 20-40 feet underwater depending on tides
- **Condition**: Partially preserved; silt and marine growth cover structures
- **Exploration Difficulty**: Moderate (requires water-breathing or diving gear)
- **Dangers**: Aggressive sea creatures claiming territory, collapsing structures underwater, strong currents, underwater wraiths (drowned residents)
- **Treasures**: 
  - Tide prediction mechanisms (complex brass/bronze instruments still functional)
  - Waterlogged libraries (magical preservation keeps some texts readable)
  - Harbor master's tower contains navigation tools and ancient maps
  - Shrines to Iquinias with active magic (dangerous to disturb)
- **Secrets**:
  - Sealed vault contains "forbidden tide charts" predicting disaster
  - Evidence druids warned city leaders, who ignored warnings
  - Underwater ghost population still carries out daily routines
- **Difficulty**: Moderate to Hard
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Modern coastal city faces similar tidal patterns; needs Tirithorn's records
  - Iquinias cultists seek to recover artifacts proving deity's power
  - Underwater wraiths begin attacking coastal settlements; source traced to Tirithorn

#### **2. Maeglin's Rest (Place of Sharp Waters)**
Whaling town and fishing port.

- **Pre-Disaster Population**: ~800
- **Location**: Western coast
- **Condition**: Foundations visible above water; most structures buried under sand
- **Exploration Difficulty**: Easy (partially above water at low tide)
- **Dangers**: Unstable footing, sudden tide surges, territorial sea birds nesting in ruins, occasional sea creature
- **Treasures**:
  - Whaling gear (harpoons, ropes, boats—some still usable)
  - Trade records showing economic networks
  - Shrine to Vuleala (Mother of Rain) intact and possibly still blessed
- **Secrets**:
  - Mass grave of 600+ elves on higher ground—they tried to flee but were caught
  - Evidence of sacrificial rituals to Iquinias performed by desperate residents
  - Ship captain's log describes "great darkness on horizon" hours before disaster
- **Difficulty**: Easy to Moderate
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Vuleala's shrine begins producing fresh water; clergy investigates
  - Mass grave disturbed by storm; spirits demand proper burial rites
  - Ship log's "darkness" matches description of modern sea threat

#### **3. Silverlake Landing (Fallen Harbor)**
Merchant port connecting inland trade routes to sea.

- **Pre-Disaster Population**: ~1,000
- **Location**: River delta region
- **Condition**: Completely submerged; 30-50 feet deep; massive silt coverage
- **Exploration Difficulty**: Hard (deep water, poor visibility, structural collapse)
- **Dangers**: 
  - Giant octopi/squid nesting in warehouses
  - Sentient sea serpent claiming ruins as lair
  - Traps set by drowned merchant ghosts protecting "their" cargo
  - Collapsing buildings (underwater cave-ins)
- **Treasures**:
  - Merchant warehouses contain preserved trade goods (gems, mithril ingots, art)
  - Customs house has records of pre-Enlightenment trade networks
  - Shipwrights' guild hall contains naval construction techniques
  - Bank vaults (magically sealed) hold massive wealth
- **Secrets**:
  - One warehouse contains "contraband"—death cult artifacts being smuggled
  - Bank vault contains evidence of corruption: city leadership knew of danger, took bribes to stay silent
  - Secret tunnel connects city to inland location (still partially accessible)
- **Difficulty**: Hard to Legendary
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Modern merchant guild needs proof of ancient trade agreements
  - Sea serpent's depredations force investigation of its lair
  - Death cult artifacts activate, causing undead sea creatures to emerge

#### **4. Falathrim (Song of Waves)**
Artistic community and resort town for wealthy elves.

- **Pre-Disaster Population**: ~900
- **Location**: Southern coast, scenic bay
- **Condition**: Submerged 15-25 feet; relatively intact due to quality construction
- **Exploration Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Dangers**:
  - Sahuagin tribe claiming ruins
  - Vengeful bardic spirits (drowned performers) whose songs cause madness
  - Rip currents and underwater sinkholes
  - Magically preserved areas with air pockets—but air is centuries old and toxic
- **Treasures**:
  - Art galleries with magically preserved paintings and sculptures
  - Musical instruments (magical ones still functional)
  - Luxury goods and jewelry
  - Great Library of Music (sheet music, theoretical texts, rare instruments)
- **Secrets**:
  - Performance hall has perfect acoustics; underwater concerts still occur (performed by ghosts)
  - One villa contains diary of survivor who escaped, describing final hours
  - Hidden cache where wealthiest residents tried to preserve treasures—never retrieved
- **Difficulty**: Moderate to Hard
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Bard seeks legendary instrument from Great Library
  - Ghostly underwater concert attracts audience—including party member
  - Sahuagin tribe willing to trade; they want "singing stones" removed (causing madness)

### DESTROYED SETTLEMENTS - PLAGUE AND DISEASE

#### **The Bloodtear Villages (9,100 BW)**
Twenty-seven coastal and riverside villages destroyed during Bloodtear Plague outbreak.

- **Total Deaths**: ~40% of coastal population (~8,000-10,000 elves)
- **Duration**: Seven years of pandemic
- **Response**: Villages quarantined and burned to prevent spread
- **Cause**: Disputed—either natural disease or Iquinias's curse (religious interpretation)

**The Quarantine Ruins:**
- **Locations**: Scattered along western/southern coasts and major river deltas
- **Features**: Burned structures, mass graves, quarantine boundary stones
- **Condition**: Most locations are just blackened foundations and overgrown cemeteries
- **Dangers**: Lingering disease spirits, cursed ground causing illness, survivors' ghosts, ecological dead zones
- **Treasures**: Minimal—most valuables burned with bodies. Occasionally:
  - Medical texts describing plague symptoms (valuable to healers/scholars)
  - Quarantine records showing outbreak progression
  - Religious artifacts used in failed healing rituals
- **Secrets**: Several villages were burned BEFORE everyone died—mercy killing or panic?
- **Difficulty**: Easy to Moderate (physical), High (ethical/spiritual)
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Modern plague outbreak matches Bloodtear symptoms; need historical records
  - Ghosts demand justice—were they murdered or mercy-killed?
  - One village wasn't fully burned; survivors' descendants seek family records

### DESTROYED SETTLEMENTS - ECONOMIC DECLINE

#### **Tauron (The Bull Kingdom) (4,600-4,100 BW)**
Peninsula kingdom that refused integration with emerging Gaearman nation.

- **Peak Population**: ~2,000
- **Duration**: ~500 years as independent kingdom
- **Cause of Fall**: Economic isolation; refused to join Gaearman confederation; trade routes bypassed them; gradual depopulation
- **Final Fate**: Last residents evacuated peacefully ~4,100 BW

**The Ruins:**
- **Location**: Southern peninsula
- **Features**: Well-preserved stone structures (palace, temples, town square, docks)
- **Condition**: Excellent—abandoned peacefully, minimal looting, nature slowly reclaiming
- **Dangers**: Minimal—some territorial animals, occasional smugglers using ruins as hideout
- **Treasures**:
  - Palace library (intact—books on peninsula culture and history)
  - Royal treasury (empty but vault mechanisms interesting)
  - Unique architectural styles and artwork
  - Fishing/sailing innovations developed by isolated community
- **Secrets**:
  - Palace contains negotiation records showing Gaearman offered generous terms—king refused out of pride
  - Hidden diary of last king expresses regret
  - Secret chamber contains "crown jewels" never recovered
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Descendants of royal line seek to recover crown jewels and restore honor
  - Architectural scholars need to document unique building techniques before ruins collapse
  - Smugglers hiding in ruins stumble upon something dangerous

#### **Deepglimmer Shaft (5,800-5,200 BW)**
Major mithril mine that produced exceptional ore.

- **Location**: Northern mountains, Aeglirech territory
- **Depth**: 2,000+ feet, twelve levels
- **Peak Employment**: ~600 elves
- **Cause of Fall**: Vein exhausted; ore quality declined; miners moved to newer, richer sites
- **Abandoned**: ~5,200 BW

**The Ruins:**
- **Condition**: Partially collapsed; upper six levels accessible with care; lower six flooded or caved in
- **Dangers**:
  - Structural instability (cave-ins)
  - Flooded levels contain blind cave creatures
  - Toxic gases in sealed sections
  - Abandoned mining equipment (rusted, dangerous)
  - Possible creatures moved into deep levels after abandonment
- **Treasures**:
  - Small amounts of overlooked mithril ore
  - Mining equipment and techniques from earlier era
  - Miners' quarters contain personal belongings and journals
  - Engineering documents showing mine layout (valuable to modern miners)
- **Secrets**:
  - Lowest accessible level shows vein didn't fully exhaust—stopped for other reason
  - Evidence of something encountered in deep levels
  - Sealed section contains bodies of miners who never made it out
- **Difficulty**: Moderate to Hard
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Modern mining company wants to reopen mine; needs survey of stability
  - Strange noises from deep levels—something alive down there?
  - Miner's ghost appears in local village, demanding party retrieve his body

#### **The Silent Villages (5,600 BW)**
Seven villages (population ~200 each, total 1,400 elves) vanished overnight.

- **Location**: Interior forests, Tawarwaith region
- **Event Date**: Single night in spring of 5,600 BW
- **Mystery**: Entire populations disappeared—no bodies, no signs of struggle, no evidence of where they went
- **Investigation**: Druids, clergy, and scholars investigated for decades; no explanation found

**The Ruins:**
- **Condition**: Pristine—exactly as residents left them; meals on tables, tools in workshops, children's toys in streets
- **Supernatural Element**: Strong; time seems to move differently; visitors report "wrongness"
- **Dangers**:
  - Temporal anomalies (time speeds up/slows down unpredictably)
  - Visitors experience visions of villagers going about normal lives
  - Strong compulsion to "join the village" (mental save required)
  - Some visitors vanish—same way original residents did
- **Treasures**: Perfectly preserved artifacts from 5,600 BW (valuable to scholars); personal belongings; everyday items
- **Secrets**:
  - Did they transcend to another plane? Taken by fey? Mass temporal displacement?
  - Fey magic signatures detected but no clear explanation
  - One village's temple shows evidence of ritual interrupted mid-ceremony
- **Difficulty**: Moderate (physical), Legendary (solving mystery)
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Party member has vision leading to Silent Villages
  - Temporal anomaly destabilizes—risk of modern village suffering same fate
  - Descendants seek closure; hire party to investigate
  - Surviving villagers suddenly return after 5,600 years, unchanged—where were they?

#### **Clan Seats of Departing Starwood Factions (9,327 BW)**

**1. Galadhon Hold**
**2. Silverroot Hall**  
**3. Ravencrest Summit**

- **Abandoned**: 9,327 BW when three expansionist clans left Starwood Confederation
- **Fate**: Clans eventually integrated into Farondrim wanderer culture
- **Locations**: Border regions of Starwood territory
- **Population**: 400-600 each
- **Features**: Fortified clan halls, residential areas, storage facilities
- **Condition**: Overgrown; wooden structures collapsed; stone foundations remain
- **Dangers**: Territorial wildlife, occasional bandits, unstable structures
- **Treasures**:
  - Clan records explaining departure decision
  - Military equipment left behind
  - Personal belongings (some valuable)
  - Maps showing intended migration routes
- **Secrets**:
  - Did clans leave peacefully or were they forced out?
  - Hidden caches suggest they intended to return
  - One hall contains evidence of internal conflict about leaving
- **Difficulty**: Easy to Moderate
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Modern Farondrim clans seek ancestral records
  - Territorial dispute requires examining old boundary markers
  - Hidden cache contains dangerous artifact

## PART II: POST-ENLIGHTENMENT RUINS (3,402 BW - 1 AW)

### THE SHADOW WAR RUINS (1,982 - 1,912 BW)

#### **The Hollow Brotherhood Stronghold (1,982-1,912 BW)**
Secret necromantic fortress operated by death cult for 70 years.

- **Location**: Remote wilderness (deliberately hidden; location varies by campaign need)
- **Built**: ~1,982 BW in abandoned Pre-Enlightenment ruins
- **Population**: ~300 necromancers, hundreds of undead servants
- **Leader**: Archlich Morthain (corrupted Minervan cleric)
- **Destroyed**: 1,912 BW during Purge of Shadows; final assault destroyed phylactery and stronghold

**The Ruins:**
- **Original Structure**: Converted Pre-Enlightenment fortress (Brotherhood of Black Flame site or similar)
- **Modifications**: Necromantic laboratories, undead barracks, libraries of death magic, torture chambers
- **Condition**: 
  - Upper levels: Heavily damaged by final assault; collapsed sections
  - Lower levels: Sealed by Minervan clergy; divine wards still active
  - Deepest vaults: Unexplored—too dangerous to breach
- **Magical Wards**: Powerful holy magic sealing entrances; designed to contain, not prevent entry (warning to intruders)
- **Dangers**:
  - Lingering undead trapped in sealed sections (including powerful specimens)
  - Necromantic traps still active after 1,900+ years
  - Taint of death magic causes corruption, madness, disease
  - Cursed artifacts that possess wielders
  - Dormant necromantic experiments that activate when disturbed
  - Area itself is spiritually corrupted—prolonged exposure causes alignment shift
- **Treasures**:
  - **Libraries**: Forbidden necromantic texts (extraordinarily dangerous knowledge)
  - **Artifacts**: Death-magic items, cursed weapons, phylactery fragments
  - **Equipment**: Ritualistic implements, experimental apparatus
  - **Records**: Cult membership lists (historically important), experimental notes (horrifying)
  - **Morthain's Personal Collection**: Stolen Minervan holy texts, corrupted divine magic research
- **Secrets**:
  - **Deepest Vault**: Rumors claim Morthain's phylactery wasn't fully destroyed; fragment remains
  - **Membership Scrolls**: Show cult recruited from all social classes, including nobility and clergy
  - **True Purpose**: Was Morthain seeking immortality, or something worse? Notes mention "transcendence"
  - **Hidden Survivors**: Did all cultists die? Some may have escaped and gone into hiding
- **Difficulty**: Hard to Legendary
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - **Seal Weakening**: Holy wards deteriorating after 1,900 years; Minervan clergy needs adventurers to reinforce or permanently destroy site
  - **Knowledge Seekers**: Scholar (or villain) seeks specific necromantic text to counter modern death cult
  - **Accidental Breach**: Earthquake/natural disaster breaches seals; undead escape into surrounding territory
  - **Historical Investigation**: Church historian needs to understand how respected cleric fell to corruption (to prevent recurrence)
  - **Cult Resurgence**: Modern death cult seeks Morthain's notes to recreate his work
  - **Redemption Quest**: Descendant of cultist seeks to destroy family's shameful legacy

#### **Purge Battlefields & Fortified Compounds (1,912 BW)**
Multiple sites across Vand'ardhon where cultists made final stands.

- **Number**: ~12-15 major sites
- **Casualties**: ~2,000 elven soldiers and crusaders; ~300 cultists
- **Features**: Burned compounds, battlefield graves, memorial stones
- **Condition**: Most sites memorialized as holy ground; some abandoned and overgrown
- **Dangers**: Residual death magic, vengeful spirits (both cultist and crusader), cursed ground
- **Treasures**:
  - Blessed weapons used in Purge (still holy, effective against undead)
  - Armor and equipment from fallen crusaders
  - Cultist caches hidden before final battles
  - Historical records of battles
- **Secrets**:
  - Were all cultists truly evil, or were some coerced/deceived?
  - Some sites show evidence of massacres—captured cultists executed without trial
  - Hidden caches suggest cultists expected to lose and planned for future resurgence
- **Difficulty**: Easy to Moderate (physical), Moderate to Hard (spiritual)
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Ghost of fallen crusader cannot rest until specific cultist weapon is destroyed
  - Memorial site desecrated; investigation reveals modern death cult responsible
  - Hidden cache discovered contains map to Morthain's secondary lab

#### **The Pale Watch Fortress (founded 1,912 BW)**
Headquarters of elite anti-necromancy order established after Purge.

- **Status**: ACTIVE—still operational in modern era
- **Location**: Strategic position for monitoring known necromantic sites
- **Purpose**: Eternal vigilance against death magic
- **Features**: Fortress, training grounds, library of counter-necromancy texts, armory with blessed weapons
- **Not technically a "ruin"**, but included for campaign reference:
  - Source of quests (Pale Watch hires adventurers to investigate necromantic activity)
  - Potential allies (provide blessed weapons, training, information)
  - Potential antagonists (zealous inquisitors who see dark magic everywhere)
  - Resource hub (anti-undead equipment, healing, sanctuary)

### WAR TO END ALL WARS RUINS (3-1 BW)

#### **Naval Disaster Site (2 BW)**
Location where three-quarters of Gaearman's fleet was destroyed.

- **Location**: Deep ocean between Vand'ardhon and Nigal
- **Event**: Zuel's daemon allies ambushed elven fleet during return voyage for reinforcements
- **Casualties**: Thousands of sailors; ~75% of elven navy
- **Wreckage**: Dozens of warships sunk

**The Underwater Battlefield:**
- **Depth**: Varies (200-1,000+ feet depending on exact location)
- **Exploration Difficulty**: Hard to Legendary (requires water breathing, pressure resistance, navigation in deep water)
- **Dangers**:
  - Daemonic corruption still lingers—area is spiritually tainted
  - Sea creatures warped by daemon magic (aggressive, unnatural)
  - Trapped spirits of drowned sailors (some hostile, some seeking aid)
  - Daemonic entities still bound to wrecks
  - Underwater terrain difficult to navigate
- **Treasures**:
  - Elven naval equipment (magical weapons, armor, navigation tools)
  - Ship cargo (supplies, treasure being transported)
  - Personal belongings of sailors (some magical)
  - Naval warfare techniques documented in captain's logs
  - Daemon corpses/remains (valuable components for magic users)
- **Secrets**:
  - How did daemons know fleet's location? Traitor or divination?
  - Some ships show evidence of fighting each other—daemon possession?
  - One ship's cargo was extraordinarily valuable—what was being transported?
  - Captain's logs describe "portal opening in ocean"—daemon summoning site nearby?
- **Difficulty**: Hard to Legendary
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Navy wants to salvage specific ship (carrying important cargo/personnel)
  - Daemonic activity increasing—source traced to wreckage site
  - Sailor's spirit appears to descendant, requesting body be retrieved for burial
  - Naval historian seeks captain's logs to document disaster
  - Daemon cult attempting to reopen summoning portal

#### **The Stranded Camps (1 BW - 1 AW)**
Temporary fortifications built by elven expeditionary forces stranded on Nigal.

- **Location**: Western Nigal coast
- **Population**: ~12,000 elven soldiers (survivors of original 30,000-strong force)
- **Duration**: Built during final year of war; maintained until return voyage became possible
- **Abandoned**: Gradually evacuated 1-5 AW as ships became available

**The Ruins:**
- **Features**: Fortified camps, field hospitals, supply depots, shrines to Minerva
- **Condition**: Mostly dismantled when evacuated, but some structures remain
- **Location Significance**: First sustained elven presence on Nigal
- **Dangers**: Minimal (long abandoned); possible orc/goblin scavengers
- **Treasures**:
  - Military equipment left behind (too damaged/heavy to transport)
  - Personal belongings of soldiers
  - Field journals and letters describing war experience
  - Maps showing war's final year
- **Secrets**:
  - Some elves chose to stay on Nigal rather than return home—why?
  - Evidence of relationships between elven soldiers and local humans
  - Hidden caches suggest plans for permanent settlement (never realized)
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Descendant of stranded soldier seeks family records
  - Modern conflict in area disturbs elven graves—spirits restless
  - Hidden cache contains lost military intelligence from war
  - Half-elf character discovers elven ancestor from stranded force

#### **Fort Hope Memorial (1 BW)**
Site of final battle where Zuel's avatar was destroyed.

- **Location**: Western Nigal
- **Status**: ACTIVE—Sacred memorial site maintained by multi-racial coalition
- **Features**: Monument, mass graves, preserved battlefield, memorial walls listing fallen
- **Not technically a ruin**, but included for campaign reference:
  - Pilgrimage destination for elves, dwarves, humans
  - Site of annual memorial ceremonies
  - Source of inspiration (heroes visit before great challenges)
  - Potential divine encounters (Minerva's presence strong here)
