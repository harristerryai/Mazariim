# Geography of the Merchant Nations: The Sabon Peninsula

**As of 842 AW**

---

## INTRODUCTION

To the south of the Inner Kingdoms lies the Merchant Nations, a large number of human city-states and nations that over the time since the War have fallen away from the Federation of Man, both due to the distance from the Inner Kingdoms and the many revolutions and changes this region has faced. There is no unified government or republic here, with control dictated more by economics than armies. The Merchant Nations are also more dangerous than the Inner Kingdoms, with monsters more common and traders needing to protect their goods themselves, at times a costly venture.

The humans originating from this part of Nigal are known as Sabonaar, named after an ancient empire from pre-history. They speak a regional dialect called Sabo, a practical language that is spelled phonetically. Common is widely spoken due to trade with the Federation and other regions.

The Merchant Nations have the equivalent of 15th and 16th-century technologies, a step beyond the Middle Ages of our world but behind the Inner Kingdoms' 17th-18th century innovations. Analogs for all of Europe, the Mediterranean, and other unique locations can be found here.

The region is defined by the Sabon Peninsula, a large landmass jutting southwest from the main continent of Nigal, bordered by the Bay of Urgot to the west, the Crescent Sea to the north, and open ocean to the south. The interior is dominated by the Jinook Valley, a broad river valley that once served as the heartland of multiple empires and now hosts the region's major city-states. The Kirak Mountains rise to the east, home to dwarven citadels and the border with the Lands of Chaos. To the south and west, the Taskaun Peninsula extends toward Tentigo and the theocratic lands.

Unlike the Inner Kingdoms, no single political entity controls the Merchant Nations. Power is fragmented among independent city-states, remnant kingdoms, and merchant houses whose influence rises and falls with the tides of commerce. Alliances shift constantly, wars erupt over trade routes and harbors, and the strong survive while the weak are absorbed. This instability creates both danger and opportunity — fortunes are made and lost in a single season, and a clever individual with enough gold and nerve can rise from nothing to rule a city.

**Current Political Organization (842 AW)**

The Merchant Nations consist of independent and often rival city-states and nations with no overarching government:

- **Free City of Amara** — Mercantile Feudalism. The largest port city and dominant trade hub, ruled by Baron Bastien.
- **Instar** — Magocracy. The "City of Mages," ruled by the Thaumaturgic Council, a center of arcane learning.
- **Naarlam** — Monarchy. The remnant of ancient Sabonaa, led by Queen Konovara, seeking to restore past glory.
- **Cecastaa** — Independent City-State. Rebellious horsebreeders who broke from Naarlam.
- **Tentigo** — Warmongering Theocracy. A militant Tiovinian nation that has launched three crusades.
- **Asim** — Autocracy. A hostile slave-trading city ruled by a Simir descendant.
- **Moamon** — Autocracy. A dark, industrial city with growing Zebulba worship.

Numerous smaller city-states, trading posts, and independent settlements dot the peninsula, along with the ruins of the Dukhan Empire and the collapsed Empire of Simir.

**Total Population (842 AW, estimated)**

- Humans (Sabonaar): ~800,000 across all nations
- Dwarves: ~120,000 (concentrated in Amara and mountain settlements)
- Elves: ~15,000 (mostly in Instar and coastal settlements)
- Halflings: ~30,000
- Gnomes: ~10,000
- Other (half-elves, half-orcs, exotic): ~25,000
- **Total: ~1,000,000**

## FREE CITY OF AMARA

**"In Amara, gold is the only king."**
— Sabonaar merchant proverb

### Overview

**Alignment:** LN
**Government:** Mercantile Feudalism
**Capital:** Amara (pop. 21,000)
**Population:** ~21,000 (humans 52%, dwarves 31%, elves 11%, other 6%)
**Languages:** Common, Sabo, Dwarven
**Religions:** Hinterhin, Iquinias, Minerva, Mordrion, Polyvin, Rivoleen, Tiovin, Xannith
**Primary Exports:** Foodstuffs, refined and manufactured goods
**Real-World Analog:** Mediterranean port city, 15th-16th century

The Free City of Amara is the largest port city in the Merchant Nations and the dominant trade hub on the western coast of the Sabon Peninsula. Situated on the Bay of Urgot where the River Cho meets the sea, Amara controls the flow of goods between the interior Jinook Valley, the coastal trade routes, and the wider world. Three generations of the Bastien family have built Amara into a prosperous and well-defended city where commerce is king and the Merchant's Guild wields power rivaling the Baron himself.

Amara's position makes it the natural gateway for trade between the Inner Kingdoms to the north, the Lands of Chaos to the east, the Ancient South far to the southeast, and even the distant elven continent of Vand'ardhon. Ships from across Nigal dock in its harbor, and caravans from a dozen nations crowd its market squares. The city is cosmopolitan by Merchant Nations standards — its substantial dwarven population (31%) is one of the largest outside the Dwarven Empire, and elves, halflings, gnomes, and more exotic visitors are commonplace.

### History

Amara's past dates back to one of the many Sabonaar fishing villages that occupied the coastline along the Bay of Urgot. The Dukhan Empire slowly assimilated the coastal cities and ruled over them for over seven hundred years. Amara's history under the Empire was unremarkable save for the widening of the harbor and the dredging of the bay to allow more ships to access the port.

In 213 BW, the Dukhan Empire fell in a single day when a magical ritual by Soros un-Kahn went catastrophically wrong, killing almost all of the major cities' populations, destroying much of the area, and leaving the remaining population in disarray. Within a few years, most of the remaining Dukhan cities fell to rampaging orcs and goblins during the War to End All Wars. Amara was no exception — destroyed and depopulated.

After the War ended, dwarves from the mountains helped rebuild and settle in the Merchant Nations, and Amara was slowly repopulated and rebuilt over the next few centuries. In 291 AW, the city-state of Asim began its conquest of the Jinook Valley, and Amara found itself assimilated into the Empire of Simir. Amara's position as the largest port city in the Empire secured its importance.

In 686 AW, the Empire of Simir fell when King Tiberius Simir was assassinated in Asim, plunging the Empire into a civil war lasting decades. In 692 AW, Baron Feraldo Bastien seized the moment and declared the "Free City of Amara," building up the city's defenses and economy. Now, three generations later, his descendant Armand rules one of the biggest trade hubs in the west of Nigal.

In 733 AW, Moamon went to war with Amara. Both sides suffered heavy losses before an uneasy truce was brokered by the wizards of Instar and the Federation of Man. The truce holds but tensions remain, particularly as Zebulba worship grows in Moamon and the slave trade in Asim threatens regional stability.

### Government Structure

Amara is ruled by Baron Armand Bastien, a shrewd and intelligent merchant lord who inherited his position from his father. He shares power with the Merchant's Guild to a degree, and they often work together to keep Amara profitable and safe. The Merchant's Guild is a collection of representatives of all the major economic players in Amara, wielding enormous influence through collective wealth and hired soldiers.

A series of courts headed by judges — almost all loyal to the Bastien family — administer justice, and the Baron has the final say on all matters of law. Armand commands a contingent of one hundred city guards as well as over two hundred militia, and if the need arises can hire thousands of sellswords from nearby regions.

### Culture

Amara's culture is defined by commerce. Social status derives from wealth, and the ability to make profitable deals commands more respect than noble birth or martial prowess. The city is a melting pot — Sabonaar humans, Bryndawi dwarves, elves from Vand'ardhon, halflings, gnomes, and travelers from across Nigal rub shoulders in its markets and taverns. This diversity creates a cosmopolitan atmosphere rare in the Merchant Nations, where most cities are more insular.

The Sabonaar cultural emphasis on practical commerce, family loyalty, and personal honor shapes daily life. Merchants conduct business with elaborate courtesy but will cut an unfavorable partner loose without sentiment. Family businesses span generations, and the oldest merchant houses trace lineages back centuries. Entertainment runs from high culture (the Amara Theater of the Performing Arts hosts acclaimed productions) to low (the Dock District's rough taverns offer cheaper diversions).

### Religion

The religious landscape of Amara is diverse, reflecting its cosmopolitan population. Tiovin is the most popular deity, with the Blessed Halls of Eternal Light serving as the city's grandest temple. Iquinias draws worship from sailors and dock workers. Polyvin (fair trade) attracts honest merchants through the Fortune's Embrace temple and gambling den. Xannith (greed) has a strong following among the less scrupulous, with the Pleasuredome in the Dock District serving as both temple and festhall. Mordrion's Halls of Iron serve the substantial dwarven community. Rivoleen's Shrine to the Wanderer conceals a secret liberation network. Hinterhin's Hearthstone temple serves the halfling population. Minerva has a small but devoted following among the elven residents.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** Instar (uneasy cooperation, particularly the trade arrangement since 715 AW), the Federation of Man (brokered the Moamon truce, trade partner), Naarlam (trade partner), the Champions of Blowing Rock (nearby force for good).

**Enemies:** Asim and the Slavelords (hostile rival, seeks to rebuild the Empire of Simir), Moamon and the Zebulbans (hostile rival, growing undead threat), the Krutan Overlords (slave-trading magocracy threatening expansion), the Pirates of the Crescent Coast (maritime raiders), the Coven of the Vengeful Lady (vampire cult operating in the shadows).

**Threats:** The ongoing cold war with Asim and Moamon, pirate raids on merchant shipping, criminal activity (particularly the Thieves' Guild under Sergeant Knox), Zebulban cult influence spreading from Moamon, Krutan slave raids, and the lingering curse from the Dukhan Empire's fall that still taints parts of the Jinook Valley.

### Major City: Amara

**Amara** — Large City (Pop. 21,000)

The Free City of Amara sprawls along the banks of the River Cho and the coastline of the Bay of Urgot. The city is divided into three distinct districts, each with its own character, dangers, and opportunities.

#### Noble District

Despite the name, most residents of the Noble District are wealthy merchants rather than true nobility. This district contains the finest homes, parks, and establishments in Amara, as well as Castle Amara itself. Security is tight, with guards patrolling regularly.

**Key Locations:**
- **Castle Amara** — The seat of power for Baron Armand Bastien and his family. A well-fortified keep overlooking the bay, serving as both residence and administrative center.
- **Barracks** — Home to the city guard and militia, adjacent to Castle Amara.
- **City Gates** — The main entrance to the Noble District, heavily guarded at all hours.
- **Noble's Market** — An upscale market featuring exotic goods, fine crafts, and luxury items unavailable in the common markets.
- **Elegant Cockatrice** — A high-end restaurant and inn serving exotic cuisine, popular with visiting merchants and diplomats.
- **Halls of the Dark Dancer** — A festhall dedicated to Sheeva, rumored to be connected to the Coven of the Vengeful Lady. The authorities tolerate it because its patrons include powerful nobles, but dark whispers surround the establishment.
- **Zander's Horse and Transport** — A premium stable and carriage service catering to wealthy travelers.
- **Noble Villas** — The luxurious homes of Amara's wealthiest residents, surrounded by gardens and private walls.

#### Market District

A maze of sellers and buyers stretching across much of the city. The Market District contains most of Amara's shops, inns, taverns, and middle-class residences. Both legitimate business and underhanded dealings occur here, and the district is always buzzing with activity from dawn to well past dark.

**Key Locations:**
- **Grand Market** — The central marketplace, a sprawling area filled with vendors selling everything imaginable. The heart of Amara's commercial life.
- **West Bay Company General Store** — A large general goods store with a wide selection of everyday necessities and imported wares.
- **Red Raven Trading Company** — A major trading house dealing in exotic goods from across Nigal and beyond.
- **Vanilo Trading Company** — Another large trading concern, rival to the Red Raven.
- **Over the Mountain** — A shop specializing in goods from the dwarven lands — tools, weapons, ale, gems, and crafted metalwork.
- **Yella's Fine Cooking** — A popular restaurant known for its authentic Sabonaar cuisine.
- **The Wayward Warrior** — A weapons and armor shop catering to adventurers, mercenaries, and guards.
- **Breath of the Forest** — An herbal shop run by elves, selling potions, remedies, and natural medicines.
- **Underhill's Axes and Armor** — A dwarven smithy producing high-quality weapons and armor at premium prices.
- **Joboba's Specialties** — A curiosity shop with strange and unusual items from distant lands. The proprietor's sourcing methods are best not questioned.
- **Taqur's Homesteads** — A real estate broker dealing in properties across Amara and the surrounding region.
- **Amara Theater of the Performing Arts** — A grand theater hosting plays, musical performances, and cultural events. One of the finest in the Merchant Nations.
- **House of Respite and Rest** — A comfortable inn for travelers of moderate means, clean and well-run.
- **Blessed Halls of Eternal Light** — The grand temple of Tiovin, the largest religious institution in Amara. Provides charity, healing, and spiritual guidance.
- **Halls of Iron** — A temple dedicated to Mordrion, popular among Amara's substantial dwarven population. Features a working forge where priests craft devotional metalwork.
- **Shrine to the Wanderer** — Outwardly a temple to Rivoleen, this wide four-story tower adorned with gardens and fountains is secretly the headquarters of the Liberators, a group that helps slaves escape from Moamon, Asim, and Kruta.
- **Hearthstone** — A warm, welcoming temple to Hinterhin, serving the halfling community and anyone seeking comfort and hospitality.
- **Fortune's Embrace** — A temple and gambling den dedicated to Polyvin, god of fair trade and shared prosperity. Combines worship with games of chance, offering practical help to common folk.
- **Tower of the Magical One** — A wizard's tower and arcane shop selling magical components, scrolls, and minor enchanted items.
- **Eccentricities** — A curiosity shop specializing in magical components, rare reagents, and unusual arcane supplies.
- **Hirak's Fine Concoctions** — An alchemist's shop producing potions, elixirs, and alchemical preparations.

#### Dock District (Warehouse District)

Located along the banks of the River Cho and the coastline of the Bay of Urgot, the Dock District is filled with warehouses, loading areas, shipyards, and all manner of goods being moved in and out of the city. While generally safe during the day, this area becomes quite dangerous at night, with thieves, smugglers, and worse prowling the shadows.

**Key Locations:**
- **Ogre's Arse** — A rough tavern frequented by sailors and dock workers. Cheap drinks, cheaper morals, and reliable rumors for those who know how to listen.
- **Marn's Respite** — A cheaper inn for those on a budget, offering basic rooms and simple meals. Clean enough, if you don't look too closely.
- **Amaran Thieves' Guild** — The secret headquarters of the city's thieves, hidden behind false fronts. Run by a mysterious figure known only as Sergeant Knox, the guild controls most organized crime in the Dock District.
- **Bounty of the Sea** — A shipbuilder and chandlery providing vessel construction, repair, and outfitting services.
- **Pleasuredome** — A temple and festhall dedicated to Xannith, offering all manner of pleasures for a price. The establishment's opulence belies its location in the roughest district.
- **Ocean's Edge** — A temple to Iquinias built out over the water on wooden pilings. Sailors make offerings before dangerous voyages, and the priests bless ships and predict weather.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Baron Armand Bastien** (LN male Sabonaar Aristocrat 9) — Ruler of Amara. A shrewd and intelligent merchant lord, third generation of the Bastien dynasty. Balances the competing interests of noble houses, merchant guilds, and foreign powers with calculated pragmatism.
- **Pinto Pinocrhius** (LE male Bryndawi Dwarf Rogue 1/Aristocrat 8) — Leader of the Merchant's Guild. A stout and round dwarf with a great deal of social cunning. Controls enormous collective wealth and influence, serving as counterbalance to the Baron's authority.
- **Sergeant Knox** (alignment and race unknown) — Mysterious leader of the Amaran Thieves' Guild. Identity unknown to all but the most trusted inner circle. Controls organized crime throughout the Dock District and has informants across the city.

## INSTAR

**"Knowledge is the only power that matters."**
— Thaumaturgic Council maxim

### Overview

**Alignment:** LN
**Government:** Magocracy (Thaumaturgic Council)
**Capital:** Instar
**Population:** Major trade hub in Jinook Valley (predominantly human, with significant elven minority)
**Languages:** Common, Sabo, Elven, Draconic (scholarly)
**Religions:** Alagalothor, Minerva, Rivoleen
**Primary Exports:** Magical goods, arcane education, alchemical products, rare specimens
**Real-World Analog:** No close parallel; a magically governed city-state

Instar, known as the "City of Mages," is one of the largest cities in the Jinook Valley and serves as the preeminent center of magical learning and arcane power in the Merchant Nations. The city is ruled by the Thaumaturgic Council, a body of powerful spellcasters who maintain strict control over magical education and practice within the city's walls. The entire city is illuminated by permanent magical lights, and the skyline is dominated by wizard towers, arcane academies, and research laboratories.

Located in the Jinook Valley north of the spectacular Larue Falls, Instar serves as a major stopover for travelers between the coast and the interior regions. However, the city closed most of its ports for trade in 715 AW, deliberately redirecting commercial traffic to Amara while focusing its own economy on magical research, education, and the production of arcane goods. This arrangement benefits both cities — Amara gains trade volume while Instar gains freedom from the distractions of mundane commerce.

### History

Instar has a long history as a center of magical study dating back to the Dukhan Empire, which recognized the Jinook Valley's unusual concentration of ambient magical energy. After the catastrophic fall of the Dukhan Empire in 213 BW, Instar was devastated along with the rest of the region. The city rebuilt slowly over the centuries that followed, always maintaining its magical traditions even through the War to End All Wars and the subsequent period of reconstruction.

In 291 AW, Instar was absorbed into the expanding Empire of Simir along with Amara and Moamon. Under Simir rule, the city's mages chafed at secular authority but lacked the organization to resist effectively. When King Tiberius Simir was assassinated in 686 AW and the Empire collapsed into civil war, the mages saw their opportunity.

In 689 AW, Yelinda Featherstep — a powerful archmage who had come to Instar from the Inner Kingdoms — wrested control of the city from the remnants of the Simir dynasty and established the Thaumaturgic Council. She has ruled jointly with Yigba, an Ejiin Wu-Jen of extraordinary power, ever since. Under their leadership, Instar has become the premier center of arcane learning in the western Merchant Nations, attracting students and scholars from across Nigal.

### Government Structure

The Thaumaturgic Council governs Instar as a magocracy — political power derives from magical ability. The Council consists of the most powerful spellcasters resident in the city, with Yelinda Featherstep and Yigba serving as co-rulers. The Council enforces strict laws regarding magic use, oversees the city's many academies, and maintains order through arcane means. Non-magical citizens have limited political voice, though the Council generally governs competently enough that serious unrest is rare.

### Culture

Instar's culture revolves entirely around magic. Social status is determined by arcane ability — the most powerful mages command the greatest respect, regardless of wealth or birth. The city's numerous academies and schools attract students from across Nigal, creating a cosmopolitan intellectual atmosphere. Research and experimentation are encouraged (within Council-defined limits), and magical innovation is celebrated.

Non-magical residents — merchants, servants, laborers — occupy a distinctly secondary social position. They are necessary for the city's functioning but hold no political power and limited social standing. This creates an unusual class dynamic where a brilliant but magically ungifted individual is considered inferior to a mediocre wizard.

The city is known for expeditions to capture rare creatures and specimens for magical study. These expeditions range from relatively safe collecting trips to dangerous ventures into unexplored wilderness. The most ambitious expedition — a 805 AW venture into the Jungles of Tintikan to capture legendary "dinosaurs" — was never heard from again.

### Religion

Alagalothor (patron of arcane magic) dominates religious life in Instar, as befits a city of mages. Minerva has a following among the elven population and those who see magic as part of natural balance. Rivoleen (curiosity and innovation) attracts gnomish residents and human inventors. Other deities have small shrines but limited influence — the Thaumaturgic Council discourages religions that might challenge arcane authority.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** Amara (trade arrangement since 715 AW, mutual benefit), the Druid Circle of Larue Falls (uneasy coexistence), the Philosophers of Ming (intellectual kinship), the Federation of Man (distant but cooperative).

**Enemies:** Asim and Moamon (remnants of the Empire of Simir resent Instar's independence), the Krutan Overlords (rival magical power), any force that threatens the free study of magic.

**Threats:** The growing Zebulba worship in nearby Moamon, the cursed remnants of the Dukhan Empire in the Jinook Valley, political instability in the surrounding region, and the ever-present danger of magical experimentation gone wrong.

### Key NPCs

- **Yelinda Featherstep** (LN female Ya'sehrim Elf Wizard(Enchantment) 17/Archmage 3) — Co-ruler of Instar. An elven enchantress of immense power who seized control of the city in 689 AW. Originally from the Inner Kingdoms, she has ruled Instar for over a century with firm but generally fair governance.
- **Yigba** (LN male Ejiin Human Wu-Jen 18/Archmage 5) — Co-ruler of Instar. An Ejiin mystic of extraordinary power, Yigba balances Yelinda's western approach to magic with eastern philosophical traditions. His presence makes Instar one of the few places in the Merchant Nations where Ejiin magical traditions are studied alongside western arcane practices.

## NAARLAM

**"We remember what was. We build what will be."**
— Queen Neven Konovara

### Overview

**Alignment:** N
**Government:** Monarchy
**Capital:** (not yet detailed)
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Sabo
**Religions:** Tiovin, Iquinias, Polyvin
**Primary Exports:** Trade goods, naval stores, agricultural products
**Real-World Analog:** Spain/Portugal, 15th-16th century

What remains of the ancient nation of Sabonaa today exists in the form of Naarlam, a nation built from a few of the oldest cities of the former Sabonaar empire. Naarlam emerged from the fragmentation of Sabonaa in 555 BW after the Plague of Madness devastated the population, and has maintained its identity through centuries of turmoil — the War to End All Wars, the rise and fall of the Empire of Simir, and the constant pressure of rival city-states.

Naarlam is home to seven noble houses with lineages stretching back over 2,000 years, giving it a cultural continuity unmatched in the Merchant Nations. The nation's seafaring traditions and mercantile heritage make it a natural trade partner for both the Federation of Man and the coastal cities of the region.

### History

Naarlam's roots reach back to the unification of the Sabon Peninsula tribes into the nation of Sabonaa in 1,328 BW. When the Plague of Madness shattered Sabonaa in 555 BW, Naarlam emerged as the primary successor state, inheriting the empire's mercantile traditions and the loyalty of the oldest noble houses.

The nation survived the Dukhan Empire's expansion, the War to End All Wars, and the rise of the Empire of Simir (which absorbed some of its territory). In 412 BW, the eastern territories rebelled and formed Cecastaa — a loss Naarlam has never fully accepted. Cecastaa gained full independence in 99 AW, and relations between the two nations remain complicated.

In the modern era, Naarlam seeks to restore some measure of the glory of ancient Sabonaa under Queen Neven Konovara's leadership, though the fractured political landscape of the Merchant Nations makes this a difficult ambition.

### Government Structure

Naarlam is a hereditary monarchy where the crown is influenced by the seven noble houses of Sabonaa. The queen rules with their counsel and support, but the houses wield enormous independent influence through their wealth, private armies, and ancient privileges. The balance of power between crown and houses shifts constantly — a strong monarch can dominate, while a weak one becomes a figurehead.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** Amara (trade partner), the Federation of Man (trade partner and occasional mediator), Cecastaa (complicated — shared heritage but deep resentment).

**Enemies:** The Krutan Overlords (threatening expansion), Asim (rival for regional influence), pirates raiding merchant shipping.

### The Seven Noble Houses of Sabonaa

Dating back to lineages established over 2,000 years ago, the seven remaining noble houses of Sabonaa continue to influence politics and economics throughout the Merchant Nations. The houses are **Romuvaa**, **Konovara** (the royal house), **Tantara**, **Larandor**, **Abselon**, **Sa'antoi**, and **Sedalnar**, each with their own agendas, territories, and rivalries.

### Key NPCs

- **Queen Neven Konovara** (N female Sabonaar Bard 6/Aristocrat 9) — Ruler of Naarlam. A charismatic leader who seeks to restore the glory of the Sabonaar past, navigating the treacherous politics of noble houses and rival city-states with charm, cunning, and a bard's gift for reading people.

## CECASTAA

**"Free as the wind. Wild as the herd."**
— Cecastaan saying

### Overview

**Alignment:** CN
**Government:** Independent City-State
**Capital:** (not yet detailed)
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Sabo
**Religions:** Tiovin, Vuleala, Polyvin
**Primary Exports:** Horses, livestock, leather goods
**Real-World Analog:** Spanish borderlands, 15th-16th century

Cecastaa is a nation of rebellious horsebreeders that broke away from Naarlam in 412 BW and fought for centuries to gain full independence, which was finally achieved in 99 AW. The nation's culture centers on horse breeding, open grasslands, and fierce independence. Cecastaans distrust centralized authority and value personal freedom above almost everything else.

### History

Eastern territories of Naarlam rebelled in 412 BW, forming Cecastaa in a civil war driven by resentment of distant royal authority and a desire for self-governance. The nation fought intermittently with Naarlam for centuries, enduring the War to End All Wars and the Empire of Simir's expansion before finally gaining full recognition of independence in 99 AW.

Modern Cecastaa maintains its independent spirit but faces pressure from all sides — the Federation of Man attempted to absorb it during the expansionist period of the early 600s AW, Naarlam still considers it a breakaway province, and the Krutan Overlords threaten its eastern borders.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** Amara (trade partner), the Federation of Man (trade treaties following the failed expansion).

**Enemies:** Naarlam (complicated — shared heritage but deep resentment), the Krutan Overlords (border threat).

## TENTIGO

**"By fire and sword, the Lord's will be done."**
— Tiovinian crusader oath

### Overview

**Alignment:** LN (with evil tendencies in practice)
**Government:** Warmongering Theocracy
**Capital:** Tiovesh
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Sabo
**Religions:** Tiovin (militant interpretation)
**Primary Exports:** Military goods, religious artifacts
**Real-World Analog:** Crusader states, Spanish Inquisition

The Holy Theocracy of Tentigo is a militant Tiovinian nation that has launched three crusades into neighboring lands. Unlike the Inner Kingdoms' churches of Tiovin — which emphasize charity, healing, and community service — Tentigo's interpretation of Tiovin's teachings demands aggressive conversion, military expansion, and the destruction of heretical faiths. The theocracy was formally established in 472 BW when the town of Vesh was renamed Tiovesh and declared the capital.

### History

Tentigo was founded around 550 BW as Sabonaar peoples on the Taskaun Peninsula embraced a militant interpretation of Tiovin's teachings. The formal establishment of the Holy Theocracy in 472 BW marked the beginning of an aggressive, expansionist religious state.

Tentigo has launched three major crusades: the First Tiovinian Crusade into the Horselands (172 AW), the Second Tiovinian Crusade into the Ancient South (219 AW), and the Third Tiovinian Crusade into the Lands of Chaos (397 AW). Each crusade brought destruction and resentment, earning Tentigo enemies across the continent while reinforcing the theocracy's martial culture.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** Few genuine allies. Tentigo's aggressive theology alienates most neighbors. Some smaller theocratic settlements along the Taskaun Peninsula follow Tentigo's lead.

**Enemies:** Nearly everyone. The Horselands peoples, the Ancient South nations, the Lands of Chaos city-states, and even the Federation of Man (which regards Tentigo's militant Tiovinism as a perversion of the faith) view the theocracy with suspicion or open hostility.

## ASIM

**"The strong rule. The weak serve."**
— Asim slavelord saying

### Overview

**Alignment:** NE
**Government:** Autocracy
**Capital:** Asim
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Sabo
**Religions:** Taovan (secretly), Xannith, Zebulba (growing)
**Primary Exports:** Slaves, manufactured goods
**Real-World Analog:** Corrupt remnant empire

From deep within the fortified city of Asim, King Byrne Simir commands one of the few remaining slave trades of the Sabon Peninsula. Asim and its sister city Moamon are known for their hostility toward non-humans. The machinations of the slavers revolve around new sources of slaves and the eventual retaking of the fallen Empire of Simir, placing the leadership of Amara and Instar squarely in the enemy camp.

### History

Asim was the capital of the Empire of Simir (291-686 AW) and has never accepted the Empire's fall. Under King Byrne Simir — a descendant of the original dynasty — the city maintains the slave trade and dreams of reconquest. The assassination of King Tiberius Simir in 686 AW remains a source of bitter resentment, and the Simir family's ambitions have only grown darker over the centuries.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** Moamon (uneasy alliance based on shared Simir heritage and anti-non-human sentiment), the Krutan Overlords (common interest in slave trade).

**Enemies:** Amara (primary target for reconquest), Instar (rival power), Rivoleen's Hand and the Liberators (actively freeing slaves), the Federation of Man (opposes slavery).

### Key NPCs

- **King Byrne Simir** (NE male Sabonaar Aristocrat 5/Rogue 7/Assassin 1) — Ruler of Asim. A cunning and ruthless descendant of the Simir dynasty, Byrne dreams of restoring the Empire and crushing Amara. His methods include assassination, slavery, and alliance with dark powers.

## MOAMON

**"All things end. Entropy is the only truth."**
— Zebulban of Moamon

### Overview

**Alignment:** NE
**Government:** Autocracy (with growing theocratic elements)
**Capital:** Moamon
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Sabo
**Religions:** Zebulba (growing dominant), Xannith, Taovan
**Primary Exports:** Industrial goods, metals
**Real-World Analog:** Dark industrial city with cult influence

In the dark and industrial city of Moamon, worship of Zebulba — the Lord of Entropy — is becoming increasingly common. The city's ruler, Baron Helfast Simir, may or may not be controlled by these cultists, but one thing is certain: intelligent undead worshiping the Lord of Entropy are becoming more and more common in the region.

### History

Moamon, like Asim, was part of the Empire of Simir and maintains the Simir name in its ruling family. After the Empire's fall in 686 AW, Moamon became an independent city-state. In 733 AW, the city went to war with Amara, suffering heavy losses before an uneasy truce was brokered.

In 757 AW, an unknown disaster caused the dwarven citadel of Gaal Tarak to collapse in the nearby Kirak Mountains, sending thousands of dwarven refugees into Moamon. Two years later, in 759 AW, massive fires destroyed the dwarven tent city outside Moamon's walls. Suspicious activity by the Zebulbans was suspected but never proven. The incident deepened the city's anti-non-human sentiment and strengthened the Zebulba cultists' influence.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** Asim (uneasy alliance), the Umbral Hand (shared Zebulba worship connects Moamon to the wider death cult network).

**Enemies:** Amara (hostile rival), the Federation of Man, the Church of Tiovin, any force opposing undead and entropy worship.

### Key NPCs

- **Baron Helfast Simir** (NE male Sabonaar Fighter 9) — Ruler of Moamon. Whether he controls the Zebulbans or they control him is an open question. Either way, the city grows darker under his rule.

## MERCHANT NATIONS ORGANIZATIONS

### Champions of Blowing Rock

The small town of Blowing Rock, unknown to most of the world, is a beacon of light and honor in the Amara region. In the Halls of Honor, a small monastery dedicated to Paladine, paladin-monks and clerics dedicated to justice and light train and periodically set out on pilgrimages and crusades into other parts of the world. The Champions seek to remove evil and undead from the neighboring lands.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Lord of Justice Lionaus Harker** (LG male Human Cleric 9 of Paladine) — Leader of the Champions. A devout and tireless champion of justice.

### Coven of the Vengeful Lady

Sabonaar children are often told stories of dark vampires coming to get them in the night, and the Coven of the Vengeful Lady is where those stories originate. Worshipers of the goddess Sheeva, these female vampires are rumored to avenge wronged Sabonaar noblewomen for the right price, though involvement with the Coven has been known to be bad not only for the victim but the client as well.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Lady Red** (CE female Vampire Cleric of Sheeva) — Leader of the Coven. Her true identity, age, and power are unknown. She is spoken of in whispers across the Sabon Peninsula.

### Druid Circle of Larue Falls

The Druids of Larue Falls are a group of elven druids that protect the falls and surrounding areas from those who would go against the natural order. Larue Falls — a spectacular set of waterfalls and small lakes north of Instar — is a well-known stopover for elves traveling from Riverhold to the ocean as well as for explorers. Although the entity known as Larue was long ago destroyed, lingering magic remains to this day. The Circle's leader has been known to turn trespassers into animals when captured and has been frustrated with what she sees as the ever-encroaching urbanization of places like Instar and Amara.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Jalanthia** (N female Tawarim Elf Druid 16) — Leader of the Druid Circle. Fiercely protective of the natural world and hostile toward urban expansion. Powerful enough to be a serious threat to anyone who crosses her.

### Krutan Overlords

The powerful spellcasters of Kruta rule over the borders of the Laveri and Sabonaar lands with an oppressive form of militarized feudalism. These overlords, while concerned with their respective walled cities, often work together on larger goals of conquest and power. Slavery of non-humans and all manner of illicit trades are common within Kruta's borders, and the Overlords are always scheming to expand their lands toward Amara and the other Merchant Nations.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Giaganzalt** (CE male, race unknown, Sorcerer 20/Archmage 5) — Leader of the Krutan Overlords. An enigmatic spellcaster of unknown race. Some say he is a dragon, others a lich, and still others an entity. His true nature remains one of the Merchant Nations' most dangerous mysteries.

### Merchant Guild of Amara

In the lands of the Sabon Peninsula, money is power, and nowhere is this more true than in Amara. The Merchant's Guild is a conglomeration of the heads and representatives of the largest merchant houses in Amara, collectively wielding enormous wealth and the hired soldiers it commands. While the Guild usually does not stray far from what Baron Bastien wants, they serve as a powerful counterbalance to the Baron's authority.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Pinto Pinocrhius** (LE male Bryndawi Dwarf Rogue 1/Aristocrat 8) — Leader of the Merchant Guild. A stout and round dwarf with a great deal of social cunning.

### Noble Houses of Sabonaa

Dating back to lineages established over 2,000 years ago, the seven remaining noble houses of Sabonaa continue to influence politics and economics throughout the Merchant Nations. The houses are Romuvaa, Konovara, Tantara, Larandor, Abselon, Sa'antoi, and Sedalnar, each with their own agendas and rivalries that play out across the entire region.

### Philosophers of Ming

An organization of sorcerer-oracle monks dedicated to Alagalothor and one of his historical followers, the Ejiin philosopher named Ming. These mystics seek enlightenment through study, meditation, and understanding the nature of magic itself. They maintain monasteries in and around Instar and are respected by the Thaumaturgic Council for their scholarly contributions.

### Rivoleen's Hand (The Liberators)

In Amara, the Shrine to the Wanderer — a wide four-story tower adorned with well-maintained gardens and small fountains dedicated to Rivoleen — serves as both a place of worship and the secret headquarters of the Liberators. This group of approximately a dozen operatives helps slaves escape from Moamon, Asim, and Kruta. Both Kruta and Asim have placed bounties on the Liberators' leader, which has only strengthened his fervor for freedom.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Finkdonklonk Shortstack** (CG male Gnome Oracle 8/Liberator 6) — Leader of the Liberators. Said to be a tactical genius, though one would never know it from his publicly quiet and simple nature. His bounty from Kruta and Asim grows every year.

### Slavelords of Asim

The slave-trading networks operating out of Asim under King Byrne Simir represent one of the Merchant Nations' greatest evils. The slavers focus on capturing non-humans and maintaining the institution that fueled the old Empire of Simir. Their operations extend across the region, with agents in many cities and connections to the Krutan Overlords.

### Thaumaturgic Council of Instar

The governing body of Instar, officially led by Yelinda Featherstep and Yigba. The Council maintains strict control over magical education and practice within the city and wields enormous influence over the wider region through its magical power and the trade arrangements it has negotiated.

### Zebulbans of Moamon

The growing cult of Zebulba in Moamon represents one of the most dangerous religious movements in the Merchant Nations. Whether Baron Helfast Simir is controlled by the cultists or merely tolerates them, intelligent undead worshiping the Lord of Entropy are becoming increasingly common in the region. The Zebulbans' connection to the wider Umbral Hand network makes them a threat that extends far beyond Moamon's walls.
