# Continent of Nigal

The sprawling continent of Nigal, one of the two known continents of Mazariim, is home to three of the major races, namely the humans, dwarves, and gnomes. The landmass is over three thousand miles wide from west to east and more than two thousand miles across from north to south. Human and dwarven lands are split into two by an enormous mountain range stretching over a thousand miles from northwest to southeast known as the Dragon Spines, and the gnomes have always shared lands with the dwarves, predominately in the eastern portion of the continent.

## Inner Kingdoms

The Inner Kingdoms lie in the northwest corner of Nigal, protected by mountains and enjoying one of the most temperate locations on the continent. Dozens of small city-states and nations cover the landscape of the Madronian Peninsula, with well-patrolled roads and a lack of roving monsters a long-established reality for this region predominately controlled by the Federation of Man. Very few human nations or cities in the area are not in this republic, and those that are not are under constant political and economic pressure to join.

These nations are bound together by the Federation of Man, the most technologically advanced and militarily powerful human government on Mazariim. Through the Federation, the Inner Kingdoms maintain well-patrolled roads, steam-powered infrastructure, and a standardized legal system that stands in stark contrast to the fractured Merchant Nations to the south. Governed by the Praetorian Council at Fort Hope, the Federation ensures mutual defense, coordinates trade policy, and arbitrates disputes between member states.

The humans originating in this area are known as Madronians, with the name of their peoples originating from the name of one of their ancient rulers from pre-history, Madron. Their regional dialect is known as Madra, a flowing language with over fifteen vowels and many silent letters.

Many of the nations in the Inner Kingdoms enjoy what would be considered real-world 17th and 18th-century technology, sometimes including bi-focal eyeglasses, the printing press, indoor plumbing, the cotton gin, bicycles, telescopes, and steam-engine technology (on a limited basis, a gnomish invention). Analogs for Western Europe and some Eastern European countries can be found here.

## Merchant Nations

To the south of the Inner Kingdoms lies the Merchant Nations, a large number of human city-states and nations that over the time since the War have fallen away from the Federation of Man, both due to the distance from the Inner Kingdoms and the many revolutions and changes this region has faced. There is no unified government or republic here, with control dictated more by economics than armies. The Merchant Nations are also more dangerous, with monsters more common and traders needing to protect their goods themselves, at times a costly venture.

The humans originating from this part of Nigal are known as Sabonaar, named after an ancient empire from pre-history. They speak a regional dialect called Sabo, a practical language that is spelled phonetically.

The Merchant Nations have the equivalent of 15th and 16th-century technologies, a little further than the Middle Ages of our world. Analogs for all of Europe, the Mediterranean, and other unique locations can be found here.

## Lands of Chaos

While the Inner Kingdoms occupy the northwestern peninsula of western Nigal and the Merchant Nations on the western coast, the Lands of Chaos reside on the southwestern peninsula of Nigal, called the Lavantine Peninsula. These lands have areas that have no major cities or nations for some distance, corrupt or evil governments that war with each other, and lawless monster-filled regions where the strong survive. Many who are on the run from other nations or enemies come to the Lands of Chaos since so few questions are asked there.

The humans that originated from this part of Nigal are known as the Laveri, a name of unknown historical origin. They speak Laveran, a language that contains only oral vowels and very contrasting consonant combinations.

The Lands of Chaos have the equivalent of technology from the Middle Ages and consequently can vary greatly. Analogs for Europe, the Mediterranean, Western Asia, Africa, and other unique locations can be found here.

## Horselands

In the center of the continent lies the Horselands, a vast dry region of nomads and inhospitable towns and nations of humans surrounding the Lake of Tears, the largest freshwater lake in Nigal. Life is not for the weak in this region of the world, where barter is more common than coinage and the horse is the most prized and traded commodity. Dwarves also have settlements and city-states along the northeastern portion of the Horselands, although most ride ponies.

The humans originating in this area are known as Chak'ti, the name of their people meaning "horse" in their language. Their dialect is known as Chak'atan, a rough and guttural language with harsh consonants and sound effects.

The Horselands have the equivalent of technology from the Middle Ages, although generally major resources are always scarce and the population is somewhat if not completely poor. Analogs for portions of Eastern Europe, Western Asia, and Africa can be found here.

## Ancient South

Far from the majority of the civilized world with barren deserts and rocky steppes, the Ancient South holds some of the oldest city-states and nations of humankind. Large pyramids that can be seen for miles have lasted thousands of years, and the peoples of this region rely on rivers and prayers to see them through year to year.

The humans originating in this part of the world are known as the Varkonan, the term for "god-king" in their ancient language. Originally made of pictograms, the language of Varkonae eventually transitioned into its own alphabet which greatly expanded the accessibility of the written form of Varkonae to its people.

Although some Middle Age technology can be found, most of the inhabitants of the Ancient South rely on pre-Middle Age technology, at times reminiscent of the Bronze Age. Analogs for Egypt, Africa, the Middle East, and Asia can be found here.

## Lands of Honor

A large string of islands known as the Yashaki, or the "Warlord's Pearls", lies to the southeast of the main continent of Nigal. There one can find the Lands of Honor, a group of city-states with different values and cultures than the rest of the world, analogs of the Asian nations of our world, such as Japan and China. Samurai are the paladins of these lands filled with warlords, ninjas, and monks.

The humans originating from this part of the world are known as the Ejiin, which in their dialect simply means "person". Their language, known as Ejao, is a flowing language with a wide variety of sounds that emphasize different parts of the same words for different meanings. The written form consists of thousands of pictograms that can be written in an artistic form, an Ejiin art form said to take decades to truly master.

The Lands of Honor generally have the technology of Asia in the 15th century, including gunpowder and advanced ships, although this too can vary wildly within the islands themselves. Character classes such as samurai, ninja, and wu-jen are appropriate for this area of the world.

## Mespoian Peninsula

The Mespoian Peninsula is a land of religion, jungles, and druidic magic. Close analogs to the Aztec, Inca, and other Meso-American nations of the 13th century, these more primitive peoples have close ties to the land as well as their gods. Terraced farming learned from the dwarven peoples is prevalent here, as are their large ziggurats that serve as city centers and religious sites.

The humans that originated in this part of the world are known as Azala, named after their first ruler dating back to pre-history. Their language is known as Zalato, a combination of letters and pictograms with strange combinations of sounds and double vowels.

These peoples live with Bronze Age technology and are closer to nature and the outdoors than almost any other peoples save the elves.

## Frozen Isles

The Frozen Isles are aptly named, islands of bitter cold and hardy people. Located in a region that most would find inhospitable far to the north of the Gnomish Lands, the Isles are filled with large evergreen forests, huge rivers and waterfalls, tall mountains, and rugged hills.

Humans originating from the Frozen Isles are called Nonds, a word that means "honor" in their regional dialect. The Nond speak a language known as Nondalhiem which contains many vowels with varied accents as well as interesting and sometimes harsh double consonant sounds.

The Frozen Isles have technology similar to the real world Middle Ages, although their shipbuilding and weapon-making are very advanced. They are analogs to the various Nordic cultures of our world.

## Northern Dwarven Nations

The northern half of the Dragon Spine encompasses the main dwarven holds and cities of their republic, known as the Dwarven Empire. Headed by a hereditary king that switches between clans and regular intervals, the Northern Dwarven Nations are of similar social order and technology as the Inner Kingdoms of humans.

The dwarves of these northern mountain ranges are known as the Gormdawi, or "High Dwarf", and have dark-colored hair and eyes, and varying shades of skin color and eye color. Their languages consist of the standard Rhundawi, or "dwarven word", as well as Common and Dal'rhundawi, or "old dwarven word".

Technology from the 16th and 17th centuries exists, and steam power for a variety of purposes is widespread, with simple train systems, steamboats, steam-powered mills, heating, and more. There is currently underway an ambitious project to create an underground rail system linking the various holds deep within the mountains, a project that will take more than two generations to complete. Gnomes live in dwarven settlements all over the northern nations.

## Southern Dwarven Nations

If the Northern Dwarven Nations are similar to the Inner Kingdoms of humankind, then the Southern Dwarven Nations are a good comparison to the Merchant Nations of humankind. Further away from the holds of noble clans and largest holds, the nations of the south are more independent and do not always give fealty to the dwarven king of his nobility. There is more warring between clans and less safety in these areas, as well as conflicts with the humans in the Horselands.

Dwarves hailing from the southern dwarven regions as well as the Gnomish Realms are known as Bryndawi, or "gold dwarves". They have varying shades of red, blonde, and brown hair, with green and blue eyes and varying skin tones, but usually tans and golds. The Bryndawi speak a number of regional dialects of Rhundawi, or "dwarven word", as well as Common and Rhunurbar, or "trade words", which is a combination of Dal'rhundawi with some Gnomish and Chak'atan.

Their technology would be comparable to the 15th and 16th centuries, with less steam power than the northern lands, and fewer gnome inhabitants as well.

## Gnomish Realms

Far to the east lies a set of mountains that the gnomes populate more than any other area in Mazariim. The Gnomish Realms are actually about equally split between gnomes and dwarves, but the original homeland of the race as well as a predominance in gem mines keeps most gnomes satisfied in this area of the world. Unbothered by the ways of the more populated nations, they rarely obey anything the dwarven monarchy has to say, and are content being out of the way of the rest of the world.

The gnomes of Mazariim, like the hin, are usually very contented in their homelands. There are many gnomes that do set out for other parts of the world, but it is rare for gnomes to settle in new parts of the world or even have their own major cities outside of their mountain ranges. Gnomes speak Rhungnom, the common language of gnomes. Rhungnom uses the dwarven alphabet for its letters.

The Gnomish Realms have 17th-century technology as well as steam power and even flying balloons, dirigibles, and clockwork automatons.
