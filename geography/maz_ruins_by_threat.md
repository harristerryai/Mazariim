# Ruins of Mazariim — Organized by Threat Type

**Compiled from:** maz_ruins_akovar.md, maz_ruins_nigal.md, maz_ruins_shalazar.md, maz_ruins_vandardhon.md
**As of 842 AW**

---

## UNDEAD

Sites primarily threatened by undead creatures, death magic, necromantic traps, or restless spirits.

- **Amaunet** *(Akovar)* — active lich presence, undead creatures, wild magic surge zone
- **Brotherhood of the Black Flame Fortress / The Obsidian Tower / Deathspire** *(Vandardhon)* — lingering undead sealed inside, necromantic traps, possible dormant archlich in lowest vault
- **The Bone Fields** *(Vandardhon)* — spontaneous undead manifestations on certain nights, death magic that warps living tissue, maddening whispers
- **The Hollow Brotherhood Stronghold** *(Vandardhon)* — trapped undead (including powerful specimens), active necromantic traps, taint causing corruption and madness, dormant experimental undead
- **Purge Battlefields & Fortified Compounds** *(Vandardhon)* — residual death magic, vengeful spirits of both crusaders and cultists, cursed ground
- **Tirithorn** *(Vandardhon, Drowning Cities)* — underwater wraiths of drowned residents, still enacting daily routines
- **Silverlake Landing** *(Vandardhon, Drowning Cities)* — drowned merchant ghosts protecting "their" cargo
- **Falathrim** *(Vandardhon, Drowning Cities)* — vengeful bardic spirits whose songs cause madness; Sahuagin tribe
- **Maeglin's Rest** *(Vandardhon, Drowning Cities)* — spirits of mass-grave victims demanding proper burial rites
- **The Bloodtear Villages** *(Vandardhon)* — disease spirits, lingering cursed ground, survivors' ghosts
- **The Gauntlight Ruins** *(Nigal, Gnomish Realms)* — Togglesprocket's necromantic experiments, hybrid undead constructs, spatial anomalies
- **The Abandoned Pyramids of the Western Desert** *(Nigal, Ancient South)* — degraded preservation magic, potential undead guardians in sealed tombs

---

## PLANAR / DAEMONIC

Sites primarily threatened by extraplanar entities, active portals, dimensional instability, or daemonic corruption.

- **Mibampes — The Planar Breach** *(Akovar)* — active planar instability, constant portal activity depositing beholders, aberrations, and Far Realm/Abyssal creatures; spatial distortion; magic unpredictable
- **Shattered Coast Ruins** *(Akovar)* — active planar portals, high concentration of wild magic zones, hostile chromatic dragon clans
- **Zanthar's Tower Ruins** *(Nigal, The Steppe)* — sealed Orceri interdimensional incursion site; monitoring ongoing
- **Gaal Tarak** *(Nigal, Dwarven Nations)* — sealed portal to alternate dimension Ta'Puh, temporal distortions, anomalous energy buildup
- **The Ice-Ruins of the Northern Islands (Grimhold)** *(Nigal, Frozen Isles)* — unknown ancient civilization, ley-line focusing devices, increasing electromagnetic and thermal activity
- **The Oasis of Whispers** *(Nigal, Ancient South)* — possible Akovarian memory archive; unexplained whispers of unknown planar origin
- **The Proto-Entity Craters — Zonth, Khemset, Menot, Nakar, Anumata** *(Nigal, Ancient South)* — active proto-entity crash sites, magical contamination; Nakar suspected of hosting Yak-Men alliance
- **The Shadow-Touched Lands** *(Nigal, Lands of Chaos)* — residual Shadow Plane merger effects, warped reality, planar thin spots, chains of unknown purpose
- **The Beneshire Ley-Line** *(Nigal, cross-regional)* — contested by multiple factions including Umbral Hand and Dark Whispers; active corruption risk
- **Naval Disaster Site** *(Vandardhon / deep ocean)* — daemonic corruption still permeating water, sea creatures warped by daemon magic, daemonic entities bound to sunken wrecks

---

## FEY

Sites primarily threatened by fey creatures, fey-adjacent magic, or unexplained supernatural phenomena tied to the fey.

- **The Seven Seats** *(Vandardhon, Starwood Confederation)* — territorial fey creatures, territorial druids, awakened treants that mistake visitors for loggers
- **The Silent Villages** *(Vandardhon)* — fey magic signatures, temporal anomalies, compulsion to "join the village," visitors occasionally vanish the same way the original 1,400 residents did
- **Spring of the Seven Vessels** *(Shalazar)* — fey territoriality, old wards that punish oath-breakers, water that induces visions
- **The Stone Circles of the Eastern Steppe** *(Nigal, Horselands)* — increasing unexplained activity since 815 AW, suspected fey/ley-line nexus of unknown ancient origin

---

## DRAGONS

Sites primarily occupied or controlled by dragons, with dragon encounters as the dominant threat.

- **Amaunet** *(Akovar)* — Red Warden clan (red dragons), contested territory; also contains lich
- **Ptei** *(Akovar, Spine of Fire)* — guarded by silver and white dragon clans; active protective wards on sensitive vaults
- **Iseueri — The Swamp Archive** *(Akovar)* — black dragons (hostile), bronze dragons (conditional access); contested dragonborn-dragon boundary
- **Dragonborn Heritage Sites** *(Akovar, various)* — clan-specific dragon presence; some sites sacred and proprietary
- **Volcanic Ruins of the Spine of Fire** *(Akovar)* — white and silver dragon clans, enhanced fire magic, toxic volcanic gases, geothermal instability
- **Shattered Coast Ruins** *(Akovar)* — black and green chromatic dragon clans, active planar portals, high wild magic concentration

---

## WILD MAGIC / MAGICAL HAZARDS

Sites primarily dangerous due to destabilized or anomalous magical energy rather than creatures.

- **Temple of the Seventh Convergence** *(Akovar)* — active stasis fields, wild magic zones, structural damage from uncontrolled magical discharge
- **The Krutan Ruins of Vel Morath** *(Nigal, Lands of Chaos)* — arcane contamination with radiation-like effects, rapid corrosion of materials, dangerous to prolonged exposure
- **The Ley-Line Scars** *(Nigal, cross-regional)* — reawakened ley-line flows causing structural damage, ley-sickness from prolonged exposure, unpredictable secondary magical behaviors

---

## ENVIRONMENTAL / NATURAL HAZARDS

Sites where the primary dangers are structural collapse, terrain, wildlife, weather, or mundane physical hazard.

- **The Smoke-Hollows** *(Shalazar)* — rockfalls, unstable chambers, aggressive cliff-nesting predators, disorientation in sea-fog
- **The Guest-Right Cache** *(Shalazar)* — rival treasure hunters, territorial beasts drawn by food smell
- **The Saltwind Wreck** *(Shalazar)* — rough surf, sea predators, smugglers using wreck as dead-drop
- **The Empty Rows** *(Shalazar)* — structural hazards, desperate squatters
- **Thornheart Amphitheater** *(Vandardhon)* — minimal; druidic guardians may be hostile
- **Expansionist Border Camps** *(Vandardhon)* — territorial wildlife, occasional bandits, unstable structures
- **Tauron / The Bull Kingdom** *(Vandardhon)* — territorial animals, smugglers using ruins as hideout
- **Deepglimmer Shaft** *(Vandardhon)* — structural instability and cave-ins, blind cave creatures in flooded levels, toxic gases in sealed sections
- **Galadhon Hold** *(Vandardhon)* — territorial wildlife, unstable structures
- **Silverroot Hall** *(Vandardhon)* — territorial wildlife, unstable structures
- **Ravencrest Summit** *(Vandardhon)* — territorial wildlife, unstable structures
- **The Stranded Camps** *(Vandardhon)* — minimal; possible goblin/orc scavengers
- **Karak Vorn** *(Nigal, Dwarven Nations)* — structural instability, collapse hazards; lower levels orc-held
- **The Ancestor Tombs of the Southern Passes** *(Nigal, Dwarven Nations)* — mechanical traps, sealed chambers
- **The Steamhowl Crater** *(Nigal, Gnomish Realms)* — historical hazard site, now largely stable
- **Tinwhisle's Observatory** *(Nigal, Gnomish Realms)* — fire damage, structurally compromised
- **Xochicallan** *(Nigal, Mespoian Peninsula)* — dense jungle overgrowth, dangerous wildlife, Wyld-Bound druids who discourage intrusion
- **The Causeways of the Lowland Swamp** *(Nigal, Mespoian Peninsula)* — swamp terrain, minimal active threats
- **The Sunken Torii of Hama Bay** *(Nigal, Lands of Honor)* — underwater location; sealed chamber of unknown contents
- **Crusader's Bane** *(Nigal, Lands of Chaos)* — historical military site, peaceful mass grave, no active threats

---

## HUMANOID THREATS

Sites primarily dangerous due to hostile humanoid occupants — bandits, raiders, hostile factions, or organized groups.

- **Karak Vorn** *(Nigal, Dwarven Nations)* — orc-held lower levels; structural instability also present
- **The Broken Guesthouse** *(Shalazar)* — no monster threat; primary danger is social/political, with families that may react violently to old grievances being reopened

---

## UNKNOWN / SUPERNATURAL

Sites where the threat is unclassified, mysterious, or does not fit the above categories.

- **The Bone Road** *(Nigal, Horselands)* — site of mysterious mass animal death; psychological unease reported; origin unknown
- **The Singing Shafts of Thoral Deeping** *(Nigal, Dwarven Nations)* — unusual acoustic phenomena; possibly geological, possibly supernatural
- **The Graniteshield Vaults** *(Nigal, Dwarven Nations)* — sealed; formerly contained a fragment of the Machine of Gyru; current status of vault unknown

---

## NOTES

Several major sites appear under multiple threat categories. The most notable overlaps:

- **Amaunet** (Akovar): Dragons *and* Undead
- **Shattered Coast Ruins** (Akovar): Dragons *and* Planar
- **Karak Vorn** (Nigal): Humanoids *and* Environmental
- **Falathrim** (Vandardhon): Undead *and* Humanoids (Sahuagin)
- **The Silent Villages** (Vandardhon): Fey *and* potential Planar explanation

The **Pale Watch Fortress** and **Fort Hope Memorial** are active sites rather than ruins and are not listed above, though both are relevant to campaigns involving undead or daemonic threats.
