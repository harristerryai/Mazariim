# Geography of the Gnomish Realms: The Yazawee Mountains

**As of 842 AW**

---

## INTRODUCTION

Far to the east of the Dragon Spine, beyond the southern reaches of the Dwarven Nations and the vast emptiness of the Horselands, rises a second mountain range — lower, greener, and stranger than its western cousin. The Yazawee Mountains are the ancestral homeland of the gnomish people, a cluster of forested peaks, deep valleys, and gem-rich hills where the smallest of the major races has built a civilization defined not by conquest or empire but by curiosity, invention, and an unshakeable conviction that the universe is best understood by taking it apart and putting it back together.

The gnomes of Mazariim — collectively known as the Hin'grom in their own tongue — share these mountains roughly equally with the Bryndawi dwarves, though the gnomish presence is dominant and the homeland's character is unmistakably gnomish. Terraced farms cling to hillsides alongside workshops belching steam and colored smoke. Clockwork windmills turn on ridgelines beside ancient stone watchtowers. Dirigibles drift between mountain peaks, their canvas envelopes painted in the brilliant colors that gnomes favor. The Yazawee Mountains hum with activity — the clatter of gears, the hiss of steam valves, the crack of experimental devices detonating in reinforced testing chambers, and the constant chatter of a people who find silence suspicious and boredom intolerable.

The gnomes originating in these mountains are divided into two subraces. The Durazgrom, or Rock Gnomes, dwell in the deeper, more subterranean settlements closer to the Dwarven Nations, sharing much of their ancestry and culture with the Bryndawi. They have earth-toned hair and skin, tend to be slightly shorter and heavier than their cousins, and are responsible for the vast majority of gnomish technological innovation — steam power, clockwork mechanisms, and the engineering marvels that have changed life across Mazariim. The Rothgrom, or Forest Gnomes, inhabit the surface forests and valleys of the Yazawee range. They are taller and thinner, with blonde, red, brown, or green-toned coloration, and they maintain a deeper connection to the natural world, living in half-buried homes and treehouses alongside animal companions. Many Rothgrom druids worship Minerva alongside Rivoleen, blending nature reverence with gnomish philosophical inquiry.

Gnomish technology is the most advanced on Mazariim. The Gnomish Realms operate at 17th-century equivalents, with steam power, flying balloons, dirigibles, clockwork automatons, and experimental devices that other races can barely comprehend, let alone reproduce. Gnomish engineers designed the steam engines that power dwarven mines and human factories, the mechanical clocks that keep time in Federation cities, and the dirigibles that provide rapid transport for the wealthy. Other races attempt to reproduce gnomish creations, but the results are invariably inferior — more expensive, less reliable, and prone to spectacular failure. Gnomish craftsmanship in this domain is as unmatched as dwarven metalwork or elven woodcraft.

All gnomes speak Rhungnom, their common ancestral language, which uses the dwarven alphabet for its written form. Common is widely spoken for trade with outsiders, and Rhunurbar — the dwarven-gnomish trade pidgin — serves for communication with neighboring Bryndawi settlements.

The Gnomish Realms are governed not by kings or emperors but by clan elders, town assemblies, and the loose consensus that gnomish society favors over centralized authority. The gnomish capital of Zhonhylite — the oldest gnomish settlement, founded around 2,100 BW — serves as the cultural and administrative heart of the realms, but its authority over other settlements is more advisory than absolute. Each community manages its own affairs, and the gnomish distrust of concentrated power ensures that no single leader or institution can dominate for long.

The patron deity of gnomekind is Rivoleen, the Liberator — an introspective entity who favors enlightenment, thought, creativity, and above all, freedom. Rivoleen's awakening around 1,780 BW transformed gnomish culture from one modeled on dwarven traditions into something uniquely its own: philosophical, inventive, communal, and fiercely protective of individual liberty. Her Seekers — the gnomish clergy — serve as philosophers, teachers, counselors, and advocates for freedom rather than as hierarchical priests demanding obedience. Rivoleen's influence permeates every aspect of gnomish life, from the Festival of Flight (when mechanical birds are released into the sky as symbols of liberation) to the Great Debate (where gnomes engage in structured philosophical arguments on topics announced a season in advance).

The Gnomish Realms are bounded by the southern reaches of the Dwarven Nations to the northwest, the Horselands to the west and south, and wild, largely unexplored territory to the east. These boundaries are more geographic than political — gnomes have never been expansionists, and the Yazawee Mountains provide everything their civilization needs. The few gnomish communities that exist outside the homeland are small enclaves in dwarven holds, human cities, and the occasional trading post, established by individual gnomes who left home to pursue knowledge or adventure rather than by any organized colonization effort.

**Current Political Organization (842 AW)**

The Gnomish Realms consist of independent settlements and communities with no centralized government:

- **Zhonhylite** — Gnomish Capital. The oldest and largest gnomish settlement, cultural and administrative heart of the realms. Founded ~2,100 BW.
- **Dammarth** — Elementalist Mageocracy. A settlement specializing in elemental magic, governed by its most powerful spellcasters. Founded ~1,750 BW.
- **Chumbawamba** — Industrial Settlement. A younger but significant city known for manufacturing, arcane research, and — more recently — political turmoil and necromantic history. Founded ~1,400 BW.

Dozens of smaller villages, mining communities, forest settlements, and research outposts are scattered throughout the Yazawee Mountains, each governed by local clan elders or communal assemblies.

**Total Population (842 AW, estimated)**

- Gnomes (Durazgrom): ~90,000
- Gnomes (Rothgrom): ~70,000
- Dwarves (Bryndawi, in shared settlements): ~40,000
- Humans (traders, scholars, visitors): ~5,000
- Halflings: ~3,000
- Other (elves, exotic visitors): ~2,000
- **Total: ~210,000**

## REGIONAL HISTORY

Gnomish history is preserved through a combination of written records, mechanical models, and an oral tradition that emphasizes stories of invention, discovery, and philosophical debate rather than wars and conquests. Gnomes keep meticulous workshop logs — detailed records of experiments, prototypes, and failed designs — that serve double duty as historical archives. The Library of Ten Thousand Questions in Zhonhylite houses the most comprehensive collection of gnomish knowledge anywhere in the world, its shelves holding not only books but also mechanical models, experimental prototypes, and artistic works created by generations of gnomes.

### Prehistoric Origins and the Alliance with the Bryndawi

Gnomes began their origins in the farthest eastern mountain ranges of Nigal, far from the Dwarven Empire's northern strongholds. As they rose from primitive ways, gnomes integrated closely with the Bryndawi dwarves, fighting alongside them against giants, orcs, and the early Chak'ti and Azala peoples who competed for territory on the mountains' fringes. This partnership was foundational — gnomes and Bryndawi developed their cultures in parallel, sharing traditions, worship practices, and practical knowledge. Gnomes adopted the dwarven alphabet for their written language and worshiped the Old Gods alongside the Bryndawi, particularly Dirgin and Pyrantor.

The capital of the Gnomish Realms, Zhonhylite, was settled around 2,100 BW in the center of the Yazawee Mountains, and it still stands as the cultural heart of the gnomish homeland. The site was chosen for its combination of defensible terrain, proximity to gem deposits, and the convergence of several mountain valleys that provided natural routes for trade and communication.

### The Coming of Rivoleen (~1,780 BW)

Less than a hundred years after the arrival of Mordrion among the dwarves, gnomekind experienced its own spiritual transformation. Around 1,780 BW, an entity named Rivoleen awakened — not in a blaze of divine light or a thunderous proclamation, but as a humble gnomish scholar carrying a journal filled with questions rather than answers. She walked among the gnomes in their cities and villages, sitting in workshops and gardens, asking them what they believed, why they believed it, and whether they had ever questioned their assumptions.

Rivoleen's arrival sparked a philosophical revolution. Within a generation, the worship of the Old Gods faded among most gnomes, replaced by Rivoleen's teachings of freedom, critical thinking, and the joy of discovery. Gnomish society transformed from one modeled closely on dwarven traditions — clan-based, ancestor-focused, respectful of hierarchy — into something uniquely its own: egalitarian, intellectually adventurous, and deeply suspicious of any authority that could not justify itself through reason.

The change did not happen overnight, and it was not universally welcomed. Some gnomish clans, particularly those with deep Bryndawi ties, resisted what they saw as an abandonment of ancient traditions. The Durazgrom of the deeper settlements were slower to adopt Rivoleen's teachings than the more surface-dwelling Rothgrom, who found her reverence for freedom and nature immediately congenial. Over centuries, however, Rivoleen's philosophy became the dominant spiritual and intellectual framework for gnomish civilization, though pockets of Old God worship persist to this day.

### The Dwarven Wars and Gnomish Independence (1,921 – 1,849 BW)

The outbreak of the Dwarven Wars in 1,921 BW placed the gnomes in an uncomfortable position. The Gormdawi of the north, seeking to extend the Dwarven Empire's reach across all dwarven and gnomish lands, viewed gnomish territory as ripe for annexation. The gnomes — peaceful, inventive, and far more interested in their workshops than in warfare — seemed like easy targets.

They were not.

Rivoleen's teachings during this period emphasized patience, diplomacy, and the importance of defensive preparation. Gnomish communities fortified their borders with ingenious traps, mechanical defenses, and the kind of creative problem-solving that would become their military hallmark. Gormdawi columns that entered gnomish territory found roads that collapsed beneath them, bridges that retracted, valleys that flooded on command, and a population that melted into forests and tunnels at the first sign of aggression, only to reappear behind the invaders to harry their supply lines.

The message was clear: conquering gnomish lands would cost far more than it was worth. When Mordrion brokered peace in 1,849 BW, the gnomish lands were explicitly excluded from the Dwarven Empire's authority. Gnomish independence was preserved, and with the threat of northern expansion removed, gnomes turned their considerable energies toward more productive pursuits.

### The Golden Age of Innovation (1,849 BW – 4 BW)

The centuries following the peace were a golden age for gnomish civilization. Freed from the threat of war, gnomes threw themselves into invention, exploration, and philosophical inquiry with characteristic enthusiasm.

The most transformative development was steam power. A few centuries before the War, Durazgrom engineers in the deep mountain holds developed the ability to harness steam through simple engines. The technology was primitive at first — unreliable, inefficient, and prone to explosive failure — but gnomish persistence and Rivoleen's encouragement of experimentation gradually refined it into a reliable power source. Steam-driven pumps drained flooded mines. Steam-powered bellows increased forge temperatures. Steam engines drove mechanical looms, grain mills, and ore crushers.

True to Rivoleen's teaching that knowledge should be shared rather than hoarded, gnomes freely shared their steam technology with the Bryndawi dwarves, who incorporated it into their holds and eventually passed it to the Gormdawi of the north. The technology spread to human lands as well — gnomish refugees in Pragos perfected steam-driven water pumps in 12 AW, kickstarting that nation's industrial dominance. By the modern era, steam power has become widespread in the wealthier parts of both human and dwarven civilization, though gnomes remain the only people who can consistently manufacture steam-powered devices without extraordinary expense and frequent failure.

Beyond steam, gnomish inventors produced a dazzling array of innovations during this period. Clockwork mechanisms of extraordinary precision — water clocks, mechanical calculators, self-winding timepieces — emerged from Durazgrom workshops. Gnomish jewelers, blessed with extraordinarily sensitive eyes and steady hands, became the finest gem-cutters in the world. Gnomish appraisers could detect flaws invisible to other races. And in the last two centuries, gnomes have begun to fly — dirigibles and hot-air balloons soar above the Yazawee peaks, their painted envelopes a vivid sight against the mountain sky.

### The Founding of Chumbawamba (~1,400 BW)

Around 1,400 BW, a younger gnomish settlement was established in the southern Yazawee Mountains. Chumbawamba — named, as gnomish settlements often are, with a term that sounds whimsical to outsiders but carries historical meaning in Rhungnom — grew into a significant city focused on manufacturing, arcane research, and the applied sciences. Its position in the lower, more accessible southern reaches of the range made it a natural point of contact with the wider world, and Chumbawamba developed a more cosmopolitan character than the insular northern settlements.

Chumbawamba's history would take a dark turn in the 8th century AW, but for most of its existence it was a productive and unremarkable gnomish city — perhaps a bit more industrialized than Zhonhylite, a bit more focused on practical application than theoretical research, but fundamentally typical of gnomish civilization.

### The War to End All Wars (4 BW – 1 AW)

The War to End All Wars hit the Gnomish Realms hard, though gnomish ingenuity helped mitigate the worst of the devastation. When Zuel's million-strong horde erupted from the mountains, orc raiding parties penetrated the eastern ranges and attacked gnomish settlements with terrifying ferocity.

Rivoleen took a defensive role, helping protect the Gnomish Realms through inspired tactical defense rather than aggressive counterattack. Gnomish engineers created steam-powered war machines — mechanical crossbow batteries, explosive traps, armored steam-carts — that turned gnomish settlements into nearly impregnable strongholds. Rothgrom druids used the forests themselves as defensive weapons, directing wildlife against invaders and collapsing trails to channel orc columns into prepared kill zones.

Gnomes also opened their doors to refugees fleeing the wider war, providing food, shelter, and protection to humans, halflings, and dwarves who sought sanctuary. Some Seekers traveled into war-torn regions to help the displaced reach safety, living out Rivoleen's teachings at the risk of their own lives.

The War's losses were significant but not as catastrophic as those suffered by the dwarves or humans. Gnomish mobility, defensive ingenuity, and the difficult terrain of the Yazawee Mountains prevented the orcs from occupying gnomish territory for any extended period. After the War ended, gnomes rebuilt quickly, their practical skills and communal ethos allowing them to restore damaged settlements and resume innovation within a generation.

### The Post-War Era and the Rise of the Gauntlight (1 AW – 842 AW)

After the War, gnomes became even more focused on family, tinkering, and the homeland. Many gnomes born after the War seemed even more willing to forget the outside world and focus on domestic concerns, increasing the already-insular character of gnomish civilization. Periodic wars with giants and their kin threatened the Gnomish Realms from time to time, but each incursion was repelled, reinforcing the desire among some gnomes to stay home for its defense.

The centuries that followed saw steady technological advancement. Dirigibles were developed and refined. Clockwork automatons became more sophisticated. The integration of magic and machinery — what some scholars call "magitech" — progressed as gnomish artificers, many of them followers of Gyru, pushed the boundaries of what was possible.

Around 738 AW, however, a shadow fell across the settlement of Chumbawamba. A brilliant but disturbed Rothgrom named Salvatore Togglesprocket — educated at the wizard schools in Chumbawamba and gifted with a genius matched only by his instability — became obsessed with necromancy. His experiments grew increasingly dark and dangerous, and he constructed a complex of underground laboratories and necromantic workshops in what would later be called the Gauntlight Ruins. His work eventually destroyed him — or so everyone believed.

The ruins were sealed and largely forgotten. Chumbawamba moved on, the incident becoming a cautionary tale told to young gnomes about the dangers of pursuing knowledge without wisdom or ethics.

Then, in 815 AW, the ley-line reawakening changed everything. The magical energies released by Asti's liberation sent ripples across Mazariim, and ley-line endpoints extended all the way to the Gnomish Lands, intersecting the sealed ruins beneath Chumbawamba. The magical energies slowly revived Salvatore Togglesprocket's ghost and reanimated his failed experiments and creations, turning the forgotten laboratories into a festering wound of undeath beneath the city.

In 838 AW, the crisis erupted. Salvatore's ghost, revived and empowered by the ley-line energies, began expanding its influence from the Gauntlight Ruins. Simultaneously, political tensions in Chumbawamba between the traditionalist Clan Azure and the radical Children of Pyre faction escalated to the brink of civil war. The dual crisis — a necromantic horror beneath the city and political collapse above — drew heroes to Chumbawamba and nearly destroyed the settlement before both threats were contained.

The events of 838 AW left lasting scars on Chumbawamba and on gnomish society more broadly. The revelation that ley-line energies could revive necromantic horrors — that the magical renaissance sweeping Mazariim carried dangers as well as opportunities — forced gnomes to confront uncomfortable questions about the relationship between progress and peril. The political rift between traditional and radical factions, though patched, has not fully healed.

As of 842 AW, the Gnomish Realms continue as they have for millennia — inventive, philosophical, and largely peaceful, but more aware than ever that the wider world's troubles can reach even the most insular mountain homeland.

## THE GNOMISH PEOPLE

**"Question everything. Build something. Share what you learn."**
— Rivoleen's teaching, simplified for children

### Overview

**Alignment:** NG (with chaotic tendencies)
**Government:** Communal (clan elders, town assemblies, consensus-based)
**Capital:** Zhonhylite (pop. ~25,000)
**Population:** ~160,000 across all settlements (Durazgrom and Rothgrom combined)
**Languages:** Rhungnom, Common, Rhunurbar
**Religions:** Rivoleen (dominant), Mordrion (secondary, particularly among Durazgrom), Dirgin (traditional minority), Minerva (among Rothgrom druids), Gyru (growing, among artificers)
**Primary Exports:** Steam engines, clockwork mechanisms, gems, jeweled goods, lenses, alchemical products, dirigible technology, mechanical devices
**Real-World Analog:** No close parallel; aspects of Renaissance workshop culture combined with Enlightenment-era philosophical societies and steampunk technology

Gnomes are the smallest of the major races and, by many measures, the most inventive. Their civilization is built not on military power or territorial expansion but on curiosity, creativity, and the relentless pursuit of understanding. A gnomish settlement is recognizable by its sound as much as its sight — the clatter of gears, the hiss of steam, the excited chatter of people who find the universe endlessly fascinating and cannot stop talking about it.

Gnomish society values independence, open-mindedness, creativity, loyalty, and freedom above all. Family is extremely important — many gnomes can trace their ancestry back many generations — but the gnomish concept of family extends beyond blood to include workshop partners, philosophical mentors, and the broader community. Ancestor worship has always been prevalent in gnomish life, though not to the extent found among dwarves.

Gnomes have very sensitive eyes that can see the minutest details and hands that are exceedingly steady and agile. These physical attributes have led to great expertise in fields requiring precision: gem-cutting, jewelry, clockwork mechanisms, lens-grinding, and the crafting of delicate mechanical devices. Gnomish appraisers can detect imperfections invisible to other races. Gnomish clockmakers produce timepieces of extraordinary accuracy. Gnomish jewelers cut stones with a perfection that human and dwarven craftsmen cannot match.

### Government Structure

Gnomish governance is decentralized by design and by temperament. Each settlement manages its own affairs through some combination of clan elders, elected councils, and communal assemblies. Decisions are typically reached through debate and consensus rather than decree — a process that is slow by human standards but produces outcomes that carry genuine community buy-in.

Zhonhylite, as the largest and oldest settlement, exercises a degree of cultural leadership over the wider Gnomish Realms, but its authority is advisory rather than executive. When matters affecting all gnomes must be decided — external threats, trade policy with non-gnomish nations, or responses to crises like the Chumbawamba affair of 838 AW — representatives from the major settlements gather for an Assembly, a gnomish equivalent of the Bryndawi Gathering. Assemblies are rare, contentious, and often inconclusive, but their decisions carry moral weight even among communities that disagreed with the outcome.

There is no gnomish military in the conventional sense. Each settlement maintains its own defensive militia — typically a combination of trained warriors, engineers operating mechanical defenses, and spellcasters — but there is no standing army, no military chain of command, and no culture of martial glory. Gnomish defense relies on terrain, ingenuity, and the fact that attacking a gnomish settlement means walking into the most elaborately trapped and mechanically defended terrain on Nigal.

### Culture

Gnomish culture revolves around three interlocking values: curiosity, community, and freedom.

Curiosity drives everything. Gnomes approach the world with an insatiable desire to understand how things work, why they work that way, and whether they could work differently. This applies equally to machines, magic, philosophy, nature, and social organization. A gnome who stops asking questions is a gnome who has given up on life. Rivoleen's teachings reinforce this trait, insisting that questioning is not merely acceptable but sacred — a faith that cannot withstand scrutiny is not worth holding.

Community provides the framework within which curiosity can flourish. Gnomish settlements are collaborative environments where knowledge is shared freely, workshops are communal, and helping a neighbor with a difficult project is considered both a social obligation and a pleasure. The gnomish distrust of secrecy — rooted in Rivoleen's teaching that hoarded knowledge benefits only the few — creates a culture of open exchange that accelerates innovation. When one gnome discovers something useful and teaches it to ten others, who each teach ten more, the entire community becomes richer.

Freedom is the non-negotiable foundation. Gnomes will tolerate discomfort, danger, and even poverty before they will accept constraints on their liberty to think, create, and live as they choose. This fierce independence makes gnomish society resistant to tyranny but also resistant to coordination — getting gnomes to agree on anything is famously difficult, and gnomish projects often stall because no one is willing to accept authority over the process.

Entertainment among gnomes tends toward the intellectual. Philosophical debates are attended as enthusiastically as human sporting events. Invention exhibitions draw crowds comparable to those at Sabonaar trade fairs. Musical traditions exist but are secondary to verbal arts — storytelling, debate, comedy, and the gnomish tradition of "riddle-chains," in which participants take turns posing increasingly difficult puzzles.

### Religion

Rivoleen is the overwhelmingly dominant deity in gnomish society. Her Seekers serve as philosophers, teachers, counselors, and advocates for freedom in every settlement. Temples are open, airy structures featuring libraries, workshops, observatories, and meditation gardens — community centers for learning and debate rather than places of ritual worship.

Mordrion retains a following among Durazgrom with strong Bryndawi connections, and his Hammerpriests operate in several of the deeper settlements where dwarven and gnomish communities overlap. The relationship between Rivoleen's Seekers and Mordrion's Hammerpriests is cordial but occasionally tense — the two faiths share a respect for craftsmanship but differ sharply on the value of tradition versus innovation, and on whether authority should flow from established hierarchy or earned wisdom.

Dirgin and Pyrantor receive informal acknowledgment in some communities, remnants of the Old God worship that predated Rivoleen's arrival. These are not organized faiths but ingrained habits of respect — a miner thanking the earth before digging, a smith greeting the fire before lighting the forge.

Minerva has a genuine following among Rothgrom druids, who see no contradiction between Rivoleen's philosophical inquiry and Minerva's reverence for nature. The gnomish Minervan worshipers exist peacefully alongside Rivoleen's Seekers, and in some forest settlements the two traditions have blended into a uniquely gnomish nature-philosophy.

Gyru's Gearwright guilds attract gnomish artificers drawn to his philosophy of innovation through artifice. The gnomish Gearwrights tend to be less concerned with Gyru's theological implications (mortal ascension challenging divine hierarchy) and more interested in his practical legacy — the techniques and principles that allow magic and machinery to be combined in ways neither could achieve alone.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** The Bryndawi dwarves (ancient alliance, deep cultural ties, shared settlements), the Dwarven Empire (trade partner, recipient of gnomish technology), the Federation of Man (trade partner, particularly Pragos and Beneshire), Graniteshield (commercial hub for gnomish exports).

**Enemies:** Giants and their kin (eternal foes who periodically raid from the higher peaks), orc remnants (persistent low-level threat), monsters of the deep mountains (various predators and aberrations).

**Threats:** Giant raids, the lingering effects of the Gauntlight Ruins beneath Chumbawamba, the political fractures revealed by the Clan Azure / Children of Pyre conflict, the unpredictable consequences of ley-line energies on gnomish workshops and magical experiments, and the ever-present risk of experimental devices going catastrophically wrong.

## ZHONHYLITE — THE GNOMISH CAPITAL

**"A question asked is worth more than an answer given."**
— Inscription above the Library of Ten Thousand Questions

### Overview

**Alignment:** NG
**Government:** Council of Elders (elected from major clans and guilds)
**Capital:** Zhonhylite (pop. ~25,000)
**Population:** ~25,000 (predominantly Durazgrom, with significant Rothgrom and Bryndawi minorities)
**Languages:** Rhungnom, Common, Rhunurbar, Rhundawi
**Religions:** Rivoleen (dominant), Mordrion, Dirgin, Gyru
**Primary Exports:** Gems, clockwork mechanisms, lenses, alchemical products, scholarly works
**Real-World Analog:** No close parallel; a university city combined with a gem-cutting center and philosophical academy

Zhonhylite is the oldest, largest, and most culturally significant gnomish settlement on Mazariim. Founded around 2,100 BW in the heart of the Yazawee Mountains, the city occupies a broad valley where three river valleys converge, providing natural routes for trade and communication in every direction. The settlement spreads across both surface and subsurface levels — Rothgrom neighborhoods of half-buried homes and treehouses occupy the valley's forested slopes, while Durazgrom quarters extend into the mountains themselves, their tunnels connecting to gem mines, workshops, and the vast underground chambers that house the city's most important institutions.

Zhonhylite is the intellectual heart of gnomish civilization. The Library of Ten Thousand Questions — the largest repository of gnomish knowledge in the world — occupies an enormous complex of chambers, reading rooms, and archives in the city's central mountain. The Seeker's Temple, the largest temple to Rivoleen anywhere, serves as a community center for philosophical debate, invention exhibition, and the training of new Seekers. The Gem Quarter, where the finest gnomish jewelers ply their trade, produces cut stones of unmatched quality.

The city hums with the energy of a civilization that finds everything interesting. Workshops line every street, their doors open to passers-by who might want to see what's being built, offer suggestions, or simply satisfy their curiosity. Debate circles form spontaneously in parks and market squares. Experimental devices are tested on designated ranges at the city's edge, the occasional explosion punctuating the general bustle with a boom and a column of colored smoke.

### Key Locations

**The Library of Ten Thousand Questions** — The greatest collection of gnomish knowledge in the world. The Library contains not only books but also mechanical models, experimental prototypes, artistic works, and the Question Journals of countless gnomish thinkers stretching back millennia. Seekers from across Mazariim make pilgrimages to study in the Library and add their own contributions to its ever-growing collection. The institution is managed by a dedicated clan of librarian-scholars who guard their collections with fierce pride while ensuring that access remains open to all who seek knowledge in good faith.

**The Seeker's Temple** — The largest temple to Rivoleen in the Gnomish Realms. An open, airy structure designed to inspire thought and creativity, featuring the Hall of Questions (a circular debate space), the Workshop of Wonders (a communal invention space), and the Garden of Contemplation (a meditative outdoor area). The temple serves as the primary training institution for new Seekers and hosts the annual Great Debate.

**The Gem Quarter** — A district devoted to the cutting, polishing, and trading of gemstones. Gnomish jewelers — with their extraordinarily sensitive eyes and steady hands — produce work of unmatched precision here. The Quarter also serves as a financial center, with gnomish gem-merchants maintaining trade relationships across Nigal.

**The Clockwork District** — A neighborhood of workshops and factories devoted to the production of clockwork mechanisms, mechanical devices, and steam-powered equipment. The district is noisy, busy, and perpetually wreathed in steam. Visitors from other races often find it simultaneously fascinating and overwhelming.

**The Dirigible Fields** — Open areas on the valley's upper slopes where dirigibles and balloons are maintained, launched, and landed. Gnomish air travel is still in its relative infancy, but the dirigible fields are among the most advanced aerospace facilities on Mazariim, and the sight of painted dirigibles drifting between the mountain peaks is one of the iconic images of gnomish civilization.

### Key NPCs

- **Elder Cogsworth Brightlens** (NG male Durazgrom Artificer/Scholar) — Head of the Council of Elders, a gentle but sharp-minded gnome who has served the community for decades. Cogsworth is a master lens-grinder whose telescopes are used in observatories across the Federation of Man. He views the political troubles in Chumbawamba with deep concern and has advocated for a more unified approach to gnomish governance — a position that makes him quietly controversial.
- **High Seeker Tamwyn Softquill** (NG female Rothgrom Cleric of Rivoleen) — The senior Seeker at the temple of Rivoleen, Tamwyn is a renowned philosopher whose writings on the nature of freedom and responsibility have been translated into Common and studied in Saerrus. She is elderly, gentle-voiced, and absolutely formidable in debate.

## DAMMARTH — THE ELEMENTALIST ENCLAVE

**"The elements are not tools. They are teachers."**
— Dammarth founding precept

### Overview

**Alignment:** N
**Government:** Mageocracy (Council of Elementalists)
**Capital:** Dammarth (pop. ~12,000)
**Population:** ~12,000 (predominantly Durazgrom, with Bryndawi and Rothgrom minorities)
**Languages:** Rhungnom, Common, Rhunurbar, Draconic (scholarly)
**Religions:** Rivoleen, Dirgin, Pyrantor, Vuleala, Iquinias
**Primary Exports:** Elemental magical components, alchemical products, enchanted materials, elemental consultancy
**Real-World Analog:** No close parallel; a gnomish magical academy-city

Dammarth is unique among gnomish settlements — a mageocracy governed by its most powerful elementalist spellcasters. Founded around 1,750 BW, the settlement was established by gnomish mages who sought to study the Old Gods' elemental forces through a combination of scientific inquiry and magical practice. Over the centuries, Dammarth has become the preeminent center of elemental magic in the Gnomish Realms and one of the most respected schools of elemental study on Nigal.

The city occupies a geologically active region of the Yazawee Mountains where hot springs, volcanic vents, strong wind patterns, and deep mineral deposits create natural concentrations of elemental energy. Dammarth's founders chose the site specifically for these features, and the city's layout is designed to harness and study the elemental forces that permeate the area. Workshops and laboratories are positioned near specific geological features depending on their research focus — fire mages work near volcanic vents, earth mages study in deep caverns, air mages conduct experiments on exposed ridgelines, and water mages operate near the hot springs and underground rivers.

Unlike the rest of gnomish society, which favors communal governance and democratic debate, Dammarth is ruled by a Council of Elementalists — the city's most powerful spellcasters, who govern through a combination of magical authority and earned respect. This mageocratic structure is unusual for gnomes but has persisted because Dammarth's population self-selects for individuals comfortable with arcane hierarchy. Those who prefer Rivoleen's egalitarian ideals tend to settle elsewhere.

The Old Gods — Dirgin, Pyrantor, Vuleala, and Iquinias — receive more formal worship in Dammarth than anywhere else in the Gnomish Realms. The city's elementalists see these entities not as objects of devotion but as subjects of study: vast, ancient intelligences whose elemental natures can be observed, measured, and (cautiously) interacted with. This pragmatic relationship with the Old Gods gives Dammarth's mages access to elemental magic that most other spellcasters cannot match.

### Key NPCs

- **Archmagister Fenella Sparkstone** (N female Durazgrom Wizard/Elementalist) — Head of the Council of Elementalists and the most powerful mage in Dammarth. Fenella specializes in earth magic and has spent decades studying the deep geological forces that shape the Yazawee Mountains. She is brilliant, focused, and has little patience for what she considers the "philosophical dithering" of mainstream gnomish society.

## CHUMBAWAMBA — THE CITY OF GEARS AND GHOSTS

**"We build the future here. Sometimes the past builds itself back."**
— Chumbawamba saying, coined after 838 AW

### Overview

**Alignment:** N (with recent chaotic tendencies)
**Government:** Fractured Council (formerly unified town assembly)
**Capital:** Chumbawamba (pop. ~15,000)
**Population:** ~15,000 (predominantly mixed Durazgrom and Rothgrom, with Bryndawi and human minorities)
**Languages:** Rhungnom, Common, Rhunurbar
**Religions:** Rivoleen (dominant, but factional), Alagalothor (among arcane researchers), Gyru (among artificers)
**Primary Exports:** Manufactured goods, arcane research, alchemical products, steam-powered devices
**Real-World Analog:** No close parallel; an industrialized gnomish city recovering from internal crisis

Chumbawamba is the youngest of the Gnomish Realms' three major settlements and the one most recently scarred by crisis. Founded around 1,400 BW in the southern Yazawee Mountains, the city developed as a manufacturing and research center — more industrialized than the scholarly Zhonhylite, more practically focused than the magical Dammarth. Its position in the lower, more accessible reaches of the range gave it more contact with the outside world than other gnomish settlements, and Chumbawamba developed a cosmopolitan character that attracted gnomes drawn to applied science, engineering, and commercial enterprise.

For most of its history, Chumbawamba was an unremarkable if productive gnomish city. Its workshops produced clockwork mechanisms, steam-powered devices, and alchemical products that were exported through dwarven and human trade networks. Its wizard schools trained arcane researchers whose work contributed to gnomish understanding of magical theory. Its population was engaged, industrious, and — by gnomish standards — conventional.

Two events transformed Chumbawamba into something darker and more complicated.

### The Gauntlight Ruins

Around 738 AW, a brilliant but disturbed Rothgrom wizard named Salvatore Togglesprocket began necromantic experiments in hidden laboratories beneath the city. Salvatore was a product of Chumbawamba's wizard schools — gifted with a genius for magical theory matched only by his psychological instability and his obsessive fascination with the boundary between life and death. Working in secret, he excavated and constructed a complex of underground laboratories that would become known as the Gauntlight Ruins — multiple levels of workshops, experimental chambers, containment cells, and increasingly warped spaces where the normal rules of reality began to break down under the weight of sustained necromantic experimentation.

Salvatore's experiments grew increasingly dark. He animated corpses, bound spirits, created hybrid creatures of flesh and machinery, and probed the boundaries of the Negative Energy Plane with reckless determination. His work eventually destroyed him — a final experiment went catastrophically wrong, killing Salvatore and collapsing the upper levels of his laboratory complex.

The ruins were sealed. Chumbawamba moved on. Salvatore's story became a cautionary tale told to young gnomes: a warning about the dangers of pursuing knowledge without wisdom, of letting brilliance blind you to ethics.

Then, in 815 AW, the ley-line reawakening sent magical energies cascading across Mazariim. Ley-line endpoints extended to the Gnomish Lands, and their paths intersected the sealed ruins of Salvatore's laboratories. Slowly — over the course of years — the magical energies revived what should have stayed dead. Salvatore's ghost reconstituted, empowered by ley-line energy. His failed experiments reanimated. The containment wards he had placed (and which had held for decades after his death) weakened and failed under the pressure of the new magical flows. The Gauntlight Ruins became a festering wound of undeath beneath Chumbawamba's streets, growing stronger and more dangerous with each passing year.

### The Political Crisis of 838 AW

Simultaneously with the growing threat beneath the city, Chumbawamba was tearing itself apart above ground. Two political factions had emerged from long-simmering disagreements about the city's future, and by 838 AW their conflict had escalated to the brink of civil war.

**Clan Azure** represented traditional gnomish values — arcane research, clockwork innovation, philosophical inquiry, and the measured, careful approach to progress that Rivoleen's teachings encouraged. Clan Azure's members were predominantly established families and guild masters who valued stability and the proven methods that had sustained gnomish civilization for millennia. Their more extreme elements, led by a radical named **Xogim Flintsweeper**, pushed for the active suppression of what they saw as dangerous new ideas — a position that sat uneasily with Rivoleen's teachings about freedom of thought but that gained support as the political crisis deepened.

The **Children of Pyre** were a radical faction seeking to harness elemental fire magic for an industrial revolution that would transform gnomish society. They argued that traditional gnomish methods were too slow, too conservative, and too insular — that the wider world was changing rapidly and that gnomes needed to change with it or be left behind. Their more moderate members wanted controlled experimentation with fire-based industrial processes. Their extremists, led by a fanatic named **Bombus Clockmort**, wanted nothing less than a complete restructuring of gnomish society around fire-powered industry, and they were willing to use sabotage, intimidation, and violence to achieve it.

The escalation followed a grimly predictable pattern. Rhetorical clashes gave way to economic sabotage. Propaganda campaigns hardened positions on both sides. Acts of violence — arson, beatings, attempted assassinations — created cycles of retaliation. Both factions formed militant wings. Outside agitators, possibly connected to the growing necromantic threat beneath the city, further inflamed tensions.

### Resolution

In 838 AW, a group of heroes — recruited by the Church of Alagalothor to investigate the reawakened ruins — arrived in Chumbawamba and found themselves dealing with both crises simultaneously. The campaign alternated between delving into the Gauntlight Ruins (fighting through levels of increasingly dangerous undead, aberrations, and reality-warped magical zones) and navigating the political crisis above (investigating sabotage, protecting key figures, gathering evidence of manipulation, and mediating between factions).

The two threads eventually connected. The ley-line instability caused by Salvatore's revived ghost was being manipulated by agents within the political factions, and artifacts recovered from the ruins were being used to fuel the conflict. The heroes ultimately laid Salvatore's ghost to rest, contained the worst of the Gauntlight's horrors, exposed the manipulators on both sides, and prevented civil war — though the margin of success was narrow.

### Chumbawamba Today (842 AW)

Four years after the crisis, Chumbawamba is healing but changed. The Gauntlight Ruins have been partially cleared and sealed, but deeper levels remain unexplored and potentially dangerous — the heroes contained the threat rather than eliminating it entirely, and scholars warn that the ley-line energies that revived Salvatore could produce other effects in the future.

The political landscape has stabilized but not healed. Clan Azure and the Children of Pyre reached a negotiated compromise under pressure from the crisis, but underlying disagreements persist. Xogim Flintsweeper and Bombus Clockmort were exposed and dealt with, but the factions they represented still exist. The city's governance — formerly a unified town assembly — has fractured into a council with formal faction representation, a structure that functions but lacks the communal trust that gnomish governance depends on.

Chumbawamba's experience has resonated across the Gnomish Realms. The revelation that ley-line energies can revive necromantic horrors has forced gnomes to confront the unintended consequences of the magical renaissance sweeping Mazariim. The political crisis has demonstrated that gnomish society is not immune to the factional conflicts that plague human nations — a sobering realization for a people who prided themselves on their philosophical maturity.

### Key Locations

**The Gauntlight Ruins (Sealed)** — The underground complex of laboratories created by Salvatore Togglesprocket, now partially cleared and sealed. The upper levels have been sanitized and converted into a cautionary exhibit maintained by the Church of Alagalothor. The middle levels — Salvatore's experimental chambers, filled with the remnants of his horrific creations — have been sealed with reinforced wards. The lower levels, where reality itself warped under sustained necromantic abuse, remain unexplored and are considered extremely dangerous. Access is strictly controlled, with both magical and mechanical security measures guarding every entrance.

**The Gear Assembly** — Chumbawamba's main government building, a large circular structure where the fractured council meets. The building's name dates from the city's founding, when the original town assembly operated with the smooth precision of a well-maintained clockwork mechanism. The name now carries an ironic edge.

**The Steam Works** — The city's industrial quarter, where workshops produce the manufactured goods that form the backbone of Chumbawamba's economy. The Steam Works were damaged during the political crisis (arson by extremists on both sides) and have been rebuilt, though production has not fully recovered.

**The Azure Hall** — Headquarters of Clan Azure, a traditional gnomish clan hall featuring a library of arcane texts, a clockwork exhibition, and meeting rooms where the faction's moderate leadership plans its political strategy.

**The Pyre Forge** — The Children of Pyre's meeting hall and experimental workshop, built around a large fire-magic forge. The facility has been significantly scaled back since the crisis, with the more dangerous experimental equipment removed or sealed, but it remains a center of fire-based industrial research under closer community oversight.

### Key NPCs

- **Councilor Pipwick Gearspark** (NG male Durazgrom Artificer) — The moderate leader who holds the fractured council together through sheer force of personality and a gift for finding compromise positions. Pipwick lost his workshop to arsonists during the crisis and has channeled his anger into a determination to prevent another such breakdown.
- **Warden Tizzle Brightspark** (LN female Rothgrom Wizard) — The official charged with maintaining the seals on the Gauntlight Ruins and monitoring the ley-line energies that flow through the area. A former student of the very wizard schools that produced Salvatore, Tizzle takes her responsibility with grim seriousness and has developed specialized detection spells for identifying necromantic activity.

## GNOMISH REALMS ORGANIZATIONS

### The Seekers of Rivoleen

The Seekers (Gendor'vol in Gnomish) are Rivoleen's clergy and the most influential organized group in gnomish society. Unlike hierarchical priesthoods, the Seekers operate with great autonomy — each follows their own path, and there is no central authority dictating doctrine or commanding obedience. Regional gatherings occur several times a year for philosophical debates and the sharing of ideas, but these are meetings of equals.

The Seekers serve as philosophers, teachers, counselors, and advocates for freedom in every gnomish settlement. They maintain the temples — which function as community centers for learning, debate, and invention — and they train new Seekers through a combination of philosophical study, practical apprenticeship, and the uniquely gnomish tradition of the Question Journal. Every follower of Rivoleen keeps a Question Journal, recording observations, questions, and philosophical musings. The most profound Question Journals are preserved in temple libraries and studied like sacred texts.

The Seekers' influence extends beyond religion into every aspect of gnomish life. They mediate disputes, advise community leaders, preserve knowledge, and — when necessary — organize the defense of gnomish communities against external threats. During the Chumbawamba crisis, local Seekers played a crucial role in preventing the political conflict from escalating into full civil war, using their moral authority to bring both factions to the negotiating table.

### The Gearwright Guilds

While Gyru is a dwarven deity, his Gearwright guilds have found fertile ground among gnomes drawn to his philosophy of innovation through artifice. Gnomish Gearwrights tend to focus on the practical rather than the theological — they are interested in Gyru's techniques for combining magic and machinery, his approach to experimental design, and his legacy of pushing the boundaries of what is possible, rather than in the broader question of mortal ascension that preoccupies dwarven Gearwrights.

The gnomish Gearwright guilds operate workshop-temples in Zhonhylite, Chumbawamba, and several smaller settlements. They set standards for artificers, certify inventions, fund research, and serve as professional networks for gnomish engineers and inventors. The guilds also maintain connections with dwarven Gearwright chapters in the Dragon Spine, facilitating the cross-pollination of ideas and techniques that has driven much of the magitech innovation in recent centuries.

### The Dirigible Corps

Gnomish aviation is still in its relative infancy, but the Dirigible Corps — a loose association of pilots, engineers, and navigators — represents one of the most exciting frontiers of gnomish technology. The Corps operates a growing fleet of dirigibles and hot-air balloons from the Dirigible Fields in Zhonhylite and smaller launch facilities in other settlements.

Dirigibles serve multiple purposes in gnomish society. They provide rapid transport between settlements, carry trade goods to and from dwarven and human markets, conduct aerial surveys and mapping of the Yazawee Mountains, and — during the giant raids that periodically threaten the Gnomish Realms — serve as observation platforms and (experimentally) weapons delivery systems. The Corps is always seeking new pilots, engineers, and navigators, and the prospect of flight draws adventurous gnomes from across the realms.

### The Gem-Cutters Guild

Gnomish gem-cutting is the finest in the world, and the Gem-Cutters Guild — headquartered in Zhonhylite's Gem Quarter — is the professional organization that maintains this standard of excellence. Guild members undergo years of training to develop the precision, patience, and aesthetic judgment that gnomish gem-cutting requires, and membership in the Guild is considered one of the highest professional achievements a gnome can attain.

The Guild controls quality standards for gnomish gems, certifies cut stones, and manages trade relationships with gem merchants across Nigal. Gnomish-cut gems carry the Guild's certification mark — a tiny symbol invisible to most non-gnomish eyes — that guarantees their quality and provenance. The Guild's certification is so respected that stones bearing it command significant premiums in every market.

### The Defenders of the Green

A loose network of Rothgrom druids, rangers, and nature-priests who protect the forests and natural areas of the Yazawee Mountains. Many of these defenders worship Minerva alongside Rivoleen, blending elven nature-reverence with gnomish philosophical inquiry. Their animal companions — squirrel scouts, badger patrols, hawk messengers — provide an early warning network that supplements the mechanical defenses of gnomish settlements.

The Defenders played a crucial role during the War to End All Wars, using the forests themselves as weapons against orc invaders. In peacetime, they manage wildlife, prevent overexploitation of forest resources, and maintain the natural barriers (dense forest, unstable terrain, wildlife corridors) that help protect gnomish settlements from external threats. Their relationship with Durazgrom engineers is sometimes tense — the Defenders view excessive industrial development as a threat to the natural environment, while engineers view the Defenders' protectiveness as an obstacle to progress.

## GEOGRAPHICAL FEATURES

### The Yazawee Mountains

The Yazawee Mountains are a distinct range separated from the Dragon Spine by lower hills and the broad grasslands of the Horselands' eastern fringe. Lower and greener than the Dragon Spine, the Yazawee peaks are heavily forested at lower elevations, with alpine meadows, rocky ridgelines, and snow-capped summits above the treeline. The range is geologically complex, with volcanic activity in the southeast (near Dammarth), extensive cave systems throughout, and gem-bearing formations that have sustained gnomish mining for millennia.

The mountains' ecology is rich and varied. Dense coniferous forests cover the lower slopes, giving way to deciduous woodlands in the valleys and alpine tundra at higher elevations. Wildlife is abundant — deer, bear, mountain goats, eagles, and a variety of smaller animals that the Rothgrom have befriended and incorporated into their daily lives. Giant-kin (hill giants, stone giants, and the occasional frost giant descending from the highest peaks) represent the most dangerous natural predators, and their periodic raids are the Gnomish Realms' most persistent military threat.

The mountains contain extensive gem deposits — diamonds, rubies, sapphires, emeralds, and more exotic stones — that form the foundation of gnomish economic wealth. Mining operations, particularly those run by Durazgrom communities, extend deep into the mountains, though gnomish mining practices (influenced by both Dirgin's ancient traditions and Rivoleen's emphasis on sustainability) tend to be more conservative than the aggressive extraction practiced in some dwarven holds.

### The Forests of the Rothgrom

The forested valleys and lower slopes of the Yazawee Mountains are home to the Rothgrom communities — surface-dwelling gnomes who live in half-buried homes, treehouses, and settlements that blend seamlessly into the natural landscape. These forests are managed rather than wild, with Rothgrom druids and the Defenders of the Green maintaining a careful balance between gnomish habitation and natural ecology.

Rothgrom settlements are difficult for outsiders to find. Homes are built into hillsides with sod roofs, nestled among tree roots, or suspended in the canopy of tall trees. Paths wind through the forest in patterns that seem random but actually follow efficient routes between communities. Animal allies — trained squirrels, badgers, hawks, and other creatures — serve as scouts, messengers, and occasionally as plowbeasts (badger-powered plows are a genuine Rothgrom innovation).

The forests also serve as a defensive barrier. During the War, Rothgrom druids channeled the forest itself against orc invaders — collapsing trails, directing wildlife to harass supply columns, and using plant growth to obscure and entrap advancing enemies. The same techniques are employed against giant raids in peacetime, making the Rothgrom forests far more dangerous for invaders than their peaceful appearance suggests.

### The Deep Mines

Beneath the Yazawee Mountains, Durazgrom mining operations extend to considerable depths. These mines produce the gems that form the backbone of gnomish economic wealth, along with iron, copper, tin, and small quantities of more exotic metals. The deepest mines approach the geological strata where rare and unusual minerals form under extreme pressure and temperature — materials that gnomish jewelers and artificers prize for their unique properties.

Mining in the Gnomish Realms follows practices influenced by both Dirgin's ancient traditions (respect the depths, take only what is needed, seal what should not be disturbed) and Rivoleen's emphasis on sustainability and shared knowledge. Gnomish mines tend to be smaller, more carefully managed, and longer-lasting than the aggressive extraction operations found in some dwarven holds. Mining techniques are documented and shared openly, and safety innovations developed in one mine are quickly adopted throughout the realms.

The deep mines are also where the most ancient gnomish history lies buried. Tunnels dating back to the earliest days of gnomish civilization — carved with primitive tools and bearing inscriptions in archaic Rhungnom — can still be found in the oldest mining complexes. Some of these ancient workings connect to natural cave systems that extend beyond the boundaries of gnomish exploration, into the dark places where Dirgin's presence is said to linger.

### The Geothermal Zones

The southeastern Yazawee Mountains, near Dammarth, are geologically active — hot springs, volcanic vents, geysers, and fumaroles create landscapes unlike anything else in the Gnomish Realms. These geothermal features provide natural heating for nearby settlements, power sources for steam engines, and concentrations of elemental energy that Dammarth's mages study and harness.

The geothermal zones are also dangerous. Sudden eruptions of superheated water or toxic gases can kill the unwary. The ground in some areas is unstable, with thin crusts over boiling mud pools. Volcanic activity, though minor by continental standards, produces occasional earthquakes and ashfalls that can disrupt life in nearby communities.

Despite the hazards, the geothermal zones are valued for their resources and their beauty. Hot springs provide bathing and healing waters prized across the Gnomish Realms. Mineral deposits formed by geothermal processes include rare crystals and metals found nowhere else. And the spectacular scenery — steaming vents wreathed in mist, pools of brilliantly colored water, geysers erupting on predictable schedules — draws visitors from across the mountains.

## TRADE AND ECONOMICS

### Gnomish Exports

Gnomish economic power rests on precision, innovation, and irreplaceability. The goods that gnomish artisans produce — clockwork mechanisms, steam engines, cut gems, lenses, alchemical products, and mechanical devices of every description — are not merely high quality but fundamentally unique. Other races can produce crude approximations of gnomish technology, but they cannot match gnomish precision, reliability, or ingenuity. A steam engine built by a human engineer costs several times more than one built by a gnome and fails several times more often. A gem cut by a dwarven jeweler is excellent; a gem cut by a gnomish jeweler is perfect.

This irreplaceability gives gnomes enormous economic leverage despite their small population and remote location. Gnomish goods command premium prices in every market on Nigal, and the demand for gnomish engineering expertise far exceeds the supply. The Federation of Man, the Dwarven Empire, and the Merchant Nations all maintain trade relationships with the Gnomish Realms, and gnomish-made products are among the most valuable trade goods on the continent.

Steam engines and clockwork mechanisms are the most strategically important gnomish exports. These technologies have transformed industry in the Inner Kingdoms and the Dwarven Empire, powering mines, mills, forges, and transportation systems. Gnomes share their technology freely — consistent with Rivoleen's teaching that knowledge should not be hoarded — but the practical reality is that only gnomish craftsmen can consistently produce devices that work reliably. This creates a permanent demand for gnomish engineering services.

Gems represent the largest single category of gnomish trade by value. The Yazawee Mountains produce stones of exceptional quality, and gnomish cutting transforms raw gems into finished products that other jewelers cannot match. The Gem-Cutters Guild's certification mark is recognized across Nigal as a guarantee of perfection.

### Gnomish Imports

The Gnomish Realms are largely self-sufficient in food (terraced farms and forest gardens produce adequate supplies), raw materials (the mountains provide metals, gems, and timber), and manufactured goods (gnomish workshops produce everything the population needs). Imports tend to be goods that the mountains cannot produce: textiles and fabrics, exotic foods and spices, books and scholarly works from human universities, artistic works from other cultures, and — increasingly — magical components and ley-line research from the Astral Circle and other organizations studying the magical renaissance.

Information is perhaps the most valuable import. Gnomish curiosity is insatiable, and news, scholarly works, and accounts of discoveries from the wider world are eagerly consumed. Trade caravans arriving in gnomish settlements find that their stories and news are valued as highly as their physical goods.
