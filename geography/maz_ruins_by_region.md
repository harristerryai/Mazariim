# Ruins of Mazariim — Organized by Region

**Compiled from:** maz_ruins_akovar.md, maz_ruins_nigal.md, maz_ruins_shalazar.md, maz_ruins_vandardhon.md
**As of 842 AW**

## AKOVAR

### Eastern Coast

- **Amaunet** *(The Sacred Precinct / The Sunken Capital)* — The capital city and grand temple complex of Akovarian civilization, built at the hub of the continental ley-line network and housing the infrastructure that managed it across six thousand years. The site was annihilated by the 6,830 BW detonation and has never been excavated; it is currently controlled by Kharazzax's Red Warden dragon clan and guarded by undead sentinels and a resident lich. It represents the single most important unexcavated Akovarian site in existence, if it could ever be reached.

- **Temple of the Seventh Convergence** *(The Convergence / The Flow Chamber)* — A ley-line nexus management facility built at a major convergence point, featuring a central pyramid, administrative structures, and underground archive vaults containing the Flow Chamber—a sophisticated network regulation system. The site was damaged by the detonation and has been partially reactivated since 815 AW, with certain sections still containing mysterious stasis fields that resist investigation. It is unclaimed but patrolled by nearby metallic dragon clans.

### Southeastern Lowlands

- **Iseueri** *(The Swamp Archive / The Drowned Library)* — A centralized archive and research facility built around 8,000 BW to preserve copies of documents from across the southern network, now half-submerged in warm stagnant water following geological subsidence after the detonation. Upper structures have collapsed but lower archive vaults may be intact beneath the waterline; the site is actively contested between hostile black dragons and preservation-minded bronze dragons. Since 815 AW, faint golden luminescence from the submerged vaults indicates ley-line reactivation.

### Interior Highlands

- **Mibampes** *(The Planar Breach / The Screaming Ruin)* — A former Akovarian settlement of unknown original function that became a catastrophic wound in reality after the ley-line detonation, where the boundary between the material plane and multiple extraplanar dimensions has nearly ceased to function. The ruins are surrounded by the most intense wild magic zone on the continent—roughly three miles in diameter—with unstable portals constantly depositing aberrations, beholders, and Far Realm entities. The planar instability has intensified since 815 AW and the site is classified Priority One by the Astral Circle.

### Spine of Fire

- **Ptei** *(The Mountain Repository / The High Archive)* — A high-altitude knowledge repository containing one of the most comprehensive surviving archives of Akovarian civilization, including engineering texts, religious records, astronomical observations, and administrative records spanning roughly 9,500 BW to 6,830 BW. The site is guarded by a coalition of silver and white dragons led by Ithilmere, with sealed lower vaults whose contents remain unknown. Since 815 AW, the repository's enchantments have grown increasingly active, suggesting successful integration with the restored ley-line network.

- **Volcanic Ruins of the Spine of Fire** *(Multiple preserved sites)* — Akovarian ruins exposed, buried, or preserved by volcanic activity over seven millennia, with some structures sealed in anaerobic conditions by lava flows—producing preservation similar to historical volcanic entombment. The volcanic environment enhances fire magic, creates a thin boundary with the Plane of Fire, and produces unpredictable results for earth-based spells. These sites are claimed by white and silver dragon clans and are extraordinarily difficult to access due to altitude, unstable terrain, and toxic gases.

### Southern Coast

- **Shattered Coast Ruins** *(Various submerged and scattered sites)* — The remains of coastal settlements physically torn apart by the ley-line detonation and redistributed across a maze of inlets, sea caves, and rocky islets; fragments of Akovarian construction are visible in cliff faces, cave walls, and the ocean floor. The region holds the highest concentration of wild magic zones and active portals on the continent, and dragonborn oral traditions reference drowned temples and crystallized mana formations. Claimed by hostile black and green dragon clans, and considered the least-explored region on the continent.

### Scattered / Archipelago

- **Subsidiary Pyramid Sites** *(Dozens of locations)* — Dozens of smaller pyramid structures (typically 20–50 feet tall) that managed tertiary ley-line nodes, served as regional administrative centers, or provided logistical support for major cities. Less individually significant than the great temples, but collectively essential for reconstructing the network's continental geometry. Since 815 AW, all subsidiary pyramids exhibit faint golden activation glows, with intensity varying by proximity to restored ley-line channels.

- **Dragonborn Heritage Sites** *(Clan-specific locations)* — Akovarian ruins adopted by dragonborn clans as ceremonial and ancestral territory, in many cases modified, repurposed, or partially dismantled over the intervening millennia. Access depends entirely on individual clan discretion; the dragonborn oral traditions preserved alongside these sites contain information about original functions that physical remains alone would not reveal.

## VAND'ARDHON

### Tawarwaith Forests

- **The Seven Seats** — Seven ancient capitals of the Starwood Confederation (11,200–9,327 BW), built into living trees and featuring council halls, druidic sacred groves, and earthwork fortifications before political fragmentation brought the confederation down. The structures remain sound but are heavily overgrown and actively protected by territorial fey creatures and druids who treat them as sacred historical repositories.

- **Thornheart Amphitheater** — A natural amphitheater with stone benches where the Thornheart Compromise was signed in 9,327 BW, ending the Starwood Wars; it remains maintained as sacred ground by druids. Hidden chambers beneath it house original treaty documents in mithril cases and maps of lost Starwood territories. Its standing stone shows signs of ancient magic and continues to attract scholarly and religious interest.

- **Expansionist Border Camps** *(Three sites)* — Three fortified villages established by departing clans before they integrated into Farondrim wanderer culture around 9,300 BW, now heavily overgrown with collapsed wooden structures and remaining stone foundations. They contain military equipment, boundary markers, and journals explaining the exodus; archaeological evidence may clarify whether the departure was peaceful or forced.

- **Galadhon Hold** — Fortified clan hall of one of the three expansionist clans that departed the Starwood Confederation around 9,327 BW, housing 400–600 elves before abandonment. Contains clan records, military equipment, and residential areas; evidence suggests the clan either intended to return or was forced out, making it significant for understanding the confederation's collapse.

- **Silverroot Hall** — Former clan headquarters of the Starwood Confederation's expansionist faction, preserving records explaining the departure decision, military equipment, personal belongings, and migration route maps. Archaeological evidence raises questions about whether the departure was a peaceful choice or a forced exile.

- **Ravencrest Summit** — Third major clan seat abandoned during the Starwood Confederation's fragmentation, housing 400–600 elves before abandonment. One hall shows clear evidence of internal conflict over the decision to leave; hidden caches suggest the clans expected eventually to return.

- **The Silent Villages** *(Seven sites)* — Seven villages totaling roughly 1,400 elves that vanished overnight in the spring of 5,600 BW in a single inexplicable event, leaving meals on tables, tools in workshops, and children's toys in streets. The ruins are permeated with temporal anomalies where time speeds up or slows unpredictably; visitors experience visions of the missing villagers going about their lives and face a compulsion to "join the village," with some visitors vanishing exactly as the original residents did.

- **The Bloodtear Villages** *(Twenty-seven sites)* — Twenty-seven coastal and riverside villages devastated by the Bloodtear Plague in 9,100 BW, killing 8,000–10,000 elves over seven years before quarantine and burning halted the spread. The blackened foundations and overgrown cemeteries contain medical texts, quarantine records, and religious artifacts from failed healing rituals; whether some villages were burned before everyone had died—mercy killing or panic—remains unresolved.

### Northern Mountains

- **Brotherhood of the Black Flame Fortress** *(The Obsidian Tower / The Deathspire)* — A seven-story tower of black volcanic glass with five underground levels, serving as a necromantic death cult stronghold for approximately 150 years before being destroyed during a holy war around 10,200 BW. Though sealed with divine barriers, it remains one of the most dangerous pre-Enlightenment sites on the continent, rumored to contain a dormant archlich on its lowest level still waiting for the seals to weaken.

- **The Bone Fields** — The barren wasteland battlefield surrounding the Obsidian Tower, where thousands died in the final holy assault on the Brotherhood of the Black Flame. Over 10,000 years later, nothing grows here; the death-magic-saturated ground spontaneously generates undead on certain nights and the whispers from the earth drive travelers to madness.

- **Deepglimmer Shaft** — A major mithril mine operating for 600 years (5,800–5,200 BW) with twelve levels extending over 2,000 feet deep, abandoned when ore quality allegedly declined. Upper levels are partially collapsed and accessible; lower levels are flooded or caved in, and miners' bodies remain in sealed sections with evidence that something in the deep levels forced abandonment before the vein actually exhausted.

### Western and Southern Coast — The Drowning Cities

- **Tirithorn** *(Watch of the Tides)* — A major coastal city renowned for tide prediction and weather forecasting, destroyed in a catastrophic flooding event around 4,800 BW and now submerged 20–40 feet underwater. It preserves tide prediction mechanisms, magically preserved waterlogged texts, navigation tools, and shrines to Iquinias; sealed vaults allegedly contain tide charts that predicted the disaster and evidence that city leaders were warned and did nothing.

- **Maeglin's Rest** *(Place of Sharp Waters)* — A whaling town and fishing port of roughly 800 elves, destroyed in the same flooding catastrophe as Tirithorn and now partially above water at low tide. Contains whaling gear, trade records, and an intact shrine to Vuleala; a mass grave of 600+ elves and evidence of last-minute sacrificial rituals suggest the disaster's true nature may be darker than simple geological misfortune.

- **Silverlake Landing** *(Fallen Harbor)* — A major merchant port connecting inland trade routes to the sea, completely submerged 30–50 feet during the 4,800 BW flooding event. Preserves merchant warehouses, a customs house with pre-Enlightenment trade records, and sealed bank vaults containing significant wealth; evidence of corruption suggests city officials accepted bribes to stay silent about the coming disaster.

- **Falathrim** *(Song of Waves)* — An artistic community and resort town of roughly 900 elves, submerged 15–25 feet during the 4,800 BW catastrophe, with the quality of its construction leaving it relatively well preserved underwater. Its performance hall with perfect acoustics still hosts ghostly underwater concerts by the drowned; hidden caches of the wealthiest residents suggest treasure was sealed away and never recovered.

### Across Vand'ardhon

- **The Hollow Brotherhood Stronghold** — A necromantic fortress operated for 70 years (1,982–1,912 BW) by approximately 300 necromancers and hundreds of undead servants under the Archlich Morthain, a corrupted Minervan cleric, before being destroyed during the Purge of Shadows. Sealed lower levels remain unexplored and extraordinarily dangerous; rumors suggest Morthain's phylactery was not fully destroyed, with a fragment remaining in the deepest vault.

- **Purge Battlefields & Fortified Compounds** *(12–15 sites)* — A dozen or more sites where cultist forces made their final stands during the 1,912 BW Purge of Shadows, resulting in approximately 2,000 elven soldiers killed alongside 300 cultists. Burned compounds, battlefield graves, and memorial stones preserve blessed weapons, crusader armor, and evidence suggesting some sites may have been massacres of captured cultists rather than battle deaths; hidden caches suggest the cult planned for future resurgence.

### Deep Ocean

- **Naval Disaster Site** — The underwater battlefield where three-quarters of Gaearman's fleet was destroyed in 2 BW when Zuel's daemon allies ambushed elven forces at sea, killing thousands of sailors. Scattered at depths of 200–1,000+ feet, the site is spiritually tainted with lingering daemonic corruption, warped sea creatures, trapped sailor spirits, and bound daemonic entities; unresolved questions remain about how the daemons knew the fleet's location and why some ships show evidence of fighting each other.

## NIGAL

### Inner Kingdoms

- **The Athenaeum of Echoes** *(Eastern Beneshire, 90 miles northeast of Wyebluff)* — A Yrugas-era magical research center founded around 3,600 BW and dedicated to studying anomalous magical phenomena, destroyed during the 2,700 BW slave revolt but with its underground Conjunction Archive preserved behind vault doors. The site has been partially reactivated since 815 AW; deeper chambers contain temporal echoes and shadow undead remnants owing to the ley-line nexus beneath it.

- **The Geometrist's Tower** *(Eastern Beneshire, near the Arogror border)* — A twisted seven-story tower built around 2,850 BW by the wizard Vorthax at the intersection of five major ley-line scars, serving as a research facility where he studied ley-line mechanics and discovered a dimensional portal to Akovar. Vorthax was transformed into a greater wraith during Yrugas's fall and remains bound to the nexus, his consciousness spread across the ley-line scars for 3,500 years.

- **The Hollow Sepulcher** *(Deep interior of the Madronian Peninsula)* — The most dangerous ruin on Nigal: a permanent breach between the material world and the Negative Energy Plane created when millions died during Yrugas's collapse around 2,700 BW. Multiple levels descend from surface ruins through catacombs of fused bones to a lowest "Breach" maintained by Void-Touched wardens; the Federation maintains Fort Ashward three miles away to prevent unauthorized access.

- **The Madron Crater** *(Five miles east of the Hollow Sepulcher)* — A half-mile diameter crater created by Yrugas's final magical detonation, with glassy black walls veined with crystallized mana; at its center stands a 60-foot spire of fused glass shaped like a robed figure, possibly a mana scar of the wizard-king Madron himself. Since 815 AW, the veins glow at night and the spire hums with apparent awareness.

- **The Shattered Towers of Calthene** *(Northern Beneshire, 40 miles northeast of Wyebluff)* — Seven tower foundations in a heptagonal pattern, remnants of a Yrugas-era magical research complex destroyed by internal explosions during the collapse. Phantom tower manifestations appear as columns of blue-white light lasting seconds to several minutes, increasing in frequency since 815 AW.

- **Fort Ashward Undercroft** *(Three miles from the Hollow Sepulcher)* — A Federation fort built on pre-War foundations that descend to Yrugas-era stonework inscribed with directions pointing toward Madron, the Hollow Sepulcher, and unknown locations. The lowest level was sealed in 798 AW after reports of voices in the walls; the sealed door has been warm to the touch since 815 AW.

- **The Weeping Stones of Anheath** *(Countryside south of Anheath, Beneshire)* — Twelve standing stones in a barley field of pre-Yrugas origin with spiral carvings that channel water in patterns resembling tears during heavy rain. Since 815 AW the stones hum during thunderstorms with ley-line resonance patterns and cause mild nausea in those standing within the circle.

- **The Haunted Keeps** *(Countryside surrounding Fort Hope)* — A series of ancient fortifications reoccupied and corrupted by elemental cults during the 819 AW Elemental Crisis, each warped by a different element—volcanic glass, perpetual gale-force winds, flooded chambers, or earthquake instability. The keeps were cleared but remain magically contaminated; some contain disrupted teleportation circles that once connected to Tyar-Zaruk.

- **Tyar-Zaruk** *(Deep underground beneath the Inner Kingdoms)* — An ancient underground complex of pre-War or unknown origin, used as the elemental cults' headquarters during the 819 AW crisis. The massive chambers with oversized proportions suggest builders of non-human scale; the structure extends far beyond explored areas with sealed corridors and deeper unexplored levels, and the Astral Circle has classified it as requiring formal expedition before further access.

- **The Yrugas Rift Site** *(Deep beneath the Madronian countryside)* — Previously uncharted Yrugas ruins where planar research had left the boundary between dimensions dangerously thin; the Far Realm entity Ry'le exploited this weakness in 824 AW to open a rift through which Ry'lehe aberrations infiltrated Mazariim. The site has been sealed and warded by the Astral Circle with monitoring equipment in place.

- **The Beneshire Ley-Line** *(Beneshire, cross-regional)* — The first mortal-created ley-line since Akovar's destruction in 6,830 BW, successfully restarted in 824 AW and formalized by 832 AW, proving mortals can create ley-lines without direct divine involvement. Its activation drew multiple competing factions—the Library of the Ages, the Astral Circle, the Umbral Hand, the Dark Whispers, and the Nergalu Dragons—each seeking to control, protect, corrupt, or study it.

### Merchant Nations

- **The Ziggurats of the Jinook Valley** *(Jinook Valley and Taskaun Peninsula)* — Between nine and fourteen surviving Dukhan Empire ziggurats of dark basalt (40–80 feet tall) built for sacrificial rituals to Zebulba and arranged in a ley-line grid; they overloaded and discharged catastrophically in 213 BW when Soros un-Kahn's ritual failed, killing everything nearby. Strongly cursed with negative energy, surrounded by dead zones where nothing grows, and infested with dangerous Dukhan Remnant undead in sealed interior chambers; their influence has been expanding 10–15 feet per year since 815 AW.

- **The Simir Bastions** *(Jinook Valley and hinterlands between Amara and Asim)* — Approximately eight squat limestone fortifications built 291–686 AW by the Empire of Simir to project military power and control trade routes. Most have been stripped of valuables; the largest (the Iron Fist) retains its original iron-banded gates and a flooded dungeon level with alleged tunnels connecting to Asim.

- **The Plague Pits of the Taskaun Road** *(Along the road connecting Jinook Valley to the Taskaun Peninsula)* — Mass graves marking the path of a plague that devastated the Dukhan Empire's western territories, visible as long depressions where no scrub grass grows. Livestock sicken near them and travelers report nightmares; the Church of Tiovin periodically blesses the pits, but no undead have yet emerged.

- **The Glass Garden of Instar** *(Hills north of Instar)* — A field of approximately 200 freestanding glass columns (3–8 feet tall) arranged without any discernible pattern, made of crystallized magical energy similar to mana veins found at the Madron Crater. The site produces a faint chiming sound and a sense of calm; since 815 AW the columns glow faintly pale green at dusk for about an hour.

- **The Drowned Temple of Cho** *(Mouth of the River Cho, Amara's harbor)* — A pre-Dukhan temple dedicated to Iquinias, submerged 15 feet underwater when sea levels were higher, with wave carvings visible at extreme low tide and bronze implements and archaic clay tablets recovered by divers. The Church of Iquinias lacks funds for formal excavation; fishermen leave offerings and report reliably better catches in its vicinity.

- **Zanthar's Tower and the Orceri Breach Site** *(Steppes of Broken Stone, between Amara and Instar)* — A wizard's tower built around 310 BW by the fugitive Zanthar, collapsed after a prototype interdimensional device malfunctioned; the device was accidentally reactivated in 811 AW by occupying orcs, drawing the Orceri of Ta'Puh through a planar breach. The machine may have been destroyed in the process; the site remains unguarded in orc-infested territory with no formal post-incursion survey conducted.

### Dwarven Nations

- **Gaal Tarak** *(Kirak Mountains, southern Dragon Spine)* — A thriving Bryndawi dwarven citadel destroyed in 757 AW when a portal to the alternate reality Ta'Puh was discovered in its ruins during the Orceri Incursion; the priest Aleron Windstrider sealed the portal at the cost of his life, causing temporal backlash that retroactively collapsed the entire citadel. The collapse killed thousands and created refugees who harbor deep resentment; recent excavation attempts face an inexplicable wrongness that intensifies nearer to the citadel's estimated location.

- **The Fallen Hold of Karak Vorn** *(Northern Dragon Spine, 80 miles south of Chambers of Slate)* — A major dwarven city of 15,000 destroyed during the War when orc sappers detonated foundation charges, collapsing the mountain peak above it. Upper levels now serve as a somber memorial, middle levels see dangerous mining operations, and the lower levels are inhabited by hundreds of orc descendants who pose a limited but real threat.

- **The Singing Shafts of Thoral Deeping** *(Central Dragon Spine)* — Abandoned mine shafts worked across three separate eras from which a sustained, never-ceasing harmonic tone emanates from the deepest tunnels; the sound's origin remains completely elusive. Since 815 AW the pitch has dropped, volume increased, and the sound occasionally forms syllable-like patterns in no known language; the deepest access points are now sealed.

- **The Ancestor Tombs of the Southern Passes** *(Southern Dragon Spine passes and valleys)* — Dozens of pre-War dwarven burial chambers carved into cliff faces, ranging from simple single rooms to multi-room complexes with mechanical locks and protective traps; many were looted during the War. The Bryndawi tattoo-keepers believe the oldest tombs contain foundational designs for their tattoo magic tradition.

- **The Graniteshield Vaults** *(Beneath Graniteshield's commercial district)* — A previously unknown pre-War vault discovered in 839 AW through ley-line resonance that contained a fragment of the legendary Machine of Gyru—an artifact created during Gyru's 1,111 BW apotheosis. The fragment was recovered by heroes; the vault remains sealed as the Astral Circle studies its construction and searches for the Machine's remaining component parts.

### Gnomish Realms

- **The Gauntlight Ruins** *(Beneath Chumbawamba)* — An underground complex built around 738 AW by Salvatore Togglesprocket for necromantic research that descended from legitimate inquiry into obsessive madness. Upper levels are sanitized and maintained as a cautionary exhibit; middle levels contain hybrid flesh-machine constructs and spirit-binding devices behind reinforced daily-checked wards; lower levels remain unexplored with spatial anomalies—rooms larger inside, looping corridors, physics anomalies. The 815 AW ley-line reawakening revived Togglesprocket's ghost and reanimated his experiments.

- **The Steamhowl Crater** *(Hills east of Zhonhylite)* — A circular 300-foot depression created around 620 AW when a high-pressure steam containment vessel exploded during a Durazgrom test, vaporizing the facility and its operators. The glassy scorched crater walls have since filled with perpetually warm rainwater; debris occasionally yields fragments allowing partial reconstruction of the experimental vessel.

- **Tinwhisle's Observatory** *(Ridgeline above Dammarth)* — A fire-damaged astronomical observatory built around 490 AW by Rothgrom inventor Pelara Tinwhisle, destroyed when interlocking crystal lenses focused sunlight onto its clockwork mechanism and ignited the structure. Tinwhisle survived, rebuilt with shielding, became an accomplished astronomer, and died in 551 AW; her descendants maintain the ruins as a family memorial.

### Horselands

- **The Stone Circles of the Eastern Steppe** *(Eastern Horselands, transitional zone between grassland and Dragon Spine foothills)* — Over 60 stone circles of varying sizes, predating Chak'ti settlement by millennia and possibly tens of millennia, with transported granite and basalt sourced from dozens to hundreds of miles away. The Chak'ti regard them as "places where the ancestors were before the ancestors." Since 815 AW, stones have shifted slightly and carved symbols glow faintly blue at dawn and dusk, suggesting they are ley-line markers built by a pre-Akovarian civilization that independently developed ley-line technology.

- **The Bone Road** *(Southern edge of the Horselands)* — A 12-mile stretch of terrain littered with ancient bleached bones arranged in parallel lines forming a 20-foot-wide road bordered by upright larger bones used as posts, with no cut marks or predator damage visible anywhere. The Chak'ti attribute the site to a great hunt that went catastrophically wrong; travelers report growing unease, a sensation of being watched, and shifting phantom hoofbeats.

### Frozen Isles

- **The Ice-Ruins of the Northern Islands** *(Grimhold and two nearby islands, within a day's sail of Nond)* — Ruins of blue-gray stone exposed by the 305 AW Glacier's Retreat, built with extraordinary precision and inscribed in an undeciphered language, with Grimhold being the largest complex featuring connected chambers, a broad avenue, and a central structure mostly still embedded in ice. The ruins generate internal warmth, strange sound behavior, and phosphorescent floor glow at night; since 815 AW, interior temperature has risen, the glow is visible in daylight, and light has been seen emanating from Grimhold miles away. A classified 836 AW Astral Circle assessment concluded the ruins predate all known Nigal civilizations and the floor patterns are consistent with ley-line focusing devices of extraordinary sophistication.

- **The Hearthstones** *(Several islands in the central Frozen Isles)* — Large flat stones (5–8 feet across, perpetually warm year-round) that the Nond have built settlements around for heating and food preservation. Their undersides show precision-cut stone and geometric inscriptions identical to the ice-ruins, appearing to be fragments of a larger system scattered across islands hundreds of miles apart.

### Ancient South

- **The Abandoned Pyramids of the Western Desert** *(Beyond the Varkonan Canal, arid interior)* — Over 30 pyramids of a settlement that thrived around 2,100 BW after the Canal's construction but was abandoned within centuries when the canal branch silted up; sealed tombs with intact contents remain in desiccating desert conditions. Dangers include degraded preservation magic with unpredictable effects, potential undead guardians, and rumors of a time-freezing preservation field. Since 815 AW the pyramids exhibit the same faint golden glow as Ra'khet's active Necropolis.

- **The Oasis of Whispers** *(Approximately two days northeast of Osymunhotep)* — A small oasis concealing an underground complex with a central pool of water that never evaporates or stagnates, its walls inscribed in archaic pre-Nepherites Varkonae hieroglyphics. Visitors reliably report audible whispered voices in an almost-comprehensible language from the walls or pool—isolated words like "remember," "light," and "home"—that cease the moment anyone leaves the chambers. Scholars theorize it may be a degraded memory archive of early Varkonan knowledge of Akovar.

- **The Proto-Entity Craters** *(Zonth, Khemset, Menot, Nakar, Anumata — northeastern Varkonan desert)* — Five impact sites created in 839 AW when five proto-entities crashed into the desert, leaving craters ranging from a quarter-mile to nearly a mile in diameter with fused glass interiors veined with active, pulsing elemental energy. Each crater has a distinct character (Zonth: mathematical precision; Khemset: hunger and acquisitiveness; Menot: color-shifting mana; Nakar: continuing geological fracture; Anumata: a harmonic tone). The Pharaonic court has declared them sacred and restricted, but enforcement is minimal and Yak-Men slavers have established camps near two sites.

### Mespoian Peninsula

- **Xochicallan — The City That Fed the Trees** *(Approximately 150 miles southeast of the Azala city-state)* — A major Azala city-state founded around 1,100 BW and abandoned around 400 BW when deforestation disrupted the watershed, pollination, and soil stability beyond recovery; the jungle has since completely reclaimed the site, with three temple-pyramids detectable only as anomalous rises in the canopy. Legends claim the priest-king sealed the innermost pyramid chambers before evacuation, potentially containing texts from the city's founding more than a millennium before its abandonment. Since 815 AW the pyramids glow faintly through the canopy during equinox periods; the Wyld-Bound druids discourage exploration through subtle environmental manipulation.

- **The Causeways of the Lowland Swamp** *(Swampy coastal lowlands, eastern shore)* — A network of raised stone causeways extending roughly 30 miles through mangrove forest, with expertly dressed stone, drainage, and graded surfaces that begin at the coast, run in parallel and intersecting routes, and terminate in open swamp without reaching any visible destination. Attributed to a pre-Azala culture—possibly Varkonan refugees or a separate group—the construction quality exceeds the earliest Azala capabilities; their original purpose remains completely unknown.

### Lands of Chaos

- **The Shadow-Touched Lands** *(Interior Lavantine Peninsula, approximately 50 square miles)* — The region where the Taovanian artifact the Devil Cube merged a portion of the peninsula with the Shadow Plane in 832 AW; the merger was eventually broken by heroes recovering three divine artifacts and destroying the fortress anchoring it, shattering the Cube. The landscape remains partially wrong—gray-green trees, darker soil, cold metallic-tasting streams, minimal wildlife—with residual Shadow Plane architectural remnants, hundreds of feet of black iron chains, and three locations of active planar thinning monitored by the Astral Circle.

- **The Krutan Ruins of Vel Morath** *(Hills between Kruta and Medan)* — A fortress-city built around 1,800 BW as a military staging ground, featuring a focusing tower designed to concentrate and direct mass-effect offensive spells from multiple simultaneous casters. Around 900 BW the tower destabilized during experimentation and discharged inward, destroying two-thirds of the complex in a detonation visible 100+ miles away; the surviving third remains magically contaminated with rapid metal corrosion and radiation-like effects from prolonged exposure. Krutan agents periodically visit under heavy magical protection to study the tower remains and potentially attempt reconstruction.

- **Crusader's Bane** *(Interior route of Tentigo's Third Crusade, 397 AW)* — The largest of a series of ruined Tentigo encampments marking the 397 AW crusade's advance and retreat, site of the engagement that halted forward progress. Earthwork fortifications, wooden palisade foundations, stone fire pits, and Tiovinian monuments remain undisturbed; a mass grave on the eastern edge shows no necromantic contamination, with the dead apparently resting peacefully.

### Lands of Honor

- **The Sunken Torii of Hama Bay** *(Waters off Great Island's eastern coast, Yashaki Islands)* — A shrine complex from the early Miyasaki period, now submerged beneath approximately 15 feet of water following earthquake-caused coastal shelf subsidence around 800 BW; its massive stone torii gates survived centuries of wave action due to their construction quality. A sealed inner chamber with active wards remains closed; the Miyasaki Shogunate forbids unauthorized exploration and enforces the ban through samurai water patrols.

### Cross-Regional

- **The Ley-Line Scars** *(Across all of Nigal)* — Dormant remnants of the Akovarian ley-line network destroyed in 6,830 BW, invisible channels that produce subtle effects on water flow, vegetation, and animal behavior along precise geometric paths. Since 815 AW these dormant scars have begun carrying active energy for the first time in over 7,600 years, creating hazards where structures occupy necessary flow paths; prolonged exposure causes ley-sickness—headaches, vertigo, compulsive spellcasting, and spells that hum after ending.

## SHALAZAR

- **The Smoke-Hollows** *(Eastern cliff coast)* — A chain of shallow caves with blackened ceilings and stone smoke-channels associated with the earliest Hin presence on the island, featuring soot-layer archaeology and fossilized primitive drying racks. Partially collapsed with some unstable sections, but contains migration-era artifacts including tools, trade beads, and ceremonial seed-stones.

- **The Guest-Right Cache** *(Inland ridge along an old quiet route)* — A hidden communal store site tied to the earliest Hin hospitality tradition, with stone-lined pantry and oath-scratch marks. Intact and sealed by careful stonework, it holds preserved containers, old oath tokens, and maps of early trade routes; its sanctity is sacred to Hin culture, making theft from it a serious cultural transgression.

- **Spring of the Seven Vessels** *(Central highlands)* — A sacred spring with carved channels feeding seven stone basins, featuring proto-Hin inscriptions and seasonal water level patterns interpreted as prophecy. The deeper chamber remains sealed with old elemental wards; the spring water can induce visions and punish oath-breakers.

- **The Broken Guesthouse** *(Along a river route between major villages)* — A burned hospitality house from around 2,900 BW tied to one of the earliest Field-Law test cases, preserving an oath-stone cracked in half and archaeological ash layers. Reopening investigation could shame families whose ancestors were involved, but may reveal whether the arson was local crime or deliberate foreign sabotage.

- **The Saltwind Wreck** *(Southern coast, near Southport)* — A wartime supply ship wreck from 2 BW scattered across a coastal debris field accessible at low tide, containing war-era trade bars, preserved medical kits, and a sealed dispatch tube alleged to name a traitor who redirected relief shipments during the war.

- **The Empty Rows** *(Near Luirennon's agricultural belt)* — A planned replacement hamlet founded around 5–40 AW to resettle families after war losses, later abandoned when recovery failed. Preserves neat foundation rows, unfinished irrigation channels, unclaimed heirlooms, and a co-op ledger showing missing grain that hints at organized theft during famine recovery.
