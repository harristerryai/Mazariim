# Geography of the Akovar Region: The Dragon Continent and the Forgotten Isles

**As of 842 AW**

---

## INTRODUCTION

Far from the continents of Nigal and Vand'ardhon, across thousands of miles of open ocean, the Akovar region encompasses the continent of Akovar itself and the scattered island chains that surround it — collectively known in most atlases as the Forgotten Isles. For the vast majority of recorded history, this region has been effectively unreachable by the civilizations of the wider world. Magical headwinds generated by the catastrophic ley-line detonation of 6,830 BW made normal ocean travel to Akovar's shores impossible, and the primitive technology of the continent's surviving inhabitants ensured that no contact was initiated from their side. For over seven thousand years, Akovar existed in total isolation — a continent haunted by the ruins of the most advanced human civilization in Mazariim's history, populated by dragonborn, dragons, and stranger things born from the magical cataclysm that destroyed it all.

That isolation ended in 815 AW. The reawakening of Asti and the restoration of Akovar's ley-line network disrupted the magical headwinds that had sealed the continent from the outside world. While the headwinds have not disappeared entirely — they remain treacherous and unpredictable, capable of driving unprepared vessels hundreds of miles off course — they are no longer the impenetrable barrier they once were. Skilled navigators with magical assistance can now reach Akovar's waters, and the explorer's outpost of New Sabonaa, located on a nearby island, has transformed from a modest colonial settlement into a vital staging ground for expeditions to the Dragon Continent.

The continent of Akovar is not large by the standards of Nigal or Vand'ardhon. The main landmass — a roughly crescent-shaped island approximately four hundred miles from tip to tip — is surrounded by dozens of smaller islands, rocky outcroppings, and submerged reefs that make coastal navigation hazardous even in calm waters. The interior is a patchwork of jungle, savanna, volcanic highlands, and coastal marshland, all of it suffused with magical energy that behaves unpredictably. Areas of wild magic — where spells produce random and sometimes spectacular effects — alternate with dead magic zones where no spell functions at all. Portals to other planes open and close without warning, depositing creatures from the Elemental Planes, the Feywild, the Abyss, and stranger dimensions into a landscape already teeming with dragons and dragonborn.

The climate is tropical and subtropical, with heavy monsoon rains during the wet season and scorching heat during the dry months. The volcanic highlands in the island's interior produce periodic eruptions that reshape the terrain and create new hazards. The coastline varies from white sand beaches to treacherous mangrove swamps to sheer cliffs of black volcanic rock. The waters surrounding the archipelago are warm, teeming with marine life — and with sea serpents, dragon turtles, and other predators that make fishing a dangerous profession.

The humans who once built the greatest civilization on Mazariim here are gone. Their pyramids stand in ruins, their cities are overgrown by jungle, their ley-line network — once the most sophisticated magical infrastructure ever created — was shattered and only recently restored. What remains are their descendants: the dragonborn, born from the magical merger of human and dragon blood during the ley-line detonation; the various cross-breed races created by the same catastrophe; and the dragons themselves — over a thousand of them by modern estimates, making Akovar the densest concentration of dragonkind anywhere on Mazariim. Some of these dragons remember the ancient civilization and guard its secrets jealously while others seek only territory and treasure.

Since 815 AW, Akovar has become the most significant frontier in Mazariim. Scholars from the Astral Circle, Line-Watchers of Asti, adventurers, treasure-seekers, and agents of various governments have begun trickling onto the continent through New Sabonaa, drawn by the promise of ancient knowledge, magical power, and the mysteries of a civilization lost for millennia. What they find is a land of extraordinary danger and extraordinary beauty — a place where the past is not buried but alive, pulsing with ley-line energy beneath the roots of ancient pyramids, guarded by creatures whose power dwarfs anything found on the tame roads of the Inner Kingdoms.

**Current Political Organization (842 AW)**

The Akovar region has no unified government and no formal political structure recognizable to outsiders. Power is distributed among:

- **Dragonborn Tribal Confederations** — Loose alliances of dragonborn clans occupying different regions of the continent. No central authority; leadership determined by strength, wisdom, and draconic heritage.
- **Dragon Territories** — Individual dragons or dragon clans that claim and defend specific regions, sometimes overlapping with dragonborn settlements in vassal-like arrangements. With over a thousand dragons inhabiting the archipelago, their territorial claims cover virtually every significant piece of land.
- **New Sabonaa** — A Sabonaar colonial outpost on a nearby island, founded by Naarlam and now jointly utilized by multiple Merchant Nations and Inner Kingdom powers.
- **Tortuganae** — A nonhuman archipelago refuge to the south, governed by a loose council of its diverse inhabitants.

Numerous independent settlements of dragonborn, genasi, tieflings, aasimar, and other cross-breed races exist across the islands, along with ancient ruins, active portals, and territories claimed by extraplanar creatures that have emerged from the continent's unstable magical geography.

**Total Population (842 AW, estimated)**

- Dragonborn: ~80,000 across the main continent and surrounding islands
- Dragons (all types): ~1,000–2,000 (estimated; precise counts impossible)
- Genasi (elemental cross-breeds): ~8,000
- Tieflings: ~3,000
- Aasimar: ~1,500
- Other cross-breed and exotic races: ~5,000
- Humans (New Sabonaa and scattered settlements): ~4,000
- Nonhumans (Tortuganae): ~12,000
- **Total: ~115,000–117,000**

## REGIONAL HISTORY

### The Akovarian Civilization (~13,000–6,830 BW)

The history of Akovar is the history of the most advanced civilization ever to exist on Mazariim — and its total destruction.

Approximately thirteen thousand years before the War to End All Wars, an entity called Asti — born from the cosmic collision that created the universe, formed around a white hole from which exotic particles spewed — turned its gaze upon the world of Mazariim. Taking female form, Asti observed the decay of death and the sorrow it caused among mortal creatures and wanted to go against the entropy, against the arrow of time. She sent her avatar to a small set of islands far from the bulk of the world — islands where seafaring people from a distant continent had settled, a group of fewer than a thousand humans suffering from the ruggedness of their new home.

Asti's appearance in a small valley was greeted with wonder and fear. Almost no entities had revealed themselves to the sentient races of Mazariim, and Asti's unspeaking, unmoving mass of energy and light hovered over a small clearing like a small sun that gave off no heat. The humans thought it a god and began to worship the silent avatar, erecting a small stone pyramid beneath it with a large temple complex. The nation of Akovar was born.

Over the following millennia, the Akovarians flourished under Asti's influence. Plagues faded. Lifespans lengthened past a hundred years. Healing magic emerged among the supplicants. The population surged as Asti's energies subtly accelerated human evolution, making the Akovarians smarter, stronger, and wiser over generations.

Around nine thousand years before the War, dragons took notice. Inquisitive metallic dragons began teaching Akovarians arcane magic alongside the divine magic Asti had fostered. The Akovarians learned the dragon language, the secrets of mana, and the fundamental principles by which magic interacted with the fabric of the universe. Arcane practitioners merged with the existing divine clergy, becoming indispensable to governmental and spiritual life.

By 8,200 BW, the Akovarians had completed massive stone pyramids at their capital and other cities, using their combined understanding of mathematics, engineering, astronomy, and arcane sorcery to energize the first ley-lines — rivers of magical energy stretching between the pyramid cities, powered by Asti's avatar. These ley-lines spread Asti's healing influence to the farthest reaches of the islands while enabling priests and magi to perform extraordinary feats of enhanced spellcasting and great rituals. The civilization reached its height around 7,000 BW, possessing knowledge and capabilities that rivaled or exceeded anything in the modern world.

### The Dragon War and the Fall (~6,900–6,830 BW)

Around 6,900 BW, dragons from across Mazariim began journeying to Akovar in growing numbers, drawn by the ley-line magic that creatures attuned to mana could feel across the globe. What began as scholarly interest turned to territorial aggression. Chromatic dragons, jealous of the metallic dragons' favored relationship with Asti, launched a coordinated campaign to destroy the pyramid temples and corrupt the ley-lines. Flights of dragons converged on the islands, battling both the Akovarians and their metallic dragon allies.

The clergy of Asti, foreseeing the danger, had prepared a desperate contingency. They created the Mask of the Pharaoh — an artifact of almost priceless jewels and exotic metals designed to hold Asti's essence if the Great Temple fell. During the final battle at the Great Temple in 6,830 BW, as powerful great wyrms assaulted the capital city of Amaunet, the arcanists and priests made their last stand. They transferred Asti's avatar — the entity's entire consciousness — into the Mask, then wove a massive transportation ritual that teleported the majority of the civilian population to a far-off, undetermined land.

The ley-lines, suddenly deprived of Asti's stabilizing presence, surged wildly and detonated. The magical explosion killed most of the attacking dragons, destroyed the capital and other major cities, and sent shockwaves of raw magical energy across the entire archipelago. One-third of the ley-line network was destroyed outright; the rest was corrupted or destabilized. The most advanced civilization in Mazariim's history was annihilated in a single day.

### The Aftermath: Mutation and Isolation

The ley-line detonation did not merely destroy — it transformed. The surging magical energy danced across the islands, ripping apart both dragon and human. A few survivors, however, were silently merged together. As some of the remaining humans bore children, they found many of them had dragon blood mixed with human blood — an impossibility of genetics, but the ever-present mana had rewritten the rules. The dragonborn began to populate the islands, competing with and eventually replacing the remaining human Akovarians. Other humans had been joined with different animals, creating various cross-breed races, many of which persist to this day.

The islands themselves were permanently altered. Large areas of wild magic and dead magic zones covered different portions of the continent and surrounding waters. Erratic magical damage created portals to otherworldly places that opened and closed unpredictably. Creatures from other dimensions — fiends, celestials, elementals — frequently emerged through these portals, leaving behind offspring in the local population and creating such rare races as the tiefling, aasimar, and elemental genasi.

The ley-line detonation also generated the magical headwinds that sealed Akovar from the rest of the world. These winds — not natural weather patterns but currents of residual magical force radiating outward from the shattered ley-line network — made normal ocean travel to the archipelago impossible. Ships attempting to approach were driven hundreds of miles off course, their compasses spinning, their sails filling with winds that came from impossible directions. For 7,645 years, this barrier held.

The transported Akovarians, meanwhile, arrived in the distant Ancient South, suffering near-total memory loss from the ritual's trauma. The high priest Thz'zar Ja'laao went mad from channeling the Mask's power. The survivors eventually founded the Varkonan culture, guarding the Mask of the Pharaoh as a sacred but poorly understood relic, and their Akovarian origins were forgotten for millennia.

### The Mad Pharaoh: Thz'zar Ja'laao

Not all of the clergy escaped through the transportation ritual. Thz'zar Ja'laao, the high priest who had led the ritual, found himself stranded on Akovar — whether by accident, by the ritual's imprecision, or by his own madness is unknown. The trauma of channeling the Mask's power and the ley-line detonation shattered his mind but did not diminish his magical ability. Over the following centuries, driven by obsession, grief, and insanity, Thz'zar used the necromantic energies saturating the ruined capital to transform himself into a lich — one of the most powerful undead beings in existence.

Thz'zar Ja'laao has existed for approximately 7,672 years as of 842 AW. He lairs in the deepest catacombs beneath Amaunet, the ruined capital, surrounded by undead servants created from the remains of Akovarians who died in the detonation. His mind, eroded by millennia of undeath and madness, oscillates between periods of lucid brilliance — during which he conducts magical research of extraordinary sophistication — and episodes of raving insanity during which he believes himself still the high priest of a living civilization, issuing orders to courtiers who have been dust for thousands of years. He is aware of the ley-line reawakening and regards it with a mixture of hope and fury — hope that Asti's return might restore what was lost, and fury that outsiders dare to touch what he considers his domain.

The lich is one of the most dangerous individual beings on the continent of Akovar. His magical knowledge spans the full breadth of Akovarian arcane and divine tradition, augmented by nearly eight millennia of undead existence. The red dragon clan that occupied Amaunet gave him wide berth; the dragonborn avoid the ruins of the capital entirely. Adventurers who have ventured into the catacombs have not returned. Whether Thz'zar Ja'laao could be reasoned with during his lucid periods — whether any fragment of the devoted priest who sacrificed everything to save his people still exists within the lich — is unknown. No one has survived long enough to find out.

### The Age of Dragons and Dragonborn (~6,830 BW–815 AW)

Over the thousands of years following the cataclysm, the dragonborn gradually became the dominant inhabitants of the archipelago. The remaining human Akovarians dwindled and eventually disappeared, absorbed into the dragonborn population or succumbing to the harsh conditions of the post-cataclysm landscape. The dragons — those that survived the ley-line detonation and those that arrived in subsequent centuries — established territories across the islands, sometimes ruling over dragonborn settlements as overlords, sometimes coexisting in arrangements that blended vassalage with mutual respect.

The dragons of Akovar multiplied over the millennia of isolation. With an entire continent to themselves, free from the organized resistance that mortal civilizations on Nigal could mount and rich in the ambient magical energy that sustains draconic life, the dragon population grew to numbers found nowhere else on Mazariim. By the modern era, an estimated one thousand to two thousand dragons of every type inhabit the archipelago — a concentration that would be unthinkable on Nigal, where centuries of conflict with humanoid civilizations have kept dragon populations comparatively low. The sheer density of dragonkind on Akovar shapes every aspect of life on the continent: dragonborn clans exist in the spaces between dragon territories, their settlements positioned to avoid provoking the powerful creatures that claim the best hunting grounds, the richest mineral deposits, and the most magically significant sites.

Dragonborn civilization on Akovar never approached the sophistication of the ancient Akovarian culture. Without Asti's guidance, without functioning ley-lines, and without the accumulated knowledge that had been teleported away with the refugees, the dragonborn developed tribal societies organized around clan structures tied to draconic heritage. A dragonborn clan's identity derives from its ancestral dragon type — red, blue, green, black, white, gold, silver, bronze, copper, or brass — though millennia of interbreeding have produced many individuals and clans of mixed heritage. The clan determines not merely social identity but magical affinity, physical characteristics, and the elemental breath weapon that every dragonborn inherits.

Dragon clans established territories that the dragonborn clans either served, avoided, or — in rare cases — challenged. The relationship between dragons and dragonborn is complex and varies enormously. Some dragons view dragonborn as lesser kin deserving of guidance and protection. Others regard them as convenient servants. A few treat them as prey. The dragonborn themselves hold similarly varied views — some revere their draconic ancestors with religious devotion, while others resent the domination of creatures that caused the cataclysm that destroyed the Akovarian civilization their forebears had built.

The continent also produced strange hybrids of dragon types — creatures born from the magical contamination of the detonation that blended characteristics of multiple draconic lineages in ways that do not occur naturally. These hybrid dragons are rare, powerful, and unpredictable, their temperaments and abilities defying the usual classifications.

Throughout these millennia, knowledge of the ancient Akovarian civilization was preserved only in fragments. Some dragonborn clans maintained oral traditions describing a "golden age" when the islands were united under a great power. Certain dragon elders — those old enough to have witnessed or heard firsthand accounts of the detonation — guarded Akovarian texts and artifacts in their hoards. But the understanding of what Akovar had been, and what had been lost, faded with each generation until the ancient civilization became legend, then myth, then barely-remembered folklore.

### The Reawakening (815 AW)

In 815 AW, a group of adventurers from Nigal made their way to Akovar through New Sabonaa, a Sabonaar outpost island that had been established within the archipelago less than two centuries earlier. Exploring the ancient ruins, they pieced together the history of the Akovarian civilization and the imprisonment of Asti within the Mask of the Pharaoh. Their subsequent journey to the Ancient South, recovery of the Mask from the Nepherites Pyramid, and return to Akovar's ruined capital to release Asti's essence at the Grand Temple constitutes one of the most consequential series of events in Mazariim's modern history.

The release of Asti at the Grand Temple in Amaunet sent a pulse of golden energy across the archipelago. Ley-lines that had been dormant for 7,645 years blazed back to life, carrying healing energy to every corner of the islands. Ancient pyramid temples filled with light for the first time since the cataclysm. The magical headwinds that had sealed the continent shifted — they did not disappear, but their patterns became less absolute, opening navigable corridors that skilled sailors with magical assistance could exploit. The wild and dead magic zones that had plagued the continent for millennia began to stabilize, though the process was slow and incomplete.

The effects were felt far beyond Akovar. The ley-line reawakening sent ripples of magical energy across all of Mazariim, stirring long-dormant entities, waking ancient dragons from slumber, and initiating the chain of events that would define the next three decades of global history — the Elemental Crisis, the Far Realm incursion, the ley-line proliferation, and ultimately the Coming of the Lines.

On Akovar itself, the reawakening transformed the continent. Areas that had been magically dead for millennia suddenly pulsed with energy. Ruins that had been inert began to exhibit magical activity — wards flickering to life, sealed doors groaning against ancient locks, crystalline matrices in pyramid temples beginning to hum with stored power. The dragonborn experienced the return of the ley-lines as a spiritual event — many clans interpreted the golden light and the surge of healing energy as a divine message, a sign that the golden age described in their oldest legends was returning. New religious movements emerged, some venerating Asti directly (guided by Line-Watchers who arrived from Nigal), others interpreting the reawakening through existing draconic spiritual traditions.

The dragons' response was more complex. Metallic dragons — whose ancestors had served as guardians of ley-line intersections during the Akovarian golden age — generally welcomed the reawakening, some actively assisting Line-Watchers in mapping and restoring ley-line nodes. Chromatic dragons were more suspicious, viewing the returning magical infrastructure as a potential threat to their territorial dominance or as a resource to be seized and controlled. With over a thousand dragons on the continent, the conflicts between metallic and chromatic factions over ley-line convergence points have become the most significant source of violence on Akovar since the reawakening — battles between dragon clans that shake the earth and can be heard for dozens of miles, fought over pyramid ruins that had stood uncontested for millennia.

### Modern Akovar (842 AW)

Twenty-seven years after the reawakening, Akovar remains a frontier — wild, dangerous, and largely unexplored by outsiders. The ley-line network is partially active, with major channels between the largest surviving pyramid sites carrying energy that Line-Watchers describe as strong but unstable, prone to surges and fluctuations that reflect the damaged infrastructure underlying the restored flows. Many minor ley-lines remain dormant or corrupted, their pathways blocked by the geological and magical changes of seven millennia. The wild and dead magic zones have shrunk but not disappeared, and the portals to other planes continue to open unpredictably, though less frequently than before the reawakening.

Contact with the outside world has increased dramatically since 815 AW. New Sabonaa has grown from a modest outpost into a bustling frontier town, serving as the primary gateway for expeditions to the continent. Scholars from the Astral Circle maintain a permanent research station there. Line-Watchers of Asti have established camps at several ley-line convergence points on the main continent, studying the restored network and attempting to understand the geometric principles that the Akovarians used to build it. Treasure-seekers, relic hunters, and agents of various governments — the Federation of Man, the Thaumaturgic Council of Instar, the Dwarven Empire, and others — arrive with increasing frequency, drawn by rumors of Akovarian artifacts, ancient spellbooks written in Draconic, and magical resources unlike anything found on Nigal.

The dragonborn view this influx with mixed feelings. Some clans have embraced contact with the outside world, trading with New Sabonaa and welcoming the Line-Watchers as bearers of knowledge about their ancestors' civilization. Others are hostile to outsiders, viewing them as looters and invaders who seek to exploit Akovar's heritage for their own gain. The dragons are similarly divided — metallic dragons who remember or value the Akovarian legacy may assist outside scholars, while chromatic dragons defend their territories against all intruders with lethal force. With over a thousand dragons inhabiting the archipelago, the practical reality is that no outsider travels far on the continent without encountering a dragon or entering a dragon's claimed territory — and the outcome of such encounters depends entirely on the temperament of the individual dragon.

The continent's dangers have not diminished with the reawakening. If anything, the restored ley-line energy has made some hazards worse — undead sustained by ambient magical energy have become more active, the lich Thz'zar Ja'laao stirs with renewed purpose in his catacombs, and creatures drawn by the ley-line energy arrive through the portals in greater numbers. The interaction between the restored ley-lines and the existing wild magic zones produces unpredictable effects that make travel across the continent hazardous even for experienced spellcasters. Expeditions that set out from New Sabonaa with adequate preparation and magical support may succeed; those that venture into the interior unprepared simply disappear.

The Geometrist's Tower — a Yrugas-era ruin in eastern Beneshire on the continent of Nigal — contains an active portal connecting to the Temple of the Seventh Convergence on Akovar. This portal, maintained by forces that no living person fully understands and haunted by the wraith of the wizard Vorthax, represents the only known permanent gateway between the continents that does not require ocean passage. The Astral Circle is aware of the portal and has documented its existence, but the logistical and safety challenges of regular use have limited traffic to occasional scholarly expeditions. The portal's existence is a closely guarded secret, as the security implications of an uncontrolled gateway to another continent are significant.

## THE MAIN CONTINENT

### Geography and Climate

The main island of Akovar is a roughly crescent-shaped landmass approximately four hundred miles from its northern tip to its southern point, with a width varying between sixty and one hundred and fifty miles. The interior is dominated by the Spine of Fire — a volcanic mountain range that runs along the crescent's inner curve, its peaks reaching heights of twelve thousand feet. Several of these volcanoes remain active, and eruptions occur every few decades, reshaping the landscape and creating new hazards while depositing mineral-rich soil that feeds the lowland jungles.

The eastern coast — the crescent's outer curve — is the most hospitable portion of the continent. Broad coastal plains give way to dense tropical jungle that extends inland to the foothills of the Spine of Fire. The major Akovarian cities were built along this coast, and the largest concentrations of dragonborn population remain here. Rivers flowing eastward from the mountains create fertile valleys where agriculture is possible, and the natural harbors along the coast provide shelter for the fishing boats and outrigger canoes that the dragonborn use for coastal travel.

The western coast — the crescent's inner curve — is far more treacherous. The Spine of Fire descends steeply to the sea, creating sheer cliffs of black volcanic rock broken by narrow beaches of dark sand. The waters within the crescent are shallow and reef-choked, heated by volcanic vents on the sea floor, and teeming with aggressive marine predators. Few dragonborn settle on this coast, and outsiders are advised to avoid it entirely.

The interior highlands between the volcanic peaks are a patchwork of cloud forest, alpine meadow, and barren lava fields. The highest elevations are claimed by white and silver dragons, who prefer the cold mountain air and the caves carved from volcanic rock. The cloud forests harbor creatures found nowhere else on Mazariim — products of millennia of magical contamination and extraplanar crossbreeding that have created an ecosystem unlike any other.

The southern portion of the continent is dominated by the Shattered Coast — a region where the ley-line detonation did the most damage to the physical landscape. Portions of the original coastline collapsed into the sea, creating a maze of inlets, sea caves, and rocky islets that extends for miles offshore. The Shattered Coast is the region with the highest concentration of wild magic zones and active portals, making it simultaneously the most dangerous and the most magically rich area on the continent. Black and green dragons favor this region, drawn by its remoteness and the magical energy that suffuses it.

The northern tip of the continent narrows to a rocky peninsula known as the Dragon's Horn, a windswept highland claimed by a powerful blue dragon clan. The Dragon's Horn is the closest point on the main continent to New Sabonaa, approximately sixty miles across open water — a crossing that takes half a day in favorable conditions and considerably longer when the residual headwinds interfere.

### Wild Magic and Dead Magic Zones

The ley-line detonation of 6,830 BW left the continent scarred with areas where mana behaves abnormally. These zones have shrunk since the 815 AW reawakening but remain significant features of the landscape.

Wild magic zones are areas where ambient mana is concentrated but chaotic. Spells cast within these zones produce unpredictable effects — a fireball might explode in the wrong direction, a healing spell might age the recipient by a decade, or a simple light cantrip might produce a blinding flash visible for miles. The wild magic zones are not uniformly dangerous; some are mild, producing minor visual distortions and harmless sparks, while others are so severe that merely entering them triggers spontaneous magical effects on any creature carrying mana — which includes all spellcasters, all magical creatures, and anyone carrying enchanted items. The largest and most intense wild magic zones are concentrated along the Shattered Coast and in the area surrounding the ruined capital of Amaunet, where the ley-line detonation was most violent.

Dead magic zones are areas where mana has been completely drained. No spell functions within these zones, no magical item activates, and supernatural abilities cease to operate. Dead magic zones are smaller and less common than wild magic zones, but they are far more dangerous in practice — a spellcaster who wanders into a dead magic zone loses all magical protection instantly, and a dragon that flies over one may find itself suddenly unable to fly. Most dead magic zones are located in the interior highlands and along the western coast, in areas where the ley-line channels physically ruptured during the detonation.

Since 815 AW, the restored ley-line energy has been slowly normalizing the worst magical disturbances. Wild magic zones near active ley-lines have become less severe, and some dead magic zones at the edges of the restored network have begun to recover faint mana flow. The process is gradual, however, and large portions of the continent remain magically hazardous. Line-Watchers estimate that full stabilization of Akovar's magical environment — if it is possible at all — will require decades or centuries of sustained ley-line activity.

### Portals and Planar Instability

The ley-line detonation tore holes in the fabric of reality across Akovar. While many of these tears healed over the millennia, others became permanent or semi-permanent portals connecting the continent to various extra-dimensional planes. Still others open and close cyclically or in response to specific conditions — lunar phases, magical storms, volcanic eruptions, or the proximity of powerful spellcasters.

The portals are the primary source of Akovar's exotic population. Tieflings trace their ancestry to fiends that emerged from Abyssal or Infernal portals and interbred with local populations. Aasimar descend from celestials that arrived through portals to the Positive Energy Plane or Heaven. Genasi — humans with elemental heritage — are products of encounters with elementals from the Planes of Fire, Water, Air, and Earth. These cross-breed populations have existed on Akovar for millennia and consider themselves as native to the continent as the dragonborn.

The portals also introduce hazards. Demons, devils, and hostile elementals emerge periodically, requiring the local population to maintain constant vigilance. Some portals are well-known and avoided — the dragonborn mark them with warning totems — while others appear without warning and vanish just as suddenly. The restored ley-line energy has somewhat stabilized the portal activity, reducing the frequency of unpredictable openings, but portals located at ley-line convergence points have become more active, not less, as the increased magical energy feeds the planar weaknesses.

The most significant known portal on the continent — aside from the erratic ones — is the terminus of the gateway maintained by the Geometrist's Tower on Nigal. This portal, located at the Temple of the Seventh Convergence on the eastern coast, has been stable for approximately 3,500 years and provides a permanent connection between the continents. The Astral Circle is aware of this portal and has used it for limited scholarly expeditions, though the dangers of the Geometrist's Tower on the Nigal side (including the wraith Vorthax) and the wild terrain surrounding the Temple on the Akovar side limit regular traffic. The portal's existence is a closely guarded secret, as the security implications of an uncontrolled gateway to another continent are significant.

## THE DRAGONBORN

### Origins and Heritage

The dragonborn of Akovar are not a naturally evolved race. They are the product of the ley-line detonation of 6,830 BW, when surging magical energy merged human and dragon blood in surviving Akovarians, creating a new race that carried characteristics of both. The first generation of dragonborn were born to human parents who had been exposed to the detonation — children with scaled skin, elongated faces, and the ability to produce elemental breath weapons. Over subsequent generations, the dragonborn bred true, their draconic characteristics stabilizing into a distinct racial identity.

Dragonborn are tall — typically six to seven feet — with powerful builds, thick scaled skin that ranges in color depending on their draconic heritage, and blunt snouts filled with sharp teeth. They lack tails and wings (unlike true dragons) but possess clawed hands and feet, keen senses, and an innate resistance to the element associated with their ancestral dragon type. Their most distinctive ability is their breath weapon — a cone or line of elemental energy (fire, cold, lightning, acid, or poison) that every dragonborn can produce, though its power varies with the individual.

The dragonborn population is genetically diverse due to millennia of interbreeding between different draconic lineages. While the "purest" clans — those that have maintained their connection to a single dragon type — prize their heritage and the strength of their associated breath weapon, the majority of dragonborn are of mixed ancestry, displaying characteristics from multiple draconic lines. Mixed-heritage dragonborn are sometimes viewed as lesser by purist clans, but in practice they are the norm rather than the exception, and their genetic diversity often produces individuals of exceptional adaptability.

### Tribal Society

Dragonborn civilization on Akovar is organized around clans — extended kinship groups ranging from a few dozen individuals to several thousand, each associated with a specific draconic heritage and occupying a defined territory. Clans are led by elders chosen for strength, wisdom, and demonstrated ability rather than hereditary succession. A clan's territory typically encompasses a village or cluster of villages, surrounding hunting and farming lands, and — in some cases — an ancient Akovarian ruin that the clan has adopted as a spiritual or defensive site.

The largest social unit above the clan is the tribal confederation — a loose alliance of clans in a geographic region that cooperate on matters of mutual defense, trade, and dispute resolution. These confederations are not formal governments; they have no written laws, no permanent institutions, and no centralized authority. Their cohesion depends on the prestige and diplomatic skill of whichever clan leaders currently hold the respect of their peers. Confederations can form and dissolve within a generation as alliances shift and leaders rise and fall.

The most significant tribal confederations as of 842 AW are the Eastern Shore Confederation (a coalition of metallic-heritage clans along the eastern coast that has been most receptive to outside contact), the Highland Clans (a fractious group of mixed-heritage clans in the interior mountains), and the Shattered Coast Tribes (chromatic-heritage clans in the dangerous south who are hostile to outsiders and maintain the closest relationships with the chromatic dragon territories).

### Culture

Dragonborn culture is oral, practical, and deeply connected to draconic heritage. History is preserved in chanted genealogies that trace clan lineages back to specific dragons — and through them, in the oldest traditions, to the human Akovarians whose blood they carry. These genealogies are the closest thing to written history that most dragonborn possess, though some clans have adopted pictographic systems influenced by the Akovarian hieroglyphs they find in the ruins they inhabit.

Craftwork is competent but not sophisticated by Nigal standards. Dragonborn work stone, bone, wood, and volcanic glass with skill, and their metalworking — using iron and bronze smelted from volcanic ore — produces functional if unrefined weapons and tools. They have not developed the advanced metallurgy, steam technology, or magical manufacturing found in the dwarven or gnomish traditions. What they lack in technological sophistication, they compensate for with physical power, breath weapons, and an intimate knowledge of their terrain that makes them formidable opponents and resourceful survivors.

Religion among the dragonborn is varied and localized. Many clans practice ancestor worship centered on their draconic forebears, with oral traditions preserving the deeds and wisdom of great dragons and clan leaders. Some clans worship dragons directly — particularly those that live in close proximity to dragon overlords — treating their patron dragon as a living deity. Since the ley-line reawakening of 815 AW, worship of Asti has begun to spread among the Eastern Shore Confederation, introduced by Line-Watchers who have shared the history of the Akovarian civilization with receptive clans. The idea that the dragonborn's human ancestors worshiped Asti and built the pyramid temples that dot the landscape has been met with fascination by some clans and hostility by others who prefer their existing spiritual traditions.

### Dragonborn and the Outside World

The arrival of outsiders since 815 AW has been the most significant disruption to dragonborn society since the ley-line detonation itself. For seven millennia, the dragonborn had the continent to themselves (aside from the dragons and the exotic cross-breed populations). The sudden appearance of humans, elves, dwarves, and gnomes — races the dragonborn had never encountered — has provoked reactions ranging from curiosity to hostility.

The Eastern Shore Confederation has been the most welcoming, establishing trade relations with New Sabonaa and permitting Line-Watchers and Astral Circle researchers to operate within their territory. The confederation's openness is partly pragmatic (outside trade goods, particularly metal tools and weapons, are highly valued) and partly spiritual (some clan leaders view the outsiders as emissaries of the golden age their legends describe). The Highland Clans are more cautious, permitting limited contact but refusing to allow outsiders into their territories without escort. The Shattered Coast Tribes are openly hostile, attacking outsiders on sight and regarding any attempt at contact as an invasion.

Individual dragonborn who have traveled to New Sabonaa and beyond have returned with stories of the wider world that are reshaping dragonborn self-perception. The discovery that their ancestors were human — that the dragonborn are not a primordial race but the product of a magical catastrophe — is as theologically disruptive for some clans as the Akovarian origin revelation was for the Varkonans of the Ancient South.

## THE DRAGONS OF AKOVAR

### Dragon Population and Territories

Akovar is home to the densest concentration of dragonkind anywhere on Mazariim. An estimated one thousand to two thousand dragons of every type inhabit the archipelago — a population that would be unthinkable on Nigal, where centuries of organized resistance from humanoid civilizations and competition for resources have kept dragon numbers comparatively low. On Akovar, seven millennia of isolation, abundant prey, rich mineral deposits, and the ambient magical energy that suffuses the continent created ideal conditions for draconic proliferation. The result is a landscape where dragon territories overlap, border disputes are constant, and the sheer weight of draconic presence shapes every aspect of life for the other inhabitants.

Every type of dragon found on Mazariim can be found on Akovar, including strange hybrids that exist nowhere else. The dragons are organized into territorial clans — extended family groups led by the oldest and most powerful individual — that claim and defend specific regions of the continent. These territories are not negotiated; they are established by force and maintained through the threat of retaliation. A dragon's territory is inviolable, and trespass — by other dragons, by dragonborn, or by outsiders — is met with lethal force unless the trespasser has been granted explicit permission.

The major dragon territories as of 842 AW include the Amaunet Crater and surrounding lowlands (claimed by a red dragon clan that has occupied the ruins of the capital since the detonation — this is the same clan whose predecessors the 815 AW heroes fought through to reach the Grand Temple), the Dragon's Horn peninsula and northern waters (a powerful blue dragon clan), the Spine of Fire's highest peaks (white and silver dragon clans in an uneasy territorial arrangement), the eastern coastal highlands (gold and bronze dragon clans that have historically protected Akovarian ruins), and the Shattered Coast's maze of islands and sea caves (black and green dragon clans). Copper and brass dragons are more scattered, inhabiting smaller territories throughout the continent, often in proximity to dragonborn settlements where their gregarious natures find an appreciative audience.

Beyond these major territories, hundreds of smaller claims dot the landscape — individual dragons or mated pairs defending a mountain valley, a stretch of coastline, a jungle basin, or a particularly rich hunting ground. The density of these smaller territories creates a patchwork of overlapping claims that makes cross-country travel a constant exercise in diplomatic navigation. Dragonborn who have lived on the continent their entire lives know which territories to avoid and which dragons can be approached safely; outsiders who lack this knowledge are at severe risk.

### Metallic and Chromatic Tensions

The division between metallic and chromatic dragons — a fundamental feature of draconic society across Mazariim — is particularly acute on Akovar due to the history of the Dragon War. Metallic dragons' ancestors served as allies and teachers of the Akovarians and as guardians of ley-line intersections during the golden age. Chromatic dragons' ancestors launched the assault that destroyed the civilization and triggered the ley-line detonation. This history informs present-day politics: metallic dragons on Akovar tend to view the ley-line reawakening as a restoration of the rightful order and cooperate (to varying degrees) with Line-Watchers and scholars. Chromatic dragons tend to view the ley-lines as a resource to be controlled or a threat to be neutralized.

The conflict is not absolute. Individual dragons of both types defy the general pattern — a few chromatic dragons have shown willingness to cooperate with outsiders, and some metallic dragons are territorial and hostile to any intrusion. But the overall trend creates a dynamic where ley-line convergence points located in metallic dragon territory are accessible to researchers, while those in chromatic territory are not — a situation that significantly constrains scholarly understanding of the ancient network.

Since the reawakening, the stakes of these territorial disputes have risen sharply. Ley-line convergence points that had been inert for millennia — fought over, if at all, for their mineral wealth or strategic position — now pulse with magical energy that enhances the power of any creature that lairs near them. Dragons are inherently attuned to mana, and the restored ley-lines represent an intoxicating increase in ambient magical power. The competition for convergence points has triggered open warfare between metallic and chromatic clans on multiple occasions since 815 AW, with battles between adult and ancient dragons that devastate the surrounding landscape and terrify the dragonborn communities caught between them.

### Hybrid Dragons

The ley-line detonation created conditions that do not occur naturally — areas where the mana field was so distorted that the genetic barriers between dragon types broke down. Over millennia, this has produced hybrid dragons: creatures that combine characteristics of two or more draconic lineages. A hybrid might have the body shape of a blue dragon but the breath weapon of a gold, or the coloring of a green dragon with the cold resistance of a white. Some hybrids display abilities that no standard dragon type possesses — unique breath weapons, unusual magical affinities, or physical characteristics that defy classification.

Hybrid dragons are uncommon but not as rare on Akovar as they would be elsewhere — perhaps fifty to a hundred exist across the archipelago, a product of the continent's unique magical environment and the sheer density of the dragon population. Other dragons view them with a mixture of fascination and unease, and the dragonborn regard them with superstitious awe. Scholars from the Astral Circle are intensely interested in hybrid dragons as evidence of how the ley-line detonation altered fundamental magical and biological processes.

## NEW SABONAA

**"Last stop before the edge of the world — and first stop on the way back, if you're lucky."**
— Common saying among New Sabonaa dock workers

### Overview

**Alignment:** CN
**Government:** Colonial Administration (nominal Naarlam authority; de facto multi-national frontier governance)
**Settlement Type:** Frontier Town
**Population:** ~3,500 (842 AW; up from ~400 before 815 AW)
**Demographics:** Humans (Sabonaar majority, with Madronian, Laveri, and other minorities) 60%, Dwarves 15%, Gnomes 10%, Elves 5%, Dragonborn (visiting traders) 5%, Other 5%
**Languages:** Common, Sabo, Draconic (increasingly common due to dragonborn contact)
**Religions:** Iquinias, Tiovin, Polyvin, Asti (growing presence among Line-Watchers)
**Primary Exports:** Akovarian artifacts, rare magical components, exotic specimens, expedition outfitting services
**Real-World Analog:** Gold-rush frontier town meets colonial trading post

New Sabonaa is a small island approximately twenty miles long and eight miles wide, located roughly sixty miles northwest of the Dragon's Horn — the nearest point on the main continent of Akovar. The island sits at the outer edge of the magical headwind barrier that has sealed Akovar from the rest of the world since the ley-line detonation of 6,830 BW. This position — close enough to Akovar to serve as a base for expeditions, but far enough from the detonation epicenter that the headwinds are manageable — made it a natural waypoint for the rare sailors who attempted the crossing before 815 AW and the obvious staging ground for the explosion of interest that followed the ley-line reawakening.

### History

The waters around the Akovar archipelago had tempted Merchant Nations sailors for centuries before New Sabonaa was successfully established. The headwinds that sealed the continent were not a secret — Sabonaar, Laveri, and even Nond mariners had encountered them and spread tales of the "impossible winds" that guarded a mysterious eastern landmass. Beginning as early as the late 400s AW, several Merchant Nations powers attempted to establish outposts in the region, hoping to find a way through the barrier or at least to exploit the rich fishing grounds at its edge.

These early attempts failed. Cecastaa funded two expeditions in the 480s AW, both of which were driven back by storms and headwinds with significant loss of life. Amara's merchant houses sponsored a venture around 520 AW that located several islands in the outer archipelago but could not sustain a permanent settlement — the colonists were evacuated within two years after supply ships proved unable to reach them reliably. A Tentigo-backed expedition in 560 AW, motivated more by religious zeal than commercial interest, disappeared entirely. The headwinds, the distance from established supply lines, and the unpredictable weather patterns of the outer archipelago defeated every attempt.

Naarlam succeeded where others failed through a combination of superior seamanship, stubbornness, and the backing of House Konovara. Around 650 AW, a Naarlam expedition led by Captain Estevo da Salmara identified the island that would become New Sabonaa — volcanic in origin, with a natural deep-water harbor on its western shore, fresh water springs, arable soil, and a position at the very edge of the headwind barrier where the magical winds were at their weakest. Da Salmara established a settlement of approximately one hundred colonists and, critically, mapped a seasonal route through the outer headwinds that supply ships could follow during a three-month window each year when the barrier weakened. This narrow but reliable supply corridor allowed the settlement to survive where previous attempts had not.

For the next century and a half, New Sabonaa existed as a modest Naarlam outpost — a resupply point for fishing fleets, a base for naturalists studying the region's unusual marine life, and a place where ambitious sailors came to test themselves against the headwinds and inevitably failed to push through to the continent beyond. The outpost's population fluctuated between two hundred and five hundred, sustained by fishing, subsistence farming, and the modest trade that Naarlam's seasonal supply ships brought. It was a posting for the ambitious who hoped it would lead somewhere and for the forgotten who had been sent somewhere out of the way.

The recovery of the Mask of the Pharaoh and the freeing of Asti in 815 AW transformed New Sabonaa overnight. The disruption of the headwinds opened navigable corridors to Akovar for the first time in millennia, and New Sabonaa — already positioned at the headwind boundary — became the obvious staging point. Within a year of the reawakening, the first expeditions to Akovar were launching from the island's harbor. Within five years, the population had tripled. Other powers, recognizing the outpost's strategic significance, moved quickly to establish presences: Amara's Merchant Guild opened a trading house, the Thaumaturgic Council of Instar sent agents and researchers, Saerrus dispatched scholars from its universities, and the Federation of Man stationed an official observer. By 842 AW, New Sabonaa is nominally a Naarlam colony but functionally an international settlement where the flags of half a dozen nations fly and the real authority belongs to whoever has the most gold, the best information, and the strongest sword arm.

### Government and Law

New Sabonaa operates under nominal Naarlam colonial law, administered by a governor appointed by Queen Konovara. In practice, the governor's authority extends little beyond the settlement's main streets, and the growing international presence has diluted Naarlam's control. The current governor, Donatella Abselon (a minor member of one of the seven noble houses of Sabonaa), was appointed to what was considered a meaningless sinecure before the reawakening — a posting to a forgotten island that no one of consequence would miss. She has since found herself governing the most strategically significant outpost in the Merchant Nations, a role for which her political training has proven surprisingly useful even as the scale of the challenge outstrips her resources.

Law enforcement is handled by a small constabulary of twenty guards — adequate for the pre-reawakening population of four hundred, woefully insufficient for a frontier town of 3,500. The governor supplements the constabulary with hired mercenaries and informal arrangements with the more reputable expedition companies, but the Dock Quarter and the Tent City remain effectively ungoverned after dark. Disputes are settled by the governor's court when the parties involved are willing to submit to jurisdiction, and by other means when they are not.

Multiple external powers maintain a presence on the island. The Astral Circle operates a permanent research station staffed by a rotating team of scholars. The Line-Watchers of Asti maintain a small temple that also serves as a way-station for clergy traveling to and from the continent. Amara's Merchant Guild has established a trading house that handles the growing commerce in Akovarian artifacts. The Federation of Man has stationed an official observer — a polite fiction for an intelligence operative — who reports on activities and developments. The Thaumaturgic Council of Instar maintains agents who monitor the flow of magical knowledge and artifacts, ensuring that nothing too dangerous reaches the wrong hands. Saerrus funds a small scholarly delegation that studies Akovarian history and culture. The competing interests of these powers create a complex political environment that the governor navigates with increasing difficulty.

### Key Locations

The Expedition Docks occupy the western harbor, the settlement's original and most developed waterfront. Here, ships are outfitted for the crossing to Akovar — a journey of sixty miles that requires magical navigation assistance to thread the residual headwinds. Expedition outfitters provide supplies, guides, maps (of varying accuracy), and the charms and enchantments necessary to navigate the headwind corridors. The docks also handle the return traffic — expeditions coming back with artifacts, specimens, and stories that fuel further investment.

The Archive is a fortified warehouse adjacent to the Astral Circle's research station, serving as a holding facility for recovered Akovarian artifacts before they are cataloged, studied, and — in most cases — shipped to institutions in Amara, Saerrus, or Fort Hope. The Archive's security is among the best on the island, maintained by a combination of mundane guards and arcane wards. The question of who owns recovered Akovarian artifacts — the dragonborn who inhabit the ruins, the Varkonans who descend from the original civilization, the scholars who recover them, or the governments that fund the expeditions — is a legal and ethical dispute that the Archive's existence has not resolved.

The Tent City sprawls across the island's central plateau east of the main settlement, a semi-permanent camp of canvas shelters, makeshift structures, and improvised markets that houses the transient population of adventurers, laborers, and fortune-seekers who cannot afford or do not want the permanent settlement's limited housing. The Tent City has its own economy, its own informal leadership (a rotating cast of the most successful expedition leaders), and its own justice system (swift and frequently violent). It is the most dangerous part of New Sabonaa and the most exciting, depending on one's perspective.

The Asti Temple is a small pyramid-shaped structure — the first building constructed according to Akovarian geometric principles in over seven thousand years — built by Line-Watchers in 820 AW. It serves as both a place of worship and a magical focusing point, the Line-Watchers having aligned its geometry with the faint ley-line flows that reach the island from the restored network on the main continent. The temple provides healing services to returning expeditions (injuries are common) and serves as a center for the growing community of Asti worshipers among both the human settlers and the dragonborn who visit the island.

The International Quarter has developed since 820 AW as the various outside powers established permanent facilities. Amara's trading house, Instar's arcane monitoring station, Saerrus's scholarly delegation, and the Federation observer's offices cluster along a single street that the locals have dubbed "Embassy Row" — though none of the buildings are formal embassies and none of the occupants have diplomatic credentials. The International Quarter is the best-policed section of New Sabonaa, as each power maintains its own security, and the cumulative effect of multiple guard forces patrolling the same few blocks creates an island of order in an otherwise chaotic settlement.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** Naarlam (colonial authority), Amara (trade partner), Instar (research partner), Saerrus (scholarly interest), the Federation of Man (strategic interest), the Astral Circle (research partnership), the Line-Watchers of Asti (mutual interest in ley-line research), the Eastern Shore Confederation of dragonborn clans (growing trade relationship).

**Enemies:** No formal enemies, but the island's position as a gateway to Akovar's riches makes it a target for pirates, smugglers, and agents of powers who would prefer to control access to the continent. Chromatic dragon clans on the main continent view New Sabonaa as the source of the unwanted outsiders encroaching on their territory.

### Key NPCs

- **Governor Donatella Abselon** (LN female Sabonaar Aristocrat/Rogue) — Governor of New Sabonaa. A political survivor from one of Naarlam's seven noble houses, appointed to a meaningless posting that became the most important colonial position in the Merchant Nations. She governs with pragmatism, charm, and a growing talent for improvisation, while navigating the competing interests of half a dozen nations that maintain presences on her island.
- **Seeker Orun Goldscale** (LG male Dragonborn [Gold heritage] Cleric of Asti) — A dragonborn cleric of the Eastern Shore Confederation who has adopted Asti worship and serves as informal ambassador between the dragonborn clans and the New Sabonaa community. One of the first dragonborn to learn Common, Orun bridges two worlds with quiet patience.

## TORTUGANAE

**"No kings. No chains. No questions."**
— Tortuganae founding compact (oral tradition)

### Overview

**Alignment:** CN
**Government:** Council of Captains (loose democratic assembly)
**Settlement Type:** Archipelago Refuge
**Population:** ~12,000 (842 AW)
**Demographics:** Lizardfolk 25%, Kobolds 20%, Goblinoids (goblins, hobgoblins, bugbears) 15%, Kenku 10%, Minotaurs 8%, Centaurs 7%, Other (various marginalized races) 15%
**Languages:** Common (trade pidgin), Draconic, Goblin, numerous racial languages
**Religions:** Garnak (common), Iquinias (coastal communities), various ancestral and tribal faiths
**Primary Exports:** Salvage, fishing, exotic tropical goods, mercenary services
**Real-World Analog:** Pirate haven meets refugee commune

Tortuganae is a cluster of approximately thirty tropical islands scattered across a hundred-mile stretch of ocean south of the main continent of Akovar. The islands range from tiny rocky outcroppings barely above the waterline to the largest — Tortuga Major — which is roughly fifteen miles long and seven miles wide. The archipelago is surrounded by treacherous coral reefs, shifting sandbars, and powerful currents that make navigation extremely hazardous for any vessel without a local pilot. These natural defenses have protected Tortuganae from invasion for the entirety of its history.

### History

Tortuganae was established around 1,500 BW as a refuge for sentient nonhuman races that found themselves unwelcome or endangered in the human-dominated, dwarf-dominated, and elf-dominated civilizations of Nigal. The exact circumstances of the founding are preserved in oral traditions that vary between the archipelago's communities, but the common thread describes a coalition of exiles — creatures driven from their homes by persecution, warfare, or the simple inability to coexist with the major races — who discovered the islands and agreed to share them under a compact of mutual non-aggression.

The founding compact was simple: no kings, no chains, no questions. Anyone who reached the islands and agreed to abide by the compact was welcome. Anyone who attempted to enslave, dominate, or rule the others would be expelled or killed. This compact — never written down, passed from generation to generation through oral tradition — has governed Tortuganae for over two thousand years. It has been imperfect in practice — disputes, feuds, and occasional violence between communities are common — but the core principle has held: Tortuganae remains a place where the unwanted can live free.

The population has grown and diversified over the centuries. Lizardfolk, who are natural coastal and island dwellers, were likely among the earliest settlers. Kobolds arrived in numbers, their natural burrowing abilities making them adept at creating sheltered communities on the rocky islands. Goblinoids — goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears — arrived in waves, many of them deserters or refugees from Zuel's orcish empire during the War to End All Wars and its aftermath, creatures who wanted no part of the god of destruction's endless conquest. Kenku, minotaurs, centaurs, and other marginalized races followed at various points, each community establishing itself on one or more islands according to its needs and preferences.

Tortuganae has never been conquered, though it has been attacked. During the War to End All Wars, Zuel's forces attempted to conscript the archipelago's goblinoid population. The attempt was repelled by a rare unified defense — every community in the archipelago contributing fighters to drive the invaders out. This shared victory cemented the compact's legitimacy and established a tradition of mutual defense that persists to the present day.

### Government

Tortuganae is governed — to the extent that the word applies — by the Council of Captains, an assembly that meets irregularly on Tortuga Major when issues affecting the entire archipelago require collective decision. Each community sends a representative (traditionally called a "captain" regardless of whether they command a vessel) to the Council, and decisions are made by majority vote. The Council has no enforcement mechanism beyond the collective willingness of the communities to abide by its decisions, which means that controversial rulings are frequently ignored by dissenting parties. The system works less through formal governance than through a web of personal relationships, mutual obligations, and the shared understanding that if the archipelago's communities do not cooperate on existential threats, they will be destroyed individually.

Between Council meetings, each island or community governs itself according to its own traditions. Lizardfolk communities are led by their eldest hunters. Kobold warrens are run by the cleverest engineers. Hobgoblin settlements follow military hierarchies. Kenku communities organize around artistic and crafting guilds. This diversity of governance creates a patchwork of local cultures that visitors find bewildering but that the inhabitants regard as natural — the whole point of Tortuganae is that no one tells anyone else how to live.

### Culture

Tortuganae's culture is defined by self-reliance, mutual aid, and a fierce attachment to personal freedom. The archipelago's inhabitants have little in common beyond their shared status as outcasts from the wider world and their shared commitment to the founding compact. Interspecies cooperation is practical rather than ideological — communities trade, share information about threats, and occasionally intermarry, but they maintain distinct cultural identities and do not attempt to create a unified "Tortuganae" culture.

The economy is based on fishing, salvage (the reefs claim ships regularly, and the inhabitants have become expert at recovering cargo from wrecked vessels), and the cultivation of tropical crops — primarily fruit, spices, and the fermented beverages that the islands produce in impressive variety and quality. Trade with the outside world is limited by Tortuganae's isolation and by the inhabitants' general suspicion of outsiders, but some commerce occurs through New Sabonaa and with independent merchants who know the safe passages through the reefs.

Mercenary work has become an increasingly significant source of income since 815 AW. The growing number of expeditions to the main continent has created demand for guides, guards, and fighters familiar with the region's dangers. Tortuganae's inhabitants — particularly the hobgoblins and minotaurs, who are formidable combatants — have found that selling their services to expedition leaders is more profitable and more interesting than fishing. This mercenary trade has created new economic opportunities but also new tensions, as some communities worry about becoming dependent on outsider gold and losing the self-sufficiency that has sustained them for millennia.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** New Sabonaa (cautious trade relationship), some dragonborn clans on the main continent (limited contact, mutual respect for territorial independence).

**Enemies:** No formal enemies. Tortuganae's isolation and natural defenses have historically been sufficient protection. The archipelago's inhabitants are wary of any outside power that might attempt to assert control — the Federation of Man, the Merchant Nations, or aggressive dragon clans.

**Threats:** Storms and natural disasters (the archipelago is vulnerable to tropical storms), the occasional emergence of dangerous creatures from the wild magic zones of the Shattered Coast to the north, and the long-term risk that increasing outside contact will erode the independence that defines Tortuganae's identity.

### Key NPCs

- **Captain Grasha** (CN female Hobgoblin Fighter/Warlord) — The most influential member of the Council of Captains. Grasha leads the largest hobgoblin settlement on Tortuga Major and has emerged as the de facto voice of the archipelago in dealings with New Sabonaa and outside merchants. She is tactically brilliant, politically shrewd, and utterly committed to Tortuganae's independence.
- **Old Sizzik** (N male Lizardfolk Druid) — The eldest living inhabitant of the archipelago, a lizardfolk shaman of extreme age whose knowledge of the islands' natural and magical features is unmatched. Sizzik serves as an informal advisor to the Council and is the archipelago's primary authority on the history and lore of Tortuganae's founding.

## RUINS OF THE AKOVARIAN CIVILIZATION

The continent of Akovar is littered with the ruins of the civilization that once called it home. Pyramid temples, administrative complexes, residential districts, and infrastructure of a sophistication that the modern world has not matched stand in various states of decay across the islands, preserved in some cases by the dry highland climate and in others being slowly consumed by the tropical jungle. These ruins are among the most significant archaeological sites on Mazariim and will be detailed in a dedicated reference document. What follows is a brief overview of the most notable locations.

### Amaunet (The Ruined Capital)

Located on the eastern coast at the widest point of the main continent, Amaunet was the capital of the Akovarian civilization and the site of the Grand Temple where Asti's avatar resided. The city was ground zero for the ley-line detonation and suffered catastrophic damage — but its massive stone construction ensured that much of the lower city survived as ruins. Today, Amaunet is claimed by a red dragon clan that lairs in the rubble of the Grand Temple complex and is haunted by the lich Thz'zar Ja'laao in the catacombs below. The ley-line reawakening has made the ruins pulse with magical energy that intensifies the closer one approaches the Grand Temple. Access is extremely dangerous, though the 815 AW heroes penetrated the city to reach the Temple and release Asti.

### Iseueri (The Swamp Archive)

In the southeastern lowlands, where the jungle gives way to coastal marshland, the ruins of Iseueri are half-submerged in warm, stagnant water. The settlement served as an Akovarian archive and research complex, and portions of its library vaults may still be intact beneath the waterline. Black and bronze dragons inhabit the area, maintaining an uneasy territorial arrangement over the ruins. Ancient archives and artifacts have been recovered from the accessible portions.

### Mibampes (The Planar Scar)

In the interior highlands, the ruins of Mibampes mark a location where the ley-line detonation tore reality so severely that the wound has never fully healed. The settlement is surrounded by an area of intense planar instability where portals open and close without warning and the boundaries between dimensions are paper-thin. Aberrations and extraplanar creatures inhabit the ruins, and the magical contamination makes extended habitation impossible for most mortal creatures.

### Ptei (The Mountain Repository)

High in the Spine of Fire, accessible only through treacherous mountain passes, the ruins of Ptei were an Akovarian repository for knowledge deemed too important or too dangerous to store in the lowland cities. Silver and white dragons guard the approach, respecting the ancient knowledge contained within and protecting it from looters. The repository's high-altitude location preserved its contents remarkably well, and the Line-Watchers have expressed strong interest in accessing whatever texts and artifacts survive.

### The Temple of the Seventh Convergence

On the eastern coast, this pyramid temple sits at the intersection of seven ley-line scars and serves as the Akovar-side terminus of the portal maintained by the Geometrist's Tower on Nigal. The temple is partially ruined but structurally sound, and its ley-line convergence point is among the most powerful on the continent. Since the reawakening, the temple has become a site of intense scholarly interest, though its remote location and the dangers of the surrounding terrain limit access.

## ORGANIZATIONS

### Line-Watchers of Akovar

The Line-Watchers of Asti have established a growing presence on Akovar since 815 AW, with approximately forty clergy and affiliated researchers operating from camps at major ley-line convergence points and a permanent way-station at the Asti Temple in New Sabonaa. Their mission is to study, map, and stabilize the restored ley-line network — the original network that the Akovarians built alongside Asti over eight thousand years ago and that modern scholars are only beginning to understand.

The Line-Watchers' work on Akovar provides the theoretical foundation for ley-line research across all of Mazariim. The Akovarian network is the original model — every subsequent mortal-made ley-line draws on principles first developed here. Understanding how the ancient network was built, how it was powered, and why its detonation produced the effects it did is essential for the safe development of ley-line technology on Nigal. The Line-Watchers coordinate their efforts with the Astral Circle's research station at New Sabonaa and maintain cautious relationships with the metallic dragon clans who guard some of the most significant convergence points.

### The Astral Circle Research Station

The Astral Circle maintains a permanent research station at New Sabonaa staffed by a rotating team of six to ten scholars. Their research focuses on Akovar's planar instability, the wild and dead magic zones, and the ancient Akovarian magical traditions preserved in the ruins. The station serves as a clearinghouse for information gathered by independent expeditions and as a coordination point for the Circle's own research missions to the main continent. The station's head researcher files regular reports with the Circle's leadership, though the distance and difficulty of communication mean that the Akovar team operates with considerable autonomy.

### Dragon Preservationists

A loose coalition of metallic dragons — primarily gold and bronze — who view the ley-line reawakening as an opportunity to restore the cooperative relationship between dragons and mortal races that existed during the Akovarian golden age. These dragons actively assist Line-Watchers and scholars, share knowledge of Akovarian history preserved in their own memories and hoards, and work to prevent chromatic dragon clans from seizing or destroying ley-line convergence points. The preservationists are not formally organized — dragons are too individualistic for institutional structure — but they communicate and coordinate through ancient draconic networks of alliance and obligation.

### The Red Wardens of Amaunet

The red dragon clan that occupies the ruins of Amaunet has controlled the former capital for millennia. Led by the ancient red great wyrm Kharazzax, the clan claims the Grand Temple and its surrounding ruins as sovereign territory and responds to any intrusion with lethal force. The 815 AW heroes fought through the clan's defenses to reach the Grand Temple and release Asti — an event that Kharazzax regards as an invasion and a humiliation that demands eventual retribution. The clan's hostility makes the most important Akovarian ruin site on the continent effectively inaccessible for scholarly investigation.

## GEOGRAPHICAL FEATURES

### The Spine of Fire

The volcanic mountain range running along the inner curve of the crescent-shaped main island. Active volcanoes, hot springs, geothermal vents, and mineral-rich highlands. Home to white and silver dragon clans at the highest elevations and various dragonborn highland clans in the foothills and valleys. The range's volcanic activity creates both hazards (eruptions, toxic gases, unstable terrain) and resources (rich mineral deposits, fertile volcanic soil, geothermal energy that some dragonborn clans have learned to exploit for heating and metalworking).

### The Shattered Coast

The southern region of the main island where the ley-line detonation caused the most severe physical damage. A maze of inlets, sea caves, rocky islets, and submerged ruins extending miles offshore. The highest concentration of wild magic zones and active portals on the continent. Claimed by black and green dragon clans. Extremely dangerous — even dragonborn who have lived in the region for generations treat the Shattered Coast with caution. The magical environment here has produced unique creatures found nowhere else, some of which are valuable to researchers and some of which are simply lethal.

### The Dragon's Horn

The rocky peninsula at the northern tip of the main continent, the closest point to New Sabonaa. Claimed by a powerful blue dragon clan that tolerates limited passage by dragonborn and outsiders traveling between the continent and the island but demands tribute from any vessel passing through its waters without permission. The peninsula's high ground provides commanding views of the approaches from New Sabonaa, making it a natural chokepoint for anyone seeking to reach the main continent by sea.

### The Eastern Shore

The most hospitable portion of the continent — broad coastal plains, dense jungle, river valleys, and natural harbors. Home to the largest dragonborn population and the site of most Akovarian coastal cities. The Eastern Shore Confederation occupies this region and maintains the closest relationships with outside traders and scholars. The restored ley-line network is most stable here, following the ancient channels between pyramid temples along the coast.

### The Headwind Barrier

Not a physical location but a permanent feature of the region's magical geography. The ley-line detonation of 6,830 BW generated currents of residual magical force that radiate outward from the archipelago, creating unpredictable winds that interfere with navigation. Before 815 AW, these headwinds were impenetrable — no vessel could reach Akovar through them. The ley-line reawakening disrupted the barrier, opening corridors where the headwinds are weak enough to be navigated with magical assistance. These corridors shift over time as the ley-line network stabilizes, requiring navigators to update their routes regularly. The Expedition Docks at New Sabonaa maintain current charts of the navigable corridors, which are among the most valuable documents on the island.
