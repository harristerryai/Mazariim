# Ruins & Fallen Sites of Shalazar (Hin Homeland)

This file is a Shalazar-focused counterpart to **maz_geography_vandardhon_ruins.md**, using the same structural approach: ruins grouped by major eras, then summarized by difficulty, hooks by theme, treasure types, and unresolved mysteries.

Shalazar is comparatively stable and continuously inhabited, so its “ruins” skew toward:
- abandoned hamlets (storms, disease, saltwater intrusion)
- drowned/coastal sites (cliff collapses, shipwrecks, storm surges)
- sealed ritual cellars and pre-awakening druidic sites
- ancient migration-era shelters and cache lines

---

## PART I: PRE-HARVEST AWAKENING SITES (100,000 BW - 2,971 BW)

### ANCIENT HEARTHLINES & EARLY SETTLEMENT LAYERS

#### **The First Hearthline Caves (~15,000+ years ago; deep antiquity)**
Early coastal cave-shelters and storage hollows associated with the earliest Hin presence on Shalazar.

**Potential Sites:**

**1. The Smoke-Hollows**
A chain of shallow caves with blackened ceilings and stone smoke-channels.
- **Location**: Eastern cliff coast (exact placement varies)
- **Features**: soot-layer archaeology, primitive drying racks fossilized into stone impressions, hidden cache niches
- **Condition**: Partially collapsed; safe chambers alternate with unstable sections
- **Dangers**: rockfalls, aggressive cliff-nesting predators, disorientation in sea-fog
- **Treasures**: migration-era tools, early Hin trade beads, sealed “seed-stones” (ceremonial)
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Scholar claims the soot layers show a “fire mountain” ash signature
  - A child goes missing during a coastal festival and tracks lead into the hollows
  - A storm exposes a newly opened chamber with older-than-expected artifacts

**2. The Guest-Right Cache**
A hidden communal store site tied to the earliest hospitality tradition.
- **Location**: Inland ridge along an old quiet route
- **Features**: stone-lined pantry, oath-scratch marks, cairn markers only Hin recognize
- **Condition**: Intact; sealed by careful stonework
- **Dangers**: rival treasure-hunters, territorial beasts drawn by food smell if breached
- **Treasures**: preserved containers, old oath tokens, maps of early quiet routes
- **Difficulty**: Easy to Moderate
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - Someone has been stealing from the cache—an unthinkable crime
  - The pantry contains a foreign coin type that shouldn’t exist in this era

### PRE-AWAKENING ELEMENTAL GROVES (Legacy of early druidic control)

#### **The Stone-and-Water Circles (~20,000 - 3,200 BW)**
Ring-groves and spring sites used by early Hin elemental druids before Hinterhin’s consolidation.

**1. Spring of the Seven Vessels**
A sacred spring with carved channels feeding seven stone basins.
- **Location**: Central highlands
- **Features**: basin inscriptions (proto-Hin), seasonal water level “prophecies,” old offering niches
- **Condition**: Maintained quietly by locals, but the deeper chamber is sealed
- **Dangers**: fey territoriality, old wards that punish oath-breakers, water that induces visions
- **Treasures**: elemental charms, stone tablets with weather patterns, a sealed reliquary
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - The spring begins flowing red after a storm—locals panic
  - A Greenwarden asks PCs to investigate whether an old site is “waking wrong”

## PART II: POST-HARVEST AWAKENING RUINS (2,971 BW - 1 AW)

### FIELD LAW DISPUTE SITES & SEA-TRADE VIOLENCE

#### **The Broken Guesthouse (c. ~2,900 BW)**
A burned hospitality house tied to one of the earliest Field-Law test cases.
- **Location**: Along a river route between major villages
- **Features**: foundation stones, an oath-stone cracked in half, ash layer preserved under later soil
- **Condition**: Buried; partially excavated
- **Dangers**: social danger more than monsters—reopening the case could shame families
- **Treasures**: legal tokens, a sealed jar of witness tallies, a hidden letter implicating an outsider
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - A family wants the truth cleared before a marriage alliance
  - Evidence suggests the arson was foreign sabotage, not local crime

#### **The Saltwind Wreck (2 BW, War-Year Sea Loss)**
A coastal wrecksite tied to wartime supply desperation.
- **Location**: Southern coast near Southport
- **Features**: shattered hull ribs, barnacled cargo cages, a prayer-charm box to Hinterhin
- **Condition**: Scattered debris field; accessible at low tide
- **Dangers**: rough surf, sea predators, smugglers using the wreck as a dead-drop
- **Treasures**: war-era trade bars, preserved medical kits, a sealed dispatch tube
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - The dispatch tube names a traitor who redirected relief shipments
  - A coastal creature is nesting in the wreck, and fishermen are vanishing

## PART III: POST-WAR ABANDONMENTS (1 AW - 842 AW)

### WAR-WIDOW HAMLETS & FAILED RECOVERY PROJECTS

#### **The Empty Rows (c. 5 AW - 40 AW)**
A planned “replacement hamlet” founded to resettle families after war losses, later abandoned.
- **Location**: Near Luirennon’s agricultural belt
- **Features**: neat foundation rows, unfinished irrigation channels, a communal oven never blackened
- **Condition**: Overgrown but readable; the plan remains visible in stone lines
- **Dangers**: mundane hazards, desperate squatters, and the emotional weight of loss
- **Treasures**: unclaimed heirlooms, a co-op ledger showing missing grain, a hidden chest of wedding gifts never delivered
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Campaign Hooks**:
  - The ledger suggests organized theft during famine recovery
  - A haunting is reported—whether real or a cover for smuggling

## SECRETS & MYSTERIES UNRESOLVED

1. **The Fire Mountains**: What catastrophe drove the earliest Hin to Shalazar, and where did they come from?
2. **Proto-Hin Inscriptions**: Do the oldest marks in the spring sites match any known script in Mazariim?
3. **Guest-Right Theft**: Who would dare steal from a communal cache, and why?
4. **The Missing War Ledger Grain**: Was post-war theft opportunistic, or organized by an outside buyer?
5. **The Sealed Chamber**: What did early druids lock away beneath the Spring of the Seven Vessels?

*Campaign Master’s Note: Shalazar is intentionally “safer” than many regions. The best Shalazar ruins are not about apocalypse—they are about community wounds, weather dangers, coastal hazards, and the rare, shocking moments when someone violates Hin values.*
