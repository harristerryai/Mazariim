# Geography of the Inner Kingdoms: The Madronian Peninsula

**As of 842 AW**

---

## INTRODUCTION

The Inner Kingdoms lie in the northwest corner of the continent of Nigal, protected by mountains to the east and south and blessed with one of the most temperate climates on the continent. Dozens of small city-states and nations cover the landscape of the Madronian Peninsula, with well-patrolled roads and a near-total absence of roving monsters — a long-established reality for this region controlled by the Federation of Man. Very few human nations or cities in the area exist outside this confederation, and those that do face constant political and economic pressure to join.

The humans originating in this area are known as Madronians, their name derived from an ancient pre-history ruler called Madron. Their regional dialect is Madra, a flowing language with over fifteen vowels and many silent letters. Common is spoken universally across the region due to Federation standardization.

The Inner Kingdoms enjoy what would be considered real-world 17th and 18th-century technology, sometimes including bi-focal eyeglasses, the printing press, indoor plumbing, the cotton gin, bicycles, telescopes, and steam-engine technology (on a limited basis, a gnomish invention). Analogs for Western Europe and some Eastern European countries can be found here.

These nations are bound together by the Federation of Man, the most technologically advanced and militarily powerful human government on Mazariim. Through the Federation, the Inner Kingdoms maintain well-patrolled roads, steam-powered infrastructure, and a standardized legal system that stands in stark contrast to the fractured Merchant Nations to the south. Governed by the Praetorian Council at Fort Hope, the Federation ensures mutual defense, coordinates trade policy, and arbitrates disputes between member states.

**Current Political Organization (842 AW)**

The Federation of Man governs the Inner Kingdoms through the Praetorian Council seated at Fort Hope. Five major member nations form the core of the Federation:

- **Beneshire** — Hereditary Monarchy. The most technologically advanced nation, known for charitable governance and 18th-century English sensibilities.
- **Saerrus** — Senatorial Republic. An intellectual democracy famous for universities, art, and Enlightenment ideals, comparable to 18th-century France.
- **Arogror** — Hereditary Chancellorship. Rugged northern hillsfolk renowned for weapons, armor, and dwarven-influenced craftsmanship, similar to 17th-century Germanic regions.
- **Xeque** — Merchant Oligarchy. A hedonistic trading nation where gold determines power, reminiscent of Renaissance Venice.
- **Grand Confederacy of Pragos** — Magocratic Confederacy. A confederation of magically industrialized states ruled by powerful spellcasters, with no close historical parallel.

Numerous smaller city-states, independent towns, and minor noble holdings also hold membership or associate status within the Federation.

**Total Population (842 AW, estimated)**

- Humans (Madronian): ~1,800,000 across all nations
- Humans (Sabonaar, Laveri, other): ~120,000
- Dwarves: ~60,000
- Elves: ~8,000
- Halflings: ~15,000
- Gnomes: ~25,000
- Other (half-elves, half-orcs, exotic): ~12,000
- **Total: ~2,040,000**

## THE FEDERATION OF MAN

**"United we stood against the darkness. United we shall remain."**
— Inscription above the gates of Fort Hope

### Overview

The Federation of Man is not a nation but a confederation — a political alliance binding the major nations and city-states of the Inner Kingdoms into a unified body for mutual defense, trade, and governance. Founded in 1 AW in the immediate aftermath of the War to End All Wars, the Federation transformed the wartime Concord of Unity into a permanent institution. It is the dominant human political entity on Mazariim and the single most powerful government on the continent of Nigal.

The Federation is governed by the Praetorian Council, a legislative senate seated at Fort Hope. Each major city-state receives one representative, the five major nations send between five and twelve representatives each (based on population), recognized guilds receive one representative, and the city of Fort Hope itself holds one seat. The Praetorian Council votes on regional laws, taxation, distribution of protective troops, infrastructure projects, and trade policy. Members of the High Court are appointed for life but must renounce citizenship in their parent nation to serve the Federation directly.

### History

In the time before the Federation, the Inner Kingdoms were a violent and dangerous place. City-states squabbled over territory, religion, and resources. Wars with the dwarves flared every few generations, with petty kings from both races seeking to prove their superiority. Monsters roamed the trails between cities, and dark cults threatened what little peace the average farmer could find.

The War to End All Wars changed everything. The threat of extinction forced a unity between races and peoples that had never existed before, and one of the first products of this unity was the Federation of Man. Founded in 1 AW, the Federation began as a loose confederation of the major remaining human city-states. Beneshire was the first unified nation to join, and soon the Arogrorian Alliance consolidated into the nation of Arogror. Within a few decades Saerrus joined in earnest, and before the turn of the century the Pragos Confederacy formed and entered the Federation, along with numerous smaller city-states.

The first two centuries after the War saw magnificent defensive forts built along the Federation's borders, as well as the beginnings of a road network connecting all the Inner Kingdom nations and their capital cities. Patrols gradually turned the tide against the monsters that thrived in the wilderness between cities, and slowly the Inner Kingdoms began to put the wars — with the orcs and with each other — in the past.

In 209 AW, the monarchy of Saerrus fell to revolutionaries after scandals and abuses came to light. The Federation of Man kept the peace on Saerrus's borders while the nation in turmoil formed a new democracy. A decade later the Charter of Rights was ratified by the Federation, outlining basic rights for life and liberty within its borders.

The year 376 AW saw the Stone-Steel Treaty with the Dwarven Empire — a historic agreement that not only kept the peace but opened trade and commerce between the two races on an unprecedented scale. By 400 AW, rails were being laid toward the Dwarven Nations and between the various forts of the Federation. A few decades later, a canal was begun to allow ships to reach Fort Hope. Although small wars between city-states still flared from time to time, the tempered hand of the Federation often turned major disputes into smaller disagreements where compromise was possible.

In the early 600s AW, a nationalistic swelling of racial pride motivated the Federation to seek membership from nations in the Merchant Nations. Quickly refused, the Federation attempted to bring Naarlam and Cecastaa into its dominion through intimidation, small skirmishes, and trade blockades. The effort became too great a drain on resources and a source of contention in the Praetorian Council, as Saerrus and Xeque voted against the expansionist moves. New treaties were signed, peace was restored, and the failed expansion marked a turning point toward diplomacy over coercion.

### Government Structure

The Federation operates through three branches:

The **Praetorian Council** serves as the legislative body. Each major city receives one representative, the five major nations send between five and twelve representatives based on population, recognized guilds receive one representative, and the city of Fort Hope holds one seat. The Council votes on regional laws, taxation, troop distribution, infrastructure, and trade policy.

The **High Court** serves as the judicial body. Members are appointed for life and must renounce citizenship in their parent nation, serving the Federation itself. The High Court adjudicates disputes between member nations, interprets Federation law, and hears appeals from national courts.

The **Federation Military** maintains border forts, road patrols, and naval forces. The military is funded through Federation taxation and coordinates with the national armies of member states during crises. The Circle of the Eastern Watch — twelve specialists (six mages and six veteran warriors) — coordinates responses to supernatural threats along the eastern frontier.

## BENESHIRE

**"Honor. Charity. Duty."**
— Motto of House Thoman

### Overview

**Alignment:** LG
**Government:** Hereditary Monarchy
**Capital:** Wyebluff (pop. 30,000)
**Population:** ~400,000 (Madronian, Sabonaar, dwarves, elves)
**Languages:** Common, Dwarven, Elven, Madra, Sabo
**Religions:** Alagalothor, Iquinias, Mau, Paladine, Tiovin
**Primary Exports:** Foodstuffs, textiles
**Real-World Analog:** England, early 18th century

As the most technologically advanced nation in all of Mazariim, the traditional people of Beneshire lead privileged lives. Beneshire is known to be one of the most benevolent and charitable nations of the Inner Kingdoms and is well-regarded across the lands. This traditional feudalistic monarchy enjoys prosperity and a smaller gap between the very rich and the very poor than most nations. Magic, steam power, the printing press, and other innovations make Beneshire a peaceful and prosperous nation.

Beneshire's landscape is a patchwork of rolling farmland, ancient forests, gentle hills, and a dramatic coastline along the Crescent Sea. Well-maintained roads connect its eleven major settlements, and the countryside is dotted with smaller villages, hamlets, and individual estates. The Misty Forest dominates the nation's northern interior, a vast woodland patrolled by rangers and home to scattered elven communities.

### History

Beneshire is the oldest continuous nation in the Inner Kingdoms, founded around 1,650 BW as a hereditary monarchy among the Madronian peoples. For over a millennium before the War, Beneshire endured the cycles of conflict common to the pre-Federation era — border wars with neighboring city-states, periodic dwarven conflicts, and the constant threat of monsters in the wilderness between settlements.

During the War to End All Wars, Beneshire's armies fought at Fort Hope and across the Inner Kingdoms. The nation suffered tremendous casualties but emerged with its institutions intact. Beneshire was the first unified nation to join the newly formed Federation of Man in 1 AW, and House Thoman — which had led the nation's war effort — solidified its position as the royal line.

In the centuries since, Beneshire has served as the moral anchor of the Federation. Its kings have consistently championed justice, charity, and the rule of law within the Praetorian Council. The nation weathered the Orceri Incursion of 811 AW, when orcs invaded through ancient ruins within its borders, and the subsequent discovery that the Far Realm entity Ry'le had infiltrated Beneshire society (824 AW), a crisis that was narrowly averted before civil war erupted.

### Government Structure

Beneshire is a hereditary monarchy where the crown passes through House Thoman, the royal line since the War. The king holds executive authority but governs through a feudal structure of noble houses, each controlling specific territories and owing fealty to the crown. The eleven major noble houses each administer a city or region, collecting taxes, maintaining local militia, and dispensing justice in the king's name. The king's court at Wyebluff serves as the final authority on disputes between houses and sets national policy.

### Culture

Beneshire's culture emphasizes tradition, duty, charity, and honor. The Madronian people here are conservative in temperament but progressive in technology — they embrace innovations that improve quality of life while maintaining social structures that have endured for millennia. Noble houses take their responsibilities seriously; a house that neglects its people loses standing at court.

The gap between rich and poor is smaller here than in most Inner Kingdom nations, owing to strong charitable traditions reinforced by the Church of Tiovin and royal edicts requiring noble houses to provide for their communities. Education is widespread, literacy is high, and the printing press has created a thriving culture of pamphlets, newspapers, and published works.

### Religion

The Church of Tiovin dominates religious life, with major churches in every settlement. Unlike the militant Tiovinians of the Merchant Nations (such as Tentigo), Beneshire's churches focus on ministry, charity, and community service rather than crusades or selling indulgences. Temples to Paladine attract the knightly and martial faithful. Alagalothor is worshiped among the scholarly and magically inclined. Mau's clergy tend the Library of the Ages in the mountains. Iquinias receives offerings from coastal communities and sailors.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** The Federation of Man (Beneshire is its strongest supporter), the Dwarven Empire (trade and mutual defense through the Stone-Steel Treaty), the Protectors of the Shire, the Emerald Shields, the Library of the Ages.

**Enemies:** The Grand Confederacy of Pragos (political rival within the Federation, ideological opposition), the Umbral Hand (Zebulba-worshiping death cult led by Noctuminalis), the Pirates of the Crescent Coast (maritime raiders threatening Beneshire's coastal trade).

**Threats:** Bandits in rural areas, wild beasts in the mountain regions, various dark cults (Taovanian, Far Realm, elemental), and persistent noble infighting between the eleven houses.

### Noble Houses of Beneshire

Beneshire is divided among eleven noble houses, each administering a major city and its surrounding territory:

- **House Thoman** (Wyebluff) — The royal line since the War. Rules the capital.
- **House Ravenwood** (Thaewood) — Traders and merchants.
- **House Silvermane** (Seton) — Military tradition and martial excellence.
- **House Cromwell** (Baefeld) — Religious devotion and clerical influence.
- **House Hargenfeld** (Anheath) — Explorers and adventurers.
- **House Sussefry** (Castow) — Magical item crafters with a darker reputation for espionage.
- **House Renoalt** (Vinshire) — Wizards and arcane scholars.
- **House Bahn** (Clahill) — Industry and manufacturing.
- **House Halycon** (Cabrook) — Farming and agricultural production.
- **House Kohler** (Magrove) — Horse breeding and cavalry.
- **House Tanishire** (Chaythorp) — Neutral negotiators and diplomats.

### Major Cities

**Wyebluff** — Metropolis (Pop. 30,000)

The capital city of Beneshire is a marvel of modern architecture, with soaring towers, a bustling marketplace, extensive docks, train systems, and even a blimp airport. Ruled by House Thoman and led by King Francent Thoman, Wyebluff is one of the most advanced cities in the Inner Kingdoms.

The city sprawls across hills overlooking the Crescent Sea, its skyline dominated by the Royal Palace (seat of House Thoman and the crown), the Grand Cathedral of Tiovin (largest temple in the Inner Kingdoms), and the University of Wyebluff (one of the premier institutions of learning on Nigal). The harbor district handles enormous trade volumes, with merchant vessels from across the Federation and beyond docking daily. A rail line connects Wyebluff to Fort Hope and other major cities, while the blimp airport — a gnomish-designed facility — provides rapid transport for the wealthy and urgent cargo.

Wyebluff's districts include the Royal Quarter (palace, government offices, noble residences), the Cathedral Quarter (religious institutions, hospitals, charity houses), the University Quarter (academies, libraries, student housing), the Market Quarter (grand bazaar, merchant offices, guild halls), the Harbor District (docks, warehouses, shipyards, sailor's taverns), and the Commons (residential areas for the working population, well-maintained but modest).

**Key NPCs:**
- **King Francent Thoman** (LG male Madronian Fighter/Battlemaster) — Ruler of Beneshire. A just and courageous king who never falters from challenges to the forces of good, whether within his nation or across the Federation.

**Cabrook** — Large City (Pop. 18,500)

This predominantly farming and trading city is ruled by House Halycon, an old and honorable house. Cabrook sits in the heart of Beneshire's most productive agricultural region, surrounded by miles of farmland in every direction. The city supplies much of the nation with its general food supply — grains, cheeses, meats, fruits, and wines.

Cabrook's architecture is more rustic than Wyebluff's, with sturdy stone and timber buildings surrounding a large central marketplace where farmers and merchants conduct daily trade. The House Halycon estate overlooks the city from a gentle hill, its orchards and gardens a testament to the family's agricultural mastery. A rail spur connects Cabrook to the main line, allowing rapid transport of perishable goods to urban markets.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Brianna Halycon** (NG female Madronian Cleric) — Lady of House Halycon. An older Madronian woman with long silver braided hair, known to wield divine powers and worship a number of deities. Her family owns most of the farmland for miles in every direction.

**Littlebridge** — Village (Pop. 500)

The small agricultural village of Littlebridge sits on the northern edge of the Misty Forest, a community of around 500 that includes many dozen farms as well as a central village containing various shops and serving as a place for trading goods between farmers and caravans heading north and west. Littlebridge is ruled by the Bakerson family, a small noble house known for its fine wines and cheeses. The village can call upon at least 25 militia under normal circumstances and more if townsfolk join to defend the village.

Littlebridge was spared destruction during the Orceri Incursion and Elemental Crisis, although it absorbed a number of refugees from each event. The village is a quiet, pastoral place where life moves slowly and the concerns of the wider world feel distant — though the presence of refugees has introduced new perspectives and occasional tension.

## SAERRUS

**"Reason. Liberty. Progress."**
— Unofficial motto of the Saerrus Republic

### Overview

**Alignment:** LN (good tendencies)
**Government:** Senatorial Republic with Prime Minister
**Capital:** (not yet detailed)
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Madra
**Religions:** Tiovin, Alagalothor, Minerva (minor)
**Primary Exports:** Art, literature, manufactured luxury goods, scholarly works
**Real-World Analog:** France, early 18th century

Independent and free-thinking, the people of Saerrus pride themselves on their open-mindedness and their many universities and schools of thought. Located near the middle of the Inner Kingdoms, Saerrus is one of the only democracies in the world, giving its people a sense of superiority and smugness. Ruled by elected councils with numerous checks and balances, the people of various towns and villages all have representatives that bring their issues to the central government.

Saerrus is a peaceful nation that seems almost obsessed with art, music, culture, and all things intellectual, with numerous galleries, theaters, and opera houses in its largest cities. The nation's landscape is gentle and cultivated — rolling countryside, manicured estates, and elegant cities connected by tree-lined boulevards.

### History

Saerrus was founded around 45 AW from the ruins of two or three Madronian nations destroyed during the War to End All Wars. The survivors, freed from the old monarchies that had failed to protect them, built something new — a revolutionary democracy where governance was earned through debate and merit rather than bloodline.

For its first century and a half, Saerrus was itself a monarchy within the Federation. In 209 AW, the monarchy fell to revolutionaries after a series of scandals and abuses came to light. The Federation of Man mediated peace on the borders while the nation in turmoil transitioned into an intellectual democracy, eventually solidifying its membership in the Federation as a republic. A decade after the revolution, the Charter of Rights was ratified by the Federation, outlining basic rights for life and liberty — a document heavily influenced by Saerrus's democratic ideals.

In the modern era, Saerrus has maneuvered itself into prime positions and allegiances within the Federation of Man, seeking to bring the light of reason to a powerful force of humankind. The nation often clashes politically with Pragos over ideology and with Beneshire over tradition, but these disputes remain within the bounds of Federation governance.

### Government Structure

Saerrus is governed by elected councils with numerous checks and balances. Town and village representatives bring local issues to regional assemblies, which in turn send delegates to the national Senate. The Prime Minister is elected by the Senate and serves as head of state, managing the executive functions of government. The judiciary is independent, with judges appointed by the Senate and confirmed by regional assemblies.

### Culture

Saerrus culture celebrates intellect, artistic achievement, philosophical inquiry, and personal freedom. Universities and academies dot the landscape, and scholarly debate is considered the highest form of discourse. The nation produces the finest artists, musicians, playwrights, and philosophers in the Inner Kingdoms. Galleries, theaters, and opera houses fill the largest cities, and even small towns maintain libraries and discussion circles.

The Saerran sense of cultural superiority — a smugness about their democratic institutions and intellectual achievements — is both their greatest strength and most annoying trait. Other Inner Kingdom nations respect Saerrus's contributions to art and learning while finding its citizens insufferably self-satisfied.

### Religion

Religious life in Saerrus is moderate and pluralistic. The Church of Tiovin is present but less dominant than in Beneshire. Alagalothor (patron of arcane magic) has a strong following among the scholarly class. Philosophy and secular humanism compete with organized religion for intellectual allegiance, creating a society where faith is respected but not mandatory.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** The Federation of Man, the Emerald Shields (based in Saerrus), intellectual and artistic communities across the Inner Kingdoms.

**Enemies:** Political rivals within the Federation (particularly Pragos), reactionary elements seeking to restore the old monarchy, and various threats common to the Inner Kingdoms.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Prime Minister Alais Senseau** (NG female Madronian Aristocrat) — Current leader of Saerrus. A skilled politician who has maneuvered the republic into influential positions within the Federation.

## AROGROR

**"Blood of the Hills. Steel of the Forge."**
— Arogrorian clan saying

### Overview

**Alignment:** LN
**Government:** Hereditary Chancellorship
**Capital:** (not yet detailed)
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Dwarven, Madra
**Religions:** Tiovin, Mordrion, Dirgin, Gyru
**Primary Exports:** Weapons, armor, raw materials, metals
**Real-World Analog:** Germanic regions, 17th century

The rugged nation of Arogror is a country of hard-working Madronians who feel a deep sense of nationalism not dissimilar to dwarven clan pride. Located in the northern reaches of the Inner Kingdoms, Arogror is a hilly and unforgiving landscape that has shaped its people into a tireless, resilient folk. Arogrorians value family above most other considerations, and their craftsmanship in weapons, armor, and raw materials — greatly influenced by the small dwarven population living within their society — is their greatest asset and specialty.

### History

Arogror was founded around 750 BW as a nation of industrious hillsfolk among the Madronian peoples. The region's harsh terrain and proximity to the Dragon Spine mountains fostered a culture of self-reliance and martial readiness. The dwarven communities scattered through Arogror's hills have influenced the nation's metalworking traditions for centuries, creating a unique blend of human and dwarven craftsmanship.

During the War, the Arogrorian Alliance fought with distinction, and the nation formally consolidated and joined the Federation of Man in the early years after the conflict. Arogror has since served as the Federation's industrial backbone, producing the weapons, armor, and raw materials that equip its armies and fuel its economy.

### Government Structure

Arogror is led by a Chancellor, currently Antioch Krichtienfels, who governs through a system of regional tribal councils. The Chancellor is nominally hereditary but succession often follows lines of merit and acclaim rather than strict bloodline. Northern hill tribes maintain strong autonomy, and the Chancellor's authority depends on maintaining the respect of these fiercely independent communities.

### Culture

Arogrorian culture prizes diligence, perseverance, family honor, and physical toughness. The people are known for their tireless work ethic and dwarven-influenced craftsmanship. Family is valued more highly here than in other Madronian nations, with extended clan structures reminiscent of dwarven holds. Social gatherings often involve demonstrations of skill — metalworking competitions, hunting contests, and feats of endurance.

The dwarven minority (larger here than in any other Inner Kingdom nation) has deeply influenced Arogrorian society. Worship of Mordrion, Dirgin, and Gyru alongside Tiovin reflects this cultural blending. Arogrorian ale, brewed with dwarven techniques, is renowned across the Federation.

### Religion

Tiovin remains the dominant human faith, but Arogror is unique among the Inner Kingdoms for its significant worship of dwarven deities. Mordrion (the Dwarf Father), Dirgin (earth and caves), and Gyru (artifice and invention) all have temples and shrines, reflecting the deep dwarven cultural influence. Worship tends to be practical and community-focused rather than elaborate or intellectual.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** The Federation of Man, the Dwarven Empire (closest human-dwarven cultural ties of any Inner Kingdom nation), Beneshire (shared values of duty and honor).

**Enemies:** External threats from mountain creatures, occasional dwarven clan disputes that spill across borders, and the persistent tension with Pragos over industrial competition.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Chancellor Antioch Krichtienfels** (LN male Madronian Ranger/Hunter) — Leader of Arogror. An older hillsman from one of the most northern tribes, a man who respects diligence and perseverance above all.

## XEQUE

**"Gold opens every door."**
— Xeque merchant proverb

### Overview

**Alignment:** N (with chaotic and evil tendencies among some factions)
**Government:** Merchant Oligarchy
**Capital:** (not yet detailed)
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Madra, Sabo
**Religions:** Xannith, Tiovin, Polyvin, Alagalothor
**Primary Exports:** Manufactured luxury goods, spices, perfumes, textiles, glassworks, lenses, telescopes
**Real-World Analog:** Venice and Italian city-states, 17th century

The merchant nation of Xeque is unique among the Inner Kingdom nation-states and would be more at home in the Merchant Nations to the south. Ruled by a merchant council made up of various noble and wealthy merchant houses, Xeque is a land where gold and power determine which rules one needs to follow. A healthy following of Xannithites adds to the decadent nature of the nation, and most cities have temples to Xannith mixed among the bathhouses, festhalls, and gentlemen's establishments.

Xeque's main specialties are manufactured or refined goods of the highest quality — spices, perfumes, foods, textiles, glassworks, lenses and telescopes, and many others. The nation's cities are vibrant, colorful, and hedonistic, with a cosmopolitan atmosphere that attracts merchants, artisans, and pleasure-seekers from across the Federation and beyond.

### History

Xeque was founded around 650 BW as a hedonistic merchant oligarchy in the Inner Kingdoms. Its position between the Inner Kingdoms and the southern trade routes gave it natural advantages in commerce, and its merchant houses grew wealthy serving as middlemen between the Madronian north and the Sabonaar south.

Xeque's relationship with the Federation has always been pragmatic rather than ideological. The nation joined for the economic benefits — access to Federation trade networks, road patrols, and military protection — while resisting the moral and legal restrictions that other members embrace. Within the Praetorian Council, Xeque's representatives often vote alongside Saerrus on issues of trade liberalization while opposing Beneshire on matters of moral regulation.

### Government Structure

Xeque is governed by a Merchant Council composed of representatives from the most powerful noble and merchant houses. The Prefect, currently Ethbeorht Tizomber, serves as the Council's presiding officer but holds limited executive power — real authority rests with the wealthiest houses, whose votes on the Council carry weight proportional to their economic contributions. This creates a system where gold literally determines political influence.

### Culture

Xeque's culture celebrates wealth, beauty, pleasure, and craftsmanship. The nation produces the finest luxury goods in the Inner Kingdoms — its glassworks, lenses, textiles, and perfumes are prized across Mazariim. Artisans here are treated as celebrities, and competition between merchant houses to sponsor the most talented craftsmen drives constant innovation.

The hedonistic atmosphere of Xeque's cities stands in stark contrast to Beneshire's orderly propriety or Arogror's stern industriousness. Bathhouses, festhalls, theaters, and gambling establishments are prominent features of every major settlement. This lifestyle attracts both admirers and critics — other Federation members view Xeque as morally decadent, while Xequians see themselves as honest about pleasures others enjoy in secret.

### Religion

Xannith (entity of greed) has a stronger presence in Xeque than anywhere else in the Federation, with temples disguised as banks and auction houses operating openly. Tiovin and Polyvin (fair trade) also have significant followings, creating religious tension between those who pursue wealth at any cost and those who advocate ethical commerce. Alagalothor draws followers among the arcane-inclined merchant class.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** The Federation of Man (pragmatic membership), trade partners across the Merchant Nations, Saerrus (aligned on trade liberalization).

**Enemies:** Xannith's enemies (Polyvin's faithful, anti-slavery activists), criminal organizations that threaten legitimate commerce, and moral reformers within the Federation who seek to regulate Xeque's excesses.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Prefect Ethbeorht Tizomber** (N male Madronian Expert/Aristocrat) — Presiding officer of the Merchant Council. A shrewd, calculating man who navigates the complex politics of Xeque's merchant houses with practiced ease.

## GRAND CONFEDERACY OF PRAGOS

**"Progress demands sacrifice."**
— Attributed to Grand Artificer Zebulon Grath

### Overview

**Alignment:** LN (with evil tendencies among leadership)
**Government:** Magocratic Confederacy
**Capital:** (not yet detailed)
**Population:** (not yet detailed)
**Languages:** Common, Madra, Draconic (scholarly)
**Religions:** Alagalothor, Gyru, Rivoleen
**Primary Exports:** Minor magical items, technological innovations, alchemical products
**Real-World Analog:** No close parallel; aspects of early magical-industrial revolution

Of all the Inner Kingdoms, the Grand Confederacy of Pragos has some of the most advanced cities as well as the largest difference between the wealthy and the poor. Focused on the creation of minor magical items and societal technologies, the Pragosians are led by powerful spellcasters, and the nation is divided into more than a dozen states, each run by a powerful mage, creating various networks of alliances within the government structure.

Many in Pragos are wealthy because of the nation's focus on exports, while many more live in abject poverty, performing menial jobs ranging from farming to working in smoke-filled factories. The stark inequality is Pragos's greatest shame and its most persistent political vulnerability within the Federation.

### History

The Grand Confederacy of Pragos was founded around 88 AW from war-torn Madronian territories. The chaos of the post-War period created opportunity for ambitious spellcasters to seize power, pooling magical resources and industrial capacity into a new political entity. The Confederacy joined the Federation of Man before the turn of the first century AW.

The current Grand Artificer, Zebulon Grath, claims lineage tracing back to Madron himself — a claim impossible to verify but politically useful. Under his leadership, Pragos has pursued aggressive magical industrialization, creating both extraordinary wealth and grinding poverty. The nation's secret police, the Dark Whispers, enforce order through fear and disappearance.

The Steam-Pump Revolution of 12 AW, when gnomish refugees in Pragos perfected steam-driven water pumps, kickstarted the region's industrial dominance. Since then, Pragos has been at the forefront of combining magical and mechanical innovation, producing goods that other nations cannot replicate.

### Government Structure

Pragos is divided into more than a dozen states, each ruled by a powerful spellcaster who serves as regional governor. These mage-lords form shifting networks of alliances, creating a complex internal political landscape. The Grand Artificer serves as the Confederacy's nominal leader, but their authority depends on maintaining alliances with enough state governors to command a majority.

The Dark Whispers serve as Pragos's secret police, doing the Confederacy's bidding in the shadows. They leave no evidence of their methods and hope to have their very existence doubted by the common people. A journey to the secret prisons of the Dark Whispers is said to be always a one-way trip.

### Culture

Pragos culture is defined by the tension between magical innovation and human cost. The nation's cities are marvels of arcane engineering — magical streetlights, automated workshops, alchemical production facilities — but the laborers who build and maintain these wonders live in poverty. The wealthy mage-class inhabits towers and estates of extraordinary luxury; the working class toils in smoke-filled factories for meager wages.

Education in Pragos is excellent for those who can afford it and virtually nonexistent for those who cannot. The nation produces exceptional wizards, artificers, and alchemists, but the majority of its population is functionally illiterate. This inequality is the source of persistent tension with more egalitarian Federation members, particularly Saerrus and Beneshire.

### Religion

Alagalothor (patron of arcane magic) is the dominant deity among Pragos's mage-class. Gyru (artifice and invention) has a strong following among engineers and craftsmen. Rivoleen (gnomish patron of curiosity and innovation) attracts gnomish residents and some human inventors. Tiovin is present but less influential than in other Federation nations — the Church of Tiovin's emphasis on charity and equality is inconvenient for Pragos's ruling class.

### Allies and Enemies

**Allies:** The Federation of Man (uneasy membership), trade partners who purchase magical goods, the Astral Circle (informal connections).

**Enemies:** Beneshire (ideological opposition within the Federation), reformist movements within Pragos itself, and external powers that seek to steal magical secrets.

**Threats:** Worker unrest, the Dark Whispers' growing power, rogue magical experiments, and the constant danger of arcane accidents in industrial facilities.

**Key NPCs:**
- **Grand Artificer Zebulon Grath** (LE male Madronian Wizard/Artificer) — Leader of the Grand Confederacy. A powerful and ruthless wizard who claims lineage back to Madron. Rules through a combination of magical power, political cunning, and the Dark Whispers' enforcement.

## FORT HOPE — CAPITAL OF THE FEDERATION

**"Where the races stood together, there shall the peace be kept."**
— Founding inscription, Fort Hope

### Overview

Fort Hope is the capital city of the Federation of Man and the political heart of the Inner Kingdoms. Originally a small walled keep in the interior of the Madronian Peninsula, Fort Hope was renamed during the War to End All Wars when humans and dwarves gathered here to forge the Concord of Unity. After the War, the site was chosen as the permanent seat of the Federation, and over eight centuries it has grown from a military outpost into a sprawling city of districts, each with its own character and purpose.

The city is not part of any member nation — it belongs to the Federation itself, administered directly by the Praetorian Council. This neutrality is essential to its function as a place where representatives from all member states can meet on equal footing.

### Districts of Fort Hope

**Fort District**

The oldest section of the city, built around the original wartime fortifications. The Fort District serves as the administrative and military heart of the Federation.

**Key Locations:**
- **Praetorian Halls** — The legislative chambers where the Praetorian Council convenes. A grand stone building with tiered seating for representatives, debate floors, and committee rooms.
- **High Court and Jails** — The judicial center of the Federation, where lifetime-appointed judges hear cases and appeals. The adjoining jails hold prisoners awaiting trial or serving sentences for Federation-level crimes.
- **Old Fort and Barracks** — The original wartime fortifications, now serving as the Federation military's command center. Barracks house the standing garrison.
- **Training Grounds** — Extensive military training facilities used by Federation troops and visiting national armies for joint exercises.
- **University Arcanum** — A prestigious institution of arcane learning, attracting students from across the Federation.
- **Scriptorium** — A center for the copying, preservation, and distribution of written works, serving both the University and the Federation's administrative needs.
- **University of the Arts** — Academy devoted to painting, sculpture, music, theater, and literary arts.
- **Academy of the Third Eye** — A school specializing in divination, psionics, and perception-enhancing magical disciplines.
- **Towers of Alchemy** — Research facilities devoted to alchemical experimentation and production.
- **Temple of the Magister** — A temple dedicated to Alagalothor, patron of arcane magic, serving the city's substantial wizarding community.
- **Gilded Festhall** — An upscale entertainment establishment catering to diplomats, representatives, and wealthy visitors.
- **Griffon's Respite** — A famous inn and tavern frequented by adventurers, soldiers, and travelers, known for excellent ale and reliable rumors.

**Temple District**

A serene quarter dominated by religious institutions, hospitals, and places of learning. The Temple District serves the spiritual needs of Fort Hope's diverse population.

**Key Locations:**
- **Halls of the Silver Dragon** — A grand temple dedicated to Paladine, god of justice and nobility. The Halls serve as headquarters for paladin orders operating within the Federation.
- **Grand Cathedral of the Lord** — The largest temple of Tiovin in the Inner Kingdoms, rivaling Wyebluff's cathedral in size and grandeur. Provides spiritual guidance, charity, and healing to the city's population.
- **Shrine of the Whispering Breeze** — A contemplative shrine dedicated to Vuleala (air and weather), popular with travelers and sailors.
- **Mordrion's Anvil** — A dwarven temple honoring Mordrion the Dwarf Father, serving the city's dwarven community and human worshipers of dwarven deities.
- **Temple of the Ancients** — A multi-faith temple honoring the Old Gods (Pyrantor, Iquinias, Vuleala, Dirgin, Gruhathyrir), maintained by traditionalist clergy.
- **Rare Books** — A specialist bookshop dealing in uncommon texts, religious manuscripts, and arcane treatises.
- **Herbs and Botanicals** — An apothecary and herbalist shop supplying both the Temple District's hospitals and private customers.

**Upper Market and Docks Districts**

The prosperous commercial heart of Fort Hope, where legitimate trade flows through well-regulated markets and busy river docks.

**Key Locations:**
- **Grand Bazaar** — A massive covered marketplace spanning several acres, handling goods from across the Federation and beyond. Merchants from every Inner Kingdom nation maintain permanent stalls.
- **Drydocks** — Shipbuilding and repair facilities serving the canal traffic that connects Fort Hope to the wider waterway network.

**Lower Market and Docks Districts**

The grittier underside of Fort Hope's commercial life. Where the Upper Market deals in legitimate trade, the Lower Market caters to those seeking goods and services that don't bear close scrutiny.

**Key Locations:**
- The Lower Docks handle bulk cargo, less reputable merchants, and the kind of trade that thrives away from official oversight. Gambling dens, dance halls, breweries, and bordellos cluster along the waterfront. A thriving black market operates in the shadows, tolerated to a degree by authorities who recognize that suppressing it entirely would simply drive it deeper underground.

**Merchant District**

A quarter devoted to guild operations, trade administration, and commercial services.

**Key Locations:**
- **Guildhouse Row** — A street of guild headquarters where the Federation's recognized trade guilds maintain offices, conduct business, and lobby the Praetorian Council.
- **Cartographer's Guild** — Headquarters of the guild responsible for mapping the Federation's territories and maintaining navigational charts for land and river routes.

**Noble District**

The wealthy residential quarter where Federation officials, visiting diplomats, wealthy merchants, and established noble families maintain residences.

**Key Locations:**
- **High Market** — An exclusive marketplace catering to the wealthy, offering luxury goods, exotic imports, and bespoke services.
- **Militia Station** — A garrison post maintaining order in the Noble District.
- **Playhouse** — An elegant theater hosting dramatic performances, musical concerts, and cultural events.
- **Bathhouse** — A luxurious public bathing facility in the classical style, serving as social gathering place for the elite.
- **Big Ben Clockhouse** — A massive clocktower visible across the city, a marvel of gnomish engineering that chimes the hours and serves as Fort Hope's most recognizable landmark.

**Industrial District**

The working heart of Fort Hope, where manufacturing, processing, and heavy industry concentrate. The district is noisier, dirtier, and more dangerous than the rest of the city.

**Key Locations:**
- **Ironworks** — Large-scale metalworking facilities producing tools, hardware, and construction materials for the city and Federation.
- **Slums** — The poorest residential areas of Fort Hope, where factory workers, laborers, and the destitute crowd into cramped, poorly maintained housing.

**Elventown**

A distinctive quarter where Fort Hope's elven population (descended from elves who chose to remain on Nigal after the War) maintains their own community, culture, and institutions.

**Key Locations:**
- **Shrine to Minerva** — A beautiful elven temple honoring Minerva, patron of the elves. The shrine serves as spiritual center for the elven community and attracts elven visitors from across Nigal.
- The district features elven-style architecture, libraries, herbalists, an alchemist's shop, a small magical academy, and a hospital using both elven and human healing traditions. Elventown is insular by choice — elves maintain their own community governance and cultural practices while participating in Fort Hope's wider life.

**Dwarven Ghetto**

The dwarven quarter of Fort Hope, a densely packed district where the city's dwarven population lives and works. Despite the name (which dwarves themselves use without embarrassment), the Ghetto is a proud community — insular, pious, and self-governing.

**Key Locations:**
- **Shrine to Mordrion** — A dwarven temple serving the Ghetto's deeply religious population.
- The district features dwarven breweries, an alchemist's workshop, gambling dens, and establishments catering to dwarven tastes. The Ghetto maintains its own informal governance and is known for racial insularity — non-dwarves are tolerated but not always welcomed warmly. A black market operates here, trading in dwarven-crafted goods, rare minerals, and items that Federation customs would question.

## FORT STONESHAPER

**"Here fell the hero. Here stands the watch."**
— Inscription on the fortress gates

### Overview

Fort Stoneshaper is a major Federation fortress complex located on the eastern frontier of the Inner Kingdoms, where Federation territory meets the wilder lands beyond. The fort was constructed between 67-73 AW during the great fortification period following the War. The site was chosen not merely for strategic value but to honor the memory of Demious Stoneshaper, the dwarven hero whose defeat of Zuel's avatar turned the tide of the War.

Over the centuries, the fort evolved from a simple border garrison into a major fortress complex, especially after the Stone-Steel Treaty of 376 AW opened formal relations with the Dwarven Empire. When the railway expansion reached the fort around 400 AW, it transformed into a vital commercial hub while maintaining its defensive mission.

### The Circle of the Eastern Watch

The fort's unique challenges — proximity to the Hollow Sepulcher, monster-infested eastern territories, and supernatural threats — led to the formation of the Circle of the Eastern Watch, a council of twelve specialists. Six powerful mages and six veteran warriors coordinate the Federation's response to the supernatural threats plaguing the eastern territories. These experts represent the finest military and magical minds the Federation can muster, each bringing decades of experience in combating specific types of supernatural dangers.

The Circle meets weekly in the fort's ancient council chamber, sharing intelligence, coordinating responses, and adjusting bounties and rewards for adventurers brave enough to venture into the dangerous lands beyond Federation control. Their combined expertise covers everything from ancient magical theory to practical monster hunting.

## INNER KINGDOMS ORGANIZATIONS

### Astral Circle

Peering into the vast depths of the extra dimensions of the universe, trying to perceive its secrets, is a group of spellcasting scholars and explorers called the Astral Circle. Unknown to most, this cabal of planar travelers quietly studies the multiverse from a number of towers and strongholds scattered across the Inner Kingdoms. The Circle's research into planar magic, ley-lines, and dimensional travel has proven invaluable during the campaign era's escalating supernatural threats.

### Church of Tiovin (Inner Kingdoms)

With major churches in almost every major city of the Inner Kingdoms, the Church of Tiovin ministers to the spiritual needs of its followers while assisting the sick, the hungry, and the poor. Unlike the Tiovinians from the southern nations such as Tentigo, the Inner Kingdoms churches of Tiovin do not set out on crusades or sell forgiveness for gold. The Church here emphasizes charity, community service, and moral guidance.

### Cults

Dozens of small cults dedicated to forces both primal and evil can be found scattered across the Inner Kingdoms. Taovanian cults twist the will of good and honest people, tempting mortals with great gifts in exchange for their souls. Cabals of warlocks dedicated to strange fey entities, powerful fiends, and the maddening creatures of the Far Realm further their patron's goals. Elemental entity worshipers follow nefarious plans to spread discord and chaos. Minor entities neither native to the prime material plane nor any established extra-dimensional plane gather followers and attempt to gain enough faithful to ascend to higher dimensional existence.

### Dark Whispers

One of the most feared groups of the Inner Kingdoms is purported to not even exist. The Dark Whispers serve as the secret police of the Pragos arcanists, doing their bidding in shadows and darkness. They never leave evidence of their methods, hoping to have their very existence doubted by common people. A journey to the secret prisons of the Dark Whispers is said to be always a one-way trip.

### Emerald Shields

Based out of Saerrus, this group of intellectual do-gooders focuses on ferreting out threats to the stability of the Inner Kingdoms. Occasionally crossing paths with the Federation of Man or individual nation-states over sovereignty, the Emerald Shields use unorthodox methods and make sacrifices for the whole of the populace, even when such actions raise ethical or moral questions. Although the Shields count members with many specialties, their ranks are complemented by a large number of bards, druids, and sorcerers.

### Library of the Ages

High in the mountains of Beneshire stands one of the greatest collections of knowledge anywhere in the world, founded just after the War to preserve humanity's knowledge against further world-shaking events. The Library of the Ages houses over ten thousand books within its walls, including a large number of pre-War books and documents — rarities after the devastation of eight centuries ago. Watched over by the clergy of Mau, the library enjoys a location difficult to access by ordinary means as well as numerous magical defenses and defenders. With minor allegiances to the Emerald Shields and the Astral Circle, the Library can call upon powerful support and provide those groups with rare and useful information.

### Pirates of the Crescent Coast

One of the biggest challenges to the Federation of Man's naval power comes from the Pirates of the Crescent Coast, a large organized force of pirates under a mysterious leader known as the Chimera. Enormous resources from Federation taxes are spent fighting these swashbuckling raiders, and merchants of the Inner Kingdoms lose incredible amounts of gold and goods to the pirates every year.

### Protectors of the Shire

Patrolling the many woodland areas of Beneshire are a group of rangers and paladins, many of them elven, all sworn to protect the remaining forests and lands from harm. Usually working with the Federation of Man and the royal guard of Beneshire, the Protectors are known for their generosity as well as their fury in battle.

### Umbral Hand

Fanatical worshipers of Zebulba, the Umbral Hand seeks nothing short of the annihilation of the universe. Led by a shadowy figure known as Noctuminalis, the ranks of the Umbral Hand are filled with clerics and oathbreaker paladins as well as all manner of undead. They constantly seek ways to bring down governments, destroy opposing religions, and help the arrow of time continue its path toward disorder and entropy.
