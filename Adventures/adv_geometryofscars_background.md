# THE WORLD OF MAZARIIM
## Setting Reference for The Geometry of Scars

### A Cypher System Fantasy Campaign Setting Document
#### For Use with Dragonsbane Games — Mazariim Campaign

---

# OVERVIEW

This document provides the setting context necessary to run
*The Geometry of Scars* adventure module. It covers the world of
Mazariim at 800 AW — the historical period, the relevant
geography, the civilizations whose ruins drive the adventure, and
the cosmological framework that gives ley-lines their significance.

GMs should read this document before Session 1 and keep it
available for reference throughout the campaign. Players may be
given access to sections marked **[PLAYER SAFE]** at the GM's
discretion. Sections marked **[GM ONLY]** contain information that
the PCs must discover through play.

---

# THE CALENDAR AND THE CURRENT ERA

## The Astrological Calendar

Mazariim uses a calendar measured in years After the War (AW),
counting forward from the conclusion of the Dragon War — the
cataclysmic conflict that destroyed the Akovarian civilization and
its ley-line network approximately 7,630 years before the events
of this adventure.

The adventure takes place in **800 AW**, during the autumn months
of **Mauian** (the month sacred to Mau, the god of observation)
and **Tiovary**.

## 800 AW in Context

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

The year 800 AW is a period of relative stability in the Inner
Kingdoms. The great wars of earlier centuries have subsided into
political maneuvering, trade disputes, and the slow work of
scholarship. Magic exists but operates without the infrastructure
that once amplified and organized it. The ley-line network that
once spanned continents is gone — destroyed so long ago that most
people do not know it ever existed.

**[GM ONLY]**

800 AW sits at a critical juncture in Mazariim's timeline. The
events of this adventure initiate a chain of consequences that
unfolds over the next forty-two years:

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 800 AW | The Geometry of Scars (this adventure) | Ley-line creation methodology recovered |
| 815 AW | Asti's Liberation | Adventurers free Asti from the Mask of the Pharaoh; a divine consciousness capable of providing the "seed" becomes active again |
| 819 AW | Noctuminalis Awakens | The shadow dragon seizes centralized control of the Umbral Hand; Mordecai's surviving ley-line project attracts attention |
| 824 AW | The First Mortal-Made Ley-Line | The Astral Circle, using methodology recovered in this adventure and refined over 24 years, creates the first new ley-line in Beneshire |
| 842 AW | The Current Campaign Era | The present day of the broader Mazariim campaign setting |

The PCs in this adventure do not know any of this. They are acting
at the beginning of a process whose full consequences will take
decades to unfold. The weight of this adventure comes from the
possibility it represents, not from certainty about its outcome.

# GEOGRAPHY

## The Inner Kingdoms

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

The Inner Kingdoms are a collection of nations occupying the
central landmass of the known world. They are connected by trade
routes, river systems, and — in some regions — the remains of
ancient roads built by civilizations that predate the current
nations by millennia.

The adventure takes place primarily in **Beneshire**, with a brief
excursion to the continent of **Akovar** via magical portal.

## Beneshire

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

Beneshire is the oldest continuous nation in the Inner Kingdoms.
It is a land of pastoral countryside, fortified cities, highland
moorland, and ancient ruins. Its capital is **Wyebluff**, a
university city built where the river valley meets the eastern
highlands.

Beneshire is ruled by **House Thoman** under the broader political
structure of the **Federation** — an alliance of Inner Kingdom
nations whose soldiers patrol the major roads. The crown's
authority is exercised through letters of passage and royal
charters rather than direct military governance in most regions.

The eastern highlands of Beneshire are sparsely populated — rocky
moorland dotted with shepherd's huts and drystone walls, giving
way to increasingly remote terrain as one moves east toward the
border with Arogror. This is where the adventure's exploration
sites are located.

### Wyebluff

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

The capital of Beneshire. A city of moderate size built around its
university and the commerce that the university attracts. Notable
features relevant to the adventure:

**The University Quarter:** Older buildings darkened by centuries,
narrow lanes, bookshops in former warehouses. Coffee houses where
scholars debate. The Cartographer's Rest inn, where the expedition
assembles, is located on Candle Street in this quarter.

**The Industrial District:** Warehouses, workshops, and commercial
operations. The Umbral Hand cult cell maintains a warehouse here
as part of its urban infrastructure.

**Steam Trams:** Wyebluff has a tram system running on iron rails,
powered by steam. This is a detail of technological level — the
Inner Kingdoms are not purely medieval but incorporate some
industrial elements alongside magic.

**The Astral Circle Tower:** Located south of Wyebluff proper.
A grey stone tower, unremarkable in appearance, serving as the
Astral Circle's Beneshire research facility. This is the
expedition's destination upon return.

### The Eastern Highlands

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

The terrain east of Wyebluff transitions through several zones
over approximately 150 miles:

**Pastoral Countryside (0–30 miles):** Farmland, orchards, stone
villages with smoking chimneys. Well-maintained roads patrolled by
Federation soldiers.

**Rough Country (30–60 miles):** The terrain rises. Villages thin
and disappear. Roads narrow to tracks. Moorland with rocky outcrops
and wind-bent trees.

**Open Moorland (60–90 miles):** Trackless highland. No habitation
beyond the occasional shepherd's hut. The Misty Forest lies to the
north. The Athenaeum of Echoes ruins are located here, approximately
90 miles northeast of Wyebluff.

**Remote Highlands (90–150 miles):** Rocky highlands, steep gullies,
exposed ridgelines. No roads ever existed this far east. The
Geometrist's Tower is located here, approximately 150 miles east
of Wyebluff, near the border with Arogror.

### The Library of the Ages

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

A mountain fortress in Beneshire's highlands, housing the greatest
collection of knowledge in the Inner Kingdoms. The Library is not
a public institution — access is granted to scholars, priests, and
researchers who demonstrate legitimate need. It is administered by
a religious order dedicated to the preservation and understanding
of all knowledge.

The Library's Archivist-General, Sister Caelith Arandel, is the
originator of the expedition. The Perceiver has worked at the
Library for decades, and it is the source of the Akovarian tome
that triggered the adventure.

The Library holds nine references to Akovarian civilization in its
collection — a number that reflects both the rarity of Akovarian
materials in the Inner Kingdoms and the Library's exceptional
holdings.

## Akovar

**[PLAYER SAFE — general knowledge only]**

Akovar is a continent across the ocean from the Inner Kingdoms,
accessible by months of sea travel from the colonial outpost of
**New Sabonaa** on its western coast. New Sabonaa is a Sabonaar
colonial settlement — a foothold of Inner Kingdoms civilization on
a continent that was once home to the most advanced magical
civilization in Mazariim's history.

**[GM ONLY]**

The interior of Akovar is largely unexplored by Inner Kingdoms
peoples. The ruins of the Akovarian civilization are scattered
across the continent, many still partially preserved by residual
magic. The Temple of the Seventh Convergence, which the PCs visit
via portal in Sessions 4-5, lies deep in the continental interior
— thousands of miles from New Sabonaa and inaccessible by any means
other than the portal in the Geometrist's Tower.

The Akovarian tome that triggered the expedition was recovered from
ruins near New Sabonaa and shipped to the Inner Kingdoms through
normal colonial trade channels. The fact that it reached the Library
of the Ages at all is a combination of scholarly networking and
fortunate circumstance — the University of Wyebluff recognized it
as significant (if untranslatable) and forwarded it to the Library.

# HISTORICAL CONTEXT

## The Akovarian Civilization (~8,000–6,830 BW)

**[PLAYER SAFE — scholarly knowledge, available to educated PCs]**

The Akovarian civilization flourished on the continent of Akovar
for over a thousand years before the Dragon War. They were the most
magically advanced civilization known to have existed in Mazariim —
builders of cities, engineers of magical systems, and creators of
the ley-line network that once spanned their continent and possibly
beyond.

What is known about the Akovarians in the Inner Kingdoms is
fragmentary. They wrote in a geometric script that has resisted
translation for millennia. Their ruins on Akovar are partially
preserved by residual magic but largely unexplored. The few
artifacts that have reached the Inner Kingdoms — carried by
Sabonaar colonial expeditions — are treated as objects of intense
scholarly interest.

**[GM ONLY]**

The Akovarians built the ley-line network under the guidance — or
at least with the cooperation — of the deity Asti. The network
was not merely a system of magical channels; it was an organizing
principle for ambient magical energy across vast distances. Ley-
lines channeled diffuse environmental mana into structured,
usable flows along geometric pathways of extraordinary precision.

The Akovarian contribution was engineering: they built the
crystalline matrices that encoded and maintained the geometric
templates originally provided by Asti's divine consciousness. This
allowed the network to function without requiring Asti's continuous
attention — a crucial innovation that made the network sustainable.

The Akovarians used a system of pyramidal structures as nodes in
their network. The Temple of the Seventh Convergence, which the PCs
visit, was one such node — an engineering facility where ley-lines
were monitored, maintained, and (when necessary) repaired.

### The Dragon War (~6,830 BW)

**[PLAYER SAFE — common historical knowledge]**

The Dragon War destroyed the Akovarian civilization. The details
are poorly understood in the Inner Kingdoms — it occurred over
seven thousand years ago on a distant continent. What is known:
chromatic dragons waged war against the Akovarians, the ley-line
network was destroyed (whether deliberately targeted or as
collateral damage is debated), and the Akovarian civilization did
not survive.

**[GM ONLY]**

The destruction of the ley-line network was catastrophic and
total. Every ley-line ceased functioning. The crystalline matrices
that maintained the geometric templates were shattered, deactivated,
or abandoned. The ambient mana that had been organized by the
network dispersed back into its natural, chaotic state.

What remains are scars — invisible to all but the most unusual
forms of perception, the ley-line scars are the afterimages of
where the network once ran. They carry no energy and serve no
function. They are, in essence, the fossil record of a destroyed
system.

The Temple of the Seventh Convergence survived relatively intact
because it was underground and because residual magic — now
manifesting as "wild magic" — preserved its structures long after
the civilization that built it was gone. Vorthax discovered the
portal to this Temple during his mortal life and has made
approximately a dozen expeditions through it over 3,500 years.

## The Yrugas Empire (~4,000–3,000 BW)

**[PLAYER SAFE — scholarly knowledge]**

The Yrugas Empire was a human civilization that rose to prominence
approximately four thousand years before the current era in what
is now Beneshire and the surrounding regions. It was a slave-holding
empire with significant scholarly and magical institutions — an
empire that valued knowledge but built its pursuit on the labor of
enslaved peoples.

The Yrugas period is relevant to the adventure because the
Athenaeum of Echoes and the Geometrist's Tower are both Yrugas-era
constructions. Yrugas scholars were the first in the Inner Kingdoms
to systematically study the remnants of the Akovarian civilization,
and some of their research facilities were built specifically on
or near ley-line scar intersections to study the phenomena.

### The Fall of Yrugas (~3,000 BW)

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

The Yrugas Empire fell to a massive slave revolt approximately
three thousand years before the current era. The revolt was swift
and violent — it reached the eastern highlands (where the Athenaeum
of Echoes was located) within three days. Surface structures were
burned and demolished. Underground facilities were sealed or
collapsed.

The fall of Yrugas is the reason the Athenaeum's surface is in
ruins while its underground Conjunction Archive is partially
preserved. The slaves burned what they could reach; what they could
not reach survived behind the wards that protected it.

**[GM ONLY]**

The Athenaeum of Echoes was founded approximately 3,600 BW as a
Yrugas research facility with three divisions: astronomical
observation (the Observatory), theoretical research (the Library
levels), and artifact study (the Conjunction Archive). It was built
on a three-scar ley-line nexus — the intersection of three ley-line
scars — because the nexus effects facilitated certain kinds of
magical research.

The Athenaeum's scholars acquired the seventeen Akovarian fragments
(crystalline tablets) and the Conjunction Stones through unknown
means — possibly trade with Sabonaar predecessors, possibly
excavation of Yrugas-era sites that had themselves acquired
Akovarian artifacts. The provenance is uncertain and lost to time.

### Vorthax the Geometrist (~500 BW – ~350 BW)

**[GM ONLY — PCs learn this through exploration in Scene 5]**

Vorthax was born approximately 500 years before the current era
(approximately 2,500 years after the fall of Yrugas). He was a
human scholar of extraordinary intellect and obsessive dedication
who spent his entire adult life — 155 years, an unnaturally long
lifespan sustained by magical means — studying ley-line phenomena.

Vorthax built his Tower on a five-scar nexus in the remote eastern
highlands of Beneshire. Unlike the three-scar nexus beneath the
Athenaeum, a five-scar nexus is exponentially more powerful — the
geometric relationships between five intersecting scars create
effects that bend space, time, and perception.

During his mortal life, Vorthax accomplished the following:

- Confirmed that the ley-line scars are artificial — remnants of
  an engineered system, not natural phenomena
- Developed the most comprehensive theoretical framework for
  ley-line mechanics ever produced by a mortal
- Identified the "seed" problem — the question of what initial
  input was required to create a ley-line
- Discovered the portal to the Temple of the Seventh Convergence
  on Akovar
- Made approximately a dozen expeditions through the portal during
  his lifetime
- Failed to solve the "seed" problem despite 155 years of effort

Vorthax's death (approximately 350 BW) did not end his work. The
five-scar nexus beneath his Tower, combined with the intensity of
his obsession, anchored his spirit to the physical world as a
greater wraith. He has existed as an undead entity for approximately
3,500 years, continuing his research in a state of fractured sanity
— lucid enough to negotiate and reason, but deeply damaged by
millennia of isolation and unresolved intellectual frustration.

Vorthax's Tower is maintained by the nexus energy. Its internal
geometry shifts according to his will (or his unconscious impulses).
It contains his complete library, his experimental apparatus, and
the portal to Akovar in its lowest basement level. The Tower is
avoided by locals, who describe it in folklore as a place of
wrongness — animals avoid it, plants grow according to unnatural
geometric patterns, and time feels distorted in its vicinity.

# LEY-LINES: WHAT THEY WERE AND WHAT REMAINS

## The Ley-Line Network

**[GM ONLY — this is the core knowledge the adventure seeks to
recover]**

Ley-lines were channels of organized magical energy that spanned
vast distances across Mazariim. They were not natural phenomena —
they were engineered by the Akovarian civilization with the
cooperation of the deity Asti.

The network functioned on the following principles:

**Ambient Mana:** Magical energy exists diffusely throughout
Mazariim's environment, like heat in the atmosphere. In its natural
state, this energy is chaotic, directionless, and difficult to
harness at scale.

**Geometric Templates:** Asti provided (or inspired) geometric
patterns of extraordinary mathematical precision. When these
patterns were imposed on ambient mana — through crystalline matrices
built by Akovarian engineers — the mana organized itself along the
pattern's contours, flowing in structured channels rather than
dispersing randomly. This is analogous to iron filings aligning to
a magnet's field.

**The "Seed":** The initial input required to create a ley-line was
not divine-level energy but divine-level precision. Asti's
contribution was the exactness of the geometric template, not the
power to fill it. The Akovarians discovered that this precision
could be encoded in physical crystalline structures, allowing the
network to function without Asti's continuous attention.

**Self-Sustaining Flow:** Once established, a ley-line maintained
itself — ambient mana continuously organized along the geometric
template, creating a permanent flow of structured magical energy
that could be tapped, directed, and utilized by anyone with the
knowledge to do so.

**Nexus Points:** Where multiple ley-lines intersected, the
geometric relationships created amplification effects — nexus
points where magical energy was concentrated and where certain
phenomena (enhanced casting, planar thinning, temporal distortion)
occurred naturally.

## Ley-Line Scars

**[PLAYER SAFE — the Perceiver knows this; other PCs learn it
during the adventure]**

When the ley-line network was destroyed during the Dragon War,
the physical channels disappeared but left traces — invisible
afterimages in the landscape where ley-lines once ran. These are
called ley-line scars.

Ley-line scars carry no energy and serve no function. They are
perceptible only through extremely rare and specialized forms of
magical sight. The Perceiver possesses this sight as a unique gift
— they perceive scars as lines of faded luminescence, blurred and
wavering, with brightness varying along their length and
intensifying dramatically at intersection points (nexus sites).

The scars have persisted for over 7,600 years. They are stable,
unchanging, and (until the final moment of this adventure)
completely inert. Their persistence is itself significant — it
suggests that the geometric templates imposed on the landscape by
the Akovarians altered something fundamental about the space they
occupied, leaving a permanent imprint even after the energy that
filled them ceased to flow.

## Nexus Sites

**[PLAYER SAFE — the Perceiver and Theorist know this]**

Where multiple ley-line scars intersect, residual geometric
relationships create observable effects even in the absence of
active ley-lines:

**Three-Scar Nexus (Athenaeum of Echoes):** Temporal echoes
(fragments of past events replaying as brief sensory impressions),
mild perceptual distortion, environmental effects on vegetation
and animal behavior, unreliable spellcasting. Uncomfortable but
not dangerous for short exposure.

**Five-Scar Nexus (Geometrist's Tower):** Significant spatial
distortion (internal geometry of structures built on the nexus
shifts and changes), temporal thickening (time feels slow and
heavy), severe environmental effects (plants grow according to
geometric rules, animals avoid the area entirely), strong influence
on spellcasting and perception. Prolonged exposure can affect
sanity. The nexus sustained Vorthax's undead existence for 3,500
years.

**Seven-Scar Nexus (Temple of the Seventh Convergence):** The most
powerful nexus site encountered in the adventure. Located on Akovar,
it generates wild magic surges, preserves physical structures
against millennia of decay, and retains ghost-data from the
network's last operational period. The PCs visit this site through
the portal in Sessions 4-5.

# RELIGION AND DIVINITY

## Relevant Deities

### Asti — The Creator of Ley-Lines

**[GM ONLY — theological context]**

Asti is the deity most directly connected to the ley-line network.
In the adventure's historical framework, Asti provided the
geometric templates — the divine-precision patterns — that allowed
the Akovarian engineers to create the ley-line network.

At the time of the adventure (800 AW), Asti is imprisoned within
an artifact called the **Mask of the Pharaoh**. This imprisonment
occurred at some point after the Dragon War (the exact circumstances
are beyond the scope of this adventure). Asti's liberation occurs
in 815 AW — fifteen years after the adventure — and is the subject
of a separate adventure arc.

Asti's imprisonment means that the "seed" — the divine-precision
geometric template — is not currently available from its original
source. The significance of the expedition's discovery is that the
Akovarian crystalline matrices encoded the seed in physical form,
meaning mortals can potentially replicate it without divine
assistance. However, once Asti is freed (815 AW), the combination
of recovered methodology and active divine cooperation accelerates
the ley-line recreation program dramatically.

### Mau — God of Observation

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

Mau is the deity of observation, perception, and the act of
bearing witness. Mau's faithful believe that observation is sacred
— that to perceive the world clearly is a form of worship, and
that the universe is made more real by being observed.

The adventure begins during **Mauian**, the month sacred to Mau.
The Perceiver's unique ability — Ley-Line Sight — is thematically
connected to Mau's domain. Whether the gift was granted by Mau
specifically or emerged from some other source is left to the
individual character's theology.

The Library of the Ages, where the Perceiver works, is
administered by a religious order that includes Mau among its
patron deities. The briefing letter from Sister Caelith Arandel
closes with "In Mau's sight" — a standard benediction of the order.

### Zebulba — God of Entropy and Undeath

**[PLAYER SAFE — Knowledge (Religion) check or Operative's
briefing]**

Zebulba is the deity of entropy, decay, and undeath. Zebulban
doctrine holds that all existence tends toward dissolution, that
prolonging life is a form of cruelty, and that death — properly
understood — is mercy. The faith is officially proscribed in most
Inner Kingdom nations.

The **Umbral Hand** is the primary organized cult of Zebulba in
the current era. At 800 AW, the Umbral Hand operates as a
decentralized network of semi-independent cells without centralized
leadership. (This changes in 819 AW when the shadow dragon
Noctuminalis awakens and seizes control, but that is beyond the
scope of this adventure.)

Mordecai Vellen's cell represents a pragmatic interpretation of
Zebulban doctrine: entropy is observable physical law, and aligning
with it is practical rather than mystical. Mordecai's interest in
ley-line methodology is not devotional — he seeks to adapt the
technology for negative energy channeling, which would serve his
personal ambition within the Umbral Hand hierarchy.

Sister Althea Crane represents a sincere interpretation: she lost
her family to plague and found meaning in the doctrine that death
is mercy. Her faith is genuine, which makes her more dangerous and
less persuadable than Mordecai.

# ORGANIZATIONS

## The Astral Circle

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

The Astral Circle is a scholarly organization dedicated to
understanding planar magic, ley-lines, and dimensional phenomena.
It operates as a decentralized network of research towers across
the Inner Kingdoms, each headed by a Castellan who reports to a
central council whose identities are kept secret.

The Beneshire tower, south of Wyebluff, is headed by **Castellan
Aldric Drevon**. The tower serves as both a research facility and
a coordination point for the Circle's Beneshire operations. It is
the expedition's destination upon return and the safest location
for the recovered materials.

The Astral Circle recruited the Theorist to the Beneshire tower
specifically because their theoretical work on ley-line behavior
aligned with the Circle's research interests. The expedition
represents the Circle's best opportunity to obtain empirical data
that could validate or refute decades of theoretical modeling.

The Circle maintains a network of teleportation circles connecting
its towers — a practical application of existing magical
infrastructure that hints at what a restored ley-line network might
enable at much larger scale.

## The Emerald Shields

**[PLAYER SAFE — the Operative knows this; other PCs learn what
the Operative chooses to share]**

The Emerald Shields are an intelligence organization that protects
regional stability through unorthodox methods — espionage,
counterintelligence, and the kind of subtle intervention that
governments cannot officially acknowledge. They are sometimes
described as "intellectual spies" — their agents tend to be
educated, culturally sophisticated, and skilled at operating in
scholarly and diplomatic environments.

The Shields' headquarters is in **Saerrus**, with a courier network
spanning the Inner Kingdoms. Agents operate under cover, and the
organization's existence is not widely known. The Operative is a
Shields agent whose investigation of Zebulban cult activity in
northern Beneshire converged with the scholarly expedition's
interests.

The Shields maintain an alliance with the Library of the Ages —
both organizations value knowledge, though for different reasons.
The Library seeks to preserve and understand; the Shields seek to
protect and control. The overlap is large enough to sustain a
productive relationship.

## The Umbral Hand

**[GM ONLY — the PCs learn this through the Operative's briefing
and through investigation during the adventure]**

The Umbral Hand is the primary organized cult of Zebulba in the
Inner Kingdoms. At 800 AW, it operates as a collection of
semi-independent cells with no centralized command structure. Cells
vary widely in size, capability, and interpretation of Zebulban
doctrine — from small groups of grief-stricken individuals seeking
meaning in entropy to sophisticated operations with military
resources and political connections.

**Mordecai Vellen's cell** is among the more capable. It consists
of approximately 20 operatives organized in concentric circles:

**Inner Circle (4 members):**
- Mordecai Vellen (cell leader, spellcaster)
- Sister Althea Crane (second-in-command, necromantic priest)
- Brother Cassius Thorne (scholar, Yrugas historian, coerced)
- The Whisper (professional assassin, hired)

**Support (1 member, non-combatant):**
- Garrett Hollis (logistics, merchant, coerced through blackmail)

**Outer Circle (~16 operatives):**
- Cult warriors (mercenary recruits, minimal indoctrination)
- Cult scouts (surveillance specialists)
- Urban operatives (watchers, message carriers)

The cell has operated in Beneshire for approximately five years,
systematically investigating Yrugas ruins across the region.
Mordecai developed a **site classification system** using geometric
symbols: Triangles (barren sites, no anomalous properties), Circles
(resonant sites with minor anomalies), and Squares (priority sites
with significant anomalous properties). The Athenaeum of Echoes is
classified as Site Seven (Square). The Geometrist's Tower is Site
Eight (Square).

The cell maintains two fixed bases:
- **The Manor:** A rural property in Beneshire's countryside used
  as a headquarters and planning facility
- **The Warehouse:** A commercial building in Wyebluff's Industrial
  District used for urban operations, surveillance coordination,
  and supply management

Mordecai's **private project** — adapting ley-line methodology for
negative energy channeling — is not authorized by the broader
Umbral Hand. It represents his personal bid for power within the
organization's informal hierarchy. If successful, it would give him
a capability that no other cell leader possesses, elevating his
status dramatically.

# TECHNOLOGY AND MAGIC

## The State of Magic at 800 AW

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

Magic in the Inner Kingdoms at 800 AW is functional but operates
at what scholars increasingly suspect is a fraction of its
historical potential. Spellcasters draw on ambient mana —
unstructured, diffuse magical energy present throughout the
environment — and channel it through learned techniques, innate
talent, or divine connection.

Without ley-lines, magic is:
- **Individualistic:** Each caster draws on their own connection to
  ambient mana. There is no infrastructure to amplify or organize
  magical energy at scale.
- **Limited in Range:** Spells function at personal or battlefield
  scale. The kind of continent-spanning magical effects described
  in Akovarian records are theoretically impossible under current
  conditions.
- **Unreliable Near Scar Sites:** At ley-line nexus points, the
  residual geometric imprint on the landscape interferes with
  normal spellcasting, causing unpredictable effects.

The Astral Circle's research program aims to understand whether
the ley-line network can be rebuilt — and what that would mean for
the practice of magic across Mazariim.

## Technology Level

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

The Inner Kingdoms operate at a technology level that blends
medieval and early industrial elements. Wyebluff has steam-powered
trams on iron rails. Spyglasses and signal mirrors are available
as standard equipment. Coal smoke is a common urban scent.
Printing exists. Navigation tools are sophisticated.

This is not a purely medieval world. The technology level
accommodates practical devices alongside magical practice, and the
two coexist without contradiction.

# LANGUAGES

## Languages Relevant to the Adventure

**[PLAYER SAFE]**

| Language | Who Speaks It | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Common | All Inner Kingdom peoples | The trade and daily language of Beneshire and surrounding nations |
| Draconic | Spellcasters, scholars | The language of magical incantation; all spells require Draconic verbal components |
| Madronian (Modern) | Scholars, historians | The scholarly and liturgical language of the Inner Kingdoms; descended from the language of the Yrugas Empire |
| Madronian (Archaic) | Scholars specializing in pre-current-era texts | The formal written language of the Yrugas Empire; inscriptions in Yrugas ruins are in this variant |
| Akovarian | No living speakers | A geometric script (not phonetic) used by the Akovarian civilization; resists translation without cross-reference materials |
| Thieves' Cant | Criminal and intelligence operatives | A coded jargon used by the underworld |

**Akovarian Script:** Akovarian is not a spoken language in the
conventional sense — it is a geometric writing system in which
meaning is encoded in spatial relationships between symbols rather
than in phonetic sequences. This is why it has resisted translation
for millennia. The cross-reference methodology developed during the
adventure (combining the tome, the fragments, and the Archive
documents) is the first successful approach to Akovarian translation
in the Inner Kingdoms.

# THE SCAR PULSE

## An Unresolved Mystery

**[GM ONLY]**

The adventure's final image — a ley-line scar pulsing with light
for the first time in the Perceiver's experience — is deliberately
left unresolved. It serves as a hook for the series' larger arc
and can be developed in multiple directions depending on the GM's
campaign plans.

**Possible explanations (choose one or develop your own):**

**The Recovery Theory:** The expedition's discoveries have somehow
awakened dormant properties in the scar network. By understanding
the geometry, the Theorist and Perceiver may have inadvertently
completed a circuit — the scar "knows" it has been observed with
understanding, and something in its ancient design responds.

**The Asti Theory:** Asti, imprisoned within the Mask of the
Pharaoh, senses that mortals have recovered the knowledge to
recreate ley-lines. The pulse is a faint signal — Asti reaching
through imprisonment to acknowledge the discovery. This connects
the adventure to the liberation arc (815 AW).

**The Ripple Theory:** The wild magic released during the Temple
exploration (Sessions 7-8) rippled back through the portal and
affected the nexus beneath the Tower, which in turn propagated
the effect along connected scar pathways to the Perceiver's
location. The pulse is an aftershock, not a signal.

**The Dragon Theory:** The recent draconic visitor to the Temple
(discovered by the Operative in Scene 8) has been monitoring the
ley-line network for its own purposes. The pulse is not a response
to the expedition but a coincidence — the dragon is conducting its
own experiments on the scar network, and the timing overlaps with
the PCs' return.

**The Awakening Theory:** The scar network has a residual
intelligence — not sentient, but a persistent pattern-recognition
function embedded in the geometric templates by the Akovarians.
The expedition's activities have triggered this function for the
first time in over 7,600 years. The pulse is the network beginning
to wake up.

The GM should select or develop an explanation that serves their
campaign's larger narrative. The adventure itself provides no
definitive answer, and the PCs have no way to determine the cause
without further investigation in future adventures.
