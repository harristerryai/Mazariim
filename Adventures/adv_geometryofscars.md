# HOW TO USE THIS MODULE

This module converts the complete narrative of *The Geometry of Scars*
into a playable Cypher System Fantasy campaign. The source material is
a 26-chapter, five-act novel outline set in the world of Mazariim at
800 AW.

## Structure

The adventure is organized into **five sessions** containing **ten
scenes** (two per session), distilled from the novel's twenty-six
chapters. Narrative bridges between scenes summarize events that occur
off-screen — travel, preparation, background exposition — and are
read aloud or paraphrased by the GM. The ten scenes represent the
moments where player choices, dice rolls, and character agency
determine outcomes.

## Tone and Theme

This is a story about the pursuit of knowledge against opposition,
about the weight of history, and about the geometry of what was lost
and what might be rebuilt. Combat exists but is not the primary
challenge. Investigation, exploration, negotiation, and the management
of competing priorities drive the narrative. Every session contains at
least one significant combat encounter, but the adventure rewards
scholarly characters as richly as martial ones.

## Player Characters

This module is written for **three player characters** filling
specific expedition roles, plus the option to add a fourth PC. The
PCs are not pre-built — players create their own characters using
standard Cypher System Fantasy character creation. The three
expedition roles and their narrative functions are:

**The Perceiver** — A character with a unique ability to perceive
ley-line scars that are invisible to all others. This character's
decades of patient mapping have prepared them for this expedition.
The Perceiver serves as the expedition's cartographer and navigator
through ley-line phenomena. Recommended types: Priest, Druid, or
any divine/nature caster with a custom focus granting Ley-Line
Sight (see below). Key skills: Perception, Navigation, Knowledge
(Religion or Nature), Research.

**The Theorist** — A character whose extensive mathematical and
arcane modeling has produced a framework for understanding ley-line
behavior — but without empirical data, their work remains unproven
speculation. The Theorist serves as the expedition's arcane expert
and interpreter of Akovarian knowledge. Recommended types: Mage or
any arcane caster with strong theoretical background. Key skills:
Knowledge (Arcana), Spellcraft, Research, Craft (Mathematical
instruments).

**The Operative** — An intelligence agent investigating Zebulban
cult activity in northern Beneshire whose investigation has
converged with legitimate scholarly research. The Operative serves
as the expedition's security specialist, social manipulator, and
threat assessor. Recommended types: Bard, Rogue, or any character
with strong social and stealth skills. Key skills: Deception,
Insight, Perception, Stealth, Investigation, Persuasion.

**Optional Fourth PC** — The module accommodates a fourth character
of the GM's design. Suggested roles: a Ranger or Fighter assigned
as expedition security, a second Astral Circle researcher, or a
dwarven artificer with expertise in ancient construction techniques.
Adjust encounter difficulties upward by 1 if a fourth PC is added.

### The Perceiver's Unique Ability — Ley-Line Sight

This ability is central to the adventure and must be possessed by
one PC. It can be granted as a custom focus ability, a descriptor
trait, or a GM intrusion at campaign start.

**Ley-Line Sight:** The Perceiver perceives ley-line scars as lines
of faded luminescence visible only to them. This perception is
passive, constant, and cannot be suppressed. Scars appear as
afterimages burned into the landscape — blurred, wavering, with
brightness varying along their length and intensifying dramatically
at intersection points (nexus sites). This ability functions as a
specialized form of Perception that detects ley-line phenomena
automatically, without a roll, within line of sight. At nexus sites,
the brightness can be overwhelming, requiring an Intellect defense
roll (difficulty varies by nexus complexity) to maintain focus.
Enabler.

## Starting Level and Progression

**Recommended Starting Level:** 4
**Progression Chart:** Standard or Veteran (GM's choice)
**Advancement:** Milestone-based. Characters advance one level at the
end of Session 2 (completing the Athenaeum) and one level at the end
of Session 4 (completing the Temple discoveries). Characters finish
the campaign at Level 6.

# ADVENTURE BACKGROUND

Fifteen years before Asti's liberation, twenty-four years before
the first mortal-made ley-line, and forty-two years before the
current campaign era, the world of Mazariim stands at the edge of
a breakthrough no one yet recognizes.

Nine days before the adventure begins, an untranslatable tome
arrived at the Library of the Ages from the colonial outpost of
New Sabonaa on the continent of Akovar. Carried across oceans by
Sabonaar ships and forwarded through the University of Wyebluff
(which could make nothing of it), the tome reached the Library's
scholars — who recognized what no one else had. Its forty-three
pages of geometric diagrams matched patterns that only one person
alive could perceive: a member of the Beneshire faithful who has
spent decades mapping invisible scars across the landscape.

The match was confirmed within hours. The tome depicts the same
network their gift reveals — an ancient engineered system of
magical channels built by the Akovarian civilization over seven
thousand years ago and destroyed during the Dragon War of
approximately 6,830 BW. This is the first primary source document
describing the ley-line network to reach the Inner Kingdoms.

The Archivist-General moved quickly. She contacted the Astral
Circle, where an elven theorist has spent a century building
mathematical models of ley-line behavior. She contacted the
Emerald Shields, where an operative has been investigating Zebulban
cult activity near the same Yrugas ruins the scholars now seek.
Within ten days, all were summoned to Wyebluff, capital of
Beneshire.

But the scholars are not the only ones interested. For five years,
a Zebulban cult cell led by Mordecai Vellen has been systematically
investigating Yrugas ruins across Beneshire, classifying sites by
their ley-line resonance. Mordecai's private project — adapting
ley-line methodology for negative energy channeling — represents
his bid for power within the Umbral Hand hierarchy. His intelligence
network has learned of the expedition's purpose, composition, and
departure date.

The race for lost knowledge has begun.

# ADVENTURE SUMMARY

The adventure unfolds across five sessions and ten scenes, tracing
an expedition from assembly through discovery to a desperate
homecoming.

**Session 1 — The Gathering:** The three PCs assemble at an inn in
Wyebluff's University Quarter, share intelligence across their
respective organizations, and depart northeast toward the Athenaeum
of Echoes. Along the way, the landscape grows strange as they
approach a three-scar ley-line nexus, and the Operative detects
signs of pursuit.

**Session 2 — The Athenaeum:** The expedition locates and enters the
underground Conjunction Archive beneath the ruined Athenaeum of
Echoes. They recover three critical caches of knowledge — the
Resonance Atlas, seventeen Akovarian fragments, and six Conjunction
Stones — while fighting undead remnants animated by residual nexus
energy. Cult scouts are spotted observing the ruins. Characters
advance to Level 5.

**Session 3 — The Tower:** Sixty miles further east, the expedition
enters the Geometrist's Tower, domain of Vorthax — a greater wraith
who was once a Yrugas-era ley-line researcher. After exploring his
preserved research and negotiating terms for knowledge-sharing and
portal access, the cult assaults the Tower. The PCs escape through
a portal to Akovar while Vorthax battles Mordecai's forces.

**Session 4 — Revelation:** On Akovar, the expedition explores the
Temple of the Seventh Convergence — an engineering complex preserved
by wild magic for over seven millennia. The Theorist interprets a
crystalline matrix that encodes the complete methodology for
ley-line creation, discovering the "seed" solution: ley-lines
require divine-level precision, not divine-level power, and
precision can be engineered. The Perceiver confirms the geometric
relationships through their unique sight. Evidence of recent
draconic visitation creates urgency to depart. Characters advance
to Level 6.

**Session 5 — Return:** The expedition returns through the portal to
a battle-scarred Tower, honors their bargain with Vorthax in the
adventure's most emotionally weighted scene, and begins the hunted
journey west. Mordecai's surviving forces ambush them within sight
of the Astral Circle tower. After the final confrontation — in which
Mordecai escapes but his cell is shattered — the PCs arrive at the
tower carrying complete methodology for ley-line creation. The
adventure closes with the Perceiver observing a ley-line scar pulse
with light for the first time — an unresolved mystery pointing
toward the series future.

## Pacing Guidance

**Session 1 — The Gathering:** Establish the world, the characters,
and the stakes. Scene 1 is heavy on roleplay, investigation, and
preparation — the PCs learn what they seek, why it matters, and what
opposes them. Scene 2 introduces the journey, the first signs of
being watched, and the growing bonds between characters. End the
session with the Athenaeum ruins visible on the horizon.

**Session 2 — The Athenaeum:** The first major exploration site.
Scene 3 is skill-heavy exploration — locating the entrance, bypassing
wards, navigating the ruins. Scene 4 escalates with the first combat
encounter (undead remnants), the recovery of critical materials (the
Resonance Atlas, Akovarian fragments, Conjunction Stones), and the
discovery that Zebulban scouts are observing the ruins. End the
session with the PCs departing under watch, carrying irreplaceable
knowledge. **Characters advance to Level 5.**

**Session 3 — The Tower:** The most complex social encounter in the
campaign. Scene 5 is an exploration of Vorthax's domain — the
increasingly wrong geometry, the preserved research, the growing
awareness that something intelligent inhabits the Tower. Scene 6 is
the negotiation with Vorthax followed immediately by the cult's
assault on the Tower, forcing the PCs through the portal to Akovar
while Vorthax holds the cultists. End the session with the PCs
emerging into the ruins of a civilization seven thousand years dead.

**Session 4 — Revelation:** The payoff for everything that came
before. Scene 7 is the exploration of the Temple of the Seventh
Convergence — an environment unlike anything the PCs have encountered,
preserved by wild magic for over seven millennia. Scene 8 is the
intellectual climax — the Theorist reads the crystalline matrix, the
Perceiver confirms the scar patterns, and together they recover the
complete methodology for ley-line creation. But the Operative
discovers signs that the Temple's isolation has not been complete,
and urgency builds to depart. End the session with the PCs carrying
knowledge that could reshape the world. **Characters advance to
Level 6.**

**Session 5 — Return:** Everything comes to a head. Scene 9 is the
return through the weakening portal to a battle-scarred Tower, the
honoring of the bargain with Vorthax (the adventure's most
emotionally weighted scene), and the beginning of the hunted journey
west. Scene 10 is the final confrontation with Mordecai's remaining
forces, followed by the resolution — arrival at the Astral Circle
tower, presentation of findings, and the parting of ways.

# PART 2: KEY ALLIES

These NPCs are not encountered in combat. They provide mission
context, expedition support, and information. Their profiles are
collected here for GM reference during social scenes.

## Sister Caelith Arandel — Archivist-General, Library of the Ages

**Level:** 6
**Role:** Ally / Mission Originator
**Location:** Library of the Ages (Session 1 briefing, not directly
present in scenes)

**Disposition:** Friendly (to expedition members)

**Motivation:** Preservation and understanding of knowledge. She
recognized the Akovarian tome's significance immediately and
assembled the expedition. Her authority within the Library and her
connections to the Astral Circle and Emerald Shields made the
expedition possible.

**What She Knows:** The tome's contents (diagrams matching ley-line
scar patterns), the Library's nine Akovarian references, the
existence and approximate locations of the Athenaeum of Echoes and
other Yrugas sites, the Astral Circle's ley-line research program,
the Operative's Zebulban investigation

**What She Provides:** Letters of passage from the Beneshire crown,
expedition funding, initial briefing materials, coordination between
the Library, Astral Circle, and Emerald Shields

## Aldric Drevon — Castellan, Astral Circle Tower (Beneshire)

**Level:** 5
**Role:** Ally / Expedition sponsor (Astral Circle side)
**Location:** Astral Circle Tower south of Wyebluff

**Disposition:** Friendly (professional respect for the expedition's
purpose)

**Motivation:** Advancing the Astral Circle's understanding of
ley-line phenomena. He recruited the Theorist to the Beneshire tower
specifically because their theoretical work aligned with the Circle's
interests. The expedition represents the potential breakthrough his
facility has been positioning for.

**What He Knows:** The Theorist's complete theoretical framework, the
Circle's broader ley-line research program, locations of known
"resonance zones" in Beneshire, political sensitivities around
planar research

**What He Provides:** Expedition supplies (cyphers, equipment),
the Theorist's participation, the Astral Circle tower as a safe
destination for the expedition's return

## Brother Halmund Dreyer — Archive-Master, Library of the Ages

**Level:** 4
**Role:** Ally / Information source
**Location:** Library of the Ages

**Disposition:** Friendly (colleague of the Perceiver's for decades)

**Motivation:** The Library's collection and its proper maintenance.
He is less interested in the expedition's theoretical implications
than in the physical materials it might recover — texts, artifacts,
and documents that would enrich the Library's holdings.

**What He Knows:** The Library's catalog, locations of relevant
references, the Akovarian tome's physical characteristics and
provenance, the Library's holdings on Yrugas ruins in Beneshire

### Scene 1: Convergence at Wyebluff

**Session:** 1 | **Type:** Social / Mixed
**Estimated Duration:** 60–75 minutes
**Source Chapters:** 1–5 (condensed)

#### Setting

**Location:** The Cartographer's Rest, an inn on Candle Street in
Wyebluff's University Quarter. A corner building with a faded sign
depicting a compass rose and a sleeping cat. Common room on the
ground floor, private meeting rooms upstairs.

**Sensory Details:**
- **Sight:** Late afternoon light through mullioned windows.
  University Quarter architecture — older buildings darkened by
  centuries, narrow lanes, bookshops in former warehouses. The
  common room is modest but clean, with well-worn tables and
  maps framed on the walls.
- **Sound:** The distant rhythm of steam trams on iron rails.
  Student chatter from nearby coffee houses. Inside, the creak
  of floorboards and the low murmur of scholars at their drinks.
- **Smell:** Coal smoke, old paper, yeast from the kitchen, a
  hint of pipe tobacco. The ozone-and-copper tang of the Astral
  Circle tower's wards, faintly perceptible to spellcasters.
- **Touch/Feel:** Autumn chill. The warmth of a well-maintained
  hearth. The smooth-worn cobblestones of the Quarter underfoot.
- **Atmosphere:** Scholarly, purposeful, slightly conspiratorial.
  Strangers meeting for the first time, each carrying
  knowledge the others need.

**Illumination:** Bright (afternoon, interior with windows and
hearth)
**Terrain:** Normal (interior)

#### Scene Goal

**What the PCs are trying to accomplish:** Meet each other, share
information, understand the expedition's purpose, prepare for
departure, and assess the threat posed by Zebulban cult activity.

**How the PCs learn this goal:** Each PC has been summoned by their
respective organization (Library, Astral Circle, Emerald Shields)
and given instructions to assemble at the Cartographer's Rest.
Caelith Arandel's briefing letter (provided as a handout or read
aloud) establishes the expedition's purpose.

#### Stakes

**On Success:** The PCs understand their mission, establish working
relationships, share critical intelligence (the Operative's cult
investigation, the Theorist's theoretical framework, the Perceiver's
scar maps), and depart prepared. The expedition has a two-day head
start on the cult.

**On Failure:** Partial success is the baseline — the PCs will
depart regardless. Failure in specific social checks means less
shared intelligence, weaker trust between characters, or missed
preparation opportunities. The cult may gain a closer tail on
the expedition.

**On Partial Success:** The PCs depart with incomplete understanding
of each other's capabilities or the threat they face. Specific
gaps depend on which checks were failed.

> lack is practice. The first line you create will be crude — a
> child's first sentence, halting and imperfect. But the language
> is learnable. That is what matters. That is what I could never
> prove."

This insight contextualizes the "seed" solution: ley-line creation
is not a single moment of divine power but a *practice* — a
discipline that must be learned, refined, and mastered over time.
The methodology the PCs carry is not a finished product but a
foundation. The actual recreation of ley-lines will take years
or decades of work.

**The Whisper's Fate:**

If the Whisper was not resolved in Scene 6 (e.g., escaped rather
than defeated), the PCs find the Whisper's body on Level 3 —
killed by Vorthax's Tower defenses during the battle. On the body:
a communication scroll (partially burnt) confirming that Mordecai
requested emergency support from Umbral Hand regional contacts.

If the Whisper was defeated in Scene 6 and left alive (restrained
or unconscious), the PCs find the Whisper where they left them,
still contained by whatever measures the PCs used. The Whisper
can be interrogated:

#### The Hunted Road (Second Half of Scene)

**Character Bonds (Narrative Beats):**

During the journey west, the GM should provide opportunities for
character interaction that reflects the bonds forged through shared
discovery:

- **The Perceiver and the Theorist:** The Theorist asks the
  Perceiver to describe what they see when they look at the scars.
  The Perceiver tries, in their careful way, and the Theorist
  realizes that the Perceiver's perception contains information
  their mathematics cannot capture. A bridge between abstract and
  physical that neither can build alone.
- **The Operative and Both Scholars:** The Operative watches them

  carrying it — and has decided that the people matter anyway.

- **All Three:** The shared weight of what they carry. Not just
  materials but responsibility. If they die, the knowledge dies.
  The first ley-line recreation may be delayed by decades or
  centuries.

#### Scene Transition

The journey west concludes with the Astral Circle tower visible
on the horizon — and Mordecai's final ambush between the expedition
and sanctuary. Transition directly to Scene 10.

**Recovery Opportunity:** Multiple rest opportunities during the
multi-day journey. The PCs should be at or near full resources
for the final confrontation — the tension comes from the pursuit,
not from resource depletion.

### Scene 10: The Last Convergence

**Session:** 5 | **Type:** Combat / Social
**Estimated Duration:** 60–75 minutes
**Source Chapters:** 25–26

#### Setting

**Location:** Open countryside within sight of the Astral Circle
tower — rolling farmland, stone walls, a small wood, and a stream
crossing. The tower is visible less than two miles ahead. Safety
is close enough to see but too far to reach before the ambush
springs.

**Sensory Details:**
- **Sight:** Late afternoon light. The Astral Circle tower rising
  above the southern treeline — grey stone, unremarkable to
  most eyes, the most welcome sight any of the PCs have seen.
  The terrain: a road crossing a shallow stream via a stone
  bridge, flanked by low stone walls and a copse of oak trees.
  Perfect ambush ground.
- **Sound:** Birdsong (the first in days — they are back in
  civilized Beneshire). Running water. Then: silence. The birds
  stop. A horse's bridle jingles from behind the stone wall.
- **Smell:** Autumn countryside — fallen leaves, damp earth, clean
  water. Then: nothing changes but something shifts. The smell
  of combat is not a smell but a feeling.
- **Touch/Feel:** The tension of being close to safety and knowing
  it is too easy. The Operative feels it first — the professional
  recognition of ground chosen for killing.
- **Atmosphere:** The final test. Everything the expedition has
  gathered, everything they have learned, everything they have
  become — tested against a desperate enemy who has nothing left
  to lose.

**Illumination:** Bright (afternoon daylight)
**Terrain:** Mixed — road (normal), stream crossing (difficult
terrain, 10 ft wide, 2 ft deep), stone walls (partial cover, 3 ft
high), copse (difficult terrain, concealment)

#### Scene Goal

**What the PCs are trying to accomplish:** Survive Mordecai's final
ambush, protect the irreplaceable knowledge they carry, and reach
the Astral Circle tower.

#### Stakes

**On Success:** The PCs defeat Mordecai's forces, protect their
materials, and reach the Astral Circle tower with complete
methodology for ley-line creation. The adventure succeeds.

**On Failure:** If the PCs are defeated, the materials are seized
by Mordecai. Ley-line creation methodology falls into Umbral Hand
hands. The consequences ripple through the campaign timeline (this
is a catastrophic failure scenario that the GM should avoid through
design — the encounter should be challenging but winnable).

**On Partial Success:** The PCs survive and reach the tower but
suffer casualties (a PC is debilitated) or lose some materials
(some documents are destroyed in the fighting). The methodology
is mostly preserved but with gaps.

#### Denouement: Lines of Light Undrawn

The adventure's final moments are social and narrative rather than
mechanical. The GM should allow the players to roleplay the
following beats:

**Presenting the Findings:** Drevon and the Astral Circle researchers
receive the expedition's discoveries. The magnitude is acknowledged
quietly — scholars recognize significance through silence, not
celebration. The Theorist presents the "seed" solution. The
Perceiver presents their confirmed maps. The Operative presents the
intelligence on Mordecai's cell.

**Credit Given:** The PCs explicitly credit Vorthax's contribution,
honoring the bargain. His name is recorded in the Astral Circle's
official documentation as a co-discoverer. Whether he perceives
this acknowledgment through the ley-line scars is unknown — but the
promise is kept.

**Parting of Ways:**

- **The Theorist** remains at the Astral Circle tower. Their path:
  experiments to design, methodology to refine, decades of work
  to transform theoretical possibility into practical reality.
  They have what they have wanted for a century — proof that their
  life's work was correct.
- **The Operative** departs for Emerald Shields headquarters. Their
  path: intelligence delivered, Mordecai's cell structure exposed,
  and bonds that transcend professional obligation. They have
  learned that some things are worth protecting not because a
  handler assigns them but because they matter.
- **The Perceiver** departs for the Library of the Ages. Their
  path: validated maps and new purpose. They have spent decades
  observing. Now they understand what they were observing — and
  why their gift was given.

# PART 8: CONTINGENCY PLANNING

## Critical Information — Three-Source Rule

### The Athenaeum's Location

1. **Primary:** Library briefing materials (Scene 1)
2. **Secondary:** The Perceiver's ley-line sight guides them to the
   three-scar intersection (Scene 2)
3. **Failsafe:** The Theorist's theoretical models predict the
   intersection; the Resonance Atlas confirms it retroactively

### The Archive Entrance Location

1. **Primary:** The Perceiver's ley-line sight identifies the scar
   convergence point directly over the entrance (Scene 3)
2. **Secondary:** Library records describe the entrance's general
   location on the complex's northern edge (Scene 3)
3. **Failsafe:** Research check finds the alternate ventilation
   shaft entrance (Scene 3)

### Vorthax's Nature and Intentions

1. **Primary:** His journals on Levels 4-5 (Scene 5)
2. **Secondary:** The Tower's atmospheric clues — cold, shadows,
   the sense of being watched (Scene 5)
3. **Failsafe:** Vorthax announces himself; his nature is
   self-evident upon manifestation (Scene 5)

### The Portal's Existence and Location

1. **Primary:** Vorthax reveals it during negotiation (Scene 6)
2. **Secondary:** His journals reference it (Scene 5, Research
   check)
3. **Failsafe:** The portal is visible on Basement Level 3 if
   the PCs explore that far; it announces itself through visual
   and sensory effects

### The "Seed" Solution

1. **Primary:** The Theorist's interpretation of the crystalline
   matrix (Scene 8)
2. **Secondary:** Archive Vault documents explain the principle
   independently (Scene 8, Research check)
3. **Failsafe:** Vorthax's final insights describe the concept in
   non-technical terms (Scene 9) — even if the matrix
   interpretation fails, Vorthax provides the answer

### Cult Pursuit

1. **Primary:** The Operative's professional instincts and
   Perception checks (Scenes 2, 4, 9)
2. **Secondary:** Evidence of cult activity at the ruins (Scenes
   3, 4)
3. **Failsafe:** The cult attacks the Tower regardless of whether
   the PCs detected pursuit (Scene 6); the final ambush occurs
   regardless (Scene 10)

## Common Player Deviations

**The PCs refuse to negotiate with Vorthax and attempt to take his
research by force:**
Vorthax is Level 8 in his nexus domain. A direct confrontation is
survivable but extremely costly and will not achieve the objective —
Vorthax's research is written on walls, stored in thousands of
volumes, and encoded in apparatus. It cannot be seized by force in
any practical timeframe. If combat occurs, Vorthax fights to drive
them out, not to kill. The GM should make clear through the combat's
difficulty that negotiation is the intended path.

**The PCs attempt to destroy Vorthax:**
He cannot be permanently destroyed while the ley-line scars exist.
Temporary banishment is possible (difficulty 8 for a Level 6 party)
but he reforms within days. The PCs could use the portal while he
is banished, but they would return to a hostile wraith — and they
still need his final insights (Scene 9) for the best outcome.

**The PCs refuse to return through the portal after discovering the
Temple:**
The portal is their only route home. Akovar is thousands of miles
from the Inner Kingdoms, reachable only by months of ocean travel
from New Sabonaa on the western coast. The PCs have limited supplies.
Staying on Akovar is not viable.

**The PCs attempt to carry the crystalline matrix:**
It is fifty feet tall, integrated into the temple's bedrock, and
sustained by seven-thousand-year-old enchantments. Moving it is
physically and magically impossible. The documentation approach
(technical drawings + selected texts) is the only viable method.

**A PC dies during the adventure:**
The adventure can continue with two PCs, though the final combat
(Scene 10) should be adjusted downward (remove one cult warrior,
reduce Althea's level by 1). If the dead PC is the Perceiver,
ley-line perception is lost — the expedition can still succeed but
the Perceiver's confirmation (Scene 8) is replaced by indirect
evidence. If the Theorist dies, the matrix interpretation (Scene 8)
becomes much harder. If the Operative dies, the social and security
dimensions are weakened but the scholarly mission can still succeed.

**The PCs split the party:**
The adventure is designed for a small party (3 PCs) that stays
together. If they split, the GM should present compelling reasons
to reunite. If they insist on splitting, run parallel scenes quickly
and bring them back together before major encounters.

*From Sister Caelith Arandel, Archivist-General, Library of the Ages
To the assembled expedition members
16 Mauian, 800 AW*

consistent. The probability of coincidence is negligible.

contain such materials in its underground Conjunction Archive.

You are assembled because your skills complement each other in ways

provide security assessment.

Go. Find what the ages have preserved. Bring it home.

In Mau's sight,

# APPENDIX C: SERIES HOOKS

## Unresolved Threads for Future Adventures

### The Scar Pulse
The novel's final image — a ley-line scar pulsing with light for
the first time in the Perceiver's experience — is deliberately
unresolved. Possible explanations for future adventures:
- The expedition's discoveries have somehow awakened dormant
  properties in the scar network
- Asti, imprisoned within the Mask of the Pharaoh, senses that
  mortals have recovered the knowledge to recreate ley-lines
- The wild magic released during the Temple exploration rippled
  back through the portal and affected the nexus
- Something else entirely — connected to the series' larger arc

### Mordecai's Survival
Mordecai escaped the final confrontation wounded but alive. His cell
is shattered — Althea captured or killed, Cassius surrendered, the
Whisper eliminated, outer circle scattered, infrastructure
compromised by Cassius's intelligence. But Mordecai himself possesses
fragments of ley-line knowledge gleaned from his cell's five-year
investigation. He is dangerous, desperate, and now personally
motivated by revenge as well as ambition.

### The Temple Visitor
The Operative discovered evidence of recent visitation at the Temple
of the Seventh Convergence — claw marks suggesting something
draconic. The Dragon War ended over 7,600 years ago, but dragons are
long-lived and some chromatic dragons may retain knowledge of or
interest in the Akovarian ley-line sites. A dragon investigating the
Temple represents a significant future threat or potential ally.

### The Archive's Contents
The Athenaeum's Conjunction Archive and the Temple's Archive Vault
both contain far more material than the expedition could recover.
Future expeditions to either site could yield additional knowledge,
but both locations have been compromised — the cult knows about the
Athenaeum, and the Temple's unknown visitor may return.

### Noctuminalis (819 AW)
Nineteen years after the adventure, Noctuminalis awakens and seizes
control of the Umbral Hand. Mordecai's ley-line project — now
partially informed by what his cell learned during the events of
this adventure — may attract Noctuminalis's attention.

### Asti's Liberation (815 AW)
Fifteen years after the adventure, adventurers free Asti from the
Mask of the Pharaoh. The ley-line methodology the PCs recovered
becomes exponentially more valuable once a divine consciousness
capable of providing the "seed" is active again.

### The First Mortal-Made Ley-Line (824 AW)
Twenty-four years after the adventure, the Astral Circle creates
the first mortal-made ley-line in Beneshire. The methodology the
PCs recovered is the direct foundation for this achievement.
