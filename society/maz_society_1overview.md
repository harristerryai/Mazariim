## Technology

The planet Mazariim has been around for billions of years, and civilizations began to rise around 20,000 years in the past. Since then, the level of what we would consider "technology" has fluctuated and has been influenced by magic as well. We can define the progress levels of society using the following descriptions.

### Progress Levels

**PL0 — Tribal / Stone Age**
The Stone Age was a period of history that encompasses the first widespread use of simple technology and the spread of sentient races across the world. It ends with the development of agriculture, the domestication of certain animals, and the smelting of copper ore to produce metal. Most societies were formed on a tribal or racial level.

**PL1 — Agrarian / Bronze Age**
The Bronze Age was a period in civilization's development when the most advanced metalworking (at least in systematic and widespread use) consisted of techniques for smelting copper and tin from naturally occurring outcroppings of ore, and then alloying those metals to cast bronze. It is towards the end of this period that iron is discovered. Most societies centered around the village or town, with farming the major part of most inhabitants' lives.

**PL2 — Feudalistic / The Middle Ages**
The Middle Ages formed the period thought of in traditional fantasy settings. Iron use became widespread, as well as various races had mastered the use of their nearby resources, such as mithril, adamantine, darkwood, and other exotic sources. It is at this time that literacy began to be slightly more commonplace, trade began spanning parts of the world in a greater amount, and news and information began to become more widespread. Land ownership and the feudal system as well as other authoritarian systems are the main types of society, although many others might exist within a similar technological level.

**PL3 — Cosmopolitan / Renaissance**
The Renaissance was a period in history during which the advances just before the use of electricity become known. Great thinkers and their writings were known to more of the common inhabitants of the world, with more and more wanting to spread knowledge, create the uncreated, and seek out the unknown. Races are discovering more and more unique inventions and pseudo-technologies (analogous technologies to our own, but using magic) to things being developed in the 17th and 18th centuries of our world. It is in this era that universities and schools are becoming more prominent. Many of the wealthy live in comfort, with self-governance beginning to take hold in some parts of society, and many of the middle or lower class enjoy a somewhat comfortable existence when compared to the lives of the typical peasant in a PL2 culture.

## Languages

There are many languages and dialects spoken across Mazariim. Although not nearly as many as in our real-world (current estimates are that there were 10,000 languages in use on Earth in the 15th century), there are still several branches of each.

Using the correct dialect gives the speaker up to a +2 circumstance bonus to Cha-based skill checks that involve speaking, and up to a -2 circumstance penalty to the same checks if speaking a different dialect.

### Human Languages

Human languages are all thought to have developed separately across the human lands, unlike the elven and dwarven languages. Some human languages generally all use the common letters that have evolved with Common (Madra, Sabo, Laveran) while others use pictograms (Ejao, Varkonae) or have combinations of both (Zelato, Chak'atan).

**Common** — The "Common" language is a more elaborate version of the "Trade" language that naturally developed as human nations interacted and bartered. Trade was originally made of words from Madra, Sabo, and Laveran, a meshing of the three used for business deals in the more civilized parts of Nigal. After the War, the Trade language was combined with many elven, dwarven, gnomish, and halfling words that were more prevalent, and thus the Common language was born, used a great deal in the Inner Kingdoms and Northern Dwarven Nations but now across the globe, even by some across the seas in Vand'ardhon.

**Chak'atan** — The Chak'ti dialect is known as Chak'atan, a rough and guttural language with harsh consonants and sound effects.

**Ejao** — The Ejiin language, known as Ejao, is a flowing language with a wide variety of sounds that emphasize different parts of the same words for different meanings. The written form consists of thousands of pictograms that can be written in an artistic form, an Ejiin art form said to take decades to truly master.

**Laveran** — The Laveri speak Laveran, a language that contains only oral vowels and very contrasting consonant combinations.

**Madra** — The regional dialect of the Madronians is known as Madra, a flowing language with over fifteen vowels and many silent letters.

**Nondalhiem** — The Nond speak a language known as Nondalhiem which contains many vowels with varied accents as well as interesting and sometimes harsh double consonant sounds.

**Sabo** — The Sabonaar predominately speak a regional dialect called Sabo, a practical language that is spelled phonetically and that the old Trade language and now Common draw from greatly.

**Varkonae** — The language of the Varkonans, originally made of pictograms, Varkonae eventually transitioned into its alphabet which greatly expanded the accessibility of the written form of Varkonae to its people.

**Zelato** — The Azala language is known as Zalato, a combination of letters and pictograms with strange combinations of sounds and double vowels.

### Elven Languages

All of the elven languages are offshoots of the original High Elven that Minerva helped the elven nations develop over centuries for their histories and writings. Elven uses its form of letters in a flowing script.

**Elda'lam (Common)** — The elven form of Common.

**Tarquesta (High Elven)** — Spoken primarily by the Ya'sehrim, Tarquesta is the ancient form of the elven language that persists in its almost exact form to this day.

**Yav'lam (Trade)** — The Yav'daerim speak Yav'lam, the elven version of a mixture of common and what few elven terms from other dialects that a Trade language would need.

**Vercalam (Wild Elven)** — Wild Elven, spoken predominately by the Tawarim, is a dialect of Elven that over centuries became less formal, less complex, and intertwined with subtle sounds and gestures.

**Aearlam** — The Gaear'rim speak Aearlam, a dialect of High Elven and Aquan, mixed in from the long use of various water elementals and creatures by the high priests of the area.

**Faronlam** — The wandering hunters known as Farondrim predominately speak Faronlam, a dialect of elven that includes a wide variety of silent hand signals in addition to its simple elven structure.

**Lirehlam** — The Aeglirim speak a dialect of elven known as Lirehlam, a form of elven that changed over the centuries with the lessened contact the elves of this nation have with other elves.

**Ra'que** — The Raladrim and wemics of the great plains regions of Vand'ardhon predominately speak Ra'que, a dialect of elven that includes many of the growls and sounds coming from the wemic language.

### Dwarven Languages

All of the forms of dwarven developed from High Dwarven, the ancient form of its language. It has its unique alphabet designed not just for writing but for sculpting and engraving.

**Rhundawi (Common)** — The dwarven form of Common.

**Dal'rhundawi (High Dwarven)** — The ancient High form of dwarven.

**Rhunurbar (Trade)** — "Trade words", which is a combination of Dal'rhundawi with some Gnomish and Chak'atan.

### Halfling Languages

**Shalaz** — Hin speak their racial language, Shalaz, with no other dialects ever developed. It uses the elven alphabet for its writing.

### Gnome Language

**Rhungnom** — Gnomes speak Rhungnom, the common ancestral language of gnomes. Rhungnom uses the dwarven alphabet for its letters.

### Draconic

Draconic is the oldest language of Mazariim, with its unique combination of symbols, pictograms, and combinations. It was the language of the Drakaii, a civilization of the progenitors of dragons that existed over 20 million years ago. Draconic deserves mention as it is the language that all arcane magics are written, and was taught to early humans by dragons. Arcane spellcasting and everything related to it from scrolls to syllables are in Draconic.

## Calendar of Mazariim

Time and the marking of weeks, months, and years are similar to our real world, with a few simplifications to make things easier to calculate.

There are 7 days in a week:

| Day | Activities |
|-----|-----------|

| Toilday | Work |
| Wealday | Work |
| Oathday | Work, pacts signed, oaths sworn |
| Fireday | Work |
| Starday | Work |
| Sunday | Rest, religion |

### Years

Years are kept track of in many systems, the main one being the years since the War. Years since are named AW (After War) and BW (Before War). It is currently the year 811 AW. Elves and dwarves both use a system from their home regions that date back to their first encounters with their patron entities, Minerva and Mordrion. It is currently the year 4213 ER (Elven Reckoning) and 2677 DR (Dwarven Reckoning). These systems of years are often used in regions of the elves and the dwarves, and with their inhabitants, namely halflings with elves and gnomes with dwarves. Most human lands and shared lands use the AW system.

## Religious Holidays & Festivals

The various cultures and faiths of Mazariim celebrate numerous holidays throughout the year. Below are the major religious festivals organized by deity and culture. Local festivals, saint days, and clan-specific celebrations add dozens more holidays to regional calendars.

### Dwarven Holidays (Followers of Mordrion)

**Day of Unity** (15 Minerva / March 15) — Commemorates the end of the Dwarven Wars in 1849 BW when Mordrion brokered peace between the Gormdawi and Bryndawi. Celebrated with feasts, the exchanging of gifts between clans, and ceremonial peace treaties.

**The Forging** (1 Mord / June 1) — The holiest day for followers of Mordrion. Every forge in every dwarven hold is blessed at dawn, and master smiths present their finest works in temple ceremonies. Young dwarves come of age and receive their first warhammer or tools.

**Stoneshaper's Vigil** (30 Zebul / December 30) — Honors the dwarven hero Demious Stoneshaper who slew Zuel's avatar. Dwarves hold vigil through the night, recounting tales of heroes past and present, and warriors sharpen their weapons in preparation for the new year.

**Ancestor's Feast** (Various dates by clan) — Each clan celebrates its own founding and honors its lineage. Hammerpriests recite the names of ancestors, and families gather to share stories of those who came before.

### Halfling Holidays (Followers of Hinterhin)

Halflings celebrate over three dozen holidays throughout the year. The most important include:

**First Planting** (1 Quarz / January 1) — The new year begins with the blessing of seeds and the planting of the first crops. Greenwardens bless every field, and families exchange seeds as tokens of good fortune.

**Hearthfire Day** (15 Mord / June 15) — Celebrates community and family. Villages organize games, feasts, and gift exchanges. It is traditional to invite someone new into your home for a meal—a stranger, a traveler, or a neighbor you wish to know better.

**Midsummer Feast** (21 Pyran / July 21) — The longest day of the year is celebrated with an all-day festival featuring music, dancing, games, and an enormous communal feast. Every family contributes a dish, and the feasting continues until sunset.

**Harvest Home** (1 Mauian / October 1) — The first major harvest is celebrated with thanksgiving, parades of decorated wagons, and harvest competitions (largest pumpkin, best pie, etc.). The day ends with a great bonfire and storytelling.

**Festival of Lanterns** (30 Zebul / December 30) — On the longest night of the year, halflings light thousands of lanterns and candles to guide Hinterhin's blessings into the new year. Families gather to share stories, sing songs, and make wishes for the year ahead.

### Gnomish Holidays (Followers of Rivoleen)

**Enlightenment Eve** (1 Quarz / January 1) — The new year begins with reflection and goal-setting. Gnomes meditate on the past year, identify what they wish to learn or create in the year ahead, and make resolutions to pursue knowledge and freedom.

**The Great Debate** (30 Quarz / January 30) — A mid-winter tradition where gnomes engage in friendly but intense debates on philosophical, scientific, and ethical topics. Winners receive not prizes but respect and the opportunity to have their ideas published and shared.

**Festival of Flight** (21 Garn / April 21) — Celebrates freedom and the release of caged birds. Gnomes craft beautiful mechanical birds and release them into the sky as symbols of liberation. It is also traditional to free any caged creatures (if safe to do so) and to help someone in need of freedom—whether from debt, a bad situation, or their own fears.

**Tinker's Jubilee** (15 Mord / June 15) — A celebration of invention and creativity. Gnomes showcase their latest inventions, hold competitions for the most ingenious gadgets, and share their discoveries with the community. Rivoleen's clergy bless workshops and offer inspiration to struggling inventors.

**Day of Questions** (1 Zebul / December 1) — The beginning of winter is marked by a day of philosophical debate and questioning. Communities gather in temples to discuss profound questions, challenge old assumptions, and share new ideas. No topic is off-limits.

### Human Holidays (Followers of Tiovin)

Human religious holidays vary greatly by region and sect. The most universal include:

**New Year's Day** (1 Quarz / January 1) — Celebrated throughout human lands with prayer, reflection, and community gatherings. Many make pilgrimages to temples of Tiovin.

**Eve of Hallows** (Month of Xan / February) — A solemn holiday remembering the dead and honoring ancestors. Families visit gravesites, light candles, and offer prayers for departed loved ones.

**Day of Thanks** (Month of Tiovary / November) — A holiday of thanksgiving and gratitude for blessings received throughout the year. Communities gather for communal feasts and charitable works.

**Various Regional Holidays** — Different sects and kingdoms celebrate unique holidays tied to local saints, historical events, and seasonal cycles.

### Elven Holidays (Followers of Minerva)

Elves celebrate solstice and equinox ceremonies as well as additional major celebrations:

**Ae'yelin** (Winter Solstice / New Year) — A day of reflection and renewal.

**Au'celu** (Spring Equinox) — Celebration of the Bloom.

**Ae'laer** (Summer Solstice) — Festival of Green Beauty.

**Ae'iavas** (Autumnal Equinox) — Honoring the Harvest.

**Enti'nim** (Winter Solstice / 1 Quarz / New Year) — "Longest Night of Cold" — The winter solstice celebration begins on the new year and continues for three days. Elves gather in meditation, storytelling, and reflection on the year past.

**Apaire'aur** (3 Quarz / January 3) — "Morning of Victory" — The concluding day of the Enti'nim celebration. Commemorates the end of the War to End All Wars and honors those who sacrificed for elvenkind. Ceremonies include lighting of sacred fires and the recitation of names of the fallen.

### Arcane Holidays (Followers of Alagalothor)

Followers of the Dragon Mage celebrate magic in all its forms:

**Night of Stars** (Summer Solstice evening) — A revered day of sharing magics, usually celebrated on the evening of the summer solstice. It is said that those who share their magic with another spellcaster on this night will gain luck for the rest of the year. Arcanologists organize public displays of magical prowess, illusions, and fireworks.

**Celebration of Mana** (Third Sunday after Autumnal Solstice, late Mauian / October) — A time of festivities, great magical shows of illusions and fireworks, and of making pacts and truces. Magic academies hold open houses, and new spells are often revealed for the first time.

### Holidays of Garnak (Beasts & The Hunt)

**The Winter Howl** (Winter Solstice / Longest Night) — On the longest, darkest night of the year, Garnak's followers venture into the wilderness to hunt nocturnal predators—wolves, owls, mountain lions, winter wolves. They hunt through the entire night, and at dawn they return to howl in unison, thanking Garnak for another year of successful hunts. This is the one true "fixed" holiday in Garnak's faith.

**The First Kill** (Personal / Coming of Age) — When a young hunter makes their first solo kill of a dangerous beast (wolf, boar, bear), they undergo a rite of passage. The young hunter returns with proof (the heart or head), and the tribe/pack celebrates by feasting on the kill. The young hunter is given their first blood-mark scar and recognized as a true hunter.

**The Great Hunt** (Autumn / Pre-Winter Stockpiling) — The entire community goes on a coordinated hunt to stockpile meat for winter. This is the largest hunt of the year, targeting herds of deer, elk, or bison. The hunt lasts 3–7 days, and the resulting feast lasts another 3 days. Offerings are made to Garnak by burning the heart of the largest kill.

### Holidays of Paladine (Honor, Justice, Chivalry)

**The Day of Oaths** (15 Tio / April 15) — The holiest day in Paladine's calendar. On this day, new paladins take the Oath of the Silver Dragon and are formally inducted into the Order. Temples hold grand ceremonies, and existing paladins renew their vows. It is a day of reflection, commitment, and celebration.

**The Vigil of Justice** (30 Tio / April 30) — A solemn day honoring paladins who have fallen in the line of duty. Followers spend the day in quiet reflection, visiting graves and memorials. At sunset, they light candles and recite the names of the fallen.

**The Silver Dawn** (1 Paladine / May 1) — Celebrates Paladine's arrival on Mazariim (1414 BW). It marks the beginning of the Age of Paladins and the spread of justice across the world. It is a day of hope, renewal, and rededication to righteous action.

**The Dragon's Justice** (15 Paladine / May 15) — A day dedicated to *doing* justice, not merely talking about it. Paladins are encouraged to actively seek out and oppose evil—whether by hunting down bandits, freeing slaves, or challenging corrupt officials. It is a day of action.

**The Feast of Honor** (1 Quarz / January 1 – New Year) — Shared with many cultures, but Paladine's followers celebrate it as a day of honor and integrity. Oaths made on this day are considered especially binding. Families gather, debts are settled, and disputes are resolved peacefully.

### Holidays of Polyvin (Luck, Trade, Opportunity, Merchants)

Polyvin worship is widely accepted and popular, especially among halflings, merchants, travelers, and the common folk.

**The Lucky Day** (1 Polyvin / March 1) — The holiest day of Polyvin's calendar. Followers believe this day is especially blessed with good luck. Fortune Halls host massive gambling tournaments, merchants offer "lucky sales," and people take risks they've been postponing.

**The Day of Open Doors** (15 Polyvin / March 15) — A day dedicated to new opportunities. Fortune Halls offer free career counseling, help people find jobs, and connect merchants with partners. Tradition says to "open a new door" on this day—start a project, meet new people, or take a journey.

**The Traveler's Toast** (1st day of each season — 1 Quarz, 1 Tio, 1 Paladine, 1 Zebul) — Four times a year, Polyvin's followers gather to honor travelers—those who have left on journeys, those who have returned, and those who died on the road. It is a bittersweet celebration.

**The Fortune's Reversal** (Personal — when luck changes dramatically) — Not a fixed date but a personal observance. When someone's luck dramatically changes (winning big, losing everything, narrowly avoiding disaster), they visit a Fortune Hall to thank Polyvin or seek guidance.

### Holidays of Eilistraee (Song, Moon, Beauty, Drow Redemption)

Eilistraee worship is widely accepted among surface elves and in progressive communities, though moon drow face prejudice in many regions.

**The First Dance** (13 Rivoleen / June 13) — Anniversary of Eilistraee's arrival on Mazariim (1377 BW). Moon drow and their allies gather in moonlit groves to reenact the "first dance"—when Eilistraee's original refugees danced for the first time in freedom. Observances include all-night dancing under moonlight, singing of the *Hymn of Freedom*, ritual sword-blessing, new Silverhair initiations, sharing of escape stories, moonflower wine, and a dawn greeting (watching sunrise together as a symbol of hope).

**The Moonlit Hunt** (Each full moon — 12 times per year) — Monthly celebration combining worship and survival training. Followers hunt under moonlight, dance to honor the prey, and feast together. Essential for teaching drow refugees wilderness survival skills.

**The Night of Silver Tears** (27 Zebulba / December 27) — Solemn remembrance of drow who died trying to escape Lolth's tyranny—those killed by Lolth's assassins, those who couldn't adapt and took their own lives, those recaptured and dragged back to the Underdark.

**The Sword Moon** (Winter Solstice / December 21, shortest day) — Celebrates the longest night—sacred to moon goddesses. Festival of music, storytelling, and swordplay. Young drow who've completed their first year of freedom receive their first blessed bastard sword.

### Holidays of Gyru (Invention, Artifice, Dwarves, Innovation)

Gyru worship is widely accepted, especially in cities with engineering communities and dwarf settlements.

**The Day of Ascension** (11 Minerva / May 11) — Anniversary of Gyru's ascension through the Apotheosis Engine (1,111 BW). Celebrates mortal potential and innovation. Workshops hold invention competitions and display their finest work.

**The Feast of Gears** (1st day of each season — 1 Quarz, 1 Tio, 1 Paladine, 1 Zebul) — Quarterly gatherings where Gearwrights share research, discuss breakthroughs, and collaborate on large-scale projects. Resembles academic conferences combined with maker faires.

**The Night of Failures** (13 Zebulba / December 13) — Solemn but hopeful observance where followers share their greatest failures—inventions that exploded, theories proven wrong, abandoned projects. Purpose is to learn, improve, and remember that failure is part of innovation.

**The Hammer's Toll** (Personal — when completing a masterwork) — Not a fixed date but a personal celebration. When a follower completes a significant invention, they ring a bell to alert the workshop. Others gather to witness the first test.

### Holidays of Ashura (Honor, Light, Healing — Ejiin Regional Deity)

Ashura worship is central to Ejiin culture but virtually unknown outside the Lands of Honor (Yashaki islands).

**The Festival of First Light** (Spring Equinox / March 20) — Celebrates Ashura's mythical awakening. Samurai renew oaths, new warriors take first vows, communities honor heroes. Observances include dawn ceremonies, oath renewals, sword blessings, tournament competitions, cherry blossom viewings, communal feasts, and storytelling of legendary samurai.

**The Day of Fallen Blossoms** (Summer — varies by region, anniversary of major battles) — Solemn remembrance of samurai who died in battle. Names read aloud, offerings made, stories retold. Honors sacrifice, not mourns it.

**The Trial of Steel** (Autumn Equinox / September 22) — Day of testing. Young samurai undergo trials; clergy demonstrate skills; communities test readiness. Observances include combat trials, endurance tests, healing demonstrations, bushido debates, challenges of courage, and promotion ceremonies.

**Tengoku's Vigil** (Winter Solstice / December 21) — All-night vigil when Khan is strongest. Resist sleep, food, comfort—proving discipline. Symbolically "defeat" Khan.

**The Duel of Shadows** (Lunar Eclipse — rare) — Ashura and Khan's battle manifests visibly. Ritual duels honor the cosmic struggle.

### Old Gods Holidays (Ancient/Elemental Deities)

The Old Gods—primal forces that predate the patron deities—are rarely worshiped in modern times, but their ancient observances persist among isolated cults, elementalists, and traditionalists.

#### Mau the Oracle (Secrets, Observation)

**The Longest Night** (Winter Solstice) — Watchers meditate sunset to sunrise, seeking visions when the Astral Plane draws closest.

**The Star Alignment** (every 19 years) — Prophecy recording at ancient observatories during rare celestial alignments.

#### Pyrantor (Fire, Sun)

**Festival of the Burning Sun** (Summer Solstice) — Fire dances, coal-walking, massive bonfires from dawn to dusk.

**Dawn Vigil** (daily in Varkona) — Ra-Priests greet the sun at pyramid peaks.

**The Ash Moon** (first new moon after Autumn Equinox) — Honoring those who died in flames.

#### Iquinias (Water, Oceans)

**Offering of First Catch** (Spring, varies by coast) — First fish returned to sea with prayers.

**Storm's End** (after major storms) — Honoring the lost at sea; names carved in driftwood cast to waves.

**Tideturning** (Spring & Autumn Equinoxes) — Rituals marking seasonal tidal shifts.

#### Vuleala (Air, Weather)

**The First Wind** (Spring Equinox) — Kites flown, birds released, petals scattered.

**The Calm** (rare windless days) — Sacred stillness when all work stops; occurs unpredictably 1–2 times yearly.

**The Last Breeze** (Autumn Equinox) — Thanking for summer winds, preparing for winter.

#### Dirgin (Earth, Caves, Stone)

**Deepdelve** (30 Iquin / September 30) — Blessing support pillars, sealing dangerous passages before winter.

**The Tremor Vigil** (during earthquakes) — Stonekeepers interpret Dirgin's geological messages.

**The Crystal Bloom** (once per century, varies by location) — Witnessing sacred crystal growth in deep caves.

#### Zebulba (Death, Entropy, Undeath)

**Night of Unmaking** (30 Zebul / December 30) — Mass necromantic rituals on the longest night (outlawed in most civilized lands).

**Silent Days** (1–3 Quarz / January 1–3) — Three days of absolute silence, fasting, meditation simulating death.

**Commemoration of Madron** (15 Xan / February 15) — Marking fall of Yrugas and creation of The Hollow Sepulcher.

#### Asti (Healing, Light, Ley-lines)

**The Convergence** (21 Garn / April 21) — Mass healing rituals at ley-line nodes; the sick travel miles to attend.

**Dragon's Flight** (15 Pyran / July 15) — Honoring metallic dragons at The Dragon Watch temple.

**Geometric Proof** (30 Rivol / August 30) — Initiates solve Asti's theorem to complete training.

**Day of the Lines** (1 Iquin / September 1) — Line-Walkers pilgrimage along ley-line routes, purging corruption.

**Golden Vigil** (Winter Solstice) — Channeling light through ley-line network to push back darkness.

#### Gruhathyrir (Wild Nature, Fey, Untamed)

**The Wild Hunt** (Autumn Equinox) — Wyld-Bound druids hunt dangerous predators using only primitive tools.

**The Long Dark** (Winter Solstice) — Fasting through longest night without food or shelter.

**The Green Wrath** (Spring storms) — Celebrating nature's destructive power.

### Evil & Outlawed Deity Observances

These deities' followers operate in secrecy, as their worship is illegal in most civilized lands. Law enforcement agencies track patterns of violence and missing persons around known cult dates.

#### Taovan (The Dark Lord — Corruption & Tyranny)

**The Night of Betrayal** (13 Zebul / December 13) — Celebrating successful corruptions of the past year.

**The Shadow Feast** (30 Zebul / December 30) — Hidden feast mocking heroes, drinking wine mixed with ash from burned holy symbols.

**Day of Chains** (15 Xan / February 15) — Honoring tyranny; cultists don iron chains symbolizing "freedom through domination."

**The Midnight Compact** (variable) — Personal observance when a cultist seals their first devil-pact.

**The Crimson Moon** (during blood moons) — Mass rituals during lunar eclipses for major devil-summonings.

#### Zuel (The Devourer — Conquest & Destruction)

**The Day of Arrival** (15 Minerva / March 15) — Commemorating Zuel's awakening in Mazariim (1866 BW); massive orcish raids.

**The Blood Moon** (every full moon) — Night raids and captive offerings to Zuel.

**Victory's Eve** (2 Quarz / January 2) — Celebrating the day before War's end when victory seemed certain.

**The Broken Hammer** (3 Quarz / January 3) — Anniversary of Zuel's defeat; brutal combat tournaments proving strength.

**The Bleeding Feast** (30 Mord / June 15) — Mocking dwarven "The Forging"; burning stolen dwarven goods.

#### Quarzel (War, Tyranny, Conquest — Historical / Dead God)

Quarzel was destroyed in 1 BW by Zuel the Devourer. These holidays are no longer widely observed, though small cults may still honor them in secret.

**The Day of Iron** (1 Quarz / March 1) — *Historical.* The first day of Quarz was a day of martial preparation. Armies drilled, weapons were blessed, and plans for spring campaigns were finalized. Modern: Largely forgotten, though some military cultures still bless weapons on this day out of tradition (without invoking Quarzel's name).

**The Feast of Victory** (15 Quarz / March 15) — *Historical.* A celebration of past conquests. Modern: Some mercenary companies still celebrate this as an informal tradition, drinking to past battles.

**The Final Stand** (30 Quarz / March 30) — *Memorial.* Originally a day honoring warriors who died in glorious last stands. After Quarzel's destruction, his remaining followers turned this into a memorial day for Quarzel himself. The Crimson Spear cult (believers in Quarzel's resurrection) hold secret vigils on this day. These gatherings are illegal in most kingdoms.

#### Og'thallith (Madness, Demons, Abyss)

**The Night of Unraveling** (Lunar Eclipses — varies) — During lunar eclipses, when the barrier between planes is thin, cultists gather in secret to perform rituals designed to summon minor demons or induce mass hallucinations. These gatherings often end in violence.

**The Spiral Awakening** (1 Zebul / December 1) — The anniversary of the Spiral Plague (4,200 BW). Cultists believe this day is especially auspicious for spreading madness. They distribute cursed items, poison water supplies with hallucinogens, or perform rituals to weaken the barrier between the Material Plane and the Abyss. Law enforcement in major cities increases patrols on this date.

**The Whisper** (Personal — when an individual first hears Og'thallith's voice) — Not a public holiday, but a personal milestone. When someone first hears Og'thallith's voice in their mind, they often mark the occasion by performing a violent act or self-harm, seen as their "true awakening."

#### Ry'le (Aberrations, Shapeshifters, Far Realm)

Ry'le is a recently born entity (awakened circa 102 AW). His cult is young and practices are not yet formalized.

**The Dreaming** (Personal — varies) — When a cultist first makes psychic contact with Ry'le (usually through dreams), they mark the occasion by performing a ritual of "Opening the Third Eye"—a painful process involving psychic meditation and often self-administered trepanation. Many do not survive.

**The Unification** (When someone is absorbed into the Hivemind) — When a mortal is successfully transformed into a thrall and connected to the Ry'lehe Hivemind, existing servants celebrate by killing and consuming a sentient creature as a "welcoming feast."

**Eclipse Rituals** (Solar/Lunar Eclipses — varies) — Like Og'thallith's cultists, Ry'lehe use eclipses to perform major rituals—attempting to summon aberrations, weaken planar barriers, or establish psychic beachheads in population centers.

#### Sheeva (Poison, Revenge, Assassination)

**The Night of Knives** (13 Taovan / November 13) — The anniversary of Sheeva's arrival on Mazariim (1492 BW). Her followers (called Widows) spend the night sharpening blades, brewing poisons, and swearing oaths of revenge. New members are inducted into the clergy on this night.

**The Day of Remembrance** (Personal — anniversary of being wronged) — Each follower observes the anniversary of their greatest grievance. They spend the day in silent meditation, feeding their hatred, planning revenge.

**The Widow's Feast** (When revenge is achieved) — Not a fixed date but a personal celebration. When a follower successfully enacts revenge, they host a feast for fellow Widows, recounting the vengeance in detail. The meal includes at least one dish with poisonous ingredients (carefully prepared to be non-lethal, testing the Widow's skill).

#### Xannith (Greed, Wealth, Corrupt Nobility)

Xannith worship is legal in some nations (where slavery and aristocratic exploitation are permitted) and illegal in others. Her followers often operate openly as "legitimate businesses."

**The Golden Feast** (1 Xan / October 1) — A celebration of wealth and excess. Xannith's followers (the Chains of Gold) host lavish banquets where they display their riches, negotiate business deals, and auction off slaves and rare goods. The poor are explicitly excluded.

**The Day of Chains** (15 Xan / October 15) — Celebrates the "virtue" of debt and slavery. New contracts are signed, debts are called in, and slaves are sold. It is the busiest day of the year for Xannith's clergy and the most profitable.

**The Tithing** (1st day of each season — Quarz, Tio, Paladine, Zebul) — Four times a year, Xannith's followers calculate their net worth and tithe 10% to her temples. In exchange, she blesses them with increased profits. Failure to tithe adequately results in financial ruin (or so the clergy claim).

#### Khan (Dishonor, Betrayal, Darkness — Ejiin Regional Deity)

Khan worship is explicitly forbidden in Ejiin lands. Practicing these rituals is punishable by death. Khan is taught as a cautionary tale, not worshiped publicly.

**The Night of Broken Blades** (Spring Equinox / March 20 — opposite Ashura's First Light) — Secret cultist gatherings to mock bushido. Ceremonially break swords, burn scrolls, swear anti-oaths.

**The Festival of Shadows** (New moon — varies) — When moonlight is absent, Khan is strongest. Cultists commit assassinations, betrayals, corruption.

**The Day of the Coward** (Autumn Equinox / September 22 — opposite Trial of Steel) — Celebrate survival over honor. Share stories of fled battles, broken oaths. Reframe cowardice as "wisdom."

**The Longest Night** (Winter Solstice / December 21 — opposite Tengoku's Vigil) — Khan most powerful. Attempt major corruptions: turning prominent samurai, destroying temples, assassinating leaders.

**The Eclipse of Honor** (Lunar Eclipse — rare) — Ashura and Khan battle visibly. Cultists believe corrupting enough souls might let Khan win. Mass betrayals, widespread assassination attempts, Oni invasions, corruption of entire clans, attempts to desecrate Tengoku.

### Shared & Overlapping Holidays

Several holidays fall on the same dates across multiple faiths, sometimes leading to joint celebrations in mixed communities and sometimes representing deliberate mockery by evil cults:

**1 Quarz / January 1 (New Year)** — Celebrated by humans (New Year's Day), halflings (First Planting), gnomes (Enlightenment Eve), and followers of Paladine (Feast of Honor), making it a truly universal holiday across Mazariim.

**15 Mord / June 15** — Dwarven "The Forging," Halfling "Hearthfire Day," and Gnomish "Tinker's Jubilee" all fall on the same date, sometimes leading to joint celebrations in mixed communities.

**30 Zebul / December 30** — Dwarven "Stoneshaper's Vigil" and Halfling "Festival of Lanterns" occur on the same evening, creating a night of lights, stories, and remembrance across many communities.

The overlap of evil holidays with good deity celebrations (30 Zebul, 30 Mord) is deliberate mockery. Taovanian cultists maintain legitimate cover identities and celebrate in hidden temples. Zuel's orcish followers celebrate openly in their territories but are hunted in civilized lands. Both cults experience increased persecution during their observances as authorities anticipate activity.

Xannith and Polyvin observances often conflict—Polyvin's followers offer alternatives to Xannith's exploitation. Eilistraee's followers often collaborate with Minerva's scholars, and Gyru's followers often work with Hinterhin's agricultural engineers on farming equipment.

During lunar eclipses, followers of Ashura believe the two Ejiin entities physically duel; Ashura always wins but at great cost. Ry'le benefits even when no actual shapeshifters are present—fear of infiltration is a weapon in itself. After Soni Sabu's heresy (831 AW), Ashura's clergy actively hunt Khan cultists and Oni summoners.
